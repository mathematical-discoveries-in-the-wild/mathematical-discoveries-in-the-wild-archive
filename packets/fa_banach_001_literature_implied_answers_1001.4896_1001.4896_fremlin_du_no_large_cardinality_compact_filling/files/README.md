# Literature-implied partial answer: Fremlin Problem DU at cardinality at least continuum

Status: `literature_implied_answer (partial subcase)`

Source problem paper: A. Aviles, G. Plebanek, J. Rodriguez, *The McShane integral in weakly compactly generated spaces*, arXiv:1001.4896.

Supporting paper: P. Dodos and V. Kanellopoulos, *On filling families of finite subsets of the Cantor set*, arXiv:0805.2031.

## Identification

The source paper records Fremlin's Problem DU:

```text
Are there compact epsilon-filling families on uncountable sets?
```

Dodos and Kanellopoulos prove that any `C`-measurable epsilon-filling family over the Cantor set contains all finite subsets of some perfect set. A compact family of finite subsets of the Cantor set is `C`-measurable, so this is impossible: containing all finite subsets of an infinite perfect set would force an infinite subset into the product-topology closure.

Therefore there is no compact epsilon-filling family on the Cantor set. More generally, there is no compact epsilon-filling family on any set of cardinality at least `c`: restrict to a subset of cardinality `c` and transfer by a bijection with the Cantor set.

## Scope

This is a partial negative answer to Problem DU. It settles all sets of cardinality at least continuum, and hence gives a full negative answer under CH. It does not settle possible uncountable sets of cardinality strictly below continuum in models where such cardinals exist.
