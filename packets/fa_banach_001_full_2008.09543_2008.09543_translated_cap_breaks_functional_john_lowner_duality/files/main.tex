\section{Source and exact conjecture}

Ivanov and Tsiutsiurupa define the John $s$-function $\J_s f$ and the
Lowner $s$-function $\Lw_s f$ of a proper log-concave function.  On PDF
page 22 they ask whether, for every finite $s>0$, there is an $f$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:question}
 (\J_s f)^\circ\ne \Lw_s(f^\circ),
\end{equation}
where $\circ$ denotes log-conjugate polarity~\cite{IT}.

\section{Result}

For $s>0$, let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:psi}
 \psi_s(t)=\frac{s}{2}\left[
 \sqrt{1+4(t/s)^2}
 -\log\!\left(\frac{1+\sqrt{1+4(t/s)^2}}{2}\right)-1\right]
 \qquad(t\ge0).
\end{equation}
This is the source's radial potential for Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal functions.

\begin{theorem}[Translated-cap counterexample]\label{thm:main}
Fix any finite $s>0$ and any $a\in(0,1)$.  In dimension one put
\begin{equation}\label{eq:f}
 f_{s,a}(x)=\bigl(1-(x-a)^2\bigr)_+^{s/2}.
\end{equation}
Then $f_{s,a}$ and $f_{s,a}^\circ$ are proper integrable log-concave
functions, and
\[
 (\J_s f_{s,a})^\circ\ne\Lw_s(f_{s,a}^\circ).
\]
Consequently the conjecture in \eqref{eq:question} is true for every $s>0$.
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof intuition}

The cap in \eqref{eq:f} is already a member of the class optimized over in
the John problem, so it is its own John function.  Translating a function by
$a$ multiplies its polar by the exponential tilt $e^{-ay}$.  That tilt is the
entire obstruction.  Every one-dimensional Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal density has
the same exponential decay rate at the two ends of the line.  The tilted
polar has decay rates $1+a$ and $1-a$, which differ when $a\ne0$; hence it
cannot itself be a Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal density and cannot equal the Lowner
optimizer.

\section{Proof}

Write
\[
 h_s(x)=(1-x^2)_+^{s/2},\qquad
 \ell_s(y)=e^{-\psi_s(|y|)}.
\]
The source's centered polar identity (Lemma 6.1) is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:centeredpolar}
 h_s^\circ=\ell_s.
\end{equation}

The function $f_{s,a}=h_s(\,\cdot-a)$ is a John $s$-ellipsoidal function.
It is feasible in the optimization defining $\J_s f_{s,a}$.  Any other
feasible nonnegative function $g$ satisfies $g\le f_{s,a}$ and therefore
$\int g\le\int f_{s,a}$.  Thus
\begin{equation}\label{eq:johnself}
 \J_s f_{s,a}=f_{s,a}.
\end{equation}
(The uniqueness theorem in the source gives the same conclusion.)

If $q=e^{-\varphi}$ and $q_a(x)=q(x-a)$, then
\[
 (-\log q_a)^*(y)=ay+\varphi^*(y),
\]
so log-conjugate polarity obeys
\begin{equation}\label{eq:translation}
 q_a^\circ(y)=e^{-ay}q^\circ(y).
\end{equation}
Combining \eqref{eq:centeredpolar} and \eqref{eq:translation} yields the
explicit polar
\begin{equation}\label{eq:polar}
 f_{s,a}^\circ(y)=e^{-ay-\psi_s(|y|)}.
\end{equation}

We next check that this function is integrable and is not Lowner
$s$-ellipsoidal.  Differentiating \eqref{eq:psi} gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:psider}
 \psi_s'(t)=
 \frac{2t}{s\bigl(1+\sqrt{1+4(t/s)^2}\bigr)}
 \longrightarrow 1
 \qquad(t\to\infty).
\end{equation}
Since $0<a<1$, equation \eqref{eq:psider} shows that the negative logarithm
$ay+\psi_s(|y|)$ grows linearly with positive rate at both ends.  Hence
$f_{s,a}^\circ$ is integrable.  It is plainly positive, continuous, and
log-concave, so the source's existence theorem defines $\Lw_s(f_{s,a}^\circ)$.

Every one-dimensional Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal function has the form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lclass}
 y\longmapsto \alpha e^{-\psi_s(b|y-c|)}
 \qquad(\alpha,b>0,\ c\in\R).
\end{equation}
The derivative of the negative logarithm in \eqref{eq:lclass} tends to $b$
as $y\to+\infty$ and to $-b$ as $y\to-\infty$.  In contrast, by
\eqref{eq:polar}--\eqref{eq:psider}, the corresponding limits for
$f_{s,a}^\circ$ are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:slopes}
 1+a\quad(y\to+\infty),
 \qquad a-1\quad(y\to-\infty).
\end{equation}
If \eqref{eq:polar} had the form \eqref{eq:lclass}, comparison of the two
limits would force
\[
 b=1+a\qquad\text{and}\qquad b=1-a,
\]
and hence $a=0$, contrary to the choice of $a$.  Therefore
$f_{s,a}^\circ$ is not Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal.  But
$\Lw_s(f_{s,a}^\circ)$ is Lowner $s$-ellipsoidal by definition, so they cannot
be equal.  Together with \eqref{eq:johnself}, this proves Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

\section{Verification report}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Exact target.}  The construction works for every finite $s>0$,
which is precisely the quantifier in the source conjecture.
\item \textbf{Admissibility.}  The translated cap is compactly supported,
proper, integrable, and log-concave.  Its polar is integrable exactly when
$|a|<1$; choosing $0<a<1$ supplies a strict margin at both tails.
\item \textbf{No optimizer computation.}  The proof uses only that every
Lowner optimizer belongs to the class \eqref{eq:lclass}.  Its parameters need
not be determined.
\item \textbf{Dimension.}  A one-dimensional example is sufficient for the
existential conjecture.  No hidden high-dimensional hypothesis is used.
\item \textbf{Limitation.}  The argument uses the finite-$s$ tail limit in
\eqref{eq:psider}; it does not assert the same construction at the Gaussian
endpoint $s=\infty$.
\end{itemize}
