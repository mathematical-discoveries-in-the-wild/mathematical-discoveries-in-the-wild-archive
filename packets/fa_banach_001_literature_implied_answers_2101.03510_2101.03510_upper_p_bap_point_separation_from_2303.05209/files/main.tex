\section*{Status}

This is a literature-implied partial answer to Remark 6.2 of
Jardon-Sanchez--Laustsen--Taylor--Tradacete--Troitsky \cite{JLTTT2022}.
The source asks whether real-valued lattice homomorphisms on
\(\FBL^{\mathcal D}[E]\) separate points, with the free upper
\(p\)-estimate lattice singled out as already unclear.  Combining the later
finite-dimensional representation and approximation machinery of Oikhberg
\cite{Oikhberg2023} gives a positive answer for
\(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\) whenever \(E\) has the bounded approximation
property.  The unrestricted case remains open.

\section*{Supporting ingredients from Oikhberg}

Oikhberg \cite{Oikhberg2023} studies the free upper \(p\)-estimate lattice
\(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\), \(1<p<\infty\).  We use two consequences from that
paper.

First, for finite-dimensional \(F\), the formal identities
\[
  \FBL[F]\longrightarrow \FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\longrightarrow
  \FBL^{(\infty)}[F]
\]
are contractive, and \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\) is represented as a lattice of
continuous positively homogeneous functions on the sphere of \(F^*\); see the
discussion around Remark 2.5 and Corollary 2.7 of \cite{Oikhberg2023}.

Second, the proof of Theorem 2.9 of \cite{Oikhberg2023}, in the same manner
as Theorem 2.8 there, uses finite-rank approximants \(u_\alpha:E\to E_\alpha\)
and the induced lattice homomorphisms
\[
  \overline{u_\alpha}\colon \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\to
  \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E_\alpha],
  \qquad
  \overline{j_\alpha}\colon \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E_\alpha]\to
  \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E],
\]
where \(j_\alpha:E_\alpha\hookrightarrow E\), and
\(\overline{j_\alpha}\,\overline{u_\alpha}\to I\) point-norm when
\((u_\alpha)\) is a bounded approximation of \(I_E\).

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The source obstruction is that a completion of the algebraic free lattice
could, a priori, contain a ``ghost'' element invisible to every point
evaluation on \(E^*\).  Bounded approximation rules this out by testing the
ghost through finite-dimensional shadows.  In finite dimensions the later
functional representation says point evaluations are faithful.  If an element
is killed by every \(\widehat{x^*}\) on the large free lattice, then every
finite-dimensional shadow \(\overline{u_\alpha}z\) is killed by all point
evaluations on \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E_\alpha]\), hence is zero.  The finite-rank
shadows approximate the identity, so \(z=0\).

\section*{Partial theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(1<p<\infty\), and let \(E\) be a Banach space with the bounded
approximation property.  Then the real-valued lattice homomorphisms
\[
  \{\widehat{x^*}:\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\to\R \mid x^*\in E^*\}
\]
separate the points of \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\).  Equivalently, the canonical
map from \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\) into the space of continuous positively
homogeneous functions on the dual ball is injective.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We first record the finite-dimensional case.  Let \(F\) be finite-dimensional
and let \(L_F\) be the algebraic free vector lattice generated by the functions
\(\delta_x\), \(x\in F\), on \(F^*\).  The construction of
\(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\) gives
\[
  \norm{f}_\infty\leq \norm{f}_{\uparrow p}\qquad(f\in L_F).
\]
The identity map on \(F\) induces a contractive lattice homomorphism
\(\FBL[F]\to \FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\), so
\[
  \norm{f}_{\uparrow p}\leq \norm{f}_{\FBL[F]}.
\]
Since \(F\) is finite-dimensional, the usual functional representation of
\(\FBL[F]\) gives \(\norm{f}_{\FBL[F]}\leq C_F\norm{f}_\infty\) on \(L_F\);
for instance one may take \(C_F=\dim F\) from the standard
\(\pi_1(I_F)\leq \dim F\) estimate.  Hence the \(\uparrow p\)-norm and the
supremum norm are equivalent on \(L_F\).  The completion
\(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\) therefore embeds injectively into the corresponding
supremum-norm completion.  Thus the point evaluations, equivalently the
homomorphisms \(\widehat{y^*}\) for \(y^*\in F^*\), separate points of
\(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[F]\).

Now let \(E\) have the \(\lambda\)-bounded approximation property.  Choose a
net of finite-rank operators \(u_\alpha:E\to E\) such that
\(\sup_\alpha\norm{u_\alpha}\leq\lambda\) and \(u_\alpha x\to x\) for every
\(x\in E\).  Put \(E_\alpha=u_\alpha(E)\), and write \(u_\alpha:E\to E_\alpha\)
for the same map and \(j_\alpha:E_\alpha\hookrightarrow E\) for inclusion.
By the universal property of \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}\), these operators induce
lattice homomorphisms
\[
  \overline{u_\alpha}:\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\to
  \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E_\alpha],
  \qquad
  \overline{j_\alpha}:\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E_\alpha]\to
  \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E].
\]
The operators \(A_\alpha=\overline{j_\alpha}\,\overline{u_\alpha}\) have
uniformly bounded norms.  On the dense algebraic free vector lattice
\(\FVL[E]\), \(A_\alpha\) sends a lattice expression in
\(\delta_{x_1},\ldots,\delta_{x_n}\) to the same expression in
\(\delta_{u_\alpha x_1},\ldots,\delta_{u_\alpha x_n}\), which converges in
norm by continuity of lattice operations.  Therefore \(A_\alpha\to I\)
point-norm on all of \(\FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\).

Let \(z\in \FBL^{\uparrow p}[E]\) satisfy
\(\widehat{x^*}(z)=0\) for every \(x^*\in E^*\).  Fix \(\alpha\) and
\(y^*\in E_\alpha^*\).  Then
\[
  \widehat{y^*}(\overline{u_\alpha}z)
  =
  \widehat{u_\alpha^*y^*}(z)
  =0.
\]
The finite-dimensional case applied to \(E_\alpha\) gives
\(\overline{u_\alpha}z=0\).  Hence \(A_\alpha z=0\) for every \(\alpha\).
Since \(A_\alpha z\to z\), we conclude that \(z=0\).  This proves point
separation.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and limitations}

This does not settle the general question from \cite{JLTTT2022}.  It covers
the free upper \(p\)-estimate lattice only under the bounded approximation
property assumption on \(E\).  It also does not address arbitrary convexity
conditions \(\mathcal D\).  The result is best viewed as a BAP/localization
corollary of \cite{Oikhberg2023}, not as an independent full resolution.
