\section*{Packet Status}

This packet records a literature-implied answer to a partial subcase, not an
original proof from the run.  The source problem in Antunes--Beanland asks for
the full Mazur--Ulam property for combinatorial Banach spaces, including
arbitrary target spaces.  Fakhoury's later theorem proves the real self-sphere
Schreier case.  Thus the result below should be treated as a provenance/status
packet for a solved subcase and not as a full answer to Problem 3.2.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source is Leandro Antunes and Kevin Beanland,
\emph{Surjective isometries on Banach sequence spaces: a survey},
arXiv:2110.11786.  Problem 3.2 asks:

\begin{quote}
Let $\F$ be a regular family and $X$ a Banach space. Suppose that
$V:S_{X_\F}\to S_X$ is a surjective, not necessarily linear, isometry.
Does there exist a linear surjective isometry $U:X_\F\to X$ so that
$U|_{S_{X_\F}}=V$?
\end{quote}

The same page states that this problem is open even when
$\F=\Sc_1$, the classical Schreier family.

\section*{Supporting Literature}

The supporting source is Micheline Fakhoury,
\emph{Tingley's problem for Schreier spaces and their $p$-convexifications},
arXiv:2412.00891.  Its Theorem 1.1 states that if
$(\Sc_\alpha)_{\alpha<\omega_1}$ is a good transfinite Schreier sequence,
$p\in[1,\infty)$, and $1\leq \alpha<\omega_1$, then every surjective
isometry of the unit sphere of $X_{\Sc_\alpha,p}$ extends to a linear
surjective isometry of $X_{\Sc_\alpha,p}$; moreover, for $p\in\{1,2\}$ the
goodness assumption is not needed.

\end{center}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The identification is direct.  The source problem singles out the classical
Schreier space $X_{\Sc_1}$ as an unsolved case.  Fakhoury's notation satisfies
$X_{\F,1}=X_\F$, and $\Sc_1$ is exactly the classical Schreier family.  Applying
Fakhoury's theorem with $\alpha=1$ and $p=1$ therefore gives extension of every
surjective isometry from $S_{X_{\Sc_1}}$ onto itself.  The result is only a
self-map subcase because Fakhoury's theorem does not allow an arbitrary target
sphere $S_X$.

\section*{Formal Statement}

\begin{theorem}
Let $X_{\Sc_1}$ be the real Schreier space.  Every surjective isometry
$T:S_{X_{\Sc_1}}\to S_{X_{\Sc_1}}$ extends to a linear surjective isometry
$\widetilde T:X_{\Sc_1}\to X_{\Sc_1}$.  Consequently, the self-map real
Schreier-space subcase of Problem 3.2 in arXiv:2110.11786 has an affirmative
answer.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fakhoury defines, for a combinatorial family $\F$ and $p\in(1,\infty)$, the
$p$-convexification $X_{\F,p}$, and sets $X_{\F,1}=X_\F$.  Her introduction
identifies $\Sc_1$ as the classical Schreier family and $X_{\Sc_1}$ as the
Schreier space.  Theorem 1.1 of arXiv:2412.00891 applies to every
$1\leq \alpha<\omega_1$ and every $p\in[1,\infty)$; when $p=1$, no additional
``good'' hypothesis on the transfinite Schreier sequence is needed.

Specialize that theorem to $\alpha=1$ and $p=1$.  Then every surjective
isometry of the unit sphere of $X_{\Sc_1,1}=X_{\Sc_1}$ extends to a linear
surjective isometry of $X_{\Sc_1,1}=X_{\Sc_1}$.  This is precisely the
subcase of Antunes--Beanland Problem 3.2 in which $\F=\Sc_1$ and the target
space is again $X_{\Sc_1}$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not prove that $X_{\Sc_1}$ has the full Mazur--Ulam property
as formulated in the source survey, because the source asks about
surjective isometries $S_{X_{\Sc_1}}\to S_X$ for arbitrary Banach target spaces
$X$.  It also does not address complex Schreier spaces.  The result recorded
here is the real self-sphere case only.
