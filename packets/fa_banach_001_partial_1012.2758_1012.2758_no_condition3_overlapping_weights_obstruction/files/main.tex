\section*{Status}

This is a partial result and obstruction packet for Argyros--Petsoulas,
\emph{A \(c_0\) saturated Banach space with tight structure},
arXiv:1012.2758 \cite{AP}.  It does not solve whether the source space
\(\Xzero\) is strictly quasi-prime.  It also does not disprove all tight
properties of the variant obtained by deleting condition (3) from the
definition of \(G_0\).  It proves a narrow obstruction: after condition (3)
is removed, bounded norm alone cannot exclude finite overlapping families
of pairwise different weights.

\section*{Definitions}

Let \((m_j)\) and \((n_j)\) be the sequences from \cite{AP}.  Let
\(\Gminus\) be the minimal subset of \(c_{00}(\N)\) satisfying the first
three closure requirements in the definition of \(G_0\): it contains the
coordinate functionals \(e_t^*\), is symmetric, and is closed under the
even operations
\[
  (f_1<\cdots<f_d,\ d\le n_{2j})
  \quad\mapsto\quad
  \frac1{m_{2j}}\sum_{i=1}^d f_i.
\]
Thus \(\Gminus\) is the \(G_0\)-core with the Hilbertian closure under
rational \(\ell_2\)-combinations of pairwise different weights deleted.
Let \(\Wminus\) be the corresponding once-special extension, defined as in
\cite{AP} but using \(\Gminus\) in place of \(G_0\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The original proof uses condition (3) exactly when many functionals of
pairwise different weights all act on the same block vector.  Condition (3)
turns such an overlapping family into one \(G_0\)-functional by an
\(\ell_2\)-combination.  Once condition (3) is deleted, the remaining even
operations only combine successive functionals.  The smallest possible
overlap, where every functional is supported on the same coordinate, already
survives with norm one.  Therefore no repair based only on boundedness and
different weights can reproduce that step.

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[finite overlapping different-weight families]
Fix \(N\in\N\), \(\eta>0\), and pairwise distinct even indices
\[
  1\le j_1<j_2<\cdots<j_N .
\]
There exists an odd level \(r\) such that for every coordinate \(t\in\N\)
the vector \(x=e_t\) satisfies
\[
  \|x\|_{\Gminus}=\|x\|_{\Wminus}=1
\]
and the functionals
\[
  g_i=\frac1{m_{2j_i}}e_t^*, \qquad i=1,\ldots,N,
\]
belong to \(\Gminus\), have pairwise different weights \(m_{2j_i}\), have
the same range \(\{t\}\), and satisfy
\[
  g_i(x)>\frac{\eta}{n_{2r+1}}
  \qquad (i=1,\ldots,N).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(r\) so large that \(n_{2r+1}>\eta m_{2j_N}\).  For each \(i\),
the \(d=1\) case of the even operation applied to \(e_t^*\) gives
\[
  g_i=\frac1{m_{2j_i}}e_t^*\in \Gminus,
\]
and by the source convention this functional has weight \(m_{2j_i}\).
The weights are pairwise different by the choice of the \(j_i\)'s, and all
the ranges are the singleton \(\{t\}\).

Since \(e_t^*\in \Gminus\subseteq \Wminus\), both norms of \(e_t\) are at
least one.  Conversely, every functional in \(\Wminus\) has
\(\ell_\infty\)-norm at most one.  This follows by induction from the
coordinate functionals: symmetry preserves \(\ell_\infty\)-norm, even
averages of successive functionals have \(\ell_\infty\)-norm at most one,
the once-special odd averages have \(\ell_\infty\)-norm at most one because
their summands are successive, and interval restrictions do not increase
\(\ell_\infty\)-norm.  Hence \(|h(e_t)|\le 1\) for all
\(h\in\Wminus\), and
\[
  \|e_t\|_{\Gminus}=\|e_t\|_{\Wminus}=1.
\]
Finally,
\[
  g_i(e_t)=\frac1{m_{2j_i}}
  \ge \frac1{m_{2j_N}}
  > \frac{\eta}{n_{2r+1}},
\]
as required.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[obstruction to the direct repair]
In the condition-(3)-free variant, finite families of pairwise different
weights can overlap completely on a single bounded vector.  Therefore the
case of the source proof that combines such a family by condition (3)
cannot be recovered from the remaining even operations plus bounded
\(\Gminus\)-norm alone.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply the theorem with arbitrary \(N\).  The resulting functionals have
common range \(\{t\}\), so no two of them are successive.  The remaining
closure operation in \(\Gminus\) combines only successive families.  Thus
the deleted Hilbertian closure is exactly the rule that allowed the source
proof to combine overlapping different-weight functionals.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes and Limitations}

The proof is finite and formal; no computational search is used.  The only
structural facts used are the \(d=1\) case of the even operation and the
standard \(\ell_\infty\)-bound for functionals generated by these norming
rules.

This packet should be reviewed as a sharp obstruction, not as a full answer
to the source paper's open questions.  It does not prove that the analogue
of the key averaging proposition fails, nor does it construct a complemented
subspace witnessing non-strict-quasi-primeness.  It shows that one natural
repair route for the condition-(3)-free variant is blocked at the finite
combinatorial level.

Bounded novelty check: local run indexes for arXiv:1012.2758 had no promoted
solution packet, only a prior attempt note.  External searches for the exact
source title, ``\(\mathfrak X_0\) strictly quasi-prime'', and the
condition-(3)-free \(G_0\) variant found the source paper and the related
Zisimopoulou Bourgain--Delbaen sum paper \cite{Z}, but no direct resolution
of the specific \(\Xzero\) question or this finite obstruction.
