# Negative answer to the nonperiodic projection strong-convergence question

Status: `literature_implied_answer`.

Source/open-problem signal: arXiv:0905.3520, P. L. Combettes and N. N.
Reyes, *Functions with Prescribed Best Linear Approximations*.

## Identification

The source paper's main inverse best approximation questions are answered in
the same paper and are not promoted here. The promoted signal is the sentence
in Section 3, p. 11, after equations (3.8)-(3.9): if finitely many projectors
are activated nonperiodically, Amemiya-Ando gives weak convergence, and the
authors record as open whether strong convergence holds.

Kopecka and Muller later identify the same Amemiya-Ando norm-convergence
problem in *A product of three projections*, Studia Mathematica 223 (2014),
175-186, DOI `10.4064/sm223-2-4`. Their abstract records Paszkiewicz's
five-subspace counterexample and then constructs three closed subspaces of an
infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, with a sequence of orthogonal projections
that does not converge in norm, resolving the problem fully.

Since strong convergence of vectors in Hilbert space is norm convergence, and
the source's affine projection framework contains the homogeneous linear
subspace case, this gives a negative answer to the source's nonperiodic
strong-convergence question.

## Scope

This packet is not a new proof. It is also not a claim about the source's
periodic projection algorithm, about the IBAP characterizations, or about the
linear convergence theorem proved later in the source paper. The negative
answer concerns arbitrary/nonperiodic activation of a finite family of closed
subspace projections in infinite-dimensional Hilbert space.

The supporting authors explicitly identify and resolve the Amemiya-Ando
norm-convergence problem. The link to this arXiv:0905.3520 sentence is an
agent-identified implication rather than a direct citation to Combettes-Reyes.
