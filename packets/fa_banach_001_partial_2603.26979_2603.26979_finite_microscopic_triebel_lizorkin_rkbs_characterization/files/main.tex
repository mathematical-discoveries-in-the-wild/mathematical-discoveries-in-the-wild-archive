\section*{Source Question}

Heeringa characterizes all pairs of Bessel potential spaces having the
Bessel (Mat\'ern) kernel
\[
  K_s(x,y)=G_{2s}(x-y)
\]
as reproducing kernel \cite{Heeringa}.  In the future-work paragraph on PDF
page 14 of arXiv:2603.26979, the paper asks for the corresponding
Triebel--Lizorkin characterization:

\begin{center}
  \includegraphics[width=0.94\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In transcription: ``The Bessel potential spaces are isomorph to the special
case $q=2$. It is natural to ask which Triebel--Lizorkin spaces admit
$K_s$ as kernel.''  The repeated letter $q$ in the source denotes the
microscopic Triebel--Lizorkin exponent.  Below we use $p,q$ for spatial
integrability and $a,b$ for the microscopic exponents.

\section*{Result and Scope}

The main theorem completely answers the question when
\[
  1<p,q<\infty,
  \qquad 1\leq a,b<\infty.
\]
Thus the spatial exponents are reflexive and the microscopic exponents are
finite; $a=1$ and $b=1$ are included.  The spatial endpoints and the
nonseparable cases $a=\infty$ or $b=\infty$ are not claimed.

We use the inhomogeneous Triebel--Lizorkin spaces on $\R^d$.  Put
\[
  \Lambdaop^t=(I-\Delta)^{t/2},
  \qquad
  \widehat{G_\alpha}(\xi)=(1+|\xi|^2)^{-\alpha/2}.
\]
This defines $G_\alpha$ as a tempered distribution for every real
$\alpha$.  In particular $G_0=\delta_0$,
$\Lambdaop^tG_\alpha=G_{\alpha-t}$, and
$\Lambdaop^{2s}G_{2s}=\delta_0$.  Heeringa's operator
$\mathcal J_{-2s}$ is our $\Lambdaop^{2s}$.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Three sharp facts fit together.

First, the kernel itself determines the pairing.  On Schwartz functions,
the two reproducing identities force
\[
  [g,f]=\ip{\Lambdaop^{2s}f}{g}.
\]
Since Schwartz functions are dense for finite microscopic exponents, no
other continuous pairing can evade this identity.  Triebel--Lizorkin
duality therefore turns existence of the pairing into the sharp embedding
\[
  \Fsp{u-2s}{p}{a}\hookrightarrow \Fsp{-v}{q'}{b'}.
\]

Second, critical Triebel--Lizorkin Sobolev embeddings behave differently
according to whether spatial integrability improves.  If $p<q'$, the
critical embedding is independent of $a,b$.  If $p=q'$, there is no
spatial gain and equality requires $a\leq b'$, equivalently
$1/a+1/b\geq1$.  This is the only new boundary clause.

Third, one dyadic calculation gives the exact membership criterion
\[
  G_\alpha\in \Fsp{t}{r}{c}
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  t<\alpha-\frac d{r'}.
\]
Taking $G_0=\delta_0$ detects bounded point evaluations; taking
$G_{2s}$ detects membership of all kernel sections.  These three facts
give both necessity and sufficiency.

\section*{Function-Space Inputs}

We record the standard facts used below.  For
$1<r<\infty$, $1\leq c<\infty$, and $z,t\in\R$,
\begin{align}
  (\Fsp{z}{r}{c})^*&=\Fsp{-z}{r'}{c'},
  \label{eq:dual}\tag{D}\\
  \Lambdaop^t:\Fsp{z}{r}{c}&\longrightarrow
  \Fsp{z-t}{r}{c}
  \quad\text{is an isomorphism}.
  \label{eq:lift}\tag{L}
\end{align}
The duality formula includes $c=1$, in which case $c'=\infty$.
Moreover, $\Sch(\R^d)$ is dense when $c<\infty$.  These are classical
Triebel--Lizorkin facts; see Triebel \cite[Sections 2.3 and 2.11]{Triebel}.

We also use the following exact unweighted embedding theorem, obtained by
specializing Theorem 1.2 of Meyries--Veraar \cite{MeyriesVeraar}.

\begin{proposition}[Sharp Triebel--Lizorkin embedding]
\label{prop:embedding}
Let $1<r_0,r_1<\infty$, $1\leq c_0,c_1\leq\infty$, and
$z_0,z_1\in\R$.  Then
\[
  \Fsp{z_0}{r_0}{c_0}\hookrightarrow
  \Fsp{z_1}{r_1}{c_1}
\]
if and only if one of the following holds:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $r_0<r_1$ and
  $z_0-d/r_0\geq z_1-d/r_1$;
  \item $r_0=r_1$, and either $z_0>z_1$, or
  $z_0=z_1$ and $c_0\leq c_1$.
\end{enumerate}
No such embedding holds when $r_0>r_1$.
\end{proposition}

The important feature is that, when $r_0<r_1$, equality of Sobolev
indices imposes no condition on $c_0,c_1$.

\section*{The Bessel-Distribution Lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Exact membership]
\label{lem:bessel-membership}
Let $\alpha,t\in\R$, $1<r<\infty$, and
$1\leq c\leq\infty$.  Then
\[
  G_\alpha\in\Fsp{t}{r}{c}(\R^d)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  t<\alpha-\frac d{r'}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the lifting property, it is enough to prove
\[
  G_\beta\in\Fsp{0}{r}{c}
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \beta>\frac d{r'},
  \qquad \beta=\alpha-t.
\]

Suppose first that $\beta>d/r'$.  Choose $\varepsilon>0$ such that
$\beta-\varepsilon>d/r'$.  The exact local integrability criterion for
Bessel kernels gives
\[
  \Lambdaop^\varepsilon G_\beta=G_{\beta-\varepsilon}
  \in L^r(\R^d);
\]
see, for example, Lemma 1 of \cite{Heeringa}.  Hence
$G_\beta\in H^{\varepsilon,r}=\Fsp{\varepsilon}{r}{2}$.
A strict loss of smoothness embeds this space into
$\Fsp{0}{r}{c}$, for every $1\leq c\leq\infty$.

For necessity, fix a nonzero smooth annular cutoff $\varphi$ from an
inhomogeneous Littlewood--Paley decomposition and write $\Delta_j$ for
the associated high-frequency blocks.  For $j\geq1$, scaling gives
\begin{equation}
  \Delta_jG_\beta(x)
  =2^{j(d-\beta)}h_j(2^jx),
  \qquad
  h_j=\mathcal F^{-1}\!\left[
    \varphi(\eta)(2^{-2j}+|\eta|^2)^{-\beta/2}
  \right].
  \label{eq:block-scaling}
\end{equation}
Because $\varphi$ is supported away from zero,
$h_j\to h=\mathcal F^{-1}[\varphi(\eta)|\eta|^{-\beta}]$ in the
Schwartz topology, and $h\neq0$.  Therefore
\begin{equation}
  \|\Delta_jG_\beta\|_{L^r}
  \asymp 2^{j(d/r'-\beta)}
  \quad (j\ \text{large}).
  \label{eq:block-norm}
\end{equation}

If $c<\infty$, the elementary embedding
\[
  \Fsp{0}{r}{c}\hookrightarrow
  \Bsp{0}{r}{\max(r,c)}
\]
and \eqref{eq:block-norm} rule out $\beta\leq d/r'$: the block norms
would not lie in the required finite $\ell^{\max(r,c)}$ space.

For $c=\infty$, the same Besov embedding rules out
$\beta<d/r'$.  At equality, \eqref{eq:block-scaling} becomes
\[
  \Delta_jG_\beta(x)=2^{jd/r}h_j(2^jx).
\]
Choose a ball $B$, centered away from the origin, on which
$|h|\geq2\kappa>0$.  Uniform convergence gives
$|h_j|\geq\kappa$ on $B$ for all large $j$.  Passing to indices
$j_n=j_0+nL$ with $L$ fixed and sufficiently large makes the annuli
$E_n=2^{-j_n}B$ pairwise disjoint.  On $E_n$,
\[
  \sup_j|\Delta_jG_\beta(x)|
  \geq \kappa 2^{j_nd/r}.
\]
Consequently each $E_n$ contributes at least
\[
  \kappa^r2^{j_nd}|E_n|=\kappa^r|B|
\]
to the $r$-th power of the $\Fsp{0}{r}{\infty}$ norm.  Summation over
the disjoint annuli diverges.  Equality is therefore impossible, completing
the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The case $\alpha=0$ says
\[
  \delta_0\in\Fsp{t}{r}{c}
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad t<-d/r'.
\]
The strict endpoint is essential below.
\end{remark}

\section*{Main Characterization}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-microscopic Triebel--Lizorkin characterization]
\label{thm:main}
Let $d\in\mathbb N$, $s>0$, $u,v\in\R$,
$1<p,q<\infty$, and $1\leq a,b<\infty$.  The spaces
\[
  X=\Fsp{u}{p}{a}(\R^d),
  \qquad
  Y=\Fsp{v}{q}{b}(\R^d)
\]
form an RKBS pair, in the sense of \cite{Heeringa}, with kernel
$K_s(x,y)=G_{2s}(x-y)$ if and only if
\begin{align}
  \frac1p+\frac1q&\geq1,
  \label{eq:pq}\\
  \frac dp<u&<2s-\frac d{p'},
  \label{eq:u}\\
  \frac dq<v&<2s-\frac d{q'},
  \label{eq:v}\\
  u+v&\geq
  2s+d\left(\frac1p+\frac1q-1\right).
  \label{eq:balance}
\end{align}
In the doubly critical case
\begin{equation}
  \frac1p+\frac1q=1,
  \qquad u+v=2s,
  \label{eq:doubly-critical}
\end{equation}
one must additionally have
\begin{equation}
  \frac1a+\frac1b\geq1.
  \label{eq:micro}
\end{equation}
There is no microscopic restriction at equality in \eqref{eq:balance}
when $1/p+1/q>1$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Necessity]
Suppose $X,Y$ form an RKBS pair with this kernel.

\emph{Point evaluations.}
Evaluation at zero is bounded on $X$.  On the dense Schwartz subspace,
this functional is the distribution $\delta_0$; hence, by
\eqref{eq:dual},
\[
  \delta_0\in X^*=\Fsp{-u}{p'}{a'}.
\]
Lemma \ref{lem:bessel-membership}, with $\alpha=0$, gives
$-u<-d/p$, that is, $u>d/p$.  The same argument on $Y$ gives
$v>d/q$.

\emph{Kernel sections.}
The RKBS-pair definition requires
$K_s(\cdot,y)\in X$ and $K_s(x,\cdot)\in Y$.  Translations are
isometries, so Lemma \ref{lem:bessel-membership}, now with
$\alpha=2s$, gives
\[
  u<2s-d/p',
  \qquad v<2s-d/q'.
\]
This proves the strict bounds in \eqref{eq:u}--\eqref{eq:v}.

\emph{The pairing is forced.}
Write $[g,f]$ for the continuous pairing on $Y\times X$.  For
$f,\psi\in\Sch(\R^d)$, the first reproducing identity and continuity of
the pairing give
\begin{align*}
  \int_{\R^d} f(x)\psi(x)\,dx
  &=\int_{\R^d}[K_s(x,\cdot),f]\psi(x)\,dx\\
  &=\left[\int_{\R^d}\psi(x)G_{2s}(x-\cdot)\,dx,f\right]\\
  &=[\Lambdaop^{-2s}\psi,f].
\end{align*}
The Bochner integral is legitimate because translations are strongly
continuous and have constant $Y$-norm.  Since
$\Lambdaop^{-2s}$ is an automorphism of $\Sch$, putting
$g=\Lambdaop^{-2s}\psi$ yields
\begin{equation}
  [g,f]=\ip{f}{\Lambdaop^{2s}g}
  =\ip{\Lambdaop^{2s}f}{g}
  \qquad(f,g\in\Sch).
  \label{eq:forced-pairing}
\end{equation}
Density and continuity therefore force
\[
  \Lambdaop^{2s}:X\longrightarrow Y^*
  =\Fsp{-v}{q'}{b'}
\]
to be bounded.  By lifting, this is precisely
\begin{equation}
  \Fsp{u-2s}{p}{a}\hookrightarrow
  \Fsp{-v}{q'}{b'}.
  \label{eq:key-embedding}
\end{equation}

Proposition \ref{prop:embedding} first forces $p\leq q'$, which is
\eqref{eq:pq}.  If $p<q'$, \eqref{eq:key-embedding} is equivalent to
\[
  u-2s-\frac dp\geq -v-\frac d{q'},
\]
namely \eqref{eq:balance}; equality permits arbitrary $a,b$.
If $p=q'$, the embedding holds exactly when $u+v>2s$, or when
$u+v=2s$ and $a\leq b'$.  The latter inequality is equivalent to
\eqref{eq:micro}.  This proves every necessary condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Sufficiency]
Assume \eqref{eq:pq}--\eqref{eq:balance}, together with
\eqref{eq:micro} when \eqref{eq:doubly-critical} holds.

The strict lower bounds imply the standard embeddings
\[
  X\hookrightarrow C_b(\R^d),
  \qquad Y\hookrightarrow C_b(\R^d).
\]
For completeness, combine
$\Fsp{u}{p}{a}\hookrightarrow\Bsp{u}{p}{\max(p,a)}$ with Bernstein's
inequality: the factor $2^{-j(u-d/p)}$ makes the Littlewood--Paley sum
converge uniformly.  Thus both spaces are Banach spaces of bounded
continuous functions with bounded evaluations.

The strict upper bounds and Lemma \ref{lem:bessel-membership} give all
kernel-section inclusions
\[
  G_{2s}(\cdot-y)\in X,
  \qquad G_{2s}(x-\cdot)\in Y.
\]
The remaining parameter conditions and Proposition
\ref{prop:embedding} give \eqref{eq:key-embedding}.  Define
\begin{equation}
  [g,f]=\ip{\Lambdaop^{2s}f}{g}_{Y^*,Y}.
  \label{eq:pairing-definition}
\end{equation}
This pairing is continuous.  It separates $X$ and $Y$: the operator
$\Lambdaop^{2s}$ is injective on tempered distributions, and Schwartz
tests separate every nonzero tempered distribution.  Self-adjointness gives
the same conclusion in the other variable.

Finally,
$\Lambdaop^{2s}G_{2s}(\cdot-y)=\delta_y$.  Hence
\[
  [g,K_s(\cdot,y)]=\ip{\delta_y}{g}=g(y).
\]
Using self-adjointness in \eqref{eq:pairing-definition} also gives
\[
  [K_s(x,\cdot),f]
  =\ip{\Lambdaop^{2s}f}{G_{2s}(x-\cdot)}
  =\ip{f}{\delta_x}=f(x).
\]
Both reproducing identities hold, so $X,Y$ form the asserted RKBS pair.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The theorem passes the following independent consistency checks.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Setting $a=b=2$ recovers exactly the part of Heeringa's main
  theorem with $1<p,q<\infty$.
  \item On the H\"older face $p=q'$, equality $u+v=2s$ reduces the
  pairing condition to the sequence-space threshold
  $a\leq b'$, equivalently $1/a+1/b\geq1$.
  \item The characterization is invariant under interchanging
  $(u,p,a)$ and $(v,q,b)$.
  \item Combining $u>d/p$ with $u<2s-d/p'$ forces $2s>d$, so a
  separate lower bound on $s$ is unnecessary.
  \item All strict endpoint claims for evaluations and kernel sections are
  instances of Lemma \ref{lem:bessel-membership}; no endpoint is inferred
  from a one-sided embedding alone.
\end{itemize}

No numerical computation is used as proof evidence.  The only nonstandard
lemma is proved explicitly by high-frequency scaling.  The decisive
external input, Proposition \ref{prop:embedding}, is an if-and-only-if
embedding theorem and its source PDF is included with this packet.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

The result does not cover $p=1$ or $q=1$, where Hardy/BMO-type duals
replace the simple formula \eqref{eq:dual}.  It also excludes
$a=\infty$ or $b=\infty$: Schwartz functions are then not dense, so
the reproducing identities need not force a pairing on the entire space by
the present argument.  Exponents below one are quasi-Banach and fall outside
the source definition of an RKBS pair.

A bounded novelty search was performed on 2026-07-22.  The run registry,
solution, attempt, and proof-gap indexes and the local 2000--2026 arXiv
title/abstract corpus were searched for arXiv:2603.26979 and combinations of
``RKBS,'' ``reproducing kernel Banach,'' ``Triebel--Lizorkin,'' ``Bessel
kernel,'' and ``Mat\'ern kernel.''  Web/arXiv searches used the exact source
title and the phrases ``reproducing kernel Banach + Triebel--Lizorkin,''
``Bessel potential kernel + Triebel--Lizorkin + RKBS,'' and ``Mat\'ern
kernel + Triebel--Lizorkin.''  They returned the March 2026 source paper but
no later or parallel characterization.  The nearest result found concerns
embedding Triebel--Lizorkin spaces into some RKHS on bounded domains, a
different question from identifying pairs with this fixed kernel.  The
search supports novelty but is not a claim of exhaustive global priority.
