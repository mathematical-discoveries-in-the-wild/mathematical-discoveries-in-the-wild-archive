\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate partial positive answer to Question (2) in Kadets--Kalton--Werner, \emph{Remarks on rich subspaces of Banach spaces}, arXiv:math/0210287.  It is not a full solution of the question.

\section*{Source Question}

The source paper ends with two questions of A. Pelczynski.  The second is:

\begin{quote}
If \(X\) is a subspace of \(L_1\) with the RNP, does \(L_1/X\) have the Daugavet property?
\end{quote}

The source records positive answers for reflexive spaces, for \(H^1\), and for a certain Talagrand space.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \((\Omega,\Sigma,\lambda)\) be a nonatomic probability space and let \((A_n)_{n\geq 1}\) be a countable measurable partition of \(\Omega\) with \(\lambda(A_n)>0\) for every \(n\).  Put
\[
  e_n=\frac{\mathbf 1_{A_n}}{\lambda(A_n)}
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  X=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\{e_n:n\geq 1\}\subset L_1(\lambda).
\]
Then \(X\) is isometric to \(\ell_1\), hence has the Schur property and the Radon--Nikodym property, and the quotient \(L_1(\lambda)/X\) has the Daugavet property.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The partition splits \(L_1(\lambda)\) as an \(\ell_1\)-sum of the pieces \(L_1(A_n)\).  The subspace \(X\) is exactly the corresponding \(\ell_1\)-sum of the one-dimensional constant subspaces on those pieces.  Therefore the quotient splits isometrically into an \(\ell_1\)-sum of the quotients \(L_1(A_n)/\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf 1_{A_n}\}\).  Each coordinate quotient is covered by the known finite-dimensional, hence reflexive, subspace case.  The only remaining point is that \(\ell_1\)-sums preserve the Daugavet property, which follows directly from the slice characterization.

\section*{Proof}

First, the map \((a_n)\mapsto \sum_n a_ne_n\) is an isometry from \(\ell_1\) onto \(X\), because the \(A_n\)'s are disjoint and \(\|e_n\|_1=1\).

Write \(E_n=L_1(A_n,\lambda|_{A_n})\) and \(F_n=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf 1_{A_n}\}\).  Then
\[
  L_1(\lambda)\cong \Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty E_n\Big)_{\ell_1},
  \qquad
  X\cong \Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty F_n\Big)_{\ell_1}.
\]
The quotient is isometric to
\[
  \Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty E_n/F_n\Big)_{\ell_1}.
\]
Indeed, for \(f=(f_n)\in(\oplus E_n)_{\ell_1}\),
\[
\operatorname{dist}(f,X)
=
\inf_{u_n\in F_n}\sum_n\|f_n-u_n\|_1
=
\sum_n \operatorname{dist}(f_n,F_n).
\]
The lower bound is immediate.  For the upper bound, choose \(u_n\in F_n\) within \(2^{-n}\eta\) of the coordinate distance and also with \(\|u_n\|\leq 2\|f_n\|+2^{-n}\eta\); this is possible because \(0\in F_n\) is already within \(\|f_n\|\) of \(f_n\).  Hence \((u_n)\in(\oplus F_n)_{\ell_1}\), and letting \(\eta\downarrow0\) gives equality.

For each \(n\), \(F_n\) is one-dimensional and hence reflexive.  The positive reflexive-subspace case cited in Kadets--Kalton--Werner therefore gives that \(E_n/F_n\) has the Daugavet property.

It remains to recall that an \(\ell_1\)-sum of Daugavet spaces has the Daugavet property.  Let \(Y=(\oplus_n Y_n)_{\ell_1}\), where each \(Y_n\) has the Daugavet property.  Take \(y=(y_n)\in S_Y\), a slice
\[
  S=\{z\in B_Y:\operatorname{Re} y^*(z)>1-\alpha\},
  \qquad y^*=(y_n^*)\in S_{Y^*}=(\oplus_n Y_n^*)_{\ell_\infty},
\]
and \(\eps>0\).  Choose \(k\) with \(\|y_k^*\|>1-\alpha/2\).  By the homogeneous form of the Daugavet slice characterization in \(Y_k\), applied to \(y_k\in Y_k\) and to the slice determined by \(y_k^*/\|y_k^*\|\), there is \(z_k\in S_{Y_k}\) such that
\[
  \operatorname{Re} \frac{y_k^*}{\|y_k^*\|}(z_k)>1-\frac{\alpha}{2\|y_k^*\|}
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \|y_k+z_k\|>\|y_k\|+1-\eps.
\]
Then \(\operatorname{Re}y_k^*(z_k)>1-\alpha\).
Let \(z\in Y\) be supported only in coordinate \(k\), with \(z_k\) as above.  Then \(z\in S\) and
\[
  \|y+z\|
  =
  \sum_{n\neq k}\|y_n\|+\|y_k+z_k\|
  >
  1-\|y_k\|+\|y_k\|+1-\eps
  =
  2-\eps.
\]
Thus \(Y\) has the Daugavet property.  Applying this to \(Y_n=E_n/F_n\) proves that \(L_1(\lambda)/X\) has the Daugavet property.

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only standard quotient-norm computations and the slice characterization of the Daugavet property.  The subspace \(X\) is a nonreflexive RNP example, since it is isometric to \(\ell_1\).  This is a genuine subcase of the source question, but it is far from the general RNP-subspace problem.

\section*{Novelty and Literature Check}

The cheap run indexes had no exact packet for arXiv:math/0210287.  Web searches on 2026-06-16 for phrases including ``rich subspace of \(L_1\) with the Schur property'', ``\(L_1/X\) Daugavet property RNP'', and ``quotient \(L_1\) Daugavet RNP subspace'' found the source paper and adjacent Daugavet/rich-subspace literature, but no exact closure of the full question and no exact match for the partition \(\ell_1\) subcase.  This packet should be reviewed as a small positive partial result, not as a claimed new full theorem.

\section*{Limitations}

The theorem only handles the very structured Schur/RNP subspaces generated by a countable measurable partition.  It does not answer whether every RNP subspace \(X\subset L_1\) has \(L_1/X\) with the Daugavet property, and it does not address the separate question of whether there is a rich subspace of \(L_1\) with the Schur property.
