\section*{Source and Original Problem}

The source paper is Baudier--Lancien--Motakis--Schlumprecht,
\emph{Coarse and Lipschitz universality}, arXiv:2004.04806 \cite{BLMS}.
Problem 5.15 asks whether the conclusion in their Theorem F can be sharpened
from nonseparability of some even iterated dual of the target to
nonseparability of the bidual itself.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The relevant subcase considered here is the case where the embedded space is
$X=\co$ and the target space is a generalized James space $\J(e_i)$ over an
unconditional basis. The general problem is not claimed to be solved.

\section*{Supporting Literature}

The supporting paper is Jackson--Krause--Sari,
\emph{On coarse geometry of separable dual Banach spaces}, arXiv:2501.01561
\cite{JKS}. Their Theorem 29 says that for every unconditional basis $(e_i)$,
\[
\co \text{ coarsely embeds into } \J(e_i)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\co \text{ linearly embeds into } \J(e_i).
\]

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/supporting_theorem_crop.png}
\end{center}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The later theorem converts the nonlinear hypothesis in the source problem,
``$\co$ coarsely embeds into $Y$'', into the linear conclusion
``$\co$ embeds linearly into $Y$'' for the particular targets
$Y=\J(e_i)$. Once a linear copy of $\co$ is present, the bidual obstruction is
standard: the second adjoint of an isomorphic embedding of $\co$ into $Y$
embeds $\co^{**}=\ellinf$ into $Y^{**}$. Since $\ellinf$ is nonseparable,
$Y^{**}$ must be nonseparable.

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let $(e_i)$ be an unconditional basis for a Banach space $E$, and let
$Y=\J(e_i)$ be the generalized James space over $(e_i)$ in the sense of
\cite{JKS}. If $\co$ coarsely embeds into $Y$, then $Y^{**}$ is nonseparable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume that $\co$ coarsely embeds into $Y=\J(e_i)$. By Theorem 29 of
\cite{JKS}, $\co$ linearly embeds into $Y$. Thus there is a bounded linear
operator $T:\co\to Y$ and a constant $a>0$ such that
\[
\|Tx\|\ge a\|x\| \qquad (x\in \co).
\]
The second adjoint $T^{**}:\co^{**}\to Y^{**}$ is also an isomorphic
embedding. For completeness, let $S:T(\co)\to \co$ be the inverse of $T$ on
its range, so $\|S\|\le a^{-1}$. If $\varphi\in B_{\co^*}$, then
\(\varphi S\) is a bounded functional on the closed subspace \(T(\co)\) of
\(Y\), and Hahn--Banach extends it to some \(\psi\in Y^*\) with
\(\|\psi\|\le \|S\|\) and \(T^*\psi=\varphi\). Hence
\[
B_{\co^*}\subset \|S\|\,T^*(B_{Y^*}).
\]
For \(x^{**}\in \co^{**}\), this gives
\[
\|x^{**}\|
=\sup_{\varphi\in B_{\co^*}} |x^{**}(\varphi)|
\le \|S\|\sup_{\psi\in B_{Y^*}} |x^{**}(T^*\psi)|
=\|S\|\,\|T^{**}x^{**}\|.
\]
Thus \(T^{**}\) is bounded below.

Since $\co^{**}$ is isometric to $\ellinf$, this embeds $\ellinf$ into
$Y^{**}$. Therefore $Y^{**}$ is nonseparable.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This is a literature-implied partial subcase of Problem 5.15, not a full
answer. It proves the bidual conclusion for targets of the form
$Y=\J(e_i)$ and embedded source $X=\co$. It does not address arbitrary Banach
targets $Y$, nor the other source spaces allowed in Problem 5.15.

The supporting paper does not appear to state that it is answering Problem
5.15. The match is by direct specialization of its Theorem 29 plus the
standard second-adjoint observation above. The run indexes were searched for
arXiv:2004.04806, arXiv:2501.01561, generalized James-space keywords,
``$c_0$ coarsely embeds'', and the ``$Y^{**}$ nonseparable'' phrasing. A
bounded web search on June 14, 2026 for exact phrases from Problem 5.15 found
only the source paper.
