\section*{Source and Target Question}

The source paper is Francisco Escudero Gutierrez,
\emph{Influences of Fourier Completely Bounded Polynomials and Classical
Simulation of Quantum Algorithms}, arXiv:2304.06713 \cite{EG2304}.

The paper introduces Fourier completely bounded norms for Boolean-cube
polynomials and proposes a weaker variant of the Aaronson--Ambainis influence
conjecture.  At the end of the paper, Question 4.5 asks:
\begin{quote}
Given a polynomial \(p\) of degree at most \(d\), is there
\((u,v,A)\in\BB^d\) such that
\[
\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle
=\frac{\widehat p(S_{\mathbf i})}{\operatorname{poly}(d,\MaxInf[p])}
\]
for any \(\mathbf i\in[n+1]^d\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The obstruction is the constant Fourier coefficient.  The maximum influence
does not see constants: adding \(M\) to a polynomial leaves every influence
unchanged, but changes \(\widehat p(\emptyset)\) by \(M\).  On the other hand,
every degree-\(d\) correlation produced by unit vectors and contractions has
absolute value at most \(1\).  Therefore no denominator depending only on
\(d\) and \(\MaxInf[p]\) can represent all unnormalised polynomials.

This is a narrow reading of the printed auxiliary question.  The normalized
conjecture earlier in the source, where \(\|p\|_{\mathrm{fcb},d}\le 1\), is not
affected by this counterexample.

\section*{Definitions}

For a Boolean-cube polynomial
\[
p(x)=\sum_{S\subset[n]}\widehat p(S)\prod_{i\in S}x(i),
\]
the influence of coordinate \(j\) is
\[
\Inf_j[p]=\sum_{S\ni j}\widehat p(S)^2,
\qquad
\MaxInf[p]=\max_j \Inf_j[p].
\]
For \(\mathbf i=(i_1,\ldots,i_d)\in[n+1]^d\), the set \(S_{\mathbf i}\subset[n]\)
contains the indices in \([n]\) that occur an odd number of times in
\(\mathbf i\); the extra coordinate \(n+1\) is ignored.

The source's Boolean-behavior tuples consist of unit vectors \(u,v\) and
contractions \(A(k)\) such that the scalar
\(\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle\) depends only on
\(S_{\mathbf i}\).  The only property used below is the immediate bound
\[
\left|\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle\right|\le 1.
\]

\begin{lemma}
Let \(u,v\) be unit vectors and \(A(1),\ldots,A(n+1)\) contractions on a real
Hilbert space.  Then, for every \(\mathbf i\in[n+1]^d\),
\[
\left|\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle\right|\le 1.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The product \(A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)\) is a contraction.  Hence
\[
|\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle|
\le \|u\|\,\|A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\|
\le 1.
\]
\end{proof}

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Under the usual reading that the displayed
\(\operatorname{poly}(d,\MaxInf[p])\) is a fixed finite nonzero denominator
depending only on \(d\) and \(\MaxInf[p]\), Question 4.5 is false without a
normalization hypothesis on \(p\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(d\ge 1\) and \(n\ge 1\).  Let \(D(d,t)\) denote any proposed finite
nonzero denominator depending only on \(d\) and \(t=\MaxInf[p]\).  Choose
\(\varepsilon>0\) such that \(D(d,\varepsilon^2)\) is finite and nonzero, and
then choose
\[
M>|D(d,\varepsilon^2)|.
\]
Consider the degree-one polynomial
\[
p(x)=M+\varepsilon x(1),\qquad x\in\{-1,1\}^n.
\]
Its only nonzero Fourier coefficients are
\[
\widehat p(\emptyset)=M,\qquad \widehat p(\{1\})=\varepsilon,
\]
and therefore
\[
\MaxInf[p]=\Inf_1[p]=\varepsilon^2.
\]

Choose any \(\mathbf i\in[n+1]^d\) with \(S_{\mathbf i}=\emptyset\), for
instance \(\mathbf i=(n+1,\ldots,n+1)\).  If the conclusion of Question 4.5
held with denominator \(D(d,\MaxInf[p])\), then some Boolean-behavior tuple
\((u,v,A)\) would satisfy
\[
\langle u,A(i_1)\cdots A(i_d)v\rangle
=\frac{\widehat p(\emptyset)}{D(d,\varepsilon^2)}
=\frac{M}{D(d,\varepsilon^2)}.
\]
The right-hand side has absolute value greater than \(1\), while the lemma
shows that every such correlation has absolute value at most \(1\).  This is a
contradiction.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses no computational input.  It only uses the contraction bound
implicit in the definition of \(\BB^d\) and the elementary fact that influences
ignore the constant Fourier coefficient.

The counterexample is stable under replacing the constant polynomial by a
nonconstant linear polynomial \(M+\varepsilon x(1)\); hence it is not merely a
division-by-zero issue at \(\MaxInf[p]=0\).

\section*{Limitations and Human-Review Recommendation}

This packet answers only the literal unnormalised wording of Question 4.5.  It
does not refute Conjecture 1.5 in the source, the Aaronson--Ambainis conjecture,
or any version of Question 4.5 that assumes \(\|p\|_{\mathrm{fcb},d}\le1\) or
allows the denominator to depend on \(|\widehat p(\emptyset)|\).

Human review should check whether the intended convention around
\(\operatorname{poly}(d,\MaxInf[p])\) silently included such a normalization.
If so, this packet should be classified as a correction to the printed
auxiliary formulation rather than as progress on the intended normalized
problem.
