\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate counterexample to an open question in the source
paper \cite{FGSZ}.  The authors ask whether there is a constant $a>0$ such
that a logarithmic Sobolev inequality of the form
\[
 H(e^{-V}\sigma\mid \sigma)
 \leq
 \frac a2 \int_{\Sph^n}
        \log\left(1+\frac{|\grad V|^2}{a^2}\right)e^{-V}\,d\sigma
\]
holds on the sphere for all regular enough normalized potentials
$V$, where $\int e^{-V}\,d\sigma=1$.  The answer is negative in every
dimension $n\geq1$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

There is almost no room for the constant.  If the inequality is linearized at
the uniform measure in the first spherical harmonic direction, the ordinary
spectral gap of $\Sph^n$ forces $a\leq n$.  On the other hand, highly
concentrated von Mises--Fisher densities
\[
        d\eta_t(\theta)=Z_t^{-1}e^{t\theta_1}\,d\sigma(\theta)
\]
have entropy asymptotic to $(n/2)\log t$.  The right-hand side has leading
term $(a/2)\log t$, so all $a<n$ fail.  The critical value $a=n$ still fails:
the leading logarithms cancel, but the next constant term is positive.  Thus
no $a>0$ can work.

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:no-constant}
Let $n\geq1$ and let $\sigma$ be the normalized surface measure on $\Sph^n$.
There is no constant $a>0$ such that, for every smooth $V$ with
$\int_{\Sph^n}e^{-V}\,d\sigma=1$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:source-lsi}
 H(e^{-V}\sigma\mid \sigma)
 \leq
 \frac a2 \int_{\Sph^n}
        \log\left(1+\frac{|\grad V|^2}{a^2}\right)e^{-V}\,d\sigma .
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Suppose first that \eqref{eq:source-lsi} holds for some $a>0$.  Let
$u(\theta)=\theta_1$ be the first coordinate on $\Sph^n$, and put
\[
        d\eta_\varepsilon
        =Z_\varepsilon^{-1}e^{\varepsilon u}\,d\sigma
        =e^{-V_\varepsilon}\,d\sigma .
\]
As $\varepsilon\to0$,
\[
 H(\eta_\varepsilon\mid\sigma)
 =\frac{\varepsilon^2}{2}\int u^2\,d\sigma+o(\varepsilon^2),
\]
while
\[
 \frac a2\int
 \log\left(1+\frac{|\grad V_\varepsilon|^2}{a^2}\right)d\eta_\varepsilon
 =
 \frac{\varepsilon^2}{2a}\int|\grad u|^2\,d\sigma+o(\varepsilon^2).
\]
Since $u$ is a first spherical harmonic,
\[
        \int_{\Sph^n}|\grad u|^2\,d\sigma
        =n\int_{\Sph^n}u^2\,d\sigma .
\]
Therefore \eqref{eq:source-lsi} can hold only if $a\leq n$.

It remains to rule out $a\leq n$.  For $t>0$, set
\[
        d\eta_t(\theta)=Z_t^{-1}e^{t\theta_1}\,d\sigma(\theta),
        \qquad
        V_t(\theta)=-t\theta_1+\log Z_t .
\]
Let $U=\theta_1$ under $\eta_t$.  Then
\[
        H(\eta_t\mid\sigma)=t\,\E_t U-\log Z_t
\]
and
\[
        |\grad V_t|^2=t^2(1-U^2).
\]
The coordinate $U$ under $\sigma$ has density
\[
        q_n(u)=c_n(1-u^2)^{n/2-1},\qquad
        c_n=\frac{\Gamma((n+1)/2)}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma(n/2)} .
\]
Laplace's method at the endpoint $u=1$ gives, with
\[
        C_n=2^{(n-2)/2}\frac{\Gamma((n+1)/2)}{\sqrt{\pi}},
\]
the asymptotics
\[
        Z_t=C_n e^t t^{-n/2}(1+o(1)),
        \qquad
        \E_t(1-U)=\frac{n}{2t}+o(t^{-1}),
\]
and
\[
        \E_t\log(1-U^2)
        =
        -\log t+\log2+\psi(n/2)+o(1),
\]
where $\psi=\Gamma'/\Gamma$.  Consequently
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:entropy-asymptotic}
        H(\eta_t\mid\sigma)
        =
        \frac n2\log t-\frac n2-\log C_n+o(1).
\end{equation}
For the right-hand side of \eqref{eq:source-lsi},
\begin{align}
\label{eq:rhs-asymptotic}
 \frac a2\E_t\log\left(1+\frac{t^2(1-U^2)}{a^2}\right)
 =
 \frac a2\log t
 +\frac a2\left(\log\frac{2}{a^2}+\psi(n/2)\right)+o(1).
\end{align}
If $a<n$, \eqref{eq:entropy-asymptotic} and \eqref{eq:rhs-asymptotic} imply
that the difference between the left and right sides of \eqref{eq:source-lsi}
tends to $+\infty$ along $\eta_t$.

The only remaining possible value is $a=n$.  In that case the difference tends
to
\[
 D_n=
 -\frac n2-\log C_n
 -\frac n2\left(\log\frac{2}{n^2}+\psi(n/2)\right).
\]
Writing $x=n/2$, this is
\[
 D_n=
 \log2+\frac12\log\pi-x-\log\Gamma(x+1/2)+2x\log x-x\psi(x).
\]
For $x\geq1/2$, the standard digamma and Stirling bounds
\[
        \psi(x)\leq \log x-\frac{1}{2x},
        \qquad
        \Gamma(x+1/2)\leq \sqrt{2\pi}\,x^x e^{-x}
\]
give
\[
        D_n\geq \frac12(1+\log2)>0 .
\]
Thus the inequality also fails for $a=n$ and all possible constants have been
excluded.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and scope}

The counterexample uses smooth potentials $V_t=-t\theta_1+\log Z_t$, so it
falls within any reasonable interpretation of ``regular enough potentials'' in
the source question.  The proof is symbolic.  The script
\texttt{code/verify\_critical\_constant.py} prints the critical constants
$D_n$ for $1\leq n\leq20$ as a sanity check; these numerical values are not
used in the proof.

This packet answers only the standalone open question about the existence of a
universal constant $a$ in the underlying spherical log-Sobolev inequality.  It
does not affect the source's proved conditional or unconditional
Blaschke--Santal\'o consequences.
