\section{Source Problem}

The source paper is Plebanek--Rondo\v{s}--Sobota,
\emph{Complemented subspaces of Banach spaces \(\C(K\times L)\)},
arXiv:2405.19120~\cite{PRS}.  Problem~4.1 asks:

\begin{sourceproblem}[Plebanek--Rondo\v{s}--Sobota]
Does \(\C(\bomega\times\bomega)\) contain a complemented isomorphic copy of
\(\C(K)\) for every separable compact space \(K\)?
\end{sourceproblem}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source paper already proves the metric compact case.  More precisely,
Corollary~1.4 of~\cite{PRS} gives complemented copies of
\(\C([0,1]^\kappa)\), and also of \(\C(2^\kappa)\), inside
\[
        Z:=\C(\bomega\times\bomega)
        \qquad (1\leq\kappa\leq\cc).
\]

\section{Definitions and Proof Intuition}

For Banach spaces \(X,Y\), write \(X\hookc Y\) if there are bounded operators
\[
        U:X\to Y,\qquad V:Y\to X,\qquad VU=\Id_X.
\]
Equivalently, \(U[X]\) is a complemented subspace of \(Y\) isomorphic to
\(X\).  The factorization constant is
\[
 \gamma_Y(X)=\inf\{\|U\|\|V\|:U:X\to Y,\ V:Y\to X,\ VU=\Id_X\}.
\]

The main mechanism is transitivity: if a class of compact spaces \(L\) has
\(\C(L)\hookc Z\), then every compact \(K\) whose \(\C(K)\) is complemented
in some \(\C(L)\) is also a positive instance.  The previous packet used
this only for extension operators into cubes.  The upgrade adds three sharper
uses of the same principle.

First, every \(\C(K)\) is norm-one complemented in the affine functions on
the probability simplex \(\Pp(K)\), and also in \(\C(B_{\M(K)})\).  Thus the
full problem is exactly as hard on separable Bauer simplices and separable
weak-star compact dual balls.  Second, an averaging operator from a Cantor
cube gives a complemented pullback copy, so all separable almost-Milyutin
compacta are positive instances.  Third, Blasco and Ivorra give a separable
dyadic compactum that is not almost Milyutin; hence the positive averaging
route cannot be the whole story.  Finally, the operator model
\(Z\cong \Kop(\ell_1,\ell_\infty)\) shows that the naive projection onto
\(\Kop(\ell_1,\C(K))\) would already imply that \(\C(K)\) is complemented in
\(\ell_\infty\), so any full positive proof must be more twisted.

\section{Closure Principles}

\begin{lemma}[Transitivity]\label{lem:transitivity}
If \(X\hookc Y\) and \(Y\hookc W\), then \(X\hookc W\).  Moreover
\[
       \gamma_W(X)\leq \gamma_Y(X)\gamma_W(Y).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(U_1:X\to Y\), \(V_1:Y\to X\), \(U_2:Y\to W\), and \(V_2:W\to Y\)
with \(V_iU_i=\Id\).  Then \((V_1V_2)(U_2U_1)=\Id_X\), and the stated norm
estimate follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Averaging closure]\label{prop:averaging}
Let \(q:L\to K\) be a continuous surjection between compact spaces.  Suppose
there is a bounded operator \(A:\C(L)\to\C(K)\) such that
\[
       A q^*=\Id_{\C(K)},\qquad q^*f=f\circ q.
\]
If \(\C(L)\hookc Z\), then \(\C(K)\hookc Z\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The identity \(Aq^*=\Id\) says that \(q^*\C(K)\) is complemented in
\(\C(L)\), with projection \(q^*A\).  Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:transitivity}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Previous packet as a special case]\label{cor:old}
If \(K\) is compact and \(\C(K)\) is complemented in
\(\C([0,1]^\kappa)\) or in \(\C(2^\kappa)\) for some
\(1\leq\kappa\leq\cc\), then \(\C(K)\hookc Z\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Combine the source Corollary~1.4 with Lemma~\ref{lem:transitivity}.  The
extension-operator and retract cases in the previous packet are concrete
ways to obtain the first complemented embedding.
\end{proof}

\section{Exact Convex Reductions}

For a compact space \(K\), let \(\Pp(K)\) be the weak-star compact space of
probability measures on \(K\), and let \(\M(K)=\C(K)^*\).

\begin{theorem}[Canonical convexification]\label{thm:convexification}
For every compact space \(K\), the Banach space \(\C(K)\) is isometric to a
norm-one complemented subspace of \(\C(\Pp(K))\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Define
\[
 \mathcal A_K:\C(K)\to\C(\Pp(K)),\qquad
 (\mathcal A_Kf)(\mu)=\int_K f\,d\mu,
\]
and
\[
 \mathcal R_K:\C(\Pp(K))\to\C(K),\qquad
 (\mathcal R_KF)(x)=F(\delta_x).
\]
The first map is an isometry because Dirac measures belong to \(\Pp(K)\);
the second is a contraction; and
\[
(\mathcal R_K\mathcal A_Kf)(x)=\int_K f\,d\delta_x=f(x).
\]
Thus \(\mathcal A_K\mathcal R_K\) is a norm-one projection onto
\(\mathcal A_K[\C(K)]\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:separable-convex}
If \(K\) is separable compact, then \(\Pp(K)\) and \(B_{\M(K)}\) are
separable compact spaces of weight at most \(\cc\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(D\subseteq K\) be countable and dense.  Finite rational convex
combinations of point masses \(\delta_d\), \(d\in D\), are weak-star dense in
\(\Pp(K)\): a basic weak-star neighbourhood only sees finitely many
continuous functions, and their integrals can be approximated by rational
convex combinations of values on \(D\).  For \(B_{\M(K)}\), use finite
rational convex combinations of \(\pm\delta_d\).  Since every member of
\(\C(K)\) is determined by its restriction to \(D\), one has
\(|\C(K)|\leq\cc\), so the weak-star topologies involved have bases of
cardinality at most \(\cc\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exact reduction]\label{thm:exact}
The following assertions are equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \(\C(K)\hookc Z\) for every separable compact space \(K\).
\item \(\C(S)\hookc Z\) for every separable compact convex space \(S\).
\item \(\C(\Pp(K))\hookc Z\) for every separable compact space \(K\).
\item \(\C(B_{\M(K)})\hookc Z\) for every separable compact space \(K\).
\end{enumerate}
Thus any counterexample may be sought among separable Bauer simplices, or
among separable symmetric weak-star compact dual balls.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The implications from (i) to (ii)--(iv) are immediate from
Lemma~\ref{lem:separable-convex}.  For (iii) implies (i), use
Theorem~\ref{thm:convexification} and transitivity.  For (iv) implies (i),
define
\[
J_K:\C(K)\to\C(B_{\M(K)}),\qquad (J_Kf)(\mu)=\mu(f),
\]
and
\[
S_K:\C(B_{\M(K)})\to\C(K),\qquad (S_KF)(x)=F(\delta_x).
\]
Then \(J_K\) is an isometry, \(\|S_K\|\leq1\), and \(S_KJ_K=\Id\).  Hence
\(\C(K)\) is norm-one complemented in \(\C(B_{\M(K)})\), and transitivity
again gives (i).
\end{proof}

\section{Almost-Milyutin Positive Class}

\begin{definition}
A compact space \(K\) is \emph{almost Milyutin} if there are a Cantor cube
\(2^\Gamma\), a continuous surjection \(q:2^\Gamma\to K\), and a bounded
averaging operator \(A:\C(2^\Gamma)\to\C(K)\) satisfying \(Aq^*=\Id\).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Coordinate reduction]\label{lem:coordinate}
Suppose \(q:2^\Gamma\to K\) is a continuous surjection onto a compact space
of weight \(\kappa\), and \(q\) admits a bounded averaging operator.  Then
there is \(\Lambda\subseteq\Gamma\), \(|\Lambda|\leq\kappa\), such that a
surjection \(q_\Lambda:2^\Lambda\to K\) also admits an averaging operator of
no larger norm.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Embed \(K\) into \([0,1]^\kappa\).  Each coordinate of the composite
\(2^\Gamma\to K\to[0,1]^\kappa\) depends on countably many coordinates of
\(2^\Gamma\).  The union of those coordinate sets has cardinality at most
\(\kappa\); call it \(\Lambda\).  Then \(q=q_\Lambda\pi_\Lambda\).  If
\(A\) averages \(q\), define \(A_\Lambda g=A(g\circ\pi_\Lambda)\).  This
has no larger norm and satisfies \(A_\Lambda q_\Lambda^*=\Id\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:milyutin-positive}
If \(K\) is a separable almost-Milyutin compact space, then
\(\C(K)\hookc Z\).  In particular, Problem~4.1 has a positive
answer for every separable Dugundji compact space.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A separable compact space has weight at most \(\cc\).  By
Lemma~\ref{lem:coordinate}, the almost-Milyutin witness can be chosen as a
surjection from \(2^\Lambda\) with \(|\Lambda|\leq\cc\).  Hence
\[
       \C(K)\hookc \C(2^\Lambda)\hookc Z,
\]
where the first complemented embedding comes from the averaging operator and
the second from the source Corollary~1.4.  Haydon proved that every Dugundji
compactum is Milyutin~\cite{Haydon}, so the final assertion follows.
\end{proof}

\section{A Separable Obstruction to the Cantor-Cube Route}

Blasco and Ivorra~\cite{BlascoIvorra} constructed a dyadic compact space
that is not almost Milyutin.  Let \(T\) be the quotient of \(2^{\omega_1}\)
obtained by identifying two distinct points, and put \(K_*=T^{\mathbb N}\).
For a compact space \(L\), define
\[
 p(L)=\inf\{\|A\|:q:2^\Gamma\twoheadrightarrow L,\;
 A:\C(2^\Gamma)\to\C(L),\; Aq^*=\Id\},
\]
with \(p(L)=+\infty\) if no such pair exists.

\begin{theorem}[Blasco--Ivorra]\label{thm:BI}
For every positive integer \(n\), \(p(T^n)\geq n+1\), although each finite
power \(T^n\) is almost Milyutin.  Moreover \(K_*=T^{\mathbb N}\) is dyadic
but not almost Milyutin; equivalently, \(p(K_*)=+\infty\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:Kstar}
The space \(K_*\) is separable, dyadic, and has weight at most \(\cc\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Hewitt--Marczewski--Pondiczery theorem implies that \(2^{\omega_1}\) is
separable, since \(\omega_1\leq\cc\); see Ross--Stone~\cite{RossStone}.
The quotient \(T\) is separable, and a countable product of separable spaces
is separable.  The quotient map \(2^{\omega_1}\to T\) induces an onto map
\[
(2^{\omega_1})^{\mathbb N}\cong2^{\omega_1\times\mathbb N}\cong2^{\omega_1}
\longrightarrow T^{\mathbb N},
\]
so \(K_*\) is dyadic.  The weight estimate is immediate.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Uniform finite-stage test]\label{prop:finite-stage}
If \(\C(K_*)\hookc Z\), then
\[
       \sup_{n\geq1}\gamma_Z(\C(T^n))<\infty.
\]
In fact \(\gamma_Z(\C(T^n))\leq\gamma_Z(\C(K_*))\) for every \(n\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(t_0\in T\).  The coordinate projection \(K_*\to T^n\) gives an
isometric embedding \(J_n:\C(T^n)\to\C(K_*)\).  Evaluation at the tail
\((t_0,t_0,\ldots)\) gives a contraction \(R_n:\C(K_*)\to\C(T^n)\) with
\(R_nJ_n=\Id\).  Composing any factorization of \(\Id_{\C(K_*)}\) through
\(Z\) with \(J_n\) and \(R_n\) gives the asserted bound.
\end{proof}

Thus a negative answer would follow from a comparison
\[
       p(T^n)\leq \Phi(\gamma_Z(\C(T^n)))
\]
for a finite-valued function \(\Phi\) independent of \(n\).  Such a
comparison is not proved here.  This is precisely where the obstruction
stops short of a counterexample: \(p(T^n)\) measures canonical averaging
through Cantor cubes, while \(\gamma_Z\) allows arbitrary isomorphic
complemented copies.

\section{Operator-Space Reformulation and No-Go Lemma}

There are natural isometric identifications
\[
 Z
 \cong \C(\bN,\C(\bN))
 \cong \C(\bN,\ell_\infty)
 \cong \Kop(\ell_1,\ell_\infty)
 \cong \ell_\infty\widehat\otimes_\varepsilon\ell_\infty.
\]
For the middle identification, an operator \(S:\ell_1\to\ell_\infty\) is
represented by the sequence \((Se_n)\); compactness is exactly relative
norm compactness of this range sequence.

If \(K\) is separable and \((d_n)\) is dense in \(K\), then
\[
       E_K:\C(K)\to\ell_\infty,\qquad E_Kf=(f(d_n))_{n\in\mathbb N},
\]
is an isometry.  The direct strategy would be to place
\(\Kop(\ell_1,E_K\C(K))\) inside \(\Kop(\ell_1,\ell_\infty)\) and project
onto it.  The next lemma shows that this strategy cannot give new cases.

\begin{lemma}[Operator-space no-go]\label{lem:no-go}
Let \(X\) be a closed subspace of a Banach space \(Y\).  If
\(\Kop(\ell_1,X)\) is complemented in \(\Kop(\ell_1,Y)\), then \(X\) is
complemented in \(Y\).  Moreover, a projection of norm \(M\) in the
operator space produces a projection of norm at most \(M\) in \(Y\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(y\in Y\), let \(R_y:\ell_1\to Y\) be the rank-one operator
\(R_y(a)=a_1y\).  If \(Q:\Kop(\ell_1,Y)\to\Kop(\ell_1,X)\) is a projection,
set
\[
       P(y)=Q(R_y)e_1.
\]
Then \(P:Y\to X\) is bounded with \(\|P\|\leq\|Q\|\).  If \(x\in X\), then
\(R_x\in\Kop(\ell_1,X)\), so \(Q(R_x)=R_x\) and \(P(x)=x\).  Hence \(P\) is
a projection onto \(X\).
\end{proof}

\section{Fibre-Supported Coupling Criterion}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:coupling}
Let \(\rho:L\to K\) be a continuous surjection between compact spaces.
Suppose there is a weak-star continuous map
\[
       K\to\Pp(L),\qquad x\mapsto\nu_x,
\]
such that \(\operatorname{supp}\nu_x\subseteq\rho^{-1}(x)\) for every
\(x\in K\).  Then
\[
       Af(x)=\int_L f\,d\nu_x
\]
defines a norm-one positive averaging operator for \(\rho\).  Consequently,
if \(\C(L)\hookc Z\), then \(\C(K)\hookc Z\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Weak-star continuity gives \(Af\in\C(K)\), and \(\|A\|\leq1\).  If
\(g\in\C(K)\), then
\[
A(g\circ\rho)(x)=\int_{\rho^{-1}(x)}g(\rho(y))\,d\nu_x(y)=g(x).
\]
Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:averaging}.
\end{proof}

The source paper's compact-group construction supplies this criterion in a
highly structured situation.  A general replacement for the group operation
is not known from the present packet.

\section{Verification Notes and Limitations}

The mathematical classification is partial.  The exact reductions and
closure principles are operator-theoretic and topological consequences of
norm-one complemented embeddings.  The positive class is genuinely larger
than the previous extension-operator packet because it records the full
almost-Milyutin/Dugundji mechanism, but it still does not cover all separable
compact spaces.

The Blasco--Ivorra space \(K_*\) is not a counterexample to
Problem~4.1; it is a counterexample only to the universal
Cantor-cube averaging route.  The operator-space no-go lemma blocks only the
direct projection onto \(\Kop(\ell_1,E_K\C(K))\), not arbitrary twisted
copies of \(\C(K)\) in \(Z\).

Local duplicate checks on June~22, 2026 found the earlier partial packet and
the full-general no-promotion attempt, but no packet already recording the
convex reductions, the Blasco--Ivorra finite-stage test, or the no-go lemma.
The supplied report records an external literature search through
June~20, 2026 and states that no full answer or counterexample was located;
this upgrade did not perform a fresh external web search.
