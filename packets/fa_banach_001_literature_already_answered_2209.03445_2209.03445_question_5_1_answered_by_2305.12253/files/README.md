# Question 5.1 Answered by arXiv:2305.12253

Status: `literature_already_answered`

## Source question

Fernando Albiac, Jose L. Ansorena, and Miguel Berasategui, *Elton's near
unconditionality of bases as a threshold-free form of greediness*,
arXiv:2209.03445, JFA 285 (2023), 110060.

Question 5.1, arXiv PDF page 19, asks whether there is a nearly unconditional
basis that is not truncation quasi-greedy.

## Explicit later answer

The same authors' separate later paper, *Linear versus nonlinear forms of
partial unconditionality of bases*, arXiv:2305.12253, JFA 287 (2024), 110594,
answers yes.

On arXiv PDF page 5, the authors explicitly say they solve the question
negatively as an implication: not every QGLC basis is truncation quasi-greedy.
Theorem 1.5 identifies bounded-oscillation unconditional bases with
truncation quasi-greedy bases. Theorem 1.6 constructs a Schauder basis of a
Banach space that is nearly unconditional but not bounded-oscillation
unconditional. Together these give exactly the requested counterexample.

This is an author-explicit answer, not an agent-discovered new theorem.
Question 5.2 on renorming is not settled by this identification.
