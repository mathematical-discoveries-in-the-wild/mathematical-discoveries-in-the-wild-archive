# AUS tensor product question answered by Causey 1705.05484

Status: literature_already_answered

Source/open-problem paper: Luis Garcia-Lirola and Matias Raja, "On strong
asymptotic uniform smoothness and convexity", arXiv:1705.02020, submitted
2017-05-04.

Supporting answer paper: R. M. Causey, "Power type asymptotically uniformly
smooth and asymptotically uniformly flat norms", arXiv:1705.05484, submitted
2017-05-15.

## Identification

The source paper asks in the introduction, on page 2, whether the injective
tensor product of AUS spaces is AUS for the canonical injective tensor norm.
The same paper proves the strong-AUS version, namely that the injective tensor
product of strongly AUS spaces is AUS.

Causey's later paper explicitly answers the canonical AUS question. Its
abstract states the Banach-space consequence, Theorem 1.3 proves the operator
version for injective tensor products, and Corollary 4.5 states that asymptotic
uniform smoothness passes to injective tensor products of Banach spaces.
Causey's introduction also explicitly says the tensor-product theorem extends
the recent Garcia-Lirola--Raja result, so this is not just an implied
connection.

Applying Causey's Theorem 1.3 to identity operators gives:
if X and Y are AUS Banach spaces, then X \hat\otimes_epsilon Y is AUS in the
canonical injective tensor norm.

## Scope limits

This packet records a literature answer, not a new proof produced by this run.
It answers the canonical AUS injective tensor-product question from the source
introduction.

It does not answer two separate nearby questions from the source paper:

- the Section 3 remark asking for a reflexive Banach space with an FDD that is
  AUC but not strongly AUC;
- the Remark 4.3 question about positive AUC tensor-product classes beyond the
  known l_p \hat\otimes_epsilon l_q case.
