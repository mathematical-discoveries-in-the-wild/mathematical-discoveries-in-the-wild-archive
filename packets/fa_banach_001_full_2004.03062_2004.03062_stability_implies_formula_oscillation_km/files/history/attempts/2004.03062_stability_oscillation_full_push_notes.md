## Question

Does model-theoretic stability imply oscillation stability? Equivalently, if
`T` is a stable Banach theory, must every unary formula be oscillation stable
on every model of `T`?

## What Was Strengthened

The prior packet
`solutions/partial/2004.03062_hilbert_operator_qf_oscillation_stability/`
proved the quantifier-free Hilbert-operator subcase. In this push, that packet
was strengthened by adding a quantifier-elimination transfer corollary and
named stable classes:

- Hilbert spaces with a bounded normal operator in the language with `T,T*`,
  using arXiv:2507.21894.
- The self-adjoint and unitary one-operator cases in the original language,
  again using arXiv:2507.21894.
- Hilbert spaces with a unitary representation of a finite group, using
  arXiv:2409.03923.

Thus Hanson Q4.3 has a positive answer for these stable Hilbert-operator
theories.

## Full-Proof Route Tried

A tempting direct route is:

1. Assume a stable unary formula `phi(x,a)` is not oscillation stable on an
   infinite-dimensional subspace `Y`.
2. Recursively build block vectors with high/low `phi` values.
3. Convert the resulting block-pattern asymmetry into an order-property
   configuration for a formula obtained from `phi` by linear combinations.

The obstruction is that non-oscillation of one fixed unary instance does not
immediately give the two-parameter order pattern required for instability. The
block construction supplies many high/low vectors, but not enough control over
mixed sums such as `phi((u+v)/||u+v||,a)` to force different iterated limits.
This route might still work with a stronger Banach-space Ramsey lemma, but no
such lemma was identified in this push.

## Counterexample Route Tried

Hanson notes that arbitrary Hilbert predicates can fail oscillation stability:
the Maurey/Odell-Schlumprecht distortion-coloring phenomenon gives uniformly
continuous colorings of the Hilbert sphere with no infinite-dimensional
almost-monochromatic subspace. This suggests expanding Hilbert space by such a
predicate.

The missing ingredient is stability of the expanded predicate theory. Known
stable Hilbert predicate/operator examples found in the literature either:

- reduce to operator-linear or subspace-distance predicates, where the
  quadratic-flattening lemma applies; or
- are not ordinary stable theories in the needed sense, e.g. only stable up to
  perturbation or unstable/TP-style generic-predicate cases.

So this did not produce a counterexample.

## Current Best Status

No full proof or counterexample was found. The durable progress is the
strengthened named-class partial result in the packet above. The broad open
case remains stable Banach theories without a Hilbert-operator quantifier
elimination reduction, especially stable expansions by general unary predicates.
