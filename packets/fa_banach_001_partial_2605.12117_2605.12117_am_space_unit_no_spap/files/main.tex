\section*{Source and Open Question}

This packet concerns Question 2 from:

\begin{quote}
Luis A. Garcia, Jose Lucas P. Luiz, Vinicius C. C. Miranda,
\emph{Norm attainment for multilinear operators and polynomials on
Banach Spaces and Banach lattices}, arXiv:2605.12117.
\end{quote}

The source location is Section 4, ``Open Questions'', Question 2. The
question appears on page 28 of the source paper:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The paper defines the sequentially positive approximation property
(SPAP) as follows: a Banach lattice $E$ has SPAP if there is a sequence
$(S_n)_n \subset B_{\calF_+(E;E)}$ such that
\[
  \lim_{n\to\infty}\norm{|S_n-\id_E|(x)}=0
\]
for every $x\in E^+$. The authors note that the known positive examples
are Banach lattices with order-continuous norm whose order is given by a
basis, and they ask whether there are other examples.

\section*{Claim}

\begin{theorem}
Let $E$ be an infinite-dimensional AM-space with a strong unit $\e$,
normalized so that $\norm{\e}=1$. Then $E$ does not have the SPAP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
This is a partial negative result for Question 2. It does not construct
a new Banach lattice with SPAP. Instead, it rules out the classical
AM-space-with-unit family, including every infinite-dimensional
$C(K)$-space with its usual unit.
\end{remark}

\section*{Proof}

We use the standard norm formula for positive operators on AM-spaces
with unit.

\begin{lemma}
Let $E$ be an AM-space with unit $\e$. If $P:E\to E$ is positive, then
\[
  \norm{P}=\norm{P\e}.
\]
Consequently, if $R:E\to E$ is regular, then
\[
  \norm{R}\leq \norm{|R|\e}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $x\in B_E$ we have $|x|\leq \e$. If $P$ is positive, then
$|Px|\leq P|x|\leq P\e$, so $\norm{Px}\leq\norm{P\e}$. Taking the
supremum over $x\in B_E$ gives $\norm{P}\leq\norm{P\e}$, while the
reverse inequality is immediate from $\norm{\e}=1$.

For regular $R$, positivity of $|R|$ and the lattice inequality
$|Rx|\leq |R|(|x|)$ give
\[
  \norm{Rx}\leq \norm{|R|(|x|)}\leq \norm{|R|\e}
\]
for every $x\in B_E$. Hence $\norm{R}\leq\norm{|R|\e}$.
\end{proof}

Assume toward a contradiction that $E$ has the SPAP. Then there is a
sequence of positive finite-rank operators
$(S_n)_n\subset B_{\calF_+(E;E)}$ such that
\[
  \norm{|S_n-\id_E|(x)}\longrightarrow 0
  \qquad (x\in E^+).
\]
Applying this at the unit $\e$ gives
\[
  \norm{|S_n-\id_E|(\e)}\longrightarrow 0.
\]
By the lemma,
\[
  \norm{S_n-\id_E}\leq \norm{|S_n-\id_E|(\e)}
  \longrightarrow 0.
\]
Thus the identity operator on $E$ is a norm limit of finite-rank
operators.

Every norm limit of finite-rank operators is compact. Therefore
$\id_E$ would be compact. But the identity operator on a Banach space is
compact if and only if the closed unit ball is compact, equivalently if
and only if the space is finite-dimensional. This contradicts the
assumption that $E$ is infinite-dimensional.

Hence no infinite-dimensional AM-space with unit has the SPAP.

\section*{Verification Notes}

No numerical verification is needed for this packet. The proof reduces
the SPAP condition at the order unit to operator-norm convergence of
finite-rank operators to the identity, and then uses the elementary
compactness obstruction for infinite-dimensional Banach spaces.
