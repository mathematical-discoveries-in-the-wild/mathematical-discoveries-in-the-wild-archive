# Literature Answer: Extreme Points Are Molecules

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source/trigger paper: Ch. Cobollo and A. Peris, *On disjoint dynamical properties and Lipschitz-free spaces*, arXiv:2405.12626v2.

Supporting answer paper: Ramon J. Aliaga, Eva Pernecka, and Richard J. Smith, *A solution to the extreme point problem and other applications of Choquet theory to Lipschitz-free spaces*, arXiv:2412.04312.

## Identification

The source paper is not posing a new numbered problem of its own here. The extraction signal comes from Section 4.1, "Lipschitz-free working preliminaries", around source line 635, where the authors recall the area-level conjecture that every extreme point in a Lipschitz-free space should be a molecule.

The later paper arXiv:2412.04312 explicitly solves that exact extreme-point problem. Its abstract states that all extreme points of the unit ball of Lipschitz-free spaces are elementary molecules. In Section 4, "The extreme point problem", it identifies the problem as dating back to Weaver and as explicitly asked in the literature, and Theorem 4.1 gives the full classification for complete metric spaces:

`Ext B_{F(M)}` consists exactly of the molecules `m_{xy}` such that no third point lies metrically between `x` and `y`.

## Scope

This clears the extracted open-problem signal from arXiv:2405.12626. It does not answer new disjoint-dynamical questions from the source paper; the source hit was only the background molecule/extreme-point conjecture.

The supporting authors explicitly knew they were solving the extreme-point problem, though not specifically a question original to Cobollo--Peris.
