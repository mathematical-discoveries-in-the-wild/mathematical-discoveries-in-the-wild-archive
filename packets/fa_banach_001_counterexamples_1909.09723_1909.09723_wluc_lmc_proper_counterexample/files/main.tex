\section*{Status}

Counterexample, likely valid. This answers affirmatively the question in
Wisnicki's \emph{Amenable semigroups and nonexpansive dynamical systems}
\cite{Wisnicki}: there is a Hausdorff topological semigroup \(S\) for which
\[
  \WLUC(S) \subsetneq \LMC(S).
\]

\section*{Source Question}

The source defines \(f\in \WLUC(S)\) when the orbit map
\[
  S\ni s\longmapsto L_s f\in C(S)
\]
is continuous for the weak topology of \(C(S)\), and \(f\in \LMC(S)\) when the
same map is continuous for the topology induced by multiplicative means on
\(C(S)\). Since multiplicative means are among the bounded linear functionals,
one always has \(\WLUC(S)\subseteq \LMC(S)\). After Theorem 3.5, the source
asks whether this inclusion can be proper.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Weak continuity tests a left-translation orbit against all bounded linear
functionals on \(C(S)\), while \(\LMC\)-continuity tests only multiplicative
means. The construction makes this gap visible. We build a rectangular band
\(S=A\times\N\), with multiplication \((a,m)(b,n)=(a,n)\). Left translates of
one function \(f\) then depend only on the \(a\)-coordinate and on the
\(\N\)-coordinate.

The space \(A\) is arranged so that a net of finite subsets
\(E\subset\beta\N\) converges to a point \(\infty\). For each \(E\), choose a
continuous function \(u_E\) on \(\beta\N\) which vanishes on \(E\) but still has
large integral against a fixed atomless probability measure. Vanishing at each
prescribed ultrafilter gives continuity against every multiplicative mean.
Large integrals give a single bounded linear functional witnessing failure of
weak continuity.

\section*{The Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
There is a Hausdorff topological semigroup \(S\) and a function
\(f\in\Cb(S)\) such that \(f\in \LMC(S)\) but \(f\notin \WLUC(S)\). Hence
\(\WLUC(S)\) is a proper subspace of \(\LMC(S)\) in general.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\N\) have the discrete topology and let \(\betaN\) be its Stone-Cech
compactification. First choose an atomless regular Borel probability measure
\(\mu\) on \(\betaN\). For example, take a map from \(\N\) to \([0,1]\) with
dense range and extend it to a continuous surjection \(q:\betaN\to[0,1]\).
The Lebesgue state on \(q^\ast C([0,1])\subset C(\betaN)\) extends to a state
on \(C(\betaN)\); by the Riesz representation theorem this state is integration
against a regular probability measure \(\mu\), and \(q_\ast\mu\) is Lebesgue
measure. If \(\mu\) had an atom at \(p\in\betaN\), then \(q_\ast\mu\) would
have an atom at \(q(p)\), so \(\mu\) is atomless.

For every finite set \(E\in\Fin\), regularity gives an open set
\(U_E\supset E\) with \(\mu(U_E)<1/2\). By Urysohn's lemma choose
\[
  u_E\in C(\betaN,[0,1])
\]
such that \(u_E=0\) on \(E\) and \(u_E=1\) on \(\betaN\setminus U_E\). Then
\[
  \int_{\betaN} u_E\,d\mu \geq 1-\mu(U_E)>1/2. \tag{1}
\]

Let
\[
  A=\{\infty\}\cup \Fin .
\]
Every point \(E\in\Fin\) is isolated, and a neighborhood basis of \(\infty\)
is
\[
  N_F=\{\infty\}\cup\{E\in\Fin: F\subseteq E\},\qquad F\in\Fin.
\]
This space is Hausdorff: finite subsets are isolated, and \(\infty\) is
separated from \(E_0\in\Fin\) by \(N_{\{p\}}\) for any \(p\in\betaN\setminus
E_0\).

Now put
\[
  S=A\times \N
\]
with the product topology and define multiplication by
\[
  (a,m)(b,n)=(a,n).
\]
This is an associative rectangular-band multiplication, and it is jointly
continuous because it is just the product of the first-coordinate projection
from the left factor and the second-coordinate projection from the right
factor. Thus \(S\) is a Hausdorff topological semigroup.

Define \(f:S\to\C\) by
\[
  f(\infty,n)=0,\qquad f(E,n)=u_E(n)\quad(E\in\Fin,\ n\in\N),
\]
where \(n\) is identified with its principal ultrafilter in \(\betaN\). The
function is bounded by \(1\). It is continuous at isolated points
\((E,n)\). At \((\infty,n)\), the neighborhood \(N_{\{n\}}\times\{n\}\) is
sent to \(0\), since \(E\supset\{n\}\) implies \(u_E(n)=0\). Hence
\(f\in\Cb(S)\).

For \(s=(a,m)\in S\), the left translate of \(f\) is
\[
  L_s f(b,n)=f((a,m)(b,n))=f(a,n).
\]
Thus \(L_s f\) depends only on \(a\) and \(n\). Let
\[
  j:\ell^\infty(\N)\longrightarrow \Cb(S),\qquad j(g)(a,n)=g(n).
\]
This is an isometric unital \(^\ast\)-homomorphism. If \(a=E\in\Fin\), then
\[
  L_{(E,m)}f=j(u_E|_{\N}),
\]
and \(L_{(\infty,m)}f=0\).

We claim first that \(f\in \LMC(S)\). Let \(\chi\) be any multiplicative mean
on \(\Cb(S)\). The restriction \(\chi\circ j\) is a character on
\(\ell^\infty(\N)=C(\betaN)\), so there is a point \(p_\chi\in\betaN\) such
that
\[
  \chi(j(g))=\widehat g(p_\chi)\qquad(g\in\ell^\infty(\N)).
\]
Consequently,
\[
  \chi(L_{(E,m)}f)=u_E(p_\chi),\qquad
  \chi(L_{(\infty,m)}f)=0.
\]
This scalar map is continuous at every isolated \(E\in\Fin\). At \(\infty\),
the neighborhood \(N_{\{p_\chi\}}\times \N\) is mapped to \(0\), because
every \(E\supset\{p_\chi\}\) satisfies \(u_E(p_\chi)=0\). Hence
\[
  s\longmapsto \chi(L_s f)
\]
is continuous for every multiplicative mean \(\chi\), exactly saying
\(f\in\LMC(S)\).

It remains to show that \(f\notin\WLUC(S)\). On the closed subspace
\(j(\ell^\infty(\N))\subset\Cb(S)\), define
\[
  M(j(g))=\int_{\betaN}\widehat g\,d\mu .
\]
This is a bounded linear functional of norm \(1\), so by Hahn-Banach it has an
extension \(\Phi\in\Cb(S)^\ast\). Direct \(\Fin\) by inclusion and consider
the net
\[
  s_E=(E,0)\longrightarrow s_\infty=(\infty,0)
\]
in \(S\). By (1),
\[
  \Phi(L_{s_E}f)=\int_{\betaN}u_E\,d\mu>1/2
\]
for every \(E\in\Fin\), while
\[
  \Phi(L_{s_\infty}f)=0.
\]
Therefore the orbit map \(s\mapsto L_s f\) is not weakly continuous at
\(s_\infty\). Thus \(f\notin\WLUC(S)\).

We have exhibited \(f\in \LMC(S)\setminus\WLUC(S)\), so the inclusion
\(\WLUC(S)\subseteq\LMC(S)\) is proper for this Hausdorff topological
semigroup \(S\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

No numerical or finite search is used. The proof verifies the required
properties directly:
\begin{itemize}
\item \(S=A\times\N\) is a jointly continuous rectangular band.
\item The constructed \(f\) is bounded and continuous because \(\N\) is
discrete and the neighborhoods \(N_{\{n\}}\) force \(u_E(n)=0\) near
\(\infty\).
\item Every multiplicative mean on \(\Cb(S)\), restricted to functions
depending only on the \(\N\)-coordinate, is evaluation at an ultrafilter
\(p\in\betaN\); the neighborhoods \(N_{\{p\}}\) make the scalar orbit
continuous.
\item A Hahn-Banach extension of integration against \(\mu\) witnesses failure
of weak continuity.
\end{itemize}

proper-subspace question.

The example is intentionally nonmetrizable and uses the Stone-Cech
compactification of a discrete countable space. It answers the source question
as stated for Hausdorff semitopological semigroups, and in fact for a
topological semigroup. It does not decide whether equality may hold under
additional hypotheses such as metrizability, local compactness of \(S\), or
first countability.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Accept as a likely valid counterexample to equality \(\WLUC(S)=\LMC(S)\).
Reviewers should focus on two points: the topology on \(A\), especially the use
of finite subsets of all of \(\betaN\), and the passage from multiplicative
means on \(\Cb(S)\) to point evaluations on the \(\N\)-coordinate subalgebra.
