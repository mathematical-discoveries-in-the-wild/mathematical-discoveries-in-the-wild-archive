\section{Source question}

Fradelizi, Meyer, and Yaskin \cite{FMY} define in Remark 3 the best constant
$\alpha_n>0$ such that, for every isotropic convex body $K\subset\R^n$ and
every orthonormal basis $u_1,\ldots,u_n$,
\begin{equation}
 \left(
 \frac{|K\cap\{x:\langle x,u_i\rangle\ge0,\ 1\le i\le n\}|}{|K|}
 \right)^{1/n}\ge\frac{\alpha_n}{2}.
 \label{eq:definition}
\end{equation}
They prove $\alpha_n\ge n^{-1}$ and ask for a better estimate, writing that
calculations suggest order $n^{-1/2}$.  Their Hadamard-cube example gives the
matching upper estimate for powers of two.  The complete source passage is on
p.~11 of the arXiv PDF:

We prove the suggested order in every dimension.

\section{Proof intuition}

The lower bound comes from an inball that is hidden in the isotropic
normalization.  Every one-dimensional marginal of the uniform measure on $K$
is log-concave.  Log-concavity prevents its variance from being generated by
a very long, very light tail on only one side.  Together with Grunbaum's
halfspace inequality, this forces the supporting distance of $K$ in every
direction to be a fixed multiple of the common standard deviation.  Hence
$K$ contains a centered Euclidean ball.  Gaussian maximum entropy compares
that standard deviation to $|K|^{1/n}$, and one sector of the ball lies in
each orthant.

For the upper bound, the source already gives the correct cube construction
when a Hadamard matrix of order $n$ is available.  We write an arbitrary $n$
as a sum of distinct powers of two, place a Sylvester-Hadamard construction in
each coordinate block, and take their product.  A bounded binary entropy loss
preserves the order $n^{-1/2}$.

\section{Two elementary lower-bound lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[One-sided log-concave moment bound]
Let $Y\ge0$ have a log-concave probability density, and put $m=\E Y$.  Then
\begin{equation}
 \E Y^2\le C_0m^2,
 \qquad C_0=4+\frac{8}{(\log2)^2}.
 \label{eq:moment}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The survival function $S(t)=\Prob(Y\ge t)$ is log-concave by the
Prekopa-Leindler theorem \cite{Prekopa}.  Markov's inequality gives
$S(2m)\le1/2$.  Since $\log S$ is concave and $S(0)=1$, for $t\ge2m$,
\[
 S(t)\le \exp\left(-\frac{(\log2)t}{2m}\right).
\]
Writing $a=(\log2)/2$ and integrating the tail,
\begin{align*}
 \E Y^2
 &=2\int_0^\infty tS(t)\,dt\\
 &\le4m^2+2\int_0^\infty t e^{-at/m}\,dt
 =\left(4+\frac{2}{a^2}\right)m^2
 =C_0m^2.
\end{align*}
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Centered inball at the covariance scale]
Let $X$ be uniform on a convex body $K\subset\R^n$ with centroid zero and
covariance matrix $\sigma^2I_n$.  Then
\begin{equation}
 c_0\sigma B_2^n\subset K,
 \qquad c_0=(1+eC_0)^{-1/2}.
 \label{eq:inball}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix a unit vector $\theta$, put $Z=\langle X,\theta\rangle$, and let
$b=h_K(\theta)=\sup_{x\in K}\langle x,\theta\rangle$.  The marginal density
of $Z$ is log-concave by Prekopa-Leindler.  Write
\[
 q=\Prob(Z\le0),\qquad
 M=\E Z_+=\E(-Z)_+,
\]
where the equality uses $\E Z=0$.  Grunbaum's centroid inequality gives
$q\ge e^{-1}$ \cite{Grunbaum,FMY}.  The conditional random variable
$Y=-Z\mid\{Z\le0\}$ has a log-concave density on $[0,\infty)$ and mean
$M/q$.  The preceding lemma therefore gives
\[
 \E[Z^2\mathbf1_{\{Z\le0\}}]
 =q\E Y^2\le \frac{C_0M^2}{q}\le eC_0b^2,
\]
because $M=\E Z_+\le b$.  Also
\[
 \E[Z^2\mathbf1_{\{Z\ge0\}}]\le b\E Z_+=bM\le b^2.
\]
Consequently $\sigma^2=\E Z^2\le(1+eC_0)b^2$, so
$h_K(\theta)\ge c_0\sigma$.  This holds for every $\theta\in S^{n-1}$,
which is equivalent to \eqref{eq:inball}.
\end{proof}

\section{Main theorem}

\begin{theorem}
For every $n\ge1$, the constant in \eqref{eq:definition} satisfies
\begin{equation}
 \frac{c}{\sqrt n}\le\alpha_n\le\frac{4e}{\sqrt n},
 \qquad
 c=\frac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi e}\sqrt{1+e(4+8/(\log2)^2)}}.
 \label{eq:main}
\end{equation}
In particular, $\alpha_n\asymp n^{-1/2}$, resolving the asymptotic-order
question in Remark 3 of the source.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Lower bound]
Let $X$ be uniform on an isotropic convex body $K$.  Thus $\E X=0$ and
$\operatorname{Cov}(X)=\sigma^2I_n$.  Nonnegativity of the relative entropy
of the uniform density $|K|^{-1}\mathbf1_K$ with respect to the centered
Gaussian of covariance $\sigma^2I_n$ gives
\[
 0\le-\log|K|+\frac n2\log(2\pi\sigma^2)+\frac n2,
\]
and hence
\begin{equation}
 \frac{\sigma}{|K|^{1/n}}\ge\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi e}}.
 \label{eq:entropy}
\end{equation}

For the orthant $O=\{x:\langle x,u_i\rangle\ge0,1\le i\le n\}$, the inball
lemma yields
\[
 \frac{|K\cap O|}{|K|}
 \ge 2^{-n}|B_2^n|\frac{(c_0\sigma)^n}{|K|}.
\]
The cube $[-n^{-1/2},n^{-1/2}]^n$ lies in $B_2^n$, so
$|B_2^n|^{1/n}\ge2/\sqrt n$.  Taking $n$th roots and using
\eqref{eq:entropy},
\[
 \left(\frac{|K\cap O|}{|K|}\right)^{1/n}
 \ge\frac{c_0}{\sqrt{2\pi e}\sqrt n}.
\]
Comparison with \eqref{eq:definition} proves the lower bound in
\eqref{eq:main}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Upper bound]
First let $d=2^r$.  Choose a Sylvester-Hadamard matrix $H_d$ with first row
equal to $(1,\ldots,1)$.  Its columns $f_1,\ldots,f_d$ are pairwise
orthogonal vertices of the facet $\{x_1=1\}$ of $B_\infty^d=[-1,1]^d$.
For the orthonormal basis $u_i=f_i/\sqrt d$, its positive orthant is the cone
$C_d=\operatorname{pos}(f_1,\ldots,f_d)$.  If
$x=\sum_i a_if_i$ with $a_i\ge0$, then $x_1=\sum_i a_i$; moreover
$|x_j|\le\sum_i a_i$ for every coordinate.  Therefore
\[
 B_\infty^d\cap C_d=\conv(0,f_1,\ldots,f_d).
\]
Since $|\det H_d|=d^{d/2}$,
\begin{equation}
 p_d:=\frac{|B_\infty^d\cap C_d|}{|B_\infty^d|}
 =\frac{d^{d/2}}{2^d d!}
 \le\left(\frac{e}{2\sqrt d}\right)^d.
 \label{eq:block}
\end{equation}

For arbitrary $n$, write its binary expansion as
$n=\sum_{d\in\mathcal D}d$, where the elements of $\mathcal D$ are distinct
powers of two.  Use the cube $B_\infty^n$, split its coordinates into these
blocks, and take the block-diagonal union of the Hadamard bases above.  The
orthant fraction is the product of the block fractions, so
\begin{equation}
 p_n\le\left(\frac e2\right)^n
       \exp\left(-\frac12\sum_{d\in\mathcal D}d\log d\right).
 \label{eq:product}
\end{equation}
If $2^m$ is the largest binary block, then $n<2^{m+1}$ and
\begin{align*}
 \sum_{d\in\mathcal D}d\log(n/d)
 &\le(\log2)\sum_{k=0}^m2^k(m+1-k)\\
 &<4n\log2.
\end{align*}
Thus $\sum d\log d>n\log n-4n\log2$.  Substitution in
\eqref{eq:product} gives
\[
 p_n^{1/n}\le\frac{2e}{\sqrt n}.
\]
The cube is isotropic, and $\alpha_n$ is twice the infimum of these $n$th-root
orthant fractions.  Hence $\alpha_n\le4e/\sqrt n$.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification, novelty, and limitations}

The script \texttt{code/verify\_hadamard\_blocks.py} verifies the exact
Hadamard orthogonality and determinant identities for powers of two through
$128$, checks the factorial bound numerically, and tests the binary entropy
and product inequalities for every $1\le n\le100{,}000$.  These computations
check finite-dimensional algebra but are not used to replace any analytic
step of the proof.

The local run indexes and three bounded web-search rounds on 21 July 2026
used the exact source phrase, title, authors, arXiv id, and combinations of
\emph{isotropic convex body}, \emph{positive orthant}, \emph{orthant
probability}, and the $n^{-n/2}$ scale.  Only the source and unrelated
isotropic/log-concave material were found.  No later answer or matching proof
was located.  This is not an exhaustive citation search, so novelty confidence
is moderate.

The result determines the sharp order of $\alpha_n$, not its exact value or
optimal leading constant.  Human review should focus on the survival-function
argument in Lemma 1, the inball implication in Lemma 2, and the binary-block
entropy estimate in the upper bound.
