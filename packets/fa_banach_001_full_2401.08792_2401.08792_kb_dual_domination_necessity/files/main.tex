\section*{Status}

This packet gives a full answer to the necessity question following Theorem 3
in Emelyanov--Erkursun-Ozcan--Gorokhova, \emph{d-Operators in Banach Lattices}
\cite{EEG2024}.  Their theorem proves that if $E'$ is a KB-space, then
domination by a positive d-compact, respectively d-weakly compact, operator
passes to positive dominated operators.  They ask whether the condition on
$E'$ is necessary.  It is necessary for both conclusions, in the natural
uniform sense over all Banach lattice ranges.

\section*{Definitions}

Let $E$ and $F$ be Banach lattices.  A bounded operator $T:E\to F$ is
d-compact, written $T\in\dK(E,F)$, if $T(D)$ is relatively norm compact for
every disjoint bounded set $D\subset E$.  It is d-weakly compact, written
$T\in\dW(E,F)$, if $T(D)$ is relatively weakly compact for every disjoint
bounded set $D\subset E$.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

If $E'$ is not a KB-space, then $E$ has a bounded positive disjoint sequence
$(x_n)$ which is not weakly null.  A positive functional $u\in E'_+$ sees a
subsequence uniformly: $u(x_n)\ge\delta>0$.  Split $u$ into disjoint positive
pieces $\varphi_n$ supported on the disjoint carriers of the $x_n$'s.  The
rank-one operator
\[
        Tx=u(x)\mathbf 1 \qquad (E\to c)
\]
is compact.  The dominated positive operator
\[
        Sx=\left(\sum_{i\ge k}\varphi_i(x)\right)_{k=1}^\infty
\]
sends $x_n$ to an initial-segment vector of height $u(x_n)$.  These
initial-segment vectors have no weakly convergent subsequence in $c$, because
coordinate functionals force any weak limit to have all coordinates equal to a
positive number, while the limit functional on $c$ forces the limit at infinity
to be zero.  Hence $S$ is not d-weakly compact, and therefore not d-compact.

\begin{lemma}[disjoint splitting of a positive functional]\label{lem:split}
Let $E$ be a Banach lattice, let $u\in E'_+$, and let $(x_n)$ be a disjoint
sequence in $E_+$.  There are pairwise disjoint functionals
$\varphi_n\in E'_+$ such that $0\le \varphi_n\le u$,
\[
        \sum_{n\in A}\varphi_n\le u
        \quad\hbox{for every finite }A\subset\N,
\]
and
\[
        \varphi_n(x_m)=
        \begin{cases}
        u(x_n), & m=n,\\
        0, & m\ne n.
        \end{cases}
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the standard carrier-projection decomposition in the order dual.  The
norm dual $E'$ is Dedekind complete as a vector lattice.  For each $n$, let
$P_n$ be the band projection in $E'$ onto the carrier band associated with the
principal band generated by $x_n$.  Since the vectors $x_n$ are disjoint, these
carrier bands are pairwise disjoint.  Put $\varphi_n=P_nu$.  Then the
$\varphi_n$ are pairwise disjoint positive components of $u$, so finite sums
are dominated by $u$.  The component $u-P_nu$ vanishes on the principal band of
$x_n$, while $P_nu$ vanishes on the disjoint principal bands generated by
$x_m$ for $m\ne n$, giving the displayed identities.
\end{proof}

\section*{Main theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
For a Banach lattice $E$, the following are equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)},leftmargin=*]
\item $E'$ is a KB-space.
\item For every Banach lattice $F$ and all $0\le S\le T$ in $\LL(E,F)$,
      $T\in\dK(E,F)$ implies $S\in\dK(E,F)$.
\item For every Banach lattice $F$ and all $0\le S\le T$ in $\LL(E,F)$,
      $T\in\dW(E,F)$ implies $S\in\dW(E,F)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The implications (a)$\Rightarrow$(b) and (a)$\Rightarrow$(c) are exactly
Theorem 3 of \cite{EEG2024}.

We prove the converse by showing that if $E'$ is not a KB-space, then both
domination principles fail with the same target lattice $F=c$.  By the
standard characterization of KB-duals used in \cite[Theorem 2.4.14]{Meyer},
there is a bounded disjoint sequence $(x_n)$ in $E_+$ which is not weakly null.
Passing to a subsequence, choose $u\in E'_+$ and $\delta>0$ such that
$u(x_n)\ge\delta$ for all $n$.

Apply Lemma \ref{lem:split} to obtain pairwise disjoint
$\varphi_n\in E'_+$ with finite sums dominated by $u$ and
$\varphi_n(x_m)=0$ for $m\ne n$, $\varphi_n(x_n)=u(x_n)$.  For $k\ge1$, define
\[
        g_k=\sup_{N\ge k}\sum_{i=k}^N\varphi_i
        =\sum_{i\ge k}\varphi_i\in E'_+ .
\]
The order sum exists in the Dedekind complete lattice $E'$ and satisfies
$0\le g_k\le u$ and $g_k\downarrow0$.  In particular, $g_k(x)\to0$ for every
$x\in E$.

Define positive operators $S:E\to c$ and $T:E\to c$ by
\[
        Sx=(g_k(x))_{k=1}^\infty,\qquad
        Tx=u(x)\mathbf 1 ,
\]
where $\mathbf 1=(1,1,\ldots)$.  Since $\|g_k\|\le\|u\|$ for all $k$, the
operator $S$ is bounded.  The operator $T$ is rank one, hence compact and in
particular d-compact and d-weakly compact.  Also, for $x\ge0$,
\[
        0\le g_k(x)\le u(x)\quad(k\ge1),
\]
so $0\le S\le T$.

It remains to see that $S$ is not d-weakly compact.  By boundedness of
$(u(x_n))$, pass to a further subsequence, still denoted $(x_n)$, such that
$u(x_n)\to a$ for some $a\ge\delta>0$.  For each $n$,
\[
        Sx_n
        = u(x_n)(\underbrace{1,\ldots,1}_{n\ \mathrm{times}},0,0,\ldots).
\]
Let $(Sx_{n_j})$ be any subsequence.  If it were weakly convergent in $c$ to
some $y\in c$, then each coordinate functional would give $y_k=a$ for every
fixed $k$.  Hence $y$ would have coordinate sequence constantly equal to $a$,
so its limit at infinity would be $a$.  On the other hand, the limit functional
$\ell_\infty:c\to\mathbb R$, $\ell_\infty((t_k))=\lim_k t_k$, satisfies
$\ell_\infty(Sx_{n_j})=0$ for every $j$, and weak convergence would force
$\ell_\infty(y)=0$.  This contradiction shows that $\{Sx_n:n\in\N\}$ is not
relatively weakly compact.

The set $\{x_n:n\in\N\}$ is disjoint and bounded, so $S\notin\dW(E,c)$.  Since
every relatively norm compact set is relatively weakly compact, $S$ is not
d-compact either.  Thus both (b) and (c) fail whenever $E'$ is not a KB-space.
\end{proof}
