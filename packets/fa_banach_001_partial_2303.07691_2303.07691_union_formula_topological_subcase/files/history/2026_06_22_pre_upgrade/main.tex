\section*{Source and Open Question}

The source paper is Ondrej F. K. Kalenda and Matias Raja,
\emph{Topologies related to (I)-envelopes}, arXiv:2303.07691
\cite{KR}.

Question 5.1 of the source paper asks whether, for a Banach space $X$ and
convex sets $A,B\subset X^*$ such that $A\cup B$ is also convex, one always
has
\[
\Ienv(A\cup B)=\Ienv(A)\cup \Ienv(B).
\]
The question is motivated by the topological-closure behavior needed for the
non-locally-convex version of the paper's Question 1.1.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The general question is difficult because it asks whether the operation
$A\mapsto \Ienv(A)$ has a finite-union behavior that ordinary topological
closures have automatically. If, however, the (I)-envelope is already known to
come from closure in a fixed topology after taking convex hulls, the desired
identity is immediate. The hypotheses in Question 5.1 make $A$, $B$, and
$A\cup B$ convex, so the convex-hull operation does not add anything; all that
remains is the elementary identity
\[
\overline{A\cup B}^{\,\tau}=\overline A^{\,\tau}\cup \overline B^{\,\tau}.
\]
The source theorem supplies such a topology when $X$ contains no copy of
$\ell^1$.

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let $X$ be a Banach space. Suppose that there is a topology $\tau$ on $X^*$
such that
\[
\Ienv(S)=\overline{\co S}^{\,\tau}
\]
for every $S\subset X^*$. Then for every pair of convex sets
$A,B\subset X^*$ such that $A\cup B$ is convex,
\[
\Ienv(A\cup B)=\Ienv(A)\cup \Ienv(B).
\]
Consequently, the equality holds for every Banach space $X$ containing no
isomorphic copy of $\ell^1$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $C=A\cup B$. Since $A$, $B$, and $C$ are convex, we have
\[
\co A=A,\qquad \co B=B,\qquad \co C=C.
\]
Using the assumed topological representation of the (I)-envelope and the
finite-union law for closure in any topological space, we obtain
\[
\Ienv(A\cup B)
=\overline{C}^{\,\tau}
=\overline{A\cup B}^{\,\tau}
=\overline A^{\,\tau}\cup \overline B^{\,\tau}
=\Ienv(A)\cup\Ienv(B).
\]

For the final assertion, apply Theorem 3.3 of \cite{KR}: if $X$ contains no
isomorphic copy of $\ell^1$, then for every $S\subset X^*$,
\[
\Ienv(S)=\overline{\co S}^{\,\sigma(X^*,B_1(X))}.
\]
Thus the preceding argument applies with
$\tau=\sigma(X^*,B_1(X))$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only two inputs.

\begin{itemize}
\item The closure of a finite union is the union of the closures in every
topological space.
\item The source paper's Theorem 3.3 represents all (I)-envelopes by the
locally convex topology $\sigma(X^*,B_1(X))$ when $X$ contains no copy of
$\ell^1$.
\end{itemize}

The convexity assumption in Question 5.1 is essential for this short proof:
without it, the terms would be closures of $\co(A\cup B)$, $\co A$, and
$\co B$, and the finite-union closure identity would not directly apply.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This is only a partial result. It does not answer Question 5.1 for spaces
where no topology is known to represent all (I)-envelopes, and it gives no new
counterexample to the topological version of Question 1.1.

The active run indexes were searched for arXiv:2303.07691, the source title,
``(I)-envelope'', ``Ienv(A union B)'', ``Ienv(A) union Ienv(B)'', and related
topology keywords. A bounded web search on June 14, 2026 for exact
union-formula phrases found only the source paper and no later explicit
answer. Since this packet is a source-theorem corollary and only a subcase,
it should be reviewed as a modest partial result.
