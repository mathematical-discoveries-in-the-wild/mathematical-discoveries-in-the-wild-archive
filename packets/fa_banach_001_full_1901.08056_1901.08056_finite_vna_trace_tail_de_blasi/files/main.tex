\noindent\textbf{Status.} Candidate full solution, likely valid, to the finite
$\sigma$-finite clause of Question 12.1 in Hamhalter--Kalenda--Peralta--Pfitzner
\cite{HKPP}. The general semifinite case is not claimed.

\section{Source question}

The source is J. Hamhalter, O. F. K. Kalenda, A. M. Peralta, and H. Pfitzner,
\emph{Measures of weak non-compactness in preduals of von Neumann algebras and
JBW$^*$-triples}, arXiv:1901.08056. Question 12.1 appears on PDF page 54.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The question asks for a formula, in terms of a normal semifinite faithful trace
$\tau$, for the De Blasi measure of weak noncompactness in $M_*$, and asks
explicitly whether this can at least be done for finite $\sigma$-finite von
Neumann algebras.

\section{Definitions and statement}

Let $M$ be a finite $\sigma$-finite von Neumann algebra. Such an algebra admits
a faithful normal finite trace; this is also recalled in Lemma 7.12(a) of the
source paper \cite{HKPP}. Fix one such trace $\tau$. We do not require
$\tau(1)=1$.

We use the standard isometric identification
\[
 M_* = L^1(M,\tau), \qquad \phi_h(x)=\tau(hx),
 \qquad \|\phi_h\|=\|h\|_1=\tau(|h|).
\]
For a bounded set $A\subset L^1(M,\tau)$, its De Blasi measure is
\[
 \omega(A)=\inf_K\ \sup_{h\in A}\dist(h,K),
\]
where $K$ ranges over the relatively weakly compact subsets of
$L^1(M,\tau)$.

For $\delta>0$, define the right trace-tail quantity
\[
 \ud(A)=\sup_{h\in A}\ \sup\bigl\{\|he\|_1:
 e\in\Proj(M),\ \tau(e)\leq\delta\bigr\},
\]
and, by monotonicity,
\[
 \uu(A)=\lim_{\delta\downarrow0}\ud(A)
       =\inf_{\delta>0}\ud(A).
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $M$ be a finite $\sigma$-finite von Neumann algebra, let $\tau$ be a
faithful normal finite trace on $M$, and let $A\subset M_*=L^1(M,\tau)$ be
bounded. Then
\[
 \boxed{\ \omega(A)=\uu(A)\ }.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
 \omega(A)=\lim_{\delta\downarrow0}
 \sup_{h\in A}\sup_{\substack{e\in\Proj(M)\\\tau(e)\leq\delta}}
 \|he\|_1.
\]
Thus Question 12.1 has an affirmative answer for finite $\sigma$-finite von
Neumann algebras.
\end{theorem}

The analogous left-tail formula follows either by the same proof or by applying
the theorem to $A^*=\{h^*:h\in A\}$.

\section{Proof Intuition}

The proposed quantity vanishes precisely on weakly compact sets for a very
concrete reason. The source paper proves that a weakly compact set in $M_*$ is
uniformly controlled by one normal state in the strong$^*$ topology. A
small-$\tau$ projection, multiplied by any contraction, is uniformly small for
that state; hence every functional in the weakly compact set has a small right
tail on that projection. This immediately gives $\uu(A)\leq\omega(A)$.

For the reverse inequality, write $h=v|h|$ and cut $|h|$ off at height $c$.
The discarded part is $he$, where $e=1_{(c,\infty)}(|h|)$ and
$\tau(e)\leq\|h\|_1/c$. The retained parts have uniformly bounded $L^2$ norm.
Because the trace is finite, the inclusion $L^2(M,\tau)\to L^1(M,\tau)$ is
bounded, so a bounded $L^2$-ball becomes a weakly compact subset of $L^1$.
The trace tail is therefore exactly the distance scale needed in the De Blasi
definition.

\section{Proof}

We first isolate the weak-compactness input.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:wc-tail}
If $K\subset L^1(M,\tau)$ is relatively weakly compact, then
$\uu(K)=0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Replacing $K$ by its weak closure, assume it is weakly compact. Theorem
11.1(b') of \cite{HKPP}, specialized to the von Neumann algebra $M$, gives a
normal state $\psi$ such that the following holds: for every $\varepsilon>0$
there is $\eta>0$ such that
\[
 x\in B_M,\quad \|x\|_\psi<\eta
 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
 |\phi_h(x)|<\varepsilon\quad(h\in K),                         \tag{1}
\]
where
\[
 \|x\|_\psi^2=\psi(x^*\circ x)
 =\frac12\psi(x^*x+xx^*).
\]
Write $\psi(x)=\tau(gx)$ with $g\in L^1(M,\tau)_+$ and $\tau(g)=1$.

We claim that
\[
 \sup_{\substack{e\in\Proj(M),\ \tau(e)\leq\delta\\a\in B_M}}
 \|ea\|_\psi\longrightarrow0\qquad(\delta\downarrow0).       \tag{2}
\]
Indeed, given $c>0$, use functional calculus to write $g=g_0+r$ with
$0\leq g_0\leq c1$, $r\geq0$, and $\|r\|_1\to0$ as $c\to\infty$.
For $e\in\Proj(M)$ and $a\in B_M$, traciality and positivity give
\begin{align*}
 \psi(a^*ea)&=\tau(eaga^*)\leq c\tau(e)+\|r\|_1,\\
 \psi(eaa^*e)&\leq\psi(e)\leq c\tau(e)+\|r\|_1.
\end{align*}
Consequently
\[
 \|ea\|_\psi^2\leq c\tau(e)+\|r\|_1.
\]
First choose $c$ so that $\|r\|_1$ is small, and then choose $\delta$ so that
$c\delta$ is small. This proves (2).

For such a small $\delta$, apply (1) to $x=ea$. Since
$\phi_h(ea)=\tau(hea)$ and $M=(L^1(M,\tau))^*$ isometrically,
\[
 \|he\|_1=\sup_{a\in B_M}|\tau(hea)|<\varepsilon
 \qquad(h\in K,\ e\in\Proj(M),\ \tau(e)\leq\delta).
\]
Thus $\ud(K)\to0$.
\end{proof}

We now prove the two inequalities in Theorem \ref{thm:main}.

Let $K$ be relatively weakly compact and set
\[
 d=\sup_{h\in A}\dist(h,K).
\]
For $\zeta>0$, each $h\in A$ has $k_h\in K$ with
$\|h-k_h\|_1<d+\zeta$. Right multiplication by a projection is contractive
on $L^1$, so
\[
 \|he\|_1\leq \|(h-k_h)e\|_1+\|k_he\|_1
 \leq d+\zeta+\ud(K)
\]
whenever $\tau(e)\leq\delta$. Lemma \ref{lem:wc-tail}, followed by
$\zeta\downarrow0$, yields $\uu(A)\leq d$. Taking the infimum over $K$ gives
\[
 \uu(A)\leq\omega(A).                                        \tag{3}
\]

For the reverse inequality, put $R=\sup_{h\in A}\|h\|_1$. The case $R=0$
is trivial, so suppose $R>0$. For $h=v|h|$ its polar decomposition and $c>0$,
set
\[
 e_{h,c}=1_{(c,\infty)}(|h|),\qquad
 k_{h,c}=v|h|1_{[0,c]}(|h|).
\]
Then
\[
 \tau(e_{h,c})\leq\frac{\|h\|_1}{c}\leq\frac Rc,             \tag{4}
\]
and
\[
 \|h-k_{h,c}\|_1=\|he_{h,c}\|_1\leq u_{\tau,R/c}(A).         \tag{5}
\]
Moreover,
\[
 \|k_{h,c}\|_2^2
 =\tau\bigl(|h|^2 1_{[0,c]}(|h|)\bigr)
 \leq c\tau(|h|)\leq cR.                                    \tag{6}
\]

Let $J:L^2(M,\tau)\to L^1(M,\tau)$ be the canonical inclusion. Since
$\tau(1)<\infty$, noncommutative Cauchy--Schwarz gives
$\|Jz\|_1\leq\tau(1)^{1/2}\|z\|_2$, so $J$ is bounded. The closed ball
$\sqrt{cR}\,B_{L^2(M,\tau)}$ is weakly compact, and therefore its image
\[
 W_c=J\bigl(\sqrt{cR}\,B_{L^2(M,\tau)}\bigr)
\]
is weakly compact in $L^1(M,\tau)$. By (6), every $k_{h,c}$ lies in $W_c$.
Equation (5) therefore implies
\[
 \omega(A)\leq\sup_{h\in A}\dist(h,W_c)
 \leq u_{\tau,R/c}(A).
\]
Letting $c\to\infty$ gives
\[
 \omega(A)\leq\uu(A).                                        \tag{7}
\]
Together, (3) and (7) prove the theorem.

\section{Verification and edge cases}

The script \texttt{code/verify\_trace\_tail.py} checks the spectral truncation
identities (4)--(6), right-multiplication contractivity, and the finite-trace
$L^2\to L^1$ estimate on 240 random complex matrices. It is only a numerical
sanity check; the proof above is analytic.

The finiteness of $\tau(1)$ is used exactly once: to make the inclusion
$L^2(M,\tau)\to L^1(M,\tau)$ bounded. For a general semifinite trace, spectral
truncation controls concentration on small-trace projections but not escape to
regions of arbitrarily large trace. Thus the theorem does not claim the first,
fully semifinite clause of Question 12.1; a separate trace-tightness term would
be needed there.

\section{Bounded novelty search}

Before promotion, the run indexes were searched for arXiv:1901.08056 and the
terms ``De Blasi'', ``weak non-compactness'', ``von Neumann predual'', and
``trace formula''. The only hit used arXiv:1901.08056 as background for a
different operator-valued quantum-measure result. Web/arXiv searches on 21 July
2026 used the phrases ``De Blasi measure noncommutative L1 projection trace
formula'', ``De Blasi von Neumann algebra L1 uniform integrability'', and
``measure of weak noncompactness noncommutative L1 projection''. They found the
commutative $L^1$ formula of Ka\v{c}ena--Kalenda--Spurn\'y \cite{KKS}, the source
paper, and later work on measures for operators, but no exact set formula of
the form proved here for finite noncommutative $L^1(M,\tau)$. This is a bounded
search, not a guarantee of novelty.

\section{Human-review recommendation}

The result should receive expert review. The two most important checks are:
(i) the specialization of Theorem 11.1(b') of \cite{HKPP} in Lemma
\ref{lem:wc-tail}, including the uniform estimate for $ea$; and (ii) the use of
the finite-trace $L^2$ ball as a weakly compact container for all spectral
truncations. Subject to those checks, the proof gives a complete exact formula
for the finite $\sigma$-finite case explicitly requested in Question 12.1.
