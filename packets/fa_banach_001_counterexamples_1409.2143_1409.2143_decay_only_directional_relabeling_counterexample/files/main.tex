\section{Source problem}

Muller and Muller \cite{source} study estimates controlling a directional
wavelet projection $W^{(\varepsilon)}$ by a Riesz transform $R_{i_0}$ when
$\varepsilon_{i_0}=1$. Their wavelets are indexed by dyadic cubes
$Q\subset\R^n$ and orientations
$\varepsilon\in\{0,1\}^n\setminus\{0\}$. The localization and decay
condition (1.2a) is
\begin{equation}
 |\varphi_Q^{(\varepsilon)}(x)|
 \leq C\left(1+\frac{\operatorname{dist}(x,Q)}{s(Q)}\right)^{-n(1+\delta)}.
 \label{eq:decay}
\end{equation}
After comparing their Holder-regular estimates with the directional Haar
estimate, they state the following problem on p.~4 of the arXiv PDF:

\begin{figure}[htbp]
 \centering
 \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
 \caption{Full-width source crop containing the complete decay-only problem.}
 \label{fig:problem}
\end{figure}

In transcription: ``It remains an open problem to prove interpolatory
estimates for directional projections $W^{(\varepsilon)}$ when the underlying
wavelets satisfy decay estimates only.'' We show that decay alone is
insufficient. The obstruction is already present in $L^2(\R^2)$.

\section{Definitions and target class of estimates}

Let $R_1$ be the first Riesz transform on $L^2(\R^2)$, with Fourier
multiplier
\[
 -i\frac{\xi_1}{(\xi_1^2+\xi_2^2)^{1/2}}.
\]
An interpolatory estimate of the source's type has the property that its
right-hand side is $o(\|u\|_2)$ whenever
$\|R_1u\|_2=o(\|u\|_2)$. To cover both the power and logarithmic estimates,
we consider the general form
\begin{equation}
 \|W^{(1,0)}u\|_2
 \leq C\|u\|_2\,
 \Psi\!\left(\frac{\|R_1u\|_2}{\|u\|_2}\right),
 \qquad u\ne0,
 \label{eq:generalestimate}
\end{equation}
where $\Psi:[0,1]\to[0,\infty)$ satisfies
$\Psi(t)\to0$ as $t\downarrow0$. The source's power controls use
$\Psi(t)=t^\beta$ with $\beta>0$, while its Lipschitz endpoint has, up to a
constant, $\Psi(t)=t(1+\log(1/t))$.

\section{Proof intuition}

Once every direction-specific condition is removed, decay sees where a
wavelet is located but not which coordinate its orientation label describes.
We exploit this literally: swap the labels of the horizontal and vertical
tensor Haar wavelets. This preserves the complete decay-only hypothesis, but
the projection now called ``$(1,0)$-directional'' actually selects wavelets
which cancel in the second coordinate.

A long horizontal strip is a finite sum of those selected wavelets, so the
relabeled projection fixes it. As the strip becomes longer, its first-coordinate
Fourier frequencies concentrate near zero, whereas its second-coordinate
Haar oscillation stays at unit scale. The first Riesz multiplier therefore
tends to zero on its Fourier mass. This contradicts every estimate whose
control vanishes with the first Riesz norm.

\section{Counterexample}

For a dyadic interval $I$, let
\[
 h_I^0=\1_I,
 \qquad h_I^1=\1_{I_{\mathrm L}}-\1_{I_{\mathrm R}},
\]
where $I_{\mathrm L},I_{\mathrm R}$ are its two dyadic children. If
$Q=I_1\times I_2$ is a dyadic square and
$\eta=(\eta_1,\eta_2)\in\A:=\{(1,0),(0,1),(1,1)\}$, put
\[
 h_Q^\eta(x_1,x_2)=h_{I_1}^{\eta_1}(x_1)
                     h_{I_2}^{\eta_2}(x_2).
\]
The family $\{h_Q^\eta/|Q|^{1/2}\}$ is the usual tensor Haar orthonormal
basis of $L^2(\R^2)$.

Let $\pi$ be the permutation of $\A$ which swaps $(1,0)$ and $(0,1)$ and
fixes $(1,1)$. Define a relabeled wavelet system by
\begin{equation}
 \varphi_Q^\varepsilon=h_Q^{\pi(\varepsilon)}.
 \label{eq:relabel}
\end{equation}
Relabeling preserves orthonormality and completeness. Moreover,
\[
 |\varphi_Q^\varepsilon(x)|\leq\1_Q(x)
 \leq\left(1+\frac{\operatorname{dist}(x,Q)}{s(Q)}\right)^{-2(1+\delta)}
\]
for every $\delta>0$. Thus \eqref{eq:decay} holds with $C=1$, while no
regularity or direction-specific cancellation is being assumed.

Let $W^{(1,0)}$ be the directional projection associated with the relabeled
system. By \eqref{eq:relabel}, it is exactly the ordinary Haar projection
$P^{(0,1)}$.

\begin{theorem}[Decay-only directional interpolation fails]
For the decay-only wavelet system \eqref{eq:relabel}, there are nonzero finite
Haar sums $u_N\in L^2(\R^2)$ such that
\begin{equation}
 W^{(1,0)}u_N=u_N,
 \qquad
 \frac{\|R_1u_N\|_2}{\|u_N\|_2}\longrightarrow0.
 \label{eq:sequence}
\end{equation}
Consequently, for this system there are no $C<\infty$ and no function
$\Psi$ with $\Psi(t)\to0$ for which \eqref{eq:generalestimate} holds for all
$u\ne0$. In particular, all positive-power interpolatory estimates and the
logarithmic endpoint estimate of the source fail under decay only.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
 a=\1_{[0,1)},
 \qquad b=\1_{[0,1/2)}-\1_{[1/2,1)},
\]
and, for a positive integer $N$, set
\begin{equation}
 u_N(x_1,x_2)=\1_{[0,N)}(x_1)b(x_2)=a(x_1/N)b(x_2).
 \label{eq:uN}
\end{equation}
For $0\leq k<N$, the square
$Q_k=[k,k+1)\times[0,1)$ is dyadic and
\[
 h_{Q_k}^{(0,1)}(x_1,x_2)=\1_{[k,k+1)}(x_1)b(x_2).
\]
Therefore
\[
 u_N=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}h_{Q_k}^{(0,1)}
     =\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\varphi_{Q_k}^{(1,0)},
\]
so $W^{(1,0)}u_N=u_N$ exactly.

It remains to estimate the first Riesz transform. With a unitary Fourier
transform, the dilation in \eqref{eq:uN} gives
\[
 \widehat{u_N}(\xi_1,\xi_2)
 =N\widehat a(N\xi_1)\widehat b(\xi_2),
 \qquad \|u_N\|_2^2=N\|a\|_2^2\|b\|_2^2=N.
\]
Plancherel and the change of variable $\zeta=N\xi_1$ now yield
\begin{align}
 \frac{\|R_1u_N\|_2^2}{\|u_N\|_2^2}
 &=\int_{\R^2}
 \frac{(\zeta/N)^2}{(\zeta/N)^2+\xi_2^2}
 |\widehat a(\zeta)|^2|\widehat b(\xi_2)|^2
 \,d\zeta\,d\xi_2.
 \label{eq:plancherel}
\end{align}
For almost every $(\zeta,\xi_2)$ the multiplier in
\eqref{eq:plancherel} tends to zero. It is bounded by one, while
$|\widehat a(\zeta)|^2|\widehat b(\xi_2)|^2$ is integrable. Dominated
convergence proves \eqref{eq:sequence}.

If \eqref{eq:generalestimate} held, applying it to $u_N$ and using
$W^{(1,0)}u_N=u_N$ would give
\[
 1\leq C\Psi\!\left(\frac{\|R_1u_N\|_2}{\|u_N\|_2}\right).
\]
The right side tends to zero, a contradiction.
\end{proof}

\section{Interpretation and limitation}

The counterexample isolates the structural issue: localization and decay do
not make the label $\varepsilon_i=1$ signify cancellation in coordinate $i$.
Accordingly, decay alone cannot order the projection by $R_i$. Some
label-compatible directional hypothesis is necessary.

The source's full admissibility definition also includes a sectional
oscillation estimate (1.2c) in every coordinate with
$\varepsilon_i=1$. The relabeled Haar system fails that condition in the
nominal first coordinate. Hence, if the intended problem was to retain
(1.2c) and discard only Holder regularity, that narrower problem is not
settled here. The theorem is a full negative answer to the words ``decay
estimates only'' and a sharp boundary obstruction to any stronger intended
formulation.

\section{Verification and novelty boundary}

Every constructed function is a finite Haar sum, the projection identity is
exact, and the only limiting step is the displayed Plancherel calculation
with an integrable dominating function. The failure at $p=2$ is enough to
rule out a decay-only theorem asserted for the full range $1<p<\infty$.

22 July 2026 using the source id, exact open-problem sentence, title, authors,
