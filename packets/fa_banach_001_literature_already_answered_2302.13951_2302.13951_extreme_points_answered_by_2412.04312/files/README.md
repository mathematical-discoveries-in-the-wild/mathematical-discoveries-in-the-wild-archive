# Literature Status: Extreme-Point Problem Answered By arXiv:2412.04312

Source/open-problem paper: Ramón J. Aliaga, Eva Pernecká, and Richard J.
Smith, *Convex integrals of molecules in Lipschitz-free spaces*,
arXiv:2302.13951.

Supporting answer paper: Ramón J. Aliaga, Eva Pernecká, and Richard J. Smith,
*A solution to the extreme point problem and other applications of Choquet
theory to Lipschitz-free spaces*, arXiv:2412.04312.

Result type: `literature_already_answered`

Scope: only the extreme-point open problem mentioned in Section 6 of the
source paper. This packet does not claim to answer the source paper's explicit
Question about non-overlapping convex series of molecules, nor its Question
about replacing an isometric copy by a bi-Lipschitz copy in Theorem 4.1.

## Identification

In Section 6, the source paper says that the main open problem in the
extremal-structure direction is the characterization of the extreme points of
the unit ball of `F(M)`, more specifically the conjecture that all extreme
points are elementary molecules.

The later supporting paper explicitly frames the same problem as the
long-standing extreme point problem for Lipschitz-free spaces and proves
Theorem `th:extreme`: if `M` is complete, then the extreme points of
`B_{F(M)}` are exactly the elementary molecules satisfying the strict
metric-segment condition.
