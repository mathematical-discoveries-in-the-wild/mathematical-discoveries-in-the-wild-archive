# Literature-Implied Answer: Two p-Free-Space Reductions

Status: `literature_implied_answer`

Source paper: Fernando Albiac, Jose L. Ansorena, Marek Cuth, and Michal Doucha, "Lipschitz algebras and Lipschitz-free spaces over unbounded metric spaces", arXiv:2011.12993.

Supporting paper: Fernando Albiac and Jose L. Ansorena, "Lipschitz extensions into p-Banach spaces, and canonical embeddings of Lipschitz-free p-spaces for 0<p<1", arXiv:2603.28193.

Source signals checked:

- Remark 2.8: whether the canonical linearization `L_j:F_p(N)->F_p(M)` of a subspace inclusion is an isomorphism for `0<p<1`; the source observes that its bounded-space construction reduces the unbounded metric case to bounded spaces.
- Remark 2.10: whether the envelope map `E_{p,1,M}:F_p(M)->F(M)` is one-to-one for every metric space `M` and `0<p<1`; the source again notes reduction to bounded spaces.

Resolution: arXiv:2603.28193 proves both in stronger form. Theorem 3.13 gives that `L[N,M;p]` is an isomorphic embedding for every pointed `p`-metric space and `0 in N subset M`. Its final theorem proves that for every `0<p<=q<=1` and pointed `q`-metric space `M`, the canonical envelope map `J_{p,q}[M]` is one-to-one; taking `q=1` gives the source's envelope-map problem.
