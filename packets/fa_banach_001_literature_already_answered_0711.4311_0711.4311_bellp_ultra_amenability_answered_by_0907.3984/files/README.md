## Identification

Daws--Runde arXiv:0711.4311 records that amenability of `B(ell^p)` was open
for `p in (1,infty) \ {2}` and studies consequences of a hypothetical positive
answer, especially amenability of `ell^infty(B(ell^p))`,
`ell^infty(K(ell^p))`, and `ell^infty(K(E))` for `L^p`-spaces.

Runde arXiv:0907.3984 later proves that `B(ell^p)` is never amenable and,
more strongly, proves non-amenability of `ell^infty(K(E))` for every
infinite-dimensional `L^p`-space. This explicitly answers the source's main
open question and the principal `ell^infty(K(E))` side frontier in the
`L^p`-space class.

## Scope

This is an already-known literature answer, not a new proof produced by the
run. It complements the existing packet
`0401122_bellp_amenability_answered_by_0907.3984`, which clears Ozawa's
earlier formulation of the same `B(ell^p)` open range.

The packet does not claim to settle Johnson's broad question whether `B(E)` can
be amenable for any infinite-dimensional Banach space `E`, nor the fully
general abstract question whether amenability of `ell^infty(A)` forces
amenability of `A**`.
