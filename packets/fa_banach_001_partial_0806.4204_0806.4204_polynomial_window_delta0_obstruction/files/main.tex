\section*{Status}

Partial result, likely valid. The result below does not settle the full
necessity of the \(\Delta^0\) condition in Lefevre--Li--Queffelec--Rodriguez-
Piazza \cite{LLQR}. It gives a general obstruction to replacing \(\Delta^0\) by
weaker growth assumptions when one wants the fixed-\(p\) estimate \((W)\).

\section*{Source Question}

The source paper proves that, if \(\Psi\in\Delta^0\) and \(X\subset M^\Psi\),
then an operator \(T:X\to Y\) is weakly compact if and only if, for some and
then for all \(1\le p<\infty\),
\[
  \forall \eps>0\ \exists C_\eps>0,\qquad
  \|T f\|\le C_\eps\|f\|_p+\eps\|f\|_\Psi,\qquad f\in X. \tag{W}
\]
Immediately before Theorem 4, the source asks whether the condition
\(\Psi\in\Delta^0\) is actually necessary for this characterization.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The obstruction is already visible on characteristic functions of small sets.
If \(\Psi(x)\) behaves like \(x^q\) along a subsequence but is much larger than
\(x^p\) there, then a spike of height \(x\) and measure \(1/\Psi(x)\) has
Orlicz norm one. Its \(L^p\)-norm tends to zero, while its \(L^q\)-norm remains
bounded below. The inclusion into the reflexive space \(L^q\) is weakly compact,
so these spikes directly contradict the fixed-\(p\) version of \((W)\).

\section*{Partial Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \((\Omega,\mathbb P)\) be a nonatomic probability space and let \(\Psi\) be
a strictly increasing Orlicz function. Fix \(1\le p<q<\infty\). Assume:
\[
  x^q\le K\Psi(x)\qquad (x\ge x_0)
\]
for some \(K,x_0>0\), and assume that there are \(x_n\to\infty\) and \(C>0\)
such that
\[
  \Psi(x_n)\le Cx_n^q,
  \qquad
  \frac{\Psi(x_n)}{x_n^p}\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
Then the inclusion operator
\[
  J_q:M^\Psi\longrightarrow L^q(\Omega)
\]
is weakly compact, but \(J_q\) does not satisfy the fixed-\(p\) estimate
\[
  \forall \eps>0\ \exists C_\eps>0,\qquad
  \|J_q f\|_q\le C_\eps\|f\|_p+\eps\|f\|_\Psi,\qquad f\in M^\Psi. \tag{\(W_p\)}
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First \(J_q\) is bounded. Indeed, if \(\|f\|_\Psi\le1\), then
\(\int_\Omega\Psi(|f|)\,d\mathbb P\le1\). Splitting into
\(\{|f|<x_0\}\) and \(\{|f|\ge x_0\}\) gives
\[
  \int_\Omega |f|^q\,d\mathbb P
  \le x_0^q+K\int_\Omega\Psi(|f|)\,d\mathbb P
  \le x_0^q+K.
\]
By homogeneity, \(J_q:M^\Psi\to L^q\) is continuous. Since \(L^q\) is reflexive
for \(1<q<\infty\), every bounded operator into \(L^q\), in particular \(J_q\),
is weakly compact.

We now show that \(J_q\) fails \((W_p)\). Since \((\Omega,\mathbb P)\) is
nonatomic and \(\Psi(x_n)\to\infty\), choose measurable sets \(A_n\) with
\[
  \mathbb P(A_n)=\frac{1}{\Psi(x_n)}.
\]
Set \(f_n=x_n\,\mathbf 1_{A_n}\). Then
\[
  \int_\Omega \Psi(|f_n|)\,d\mathbb P
  =\Psi(x_n)\mathbb P(A_n)=1,
\]
and strict monotonicity of \(\Psi\) gives \(\|f_n\|_\Psi=1\). Also
\[
  \|f_n\|_p
  =
  x_n\,\Psi(x_n)^{-1/p}
  =
  \left(\frac{x_n^p}{\Psi(x_n)}\right)^{1/p}
  \longrightarrow0,
\]
whereas
\[
  \|J_q f_n\|_q
  =
  x_n\,\Psi(x_n)^{-1/q}
  \ge C^{-1/q}.
\]
Choose \(0<\eps<C^{-1/q}/2\). If \((W_p)\) held, then for some \(C_\eps\) and
all \(n\),
\[
  C^{-1/q}
  \le
  \|J_q f_n\|_q
  \le
  C_\eps\|f_n\|_p+\eps\|f_n\|_\Psi
  =
  C_\eps\|f_n\|_p+\eps.
\]
Letting \(n\to\infty\) gives \(C^{-1/q}\le\eps\), a contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
The factorial Orlicz function used in Remark 3 of the source paper fits the
mechanism with \(p=2\) and \(q=3\): along \(x_n=n!\) one has
\(\Psi(x_n)\lesssim x_n^3\) while \(\Psi(x_n)/x_n^2\to\infty\). Thus its
inclusion \(M^\Psi\to L^3\) gives an instance of the obstruction to the
fixed-\(L^2\) estimate.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof is analytic and uses only the Luxemburg norm of indicators and the
reflexivity of \(L^q\) for \(1<q<\infty\). No computational verification was
used.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Search}

This packet does not prove that \(\Delta^0\) is necessary in the full sense
asked by the source. It proves that a fixed lower exponent \(p\) cannot control
weakly compact inclusions \(M^\Psi\to L^q\) when \(\Psi\) has a polynomial window
between \(p\) and \(q\). Web searches on 2026-07-19 for the source title,
``Delta\^{}0 necessary'', ``Morse-Transue Delta\^{}0 weakly compact operators'',
and Lavergne/Alexopoulos related terms did not locate a later direct answer to
the source question.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Accept as a scoped partial obstruction. Do not count it as a full answer to the
source's \(\Delta^0\)-necessity question.
