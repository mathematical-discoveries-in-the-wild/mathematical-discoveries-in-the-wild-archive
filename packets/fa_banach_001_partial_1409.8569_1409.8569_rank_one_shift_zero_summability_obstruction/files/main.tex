\section*{Status}

\textbf{Status:} partial result, likely valid.  This does not solve the
problem of determining $q_B(p)$, but it proves that the rank-one
$\ell_1$ shift construction used in \cite{DHHK} cannot produce divergent
eigenvalue-gap sums at any exponent $q>1$.

\section*{Source Problem}

Demuth, Hanauska, Hansmann, and Katriel study compact perturbations
$L=L_0+K$ of bounded operators on Banach spaces.  If
$(\alpha_j(K))\in \ell^p$, their Corollary 1 implies, for $L_0\ne 0$, the
gap summability
\[
  \sum_{\lambda\in\sigma_d(L),\,|\lambda|>\|L_0\|}
       (|\lambda|-\|L_0\|)^q<\infty
  \qquad(q>p+1).
\]
They define $q_B(p)$ as the infimum of exponents for which the corresponding
implication holds for all Banach spaces, all $L_0$, and all compact $K$ with
$(\alpha_j(K))\in\ell^p$.  Their results give
$\max(1,p)\le q_B(p)\le p+1$, and the final page asks whether
$q_B(p)=p$ for $p\ge1$, whether $q_B(p)>p$, or whether the upper bound
$p+1$ is sharp.

\section*{Partial Result}

The source paper obtains the lower bound $q_B(p)\ge1$ from a rank-one
perturbation of the unilateral shift on $\ell^1(\mathbb N)$.  In that model,
a bounded scalar sequence $b=(b_k)_{k\ge1}$ defines
\[
  h(w)=1-\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} b_k w^k,\qquad |w|<1,
\]
and the eigenvalues $\lambda$ of the perturbed shift outside the unit disk
correspond to zeros $w=\lambda^{-1}$ of $h$ in the unit disk.  Formula (5.6)
in \cite{DHHK} identifies the gap sum with
\[
  \sum_{\lambda\in\sigma_d(L),\,|\lambda|>1}(|\lambda|-1)
  =
  \sum_{w\in\mathbb D,\,h(w)=0}\frac{1-|w|}{|w|}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[rank-one shift zero summability]
Let $(b_k)$ be bounded and let
$h(w)=1-\sum_{k\ge1}b_k w^k$ in $\mathbb D$.  If $(z_n)$ is the zero
sequence of $h$ in $\mathbb D$, counted with multiplicity, then
\[
  \sum_n (1-|z_n|)^q <\infty
  \qquad\text{for every } q>1.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
For every operator in the rank-one $\ell^1$ shift model of \cite[Section
5]{DHHK}, the eigenvalues outside the unit disk satisfy
\[
  \sum_{\lambda\in\sigma_d(L),\,|\lambda|>1} (|\lambda|-1)^q<\infty
  \qquad\text{for every } q>1.
\]
Consequently this model cannot prove $q_B(p)>1$, and hence cannot prove
$q_B(p)>p$ for any $p\ge1$ or sharpness of the upper bound $p+1$.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The rank-one example is equivalent to prescribing zeros of an analytic
function whose Taylor coefficients are bounded.  Such functions may have
non-Blaschke zero sets, which is exactly what the source paper uses to force
divergence of the first power.  But bounded coefficients impose the growth
condition $|h(z)|=O((1-|z|)^{-1})$.  Jensen's formula converts that mild
growth into a dyadic zero-counting estimate: near radius $1-2^{-m}$ there
can be at most order $m2^m$ zeros.  That count is just too small to make
any $q$-power with $q>1$ diverge.

\section*{Proof}

Set $M=\sup_k |b_k|$.  If $M=0$, then $h\equiv1$ and there are no zeros, so
assume $M>0$.  For $0<r<1$,
\[
  |h(re^{it})|
  \le 1+M\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}r^k
  =1+\frac{Mr}{1-r}
  \le \frac{1+M}{1-r}.
\]
Since $h(0)=1$, Jensen's formula gives, for every $0<r<1$,
\[
  \sum_{|z_n|<r}\log\frac{r}{|z_n|}
  =
  \frac1{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}\log|h(re^{it})|\,dt
  \le C+\log\frac1{1-r},
\]
where $C=\log(1+M)$.

Let $N(t)=\#\{n:|z_n|\le t\}$, counting multiplicity.  For each integer
$m\ge1$, put $t_m=1-2^{-m}$ and $r_m=1-2^{-(m+1)}$.  Then $t_m<r_m$, and
each zero with $|z_n|\le t_m$ contributes at least
\[
  \log\frac{r_m}{t_m}
  =
  \log\left(\frac{1-2^{-(m+1)}}{1-2^{-m}}\right)
  \ge 2^{-(m+1)}
\]
to Jensen's sum at radius $r_m$.  Hence
\[
  N(t_m)\,2^{-(m+1)}
  \le C+\log\frac1{1-r_m}
  = C+(m+1)\log 2.
\]
Thus $N(t_m)\le C_1 m2^m$ for a constant $C_1$ independent of $m$.

Now decompose the disk into dyadic annuli
\[
  A_m=\{z:1-2^{-m}<|z|\le 1-2^{-(m+1)}\}\qquad(m\ge1),
\]
together with the compact disk $|z|\le1/2$.  The compact disk contains only
finitely many zeros because $h$ is not identically zero.  The number of zeros
in $A_m$ is at most $N(t_{m+1})\le C_2 m2^m$, while every $z\in A_m$
satisfies $1-|z|\le2^{-m}$.  Therefore
\[
  \sum_n(1-|z_n|)^q
  \le C_3+\sum_{m=1}^{\infty} C_2 m2^m\,2^{-mq}
  = C_3+C_2\sum_{m=1}^{\infty}m\,2^{-m(q-1)}<\infty
\]
whenever $q>1$.  This proves the theorem.

For the corollary, the source model has $w=\lambda^{-1}$ and $h(w)=0$.
There are only finitely many zeros with $|w|\le1/2$.  For the remaining
zeros,
\[
  |\lambda|-1=\frac1{|w|}-1=\frac{1-|w|}{|w|}\le2(1-|w|),
\]
so the theorem implies the asserted $q$-summability of eigenvalue gaps.

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not determine $q_B(p)$ and does not rule out other Banach
spaces, other compact perturbations, or more elaborate finite-rank or
infinite-rank constructions.  It only shows that the natural attempt to
strengthen the source paper's own rank-one $\ell^1$ shift example cannot
cross the exponent $1$ threshold.
