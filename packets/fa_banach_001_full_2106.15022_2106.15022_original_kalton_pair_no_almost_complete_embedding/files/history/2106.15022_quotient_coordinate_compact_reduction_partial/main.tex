\section{Source question and setup}

Braga's Theorem 1.8 constructs operator spaces $X$ and $Y$ such that $X$
embeds isomorphically but not completely isomorphically into $Y$, while their
bounded subsets are almost completely coarsely equivalent
\cite{Braga2021}.  The paragraph following the theorem asks whether the
specific examples also satisfy that $X$ does not \emph{almost} completely
isomorphically embed into $Y$.

\begin{figure}[ht]
  \centering

  \caption{Theorem 1.8 and the source question on PDF page 4 of
  \cite{Braga2021}.}
\end{figure}

For the explicit construction, put $H=R\cap C$ and let
$Q:L_\infty[0,1]\to H$ be the complete quotient dual to the standard complete
embedding $R+C\to L_1[0,1]$.  On the vector space $L_\infty[0,1]$, define
\[
 \|y\|_{Y_j}=\max\{2^{-j}\|y\|_\infty,\|Qy\|_H\},
 \qquad
 \cZ(Q)=\Big(\bigoplus_{j\geq1}Y_j\Big)_{\ell_1}.
\]
The source pair is $Y=\cZ(Q)$ and $X=H\oplus\ker\widetilde Q$.  Thus it is
enough to obstruct an almost complete embedding $H\to\cZ(Q)$.

Let
\[
 W=\Big(\bigoplus_{j\geq1}\MIN(L_\infty[0,1])\Big)_{\ell_1},
 \qquad
 U=\Big(\bigoplus_{j\geq1}H\Big)_{\ell_1}.
\]
The canonical coordinate maps are
\[
 P_1((y_j)_j)=(2^{-j}y_j)_j\in W,
 \qquad
 P_2((y_j)_j)=(Qy_j)_j\in U.
\]
The map $(P_1,P_2)$ is a complete isomorphic embedding of $\cZ(Q)$ into
$W\oplus_\infty U$ with distortion at most $2$.  This follows directly from
the coordinate maximum defining $Y_j$ and the universal construction of the
operator-space $\ell_1$ sum.

\section{Proof intuition}

The quotient part of any linear map $H\to\cZ(Q)$ is compact.  Indeed, a
noncompact coordinate $H\to H$ is bounded below on an infinite-dimensional
Hilbert subspace.  Its lift to $L_\infty$ would then yield, using the quotient
$Q$ and an orthogonal projection in $H$, a bounded projection of $L_\infty$
onto a copy of $\ell_2$, which is impossible.

Compactness lets us make all quotient coordinates simultaneously as small as
needed on an infinite-dimensional subspace.  At matrix level $n$ we make the
scalar norm $O(1/n)$; the standard estimate $\|S_n\|\leq n\|S\|$ then makes
the $n$th amplification negligible.  Hence the minimal $L_\infty$
coordinates alone must preserve the lower matrix estimate.

\section{Compact quotient-coordinate theorem}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:coordinate}
Let $T:H\to L_\infty[0,1]$ be bounded.  Then $QT:H\to H$ is compact.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose $A=QT$ is not compact.  Since both its domain and range are Hilbert
spaces, there is an infinite-dimensional closed subspace $E\subset H$ and
$c>0$ such that $\|Ax\|\geq c\|x\|$ on $E$.  Thus $A(E)$ is closed.  Let
$P_{A(E)}$ be the orthogonal projection of $H$ onto $A(E)$.  Then
\[
 \Pi=T|_E\,(A|_E)^{-1}P_{A(E)}Q:L_\infty[0,1]\longrightarrow T(E)
\]
is bounded and satisfies $\Pi(Tx)=Tx$ for $x\in E$.  Hence $T(E)$ is a
complemented copy of $\ell_2$ in $L_\infty[0,1]$, contradicting the standard
complemented-subspace theorem for $L_\infty$ spaces used in the proof of
Proposition 4.6 of \cite{Braga2021}.  Therefore $A$ is compact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:compact}
Let $u:H\to\cZ(Q)$ be bounded and write $u_j$ for its $j$th coordinate.
Then
\[
 V_u:H\longrightarrow\ell_1(H),\qquad
 V_ux=(Qu_jx)_{j\geq1},
\]
is compact.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The definition of the $\cZ(Q)$ norm gives
\[
 \sum_j\|Qu_jx\|\leq\|ux\|_{\cZ(Q)},
\]
so $V_u$ is bounded.  By Lemma \ref{lem:coordinate}, every coordinate
$Qu_j:H\to H$ is compact.

Since $H$ is reflexive, $V_u$ is weakly compact.  The standard weak
compactness criterion in the vector-valued sum $\ell_1(H)$ says that a
relatively weakly compact set has uniformly small coordinate tails (the
coordinate sets are weakly compact because $H$ is reflexive).  Consequently,
if $p_N$ is the projection onto the first $N$ coordinates, then
$\|(I-p_N)V_u\|\to0$.  Each $p_NV_u$ is compact, being a finite tuple of
compact operators.  Hence $V_u$ is a norm limit of compact operators and is
compact.
\end{proof}

\section{Reduction of the source question}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:reduction}
If $H=R\cap C$ almost completely isomorphically embeds into $\cZ(Q)$, then
$H$ almost completely isomorphically embeds into
$W=(\bigoplus_j\MIN(L_\infty[0,1]))_{\ell_1}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume there are linear maps $u^{(n)}:H\to\cZ(Q)$ and $D\geq1$ such that
\[
 D^{-1}\|a\|_{\M_n(H)}\leq
 \|u^{(n)}_n(a)\|_{\M_n(\cZ(Q))}\leq
 D\|a\|_{\M_n(H)}
\]
for every $n$ and $a\in\M_n(H)$.

Fix $n$.  The operator $S_n=P_2u^{(n)}:H\to U$ is compact by Theorem
\ref{thm:compact}.  Approximate it in norm by a finite-rank operator and take
an infinite-dimensional subspace $F_n\subset H$ in the kernel of that
approximant.  We may arrange
\[
 \|S_n|_{F_n}\|\leq (8Dn)^{-1}.
\]
Because $H=R\cap C$ is unitarily homogeneous, there is a complete isometry
$J_n:H\to F_n$.  Set $T_n=P_1u^{(n)}J_n:H\to W$.

For any bounded linear map between operator spaces,
$\|S_n^{(n)}\|\leq n\|S_n\|$.  Hence the $n$th amplification of
$P_2u^{(n)}J_n$ has norm at most $(8D)^{-1}$.  On the other hand, the complete
distortion-$2$ coordinate embedding $(P_1,P_2)$ and the lower estimate for
$u^{(n)}_n$ force
\[
 \|(T_n)_n(a)\|\geq (2D)^{-1}\|a\|
\]
after harmlessly weakening constants.  The upper estimate is
$\|(T_n)_n(a)\|\leq D\|a\|$.  Thus $((T_n)_n)_n$ is an equi-isomorphic family,
which is precisely an almost complete isomorphic embedding of $H$ into $W$.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and limitations}

The compactness argument is independent of the later construction in
\cite{BragaOikhberg2022}.  That paper proves the desired existential
strengthening using the different quotient
$\MAX(L_1)\to\MIN(\ell_2)$; it does not establish nonembedding for the exact
$L_\infty\to R\cap C$ pair above.

Theorem \ref{thm:reduction} is only a necessary reduction.  This packet does
not prove that $R\cap C$ fails to almost completely embed into $W$.  That
uniform minimal-sum obstruction is the remaining problem.  The main point for
human verification is the complete distortion bound for $(P_1,P_2)$ under the
standard operator-space $\ell_1$-sum convention.

Local lightweight indexes, the source of arXiv:2211.11854, and bounded web
searches for the exact phrase and close variants were checked on 19 July 2026.
No explicit resolution of the exact original pair or this reduction was
found.
