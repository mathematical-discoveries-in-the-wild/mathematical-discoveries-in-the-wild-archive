\section{Source Question}

For a graph $G$ on $L$ vertices, the source writes
\[
   T_G=\frac{1}{\sqrt L}\sum_{i=1}^L s_i,
\]
where the $s_i$ are $G$-independent semicircle variables.  Section 6 asks whether
the following refined monotonicity property holds: if $G,G'$ are graphs on
$[L]$ with the same clique number and $|E(G)|\le |E(G')|$, must
$\norm{T_G}\le \norm{T_{G'}}$?

The relevant passage is on page 16 of \cite{STY}.

\section{Idea of the Proof}

The proposed monotonicity is already too strong at equal edge count.  If two
graphs have the same number of edges and the same clique number, the proposed
statement applies in both directions and therefore predicts equality of
$\norm{T_G}$.  Complete multipartite graphs give an explicit norm formula:
the norm is proportional to the sum of square roots of the part sizes.  Edge
count fixes the sum of the squares of the part sizes, but it does not fix the
sum of their square roots.  Two complete 4-partite graphs on 10 vertices exploit
exactly this gap.

\section{Complete Multipartite Norm Formula}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:multipartite}
Let $K_{I_1,\ldots,I_r}$ be the complete $r$-partite graph on
$L=\sum_{k=1}^r |I_k|$ vertices, and let $T_K$ be the normalized operator from
the source paper.  Then
\[
   \norm{T_K}=\frac{2}{\sqrt L}\sum_{k=1}^r \sqrt{|I_k|}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
In the complete multipartite graph, vertices in distinct parts are adjacent, so
the corresponding algebras commute and are classically independent.  Vertices in
the same part are nonadjacent, so within each part the corresponding semicircle
variables are free.

For each part set
\[
   X_k=\sum_{i\in I_k}s_i.
\]
The free sum of $|I_k|$ standard semicircle variables is a semicircle variable
of variance $|I_k|$, hence has spectrum
$[-2\sqrt{|I_k|},2\sqrt{|I_k|}]$.  The variables $X_1,\ldots,X_r$ live in
commuting classically independent subalgebras.  In the standard tensor-product
model, the spectrum of $\sum_k X_k$ is the Minkowski sum of the individual
spectra.  Therefore
\[
  \norm{\sum_{k=1}^r X_k}=2\sum_{k=1}^r\sqrt{|I_k|}.
\]
Dividing by $\sqrt L$ gives the formula for $T_K$.
\end{proof}

\section{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:counterexample}
The Section 6 edge-count monotonicity question in \cite{STY} has a negative
answer.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $G$ be the complete 4-partite graph on 10 vertices with part sizes
\[
   (1,1,4,4),
\]
and let $H$ be the complete 4-partite graph on 10 vertices with part sizes
\[
   (1,2,2,5).
\]
Both graphs have clique number $4$.  The number of edges in a complete
multipartite graph with part sizes $n_1,\ldots,n_r$ is
\[
   \frac12\left(L^2-\sum_{k=1}^r n_k^2\right).
\]
For both displayed partitions, $L=10$ and
\[
   1^2+1^2+4^2+4^2=1^2+2^2+2^2+5^2=34.
\]
Thus
\[
   |E(G)|=|E(H)|=\frac{100-34}{2}=33.
\]

By Lemma \ref{lem:multipartite},
\[
   \norm{T_G}
   =\frac{2}{\sqrt{10}}(1+1+2+2)
   =\frac{12}{\sqrt{10}},
\]
whereas
\[
   \norm{T_H}
   =\frac{2}{\sqrt{10}}(1+\sqrt2+\sqrt2+\sqrt5)
   =\frac{2(1+2\sqrt2+\sqrt5)}{\sqrt{10}}.
\]
These values are not equal: equality would imply
$5=2\sqrt2+\sqrt5$, which is impossible since squaring
$5-\sqrt5=2\sqrt2$ gives $30-10\sqrt5=8$.

Since $|E(G)|=|E(H)|$, the proposed monotonicity would imply both
$\norm{T_G}\le\norm{T_H}$ and $\norm{T_H}\le\norm{T_G}$, hence equality.  The
strict inequality above contradicts this.  Therefore the proposed monotonicity
property is false.
\end{proof}

\section{Novelty Check and Limitations}

The run's cheap indexes were searched for the arXiv id, title terms,
``trace monoids'', ``Turan'', ``Khintchine'', and ``monoid''.  No prior packet
for this target was found.  Local source inspection showed that the first
Turan extremal question is answered in the same paper, while the Section 6
edge-count monotonicity question is posed as unclear.  Web searches on
2026-07-03 for exact phrases from the title and question did not reveal a
separate later answer.

This counterexample refutes the proposed monotonicity as stated.  It does not
rule out monotonicity under stronger hypotheses, for example when $G$ is a
subgraph of $G'$, which is already observed in the source, or within a fixed
part-size majorization class.

normalization in Lemma \ref{lem:multipartite} matches the source definition of
$T_G=(1/\sqrt L)\sum_i s_i$.
