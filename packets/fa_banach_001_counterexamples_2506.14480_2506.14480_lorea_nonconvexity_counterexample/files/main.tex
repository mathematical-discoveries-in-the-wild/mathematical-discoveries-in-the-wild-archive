\section*{Source and question}

The source paper is Francesca La Piana and Alexander Mueller-Hermes,
\emph{Annihilating and breaking Lorentz cone entanglement}, arXiv:2506.14480
\cite{lapiana-muellerhermes}.
On page 3, after defining the Lorentzian tensor product and the class
\(\LorEA_2(\cC_A,\cC_B)\), the authors write:
\begin{quote}\small
``The set of these maps will be denoted by
\(\LorEA_2(\cC_A,\cC_B)\), and we do not know whether it is convex in general.''
\end{quote}
They then prove convexity in the special case where \(\cC_B=\cL_n\) is a
Lorentz cone.

\section*{Definitions}

All cones are finite-dimensional proper cones.  For proper cones \(\cC,\cD\),
write
\[
\maxEA_2(\cC,\cD)=\{T\in\Pos(\cC,\cD):
(T\otimes T)(\cC\otimes_{\max}\cC)\subseteq \cD\otimes_{\min}\cD\}.
\]
The Lorentzian tensor product is
\[
\cC\otimes_L\cD
=\conv\{(A\otimes B)(\widehat I_k):
A\in\Pos(\cL_k,\cC),\ B\in\Pos(\cL_k,\cD),\ k\in\N\},
\]
and
\[
\LorEA_2(\cC,\cD)=\{T\in\Pos(\cC,\cD):
(T\otimes T)(\cC\otimes_L\cC)\subseteq \cD\otimes_{\min}\cD\}.
\]
For a finite-dimensional normed space \(X\), let
\(\cC_X=\{(t,x)\in\R\times X:t\geq \|x\|\}\).  We use the standard
self-duality of \(\cL_n\) and the identification \((\cC_X)^*=\cC_{X^*}\).

\section*{Proof intuition}

The obstruction is already present in the source paper's appendix, but in the
wrong orientation for the question as stated there.  The appendix gives two
maps \(P_1,P_2:\cC_{\ell_\infty^2}\to\cL_2\) that are individually
\(2\)-max-entanglement annihilating, while \(P_1+P_2\) is not.  Taking trace
adjoints reverses the cones and preserves the max-annihilation property, so
the adjoints \(P_1^*,P_2^*:\cL_2\to\cC_{\ell_1^2}\) are again individually
max-annihilating and have a non-annihilating sum.  Since the input cone is
Lorentz, the Lorentzian and maximal tensor products agree on the input side.
Thus the same adjoints are a counterexample to convexity of
\(\LorEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2})\).

\begin{lemma}[Lorentz input reduces LorEA to maxEA]\label{lem:lorentz-input}
For every proper cone \(\cD\) and every \(n\),
\[
\cL_n\otimes_L\cD=\cL_n\otimes_{\max}\cD.
\]
Consequently
\[
\LorEA_2(\cL_n,\cD)=\maxEA_2(\cL_n,\cD).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The inclusion \(\otimes_L\subseteq\otimes_{\max}\) is part of the general
construction.  Conversely, identify \(\cL_n\) with its dual.  A tensor
\(z\in\cL_n\otimes_{\max}\cD\) corresponds, by the Choi-Jamiolkowski
identification for cones, to a positive map \(B:\cL_n\to\cD\), and
\[
z=(\id_{\cL_n}\otimes B)(\widehat I_n).
\]
This tensor is one of the generators in the definition of
\(\cL_n\otimes_L\cD\), with \(A=\id_{\cL_n}\).  Hence the two tensor products
coincide.  Applying this equality to the input tensor product
\(\cL_n\otimes_L\cL_n\) gives the stated equality of map classes.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Adjoint duality for max-annihilation]\label{lem:adjoint}
Let \(T:\operatorname{span}\cC\to\operatorname{span}\cD\) be linear.  Then
\[
T\in\maxEA_2(\cC,\cD)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
T^*\in\maxEA_2(\cD^*,\cC^*).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The equivalence of positivity of \(T\) and \(T^*\) is standard.  The remaining
condition is the dual of a cone inclusion.  Indeed,
\[
(T\otimes T)(\cC\otimes_{\max}\cC)\subseteq \cD\otimes_{\min}\cD
\]
holds if and only if
\[
(T^*\otimes T^*)((\cD\otimes_{\min}\cD)^*)\subseteq
(\cC\otimes_{\max}\cC)^*.
\]
Using
\[
(\cD\otimes_{\min}\cD)^*=\cD^*\otimes_{\max}\cD^*,
\qquad
(\cC\otimes_{\max}\cC)^*=\cC^*\otimes_{\min}\cC^*
\]
gives precisely \(T^*\in\maxEA_2(\cD^*,\cC^*)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
The set \(\LorEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2})\) is not convex.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(X=\ell_\infty^2\), so \(X^*=\ell_1^2\).  Consider the two maps
\(\phi^{(1)},\phi^{(2)}:X\to\ell_2^2\) from Appendix B of
\cite{lapiana-muellerhermes}:
\[
\phi^{(1)}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1&0\\
0&1/5
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\phi^{(2)}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1&3/10\\
0&1/2
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
For such \(\phi\), the source paper defines
\[
\alpha(\phi)=\max\left\{
\|\phi_1+\phi_2\|_2,\ \|\phi_1-\phi_2\|_2,\
\sqrt{\|\phi H\phi^T\|_1}: H\in\mathcal H
\right\},
\]
where
\[
\mathcal H=\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}-1&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix},
\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\1&1\end{pmatrix},
\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\-1&1\end{pmatrix},
\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}
\right\}.
\]
Their Appendix B criterion says that
\[
\lambda\oplus\phi\in \maxEA_2(\cC_{\ell_\infty^2},\cL_2)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\alpha(\phi)\leq\lambda.
\]
The displayed matrices satisfy
\[
\alpha(\phi^{(1)})+\alpha(\phi^{(2)})
<\alpha(\phi^{(1)}+\phi^{(2)}).
\]
For example, exact arithmetic gives
\[
\alpha(\phi^{(1)})=\frac{2^{3/4}97^{1/4}}{5}\approx 1.055590386,
\quad
\alpha(\phi^{(2)})=\frac{\sqrt{194}}{10}\approx 1.392838828,
\]
while
\[
\alpha(\phi^{(1)}+\phi^{(2)})
=\frac{\sqrt{14}\,1889^{1/4}}{10}\approx 2.466732225.
\]
Hence, with \(P_i=\alpha(\phi^{(i)})\oplus\phi^{(i)}\), the two maps
\(P_1,P_2\) belong to \(\maxEA_2(\cC_{\ell_\infty^2},\cL_2)\), but
\[
P_1+P_2=(\alpha(\phi^{(1)})+\alpha(\phi^{(2)}))
\oplus(\phi^{(1)}+\phi^{(2)})
\notin \maxEA_2(\cC_{\ell_\infty^2},\cL_2).
\]

Set \(Q_i=P_i^*:\cL_2\to(\cC_{\ell_\infty^2})^*=\cC_{\ell_1^2}\).  By
Lemma~\ref{lem:adjoint},
\[
Q_i\in\maxEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2})
\quad (i=1,2),
\]
and by Lemma~\ref{lem:lorentz-input} this is the same as
\[
Q_i\in\LorEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2}).
\]
However,
\[
Q_1+Q_2=(P_1+P_2)^*
\notin\maxEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2})
=\LorEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2}),
\]
again by Lemmas~\ref{lem:adjoint} and~\ref{lem:lorentz-input}.  Since this
class is closed under positive scalar multiplication, the midpoint
\((Q_1+Q_2)/2\) also fails to belong to it.  Therefore
\(\LorEA_2(\cL_2,\cC_{\ell_1^2})\) is not convex.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes}

The accompanying script \texttt{code/verify\_constants.py} computes the six
terms in \(\alpha\) for \(\phi^{(1)}\), \(\phi^{(2)}\), and
\(\phi^{(1)}+\phi^{(2)}\), and verifies the strict inequality.  The proof uses
the source paper's Appendix B criterion, trace-duality of minimal and maximal
tensor products, and the equality \(\cL_n\otimes_L\cD=\cL_n\otimes_{\max}\cD\)
for Lorentz input.

\section*{Novelty check and limitations}

On 2026-06-20, I searched the web for the exact phrases
\begin{quote}\small
\texttt{"LorEA\_2" convex "Lorentz-entanglement annihilating"},\\
\texttt{"Lorentz-entanglement annihilating" "convex"},
\end{quote}
and for the source title together with \texttt{convex}.  I found the source
arXiv paper but no later paper or note resolving the general convexity
question.  The argument is intentionally narrow: it disproves general
convexity, but it does not classify which output cones still give convex
Lorentz-entanglement annihilating classes.
