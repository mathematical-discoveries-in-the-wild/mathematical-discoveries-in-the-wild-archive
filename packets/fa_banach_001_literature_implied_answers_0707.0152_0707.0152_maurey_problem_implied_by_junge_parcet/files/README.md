# Literature-Implied Answer: 0707.0152

Status: `literature_implied_answer`.

Source problem: Marius Junge and Hun Hee Lee, "A Maurey type result for operator spaces", arXiv:0707.0152.  In the introduction, after defining completely \(p\)-summing maps, the paper asks whether for every \(2<q<\infty\)
\[
  CB(B(H),OH)=\Pi_q^o(B(H),OH).
\]
The paper also records the dual reformulation: with \(p=q'\in(1,2)\), the desired strengthening is equivalent to
\[
  S_p(OH)=\Pi_1^o(OH,S_p),
\]
and Remark 2.2 asks the related Schatten target inclusion
\[
  \Pi_p^o(OH,S_p)\subseteq \Pi_1^o(OH,S_p).
\]

Supporting theorem: Marius Junge and Javier Parcet, "Maurey's factorization theory for operator spaces", arXiv:0901.1928, Corollary B.  Corollary B states, in particular, that
\[
  \Pi_{s'}^o(OH,Y)=\Pi_1^o(OH,Y)
\]
for every \(2<s<\infty\) and every operator space \(Y\).  Taking \(s=q\), \(p=s'=q'\), and \(Y=S_p\), this gives the Schatten-side equality needed in the 2007 dual formulation.

Why this bucket: the 2009 paper cites the 2007 Junge-Lee paper and says its corollary vastly improves those results, but it does not appear to restate the exact 2007 problem as a named answered question.  The answer is therefore recorded as a direct literature implication, not as a new partial result and not as an original solution by this agent.
