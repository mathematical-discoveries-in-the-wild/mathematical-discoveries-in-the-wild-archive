\section*{Status}

This is a \textbf{literature-implied answer}.  No original proof is claimed in
this packet.  The role of the packet is to record that a later theorem answers
the 2007 source problem after matching the source's dual reformulation with
the later notation.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source is Junge--Lee, \emph{A Maurey type result for operator spaces},
arXiv:0707.0152 \cite{JHL}.  In the introduction, after defining completely
\(p\)-summing maps, the authors ask whether for \(2<q<\infty\)
\[
  \CB(\BH,\OH)=\PiO_q(\BH,\OH).
\]
They note that, if \(p=q'\in(1,2)\), this problem is equivalent to the
Schatten-side dual formulation
\[
  S_p(\OH)=\PiO_1(\OH,S_p).
\]
Remark 2.2 also states that the authors could not answer the related Schatten
target inclusion
\[
  \PiO_p(\OH,S_p)\subseteq \PiO_1(\OH,S_p).
\]

\section*{Supporting Theorem}

The supporting source is Junge--Parcet, arXiv:0901.1928 \cite{JP}.
Their Corollary B states, in particular, that for every \(2<s<\infty\)
and every operator space \(Y\),
\[
  \PiO_{s'}(\OH,Y)=\PiO_1(\OH,Y).
\]
Taking \(s=q\), \(p=s'=q'\), and \(Y=S_p\), this gives
\[
  \PiO_p(\OH,S_p)=\PiO_1(\OH,S_p).
\]
Together with the 2007 paper's stated dual equivalence, this supplies the
desired answer to the source problem
\[
  \CB(\BH,\OH)=\PiO_q(\BH,\OH)\qquad (2<q<\infty).
\]

\section*{Provenance And Scope}

This is not filed as \texttt{literature\_already\_answered}: the 2009 paper
cites Junge--Lee and says its corollary vastly improves those results, but the
exact 2007 question is not restated there as a named problem being resolved.
The match uses the source paper's own dual formulation and the later
Corollary B.  Hence the durable status is
\[
  \texttt{literature\_implied\_answer}.
\]
