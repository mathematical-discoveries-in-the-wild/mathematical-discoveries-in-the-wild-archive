\section*{Status and scope}

Pellegrino and Santos ask in Problem~7.2 of \cite{PS} whether the family
$(\Lcal_{ss,p})_{p\geq1}$ of strongly $p$-summing multilinear operators is a
maximal or minimal desired family.  This packet gives a complete negative
answer to the \emph{minimality} half.  The maximality half is not addressed,
so the packet is classified as a partial solution to the source problem.

The exact question is:
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Problem 7.2.} Is $(\Lcal_{ss,p})_{p\geq1}$ a maximal or minimal
desired ideal?
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions from the source}

For Banach spaces $E_1,\ldots,E_n,F$ and $p\geq1$, an $n$-linear map
$T:E_1\times\cdots\times E_n\to F$ is strongly $p$-summing if
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ss}
 \left(\sum_{i=1}^m
 \|T(x_i^{(1)},\ldots,x_i^{(n)})\|^p\right)^{1/p}
 \leq C\sup_{\varphi\in B_{\Lcal(E_1,\ldots,E_n;\K)}}
 \left(\sum_{i=1}^m
 |\varphi(x_i^{(1)},\ldots,x_i^{(n)})|^p\right)^{1/p}.
\end{equation}
The least $C$ is denoted by $\|T\|_{ss,p}$.  Strongly multiple
$p$-summability is the corresponding inequality on full Cartesian arrays;
the source proves the norm-one inclusion
$\Lcal_{ss,p}\subseteq\Lcal_{sm,p}$.

Write $\Lcal_{d,p}$ for the Banach multi-ideal of $p$-dominated maps.  In the
notation of \cite{PS}, a family $(\mathcal I_p)_{p\geq1}$ is \emph{desired}
when:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Lcal_{d,p}\subseteq\mathcal I_p\subseteq\Lcal_{sm,p}$ with both
      inclusions of norm at most one;
\item every $\mathcal I_p$ is closed for scalar multiplication (csm) and
      under differentiation (cud);
\item $\mathcal I_p\subseteq\mathcal I_q$ for $p\leq q$; and
\item the multilinear Grothendieck and Dvoretzky--Rogers theorems hold.
\end{enumerate}
A desired family is minimal if every desired family contractively contained
in it is equal to it at every $p$.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The Grothendieck requirement only forces a desired family to contain all maps
$(\ell_1)^n\to\ell_2$ at $p=1$.  We put in exactly the Banach multi-ideal
generated by these maps, add the mandatory dominated ideal, and no more.
The generated part is given an atomic norm whose central cost is the
$p$-summing norm of the linearization.  This makes completeness and all
norm-one inclusions transparent.

To see that the resulting family is smaller than the strongly summing family,
test trilinear forms on $(e_i,e_i,e_i)$ in $(\ell_2)^3$.  Every factorization
through $\ell_1$ is diagonal-null by Pitt's compactness theorem, and the same
is true of every dominated trilinear form by an exact finite-dimensional
weak-$p$ calculation.  The diagonal form
$\Delta(x,y,z)=\sum_i x_i y_i z_i$ is not diagonal-null, while scalar-valued
forms are automatically strongly $p$-summing.

\section*{The atomic Grothendieck hull}

We use $\pi_p$ for the absolutely $p$-summing norm of a linear operator.  For
$A\in\Lcal({}^n\ell_1;\ell_2)$, let
\[
 \widetilde A:\underbrace{\ell_1\wh\cdots\wh\ell_1}_{n\text{ factors}}
 \longrightarrow\ell_2
\]
be its linearization.  The domain is canonically isometric to
$\ell_1(\N^n)$.  Grothendieck's $\ell_1$--$\ell_2$ theorem therefore gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:groth-kernel}
 \pi_1(\widetilde A)<\infty,
 \qquad
 \pi_q(\widetilde A)\leq\pi_p(\widetilde A)
 \quad(1\leq p\leq q<\infty).
\end{equation}

Fix $p,n,E_1,\ldots,E_n,F$.  A \emph{$p$-atom} is a map
\begin{equation}\label{eq:atom}
 S=u\circ A\circ(B_1,\ldots,B_n),
\end{equation}
where $B_j:E_j\to\ell_1$, $A:{}^n\ell_1\to\ell_2$, and
$u:\ell_2\to F$ are bounded, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cost}
 \|u\|\,\pi_p(\widetilde A)\prod_{j=1}^n\|B_j\|\leq1.
\end{equation}
Let $\mathcal A_p(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F)$ be the set of all such atoms.  Define the
synthesis operator
\[
 Q_p:\ell_1(\mathcal A_p(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F))
       \longrightarrow \Lcal_{ss,p}(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F),
 \qquad Q_p(c)=\sum_{S}c_S S.
\]

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:atomic}
The synthesis map is well-defined and contractive.  Its range
\[
 \Gcal_p(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F):=Q_p(\ell_1(\mathcal A_p))
\]
is a Banach space for the quotient norm
\[
 \gamma_p(T)=\inf\{\|c\|_1:Q_p(c)=T\},
\]
and $\|T\|_{ss,p}\leq\gamma_p(T)$.  As the spaces vary,
$(\Gcal_p,\gamma_p)$ is a Banach multi-ideal.  Moreover, $\Gcal_p$ is csm and
cud, and $\Gcal_p\subseteq\Gcal_q$ contractively whenever $p\leq q$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For one factorization \eqref{eq:atom}, put
$y_i=B_1x_i^{(1)}\otimes\cdots\otimes B_nx_i^{(n)}$.  Absolute
$p$-summability of $\widetilde A$ gives
\begin{align*}
 &\left(\sum_i\|uA(B_1x_i^{(1)},\ldots,B_nx_i^{(n)})\|^p\right)^{1/p}\\
 &\quad\leq
 \|u\|\pi_p(\widetilde A)
 \sup_{\psi\in B_{(\ell_1\wh\cdots\wh\ell_1)^*}}
 \left(\sum_i|\psi(y_i)|^p\right)^{1/p}\\
 &\quad\leq
 \|u\|\pi_p(\widetilde A)\prod_j\|B_j\|
 \sup_{\varphi\in B_{\Lcal(E_1,\ldots,E_n;\K)}}
 \left(\sum_i|\varphi(x_i^{(1)},\ldots,x_i^{(n)})|^p\right)^{1/p}.
\end{align*}
Thus every $p$-atom has strongly summing norm at most one.  Since
$\Lcal_{ss,p}$ is Banach, $Q_p$ is well-defined and contractive.  Its kernel
is closed, so
$\ell_1(\mathcal A_p)/\ker Q_p$, identified with the range and equipped with
the quotient norm, is Banach.  This also proves
$\|T\|_{ss,p}\leq\gamma_p(T)$.

Pre- and postcomposition in \eqref{eq:atom} gives the multi-ideal property and
its norm inequality.  Finite-type maps belong to the range: for a scalar
monomial use $B_jx=\varphi_j(x)e_1$, the central coordinate-product map
$(z_1,\ldots,z_n)\mapsto z_1(1)\cdots z_n(1)e_1$, and a rank-one
postcomposition.  For the canonical monomial on $\K^n$ this representation
has cost one, while its strongly summing norm is one; hence its $\gamma_p$
norm is exactly one.

For csm, given $\phi\in E_{n+1}^*$, append
$B_{n+1}x=\phi(x)e_1$ and replace $A$ by
\[
 A'(z_1,\ldots,z_n,w)=e_1^*(w)A(z_1,\ldots,z_n).
\]
The linearization of $A'$ is $\widetilde A$ after a norm-one tensor
contraction, so $\pi_p(\widetilde A')\leq\pi_p(\widetilde A)$.  Applying this
termwise to an atomic $\ell_1$ expansion proves csm.

For cud, fix all variables except the $j$th.  In each atom, partially evaluate
$A$ at $B_k a_k$, $k\ne j$.  The resulting central linear map is
$\widetilde A$ after precomposition by
\[
 z\longmapsto
 B_1a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes z\otimes\cdots\otimes B_na_n,
\]
whose norm is at most $\prod_{k\ne j}\|B_ka_k\|$.  The ideal property of
$\pi_p$ again preserves summability of the atomic expansion.

Finally, \eqref{eq:groth-kernel} says that every $p$-atom is a $q$-atom when
$p\leq q$.  Hence $\gamma_q(T)\leq\gamma_p(T)$ and the inclusion is
contractive.
\end{proof}

\section*{A proper desired subfamily}

For each $p\geq1$, define the componentwise sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Mdef}
 \Mcal_p=\Lcal_{d,p}+\Gcal_p,
 \qquad
 \|T\|_{\Mcal_p}
 =\inf_{T=D+G}\bigl(\|D\|_{d,p}+\gamma_p(G)\bigr).
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:desired}
The family $(\Mcal_p)_{p\geq1}$ is desired.  For every $p$,
\[
 \Lcal_{d,p}\xhookrightarrow{\ \|\cdot\|\leq1\ }\Mcal_p
 \xhookrightarrow{\ \|\cdot\|\leq1\ }\Lcal_{ss,p}
 \xhookrightarrow{\ \|\cdot\|\leq1\ }\Lcal_{sm,p}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The source records the norm-one inclusion
$\Lcal_{d,p}\subseteq\Lcal_{ss,p}$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic} gives the same
estimate for $\Gcal_p$.  Therefore the addition map
\[
 \Lcal_{d,p}(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F)\oplus_1
 \Gcal_p(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F)\longrightarrow\Lcal_{ss,p}(E_1,\ldots,E_n;F)
\]
is contractive.  Its kernel is closed; consequently its range, equipped with
the quotient norm \eqref{eq:Mdef}, is Banach.  It is a Banach multi-ideal, and
the first two displayed inclusions are contractive.  The last is the standard
norm-one inclusion from \cite{PS}.  The normalization of the multi-ideal norm
follows from the cost-one representation of the canonical scalar monomial and
the lower bound supplied by its strongly summing norm.

The $p$-dominated family is csm and cud and satisfies the inclusion theorem,
as recalled in \cite{PS}; Lemma~\ref{lem:atomic} gives the same properties for
$\Gcal_p$.  They pass to the quotient sum, and in fact
$\|T\|_{\Mcal_q}\leq\|T\|_{\Mcal_p}$ for $p\leq q$.

It remains to check the coincidence axiom.  If
$A:{}^n\ell_1\to\ell_2$ is arbitrary, then \eqref{eq:groth-kernel} makes
$A$ one of the admissible central generators of $\Gcal_1$ (after a scalar
normalization).  Hence every such $A$ belongs to $\Mcal_1$: the required
multilinear Grothendieck theorem holds.  The source observes that any family
sandwiched between $\Lcal_{d,p}$ and $\Lcal_{sm,p}$ has the required
Dvoretzky--Rogers theorem.  Thus all desired-family axioms hold.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:diagonal-null}
For every $p\geq1$ and every
$T\in\Mcal_p(\ell_2,\ell_2,\ell_2;\K)$,
\[
 T(e_i,e_i,e_i)\longrightarrow0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First let $G\in\Gcal_p(\ell_2,\ell_2,\ell_2;\K)$.  In every atom, each
$B_j:\ell_2\to\ell_1$ is compact by Pitt's theorem \cite{Pitt}.  Since
$e_i\to0$ weakly in $\ell_2$, one has $\|B_je_i\|_1\to0$.  Bounded
trilinearity of the central kernel shows that every atom is diagonal-null.
The property passes to an atomic $\ell_1$ sum: a finite initial sum is
diagonal-null, while the tail is uniformly small in operator norm because
each atom has operator norm at most one.  Thus
$G(e_i,e_i,e_i)\to0$.

Now let $D\in\Lcal_{d,p}({}^3\ell_2;\K)$.  Suppose that
$|D(e_i,e_i,e_i)|\geq\eps$ on infinitely many indices.  Choose $m$ of them.
The $p$-domination inequality yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dom-growth}
 \eps m^{3/p}
 \leq \|D\|_{d,p}
 \left(\sup_{\|a\|_2\leq1}
       \left(\sum_{i=1}^m|a_i|^p\right)^{1/p}\right)^3.
\end{equation}
The supremum in parentheses is exactly
\[
 \begin{cases}
 m^{1/p-1/2},&1\leq p<2,\\
 1,&p\geq2.
 \end{cases}
\]
For $p<2$, \eqref{eq:dom-growth} would say
$\eps m^{3/p}\leq\|D\|_{d,p}m^{3/p-3/2}$; for $p\geq2$ its right side is
constant.  Either is impossible as $m\to\infty$.  Therefore $D$ is
diagonal-null.  The conclusion follows for every $T=D+G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
The desired family $(\Lcal_{ss,p})_{p\geq1}$ is not minimal.
More precisely, the desired family $(\Mcal_p)_{p\geq1}$ of
\eqref{eq:Mdef} is contractively contained in it and
\[
 \Mcal_p\ne\Lcal_{ss,p}\qquad\text{for every }p\geq1.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Define
\[
 \Delta:\ell_2\times\ell_2\times\ell_2\longrightarrow\K,
 \qquad
 \Delta(x,y,z)=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}x_i y_i z_i.
\]
By Cauchy--Schwarz and $\|z\|_\infty\leq\|z\|_2$,
$\Delta$ is bounded and $\|\Delta\|=1$.  Every scalar-valued multilinear form
$\Phi$ is strongly $p$-summing with
$\|\Phi\|_{ss,p}=\|\Phi\|$: in \eqref{eq:ss}, the defining supremum contains
$\Phi/\|\Phi\|$, while the one-point case gives the reverse norm inequality.
Consequently $\Delta\in\Lcal_{ss,p}({}^3\ell_2;\K)$ for every $p\geq1$.

But $\Delta(e_i,e_i,e_i)=1$ for every $i$, whereas
Lemma~\ref{lem:diagonal-null} says that every member of
$\Mcal_p({}^3\ell_2;\K)$ is diagonal-null.  Thus
$\Delta\notin\Mcal_p$ for every $p$.  Proposition~\ref{prop:desired} supplies
a proper, contractively contained desired family, which is exactly the
negation of minimality in the definition of \cite{PS}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification, limitations, and novelty status}

The argument is analytic; no finite computation is being used as proof.  The
completeness point is explicit: $\Gcal_p$ is an $\ell_1$ quotient and
$\Mcal_p$ is a Banach direct-sum quotient.  The diagonal-null property is
proved after completion, not only for finite atomic sums.  The norm-one
inclusion $\Mcal_p\subseteq\Lcal_{ss,p}$ is also explicit, so the construction
meets the order convention in the source definition of minimality.

Before promotion, the run's cheap indexes were searched for arXiv:1009.4807
and the core terminology.  A bounded literature search on 22 July 2026 used
the exact title, Problem~7.2, ``strongly $p$-summing'' with ``minimal'',
``desired family'', and notation variants.  Later papers on strongly multiple
inclusions, factorable strongly summing maps, and tensor-norm summability were
also checked at the title/abstract or full-text level when locally available.
No explicit answer to this minimality question and no matching atomic-hull
construction was found.  This supports novelty only within the stated search
bounds; it is not a claim of publication priority.

The theorem does not decide whether $(\Lcal_{ss,p})_{p\geq1}$ is maximal, and
it does not answer Problems~7.1, 7.3, or 7.4 of \cite{PS}.

\section*{Human-review recommendation}

The candidate is recommended for expert review as a likely valid partial
solution.  The highest-value checks are the atomic quotient's csm/cud
stability, the normalization and contractive inclusions, and the use of the
dominated norm in \eqref{eq:dom-growth}.  Pitt's theorem and the weak-$p$ norm
calculation then make the separating example short and rigid.
