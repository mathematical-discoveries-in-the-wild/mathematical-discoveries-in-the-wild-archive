\section*{Target}

We focus on the stronger unit-ball version suggested by the journal
screenshot:
\begin{quote}
For an infinite-dimensional Banach space \(X\), is there a uniformly
Lipschitz mapping \(T:B_X\to B_X\) satisfying
\(\mathrm d(T,B_X)=0\) and \(\Fix(T)=\varnothing\)?
\end{quote}

The result below answers this question for complex Banach spaces with an
unconditional basis, and for real Banach spaces that admit a uniformly
power-bounded two-dimensional block rotation structure.  This is a partial
result only; it does not settle the arbitrary Banach-space question.

\section*{A Useful Sphere Criterion}

\begin{lemma}
Let \(X\) be a Banach space.  Suppose there is a bounded linear isomorphism
\(A:X\to X\) such that
\[
M:=\sup_{n\in\mathbb Z}\|A^n\|<\infty,
\]
\(A\) has no eigenvector with positive real eigenvalue, and there are
vectors \(z_k\in S_X\) such that \(\|Az_k-z_k\|\to0\).  Then there is a
mapping \(T:B_X\to B_X\) such that
\[
\Fix(T)=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm d(T,B_X)=0,\qquad
\sup_{n\ge1}\Lip(T^n)<\infty .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Define \(U:S_X\to S_X\) by
\[
U(x)=\frac{Ax}{\|Ax\|}.
\]
Since \(A\) is bounded below by \(M^{-1}\), the normalization estimate gives,
for \(x,y\in S_X\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
\|U(x)-U(y)\|
&=\left\|\frac{Ax}{\|Ax\|}-\frac{Ay}{\|Ay\|}\right\|\\
&\le M\|A(x-y)\|+M^2\|Ay\|\,\bigl|\|Ax\|-\|Ay\|\bigr|\\
&\le (M\|A\|+M^2\|A\|^2)\|x-y\|.
\end{aligned}
\]
The same estimate applied to \(A^n\) shows
\[
\sup_{n\ge1}\Lip(U^n)<\infty,
\]
because
\[
U^n(x)=\frac{A^n x}{\|A^n x\|}.
\]

If \(U(x)=x\), then \(Ax=\|Ax\|x\), so \(x\) is an eigenvector of \(A\)
with positive real eigenvalue.  This is impossible by hypothesis.  Also,
\[
\|U(z_k)-z_k\|
\le M\|Az_k-z_k\|\to0,
\]
so \(U\) has null minimal displacement on the sphere.

By Benyamini--Sternfeld's theorem, the unit sphere of every
infinite-dimensional Banach space is a Lipschitz retract of its unit ball.
Let \(R:B_X\to S_X\) be such a retraction and set
\[
T=U\circ R.
\]
Then \(T(B_X)\subset S_X\), \(R|_{S_X}=I\), and
\[
T^n=U^n\circ R\qquad(n\ge1).
\]
Hence \(T\) is uniformly Lipschitz.  If \(T(x)=x\), then \(x\in S_X\) and
\[
x=T(x)=U(Rx)=U(x),
\]
contradicting \(\Fix(U)=\varnothing\).  Finally the same vectors \(z_k\in
S_X\) witness \(\mathrm d(T,B_X)=0\).
\end{proof}

\section*{The Complex Unconditional-Basis Construction}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be an infinite-dimensional complex Banach space with an
unconditional basis.  Then there is a map \(T:B_X\to B_X\) satisfying
\[
\Fix(T)=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm d(T,B_X)=0,\qquad
\sup_{n\ge1}\Lip(T^n)<\infty .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \((e_j)_{j=1}^\infty\) be a normalized unconditional basis of \(X\).
For an unconditional basis, unimodular coordinate multipliers are uniformly
bounded.  Choose angles \(0<\theta_j<\pi\) with \(\theta_j\to0\), and define
\[
A e_j=e^{i\theta_j}e_j .
\]
Then \(A\) is a bounded isomorphism and, because every power \(A^n\) is again
a unimodular coordinate multiplier,
\[
\sup_{n\in\mathbb Z}\|A^n\|<\infty .
\]

The operator \(A\) has no positive real eigenvector.  Indeed, if
\[
Ax=\lambda x,\qquad \lambda>0,
\]
and \(x=\sum_j a_j e_j\), then
\[
e^{i\theta_j}a_j=\lambda a_j
\]
for every \(j\).  Since no \(e^{i\theta_j}\) is a positive real number, all
\(a_j\) vanish, contradiction.

Finally, with \(z_j=e_j\),
\[
\|Az_j-z_j\|=|e^{i\theta_j}-1|\to0.
\]
The preceding lemma now gives the required map on \(B_X\).
\end{proof}

\section*{The Real Two-Dimensional Block Version}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be a real infinite-dimensional Banach space admitting a Schauder
decomposition
\[
X=\overline{\bigoplus_{k=1}^{\infty}E_k},\qquad \dim E_k=2,
\]
with the following uniform rotation property: after choosing bases in the
blocks, every block diagonal operator whose \(k\)-th block is a Euclidean
rotation on \(E_k\) is bounded by a common constant, uniformly over all
choices of rotation angles.  Then there is a map \(T:B_X\to B_X\) satisfying
\[
\Fix(T)=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm d(T,B_X)=0,\qquad
\sup_{n\ge1}\Lip(T^n)<\infty .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(0<\theta_k<\pi\) with \(\theta_k\to0\), and let \(A\) be the block
diagonal operator whose restriction to \(E_k\) is rotation by \(\theta_k\).
The same uniform rotation property applied to the angles \(n\theta_k\) gives
\[
\sup_{n\in\mathbb Z}\|A^n\|<\infty .
\]
No block rotation through an angle in \((0,\pi)\) has a positive real
eigenvalue, so \(A\) has no positive real eigenvector.  Choosing any
\(z_k\in S_{E_k}\), the uniform equivalence of the two-dimensional block
norms gives
\[
\|Az_k-z_k\|\le C\theta_k\to0.
\]
The sphere criterion applies again.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The real theorem applies to the standard sequence spaces such as
\(\ell_p\), \(1\le p<\infty\), and \(c_0\), after pairing the usual unit
vectors.  It also applies to any real space whose basis or finite-dimensional
decomposition supports these uniformly bounded pairwise rotations.  A plain
real unconditional basis is not by itself recorded here as sufficient,
because unconditionality controls scalar sign and modulus multipliers, while
arbitrary pairwise rotations require a stronger block-multiplier estimate.
\end{remark}

\section*{Complemented Subspace Extension}

The same argument applies whenever \(X\) contains a complemented
infinite-dimensional subspace \(Y\) satisfying one of the two structural
hypotheses above.  Construct the sphere map \(U:S_Y\to S_Y\).  If
\(P:X\to Y\) is a bounded projection, choose a Lipschitz retraction
\(\rho:\|P\|B_Y\to S_Y\) that is the identity on \(S_Y\): use the
Benyamini--Sternfeld retraction inside \(B_Y\), and radial projection on the
annulus \(1\le\|y\|\le\|P\|\).  Then
\[
T=U\circ \rho\circ P:B_X\to B_X
\]
lands in \(S_Y\), has no fixed point, has null minimal displacement, and has
uniformly bounded Lipschitz iterates because
\[
T^n=U^n\circ \rho\circ P,\qquad n\ge1.
\]

\section*{Why This Is Still Partial}

The proof uses either complex unconditional multiplier control or real
two-dimensional block rotation control in an essential way.  If one only has
a conditional basic sequence, or a real unconditional basis without the
needed pairwise rotation estimates, then block rotations with angles
\(\theta_k\to0\) lead back to the multiplier obstruction: although every
individual block rotation is harmless, the block diagonal powers need not be
uniformly bounded on the whole space.

Thus the packet gives a genuine strengthening of the earlier Hilbert special
case, but it does not solve the arbitrary Banach-space problem.  Spaces with
no compatible unconditional or rotational block structure remain outside the
proof.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review this as a partial answer to the journal-style unit-ball Q2.  The main
checks are:
\begin{itemize}
\item complex unimodular multipliers, or real block rotations, are uniformly
power-bounded under the stated structural hypothesis;
\item \(A\) has no positive real eigenvector;
\item the normalization \(x\mapsto Ax/\|Ax\|\) preserves uniform power
Lipschitz bounds;
\item the Benyamini--Sternfeld sphere retraction transfers the sphere
dynamics to the whole unit ball.
\end{itemize}
