# A power-compact weighted-shift subcase of the radical question

Status: `candidate_partial_likely_valid`

Source: Marko Kandić and Klemen Šivic, *On the positive commutator in the
radical*, arXiv:1405.4988, open question (b) on page 17.

## Result

Let `E` be `c0(N_0)` or `ell^p(N_0)`, `1 <= p < infinity`, and let

`A e_j = alpha_j e_(j+1)`, `alpha_j > 0`,

be a positive unilateral forward weighted shift. Suppose `A^m` is compact.
If `B` is any positive operator satisfying `AB >= BA`, put `C = AB-BA` and
let `Alg(A,B)` denote the norm-closed Banach algebra generated by `A` and
`B`. The packet proves

`C^(3m) in Rad(Alg(A,B))`.

Thus the source paper's one-power-compact conjecture has an affirmative
answer when the power-compact member is a strictly positive forward weighted
shift on a standard one-sided sequence lattice. This is a genuine partial
result, not a proof of the conjecture for arbitrary Banach lattices.

## Proof mechanism

Writing `B=(b_ij)`, positivity gives `b_ij >= 0`. The coefficient inequality
from `AB >= BA` is

`alpha_(i-1) b_(i-1,j) >= alpha_j b_(i,j+1)`.

The boundary row first forces `b_(0,k)=0` for `k>0`; induction then forces
`b_(i,k)=0` for every `k>i`. Hence `A` and `B` preserve the same maximal tail
nest, and the commutator has zero diagonal on that nest.

Also `0 <= C^m <= (AB)^m <= A^m B^m`. The last operator is compact, so the
Aliprantis-Burkinshaw domination theorem makes `C^(3m)` compact. For every
`D` in the unitization of `Alg(A,B)`, the compact operator `D C^(3m)` is
triangular on the tail nest with zero diagonal. Ringrose's compact-operator
theorem makes it quasinilpotent, which is exactly the radical criterion.

## Novelty check and limitation

A bounded arXiv/web search on 2026-07-21 used the phrases `weighted shift
positive commutator Banach lattice`, `weighted shifts semi-commuting positive
operators`, `AB >= BA weighted shift positive operator`, and `super
left-commutant weighted shift`. The search recovered the source literature's
weighted-shift counterexamples when neither factor is power compact, but no
statement of the tail-nest lemma or the radical conclusion above. Novelty
confidence is moderate rather than definitive.

The full conjecture remains open in this packet. The decisive step here is
the boundary recursion forcing a common atomic nest; no replacement was
found for a general positive power-compact operator.

## Human review recommendation

Check the two coefficient identities in the triangularity lemma, then verify
that the tail nest is maximal and that the cited domination theorem has the
standard exponent three. If those pass, the Ringrose/radical conclusion is
immediate.
