\section*{Status and source}

This packet records a candidate partial result, likely valid, for Question 1
of Gonzalez and Martinon, \emph{A quantitative approach to disjointly
non-singular operators}, arXiv:2108.01052 \cite{GM2021}.  The question appears
in Section 7.1 on PDF page 11.

\begin{center}
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

The notation in the published PDF is transcribed as follows:
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Question 1.} Is $\kappa_d\leq D\cdot\beta$ for some constant
$D>0$?
\end{quote}

The general question remains open in this packet.  We prove it for Banach
lattices with a uniform lower $1$-estimate, including every abstract
$L_1$-space (AL-space), with the natural constant.

\section*{Definitions}

Let $E$ be a Banach lattice admitting an infinite disjoint sequence, let $Y$ be a Banach space, and
$T\in\Lin(E,Y)$.  Write $\dd(E)$ for the set of disjoint sequences of
nonzero vectors in $E$, and let $j(S)=\inf_{\|x\|=1}\|Sx\|$ be the
injection modulus.  The two quantities used below are
\[
  \tau_d(T)=\sup_{(x_n)\in\dd(E)}j\bigl(T|_{[x_n]}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \kappa_d(T)=\inf_{(x_n)\in\dd(E)}
     \tau_d\bigl(T|_{[x_n]}\bigr),
\]
and
\[
  \beta(T)=\inf\left\{
    \liminf_{n\to\infty}\|Tx_n\|:
    (x_n)\subset S_E\text{ is disjoint}
  \right\}.
\]

We say that $E$ has a lower $1$-estimate with constant $C\geq1$ if
every finite disjoint positive family $z_1,\ldots,z_m$ satisfies
\[
  \sum_{i=1}^m\|z_i\|\leq C\left\|\sum_{i=1}^m z_i\right\|.
\]
In an AL-space the norm is additive on disjoint positive vectors, so one may
take $C=1$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The definition of $\beta(T)$ supplies a normalized disjoint sequence whose
individual image norms are all as close to $\beta(T)$ as desired after
passing to a subsequence.  A pointwise bound on basis vectors normally does
not control an operator on their whole span.  The lower $1$-estimate is the
missing mechanism: it makes every normalized disjoint sequence dominate the
unit vector basis of $\ell_1$.  The triangle inequality then upgrades the
pointwise image bound to an operator-norm bound on the entire disjoint span.
Finally, $\kappa_d$ is no larger than the $\tau_d$-quantity of this one
span, and that quantity is no larger than the restriction norm.

\section*{The partial result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:lower-one}
Suppose that $E$ admits an infinite disjoint sequence and has a lower
$1$-estimate with constant $C\geq1$.
Then for every Banach space $Y$ and every $T\in\Lin(E,Y)$,
\[
  \kappa_d(T)\leq C\,\beta(T).
\]
\par\noindent
No order-continuity hypothesis is needed for this estimate.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix $\varepsilon>0$.  By the definition of $\beta(T)$, there is a
normalized disjoint sequence $(u_n)$ such that
\[
  \liminf_{n\to\infty}\|Tu_n\|<\beta(T)+\varepsilon.
\]
Passing to a subsequence and relabeling, we may assume that
\[
  \|Tu_n\|\leq\beta(T)+2\varepsilon
  \qquad(n\in\mathbb N).
\]

For finitely supported scalars $(a_n)$, disjointness gives the lattice
identity
\[
  \left|\sum_n a_nu_n\right|=\sum_n|a_n|\,|u_n|.
\]
Since the $u_n$ are normalized, the lower $1$-estimate therefore yields
\[
  \sum_n|a_n|
  \leq C\left\|\sum_n a_nu_n\right\|.
\]
Consequently,
\begin{align*}
  \left\|T\left(\sum_n a_nu_n\right)\right\|
  &\leq\sum_n|a_n|\,\|Tu_n\|\\
  &\leq C\bigl(\beta(T)+2\varepsilon\bigr)
       \left\|\sum_n a_nu_n\right\|.
\end{align*}
By density, this proves
\[
  \left\|T|_{[u_n]}\right\|
  \leq C\bigl(\beta(T)+2\varepsilon\bigr).
\]
\newpage
Every injection modulus occurring in
$\tau_d(T|_{[u_n]})$ is bounded above by this restriction norm.  Hence
\[
  \kappa_d(T)
  \leq\tau_d\bigl(T|_{[u_n]}\bigr)
  \leq\left\|T|_{[u_n]}\right\|
  \leq C\bigl(\beta(T)+2\varepsilon\bigr).
\]
Letting $\varepsilon\downarrow0$ proves the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:al}
If $E$ is an AL-space, then every $T\in\Lin(E,Y)$ satisfies
\[
  \kappa_d(T)\leq\beta(T).
\]
Thus Question 1 has an affirmative answer with $D=1$ on every abstract
$L_1$-space.  The constant is optimal whenever $E$ contains an infinite
normalized disjoint sequence.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
An AL-space has the lower $1$-estimate with $C=1$, so the inequality is
Theorem \ref{thm:lower-one}.  For sharpness, take $T=I_E$.  Then every
normalized disjoint sequence satisfies $\|Ix_n\|=1$, so $\beta(I_E)=1$.
Also every restriction of the identity has injection modulus $1$, whence
$\kappa_d(I_E)=1$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes}

The proof is purely analytic and uses no computation.  The potentially
delicate step is the scalar-coefficient inequality.  It follows directly from
the lattice modulus identity for disjoint vectors, so it is valid over both
the real and complex scalars.  The argument does not assume that $T$ is
DN-S and remains valid when $\beta(T)=0$.

An adversarial step check is included as
\texttt{verification\_report.md}.  Its verdict is \emph{likely valid}; no
missing case or external theorem dependency was found.

\section*{Limitations and novelty check}

This is a partial result only.  It supplies no universal constant for order
continuous lattices without a lower $1$-estimate, and it does not settle the
source question for $L_p$ when $p>1$.

Before promotion, the run's registry, solution, attempt, and proof-gap indexes
were searched for arXiv:2108.01052, the exact $\kappa_d$--$\beta$
comparison, and lower $1$-estimate/AL-space variants.  Related packets on
dispersed quotient maps and LSS/DSS operators were inspected, but no duplicate
of this theorem was found.  A bounded web search for the exact question, the
paper title, and the notation found the source paper and adjacent later DN-S
papers, but no explicit later answer to this subcase.  This is not a
literature-complete novelty claim.
