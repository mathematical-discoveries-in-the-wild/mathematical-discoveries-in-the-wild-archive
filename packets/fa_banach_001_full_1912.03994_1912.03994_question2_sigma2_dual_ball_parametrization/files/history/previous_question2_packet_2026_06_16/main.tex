\section*{Status}

\textbf{candidate full solution to Question 2 of arXiv:1912.03994.}

Cuth--Dolezal--Doucha--Kurka ask whether, for every isometrically universal
separable Banach space \(X\) and every admissible topology \(\tau\) on
\(SB(X)\), there is a \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_2\)-measurable map
\[
\Phi:\PP_\infty\to (SB(X),\tau)
\]
such that \(X_\mu\) is linearly isometric to \(\Phi(\mu)\) for every
\(\mu\in\PP_\infty\). This packet proves that the answer is yes.

The argument does \emph{not} answer Question 1, which asks for a continuous
map \(\PP_\infty\to\BB\). Instead it proves the sufficient condition for
Question 2 highlighted in the source paper, using the continuous dual-ball
coding from the later paper arXiv:2204.06834.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The source paper proves that Question 2 would follow if every pseudonorm code
could be realized by continuous vectors \(\chi_k(\mu)\) in one separable
Banach space:
\[
\left\|\sum_{k=1}^n a_k\chi_k(\mu)\right\|
  =\mu\left(\sum_{k=1}^n a_ke_k\right).
\]
The authors prove this for actual norms after normalizing all coordinates, but
state that they do not know it for pseudonorms.

The missing ingredient is to work on the dual side. The sequel
arXiv:2204.06834 constructs, continuously in \(\mu\), the weak-star compact
dual unit ball \(\Omega(\mu)\) as a compact convex subset of the Hilbert cube.
For compact convex subsets of a Hilbert space, projecting a fixed dense
sequence onto the moving compact convex set gives continuous dense selections.
Evaluating the corresponding dense sequence of dual functionals on the
coordinates produces the desired vectors \(\chi_k(\mu)\) in a separable
subspace of \(\ell_\infty\).

\section*{A projection parametrization lemma}

Let \(Q=\prod_{m=1}^\infty[-1,1]\), and embed \(Q\) linearly and
homeomorphically into the Hilbert space \(\ell_2\) by
\[
J((a_m)_{m=1}^\infty)=(2^{-m}a_m)_{m=1}^\infty.
\]
Write \(Q_H=J(Q)\).

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:projection-selectors}
There are continuous maps
\[
p_r:\K_c(Q_H)\to Q_H,\qquad r\in\Nat,
\]
where \(\K_c(Q_H)\) denotes the hyperspace of nonempty compact convex subsets
of \(Q_H\), such that \(\{p_r(F):r\in\Nat\}\) is dense in \(F\) for every
\(F\in\K_c(Q_H)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix a countable dense set \((d_r)\) in \(Q_H\). For \(F\in\K_c(Q_H)\), let
\(p_r(F)\) be the Hilbert-space metric projection of \(d_r\) onto \(F\). This
projection exists and is unique because \(F\) is nonempty, compact, and convex.

We first check continuity. Suppose \(F_n\to F\) in the Hausdorff metric and
\(z_n\to z\) in \(Q_H\). Put \(y_n=P_{F_n}(z_n)\). Passing to a subsequence if
necessary, compactness gives \(y_n\to y\in F\). For every \(x\in F\), choose
\(x_n\in F_n\) with \(x_n\to x\). Since \(y_n\) is the metric projection,
\[
\|z_n-y_n\|\leq \|z_n-x_n\|.
\]
Passing to the limit gives \(\|z-y\|\leq \|z-x\|\) for every \(x\in F\).
Thus \(y=P_F(z)\), by uniqueness of the projection. Hence
\((F,z)\mapsto P_F(z)\) is continuous on \(\K_c(Q_H)\times Q_H\). In
particular, each \(p_r\) is continuous.

Finally let \(x\in F\) and \(\varepsilon>0\). Pick \(r\) with
\(\|d_r-x\|<\varepsilon\). Since \(P_F(d_r)\) is at least as close to \(d_r\)
as \(x\) is,
\[
\|p_r(F)-d_r\|\leq \|x-d_r\|<\varepsilon.
\]
Therefore \(\|p_r(F)-x\|<2\varepsilon\), proving density.
\end{proof}

\section*{Continuous realization of all pseudonorms}

Let \(V\) be the countable rational vector space with basis
\((e_k)_{k\in\Nat}\). Fix an enumeration \((v_m)_{m\in\Nat}\) of \(V\) such
that every \(e_k\) occurs, and let \(m(k)\) be an index with \(v_{m(k)}=e_k\).
Let \(g:[0,\infty)\to[1,\infty)\) be \(g(t)=1\) for \(t\leq 1\) and \(g(t)=t\)
for \(t>1\).

We use the following result from arXiv:2204.06834, the lemma labelled
\(\Omega\) in the subsection ``Dual unit balls and the Szlenk derivative.'' For
\(\mu\in\PP\), define \(x_m^\mu=v_m/g(\mu(v_m))\in B_{X_\mu}\). The set
\(\Omega(\mu)\subseteq Q\) of all sequences
\[
\bigl(x^*(x_m^\mu)\bigr)_{m=1}^\infty,\qquad x^*\in B_{X_\mu^*},
\]
is a weak-star compact convex subset of \(Q\), and the map
\[
\Omega:\PP\to \K(Q)
\]
is continuous. Moreover \(\Omega(\mu)\) is exactly the dual unit ball of
\(X_\mu\) written in the coordinates \(x_m^\mu\).

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:continuous-realization}
There are a separable Banach space \(U\) and continuous maps
\[
\chi_k:\PP\to U,\qquad k\in\Nat,
\]
such that for every \(\mu\in\PP\) and every finite scalar family
\((a_k)_{k=1}^n\),
\[
\left\|\sum_{k=1}^n a_k\chi_k(\mu)\right\|_U
 =
\mu\left(\sum_{k=1}^n a_ke_k\right).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For \(\mu\in\PP\), put \(F_\mu=J(\Omega(\mu))\in\K_c(Q_H)\). Let \(p_r\) be
the maps from Lemma~\ref{lem:projection-selectors}, and put
\[
q_r(\mu)=J^{-1}(p_r(F_\mu))\in \Omega(\mu).
\]
For each \(k\in\Nat\), define a bounded scalar sequence
\[
\chi_k(\mu)
 =
\left(g(\mu(e_k))\, q_r(\mu)_{m(k)}\right)_{r=1}^\infty
\in \ell_\infty.
\]

The map \(\mu\mapsto\chi_k(\mu)\) is continuous as a map into \(\ell_\infty\).
Indeed, \(\mu\mapsto F_\mu\) is continuous; the projection map
\((F,z)\mapsto P_F(z)\) is continuous on the compact space
\(\K_c(Q_H)\times Q_H\), hence uniformly continuous there; and the fixed
coordinate \(J^{-1}(\cdot)_{m(k)}\) is continuous on \(Q_H\). The scalar factor
\(g(\mu(e_k))\) is also continuous.

Let \(U\) be the closed linear span in \(\ell_\infty\) of
\[
\bigcup_{k\in\Nat}\chi_k(\PP).
\]
Since \(\PP\) is separable and every \(\chi_k\) is continuous into the metric
space \(\ell_\infty\), each \(\chi_k(\PP)\) is separable. Therefore \(U\) is
separable.

It remains to compute the norm. Fix \(\mu\in\PP\) and
\(u=\sum_{k=1}^n a_ke_k\). Since \(\{q_r(\mu):r\in\Nat\}\) is dense in
\(\Omega(\mu)\),
\begin{align*}
\left\|\sum_{k=1}^n a_k\chi_k(\mu)\right\|_{\ell_\infty}
&=
\sup_{r\in\Nat}
\left|
\sum_{k=1}^n a_k\,g(\mu(e_k))\,q_r(\mu)_{m(k)}
\right|\\
&=
\sup_{b\in\Omega(\mu)}
\left|
\sum_{k=1}^n a_k\,g(\mu(e_k))\,b_{m(k)}
\right|.
\end{align*}
If \(b\in\Omega(\mu)\), then \(b_{m(k)}=x^*(e_k/g(\mu(e_k)))\) for some
\(x^*\in B_{X_\mu^*}\). Hence the last supremum is
\[
\sup_{x^*\in B_{X_\mu^*}} |x^*(u)|=\|u\|_{X_\mu}=\mu(u),
\]
by the Hahn--Banach theorem. This proves the theorem.
\end{proof}

\section*{Answer to Question 2}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:q2}
Let \(X\) be an isometrically universal separable Banach space and let
\(\tau\) be an admissible topology on \(SB(X)\). There exists a
\(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_2\)-measurable map
\[
\Phi:\PP_\infty\to (SB(X),\tau)
\]
such that \(X_\mu\) is linearly isometric to \(\Phi(\mu)\) for every
\(\mu\in\PP_\infty\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-realization}, choose \(U\) and
\(\chi_k:\PP\to U\). Since \(X\) is isometrically universal, regard \(U\) as a
subspace of \(X\). Define
\[
\Phi(\mu)=\closedSpan\{\chi_k(\mu):k\in\Nat\}\subseteq X.
\]
The norm identity from Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-realization} says that the
linear map \(e_k\mapsto\chi_k(\mu)\) extends to an onto linear isometry from
\(X_\mu\) to \(\Phi(\mu)\).

It remains only to record the descriptive complexity. The source paper,
Lemma 2.10, proves that whenever the realizing maps \(\chi_k\) are
\(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_n\)-measurable, the map
\(\mu\mapsto\closedSpan\{\chi_k(\mu)\}\) into any admissible \(SB(X)\)
topology is \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_{n+1}\)-measurable. Here the maps
\(\chi_k\) are continuous, i.e. \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_1\)-measurable, so
\(\Phi\) is \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_2\)-measurable. Restricting the domain to
\(\PP_\infty\) gives the desired map.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and limitations}

The proof is non-computational. The key continuity point is the uniform
continuity of Hilbert-space projection on the compact set
\(\K_c(Q_H)\times Q_H\); this gives continuity of the coordinate maps into
\(\ell_\infty\), not merely pointwise continuity of each scalar coordinate.

This answers Question 2 as stated. It does not answer Question 1: the
construction realizes \(X_\mu\) as a closed subspace of a fixed universal
Banach space, but it does not produce a continuous norm code
\(\PP_\infty\to\BB\) with an independent coordinate sequence.

\section*{Novelty check}

Local index searches for arXiv:1912.03994, \(\PP_\infty\), \(\BB\),
\(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}^0_2\), admissible \(SB(X)\), and the source questions
found only the prior fixed-representative partial packet for Question 1.
Web searches on 2026-06-16 for the exact question and author/title phrases
found arXiv:1912.03994 and the sequel arXiv:2204.06834, but no statement that
Question 2 had been answered. The present proof uses the continuous
dual-ball lemma from arXiv:2204.06834 as an input and adds the
projection-parametrization argument.
