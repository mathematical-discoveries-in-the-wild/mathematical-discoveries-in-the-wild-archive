\section*{Source Question}

This packet concerns Genrich Belitskii and Victoria Rayskin,
\emph{Extension of differentiable local mappings on linear topological
spaces}, arXiv:1812.11064 \cite{BelitskiiRayskin2018}.  The paper defines the
differentiable blid property for a linear topological space \(X\): for every
neighborhood \(U\) of \(0\), there is a differentiable map \(H:X\to X\) which
coincides locally with the identity and satisfies \(H(X)\subset U\).

\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source asks on PDF page 6:
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Question 5.8.} Which linear topological spaces have differentiable
blid property?
\end{quote}
It immediately adds that this question was not answered even for Banach
spaces.

\section*{Result}

We give a broad positive class outside the normed setting.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:weak}
Let \(X\) be a real vector space endowed with the weak locally convex topology
\(\sigma(X,\mathcal F)\) generated by a family \(\mathcal F\) of linear
functionals on \(X\).  Then \(X\) has \(C^\infty\) differentiable blid
property.  More precisely, for every neighborhood \(U\) of \(0\) there is a
\(C^\infty\) map \(H:X\to X\) such that \(H=\id\) on a neighborhood of \(0\)
and \(H(X)\subset U\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
For every index set \(I\), the product space \(\R^I\) with the product
topology has \(C^\infty\) differentiable blid property.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

In a weak topology, every neighborhood of zero is controlled by finitely many
linear coordinates.  One can therefore reduce the problem to a finite
dimensional cutoff.  If \(\Phi:X\to E\) is the finite coordinate map defining a
smaller neighborhood \(V\subset U\), choose a smooth bounded local identity
\(b:E\to E\) whose image lies in the coordinate set \(\Phi(V)\).  A linear
section \(R:E\to X\) lets us replace only the finite coordinate component of
\(x\):
\[
  H(x)=x-R\Phi x+R b(\Phi x).
\]
Then \(H\) is identical when \(\Phi x\) is small, but all values of \(\Phi(Hx)\)
lie in the desired finite coordinate neighborhood.

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:weak}}

Let \(U\) be a neighborhood of \(0\) in \(\sigma(X,\mathcal F)\).  By the
definition of the weak topology, there are functionals
\(f_1,\ldots,f_n\in\mathcal F\) and positive numbers
\(\varepsilon_1,\ldots,\varepsilon_n\) such that
\[
  V=\{x\in X: |f_j(x)|<\varepsilon_j,\ 1\le j\le n\}\subset U .
\]
If \(n=0\), take \(H=\id\).  Otherwise set
\[
  \Phi:X\to\R^n,\qquad \Phi x=(f_1(x),\ldots,f_n(x)),
\]
and let \(E=\Phi(X)\), a finite-dimensional subspace of \(\R^n\).  Put
\[
  W=\{y\in E: |y_j|<\varepsilon_j,\ 1\le j\le n\}.
\]
Choose a Euclidean norm \(|\cdot|\) on \(E\).  There is \(\rho>0\) such that
\(\{y\in E: |y|<\rho\}\subset W\).

Choose a scalar \(C^\infty\) function \(\lambda:[0,\infty)\to[0,1]\) such that
\(\lambda(s)=1\) for \(s\) near \(0\) and
\[
  \lambda(s)\sqrt{s}<\rho\qquad(s\ge0).
\]
For instance, take \(\lambda=1\) near \(0\), make \(\lambda\) compactly
supported, and shrink its tail if necessary.  Define
\[
  b:E\to E,\qquad b(y)=\lambda(|y|^2)y .
\]
Then \(b\) is \(C^\infty\), it coincides with the identity near \(0\), and
\(b(E)\subset W\).

Since \(\Phi:X\to E\) is onto, choose an algebraic linear section
\(R:E\to X\), so \(\Phi R=\id_E\).  This section is continuous: every
functional \(g\in\mathcal F\) gives \(g\circ R\), a linear functional on the
finite-dimensional space \(E\), hence a continuous map on \(E\).  Define
\[
  H:X\to X,\qquad H(x)=x-R\Phi x+R b(\Phi x).
\]
Then
\[
  \Phi(Hx)=\Phi x-\Phi R\Phi x+\Phi R b(\Phi x)=b(\Phi x)\in W ,
\]
so \(H(X)\subset \Phi^{-1}(W)=V\subset U\).

Also \(H(x)=x\) whenever \(\Phi x\) lies in the neighborhood of \(0\) on which
\(b=\id_E\).  Therefore \(H\) is locally identical to the identity.

It remains only to record differentiability.  The map \(H\) is the identity
plus the finite-dimensional cylinder map
\[
  R\circ(b-\id_E)\circ\Phi .
\]
For \(k\ge2\),
\[
  D^kH(x)(h_1,\ldots,h_k)
  =R\,D^k b(\Phi x)(\Phi h_1,\ldots,\Phi h_k),
\]
and
\[
  DH(x)h=h+R\bigl(Db(\Phi x)-I_E\bigr)\Phi h .
\]
These are continuous multilinear maps in the weak topology, because they are
compositions of continuous linear maps with finite-dimensional smooth
derivatives.  Thus \(H\) is \(C^\infty\) in the standard differentiability
senses used in the source paper, including bounded and compact/Hadamard
differentiability.  This proves the theorem.

\section*{Proof of the Corollary}

The product topology on \(\R^I\) is the weak topology generated by the
coordinate functionals \(x\mapsto x_i\), \(i\in I\).  The corollary follows
from Theorem \ref{thm:weak}.

\section*{Limitations and Human Review Recommendation}

This is a partial result.  It does not classify Banach spaces, and it does not
address strong locally convex topologies defined by infinite-dimensional
seminorms.  Review should focus on the differentiability claim for weak
topologies and on the continuity of the finite-dimensional section \(R:E\to X\).
