\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Miguel Martin, Yoel Perreau, and Abraham Rueda Zoca,
\emph{Diametral notions for elements of the unit ball of a Banach space},
arXiv:2301.04433 \cite{MPRZ}.

Question 7.7 asks for isomorphic restrictions on Banach spaces containing
\(\Delta\)-points and the other diametral notions. In particular, it asks
whether a reflexive or even super-reflexive Banach space can contain
\(\Delta\)-, super \(\Delta\)-, ccs \(\Delta\)-, Daugavet, or super Daugavet
points.

\section*{Packet Status}

This is a literature-known partial subcase, not a new partial result claimed
by the run.  The later paper arXiv:2303.00511 explicitly answers this part of
Question 7.7.  The purpose of this packet is to keep an additional direct proof
found during the run, using a simpler explicit equivalent norm on \(\ell_2\).

\section*{Definitions Used}

For \(x\in S_X\), \(x\) is a super \(\Delta\)-point if
\[
  \sup_{y\in W}\|x-y\|=2
\]
for every non-empty relatively weakly open subset \(W\) of \(B_X\) containing
\(x\).  It is a ccs \(\Delta\)-point if the same equality holds for every
convex combination of slices of \(B_X\) containing \(x\).  Since slices are
relatively weakly open, every super \(\Delta\)-point is a \(\Delta\)-point.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The obstruction in Question 7.7 is isomorphic: can a space still be
superreflexive while its norm has diametral points?  We keep the underlying
linear space equal to \(\ell_2\), so superreflexivity is automatic once the new
norm is equivalent to the Hilbert norm.

The norm is designed so that \(e_1+e_n\) remains on the unit sphere, while the
single coordinate \(e_n\) has norm \(2\).  Since \(e_n\to0\) weakly, every
weak neighborhood of \(e_1\) in the unit ball contains some \(e_1+e_n\), and
that point is exactly distance \(2\) from \(e_1\).  A separate coordinate
inequality makes \(e_1\) extreme in the unit ball; once a convex combination
of slices contains an extreme point, all slices in the representation must
contain that point, so the same weak-null sequence proves the ccs version.

\section*{Direct Proof}

\begin{theorem}
There exists a superreflexive Banach space containing a point that is
simultaneously a \(\Delta\)-point, a super \(\Delta\)-point, and a ccs
\(\Delta\)-point.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \((e_n)\) be the usual basis of \(\ellTwo\).  For
\(x=(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}\in\ellTwo\), define
\[
  \normN{x}
  =
  \max\left\{
    |x_1|,
    \left(\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}|x_n|^2\right)^{1/2},
    \sup_{n\ge 2}|x_1-2x_n|
  \right\}.
\]
This is a norm: it is the maximum of seminorms, and the first two terms
separate points.  It is equivalent to the Hilbert norm.  Indeed,
\[
  \|x\|_2
  \le \sqrt 2\max\left\{|x_1|,
    \left(\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}|x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\}
  \le \sqrt 2\,\normN{x},
\]
while
\[
  \normN{x}
  \le \max\{\|x\|_2,\|x\|_2, |x_1|+2\sup_{n\ge2}|x_n|\}
  \le 3\|x\|_2.
\]
Let \(Y=(\ellTwo,\|\cdot\|_N)\).  Since \(Y\) is linearly isomorphic to
Hilbert space, it is superreflexive.

We next record three elementary norm values.  For every \(n\ge2\),
\[
  \normN{e_1}=1,\qquad
  \normN{e_1+e_n}=1,\qquad
  \normN{e_n}=2.
\]
The sequence \(e_1+e_n\) converges weakly to \(e_1\) in \(Y\), because the new
norm is equivalent to the Hilbert norm and \(e_n\to0\) weakly in \(\ell_2\).
Let \(W\) be any relatively weakly open subset of \(B_Y\) containing \(e_1\).
Then \(e_1+e_n\in W\) for all sufficiently large \(n\), and for such \(n\)
\[
  \normN{e_1-(e_1+e_n)}
  =
  \normN{-e_n}
  =
  2.
\]
Thus \(\sup_{y\in W}\normN{e_1-y}=2\), and \(e_1\) is a super
\(\Delta\)-point.  In particular, \(e_1\) is a \(\Delta\)-point.

It remains to prove the ccs assertion.  First we show that \(e_1\) is an
extreme point of \(B_Y\).  Suppose \(u,v\in B_Y\) and
\[
  e_1=\frac{u+v}{2}.
\]
Since \(|u_1|\le1\), \(|v_1|\le1\), and \((u_1+v_1)/2=1\), the equality case
in the unit disc gives \(u_1=v_1=1\).  Fix \(n\ge2\).  From \(u,v\in B_Y\)
we have
\[
  |1-2u_n|\le1,\qquad |1-2v_n|\le1,
\]
and from \((u_n+v_n)/2=0\) we have \(v_n=-u_n\).  Hence
\[
  |1-2u_n|\le1
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  |1+2u_n|\le1.
\]
The two closed discs \(\{z:|1-2z|\le1\}\) and
\(\{z:|1+2z|\le1\}\) are tangent at \(0\), so \(u_n=0=v_n\).  This holds
for every \(n\ge2\), and therefore \(u=v=e_1\).  Thus \(e_1\in\operatorname
{ext} B_Y\).

Now let
\[
  C=\sum_{i=1}^m\lambda_i S_i
\]
be a convex combination of slices of \(B_Y\), with \(\lambda_i>0\) and
\(\sum_i\lambda_i=1\), and suppose \(e_1\in C\).  Choose
\(z_i\in S_i\) such that \(e_1=\sum_i\lambda_i z_i\).  Since \(e_1\) is an
extreme point of the convex set \(B_Y\), every \(z_i\) must equal \(e_1\).
Thus \(e_1\in S_i\) for each \(i\).  The finite intersection
\[
  W=\bigcap_{i=1}^m S_i
\]
is a relatively weakly open subset of \(B_Y\) containing \(e_1\).  Therefore
some \(e_1+e_n\) belongs to \(W\).  For this \(n\),
\[
  e_1+e_n=\sum_{i=1}^m\lambda_i(e_1+e_n)\in C,
\]
and, as above, \(\normN{e_1-(e_1+e_n)}=2\).  Hence
\[
  \sup_{y\in C}\normN{e_1-y}=2.
\]
So \(e_1\) is a ccs \(\Delta\)-point.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This is a partial answer to Question 7.7.  It proves that superreflexive
spaces can contain \(\Delta\)-, super \(\Delta\)-, and ccs
\(\Delta\)-points.  It does not prove that a reflexive or superreflexive
space can contain a Daugavet point or a super Daugavet point.

\section*{Novelty and Provenance Check}

The result itself is already known in later literature:
Abrahamsen--Aliaga--Lima--Martiny--Perreau--Prochazka--Veeorg
\cite{AALMPPV} explicitly cite the MPRZ question and construct a
superreflexive renorming of \(\ell_2\) with super \(\Delta\)- and ccw
\(\Delta\)-points.  The present packet is therefore not counted as a new
partial result.  Its purpose is to record a direct proof found during the run,
using the explicit norm above rather than invoking the later theorem.

The run indexes were checked for arXiv:2301.04433, superreflexive
\(\Delta\)-points, ccs \(\Delta\)-points, and the existing
arXiv:2303.00511 literature-status packet.  This packet should be read
together with that provenance packet.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a literature-known partial subcase with an additional direct proof.
The most
important checks are the equivalence of \(\|\cdot\|_N\) with the Hilbert norm,
the weak convergence \(e_1+e_n\to e_1\), the extremality proof for \(e_1\),
and the final reduction from a ccs containing \(e_1\) to a finite intersection
of slices containing \(e_1\).
