\section*{Source Statement}

The source is S. J. Montgomery-Smith and E. M. Semenov,
\emph{Embeddings of rearrangement invariant spaces that are not strictly
singular}, arXiv:math/0007058, Positivity 4 (2000), 397--404
\cite{mss0007058}. On PDF page 2 it states Conjecture 3:

\begin{quote}
Given \(x_1,\dots,x_n\in L_1([0,1])\), and a rearrangement invariant space
\(E\) on \([0,1]\), there exists signs \(\epsilon_1,\dots,\epsilon_n=\pm 1\)
such that
\[
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_i x_i\right\|_{L_1}
\ge c^{-1}
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n r_i\|x_i\|_1\right\|_E .
\]
\end{quote}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The printed conjecture quantifies over all rearrangement invariant spaces.
For \(E=L_\infty\), the right side is the largest pointwise size of the
Rademacher sum with positive coefficients. If the test functions themselves
are the Rademachers, changing signs only changes which Rademacher sum appears
on the left. Its \(L_1\)-size is of order \(\sqrt n\), while its \(L_\infty\)
size is exactly \(n\).

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Conjecture 3 of \cite{mss0007058} is false as printed.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(E=L_\infty[0,1]\). For \(n\ge 1\), set \(x_i=r_i\), where
\((r_i)_{i=1}^n\) are the first \(n\) Rademacher functions on \([0,1]\).
Then \(\|x_i\|_1=1\) for all \(i\), and hence
\[
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n r_i\|x_i\|_1\right\|_{L_\infty}
=
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n r_i\right\|_{L_\infty}
=n,
\]
because the finite Rademacher vector attains the all-\(+1\) sign pattern on
a dyadic atom of positive measure.

On the other hand, for any fixed signs \(\epsilon_i=\pm1\),
\[
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_i r_i\right\|_{L_1}
\le
\left\|\sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_i r_i\right\|_{L_2}
=\sqrt n.
\]
Thus the conjectured inequality would require
\[
\sqrt n \ge c^{-1}n
\]
for every \(n\). Equivalently, \(c\ge\sqrt n\) for every \(n\), impossible
for a fixed finite constant \(c\). Therefore the printed Conjecture 3 fails.
\end{proof}

\section*{Relation to Conjecture 2}

This counterexample does not refute the paper's main Conjecture 2. A later
paper of Hernandez, Ruiz, and Sanchiz records the corresponding classification
for symmetric, equivalently rearrangement invariant, function spaces as known:
the inclusion \(E(\mu)\hookrightarrow L_1(\mu)\) is strictly singular if and
only if \(L^{\exp x^2}_0\) is not included in \(E(\mu)\), citing
Hernandez--Novikov--Semenov and Astashkin--Hernandez--Semenov
\cite{hrs2406}.
