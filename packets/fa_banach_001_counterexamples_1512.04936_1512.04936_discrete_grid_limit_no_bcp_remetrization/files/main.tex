\section{Source question}

Le Donne and Rigot ask in Section 8.3 of \cite{LeDonneRigot}, on printed page 55:
\begin{quote}
To our knowledge, there is no known example of a metric space $(X,d)$ of finite topological dimension for which there is no distances biLipschitz equivalent to $d$ satisfying BCP.
\end{quote}
Equivalently: does finite Lebesgue covering dimension guarantee that the
bi-Lipschitz class of a metric contains a distance satisfying the
Besicovitch Covering Property?

\section{Definitions}

A metric space $(Y,\delta)$ satisfies \emph{BCP with constant $Q$} if every
centered family of balls covering a bounded set of centers admits a finite or
countable covering subfamily of multiplicity at most $Q$.

A finite family $\{B_\delta(y_i,r_i)\}_{i=1}^s$ of closed balls is a
\emph{Besicovitch family} if its balls have a common point and no ball contains
the center of another.  The \emph{weak Besicovitch Covering Property} (WBCP)
with constant $Q$ says that every such family has cardinality at most $Q$.
BCP with constant $Q$ implies WBCP with the same constant
\cite[Section 3.1]{LeDonneRigot}.
We use closed balls in BCP and WBCP, matching the convention of the source;
open balls will be denoted by $B_\delta^\circ(y,r)$.

Two metrics $d$ and $\rho$ on the same set are bi-Lipschitz equivalent if
there is $L\geq 1$ such that
\[
 L^{-1}d(x,y)\leq \rho(x,y)\leq Ld(x,y)
 \qquad(x,y\in X).
\]

\section{Proof intuition}

Put increasingly fine finite grids of cubes of \emph{every} dimension into
mutually separated components.  The resulting space is countable and
discrete, hence has topological dimension zero.  Nevertheless, any single
metric bi-Lipschitz equivalent to it must use one distortion constant on all
the grids, and if it satisfied BCP it would use one Besicovitch constant on
all of them as well.

Fix a dimension larger than that hypothetical Besicovitch constant.  The
restricted metrics on the finer and finer grids have a uniformly convergent
subsequence.  Their limit is a metric on the full cube, bi-Lipschitz to the
usual cube metric.  WBCP passes to this limit because a Besicovitch family is
described by finitely many strict center-exclusion inequalities; slightly
enlarging the radii preserves those inequalities on a sufficiently fine
grid.  Finally, WBCP with constant $Q$ on a compact space yields open-cover
refinements of multiplicity $Q$ by a direct equal-radius greedy selection.
Thus the limiting cube would have covering dimension at most $Q-1$, in
conflict with its actual dimension.

\section{The counterexample}

For $m,k\in\N$, $m,k\geq1$, let
\[
 D_{m,k}:=\left\{0,2^{-k},2\cdot2^{-k},\ldots,1\right\}^{m}
 \subset [0,1]^m.
\]
Regard the sets $D_{m,k}$ as pairwise disjoint labelled copies and set
\[
 X:=\bigsqcup_{m,k\geq1}D_{m,k}.
\]
Define a metric $d$ on $X$ by
\[
 d(x,y):=
 \begin{cases}
   \|x-y\|_\infty,&x,y\in D_{m,k}\text{ for the same }(m,k),\\
   3,&x,y\text{ lie in distinct components}.
 \end{cases}
\]
The triangle inequality is immediate because every component has diameter at
most $1$.  Each point is isolated: it lies in a finite component, so its
distance from all other points has a positive minimum.  Consequently $X$ is a
countable discrete topological space and
\[
 \dim_{\mathrm{top}}X=0.
\]
It is also complete and bounded, though neither extra fact is needed.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
No metric bi-Lipschitz equivalent to $d$ satisfies the Besicovitch Covering
Property.  Hence $(X,d)$ is a zero-dimensional counterexample to the question
of Le Donne and Rigot.
\end{theorem}

We isolate the compactness step.

\begin{lemma}[Grid-limit lemma]\label{lem:gridlimit}
Let $K=[0,1]^m$ with its $\ell_\infty$ metric, and let
$D_k=\{0,2^{-k},\ldots,1\}^m$.  Suppose $\delta_k$ is a metric on $D_k$ such
that, for fixed $L,Q$ independent of $k$,
\[
 L^{-1}\|u-v\|_\infty\leq\delta_k(u,v)
 \leq L\|u-v\|_\infty,
\]
and $(D_k,\delta_k)$ satisfies WBCP with constant $Q$.  Then a subsequence
converges on approximating grid points to a metric $\delta_\infty$ on $K$
which is $L$-bi-Lipschitz equivalent to $\|\cdot\|_\infty$ and satisfies WBCP
with constant $Q$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $D_k\times D_k$, the function $(u,v)\mapsto\delta_k(u,v)$ satisfies
\[
 |\delta_k(u,v)-\delta_k(u',v')|
 \leq L\bigl(\|u-u'\|_\infty+\|v-v'\|_\infty\bigr).
\]
Extend it to $K\times K$ without increasing this Lipschitz constant, for
example by the McShane extension formula \cite{McShane}, and clip the result
to $[0,L]$.  The extensions are uniformly bounded and equicontinuous, so a
subsequence converges uniformly to a continuous function
$\delta_\infty:K\times K\to[0,L]$.

If $u_k,v_k\in D_k$ converge to $u,v\in K$ along this subsequence, uniform
convergence and equicontinuity give
\[
 \delta_k(u_k,v_k)\longrightarrow\delta_\infty(u,v).
\]
Approximating two or three arbitrary points of $K$ by grid points now passes
symmetry, the triangle inequality, and the two bi-Lipschitz bounds to the
limit.  The lower bound separates distinct points, so $\delta_\infty$ is a
metric.

It remains to pass WBCP.  Let
$\{B_{\delta_\infty}(x_i,r_i)\}_{i=1}^s$ be a Besicovitch family and choose
$x_0$ in the intersection of all its balls.  Thus
\[
 \delta_\infty(x_0,x_i)\leq r_i,
 \qquad
 \delta_\infty(x_i,x_j)>\max\{r_i,r_j\}\quad(i\ne j).
\]
Choose $\varepsilon>0$ so small that
\[
 \delta_\infty(x_i,x_j)>
 \max\{r_i,r_j\}+3\varepsilon\quad(i\ne j).
\]
Choose grid approximants $x_{i,k}\to x_i$, including $i=0$.  For all large
$k$, convergence of the finitely many relevant distances shows that the
balls
\[
 B_{\delta_k}(x_{i,k},r_i+\varepsilon),\qquad i=1,\ldots,s,
\]
have common point $x_{0,k}$ and contain none of each other's centers.  They
form a Besicovitch family in $(D_k,\delta_k)$, hence $s\leq Q$.  Therefore
$\delta_\infty$ satisfies WBCP with constant $Q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Compact WBCP dimension bound]\label{lem:dimension}
If a compact metric space $(K,\delta)$ satisfies WBCP with constant $Q$, then
its Lebesgue covering dimension is at most $Q-1$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal U$ be an open cover of $K$ and let $\lambda>0$ be a Lebesgue
number.  Choose $r>0$ with $4r<\lambda$.  Greedily choose centers
$c_1,\ldots,c_t$ so that each new center is outside all previously selected
closed $r$-balls.  Compactness makes the process finite, since the centers are
$r$-separated.  A maximal such family of closed $r$-balls covers $K$, and
$\delta(c_i,c_j)>r$ for $i\ne j$.

Since the family is finite, choose $\eta>0$ so small that
\[
 r+\eta<\min_{i\ne j}\delta(c_i,c_j)
 \quad\text{and}\quad 2(r+\eta)<\lambda,
\]
with the first condition omitted if $t=1$.  The open balls
$B_\delta^\circ(c_i,r+\eta)$ still cover $K$ and refine $\mathcal U$.
To bound their multiplicity, fix $z$ and let $I$ index the open balls that
contain $z$.  For every $i\in I$, choose
\[
 \delta(z,c_i)<s_i<r+\eta.
\]
The closed balls $B_\delta(c_i,s_i)$, $i\in I$, have common point $z$, while
$\delta(c_i,c_j)>r+\eta>\max\{s_i,s_j\}$ for distinct indices.  They form a
Besicovitch family, so $|I|\leq Q$.  Thus the open refinement has
multiplicity at most $Q$, and
$\dim_{\mathrm{top}}K\leq Q-1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
Suppose, toward a contradiction, that a metric $\rho$ on $X$ is
$L$-bi-Lipschitz equivalent to $d$ and satisfies BCP with an integer constant
$Q$.  BCP implies WBCP with constant $Q$.  A Besicovitch family whose centers
and common point lie in one component $D_{m,k}$ is also a Besicovitch family
in the ambient space, because the defining distance inequalities are
unchanged.  Therefore every restriction $\rho|_{D_{m,k}}$ satisfies WBCP with
constant $Q$.

Fix $m=Q+1$ and write $\delta_k=\rho|_{D_{m,k}}$ after identifying the
labelled component with its grid in $[0,1]^m$.  The bi-Lipschitz inequalities
give
\[
 L^{-1}\|u-v\|_\infty\leq\delta_k(u,v)
 \leq L\|u-v\|_\infty.
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:gridlimit} produces on $[0,1]^m$ an $L$-bi-Lipschitz equivalent
metric $\delta_\infty$ satisfying WBCP with constant $Q$.  The bi-Lipschitz
bounds give the usual topology, while Lemma~\ref{lem:dimension} gives
\[
 m=\dim_{\mathrm{top}}[0,1]^m\leq Q-1.
\]
This contradicts $m=Q+1$.  No such $\rho$ exists.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification notes}

\begin{itemize}
\item The construction is topologically discrete even though the within-block
      mesh tends to zero: every individual point belongs to one finite block.
\item The proof uses only the two constants attached to one hypothetical
      remetrization, namely its global bi-Lipschitz constant $L$ and global BCP
      constant $Q$.
\item Restricting WBCP to a component is legitimate even though an ambient
      ball may contain points in other components; the common point and all
      center-exclusion inequalities already occur inside the component.
\item The grid-limit argument does not assume that BCP itself is closed under
      limits.  It passes only WBCP, whose obstruction is a finite collection
      of strict inequalities.  This is the same stability mechanism used in
      \cite[Lemma 7.7]{LeDonneRigot}, here adapted to varying finite grids.
\item The final dimension estimate is proved directly for compact spaces and
      does not require doubling, completeness of the hypothetical metric, or
      a measure-differentiation theorem.
\end{itemize}

\section{Limitations and novelty check}

This answers the source question negatively in the class of complete bounded
countable metric spaces, indeed with a topologically discrete example.  It
does not produce a connected, locally connected, geodesic, or doubling
counterexample; those stronger variants remain untouched.

On July 21, 2026, the run registry, solution, attempt, and proof-gap indexes
were searched for arXiv:1512.04936 and the core BCP/bi-Lipschitz/topological
dimension terminology.  Bounded web and arXiv searches used the exact source
sentence and close variants involving ``finite topological dimension'',
``bi-Lipschitz equivalent'', and ``Besicovitch Covering Property''.  They found
the source paper, its antecedent Heisenberg-group paper, Aldaz's 2022 survey of
Besicovitch and doubling properties, and adjacent work, but no later paper
claiming this counterexample or resolving the exact remetrization question.
This is a bounded novelty check, not a guarantee of originality.

\section{Human-review recommendation}

The highest-value review is the grid-limit lemma: verify the uniform extension
argument and the $+\varepsilon$ transfer of an arbitrary Besicovitch family to
a sufficiently fine grid.  Next check that ambient WBCP restricts to each
labelled component exactly as used.  The remaining steps are the standard
covering-dimension characterization and the classical identity
$\dim[0,1]^m=m$ \cite{HurewiczWallman}.
