## Identification

The source overview lists as a further-development item that the existence of
universal differentiability sets (UDS) in Laakso spaces is still open. It says
that Capolli--Pinamonti--Speight only proved that the classical maximal
directional derivative approach does not work in the Laakso space, leaving
the existence of UDS on the table.

The later paper arXiv:2406.07660 explicitly identifies that same open question
and answers it. In its introduction it says the previous work left open
whether measure-zero UDS exist in Laakso space, and that the present paper
answers the question affirmatively by a different method. Theorem 2 then
constructs a Borel set \(N\subset F\) with \(\mathcal H^Q(N)=0\) such that
every real-valued Lipschitz function on the Laakso space \(F\) is
differentiable at some point of \(N\).

## Scope

This packet records only the UDS existence question for the standard Laakso
space discussed in the 2022 overview. The source paper's broader speculative
items about non-Abelian Carnot-model Laakso-type spaces, different
self-similar bases, and inverse-limit spaces are not answered by this packet.
