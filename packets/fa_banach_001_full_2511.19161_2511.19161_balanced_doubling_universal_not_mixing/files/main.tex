This packet gives a full solution candidate, likely valid pending expert

\section*{Setup and Source Criterion}

Let \(\Omega=\T\) with normalized Lebesgue measure, let
\(\tau x=2x \pmod 1\), and set
\[
  A_1=[0,1/2), \qquad A_2=[1/2,1).
\]
Let \(X=\ell_p\), \(1\le p<\infty\), or \(X=c_0\).  Define weighted shifts by
\[
  w_l=1+\frac{1}{l+1}, \qquad
  v_l=1-\frac{1}{l+1}\qquad(l\ge 1),
\]
and
\[
  T(\omega)=
  \begin{cases}
    B_w, & \omega\in A_1,\\
    B_v, & \omega\in A_2.
  \end{cases}
\]
Write
\[
  T_n(\omega)=T(\tau^{n-1}\omega)\cdots T(\tau\omega)T(\omega).
\]
By the source paper's criterion for \(X=\ell_p\) and \(X=c_0\), the sequence
\((T_n(\omega))\) is universal, equivalently weakly mixing, iff there is an
increasing sequence \(n_i\) such that for every fixed \(j\ge0\),
\[
  \prod_{l=1}^{n_i+j}
  \left|\varepsilon_l(\tau^{n_i+j-l}\omega)\right|
  \longrightarrow \infty.
\]
It is mixing iff the same product tends to \(\infty\) along the full sequence
\(n\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

In the balanced doubling-map case the variables
\[
  \xi_l(\omega)=2\one_{A_1}(\tau^l\omega)-1
\]
are independent Rademacher variables.  The logarithm of the product controlling
universality is
\[
  V_n(\omega)=d_n+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n} b_k \xi_{n-k}(\omega),
  \qquad b_k=\log\left(1+\frac2k\right),
\]
where \(d_n\) is bounded and converges.  Since \(b_k\sim 2/k\), the coefficients
are square summable but not summable.  Thus the associated two-sided stationary
Rademacher series converges, is ergodic under the shift, and has unbounded
support in both directions.  Ergodicity forces arbitrarily large positive and
negative values with positive frequency.  A short tail estimate transfers this
back to the one-sided finite sums \(V_n\).

Consequently \(V_n\) has \(\limsup V_n=+\infty\) and
\(\liminf V_n=-\infty\) almost surely.  The positive limsup gives the
subsequence required for universality.  The negative liminf prevents mixing.

\section*{The Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
In the balanced doubling-map setup above, for \(X=\ell_p\),
\(1\le p<\infty\), and for \(X=c_0\), the random product sequence
\((T_n(\omega))_{n\ge1}\) is universal and weakly mixing for Lebesgue-almost
every \(\omega\in\T\).  It is not mixing for Lebesgue-almost every
\(\omega\in\T\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
  X_l(\omega)=\one_{A_1}(\tau^l\omega), \qquad
  \xi_l(\omega)=2X_l(\omega)-1 .
\]
Under the binary coding of the doubling map, the \(\xi_l\)'s are independent
Rademacher variables.  Put
\[
  b_k=\log\left(1+\frac2k\right), \qquad
  V_n(\omega)=
  \log\prod_{l=1}^{n}
  \left|\varepsilon_l(\tau^{n-l}\omega)\right|.
\]
The computation in the source paper gives
\[
  V_n
  =
  -\log(n+1)+\sum_{k=1}^{n} b_k X_{n-k}
  =
  d_n+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n} b_k \xi_{n-k},
\]
where
\[
  d_n=-\log(n+1)+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n} b_k
      =-\log(n+1)+\frac12\log\frac{(n+1)(n+2)}2
      \longrightarrow -\frac12\log 2 .
\]

We first prove
\[
  \limsup_{n\to\infty} V_n=+\infty,
  \qquad
  \liminf_{n\to\infty} V_n=-\infty
  \quad\text{almost surely.}
\]
Work for the moment on the two-sided Bernoulli shift
\((\{-1,1\}^{\mathbb Z},\nu,\sigma)\), and denote the coordinates again by
\(\xi_j\).  Since \(\sum b_k^2<\infty\), the Rademacher series
\[
  Y_n=d+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} b_k \xi_{n-k},
  \qquad d=-\frac12\log2,
\]
converges almost surely and in \(L^2\).  The process \(Y_n=Y_0\circ\sigma^n\)
is stationary and ergodic.

The law of \(Y_0\) has unbounded support above and below.  Indeed,
\(\sum b_k=\infty\).  Given \(M>0\), choose \(K\) so large that
\[
  d+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{K}b_k > M+L
\]
for some \(L\) with
\[
  \nu\left\{\frac12\sum_{k>K}b_k\xi_{-k}>-L\right\}>0.
\]
On the positive-probability event where
\(\xi_{-1}=\cdots=\xi_{-K}=1\) and the tail is larger than \(-L\), one has
\(Y_0>M\).  The lower tail is identical, using
\(\xi_{-1}=\cdots=\xi_{-K}=-1\).  Hence
\[
  \nu(Y_0>M)>0,\qquad \nu(Y_0<-M)>0
  \quad (M>0).
\]
By Birkhoff's ergodic theorem, for almost every two-sided sequence the sets
\(\{n:Y_n>M\}\) and \(\{n:Y_n<-M\}\) have positive lower frequency.

It remains to transfer this statement from \(Y_n\) to \(V_n\).  On the
two-sided extension,
\[
  Y_n-V_n
  =
  (d-d_n)+\frac12\sum_{r=1}^{\infty}b_{n+r}\xi_{-r}.
\]
Therefore
\[
  \E |Y_n-V_n|^2
  \le C|d-d_n|^2+C\sum_{r=1}^{\infty}b_{n+r}^2
  \le \frac{C'}{n}.
\]
Consequently
\[
  \E\left[\frac1N\sum_{n=1}^{N}|Y_n-V_n|^2\right]
  \le C''\frac{\log N}{N}\longrightarrow0.
\]
Applying this estimate along dyadic \(N\)'s and using Borel--Cantelli gives
\[
  \frac1N\sum_{n=1}^{N}|Y_n-V_n|^2\longrightarrow0
  \quad\text{almost surely}.
\]
Thus, for every fixed \(a>0\), the set of \(n\) such that
\(|Y_n-V_n|>a\) has zero asymptotic density.  Combining this zero-density
exceptional set with the positive-frequency visits of \(Y_n\) above \(M+1\)
and below \(-M-1\), we obtain
\[
  \limsup_{n\to\infty}V_n=+\infty,
  \qquad
  \liminf_{n\to\infty}V_n=-\infty
\]
for almost every two-sided sequence.  Since this assertion depends only on
the nonnegative coordinates, it holds for almost every point of the original
one-sided doubling system.

Choose an increasing sequence \(n_i\) such that \(V_{n_i}\to+\infty\).  For
each fixed \(j\ge0\), the source criterion needs \(V_{n_i+j}\to+\infty\).  This
follows because the drop from \(V_n\) to \(V_{n+j}\) is bounded below by a
constant depending only on \(j\).  Indeed, using the formula
\[
  V_n=-\log(n+1)+\sum_{k=1}^{n}b_k X_{n-k}
\]
and the monotonicity of \(b_k\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
  V_{n+j}-V_n
  &\ge -\log\frac{n+j+1}{n+1}
      -\sum_{k=1}^{n}(b_k-b_{k+j})X_{n-k}  \\
  &\ge -\log(j+1)-\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}(b_k-b_{k+j})  \\
  &= -\log(j+1)-\sum_{k=1}^{j}b_k .
\end{aligned}
\]
Therefore \(V_{n_i+j}\to+\infty\) for every fixed \(j\ge0\).  By the source
criterion, \((T_n(\omega))\) is universal and weakly mixing almost surely.

On the other hand, \(\liminf V_n=-\infty\) implies in particular that
\(V_n\) does not tend to \(+\infty\) along the full sequence.  The source
mixing criterion therefore shows that \((T_n(\omega))\) is not mixing almost
surely.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof uses the source paper's exact universality/mixing criteria for
\(\ell_p\) and \(c_0\), the binary independence of the doubling map already
identified in the source, and standard facts about square-summable Rademacher
series and ergodic stationary processes.

Local run indexes were searched for arXiv:2511.19161, random products of
weighted shifts, balanced doubling, Nörlund sums, and related terms; no
duplicate packet was found.  External web searches on July 3, 2026 for the
arXiv id, the exact title, the exact balanced-case phrase, and combinations of
``Norlund'', ``doubling map'', ``weighted shifts'', and ``universal'' did not
surface a separate answer.  The arXiv page for \cite{Gillet} records version 2
from December 15, 2025 and its abstract describes criteria plus examples, not
the balanced-case solution proved here.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

This should be reviewed as a full answer to the specific balanced
doubling-map case for \(X=\ell_p\) and \(X=c_0\).  The main proof point to
audit is the transfer from the two-sided stationary comparison process to the
finite one-sided sums \(V_n\), especially the density-zero estimate for
\(|Y_n-V_n|>a\).  The conclusion does not claim to settle balanced cases for
arbitrary ergodic systems or arbitrary partitions.
