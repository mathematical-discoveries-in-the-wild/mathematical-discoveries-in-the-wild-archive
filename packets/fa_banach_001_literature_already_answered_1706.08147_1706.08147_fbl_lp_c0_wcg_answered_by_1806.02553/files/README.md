# Literature Already Answered: FBL[c0(Gamma)] and FBL[ell_p(Gamma)] are WCG for p>2

Status: literature_already_answered

Source paper: Antonio Aviles, Jose Rodriguez, Pedro Tradacete, "The free Banach lattice generated by a Banach space", arXiv:1706.08147.

Supporting answer paper: Antonio Aviles, Pedro Tradacete, Ignacio Villanueva, "The free Banach lattices generated by ell_p and c_0", arXiv:1806.02553.

## Source Question

In the final paragraph of Section 5, after Corollary 5.5, arXiv:1706.08147 states that the authors do not know whether
\[
FBL[c_0(\Gamma)] \quad\text{or}\quad FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\quad (2<p<\infty)
\]
are weakly compactly generated when \(\Gamma\) is uncountable. They also note that they do not know any non-separable Banach space \(E\) for which \(FBL[E]\) is WCG.

## Supporting Answer

The later paper arXiv:1806.02553 explicitly continues this research direction. Its introduction says it completes the \(p>2\) direction and that the spaces \(FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\) for \(p>2\) and \(FBL[c_0(\Gamma)]\) are WCG independently of the size of \(\Gamma\). The decisive formal statement is Corollary `wcg` in Section 3:
\[
\text{For any set }\Gamma\text{ and }p>2,\quad FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\text{ and }FBL[c_0(\Gamma)]\text{ are WCG.}
\]

## Scope

This answers the exact residual WCG question from arXiv:1706.08147 for \(c_0(\Gamma)\) and \(\ell_p(\Gamma)\), \(2<p<\infty\), including uncountable \(\Gamma\). It is not a new proof from this run; it is a later explicit literature answer. The source paper's negative result for \(1<p\le 2\) remains as in the source.
