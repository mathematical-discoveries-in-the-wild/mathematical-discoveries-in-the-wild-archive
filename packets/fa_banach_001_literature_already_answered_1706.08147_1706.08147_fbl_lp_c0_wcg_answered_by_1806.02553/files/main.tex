\paragraph{Status.}
\textbf{literature\_already\_answered.}  This packet records an exact later-paper answer, not a new proof from the run.

\paragraph{Original source.}
Antonio Aviles, Jose Rodriguez, and Pedro Tradacete, \emph{The free Banach lattice generated by a Banach space}, arXiv:1706.08147.
In Section 5, after Corollary 5.5, the paper says that it is not known whether
\[
  FBL[c_0(\Gamma)]
  \quad\text{or}\quad
  FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\quad (2<p<\infty)
\]
are weakly compactly generated for uncountable \(\Gamma\).  The same paragraph notes that no non-separable Banach space \(E\) was known there with \(FBL[E]\) WCG.

\paragraph{Supporting answer.}
Antonio Aviles, Pedro Tradacete, and Ignacio Villanueva, \emph{The free Banach lattices generated by \(\ell_p\) and \(c_0\)}, arXiv:1806.02553.
The introduction explicitly says that the paper completes the \(p>2\) direction from arXiv:1706.08147.  The decisive formal statement is Corollary \texttt{wcg} in Section 3:
\[
  \text{For every set }\Gamma\text{ and every }p>2,\quad
  FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\text{ and }FBL[c_0(\Gamma)]\text{ are WCG.}
\]

\paragraph{Why this answers the target.}
The source question asks exactly for the WCG status of \(FBL[c_0(\Gamma)]\) and \(FBL[\ell_p(\Gamma)]\), \(2<p<\infty\), for uncountable \(\Gamma\).  The supporting corollary proves WCG for every set \(\Gamma\), hence in particular for all uncountable \(\Gamma\).  The supporting authors explicitly cite the source construction and present the result as the opposite \(p>2\) counterpart to the \(1\le p\le2\) non-WCG result of arXiv:1706.08147.

\paragraph{Scope and limitations.}
The packet settles only the source's residual WCG question for the classical spaces \(c_0(\Gamma)\) and \(\ell_p(\Gamma)\), \(2<p<\infty\).  It does not claim a characterization of all non-separable Banach spaces \(E\) for which \(FBL[E]\) is WCG.
