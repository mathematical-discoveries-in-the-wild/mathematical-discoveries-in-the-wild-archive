\paragraph{Status.}
\texttt{partial\_result\_likely\_valid}.  The result below is a partial answer to
the smoothness question raised in arXiv:1412.1279: it shows that the hypothesis
does not force superreflexivity.

\paragraph{Source question.}
Talponen asks what geometric properties are implied by the Frechet smoothness
of the canonical ultrapower point \(j(x)\), for every \(x\in S_X\)
\cite[p. 4]{Talponen}.

\includegraphics[width=0.94\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Result.}
There is a reflexive, non-superreflexive Banach space \(X\) such that, for
every free ultrafilter \(\U\) on \(\N\) and every \(x\in S_X\), the canonical
point \(j_\U(x)\in S_{X^\U}\) is a Frechet smooth point of \(X^\U\).

\paragraph{Idea of the Proof.}
Take an \(\ell_2\)-sum of finite-dimensional smooth spaces which become closer
and closer to \(\ell_\infty^n\).  The summands force failure of
superreflexivity, but the outer \(\ell_2\)-sum gives a strong orthogonality
phenomenon in ultrapowers: after taking the weak ultralimit projection
\(\Pi:X^\U\to X\), the ultrapower splits isometrically as
\[
  X^\U = j_\U(X)\oplus_2 \ker \Pi .
\]
Thus the canonical point \(j_\U(x)\) is just \((x,0)\) in an \(\ell_2\)-sum,
and Frechet smoothness reduces to the ordinary Frechet smoothness of \(x\) in
\(X\).

\begin{theorem}
Let \(p_1=2\) and \(p_n=\max\{2,\log n\}\) for \(n\geq 2\), and put
\[
  X=\left(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty \ell_{p_n}^n\right)_2 .
\]
Then \(X\) is reflexive and not superreflexive.  Nevertheless, for every free
ultrafilter \(\U\) on \(\N\) and every \(x\in S_X\), \(j_\U(x)\) is a Frechet
smooth point of \(X^\U\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The space \(X\) is reflexive because it is an \(\ell_2\)-sum of
finite-dimensional spaces.

It is not superreflexive.  Indeed, for every \(n\),
\[
  \|a\|_\infty \leq \|a\|_{p_n}\leq n^{1/p_n}\|a\|_\infty
  \qquad(a\in\mathbb R^n),
\]
and \(n^{1/p_n}\leq e\).  Thus \(X\) contains subspaces uniformly isomorphic
to \(\ell_\infty^n\) for all \(n\).  By the Enflo-Pisier finite
representability criterion for superreflexivity, this rules out
superreflexivity.

Each summand \(\ell_{p_n}^n\), \(1<p_n<\infty\), is Frechet smooth away from
zero.  The \(\ell_2\)-sum norm on \(X\) is therefore Frechet smooth away from
zero: for the squared norm this follows by differentiating the finite head and
controlling the tail with
\[
  \bigl|\|a+b\|^2-\|a\|^2\bigr|\leq 2\|a\|\|b\|+\|b\|^2
\]
and Cauchy-Schwarz.  Hence every \(x\in S_X\) is a Frechet smooth point of
\(X\).

Fix a free ultrafilter \(\U\).  Since \(X\) is reflexive, every bounded
sequence has a weak \(\U\)-limit.  Define the contractive projection
\[
  \Pi:X^\U\to X,\qquad
  \Pi((x_m)^\U)=w\text{-}\lim_{\U} x_m .
\]
Then \(\Pi j_\U=\mathrm{id}_X\).  We claim that for every \(x\in X\) and every
\(w=(w_m)^\U\in\ker\Pi\),
\[
  \|j_\U(x)+w\|^2=\|x\|^2+\|w\|^2. \tag{1}
\]
Write \(x=(x_r)_{r\geq1}\), \(w_m=(w_{m,r})_{r\geq1}\), and let \(P_N\) be
the projection onto the first \(N\) coordinates.  Because each coordinate is
finite-dimensional and \(w\in\ker\Pi\), \(P_Nw_m\to0\) in norm along \(\U\).
Therefore
\[
  \lim_\U \|P_Nx+P_Nw_m\|^2=\|P_Nx\|^2 .
\]
For the tail \(Q_N=I-P_N\), if \(\sup_m\|w_m\|\leq M\), then
\[
 \left|
 \|Q_Nx+Q_Nw_m\|^2-\|Q_Nx\|^2-\|Q_Nw_m\|^2
 \right|
 \leq 2M\|Q_Nx\|.
\]
Letting first \(m\to\U\) and then \(N\to\infty\) gives (1).  Consequently
\[
  X^\U = j_\U(X)\oplus_2\ker\Pi
\]
isometrically.

Finally, a point \((x,0)\) in an \(\ell_2\)-sum \(X\oplus_2 Z\) is Frechet
smooth whenever \(x\in S_X\) is Frechet smooth.  To see this, use the dual
decomposition \(X^*\oplus_2 Z^*\) and Smulyan's criterion: if
\((f_k,g_k)\in S_{X^*\oplus_2 Z^*}\) satisfies \(f_k(x)\to1\), then
\(\|f_k\|\to1\), \(\|g_k\|\to0\), and the normalized \(f_k/\|f_k\|\) converge
in norm to the unique support functional of \(x\).  Hence
\((f_k,g_k)\) converges in norm to the unique support functional of \((x,0)\).
Applying this to \(X^\U=j_\U(X)\oplus_2\ker\Pi\) proves that \(j_\U(x)\) is
Frechet smooth for every \(x\in S_X\).
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Limitations and novelty check.}
This does not settle whether the source hypothesis forces reflexivity or any
other precise geometric property.  It only eliminates superreflexivity as a
necessary consequence.  Cheap run indexes and web searches for the exact
phrases ``Frechet smooth canonical ultrapower'', ``Frechet smooth ultrapower
superreflexive'', and variants did not reveal this explicit example as an
existing answer.
