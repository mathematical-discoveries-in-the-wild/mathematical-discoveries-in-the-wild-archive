\section*{Source and Open Question}

This packet concerns Open Problem (5) from:

\begin{quote}
William B. Johnson and Tomasz Kania, \emph{Uniform Property (S)},
arXiv:2602.09106.
\end{quote}

The source location is the open-problems section of the paper.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

Problem (5) asks:

\begin{quote}
If $X$ has uniform property $(S)$, must $X^{**}$ have it too?
\end{quote}

We give a negative answer, in the real Banach-space category.

\section*{Definitions}

For a normed space $E$, the paper defines
\[
  U_E(x,y;a)
  =
  \sup_{\norm{z}\le a}
  \left|\,\norm{x+z}-\norm{y+z}\,\right|
\]
for $x,y\in S_E$ and $a>0$, and
\[
  U_E(d;a)
  =
  \inf_{\substack{x,y\in S_E\\ \norm{x-y}\ge d}}
  U_E(x,y;a).
\]
The space $E$ has uniform property $(S)$ precisely when
$U_E(d;a)>0$ for every $0<d<2$ and every $a>0$.

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
There is a Banach space $X$ with uniform property $(S)$ such that
$X^{**}$ fails uniform property $(S)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
  X=L_1[0,1]
\]
over the real scalars. By Theorem 1.2 of the source paper,
$L_1(\mu)$ has uniform property $(S)$ whenever $\mu$ is atomless.
Thus $X$ has uniform property $(S)$.

We now show that $X^{**}$ does not. Since $X^*=L_\infty[0,1]$, we
have
\[
  X^{**}=(L_\infty[0,1])^*.
\]
Represent the commutative unital Banach lattice algebra
$L_\infty[0,1]$ as $C(K)$ for its Stone/Gelfand compact space $K$.
The space $K$ has at least two distinct points: for instance, the
non-trivial idempotent $\chi_{[0,1/2]}$ gives a non-trivial clopen
set in the Stone representation. Hence
\[
  X^{**}\cong C(K)^*=M(K),
\]
the Banach lattice of regular signed measures on $K$ with the
total-variation norm, and there exist two distinct Dirac measures
$\delta_s,\delta_t$ in $M(K)$.

Define two positive norm-one measures
\[
  u=\frac34\delta_s+\frac14\delta_t,
  \qquad
  v=\frac14\delta_s+\frac34\delta_t.
\]
Then
\[
  \norm{u-v}
  =
  \left|\frac34-\frac14\right|
  +
  \left|\frac14-\frac34\right|
  =
  1.
\]
We claim that
\[
  U_{X^{**}}(u,v;1/8)=0.
\]
Let $z\in M(K)$ satisfy $\norm{z}\le 1/8$. Write
\[
  z=\alpha\delta_s+\beta\delta_t+z_0,
\]
where $z_0$ has no mass at $s$ or $t$. Then
$|\alpha|\le 1/8$ and $|\beta|\le 1/8$. In particular, all four
numbers
\[
  \frac34+\alpha,\quad \frac14+\beta,\quad
  \frac14+\alpha,\quad \frac34+\beta
\]
are positive. Since the atoms $\{s\}$, $\{t\}$, and
$K\setminus\{s,t\}$ are disjoint for the variation norm,
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \norm{u+z}
  &=
  \left(\frac34+\alpha\right)
  +
  \left(\frac14+\beta\right)
  +
  \norm{z_0}       \\
  &=1+\alpha+\beta+\norm{z_0},
\end{aligned}
\]
and similarly
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \norm{v+z}
  &=
  \left(\frac14+\alpha\right)
  +
  \left(\frac34+\beta\right)
  +
  \norm{z_0}       \\
  &=1+\alpha+\beta+\norm{z_0}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Thus $\norm{u+z}=\norm{v+z}$ for every $z$ with $\norm{z}\le 1/8$.
Consequently,
\[
  U_{X^{**}}(u,v;1/8)=0.
\]
Since $u,v\in S_{X^{**}}$ and $\norm{u-v}=1$, this implies
\[
  U_{X^{**}}(1;1/8)=0.
\]
Therefore $X^{**}$ fails uniform property $(S)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof is exact. The script
\texttt{code/check\_atomic\_band.py} samples perturbations in the
finite-dimensional model $\ell_1^2\oplus_1\R^k$ and checks the identity
\[
  \norm{u+z}_1=\norm{v+z}_1
  \qquad(\norm{z}_1\le 1/8)
\]
for the same atom coefficients. This is only a sanity check; the
measure-theoretic calculation above is the certificate.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This packet answers Problem (5) as stated for real Banach spaces. The
example is deliberately classical: $X=L_1[0,1]$ is one of the paper's
basic positive examples, and the failure in the bidual is caused by two
Dirac atoms in the measure representation of $(L_\infty[0,1])^*$.

I have not performed a literature search beyond the source paper and the
local pipeline corpus, so the novelty status is ``candidate'' pending
human and literature review.
