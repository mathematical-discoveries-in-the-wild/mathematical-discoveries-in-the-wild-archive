\section*{Source and Open Question}

The source paper is Han--Hu--Larson--Liu, \emph{Dilations for operator-valued
quantum measures}, arXiv:2109.09042 \cite{HHLiu}.  Its abstract states that a
general projection-valued dilation is known for bounded quantum measures on
projection lattices of von Neumann algebras with no type \(I_2\) direct summand,
but that such a dilation need not preserve countable additivity.  The extracted
open question is:

\begin{quote}
Does every countably additive \(B(X)\)-valued quantum measure on the projection
lattice of a von Neumann algebra with no type \(I_2\) direct summand admit a
countably additive projection-valued dilation?
\end{quote}

The source paper proves this under extra hypotheses, namely \(X=\ell_p\) for
\(1\leq p<2\), \(X\) has the Schur property, or the measure has bounded
\(p\)-variation.  The result below removes those extra hypotheses.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The obstruction in the elementary dilation norm of \cite{HHLiu} is uniformity:
after cutting by a tail projection \(q_n\downarrow0\), one has to control
\(\overline U(aq_n)x\) uniformly in \(\|a\|\leq1\).  Rather than force this in
the original Banach space \(X\), place the dilation vectors in the von Neumann
predual \(M_*\).  For each \(x\in X\), the normal map
\(a\mapsto \overline U(a)x\) gives a weakly compact operator
\[
        T x:X^*\longrightarrow M_* .
\]
Right multiplication by projections on \(M_*\) is automatically countably
additive on weakly compact subsets, by the Akemann--Jarchow weak compactness
criterion.  This supplies exactly the missing uniform tail estimate, while
evaluation at the identity compresses the dilation back to \(X\).

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(M\) be a sigma-finite von Neumann algebra with no direct summand of type
\(I_2\), let \(X\) be a Banach space, and let
\[
        U:\cP(M)\longrightarrow B(X)
\]
be a bounded countably additive operator-valued quantum measure.  Then there are
a Banach space \(Z\), bounded operators \(T:X\to Z\) and \(S:Z\to X\), and a
countably additive projection-valued quantum measure
\[
        V:\cP(M)\longrightarrow B(Z)
\]
such that
\[
        U(p)=S V(p)T\qquad (p\in\cP(M)).
\]
In fact, the dilation extends to a normal bounded homomorphism
\(\pi:M\to B(Z)\) satisfying \(\overline U(a)=S\pi(a)T\) for every \(a\in M\),
where \(\overline U:M\to B(X)\) is the normal Bunce--Wright extension of \(U\).
\end{theorem}

\section*{External Lemma Used}

We use the following standard form of the Akemann--Jarchow weak compactness
criterion for von Neumann preduals; see Akemann \cite{Akemann}, Jarchow
\cite{Jarchow}, and the modern JBW\(^*\)-triple formulation in
Hamhalter--Kalenda--Peralta--Pfitzner \cite[Theorem 11.3]{HKPP}.

\begin{lemma}[uniform one-sided absolute continuity]\label{lem:uniform}
Let \(M\) be a von Neumann algebra and let \(K\subset M_*\) be relatively weakly
compact.  If \((q_n)\) is a decreasing sequence of projections in \(M\) with
\(q_n\downarrow0\) strongly, then
\[
        \sup_{\rho\in K}\|\rho\cdot q_n\|\longrightarrow0,
        \qquad
        \sup_{\rho\in K}\|q_n\cdot\rho\|\longrightarrow0,
\]
where \((\rho\cdot q)(a)=\rho(aq)\) and \((q\cdot\rho)(a)=\rho(qa)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch of Lemma \ref{lem:uniform}]
One form of the Akemann--Jarchow criterion says that for a relatively weakly
compact \(K\subset M_*\) and every \(\varepsilon>0\), there is a positive normal
functional \(\varphi\in M_*^+\) and \(\delta>0\) such that
\[
        \|a\|\leq1,\quad \varphi(a^*a)<\delta
        \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
        |\rho(a)|<\varepsilon\quad(\rho\in K).
\]
Since \(q_n\downarrow0\), normality gives \(\varphi(q_n)\to0\).  For
\(\|b\|\leq1\),
\[
        \varphi((bq_n)^*(bq_n))=\varphi(q_n b^*b q_n)\leq \varphi(q_n),
\]
so \(\sup_{\rho\in K,\|b\|\leq1}|\rho(bq_n)|\to0\), which is exactly
\(\sup_{\rho\in K}\|\rho\cdot q_n\|\to0\).  The left-hand version follows by
applying the same argument in the opposite von Neumann algebra.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of the Theorem}

By the Bunce--Wright extension theorem as used in \cite{HHLiu}, \(U\) extends
uniquely to a bounded linear map
\[
        \overline U:M\longrightarrow B(X).
\]
Because \(U\) is countably additive and \(M\) is sigma-finite, the extension is
normal in the weak-operator sense: for every \(x\in X\) and \(x^*\in X^*\), the
functional
\[
        a\longmapsto x^*(\overline U(a)x)
\]
belongs to \(M_*\).

Let \(\cW=\cW(X^*,M_*)\) denote the Banach space of weakly compact operators from
\(X^*\) to \(M_*\), with the usual operator norm.  For \(a\in M\), define
\(\pi(a):\cW\to\cW\) by right multiplication on \(M_*\):
\[
        (\pi(a)A)(x^*)=A(x^*)\cdot a,\qquad
        ((\rho\cdot a)(b)=\rho(ba)).
\]
Then \(\|\pi(a)\|\leq\|a\|\), and \(\pi(ab)=\pi(a)\pi(b)\).  Hence
\(\pi:M\to B(\cW)\) is a bounded homomorphism.  In particular, for
\(p\in\cP(M)\), \(\pi(p)\) is an idempotent and
\(\pi(p+q)=\pi(p)+\pi(q)\) whenever \(p\perp q\).

For \(x\in X\), define \(T_0x:X^*\to M_*\) by
\[
        (T_0x)(x^*)(a)=x^*(\overline U(a)x),\qquad a\in M.
\]
This is bounded with \(\|T_0x\|\leq\|\overline U\|\,\|x\|\).  It is weakly
compact: the unit ball of \(X^*\), with its weak-star topology, is compact, and
the map \(x^*\mapsto (T_0x)(x^*)\) is weak-star to weak continuous because
evaluation at each \(a\in M\) is the scalar map
\[
        x^*\longmapsto x^*(\overline U(a)x).
\]
Thus \(T_0x\in\cW\).

Let
\[
        Z=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\{\pi(a)T_0x:\ a\in M,\ x\in X\}
        \subseteq \cW,
\]
and let \(T:X\to Z\) be \(T=T_0\).  Define \(S\) first on the algebraic span by
\[
        S\left(\sum_{j=1}^m \pi(a_j)T x_j\right)
        =\sum_{j=1}^m \overline U(a_j)x_j .
\]
This is well-defined.  Indeed, if the vector inside \(Z\) is zero, then for every
\(x^*\in X^*\),
\[
        0=\left(\sum_j \pi(a_j)T x_j\right)(x^*)(1)
        =x^*\left(\sum_j \overline U(a_j)x_j\right),
\]
so the displayed vector in \(X\) is zero.  Moreover,
\[
\begin{aligned}
\left\|S\left(\sum_j \pi(a_j)T x_j\right)\right\|
&=\sup_{\|x^*\|\leq1}
 \left|\left(\sum_j \pi(a_j)T x_j\right)(x^*)(1)\right|  \\
&\leq
\left\|\sum_j \pi(a_j)T x_j\right\|_{\cW}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Therefore \(S\) extends to a contraction \(S:Z\to X\).

For every \(a\in M\) and \(x\in X\),
\[
        S\pi(a)Tx=\overline U(a)x.
\]
Indeed, applying \(x^*\in X^*\) to the left side gives
\[
        (\pi(a)Tx)(x^*)(1)=(Tx)(x^*)(a)=x^*(\overline U(a)x).
\]

It remains to check countable additivity on projections.  Let
\((p_j)_{j\geq1}\) be mutually orthogonal projections, and put
\[
        p=\sum_{j=1}^\infty p_j,\qquad
        q_n=p-\sum_{j=1}^n p_j .
\]
Then \(q_n\downarrow0\) strongly.  For any \(A\in\cW\), the set
\(K=A(\Ball_{X^*})\) is relatively weakly compact in \(M_*\), so Lemma
\ref{lem:uniform} yields
\[
        \|\pi(q_n)A\|
        =\sup_{\|x^*\|\leq1}\|A(x^*)\cdot q_n\|
        =\sup_{\rho\in K}\|\rho\cdot q_n\|\longrightarrow0.
\]
Consequently \(\pi(p)A=\sum_{j=1}^{\infty}\pi(p_j)A\) in norm for every
\(A\in Z\).  Thus
\[
        V(p)=\pi(p)|_Z,\qquad p\in\cP(M),
\]
is a countably additive projection-valued quantum measure on \(Z\).  The
compression identity \(U(p)=S V(p)T\) follows from the identity already proved
for all \(a\in M\).

Finally, the same estimate shows that \(\pi\) is normal in the strong operator
sense relevant to the projection lattice: decreasing projection tails act
strongly as zero on \(Z\).  This is stronger than what is needed for the stated
dilation problem.
\qed

\section*{Verification Notes}

No computational search was used.  The proof reduces the source problem to a
standard weak compactness criterion for subsets of \(M_*\).  The key checks for a
human reviewer are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \(T_0x:X^*\to M_*\) is weakly compact because it is weak-star to weak
continuous on the weak-star compact unit ball of \(X^*\).
\item The compression \(S\) is well-defined and contractive because evaluation at
the identity is bounded on \(M_*\).
\item Countable additivity is exactly Lemma \ref{lem:uniform} applied to the
weakly compact set \(A(\Ball_{X^*})\).
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Novelty Check and Limitations}

I searched the run indexes for arXiv:2109.09042 and for the phrases
``operator-valued quantum measure'', ``projection-valued quantum measure'',
``countably additive'', ``Naimark'', ``Stinespring'', and ``dilation''.  No
existing packet or attempt for this source paper was present.  I also performed a
bounded web search for exact phrases from the question and for later papers on
countably additive projection-valued dilations of operator-valued quantum
measures.  I found the source paper and background weak compactness literature,
but no later paper explicitly resolving this exact question.

The result is therefore recorded as a candidate full solution, subject to human
review.  The main review focus should be the exact form of the
Akemann--Jarchow criterion in Lemma \ref{lem:uniform}; once that standard lemma is
accepted, the dilation construction is direct.
