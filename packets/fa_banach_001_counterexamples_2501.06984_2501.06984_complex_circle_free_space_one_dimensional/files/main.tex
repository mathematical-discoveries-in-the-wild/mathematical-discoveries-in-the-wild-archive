\section*{Source Question}

The source is Valentin Ferenczi, Pedro L. Kaufmann, and Eva Perneck\'a,
\emph{Equivariant liftings in Lipschitz-free spaces}, arXiv:2501.06984.  In Section 3.2
the authors define, for a pointed metric space \(M\) with an isometric action of the unit
circle \(\T\), the complex quotient
\[
Y_\T=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\{\delta(e^{i\theta}.m)-\cos\theta\,\delta(m)
-\sin\theta\,\delta(i.m):m\in M,\ \theta\in\mathbb R\}
\]
and
\[
\F_\C(M)=\F(M)/Y_\T,
\]
with complex scalar multiplication determined by
\[
i\bigl(\delta(m)+Y_\T\bigr)=\delta(i.m)+Y_\T.
\]
Writing \(\delta_\C=\pi_\C\delta\), the paper then asks:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Question 23.} Assume \(\{0\}\cup\T\) is equipped with the natural action of \(\T\).
Is the space \(\F_\C(\{0\}\cup\T)\) isomorphic to \(L_1([0,1],\C)\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Answer}

Under the natural realization of \(\{0\}\cup\T\) as a pointed subset of the complex plane
and the action \(e^{i\theta}.z=e^{i\theta}z\), the answer is negative.  More precisely,
\(\F_\C(\{0\}\cup\T)\) is complex one-dimensional.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The quotient defining \(\F_\C(M)\) is designed to force the circle action to agree with
complex scalar multiplication.  When \(M=\{0\}\cup\T\), the whole nonzero part of \(M\)
is a single \(\T\)-orbit.  Therefore every generator \(\delta_\C(e^{i\theta})\) is forced
to equal the scalar multiple \(e^{i\theta}\delta_\C(1)\).  Thus the complex free space has
only one possible generator.  The source paper's Observation 21 then says this generator is
not zero when \(M\subset \C\) carries the inherited action.

\section*{Theorem and Proof}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(M=\{0\}\cup\T\subset\C\), pointed at \(0\), with its usual metric and the usual circle
action \(e^{i\theta}.z=e^{i\theta}z\).  Then \(\F_\C(M)\) is complex-linearly isomorphic
to \(\C\).  In particular, \(\F_\C(\{0\}\cup\T)\) is not isomorphic to \(L_1([0,1],\C)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
u=\delta_\C(1)=\delta(1)+Y_\T\in \F_\C(M).
\]
The defining relation for \(Y_\T\), applied to \(m=1\), gives for every \(\theta\in\mathbb R\)
\[
\delta_\C(e^{i\theta})
=\cos\theta\,\delta_\C(1)+\sin\theta\,\delta_\C(i).
\]
By the definition of the complex structure on \(\F_\C(M)\), we have
\[
\delta_\C(i)=i\,\delta_\C(1)=iu.
\]
Therefore
\[
\delta_\C(e^{i\theta})
=\cos\theta\,u+\sin\theta\,iu
=e^{i\theta}u .
\]
Also \(\delta_\C(0)=0\), since \(0\) is the base point of the Lipschitz-free space.  Hence
all generators \(\delta_\C(M)\) lie in the one-dimensional complex subspace \(\C u\).
Because \(\F_\C(M)\) is the closed complex linear span of \(\delta_\C(M)\), it follows that
\[
\dim_\C \F_\C(M)\leq 1.
\]

It remains only to rule out the zero space.  The source paper proves in Observation 21 that
if \(M\) is a subset of a complex Banach space \(X\) and the \(\T\)-action on \(M\) is induced
by the complex scalar multiplication of \(X\), then \(\delta_\C:M\to\F_\C(M)\) is isometric.
Here \(M\subset \C\), so this applies.  In particular,
\[
\|u\|=\|\delta_\C(1)-\delta_\C(0)\|=|1-0|=1,
\]
and \(u\neq 0\).  Thus \(\F_\C(M)=\C u\), a one-dimensional complex Banach space.

The Banach space \(L_1([0,1],\C)\) is infinite-dimensional, so it cannot be linearly
isomorphic to \(\F_\C(M)\).  This gives a negative answer to Question 23 under the natural
metric/action interpretation.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The dimension collapse is purely algebraic inside the quotient: for any metric on an abstract
pointed \(\T\)-orbit for which the construction is defined, the same computation gives
\(\dim_\C \F_\C(\{0\}\cup\T)\leq 1\).  The nonzero conclusion above uses the source paper's
Observation 21, hence the natural interpretation that \(\{0\}\cup\T\) is the subset of the
complex Banach space \(\C\) with the induced metric and scalar action.
