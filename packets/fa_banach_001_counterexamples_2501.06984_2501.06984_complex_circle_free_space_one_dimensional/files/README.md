## Source

- Paper: Valentin Ferenczi, Pedro L. Kaufmann, Eva Pernecka, "Equivariant liftings in Lipschitz-free spaces"
- arXiv: `2501.06984`
- Source target: Question 23 asks whether `F_C({0} union T)` is isomorphic to `L_1([0,1], C)`.

## Result

Under the natural realization of `{0} union T` as a pointed subset of the complex plane with
the usual circle action, the answer is negative.

In fact, the quotient definition of `F_C(M)` collapses the whole circle orbit to the complex
line generated by `delta_C(1)`:

```text
delta_C(e^{it}) = cos(t) delta_C(1) + sin(t) delta_C(i)
                = e^{it} delta_C(1).
```

Since `delta_C({0} union T)` generates `F_C({0} union T)` as a complex Banach space, the
space is at most one-dimensional.  It is nonzero by the source paper's Observation 21, because
`{0} union T` is a subset of the complex Banach space `C` and `delta_C` is isometric in this
case.  Hence `F_C({0} union T)` is complex one-dimensional, and therefore cannot be
isomorphic to the infinite-dimensional space `L_1([0,1], C)`.

## Scope

This packet addresses Question 23 with the standard metric/action interpretation used by the
surrounding discussion: `{0} union T` is the pointed subset of `C`, and `T` acts by complex
multiplication.  If a different metric on the abstract orbit is intended, the dimensional
collapse still gives `dim_C F_C({0} union T) <= 1`, but the nonzero/isometric conclusion
requires a separate nondegeneracy check.
