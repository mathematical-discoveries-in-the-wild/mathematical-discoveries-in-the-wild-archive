\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Bauschke--Borwein--Wang--Yao,
\emph{Construction of pathological maximally monotone operators on
non-reflexive Banach spaces}, arXiv:1108.1463. Near the end of the
paper the authors list four open questions:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

This packet concerns question (iii):
\[
\text{Is every maximally monotone linear relation necessarily of type (ANA)?}
\]
Here type (ANA) means ``almost negative alignment''. In the notation of
Simons \cite[definition labeled \texttt{ANAdef}]{SimonsDensities},
a maximally monotone subset \(A\subset E\times E^*\) is of type (ANA) if,
whenever \((w,w^*)\notin A\), there are graph points
\((s,s^*)\in A\) with \(s\ne w\), \(s^*\ne w^*\), and
\[
\frac{\ip{s-w}{s^*-w^*}}{\norm{s-w}\norm{s^*-w^*}}
\]
as close to \(-1\) as desired.

\section*{Result Type}

This is a \textbf{partial result}. It proves that any possible failure
of type (ANA) for a maximally monotone linear relation must occur at a
boundary point of its domain. In particular, it proves the source
question for all maximally monotone linear relations with closed domain.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Let \(A:X\rightrightarrows X^*\) be a maximally monotone linear relation,
put \(D=\dom A\), and let \(M=\overline D\). Maximality gives
\[
A(0)=M^\perp.
\]
Thus the fibers of \(A\), after restriction to \(M\), are single-valued:
there is a linear monotone operator
\[
T:D\subset M\to M^*,\qquad T x=x^*\restr M\quad(x^*\in Ax),
\]
and each fiber \(Ax\) consists exactly of all Hahn--Banach extensions of
\(Tx\) from \(M\) to \(X\).

The ANA construction then splits into two robust cases. If
\(w\notin M\), the positive quotient distance from \(w\) to \(M\)
produces a norming annihilator \(n^*\in M^\perp\); adding a large
multiple of \(n^*\) in the fiber forces the angle to tend to \(-1\). If
\(w\in D\), a small-scaling argument on the normed space \(D\) gives
negative alignment for the induced single-valued operator, and
Hahn--Banach lifts it back to \(X^*\) without changing the numerator or
the relevant norm.

The only place where both mechanisms disappear is the genuine boundary
case \(w\in\overline{\dom A}\setminus\dom A\).

\section*{Main Reduction}

\begin{theorem}[Boundary reduction]
Let \(X\) be a real Banach space and let \(A:X\rightrightarrows X^*\) be
a maximally monotone linear relation. Put \(D=\dom A\) and
\(M=\overline D\). Then \(A\) has the type-(ANA) conclusion at every
\((w,w^*)\in X\times X^*\setminus\gra A\) such that either
\[
w\notin M \qquad\text{or}\qquad w\in D.
\]
Consequently, any counterexample to the assertion that every maximally
monotone linear relation is of type (ANA) must fail at a point
\[
w\in \overline{\dom A}\setminus\dom A.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since \(A\) is a linear relation, monotonicity is equivalent to
\[
\ip{x}{x^*}\ge 0\qquad ((x,x^*)\in \gra A).
\]
We first identify the multivalued part. If \(u^*\in A(0)\), then for
each \((x,x^*)\in\gra A\) and every \(t\in\R\),
\((x,x^*+tu^*)\in\gra A\). Hence
\[
0\le \ip{x}{x^*+tu^*}\qquad(t\in\R),
\]
which forces \(\ip{x}{u^*}=0\) for all \(x\in D\). Thus
\(A(0)\subset D^\perp=M^\perp\). Conversely, if \(u^*\in M^\perp\), then
for every \((x,x^*)\in\gra A\),
\[
\ip{0-x}{u^*-x^*}
=-\ip{x}{u^*}+\ip{x}{x^*}
=\ip{x}{x^*}\ge 0.
\]
Thus \((0,u^*)\) is monotonically related to \(\gra A\), and maximality
gives \(u^*\in A(0)\). Therefore
\[
A(0)=M^\perp. \tag{1}
\]

Define \(T:D\to M^*\) by
\[
T x=x^*\restr M\qquad\text{for any }x^*\in A x.
\]
This is well-defined by (1). It is linear and monotone. Moreover, for
\(x\in D\), the fiber \(Ax\) consists exactly of all extensions of
\(Tx\) from \(M\) to \(X\): one inclusion is the definition of \(T\), and
the other follows because two extensions differ by an element of
\(M^\perp=A(0)\).

The induced operator \(T\) is maximally monotone as a relation in
\(M\times M^*\). Indeed, suppose \((y,y^*)\in M\times M^*\) is
monotonically related to \(\gra T\). Choose an extension
\(x^*\in X^*\) of \(y^*\). Then \((y,x^*)\) is monotonically related to
\(\gra A\), so maximality of \(A\) gives \((y,x^*)\in\gra A\). Hence
\(y\in D\) and \(y^*=Ty\).

Fix \((w,w^*)\notin\gra A\) and \(0<\eps<1\).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Case 1: \(w\notin M\).}
Let \(\delta=\dist(w,M)>0\). By Hahn--Banach in the quotient \(X/M\),
choose \(n^*\in M^\perp\) with \(\norm{n^*}=1\) and
\(\ip{w}{n^*}=\delta\). Since \(D\) is dense in \(M\), choose \(s\in D\)
such that
\[
\norm{s-w}<\frac{\delta}{1-\eps/2}.
\]
Then \(\ip{s-w}{n^*}=-\delta\). Pick any \(a^*\in A s\). For
\(\lambda>0\), put \(s^*_\lambda=a^*+\lambda n^*\). By (1),
\(s^*_\lambda\in A s\). Consequently
\[
\frac{\ip{s-w}{s^*_\lambda-w^*}}
{\norm{s-w}\norm{s^*_\lambda-w^*}}
=
\frac{\ip{s-w}{a^*-w^*}-\lambda\delta}
{\norm{s-w}\norm{a^*-w^*+\lambda n^*}}
\longrightarrow
-\frac{\delta}{\norm{s-w}}
<-1+\frac{\eps}{2}.
\]
For large \(\lambda\), this ratio is \(<-1+\eps\).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Case 2: \(w\in D\).}
Let \(z^*=Tw-w^*\restr M\in M^*\). If \(z^*=0\), then \(w^*\) is an
extension of \(Tw\), hence \(w^*\in Aw\), contrary to
\((w,w^*)\notin\gra A\). Thus \(z^*\ne0\).

Because \(D\) is dense in \(M\), the norm of \(z^*\) is computed on
vectors from \(D\). Choose \(h\in D\) with \(\norm h=1\) and
\[
\ip{h}{z^*}<-(1-\eps/2)\norm{z^*}.
\]
For \(t>0\), set \(s_t=w+th\in D\). The corresponding functional on
\(M\) is
\[
T s_t-w^*\restr M=z^*+tTh.
\]
Choose \(u_t^*\in X^*\) extending \(z^*+tTh\) from \(M\) to \(X\) with
\[
\norm{u_t^*}_{X^*}=\norm{z^*+tTh}_{M^*},
\]
and put \(s_t^*=w^*+u_t^*\). Then \(s_t^*\) extends \(T s_t\), so
\(s_t^*\in A s_t\). Also \(s_t\ne w\) and \(s_t^*\ne w^*\) for small
\(t>0\). Finally,
\[
\frac{\ip{s_t-w}{s_t^*-w^*}_{X,X^*}}
{\norm{s_t-w}\norm{s_t^*-w^*}_{X^*}}
=
\frac{\ip{th}{z^*+tTh}_{M,M^*}}
{t\norm h\,\norm{z^*+tTh}_{M^*}}
\longrightarrow
\frac{\ip{h}{z^*}}{\norm{z^*}}
<-1+\frac{\eps}{2}.
\]
For sufficiently small \(t>0\), the ratio is \(<-1+\eps\).

These two cases prove the claimed type-(ANA) conclusion away from the
boundary \(M\setminus D\). The final statement follows immediately.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Closed-domain subcase]
Let \(A:X\rightrightarrows X^*\) be a maximally monotone linear relation.
If \(\dom A\) is closed, then \(A\) is of type \((ANA)\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If \(D=\dom A\) is closed, then \(M=\overline D=D\). Every point
\((w,w^*)\notin\gra A\) has either \(w\notin M\) or \(w\in D\), so the
theorem applies.
\end{proof}

\section*{Relation To Known Literature}

Simons proves that every closed monotone quasidense set is of type (ANA)
\cite[theorem labeled \texttt{ANAthm}]{SimonsDensities}. For linear
relations, quasidensity is equivalent to monotonicity of the adjoint in
the same paper \cite[theorem labeled \texttt{LINMONthm}]{SimonsDensities}.
The reduction above is different: it does not assume quasidensity, and
the closed-domain corollary includes non-quasidense full-domain examples
such as the tail/Gossez-type operators.

The later survey \cite{SimonsSurvey} records that Bauschke--Simons
proved arbitrary-domain maximally monotone linear operators are strongly
maximal, and also that convex-graph maximally monotone multifunctions
are strongly maximal. This is related but does not by itself give the
type-(ANA) conclusion used here.

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not settle the source question. It reduces the possible
failure to the case
\[
w\in\overline{\dom A}\setminus\dom A.
\]
Equivalently, after replacing \(X\) by \(M=\overline{\dom A}\), the
remaining problem is the dense, non-full-domain case for a single-valued
maximally monotone linear operator \(T:D\subset M\to M^*\).
