\section*{Status}

This packet records a likely valid partial result related to Talponen's
question in \cite[Section 2.1]{TalponenDES}: what kind of Banach spaces admit a
continuous non-degenerate inner product?  The source proves that
\(\omega^*\)-separable dual is sufficient and notes that a continuous inner
product yields a continuous linear injection into some \(\cnull(\GammaSet)\).
The result below gives a natural obstruction and shows that the latter
\(\cnull(\GammaSet)\)-injection condition is not sufficient.

\section*{Source Question}

In the source, a continuous non-degenerate inner product on a Banach space
\(X\) is a positive definite bilinear form whose induced seminorm is bounded by
the original norm.  After Proposition 2.5, the source says that it is not known
exactly which Banach spaces admit such an inner product.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

A continuous inner product on \(X\) is the same thing as a bounded linear
injection from \(X\) into a Hilbert space: complete \(X\) in the Hilbert norm
induced by the inner product.  For \(X=\cnull(\GammaSet)\), any bounded operator
into a Hilbert space sends the coordinate unit vectors to a Hilbert family with
uniformly bounded signed finite sums.  Averaging over random signs turns that
uniform bound into a square-summability estimate.  Hence only countably many
coordinate vectors can have nonzero image.  If \(\GammaSet\) is uncountable,
injectivity is impossible.

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:operator}
Let \(\GammaSet\) be any set, \(H\) a Hilbert space, and
\(T:\cnull(\GammaSet)\to H\) a bounded linear operator.  Then
\[
  \{\gamma\in\GammaSet: T e_\gamma\neq 0\}
\]
is countable, where \(e_\gamma\) denotes the \(\gamma\)-th unit vector.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put \(x_\gamma=T e_\gamma\).  For every finite \(F\subset\GammaSet\) and every
choice of signs \(\varepsilon_\gamma\in\{-1,1\}\), one has
\[
  \left\|\sum_{\gamma\in F}\varepsilon_\gamma x_\gamma\right\|
  =
  \left\|T\left(\sum_{\gamma\in F}\varepsilon_\gamma e_\gamma\right)\right\|
  \leq \|T\|,
\]
because the signed coordinate sum has \(\cnull(\GammaSet)\)-norm one.
Averaging the square of this inequality over independent random signs gives
\[
  \sum_{\gamma\in F}\|x_\gamma\|^2
  =
  \mathbb E\left\|\sum_{\gamma\in F}\varepsilon_\gamma x_\gamma\right\|^2
  \leq \|T\|^2.
\]
Thus, for each \(n\in\N\), the set
\(\{\gamma:\|x_\gamma\|\geq 1/n\}\) is finite.  The union of these finite sets
over \(n\) contains every \(\gamma\) with \(x_\gamma\neq 0\), so the support of
the family \((x_\gamma)\) is countable.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:c0}
If \(\GammaSet\) is uncountable, then \(\cnull(\GammaSet)\) admits no
continuous non-degenerate inner product.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume first that \(B\) is a continuous non-degenerate inner product on
\(\cnull(\GammaSet)\).  By continuity, \(B(x,x)\leq C\|x\|_\infty^2\) for some
\(C<\infty\).  Let \(H\) be the Hilbert completion of
\(\cnull(\GammaSet)\) under the norm \(x\mapsto B(x,x)^{1/2}\), and let
\(J:\cnull(\GammaSet)\to H\) be the canonical map.  The displayed estimate
makes \(J\) bounded, and non-degeneracy makes it injective.

This contradicts Lemma \ref{lem:operator}, since no bounded operator from
\(\cnull(\GammaSet)\) into a Hilbert space can be injective when
\(\GammaSet\) is uncountable.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
The condition that a Banach space admit a continuous linear injection into
some \(\cnull(\GammaSet)\) is not sufficient for the existence of a continuous
non-degenerate inner product.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For uncountable \(\GammaSet\), the identity map is a continuous injection
\(\cnull(\GammaSet)\to\cnull(\GammaSet)\), while Theorem \ref{thm:c0} rules out
a continuous non-degenerate inner product on \(\cnull(\GammaSet)\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only the standard random-sign identity in Hilbert spaces.
No computational check is needed.  A local duplicate search in the run indexes
for the phrases ``continuous inner product'', ``Directionally Euclidean'',
``inject Hilbert'', and ``uncountable c0'' found no existing packet for this
obstruction.

\section*{Limitations}

This is a partial result.  It does not classify all Banach spaces admitting
continuous non-degenerate inner products.  It also does not answer the source's
separate question whether DES in all or some directions passes to subspaces,
ultrapowers, ultra-roots, or almost isometric copies.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Check the equivalence between continuous inner products and bounded Hilbert
injections, and the random-sign proof of Lemma \ref{lem:operator}.  If both are
accepted, this packet should be treated as a small but clean partial result for
the source's continuous-inner-product classification question.
