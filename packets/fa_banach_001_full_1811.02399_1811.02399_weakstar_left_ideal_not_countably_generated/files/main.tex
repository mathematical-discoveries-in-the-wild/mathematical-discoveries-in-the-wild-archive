\section*{Source question and theorem}

The source is Niels Jakob Laustsen and Jared T. White, \emph{Subspaces that can and cannot be the kernel of a bounded operator on a Banach space}, arXiv:1811.02399v3.  The paper asks which closed subspaces \(F\) of a Banach space \(E\) are kernels of bounded operators \(E\to E\).  It also uses this to produce a dual Banach algebra \(\B(E)\) which is not weak-star topologically left Noetherian.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

More precisely, let \(X\) be a non-separable reflexive Banach space with few operators in the sense that
\[
        \B(X)=\C\,\id_X+\mathscr X(X),
\]
where \(\mathscr X(X)\) is the ideal of operators with separable range.  Wark constructed such spaces, and the source paper takes \(E=X^*\).  Laustsen--White prove that \(\B(E)\) has a weak-star closed left ideal which is not weak-star topologically finitely generated.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The source proof uses a finite-intersection dichotomy: if a subspace \(D\subseteq E=X^*\) is a finite intersection of kernels of operators on \(E\), then either \(D\) or \(E/D\) is separable.  The same proof survives for countably many kernels.  The only change is that a countable closed linear span of separable ranges is still separable.

Laustsen--White choose a closed subspace \(D\subseteq E\) such that both \(D\) and \(E/D\) are non-separable.  The countable dichotomy therefore says that \(D\) is not a countable intersection of kernels.  But if their weak-star closed left ideal
\[
        \I_D=\{T\in\B(E):D\subseteq\ker T\}
\]
were weak-star countably generated by \(T_1,T_2,\ldots\), then the intersection of the kernels of all operators in \(\I_D\) would equal \(\bigcap_n\ker T_n\).  The left-hand side is exactly \(D\), contradicting the countable dichotomy.

\section*{Definitions}

Call a weak-star closed left ideal \(\I\) of a dual Banach algebra \(A\) \emph{weak-star topologically countably generated} if there are \(a_1,a_2,\ldots\in\I\) such that
\[
        \I=\overline{\operatorname{span}\{ba_n:b\in A,\ n\in\N\}}^{\,w^*}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Countable kernel-intersection dichotomy]
\label{lem:countable-dichotomy}
Let \(X\) be a non-separable reflexive Banach space such that
\[
        \B(X)=\C\,\id_X+\mathscr X(X),
\]
where \(\mathscr X(X)\) consists of the operators with separable range.  Put \(E=X^*\).  If \(T_1,T_2,\ldots\in\B(E)\) and
\[
        D=\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty\ker T_n,
\]
then either \(D\) or \(E/D\) is separable.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since \(X\) is reflexive, every \(T_n\in\B(E)=\B(X^*)\) is the adjoint of some \(S_n\in\B(X)\).  Let
\[
        F=D_\perp=\{x\in X: f(x)=0\text{ for every }f\in D\}.
\]
Using the standard annihilator identity,
\[
        F=\left(\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty\ker S_n^*\right)_\perp
          =\overline{\operatorname{span}}\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty \im S_n .
\]
Write \(S_n=\alpha_n\id_X+R_n\), where \(\alpha_n\in\C\) and \(R_n\in\mathscr X(X)\).

If \(\alpha_n=0\) for every \(n\), then \(F\) is the closed span of countably many separable subspaces, hence is separable.  Since \(F\) is a closed subspace of the reflexive space \(X\), it is reflexive, and therefore \(F^*\) is separable.  The quotient \(E/D\) is naturally isomorphic to \(F^*\), so \(E/D\) is separable.

If \(\alpha_m\ne0\) for some \(m\), then for every \(x\in X\),
\[
        x=\alpha_m^{-1}(S_mx-R_mx)\in F+\overline{\im R_m}.
\]
Thus \(X/F\) is a quotient of the separable space \(\overline{\im R_m}\), and hence \(X/F\) is separable.  Since \(X/F\) is reflexive, its dual is separable.  But \(D\) is naturally isomorphic to \((X/F)^*\), so \(D\) is separable.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:not-countably-generated}
Let \(X\) be one of Wark's non-separable reflexive Banach spaces with few operators, and put \(E=X^*\).  Then \(\B(E)\), with its canonical dual Banach algebra weak-star topology, has a weak-star closed left ideal which is not weak-star topologically countably generated.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The source paper proves, using a Markushevich-basis argument, that \(E\) contains a closed subspace \(D\) such that both \(D\) and \(E/D\) are non-separable.  Define
\[
        \I_D=\{T\in\B(E):D\subseteq\ker T\}.
\]
This is a left ideal.  It is weak-star closed because, under the standard predual \(E\widehat\otimes E^*\) of \(\B(E)\),
\[
        \I_D=\{d\otimes \lambda:d\in D,\ \lambda\in E^*\}^{\perp}.
\]

Assume, toward a contradiction, that \(\I_D\) is weak-star topologically countably generated by \(T_1,T_2,\ldots\in\I_D\).  We first record the kernel identity
\[
        \bigcap_{T\in\I_D}\ker T=\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty \ker T_n.
\]
The inclusion from left to right is immediate because \(T_n\in\I_D\).  Conversely, let \(x\in\bigcap_n\ker T_n\).  Every finite linear combination of operators of the form \(A T_n\), \(A\in\B(E)\), annihilates \(x\).  If \(T\in\I_D\), choose a net from this linear span converging to \(T\) in the weak-star topology.  Pairing with \(x\otimes\lambda\in E\widehat\otimes E^*\) for arbitrary \(\lambda\in E^*\) gives \(\lambda(Tx)=0\).  Hence \(Tx=0\), proving the reverse inclusion.

Finally,
\[
        \bigcap_{T\in\I_D}\ker T=D.
\]
Indeed \(D\) is contained in every kernel by definition of \(\I_D\).  If \(x\notin D\), choose \(\lambda\in E^*\) with \(\lambda|_D=0\) and \(\lambda(x)=1\).  The rank-one operator \(y\mapsto \lambda(y)x\) belongs to \(\I_D\), but it does not annihilate \(x\).

Thus \(D=\bigcap_n\ker T_n\).  Lemma~\ref{lem:countable-dichotomy} implies that either \(D\) or \(E/D\) is separable, contradicting the choice of \(D\).  Therefore \(\I_D\) is not weak-star topologically countably generated.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and novelty check}

The proof is a countable version of the finite argument in Laustsen--White, with the additional observation that countably many separable ranges still have separable closed span.  The theorem is strictly stronger than the source's failure of weak-star topological finite generation for the same ideal.

Bounded novelty checks used the run indexes for `1811.02399`, kernel-intersection terms, weak-star Noetherian terms, and countable-generation phrases.  Web searches for exact phrases such as ``weak-star topologically countably generated'', ``weak*-topologically countably generated'', and Laustsen--White/Wark countable-generation variants did not locate this strengthening.  A later paper, Arnott--Laustsen arXiv:2503.12940, studies kernels on classical transfinite sequence spaces, but its abstract concerns \(\ell_p(\Gamma)\), \(c_0(\Gamma)\), and \(\ell_1(\Gamma)\), not the Wark-dual weak-star ideal considered here.

\section*{Human review focus}

The main points to verify are the annihilator identity for countable intersections,
\[
        \left(\bigcap_n\ker S_n^*\right)_\perp
        =\overline{\operatorname{span}}\bigcup_n\im S_n,
\]
and the weak-star closure argument showing that countable generators force
\[
        \bigcap_{T\in\I_D}\ker T=\bigcap_n\ker T_n.
\]
Both are standard, but they are the exact places where the finite proof is extended.
