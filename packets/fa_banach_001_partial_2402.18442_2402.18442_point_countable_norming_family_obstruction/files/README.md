# Point-Countable Norming-Family Obstruction

Source paper: P. Hajek and T. Russo, "Norming Markushevich bases: recent
results and open problems", arXiv:2402.18442.

Status: likely valid partial/obstruction result for Problem 6.1. This packet
does not solve the heredity problem for norming M-bases. It disproves a
natural abstract reduction that arose during the run: a norming subspace
generated by a point-countable, even `c0`-valued, family of functionals need
not imply the existence of a norming M-basis.

## Result

There is a Banach space `Y` and a family `(g_alpha)_{alpha<omega_1}` in `Y*`
such that:

- `span{g_alpha}` is 1-norming for `Y`;
- for every `y in Y`, the support
  `{alpha<omega_1 : g_alpha(y) != 0}` is countable, indeed
  `(g_alpha(y))` belongs to `c0(omega_1)`;
- nevertheless `Y` has no norming M-basis.

Take

```text
Y = C_0([0, omega_1]) = {f in C([0, omega_1]) : f(omega_1)=0}
```

and `g_alpha = delta_alpha|_Y` for `alpha<omega_1`.

## Why This Matters

For any subspace `Z` of a Banach space with a norming M-basis, the restricted
coordinate functionals form a norming point-countable, in fact `c0`-valued,
family. It was tempting to try to prove that this abstract datum alone forces
a norming M-basis on `Z`.

This packet shows that the abstract datum alone is insufficient. Any proof of
Problem 6.1 must use more structure from the ambient norming M-basis than just
the restricted finite-span norming family and its point-countable supports.

## Human Review Recommendation

Review as a sharp obstruction to a reduction route, not as a counterexample
to Problem 6.1. The key checks are:

1. every function in `C_0([0,omega_1])` is eventually zero;
2. the point evaluations below `omega_1` are 1-norming for the sup norm;
3. `C_0([0,omega_1])` is isomorphic to `C([0,omega_1])`;
4. Alexandrov--Plichko's theorem rules out norming M-bases on that isomorphism
   class.
