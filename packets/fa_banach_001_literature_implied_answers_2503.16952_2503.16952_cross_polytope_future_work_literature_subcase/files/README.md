# Partial Literature Answer: Cross-Polytope Small-Radii Maximal Functions

Status: `literature_implied_answer (partial future-work subcase)`

Source target: arXiv:2503.16952, J. Niksinski and B. Wrobel,
*Dimension-free estimates for discrete maximal functions and lattice points in
high-dimensional spheres and balls with small radii*.

Source question/direction: Introduction, Remark 3, says that the authors'
methods should extend to maximal functions associated with other \(B^q\) balls,
with the supremum restricted to small radii, and that they plan to study this
in future work.

Identification: Kosz--Niksinski--Wrobel, arXiv:2604.15844, treats the
cross-polytope case, i.e. the \(q=1\) ball. Theorem 1.15 gives dimension-free
bounds for the full cross-polytope maximal function on \(\ell^2(\mathbb Z^d)\)
for small radii \(R \le d^{1-\varepsilon}\). The same paper also proves
large-scale comparison bounds (Theorem 1.14) and dyadic all-scale bounds
(Theorem 1.17).

Scope: this does not solve Stein's original Euclidean-ball problem from
arXiv:2503.16952, does not remove the epsilon in that paper's Theorems 1.1 and
1.2, and does not solve the future-work direction for all \(q\). It records a
later literature answer to the \(q=1\) cross-polytope subcase.
