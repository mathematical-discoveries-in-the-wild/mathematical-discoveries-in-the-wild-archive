\section{Source Question}

The source is Stefano Ciaci, Johann Langemets, and Aleksei Lissitsin,
\emph{A Characterization of Banach Spaces Containing
$\ell_1(\kappa)$ via ball-covering properties}, arXiv:2102.13525
\cite{CLL}.  Question 6.2 appears on page 15 of the compiled source paper:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The question asks whether, for an infinite cardinal $\kappa$, every
$\kappa$-octahedral Banach space contains an isomorphic copy of
$\ell_1(\kappa^+)$.

\section{Definitions and Source Input}

The source paper defines a normed space $X$ to be $\kappa$-octahedral if for
every subspace $Y\subset X$ with $\dens(Y)\leq \kappa$ and every
$\varepsilon>0$, there is $x\in S_X$ such that
\[
  \|\lambda x+y\|\geq (1-\varepsilon)(|\lambda|+\|y\|)
  \quad(\lambda\in\mathbb R,\ y\in Y).
\]

We use the construction from Theorem 5.13 of \cite{CLL}.  Fix an infinite
cardinal $\kappa$, put $\Gamma=\kappa^+$, choose
$\alpha_n\in(1/2,1)$ with $\sup_n\alpha_n=1$, and set
\[
  p_n=(1+\log_2(\alpha_n))^{-1}\in(1,\infty),\qquad
  X_n=\ell_{p_n}(\Gamma),\qquad
  X=\left(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty X_n\right)_{\ell_1}.
\]
Theorem 5.13 of \cite{CLL} proves that this space $X$ is
$\kappa$-octahedral.

\section{Proof Intuition}

The source construction is an $\ell_1$-sum of reflexive blocks whose exponents
approach the endpoint needed for $\kappa$-octahedrality.  The possible
embedding of $\ell_1(\kappa^+)$ has one vector $Te_\gamma$ for each
$\gamma<\kappa^+$.  Since each individual vector in an $\ell_1$-sum has small
tail after sufficiently many blocks, and since $\kappa^+$ is a regular
uncountable cardinal, a subfamily of size $\kappa^+$ has a common finite tail
cutoff.  On that subfamily the finite initial block projection remains bounded
below.  But a finite sum of the blocks is reflexive, so it cannot contain
$\ell_1$ of any infinite index set.  This rules out the embedding.

\section{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
For every infinite cardinal $\kappa$ there exists a $\kappa$-octahedral Banach
space which contains no isomorphic copy of $\ell_1(\kappa^+)$.  Consequently,
Question 6.2 of \cite{CLL} has a negative answer, already for
$\kappa=\omega$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $X$ be the space displayed above:
\[
  X=\left(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty \ell_{p_n}(\kappa^+)\right)_{\ell_1},
  \qquad 1<p_n<\infty .
\]
By Theorem 5.13 of \cite{CLL}, $X$ is $\kappa$-octahedral.  It remains to show
that $X$ does not contain an isomorphic copy of $\ell_1(\kappa^+)$.

Write $\Gamma=\kappa^+$ and suppose, toward a contradiction, that
$T:\ell_1(\Gamma)\to X$ is an isomorphic embedding.  Choose $c>0$ such that
\[
  \|Tu\|\geq c\|u\|_{\ell_1(\Gamma)}
  \qquad (u\in \ell_1(\Gamma)).
\]
For $N\in\mathbb N$, let $P_N:X\to \bigoplus_{n=1}^N \ell_{p_n}(\Gamma)$ be
the coordinate projection onto the first $N$ summands and set
$R_N=I-P_N$.  Fix $\eta$ with $0<\eta<c$.  For every $\gamma\in\Gamma$,
the vector $Te_\gamma$ belongs to the countable $\ell_1$-sum $X$, hence there
is $N_\gamma\in\mathbb N$ such that
\[
  \|R_{N_\gamma}Te_\gamma\|<\eta .
\]
Since the successor cardinal $\Gamma=\kappa^+$ is regular and uncountable,
one of the sets
\[
  \Lambda_N=\{\gamma\in\Gamma:N_\gamma\leq N\}
\]
has cardinality $\Gamma$.  Fix such an $N$ and put $\Lambda=\Lambda_N$.

For every finitely supported
$u=\sum_{\gamma\in F}a_\gamma e_\gamma$ with $F\subset\Lambda$, we have
\[
  \|R_NTu\|
  \leq \sum_{\gamma\in F}|a_\gamma|\,\|R_NTe_\gamma\|
  \leq \sum_{\gamma\in F}|a_\gamma|\,\|R_{N_\gamma}Te_\gamma\|
  < \eta\sum_{\gamma\in F}|a_\gamma|
  = \eta\|u\|_{\ell_1}.
\]
Therefore
\[
  \|P_NTu\|\geq \|Tu\|-\|R_NTu\|
  \geq (c-\eta)\|u\|_{\ell_1}.
\]
By density of finitely supported vectors, $P_NT$ restricts to an isomorphic
embedding of $\ell_1(\Lambda)$ into the finite direct sum
\[
  F_N=\left(\bigoplus_{n=1}^N \ell_{p_n}(\Gamma)\right)_{\ell_1}.
\]
Each space $\ell_{p_n}(\Gamma)$ is reflexive because $1<p_n<\infty$, and a
finite direct sum of reflexive spaces is reflexive.  Thus $F_N$ is reflexive.
Its closed subspace $(P_NT)(\ell_1(\Lambda))$ would also be reflexive, which is
impossible because $\ell_1(\Lambda)$ is nonreflexive for every infinite
$\Lambda$.  This contradiction proves that no such $T$ exists.
\end{proof}

\section{Limitations and Novelty Check}

Question 6.1 about equivalent renormings failing the $(-1)$-BCP$_\kappa$.

of the source paper, with the additional observation that it cannot contain
$\ell_1(\kappa^+)$.
