\section*{Status}

This packet records a candidate partial result, likely valid.  It gives a
permanence theorem for the gradient-cone property in Open Problem 4.5 of
Azagra--Jimenez-Sevilla, arXiv:math/0002108.  It does not solve the problem for
all separable reflexive spaces.

\section*{Source Context}

For a smooth bump \(b\) on a Banach space \(X\), Azagra and
Jimenez-Sevilla consider the cone
\[
  \C(b)=\{\lambda b'(x):\lambda\geq0,\ x\in X\}\subset X^*.
\]
They note that this cone is always norm dense, and ask how small it can be.
After proving that in \(X=\ell_2\) there is a \(C^1\) Lipschitz bump whose
gradient cone has empty interior, they formulate the following problem.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}[Complemented-summand transfer]
\label{thm:transfer}
Let \(X=Y\oplus Z\) be a Banach-space direct sum.  Suppose that \(Y\) admits a
\(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump \(b:Y\to\mathbb R\) such that
\(\C(b)\) has empty interior in \(Y^*\), and that \(Z\) admits a nonnegative
\(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump \(\eta:Z\to\mathbb R\).  Then \(X\) admits a
\(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump \(F:X\to\mathbb R\) such that
\(\C(F)\) has empty interior in \(X^*\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(y_0\in Y\) and \(z_0\in Z\) with \(b(y_0)\neq0\) and
\(\eta(z_0)>0\).  Define
\[
  F(y,z)=b(y)\eta(z),\qquad (y,z)\in Y\oplus Z.
\]
This is a nonzero \(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump: its support is contained in
\(\operatorname{supp} b\times \operatorname{supp}\eta\), hence is bounded in
the direct-sum norm, and products of bounded Lipschitz functions with bounded
support are Lipschitz.

Under the canonical identification \(X^*=Y^*\oplus Z^*\), the derivative is
\[
  F'(y,z)=\bigl(\eta(z)b'(y),\, b(y)\eta'(z)\bigr).
\]
Let \(P:X^*\to Y^*\) be the continuous open projection.  Since \(\eta\geq0\),
for every \(\lambda\geq0\) one has
\[
  P\bigl(\lambda F'(y,z)\bigr)
  =\lambda\eta(z)b'(y)\in \C(b).
\]
Thus \(P(\C(F))\subseteq \C(b)\).  If \(\C(F)\) had nonempty interior in
\(X^*\), then, because \(P\) is an open surjection, \(P(\C(F))\) would have
nonempty interior in \(Y^*\).  This contradicts the assumption that \(\C(b)\)
has empty interior.  Therefore \(\C(F)\) has empty interior in \(X^*\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Complemented Hilbert subspaces]
\label{cor:l2}
Let \(X\) be a Banach space with a \(C^1\) smooth Lipschitz bump.  If \(X\)
contains a complemented subspace isomorphic to \(\ell_2\), then \(X\) satisfies
the \(C^1\) Lipschitz-bump conclusion of Open Problem 4.5: it has a \(C^1\)
smooth Lipschitz bump whose derivative cone has empty interior in \(X^*\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The source paper proves the required small-cone bump on \(\ell_2\).  Transport
that bump through the linear isomorphism from the complemented subspace \(Y\)
onto \(\ell_2\); empty interior is preserved by the adjoint isomorphism.

Write \(X=Y\oplus Z\).  It remains only to get a bump on \(Z\).  Let
\(\beta:X\to\mathbb R\) be a nonzero \(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump on \(X\),
and choose a point \(x_0=y_0+z_0\) with \(\beta(x_0)\neq0\).  Then
\[
  \eta(z)=\beta(y_0,z+z_0)^2,\qquad z\in Z,
\]
is a nonnegative nonzero \(C^p\) smooth Lipschitz bump on \(Z\).  Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:transfer}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The construction uses a product bump.  The derivative in the solved summand
\(Y\) is only multiplied by a nonnegative scalar coming from the other factor,
so the \(Y^*\)-projection of the new derivative cone stays inside the old
small cone \(\C(b)\).  Since projections from \(Y^*\oplus Z^*\) onto \(Y^*\)
are open maps, a cone with interior upstairs would project to a set with
interior downstairs.  This is impossible.

\section*{Implication for Open Problem 4.5}

Open Problem 4.5 remains open for arbitrary separable reflexive spaces.  The
transfer theorem shows that the source's Hilbert-space construction is not
isolated: it automatically solves the problem for every smooth space with a
complemented Hilbert summand, and more generally for every complemented
extension of a space already known to have a small derivative cone bump.
