\paragraph{Transcription of the target question.}
Question 4.12 asks whether the inequalities of the two-dimensional section can
hold when the fields are constrained to the double cone
\[
  \Sym_2\cap\{\det\geq 0\}
  =\Sym_2^+\cup\Sym_2^- .
\]
The inequalities in that section include the row-divergence determinant
estimate of Theorem 4.1, its \(L^2\) consequence, and the row-curl determinant
estimate of Corollary 4.7.

\paragraph{Notation.}
If \(A=(a_{ij})\) is a \(2\times2\) matrix field, let \(A_i\) be its \(i\)-th
row and define
\[
  d_i(A)=\operatorname{div} A_i=\partial_1 a_{i1}+\partial_2 a_{i2},
  \qquad
  r_i(A)=\curl A_i=\partial_1 a_{i2}-\partial_2 a_{i1}.
\]
We use the Frobenius matrix norm in the proof.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

A smooth field valued in \(\Sym_2^+\cup\Sym_2^-\) can change branches only by
passing through the zero matrix: if a matrix in the double cone has trace zero,
then both eigenvalues vanish. Therefore the sign of \(\tr A\) can be used to
flip the negative branch into the positive one without creating a hidden
first-order term. To keep the proof smooth, use
\[
  B_\varepsilon
  =
  \frac{\tr A}{\sqrt{(\tr A)^2+\varepsilon^2}}\,A .
\]
Each \(B_\varepsilon\) is positive semidefinite, so the source paper's
positive-cone estimates apply. The determinant converges to \(\det A\), and the
extra terms coming from differentiating the scalar factor vanish in \(L^1\)
because \(|A|\leq |\tr A|\) on the double cone.

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}[Smooth double-cone estimates]
Let \(A\in C_c^\infty(\R^2,\Sym_2^+\cup\Sym_2^-)\). Then
\[
  \int_{\R^2}\det A\,dx
  \leq
  \|d_1(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)}\,\|d_2(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)}
\]
and
\[
  \int_{\R^2}\det A\,dx
  \leq
  \|r_1(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)}\,\|r_2(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)} .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write \(\tau=\tr A\), and for \(\varepsilon>0\) define
\[
  \eta_\varepsilon(t)=\frac{t}{\sqrt{t^2+\varepsilon^2}},
  \qquad
  B_\varepsilon=\eta_\varepsilon(\tau)A .
\]
Since \(A(x)\) is either positive semidefinite or negative semidefinite, the
scalar \(\eta_\varepsilon(\tau(x))\) has the sign needed to make
\(B_\varepsilon(x)\in\Sym_2^+\). Thus \(B_\varepsilon\in
C_c^\infty(\R^2,\Sym_2^+)\). Also
\[
  \det B_\varepsilon
  =
  \eta_\varepsilon(\tau)^2 \det A
  \to
  \det A
\]
pointwise and in \(L^1\), since \(A\) is smooth and compactly supported.

We record the elementary bound used to control the derivative of
\(\eta_\varepsilon(\tau)\). If \(M\in\Sym_2^+\cup\Sym_2^-\), then
\[
  |M|_F\leq |\tr M|.
\]
Indeed, on either branch the eigenvalues have the same sign, so
\((\lambda_1^2+\lambda_2^2)^{1/2}\leq |\lambda_1+\lambda_2|\).
In particular, \(|A_{ij}|\leq |\tau|\).

For a row divergence we have
\[
  d_i(B_\varepsilon)
  =
  \eta_\varepsilon(\tau)d_i(A)
  +
  \eta_\varepsilon'(\tau)\bigl(A_{i1}\partial_1\tau
  +A_{i2}\partial_2\tau\bigr).
\]
For a row curl,
\[
  r_i(B_\varepsilon)
  =
  \eta_\varepsilon(\tau)r_i(A)
  +
  \eta_\varepsilon'(\tau)\bigl(A_{i2}\partial_1\tau
  -A_{i1}\partial_2\tau\bigr).
\]
Here
\[
  \eta_\varepsilon'(t)=\frac{\varepsilon^2}{(t^2+\varepsilon^2)^{3/2}},
  \qquad
  |t\,\eta_\varepsilon'(t)|\leq 1,
  \qquad
  t\,\eta_\varepsilon'(t)\to0 .
\]
Using \(|A_{ij}|\leq|\tau|\), the error terms above are dominated by an
integrable multiple of \(|\nabla\tau|\) and converge pointwise to zero.
Dominated convergence gives
\[
  \limsup_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}\|d_i(B_\varepsilon)\|_1
  \leq \|d_i(A)\|_1,
  \qquad
  \limsup_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}\|r_i(B_\varepsilon)\|_1
  \leq \|r_i(A)\|_1 .
\]

Since \(\Sym_2^+\subset\{a_{11},a_{22}\geq0\}\), Theorem 4.1 of the source
paper applies to each \(B_\varepsilon\):
\[
  \int_{\R^2}\det B_\varepsilon\,dx
  \leq
  \|d_1(B_\varepsilon)\|_1\|d_2(B_\varepsilon)\|_1 .
\]
Letting \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) gives the first estimate. Likewise,
Corollary 4.7 of the source paper gives
\[
  \int_{\R^2}\det B_\varepsilon\,dx
  \leq
  \|r_1(B_\varepsilon)\|_1\|r_2(B_\varepsilon)\|_1 ,
\]
and the same limit gives the row-curl estimate.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Smooth \(L^2\) consequences]
Let \(\mathcal C\subset\Sym_2^+\cup\Sym_2^-\) be a closed cone such that
\[
  |M|^2\leq K\det M\qquad(M\in\mathcal C)
\]
for some \(K<\infty\). Then every
\(A\in C_c^\infty(\R^2,\mathcal C)\) satisfies
\[
  \|A\|_{L^2(\R^2)}
  \leq C_K\|\Div A\|_{L^1(\R^2)}
\]
and
\[
  \|A\|_{L^2(\R^2)}
  \leq C_K\bigl(\|r_1(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)}+\|r_2(A)\|_{L^1(\R^2)}\bigr).
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The cone assumption and the theorem give
\[
  \|A\|_2^2
  \leq
  K\int_{\R^2}\det A
  \leq
  K\|d_1(A)\|_1\|d_2(A)\|_1
  \leq
  C_K\|\Div A\|_1^2 .
\]
The curl estimate is identical, using
\(\|r_1(A)\|_1\|r_2(A)\|_1
\leq(\|r_1(A)\|_1+\|r_2(A)\|_1)^2/4\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This resolves Question 4.12 for the smooth compactly supported estimates in
Section 4. The paper separately discusses rough fields in
\(\mathrm{BV}^{\Div}\), where the usual convex-cone mollification argument
fails. The theorem above does not solve that rough problem. For a rough field
with a jump directly from a positive semidefinite value to a negative
semidefinite value, a branch flip can introduce a new jump contribution in the
divergence measure.
