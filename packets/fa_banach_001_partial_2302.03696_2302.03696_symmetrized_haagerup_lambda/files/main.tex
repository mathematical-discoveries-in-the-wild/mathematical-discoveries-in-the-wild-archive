\section*{Status}

This is a partial result for Problem 1 in Chavez-Dominguez, Dimant and
Galicer, \emph{Operator space tensor norms}, arXiv:2302.03696v1.  It gives a
closure theorem producing new \(\lambda\)-operator-space tensor norms, but does
not classify such norms.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper concludes with the following problem list.  Problem 1 asks for
more examples of \(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norms.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In the source notation, the already listed examples include the projective
operator-space tensor norm \(\mathrm{proj}\), the Haagerup tensor norm \(h\),
its transpose \(h^t\), and the family \(\odot^\theta\) constructed in Section
2.6 of the source paper.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The usual Haagerup tensor norm is induced by matrix multiplication
\((A,B)\mapsto AB\), while the transposed Haagerup tensor norm is induced by
\((A,B)\mapsto BA\).  Instead of averaging these two products, as in the
source's \(\odot^\theta\) family, put both products in orthogonal diagonal
blocks:
\[
  (A,B)\longmapsto
  \begin{pmatrix}
  AB & 0\\
  0 & BA
  \end{pmatrix}.
\]
The same idea works for any finite family of \(\lambda\)-products.  Put their
matrix products in diagonal blocks.  The \(\lambda\)-axioms are inherited by
compressing to one block for (E1) and by permuting blocks for (E2).  On the
dual side, the bilinear norm becomes the maximum of the original dual bilinear
norms, which is exactly the dual norm of the operator-space infimal sum.  Thus
finite infimal sums of \(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norms are again
\(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norms.

\section*{The Result}

\begin{theorem}[Finite infimal-sum closure]
Let \(N\ge 1\), and let \(\lambda^1,\ldots,\lambda^N\) be
\(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norms in the sense of the source paper.  Write
\[
  B^i_k:M_k\times M_k\longrightarrow M_{\tau_i(k)}
\]
for the bilinear maps defining \(\lambda^i\).  Define
\[
  \nu_k(A,B)=\diag
  \big(B^1_k(A,B),\ldots,B^N_k(A,B)\big)
  \in M_{\tau_1(k)+\cdots+\tau_N(k)} .
\]
Then \(\nu=(\nu_k)\) is a \(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norm.  Moreover, for every
pair of operator spaces \(E,F\), the induced operator-space tensor norm is the
infimal sum
\[
  \lambda^1+\cdots+\lambda^N,
\]
meaning that at each matrix level
\[
  \|u\|_{\nu,n}
  =
  \inf\Big\{
      \sum_{i=1}^N \|u_i\|_{\lambda^i,n}:
      u=u_1+\cdots+u_N
  \Big\}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We verify the source paper's conditions (E1), (E2), and (E3).

For (E3), since each \(\lambda^i\) satisfies (E3),
\[
  \nu_1(1,1)=\diag(B^1_1(1,1),\ldots,B^N_1(1,1))
\]
is the identity matrix of the codomain, and
\[
  \|\nu_k(A,B)\|=\max_i\|B^i_k(A,B)\|
  \le \Big(\max_i\|B^i_k\|\Big)\|A\|\,\|B\|.
\]
Hence \(\sup_k\|\nu_k\|<\infty\).

For (E1), fix \(k\).  Use the (E1) witnesses for \(\lambda^1\): some \(p\),
matrices \(S_1,T_1\), and matrices \(a_1,\ldots,a_k\in M_p\).  Let
\[
  S=\begin{pmatrix}S_1&0&\cdots&0\end{pmatrix},\qquad
  T=\begin{pmatrix}T_1\\0\\\vdots\\0\end{pmatrix},
\]
with zeros in the remaining block columns and rows.  Then
\[
  S\nu_p(a_{j_1},a_{j_2})T
  =
  S_1B^1_p(a_{j_1},a_{j_2})T_1,
\]
which is the required diagonal matrix unit when \(j_1=j_2\) and zero
otherwise.  Thus \(\nu\) satisfies (E1).

For (E2), fix \(r,s\).  For each \(i\), let \(P_i\) be an (E2) matrix for
\(\lambda^i\).  The block-diagonal matrix
\[
  Q=\diag(P_1,\ldots,P_N)
\]
has norm at most one and sends \(\nu_{r+s}\) to the block diagonal matrix
\[
  \diag
  \big(B^1_r,B^1_s,B^2_r,B^2_s,\ldots,B^N_r,B^N_s\big)
\]
on the matrix-unit pairs appearing in (E2).  A permutation unitary reorders
these blocks as
\[
  \diag
  \big(B^1_r,\ldots,B^N_r,B^1_s,\ldots,B^N_s\big)
  =
  \diag(\nu_r,\nu_s).
\]
Multiplying \(Q\) by this unitary preserves the norm bound, giving (E2).

It remains to identify the induced tensor norm.  Let
\(\Phi:E\times F\to M_m\) be a completely bounded bilinear map.  For every
\(k\) and \(x\in M_k(E)\), \(y\in M_k(F)\),
\[
  \Phi_{\nu_k}(x,y)
  =
  \diag
  \big(\Phi_{B^1_k}(x,y),\ldots,\Phi_{B^N_k}(x,y)\big).
\]
Therefore
\[
  \|\Phi\|_{\cb,\nu}
  =
  \max_{1\le i\le N}\|\Phi\|_{\cb,\lambda^i}
\]
at every matrix level.  By the source paper's duality theorem for
\(\lambda\)-tensor products, the dual of \(E\otimes_\nu F\) is the intersection
of the duals of \(E\otimes_{\lambda^i}F\), equipped with the maximum of the
dual norms.

The dual norm of the matrix-level infimal sum
\[
  \inf\Big\{
      \sum_i\|u_i\|_{\lambda^i,n}: u=\sum_i u_i
  \Big\}
\]
is exactly the maximum of the corresponding dual norms.  Hence the
matrix-level norms induced by \(\nu\) agree with the operator-space infimal
sum of the \(\lambda^i\)'s.  Since finite infimal sums of operator-space tensor
norms are again operator-space tensor norms, \(\nu\) is a
\(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norm.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Symmetrized Haagerup corollary]
Let \(\nu=(\nu_k)_{k\ge 1}\) be the sequence of bilinear maps
\[
  \nu_k:M_k\times M_k\longrightarrow M_{2k},\qquad
  \nu_k(A,B)=
  \begin{pmatrix}
  AB & 0\\
  0 & BA
  \end{pmatrix}.
\]
Then \(\nu\) defines a \(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norm.  For every pair of
operator spaces \(E,F\), the induced tensor norm on \(E\otimes F\) is
completely isometric to the symmetrized Haagerup sum \(h+h^t\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply the finite infimal-sum closure theorem to the \(\lambda\)-products
defined by \(AB\) and \(BA\), which induce \(h\) and \(h^t\), respectively.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Limitations}

The proof is purely formal from the definitions in Section 2.6 of the source
paper and from its duality theorem for \(\lambda\)-tensor products.  No
computation was used.

The result gives a systematic finite-sum closure construction and, in
particular, the concrete additional \(\lambda\)-o.s. tensor norm \(h+h^t\).
It does not address the existence of spaces with W*CBAP but without CBAP, the
accessibility questions, the full natural-norm classification, or the duality
identity \((\backslash\min /)'=/\mathrm{proj}\backslash\).

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review the convention in condition (E3), where \(1\) denotes the identity of
the codomain matrix algebra, and verify that the block-permutation argument for
(E2) matches the source paper's indexing.  The main mathematical mechanism is
the dual identification of the block-diagonal product with the matrix-level
infimal sum of the original tensor norms.
