# Literature-implied partial answer: convex-transitive Flinn question

Status: `literature_implied_answer (partial subcase)`.

Source question: Beata Randrianantoanina, *A note on Banach--Mazur problem*,
arXiv:math/0110202.  The source asks whether its almost-transitive theorem
for spaces with a 1-complemented hyperplane can be generalized to convex
transitive spaces.

Supporting paper: Jarno Talponen, *Convex-transitive characterizations of
Hilbert spaces*, arXiv:0705.2526.

Identification: Talponen explicitly reformulates Randrianantoanina's question
as Question (QR): whether every real convex-transitive Banach space with a
1-codimensional 1-complemented subspace is Hilbert.  His Theorem 3.2 gives an
affirmative answer when the space is weakly locally uniformly rotund and has
arbitrarily nearly 1-complemented hyperplanes; in particular it covers the
source projection assumption under the added weak-LUR hypothesis.  His Theorem
5.4 also gives an affirmative answer when the space is convex-transitive, has
one weak-LUR point, and admits a one-dimensional bicontractive projection.

Scope: this is not a full solution of Randrianantoanina's convex-transitive
question.  It records a later literature theorem that settles substantial
geometric subcases.  The unrestricted real convex-transitive case with merely a
1-complemented hyperplane remains open in this packet.
