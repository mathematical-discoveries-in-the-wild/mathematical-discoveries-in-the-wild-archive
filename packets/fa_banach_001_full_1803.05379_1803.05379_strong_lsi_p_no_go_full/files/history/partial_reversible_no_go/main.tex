\paragraph{Source question.}
Bardet--Rouze, arXiv:1803.05379, ask for the range of \(p\)'s for which a non-primitive QMS can satisfy a strong inequality \(\LSI_{p,\cN}(c,0)\).

\section*{Historical Partial Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(1<p<\infty\). Let \((P_t)_{t\ge0}\) be a finite-dimensional reversible quantum Markov semigroup whose generator \(\cL\) has nontrivial fixed algebra \(\cN\). Then no finite constant \(c\) satisfies
\[
  \Ent_{p,\cN}(X)\le c\,\cE_{p,\cL}(X)\qquad(X>0).
\]
In particular, the canonical decoherence generator \(\cL_\cN=E_\cN-\id\) has zero strong \(p\)-log-Sobolev constant for every finite \(p>1\).
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The construction is the two-level perturbation used in the source paper for \(p=2\). Choose density matrices \(\rho_{\cN,k}\in E_{\cN *}(\mathcal D(\cH))\) and an off-diagonal self-adjoint perturbation \(\Delta\in\ker E_{\cN *}\) joining eigenvectors with eigenvalues
\[
  x_k=\lambda_1/k,\qquad y_k=(1-1/k)\lambda_2.
\]
For \(\rho_{k,\epsilon}=\rho_{\cN,k}+\epsilon\Delta\), set
\[
  X_{k,\epsilon}=\Gammaop^{-1/p}(\rho_{k,\epsilon}^{1/p}).
\]
The entropy identity of Bardet--Rouze gives
\[
  \Ent_{p,\cN}(X_{k,\epsilon})
  =
  \frac{\epsilon^2}{p}
  \frac{\log x_k-\log y_k}{x_k-y_k}
  +O_k(\epsilon^3),
\]
up to a fixed nonzero matrix-entry factor. The displayed divided difference diverges as \(k\to\infty\).

On the other hand, the \(p\)-Dirichlet expansion contains only divided differences of \(t^{1/p}\) and \(t^{1/q}\), where \(q=p/(p-1)\). These remain bounded as \(x_k\downarrow0\) and \(y_k\to\lambda_2>0\). Reversibility cancels the fixed-algebra second-order terms, leaving a uniformly bounded energy coefficient. Hence
\[
  \lim_{k\to\infty}\limsup_{\epsilon\downarrow0}
  \frac{\cE_{p,\cL}(X_{k,\epsilon})}{\Ent_{p,\cN}(X_{k,\epsilon})}=0,
\]
contradicting any finite strong \(p\)-LSI constant.

\section*{How This Was Upgraded}

The parent full packet observes that the reversible cancellation can be replaced by a fixed-algebra Hamiltonian cancellation: on the decoherence-free algebra, the generator is a derivation, and the singular projected second-order terms commute with the relevant zeroth-order function of \(\rho_{\cN,k}\). Traciality of \(\sigma_{\rm tr}\) on \(\cN\) then kills those terms without assuming reversibility.
