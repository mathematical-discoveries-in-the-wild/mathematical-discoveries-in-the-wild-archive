\section{Source question}

Dimovski, Pilipovi\'c, and Vindas introduce a translation-invariant Banach
space $E$ of tempered distributions and the associated test-function space
$\Dspace$ in \cite{DPV}.  In Remark~6 on page~17 they ask:
\begin{quote}
``In general: Can $\mathcal D_E$ be Montel?''
\end{quote}
The source passage is reproduced below at full readable width.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

Debrouwere later restated the question as Problem~5.8 and proved that
$\Dspace$ is not Montel when $E$ is a \emph{solid} translation-invariant
Banach space of distributions \cite[Problem~5.8 and Theorem~5.9]{Debrouwere}.
That paper explicitly says that the general case was not known.  The proof
below removes solidity.

\section{Setting and definitions}

We use the source convention $T_xf=f(\,\cdot+x)$.  A
translation-invariant Banach space of tempered distributions is a Banach
space $E$ for which
\[
  \Schwartz\hookrightarrow E\hookrightarrow\Tempered,
  \qquad T_xE\subset E,
\]
the translation orbit maps are continuous, and for some $M>0$ and
$\tau\geq0$,
\[
  \|T_xf\|_E\leq M(1+|x|)^\tau\|f\|_E.
\]
Its translation weight is
\[
  \omega(x)=\|T_{-x}\|_{\mathcal L(E)}
  \leq M(1+|x|)^\tau.
\]
The source proves that $E$ is a Banach module over the Beurling algebra
$L^1_\omega$ and that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:module}
  \|u*f\|_E\leq \|u\|_{1,\omega}\|f\|_E.
\end{equation}
The Fr\'echet space in the question is
\[
  \Dspace=\{f\in\Tempered:\partial^\alpha f\in E
           \text{ for every }\alpha\in\mathbb N_0^n\},
\]
with increasing seminorms
\[
  p_N(f)=\max_{|\alpha|\leq N}\|\partial^\alpha f\|_E.
\]
The embeddings $\Dspace\hookrightarrow E\hookrightarrow\Tempered$ are
continuous \cite[Proposition~7]{DPV}.

\section{Proof intuition}

Unnormalized translates need not form a bounded set because the translation
weight may grow.  Normalize a translate $T_x\phi$ by its own $E$-norm.  This
fixes its zeroth seminorm at one, but could in principle make higher
derivatives unbounded.  A band-limited Schwartz function prevents that:
every derivative of $\phi$ is a fixed $L^1_\omega$ convolution of $\phi$.
The module estimate then controls every derivative of every normalized
translate uniformly.

The normalization also cannot grow faster than a polynomial, because applying
$T_{-x}$ recovers $\phi$.  Against a Schwartz test function, however, the
translate $T_x\phi$ decays faster than every polynomial.  Thus the normalized
translates converge to zero in $\Tempered$, although their $E$-norms remain
one.  They therefore have no convergent subsequence in $\Dspace$.

\section{Band-limited derivative control}

Fix the Fourier convention
\[
  \widehat f(\xi)=\int_{\mathbb R^n}e^{-ix\cdot\xi}f(x)\,dx.
\]

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:bandlimited}
There is a nonzero $\phi\in\Schwartz$ such that, for every multi-index
$\alpha$, there is $u_\alpha\in\Schwartz\subset L^1_\omega$ satisfying
\[
  \partial^\alpha\phi=u_\alpha*\phi.
\]
Consequently, the family
\[
  g_x:=\frac{T_x\phi}{\|T_x\phi\|_E},\qquad x\in\mathbb R^n,
\]
is bounded in $\Dspace$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose a nonzero $\widehat\phi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^n)$ and let
$\phi=\mathcal F^{-1}\widehat\phi$.  Choose
$\chi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^n)$ with $\chi=1$ on
$\operatorname{supp}\widehat\phi$, and define
\[
  \widehat u_\alpha(\xi)=\chi(\xi)(i\xi)^\alpha.
\]
Then $u_\alpha\in\Schwartz$.  Since $\omega$ is polynomially bounded,
$u_\alpha\in L^1_\omega$.  Fourier transformation gives
\[
  \widehat{u_\alpha*\phi}
  =\chi(\xi)(i\xi)^\alpha\widehat\phi(\xi)
  =(i\xi)^\alpha\widehat\phi(\xi)
  =\widehat{\partial^\alpha\phi}.
\]
Translations commute with derivatives and convolution, so \eqref{eq:module}
implies
\[
 \|\partial^\alpha g_x\|_E
 =\frac{\|u_\alpha*T_x\phi\|_E}{\|T_x\phi\|_E}
 \leq \|u_\alpha\|_{1,\omega}.
\]
For every fixed $N$, taking the maximum over $|\alpha|\leq N$ proves that
$\sup_xp_N(g_x)<\infty$.  The denominator is nonzero because translations
are invertible and $\phi\neq0$.
\end{proof}

\section{Full negative answer}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $E$ be any translation-invariant Banach space of tempered distributions
in the sense of \cite{DPV}.  Then $\Dspace$ is not Montel.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $\phi$ and $g_x$ be as in Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited}.  Since
$T_{-x}T_x\phi=\phi$ and $\omega(x)=\|T_{-x}\|_{\mathcal L(E)}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:inverse}
  \|\phi\|_E\leq\omega(x)\|T_x\phi\|_E,
  \qquad
  \frac1{\|T_x\phi\|_E}
       \leq\frac{M(1+|x|)^\tau}{\|\phi\|_E}.
\end{equation}
For every $\psi\in\Schwartz$, the function
\[
  x\longmapsto\langle T_x\phi,\psi\rangle
\]
is a Schwartz function of $x$: after a reflection it is the convolution of
two Schwartz functions.  Combining this rapid decay with
\eqref{eq:inverse} yields
\[
  \langle g_x,\psi\rangle
  =\frac{\langle T_x\phi,\psi\rangle}{\|T_x\phi\|_E}
  \longrightarrow0
  \qquad(|x|\to\infty).
\]
Thus $g_x\to0$ in the weak topology $\sigma(\Tempered,\Schwartz)$.

Choose any sequence $x_j$ with $|x_j|\to\infty$.  By
Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited}, $(g_{x_j})$ is bounded in $\Dspace$.  If
$\Dspace$ were Montel, the closure of this bounded set would be compact.
Because $\Dspace$ is Fr\'echet and hence metrizable, $(g_{x_j})$ would have a
subsequence converging in $\Dspace$.  The continuous embedding
$\Dspace\hookrightarrow\Tempered$ and the weak convergence above force its
limit to be zero.  But
\[
  p_0(g_{x_j})=\|g_{x_j}\|_E=1
\]
for every $j$, so no subsequence can converge to zero in $\Dspace$.  This
contradiction proves the theorem.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and scope}

The proof uses only four source-paper facts: polynomial growth of
$\omega$, the convolution estimate \eqref{eq:module}, the definition of the
$\Dspace$ seminorms, and the continuous embedding
$\Dspace\hookrightarrow\Tempered$.  The normalization estimate
\eqref{eq:inverse} was checked against the source sign convention
$\omega(x)=\|T_{-x}\|$.  No numerical or computer-assisted step enters the
proof.

A bounded novelty search performed on 22 July 2026 covered arXiv and web
searches for arXiv ids 1310.4047 and 1901.10041, the exact source question,
``$\mathcal D_E$ is never Montel,'' ``general TIBD,'' the paper titles, and
the relevant authors.  It found the 2019 solid-space theorem and its explicit
statement that the general case remained open, but no later general solution.
This is evidence, not an exhaustive bibliographic guarantee; novelty
confidence is therefore \emph{moderate to high}.

The theorem fully answers Remark~6 negatively.  It does not address the
separate barreledness question in Remark~7.  Recommended human review:
\textbf{verify and circulate}, with particular attention to the multiplier
identity in Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited} and the compactness-to-subsequence
step.
