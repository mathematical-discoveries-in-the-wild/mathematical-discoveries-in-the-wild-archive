\section{Source statement}

Gladkov, Kolesnikov, and Zimin study a $(3,2)$ multistochastic problem on
$[0,1]^3$. Its cost is $c(x,y,z)=xyz$. In Example 2.11, the primal measure is
supported on the plane
\[
 \Gamma=\{(x,y,z)\in[0,1]^3:x+y+z=1\},
\]
and its three pair projections are prescribed. Example 2.20 displays a
one-parameter family of symmetric dual optimizers
\[
 \bigl(f_A(x,y),f_A(x,z),f_A(y,z)\bigr),\qquad A\geq0,
\]
and then states: ``We believe that there are no other dual solution, but can
not prove this'' \cite{GKZ}. The relevant passage is on PDF page 8.

The journal version retains Example 2.20 but removes the quoted sentence.
Accordingly, the result below is scoped to the literal arXiv-version belief.

\section{Setup}

For reference, the source polynomial is
\begin{align*}
 f_A(u,v)={}&-\frac{u^3+v^3}{12}-\frac12uv(u+v)
 -(A-2)\left(\frac{u^2}{12}+\frac{uv}{3}+\frac{v^2}{12}\right)\\
 &-\frac{1-2A}{12}(u+v)-\frac{A}{18}.
\end{align*}
Writing $s=x+y+z$, direct expansion gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:source-gap}
 xyz-f_A(x,y)-f_A(x,z)-f_A(y,z)
 =\frac{(s-1)^2(s+A)}6.
\end{equation}
This is nonnegative on the cube and vanishes on $\Gamma$, proving feasibility
and complementary optimality of the source family.

\section{Proof intuition}

The square $(s-1)^2$ is exactly what forces equality on the support plane.
The source multiplies it by the symmetric affine function $s+A$. To leave the
class of pairwise potentials, a perturbation would need a genuine
three-variable interaction. But
\[
 (s-1)^2(x-y)
\]
has no $xyz$ monomial: its mixed third derivative is zero. It therefore splits
as a sum of three pair polynomials. A small multiple can be subtracted from
the positive source gap without changing its sign. This produces an
anisotropic optimal triple whose total sum cannot be obtained from the
symmetric family by either changing $A$ or applying the usual gauge freedom.

\section{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
For the planar multistochastic problem of Examples 2.11 and 2.20, define
\begin{align*}
 h_{12}(x,y)&=(x-y)(x+y-1)^2,\\
 h_{13}(x,z)&=xz(2x+z-2),\\
 h_{23}(y,z)&=-yz(2y+z-2),
\end{align*}
and set
\begin{align*}
 g_{12}(x,y)&=f_1(x,y)+\frac1{12}h_{12}(x,y),\\
 g_{13}(x,z)&=f_1(x,z)+\frac1{12}h_{13}(x,z),\\
 g_{23}(y,z)&=f_1(y,z)+\frac1{12}h_{23}(y,z).
\end{align*}
Then $(g_{12},g_{13},g_{23})$ is a polynomial dual optimizer. It is not
gauge-equivalent to the source triple for any parameter $A\geq0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First, exact expansion gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:decomp}
 h_{12}(x,y)+h_{13}(x,z)+h_{23}(y,z)
 =(s-1)^2(x-y).
\end{equation}
Combining \eqref{eq:source-gap} at $A=1$ with \eqref{eq:decomp} yields
\begin{align}
 xyz-g_{12}(x,y)-g_{13}(x,z)-g_{23}(y,z)
 &=\frac{(s-1)^2(1+s)}6-\frac{(s-1)^2(x-y)}{12}\notag\\
 &=\frac{(s-1)^2(2+x+3y+2z)}{12}.\label{eq:new-gap}
\end{align}
Every factor on the last line is nonnegative on $[0,1]^3$. Thus the new
triple is dual feasible. On $\Gamma$ the right side vanishes, so the dual sum
equals $xyz$ on the support of the primal measure. Integrating against that
measure shows that the dual value equals the primal value. Hence the new
triple is optimal.

It remains to prove that this is not a disguised member of the displayed
family. Gauge transformations of pair potentials have identically zero total
sum, so gauge-equivalent triples must have the same total sum at every
$(x,y,z)$. If the new total were the total associated with $f_B$ for some
$B$, comparison of \eqref{eq:new-gap} and \eqref{eq:source-gap} would require
\[
 2+x+3y+2z=2(s+B)
\]
identically. This is impossible: the coefficients of $x$ and $y$ on the left
are $1$ and $3$, whereas both coefficients on the right are $2$.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and scope}

The identities were checked over the exact rational polynomial ring by the
included SymPy script. No numerical approximation enters the proof. The sign
in \eqref{eq:new-gap} is coefficientwise transparent, and complementary
optimality uses only the source's planar support statement.

The construction disproves exhaustiveness of the displayed symmetric
one-parameter family; it does not classify all optimizers. It also does not
answer the paper's broader Open Problem 2 about sufficient conditions for an
optimal-map representation.

\section{Novelty check}

The run indexes and bounded web searches on July 23, 2026 used
arXiv:2008.07926, the exact quoted sentence, the authors, Example 2.20,
the notation $f_A$, ``dual uniqueness,'' and the perturbation
$(x+y+z-1)^2(x-y)$. No explicit later answer or matching construction was
found. Novelty confidence is moderate-to-low because the published 2022
version silently removes the uniqueness sentence.

\section{Human review recommendation}

The decisive checks are the decomposition \eqref{eq:decomp}, the exact gap
\eqref{eq:new-gap}, and the scope of the informal arXiv-only statement. The
first two are elementary polynomial identities; the last determines how the
result should be described historically.
