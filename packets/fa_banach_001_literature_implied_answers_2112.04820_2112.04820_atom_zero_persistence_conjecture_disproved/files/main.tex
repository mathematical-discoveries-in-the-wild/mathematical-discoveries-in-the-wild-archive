\section{Source conjecture}

Feldheim, Feldheim, and Mukherjee~\cite{FFM25} define
\[
 \rho'(0)=\lim_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}
 \frac{\rho([-\varepsilon,\varepsilon])}{2\varepsilon},
 \qquad
 \cM=\{\rho:\rho'(0)\in(0,\infty]\},
\]
within their finite log-moment class $\cL$. Their Remark 3, PDF page 3,
conjectures that for every $\ell>0$ and $\rho\in\cM$, the exponential
persistence exponent
\[
 \theta_\rho^\ell=-\lim_{T\to\infty}\frac1T
 \log\mathbb P\!\left(\inf_{t\in[0,T]}f_\rho(t)>\ell\right)
\]
is always positive.

\section{Supporting result and identification}

Feldheim, Feldheim, and Muirhead~\cite{FFMu26} later consider stationary
Gaussian fields on $\mathbb R^d$ or $\mathbb Z^d$. Their Theorem 3(iv), PDF
pages 5--6, applies when the spectral measure has an atom at the origin, a
nonzero absolutely continuous part, and no singular-continuous part. Writing
$m=\|\rho_{\mathrm{ac}}\|$, their formula (1.10), in the case $d=1$ and
$\alpha=0$, gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:later}
 -\log\mathbb P\!\left(f_\rho(t)\ge q
          \text{ for every }t\in[-T,T]\right)
 \sim \frac{m}{\rho(\{0\})}\log T .
\end{equation}
The paper states immediately before its main theorem that the results extend
from level zero to every fixed level $q\in\mathbb R$.

\begin{proposition}
The conjecture in Remark 3 of~\cite{FFM25}, as stated, is false. In fact it
fails for a nondegenerate process with a smooth nonzero absolutely continuous
spectral component.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Fix $a>0$ and set
\[
 d\rho(\lambda)=a\,d\delta_0(\lambda)+e^{-\lambda^2}\,d\lambda.
\]
This is a finite symmetric nonnegative spectral measure with finite log-moment,
so $\rho\in\cL$. Moreover,
\[
 \rho'(0)=\lim_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}
 \frac{a+\int_{-\varepsilon}^{\varepsilon}e^{-\lambda^2}\,d\lambda}
      {2\varepsilon}=+\infty,
\]
hence $\rho\in\cM$. It has no singular-continuous part and has
$m=\|\rho_{\mathrm{ac}}\|=\sqrt\pi>0$, so the later theorem applies.

Fix the conjecture's level $\ell>0$ and apply~\eqref{eq:later} at the higher
fixed level $q=\ell+1$. Stationarity identifies persistence on $[0,T]$ with
persistence on $[-T/2,T/2]$ after translation. Consequently
\[
 \mathbb P\!\left(\inf_{t\in[0,T]}f_\rho(t)>\ell\right)
 \ge
 \mathbb P\!\left(f_\rho(t)\ge\ell+1
             \text{ for all }t\in[-T/2,T/2]\right)
 =T^{-\sqrt\pi/a+o(1)}.
\]
The quantity $-T^{-1}\log$ of the left-hand probability is nonnegative and
has limsup at most $0$. Its limit therefore exists and equals $0$. Thus
$\theta_\rho^\ell=0$, contrary to the conjectured strict positivity.
\end{proof}

\section{Status, limitations, and search record}

The pure atom $\rho=\delta_0$ gives the still simpler constant-process
counterexample: $f_\rho(t)=Z$ and the persistence probability is
$\mathbb P(Z>\ell)$ for every $T$. The construction above is stronger because
it remains nondegenerate and is directly covered by the later literature.

The run indexes were searched for arXiv:2112.04820 and the core persistence,
spectral-measure, and atom-at-zero phrases. Targeted arXiv searches through
19 July 2026 found~\cite{FFMu26}. That paper cites~\cite{FFM25} and proves the
decisive asymptotic, but does not explicitly announce that Remark 3 is being
disproved. Accordingly this packet is classified as a literature-implied
answer, not an original counterexample. Human review should check the fixed
level extension stated on PDF page 3 and the specialization of Theorem 3(iv).
