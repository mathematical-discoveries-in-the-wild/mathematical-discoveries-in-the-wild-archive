\section*{Status}

This packet gives a likely valid negative answer to Question 19(a) of
Laitila--Tylli \cite{LT2015}.  The question asks whether the norm estimate in
Theorem 18(2), valid for \(2<p<\infty\), extends to \(1\leq p<2\).  It does not:
radial composition symbols on \(X=\ell_2\) have norm of order
\(\|C_\varphi\|_{HS}\), not \(\|C_\varphi\|_{HS}^{2/p}\).

\section*{Source Question}

The source is J. Laitila and H.-O. Tylli, \emph{Composition operators on
vector-valued analytic function spaces: a survey}, arXiv:1505.01945
\cite{LT2015}.  The relevant statement appears as Question 19(a) on PDF page
17, after Theorem 18.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In the notation of \cite{LT2015}, \(H^p(X)\) is the strong vector-valued Hardy
space and \(wH^p(X)\) is the weak Hardy space with norm
\[
 \|f\|_{wH^p(X)}
 =
 \sup_{\|x^*\|\leq 1}\|x^*\circ f\|_{H^p}.
\]
Question 19(a) asks whether
\[
 \|C_\varphi:wH^p(X)\to H^p(X)\|
 \asymp
 \|C_\varphi\|_{HS}^{2/p}
\]
continues to hold for \(1\leq p<2\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

For \(p\leq2\), the weak \(H^p\) norm controls each Taylor coefficient of an
\(\ell_2\)-valued function separately.  After radial composition by
\(\varphi_r(z)=rz\), the strong \(H^p(\ell_2)\) norm is bounded by the strong
\(H^2(\ell_2)\) norm, and Parseval reduces this to the square sum
\(\sum r^{2n}\|x_n\|^2\).  This gives the upper bound
\((\sum r^{2n})^{1/2}\).  The test function
\[
 F(z)=(1,z,z^2,\ldots)
\]
has weak \(H^p(\ell_2)\)-norm \(1\), and after radial composition its
\(\ell_2\)-norm on the boundary is exactly \((\sum r^{2n})^{1/2}\).  Thus the
operator norm is exactly the Hilbert--Schmidt norm, which grows too slowly to
be comparable with \(\|C_{\varphi_r}\|_{HS}^{2/p}\) when \(p<2\).

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(1\leq p\leq2\), let \(X=\ell_2\), and let
\(\varphi_r(z)=rz\) for \(0<r<1\).  Then
\[
 \|C_{\varphi_r}:wH^p(\ell_2)\to H^p(\ell_2)\|
 =
 \|C_{\varphi_r}:H^2\to H^2\|_{HS}
 =
 (1-r^2)^{-1/2}.
\]
Consequently the estimate
\[
 \|C_\varphi:wH^p(X)\to H^p(X)\|
 \asymp
 \|C_\varphi\|_{HS}^{2/p}
\]
from Theorem 18(2) of \cite{LT2015} cannot extend to any \(1\leq p<2\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write an arbitrary \(f\in wH^p(\ell_2)\) as
\[
 f(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x_n z^n,\qquad x_n\in\ell_2.
\]
For every unit vector \(y\in\ell_2\), the scalar function
\[
 \langle f,y\rangle(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\langle x_n,y\rangle z^n
\]
belongs to \(H^p\) and has norm at most \(\|f\|_{wH^p(\ell_2)}\).  The \(n\)-th
Taylor coefficient functional on \(H^p\) has norm at most \(1\), since
\[
 |a_n|
 =\left|\int_{\T} g(\zeta)\overline{\zeta}^{\,n}\,dm(\zeta)\right|
 \leq \|g\|_{L^1(\T)}
 \leq \|g\|_{H^p}.
\]
Therefore
\[
 \|x_n\|_{\ell_2}
 =
 \sup_{\|y\|_2=1}|\langle x_n,y\rangle|
 \leq \|f\|_{wH^p(\ell_2)}
\]
for every \(n\).

Since \(1\leq p\leq2\), the normalized measure on \(\T\) gives
\(\|h\|_{L^p(\T;\ell_2)}\leq\|h\|_{L^2(\T;\ell_2)}\).  Thus Parseval yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \|C_{\varphi_r}f\|_{H^p(\ell_2)}
 &\leq
 \|C_{\varphi_r}f\|_{H^2(\ell_2)}  \\
 &=
 \left(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^{2n}\|x_n\|_{\ell_2}^2\right)^{1/2}  \\
 &\leq
 \left(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^{2n}\right)^{1/2}
 \|f\|_{wH^p(\ell_2)}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Hence
\[
 \|C_{\varphi_r}:wH^p(\ell_2)\to H^p(\ell_2)\|
 \leq
 (1-r^2)^{-1/2}.
\]

For the reverse inequality, define
\[
 F(z)=(1,z,z^2,\ldots)\in\ell_2\qquad (z\in\D).
\]
For any \(y=(y_n)\in\ell_2\) with \(\|y\|_2\leq1\),
\[
 \langle F,y\rangle(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\overline{y_n}z^n
\]
has \(H^2\)-norm \(\|y\|_2\leq1\), and hence \(H^p\)-norm at most \(1\).
Taking \(y=e_0\) shows \(\|F\|_{wH^p(\ell_2)}=1\).  On the boundary,
\[
 \|F(r\zeta)\|_{\ell_2}
 =
 \left(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}r^{2n}\right)^{1/2}
 =
 (1-r^2)^{-1/2},
\]
independently of \(\zeta\in\T\).  Therefore
\[
 \|C_{\varphi_r}F\|_{H^p(\ell_2)}
 =
 (1-r^2)^{-1/2},
\]
which proves equality.

Finally, on scalar \(H^2\), \(C_{\varphi_r}\) is diagonal with respect to the
orthonormal monomial basis \(z^n\):
\[
 C_{\varphi_r}(z^n)=r^n z^n.
\]
Thus
\[
 \|C_{\varphi_r}:H^2\to H^2\|_{HS}^2
 =
 \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} r^{2n}
 =
 (1-r^2)^{-1}.
\]
For \(1\leq p<2\), the quotient
\[
 \frac{\|C_{\varphi_r}:wH^p(\ell_2)\to H^p(\ell_2)\|}
      {\|C_{\varphi_r}:H^2\to H^2\|_{HS}^{2/p}}
 =
 \|C_{\varphi_r}\|_{HS}^{\,1-2/p}
\]
tends to \(0\) as \(r\uparrow1\).  Hence no lower equivalence constant can hold
for the proposed extension of Theorem 18(2) to \(1\leq p<2\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only the elementary coefficient estimate
\(|\widehat g(n)|\leq\|g\|_{H^p}\), the monotonicity
\(\|h\|_{L^p}\leq\|h\|_{L^2}\) on a probability space for \(p\leq2\), and
Parseval in \(H^2(\ell_2)\).  The example is within the hypotheses of the
source question because \(\ell_2\) is an infinite-dimensional complex Banach
space and \(\varphi_r(z)=rz\) is an analytic self-map of \(\D\) with
Hilbert--Schmidt scalar composition operator on \(H^2\).

The result disproves only the norm-equivalence extension asked in Question
19(a).  It does not challenge Theorem 18(1), since the radial symbols still
give bounded maps \(wH^p(\ell_2)\to H^p(\ell_2)\).

The key review point is interpretive: confirm that ``extends'' in Question
19(a) means a uniform two-sided equivalence with constants independent of the
symbol \(\varphi\), as in Theorem 18(2).  Under that standard reading, the
radial family \(\varphi_r(z)=rz\) gives a direct negative answer.
