\section*{Definitions}

Let $V$ be a real vector space and let $(W,\succeq)$ be a partially ordered real
vector space. A map $f:V\to W$ is \emph{$\succeq$-convex} if
\[
  f(\lambda x+(1-\lambda)y)
  \preceq \lambda f(x)+(1-\lambda)f(y)
\]
for all $x,y\in V$ and all $\lambda\in[0,1]$.

An affine map $a:V\to W$ is a map of the form
\[
  a(x)=Lx+c,\qquad L\in \Lin(V,W),\ c\in W.
\]
For two maps $a,f:V\to W$, write $a\preceq f$ when $a(x)\preceq f(x)$ for every
$x\in V$. Define
\[
  \Aff(f)=\{a:V\to W: a \text{ is affine and } a\preceq f\}.
\]
If $V$ is also partially ordered by $\succeq_V$, let $\Aff_+(f)$ denote the
members of $\Aff(f)$ whose linear part is
$(\succeq_V,\succeq)$-positive.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

For a convex real-valued function, a supporting affine minorant at $x$ can be
obtained from a subgradient at $x$. The same idea works here, but the
subgradient is produced algebraically rather than topologically. At a base
point $x$, take the order-valued directional derivative
\[
  p_x(y)=\inf_{t>0}\frac{f(x+ty)-f(x)}{t}.
\]
Convexity makes these quotients decreasing as $t\downarrow0$ and also gives a
lower bound, so the infimum exists in the Dedekind complete Riesz space $W$.
The function $p_x$ is sublinear. The Hahn-Banach extension theorem quoted in
the source paper therefore gives a linear $L_x\preceq p_x$. Since
$p_x(y)\preceq f(x+y)-f(x)$, the map
\[
  a_x(z)=f(x)+L_x(z-x)
\]
is an affine minorant of $f$ touching $f$ at $x$. Taking one such minorant for
each base point gives the desired envelope representation. If $f$ is monotone,
then $p_x$ is monotone, and the Hahn-Banach-Kantorovich variant gives $L_x$
positive.

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}[Affine envelope for order-convex maps]\label{thm:main}
Let $V$ be a real vector space and let $(W,\succeq)$ be a Dedekind complete
Riesz space. If $f:V\to W$ is $\succeq$-convex, then
\[
  f(x)=\sup_{a\in\Aff(f)} a(x),\qquad x\in V.
\]
The supremum is the lattice supremum in $W$.

If, in addition, $(V,\succeq_V)$ is a partially ordered vector space and $f$ is
$(\succeq_V,\succeq)$-monotone, then
\[
  f(x)=\sup_{a\in\Aff_+(f)} a(x),\qquad x\in V.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{lemma}[Directional quotient]\label{lem:directional}
Fix $x\in V$ and define
\[
  q_t(y)=\frac{f(x+ty)-f(x)}{t},\qquad t>0,\ y\in V.
\]
Then, for each $y\in V$, the family $\{q_t(y):t>0\}$ is bounded below and has an
infimum
\[
  p_x(y):=\inf_{t>0}q_t(y)
\]
in $W$. The map $p_x:V\to W$ is $\succeq$-sublinear, i.e.
\[
  p_x(y)+p_x(z)\succeq p_x(y+z),\qquad
  p_x(\alpha y)=\alpha p_x(y)\quad(\alpha\geq0).
\]
If $V$ is ordered and $f$ is monotone, then $p_x$ is monotone.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If $0<s<t$, then
\[
  x+sy=\left(1-\frac{s}{t}\right)x+\frac{s}{t}(x+ty).
\]
Convexity gives
\[
  f(x+sy)\preceq \left(1-\frac{s}{t}\right)f(x)+\frac{s}{t}f(x+ty),
\]
and hence $q_s(y)\preceq q_t(y)$. Thus the quotients are increasing in the
ordinary parameter $t$, or decreasing as $t\downarrow0$.

They are bounded below. Indeed, for $t>0$,
\[
  x=\frac{1}{1+t}(x+ty)+\frac{t}{1+t}(x-y).
\]
Convexity yields
\[
  f(x)\preceq \frac{1}{1+t}f(x+ty)+\frac{t}{1+t}f(x-y),
\]
which rearranges to
\[
  f(x)-f(x-y)\preceq q_t(y).
\]
Since $W$ is a Dedekind complete Riesz space, lower bounded nonempty subsets
have infima. Hence $p_x(y)$ is well-defined.

Positive homogeneity is immediate for $\alpha>0$ from
\[
  q_t(\alpha y)=\alpha q_{\alpha t}(y),
\]
and the case $\alpha=0$ follows from $q_t(0)=0$.

For subadditivity, convexity gives
\[
  x+t(y+z)=\frac12(x+2ty)+\frac12(x+2tz),
\]
so
\[
  q_t(y+z)\preceq q_{2t}(y)+q_{2t}(z),\qquad t>0.
\]
It remains only to pass to infima. We use the elementary order fact that if
$a_t\downarrow a$ and $b_t\downarrow b$ over the same directed parameter set,
then $a_t+b_t\downarrow a+b$. To verify it, let $c$ be a lower bound of
$a_t+b_t$. For any two parameters $s,u$, choose $t$ below both. Then
$c\preceq a_s+b_u$. Taking the infimum first over $s$ and then over $u$ gives
$c\preceq a+b$. Applying this to $a_t=q_t(y)$ and $b_t=q_t(z)$ gives
\[
  p_x(y+z)
  \preceq \inf_{t>0}\big(q_{2t}(y)+q_{2t}(z)\big)
  =p_x(y)+p_x(z).
\]

Finally, if $f$ is monotone and $y\succeq_V z$, then $x+ty\succeq_V x+tz$ for
all $t>0$, hence $q_t(y)\succeq q_t(z)$ for all $t>0$. Taking infima gives
$p_x(y)\succeq p_x(z)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:main}]
Fix $x\in V$ and form $p_x$ as in Lemma \ref{lem:directional}. By Theorem A.1
of the source paper, applied to the zero subspace and the zero linear map,
there is a linear $L_x:V\to W$ such that
\[
  L_x(y)\preceq p_x(y),\qquad y\in V.
\]
For any $z\in V$, putting $y=z-x$ and using the term $t=1$ in the infimum gives
\[
  L_x(z-x)\preceq p_x(z-x)\preceq f(z)-f(x).
\]
Therefore
\[
  a_x(z):=f(x)+L_x(z-x)
\]
is an affine map satisfying $a_x\preceq f$, and it touches $f$ at $x$:
$a_x(x)=f(x)$.

For each fixed $z$, the set $\{a(z):a\in\Aff(f)\}$ is nonempty and bounded
above by $f(z)$, so its supremum exists in $W$. Since every $a\in\Aff(f)$ is
dominated by $f$, we have
\[
  \sup_{a\in\Aff(f)}a(z)\preceq f(z).
\]
Taking the touching minorant $a_z$ constructed at $z$ gives the reverse
inequality, because $a_z(z)=f(z)$. Hence
\[
  f(z)=\sup_{a\in\Aff(f)}a(z).
\]

If $V$ is ordered and $f$ is monotone, Lemma \ref{lem:directional} shows that
$p_x$ is monotone. Applying Theorem A.2 of the source paper instead of Theorem
A.1 gives $L_x$ positive. The same touching-minorant argument then gives the
representation over $\Aff_+(f)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Largest representing family]\label{cor:largest}
The family $\Aff(f)$ is convex and is the largest family of affine minorants
whose pointwise lattice supremum represents $f$. In the monotone case,
$\Aff_+(f)$ has the corresponding largest-family property among affine
minorants with positive linear part.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Convexity of $\Aff(f)$ is immediate: if $a,b\preceq f$ and
$\lambda\in[0,1]$, then $\lambda a+(1-\lambda)b\preceq f$. If a family
$\mathcal A$ of affine maps represents $f$ by a pointwise supremum, then each
$a\in\mathcal A$ satisfies $a(x)\preceq f(x)$ for all $x$, hence
$\mathcal A\subseteq\Aff(f)$. The same proof applies to $\Aff_+(f)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The source paper's Appendix A proves the Hahn-Banach extension theorem
(Theorem A.1) and the positive Hahn-Banach-Kantorovich variant (Theorem A.2)
for maps into Dedekind complete Riesz spaces. Those are the only external
mathematical tools used here.

The theorem is finite-valued, matching the source paper's maps $h:V\to W$.
It does not address proper extended-valued convex operators, topological
lower-semicontinuity issues, or conditional-expectation penalty
representations. It does, however, give the direct affine-minorant envelope
generalization of the source paper's sublinear envelope theorem and its
monotone-positive refinement.

No computational verification is relevant. The packet was rendered from this
LaTeX source, and the source paper PDF was rebuilt locally from the arXiv
source bundle. The open-question crop is from page 22 of that rebuilt PDF.
