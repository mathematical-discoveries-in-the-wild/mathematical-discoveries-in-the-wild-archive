\section*{Source and Question}

The source paper is Hana Krulisova, \emph{C*-algebras have a quantitative
version of Pelczynski's property (V)}, arXiv:1605.04900. In Section 5, after
recalling Bendova's quantitative Grothendieck property, the paper introduces a
second possible quantification:
\[
  \wck_{X'}\bigl(\{x_n':n\in\N\}\bigr)
  \le c\,\delta_{w^*}(x_n')
\]
for every bounded sequence $(x_n')$ in $X'$. The paper then asks whether this
``latter quantitative Grothendieck property implies the former one (with a
larger constant).'' The former one is Bendova's inequality
\[
  \delta(x_n') \le C\,\delta_{w^*}(x_n')
\]
for every bounded sequence in $X'$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The only possible obstruction is that weak compactness of the range is a
property of the set $\{x_n'\}$, while $\delta(x_n')$ depends on the order of the
sequence. The gap is bridged by looking at two weak-star cluster points
$u,v\in X'''$ obtained from two far-out subsequences of $(x_n')$. Their
difference has two parts. The part detected on the canonical copy of $X$ is
controlled exactly by $\delta_{w^*}(x_n')$. The remaining part annihilates
$X$, and an elementary quotient estimate bounds it by the distances of $u$ and
$v$ from the canonical copy of $X'$, hence by the weak non-compactness measure
$\wck_{X'}(\{x_n'\})$.

\section*{Notation}

For a bounded sequence $(x_n')$ in $X'$, set
\[
\delta(x_n')
= \sup_{x''\in \B_{X''}}
   \lim_{m\to\infty}\sup_{k,\ell\ge m}
   |x_k'(x'')-x_\ell'(x'')|
\]
and
\[
\delta_{w^*}(x_n')
= \sup_{x\in \B_X}
   \lim_{m\to\infty}\sup_{k,\ell\ge m}
   |x_k'(x)-x_\ell'(x)|.
\]
These are the quantities used in the source paper. We regard $X'$ canonically
as a subspace of $X'''$ and write $J:X'\to X'''$ for this embedding. Let
$\rho:X'''\to X'$ be the restriction map to the canonical copy of $X$ in $X''$:
\[
  \rho(u)(x)=u(J_Xx),\qquad u\in X''',\ x\in X.
\]

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:annihilator}
If $h\in X'''$ satisfies $\rho(h)=0$, then
\[
  \|h\| \le 2\,\dist(h,JX').
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $y'\in X'$. Since $\rho(h)=0$, the restriction of $h-Jy'$ to $X$ is
$-y'$. Hence
\[
  \|y'\| \le \|h-Jy'\|.
\]
Therefore
\[
  \|h\| \le \|h-Jy'\|+\|Jy'\|
       = \|h-Jy'\|+\|y'\|
       \le 2\|h-Jy'\|.
\]
Taking the infimum over $y'\in X'$ gives the result.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main-inequality}
Let $X$ be a Banach space and let $(x_n')$ be a bounded sequence in $X'$. Put
$A=\{x_n':n\in\N\}$. Then
\[
  \delta(x_n') \le \delta_{w^*}(x_n')+4\,\wck_{X'}(A).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
  r=\wck_{X'}(A), \qquad d=\delta_{w^*}(x_n').
\]
Fix $x''\in \B_{X''}$. We show that the tail oscillation of the scalar
sequence $(x_n'(x''))$ is at most $d+4r$.

Let $L$ denote this tail oscillation. If $L=0$ there is nothing to prove. For
$\varepsilon>0$, choose subsequences $(x_{p_j}')$ and $(x_{q_j}')$ with
$p_j,q_j\to\infty$ such that
\[
  |x_{p_j}'(x'')-x_{q_j}'(x'')|>L-\varepsilon
\]
along a further scalar-convergent choice. By weak-star compactness in $X'''$,
pass to weak-star cluster points $u$ of $(x_{p_j}')$ and $v$ of $(x_{q_j}')$
so that
\[
  |u(x'')-v(x'')| \ge L-\varepsilon.
\]
Both $u$ and $v$ are weak-star cluster points of sequences in $A$, so by the
definition of $\wck$,
\[
  \dist(u,JX')\le r,\qquad \dist(v,JX')\le r.
\]

For each $x\in \B_X$, the two numbers $u(J_Xx)$ and $v(J_Xx)$ are cluster
values of the scalar sequence $(x_n'(x))$ along tails. Hence
\[
  |\rho(u-v)(x)| \le d.
\]
Thus $\|\rho(u-v)\|\le d$.

Set
\[
  h=(u-v)-J\rho(u-v).
\]
Then $\rho(h)=0$. Moreover, since $J\rho(u-v)\in JX'$,
\[
  \dist(h,JX')=\dist(u-v,JX')
  \le \dist(u,JX')+\dist(v,JX')\le 2r.
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:annihilator} gives $\|h\|\le 4r$. Therefore
\[
\begin{aligned}
  L-\varepsilon
  &\le |u(x'')-v(x'')| \\
  &\le |J\rho(u-v)(x'')|+\|h\| \\
  &= |x''(\rho(u-v))|+\|h\| \\
  &\le d+4r.
\end{aligned}
\]
Letting $\varepsilon\downarrow 0$ and then taking the supremum over
$x''\in\B_{X''}$ proves the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:source-question}
Suppose that $X$ satisfies the source paper's range-weak-compactness
quantitative Grothendieck property with constant $c$, i.e.
\[
  \wck_{X'}\bigl(\{x_n':n\in\N\}\bigr)
  \le c\,\delta_{w^*}(x_n')
\]
for every bounded sequence $(x_n')$ in $X'$. Then $X$ is
$(1+4c)$-Grothendieck in Bendova's sense:
\[
  \delta(x_n')\le (1+4c)\,\delta_{w^*}(x_n')
\]
for every bounded sequence $(x_n')$ in $X'$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:main-inequality} and substitute the assumed bound for
$\wck_{X'}(\{x_n':n\in\N\})$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequence for the Source Paper}

This gives an affirmative answer to the question asked on page 9 of
arXiv:1605.04900: the second quantitative Grothendieck property does imply
Bendova's quantitative Grothendieck property, with an explicit larger constant.
In particular, combining this packet with Corollary 5.2 of the source paper,
any dual Banach space whose dual has the quantitative property $(V_q)$ is
quantitatively Grothendieck in Bendova's sense with the corresponding enlarged
constant. For a von Neumann algebra, the source paper gives
\[
  \wck_{A'}(\{x_n':n\in\N\})\le \frac{\pi}{2}\,\delta_{w^*}(x_n'),
\]
so the present result yields the Bendova inequality with constant
$1+2\pi$.

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not settle the separate question, asked earlier in the same
paper, whether all $C^*$-algebras have the property $(V_q)_{\omega^*}$, nor
the broader question whether every Banach space with $(V_q)$ has
$(V_q)_{\omega^*}$. The result here is only about the two quantitative
Grothendieck formulations in Section 5.
