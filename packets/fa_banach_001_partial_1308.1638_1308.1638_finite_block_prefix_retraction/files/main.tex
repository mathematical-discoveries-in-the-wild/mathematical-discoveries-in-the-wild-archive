\section*{Source and question}

The source is Sun Kwang Kim and Han Ju Lee,
\emph{Simultaneously continuous retraction and Bishop-Phelps-Bollobas type
theorem}, arXiv:1308.1638.  A dual space \(X^*\) is said there to admit a
uniformly simultaneously continuous retraction if there is a retraction
\(r:X^*\to B_{X^*}\) which is weak-star continuous and uniformly continuous for
the norm topology.

After proving the \(\ell_1\)-sum stability theorem in Proposition 2.5, the
paper records the following open point:

\begin{quote}
``We do not know that the similar result of [Proposition 2.5] holds for
\(c_0\) or \(\ell_p\) sums for \(1<p<\infty\).''
\end{quote}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=.92\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The paper then gives a positive result for separable uniformly smooth summands
whose duals have uniformly convex moduli bounded away from zero.  The result
below gives a different positive subcase: arbitrary finite-dimensional blocks,
with no rotundity or smoothness assumption on their norms.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The usual radial projection \(z\mapsto z/\max\{1,\|z\|\}\) is uniformly norm
continuous, but it is not weak-star continuous on dual sequence spaces.  The
replacement is an ordered prefix truncation.  Given
\(z=(z_n)\in(\oplus E_n)_{\ell_q}\), keep the initial blocks until the
cumulative \(q\)-mass first reaches one, scale only that last block so that
the new \(q\)-mass is exactly one, and erase the later tail.  This is the
same geometric idea as the standard truncation of \(\ell_1\), but applied to
block norms and \(q\)-powers.

The key point is that weak-star convergence sees every fixed finite group of
blocks in norm when the blocks are finite-dimensional.  If the crossing block
does not stabilize, it can only run to infinity, and the erased or newly
created tail disappears against the \(c_0\) or \(\ell_p\) predual.  Norm
continuity follows from a uniform Holder estimate for this first-crossing
map.

\section*{The partial result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \((F_n)_{n\ge 1}\) be finite-dimensional real or complex Banach spaces.
Let either
\[
  X=\Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^{\infty} F_n\Big)_{c_0}
  \qquad\text{or}\qquad
  X=\Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^{\infty} F_n\Big)_{\ell_p},\quad 1<p<\infty .
\]
Then \(X^*\) admits a uniformly simultaneously continuous retraction onto
\(B_{X^*}\).

Consequently, in this finite-dimensional-block case, the source paper's
direct-sum stability question has an affirmative answer for \(c_0\)-sums and
for \(\ell_p\)-sums, \(1<p<\infty\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write
\[
  E_n=F_n^*, \qquad
  Z=\Big(\bigoplus_{n=1}^{\infty}E_n\Big)_{\ell_q},
\]
where \(q=1\) in the \(c_0\)-sum case and \(q=p/(p-1)\) in the
\(\ell_p\)-sum case.  Then \(Z=X^*\) with its canonical weak-star topology.

For \(z=(z_n)\in Z\), put \(a_n(z)=\|z_n\|\).  If \(\|z\|_q\le 1\), define
\(Rz=z\).  If \(\|z\|_q>1\), let \(m=m(z)\) be the first integer such that
\[
  \sum_{n=1}^{m} a_n(z)^q\ge 1.
\]
Then \(\sum_{n<m}a_n(z)^q<1\), and \(a_m(z)>0\).  Define
\[
  \lambda(z)=
  \frac{\bigl(1-\sum_{n<m}a_n(z)^q\bigr)^{1/q}}{a_m(z)}
  \in(0,1],
\]
and set
\[
  (Rz)_n =
  \begin{cases}
  z_n, & n<m,\\
  \lambda(z)z_m, & n=m,\\
  0, & n>m.
  \end{cases}
\]
It is immediate that \(Rz\in B_Z\), that \(\|Rz\|_q=1\) whenever
\(\|z\|_q>1\), and that \(R\) is the identity on \(B_Z\).  Thus \(R\) is a
retraction onto \(B_Z\).

We first record the norm estimate.  For \(1\le q<\infty\), the map \(R\) is
uniformly continuous on every \(\ell_q\)-sum of normed spaces.  More precisely,
there is a constant \(C_q\) such that
\[
  \|Ru-Rv\|_q \le C_q \|u-v\|_q^{1/q}
  \quad\text{whenever } \|u-v\|_q\le 1,
\]
and the trivial bound \(\|Ru-Rv\|\le 2\) handles larger distances.

Here is the estimate.  Let \(\delta=\|u-v\|_q\le 1\).  If both \(u\) and \(v\)
belong to \(B_Z\), there is nothing to prove.  If, say, \(\|u\|_q\le 1\) and
\(\|v\|_q>1\), then \(\|v\|_q\le 1+\delta\), and the erased part satisfies
\[
  \|v-Rv\|_q^q
  \le \|v\|_q^q-1
  \le (1+\delta)^q-1
  \le 2^q q\,\delta .
\]
Hence \(\|Ru-Rv\|_q\le \delta+2q^{1/q}\delta^{1/q}\).

It remains to consider the case where both points are outside \(B_Z\).  Let
\(m(u)\) and \(m(v)\) be their crossing indices.  If \(m(u)=m(v)=m\), then all
coordinates except \(m\) are either unchanged or erased in the same way.  For
the \(m\)-th coordinate, write \(u_m=a\xi\), \(v_m=b\eta\) with
\(\|\xi\|=\|\eta\|=1\) when the coordinate is nonzero, and let
\[
  s=\Big(1-\sum_{n<m}\|u_n\|^q\Big)^{1/q},\qquad
  t=\Big(1-\sum_{n<m}\|v_n\|^q\Big)^{1/q}.
\]
The scalar inequality \(|\alpha^{1/q}-\beta^{1/q}|\le
|\alpha-\beta|^{1/q}\) for \(\alpha,\beta\ge 0\), together with
\(\|P_{m-1}u-P_{m-1}v\|_q\le\delta\), gives \(|s-t|\le C_q\delta^{1/q}\).
Also \(|a-b|\le\|u_m-v_m\|\).  Since
\[
  s\xi-t\eta=(u_m-v_m)-\bigl((a-s)\xi-(b-t)\eta\bigr),
\]
and
\[
  \|(a-s)\xi-(b-t)\eta\|
  \le |(a-s)-(b-t)|+2\|u_m-v_m\|,
\]
we get \(\|s\xi-t\eta\|\le C_q\delta^{1/q}\).  Adding the unchanged prefix
gives the desired bound in the equal-crossing case.

If \(m(u)<m(v)\), then the \(m(u)\)-prefix of \(v\) has norm \(<1\), while the
\(m(u)\)-prefix of \(u\) has norm at least \(1\).  Since these prefixes are
within \(\delta\), the \(m(u)\)-prefix of \(u\) has norm at most \(1+\delta\).
The same estimate as above shows that replacing this prefix by the truncated
prefix \(Ru\) changes it by at most \(C_q\delta^{1/q}\).  Moreover, the part
of \(Rv\) after the \(m(u)\)-th block has \(q\)-mass at most
\[
  1-\|P_{m(u)}v\|_q^q \le C_q\delta .
\]
Therefore \(\|Ru-Rv\|_q\le C_q\delta^{1/q}\).  The case \(m(v)<m(u)\) is
symmetric.  This proves uniform norm continuity.

It remains to prove weak-star continuity.  Let \(z_\alpha\to z\) weak-star in
Z.  Since the blocks \(F_n\) are finite-dimensional, each coordinate
\((z_\alpha)_n\to z_n\) in norm, and hence every finite prefix norm converges.
The net \((Rz_\alpha)\) is contained in \(B_Z\), so coordinatewise weak-star
convergence is enough to test against elements of the predual
\((\oplus F_n)_{c_0}\) when \(q=1\), and against elements of
\((\oplus F_n)_{\ell_p}\) when \(1<q<\infty\), by first truncating the
predual vector to finitely many coordinates.

Fix a coordinate \(k\).  If \(z\) is outside the unit ball and its crossing
index is \(m\), then for every \(j<m\) the crossing inequalities are eventually
unchanged, so \((Rz_\alpha)_j\to (Rz)_j\).  If the crossing of \(z\) is strict
at \(m\), the crossing index of \(z_\alpha\) is eventually \(m\), and the
\(m\)-th coordinate also converges in norm.  If the \(m\)-th prefix of \(z\)
has norm exactly one, then later crossings of \(z_\alpha\), if they occur,
have remaining \(q\)-mass tending to zero; hence every fixed coordinate
after \(m\) tends to zero, while the first \(m\) coordinates converge to those
of \(Rz\).

If \(z\in B_Z\), then each finite prefix of \(z\) has norm strictly less than
one unless \(z\) has a finite prefix of norm one.  In the first subcase the
crossing index of \(z_\alpha\), when it exists, eventually lies after any
prescribed finite prefix, so the fixed coordinates of \(Rz_\alpha\) agree
eventually with those of \(z_\alpha\).  In the finite-prefix boundary subcase,
the same zero-remaining-mass argument used above applies.  Thus
\((Rz_\alpha)_k\to (Rz)_k\) for each fixed \(k\), and consequently
\(Rz_\alpha\to Rz\) weak-star.

Therefore \(R:X^*\to B_{X^*}\) is a weak-star continuous, uniformly norm
continuous retraction.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and novelty notes}

The construction was checked against the exact source passage on page 6 of
arXiv:1308.1638.  The run indexes were searched for `1308.1638`,
`simultaneously continuous retraction`, `Bishop-Phelps-Bollobas`,
`continuous retraction`, `BPBp`, and related direct-sum terms; no existing run
packet for this paper or this finite-dimensional-block subcase was found.
Web and citation checks found the source arXiv page and one Semantic Scholar
citation, arXiv:1407.7872, concerning BPBp for operators from \(C(K)\) into
uniformly convex spaces rather than this retraction stability question.  Local
corpus searches for the exact retraction terminology found no later closure of
the direct-sum stability problem.

The proof is independent of the later uniformly convex-dual theorem in the
source paper.  Its finite-dimensional hypothesis is essential for this method:
it is used precisely to make coordinate norms weak-star continuous.

\section*{Human review recommendation}

Recommended review focus: check the uniform-continuity estimate for the
first-crossing truncation map, especially the equal-crossing case.  The
weak-star continuity argument is the conceptual core and should be compared
with the source paper's basis truncation proof.
