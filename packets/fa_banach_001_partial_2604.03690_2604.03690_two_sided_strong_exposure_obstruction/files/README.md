## Result

The packet proves a geometric obstruction adjacent to the source paper's open
problem asking whether every complex reflexive Banach space with numerical
index one must be finite-dimensional.

If a complex Banach space `X` of dimension at least two has a support pair
`(x,x*)` such that `x*` strongly exposes `B_X` at `x` and `x` strongly exposes
`B_{X*}` at `x*`, then `n(X)<1`. Equivalently, a complex Banach space with
`n(X)=1` and dimension bigger than one cannot contain such a two-sided strongly
exposed support pair.

In particular, any possible infinite-dimensional complex reflexive example with
`n(X)=1` must avoid even one point where the norm is both locally uniformly
rotund and Frechet smooth in this two-sided support sense.

## Scope

This does not settle the full complex reflexive open problem. Reflexivity gives
dentability/RNP structure, but not necessarily a matching two-sided strongly
exposed support pair for the original norm. The packet records the resulting
obstruction as a partial result and a useful structural restriction on any
counterexample.
