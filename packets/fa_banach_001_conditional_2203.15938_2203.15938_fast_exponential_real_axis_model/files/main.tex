\section*{Status}

This is a conditional reduction packet.  It does not claim a full solution of
the open question in Arnal--Siegl.  The packet proves the limiting first-order
model and records the exact localization dependency that would promote the
result to a full real-axis asymptotic for the source Schrodinger operator.

\section*{Source and Open Question}

The source is Antonio Arnal and Petr Siegl, ``Resolvent estimates for
one-dimensional Schrodinger operators with complex potentials'', arXiv:2203.15938,
published in \emph{Journal of Functional Analysis} 284 (2023), Article 109856
\cite{ArnalSiegl2023}.  Section 7.3 considers
\[
    H=-\partial_x^2+i\e^{x^2},\qquad
    \Dom(H)=W^{2,2}(\R)\cap \Dom(\e^{x^2}),
\]
and gives the imaginary-axis asymptotic.  It then states that the real-axis
Theorem 4.2 is not applicable and leaves the critical region next to the real
axis open.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Arnal--Siegl's real-axis proof localizes the Fourier variable near the turning
frequencies \(\pm\sqrt a\), rescales by a parameter \(t_a\) satisfying
\(t_aV_2(t_a)=2\sqrt a\), and obtains a first-order model
\(-\partial_x+V_2(t_ax)/V_2(t_a)\).  For regularly varying \(V_2\), this model
converges to \(-\partial_x+|x|^\beta\).

For \(V_2(x)=\e^{x^2}\), the normalized model is instead
\[
    B_t=-\partial_x+\e^{t^2(x^2-1)}.
\]
The potential tends to zero on \((-1,1)\) and to \(+\infty\) outside that
interval.  Thus \(B_t^{-1}\) should converge to Volterra integration on
\((-1,1)\), with a one-sided boundary condition at \(1\).  The Volterra norm on
an interval of length \(2\) is \(4/\pi\).  This packet proves that model
statement rigorously.

\section*{Conditional Dependencies}

\begin{dependency}[real-axis localization transfer]\label{dep:transfer}
Let \(t_a>0\) be determined by \(t_a\e^{t_a^2}=2\sqrt a\), and let
\[
    B_t=-\partial_x+\e^{t^2(x^2-1)}
\]
be realized in \(L^2(\R)\) with maximal domain
\[
    \Dom(B_t)=\{u\in L^2(\R):-u'+\e^{t^2(x^2-1)}u\in L^2(\R)\}.
\]
The required transfer assertion is
\[
    \|(H-a)^{-1}\|
    = \e^{-t_a^2}\,\|B_{t_a}^{-1}\|\,(1+o(1)),
    \qquad a\to+\infty.
\]
\end{dependency}

Dependency \ref{dep:transfer} is exactly where the source proof does not apply:
the regular-variation limit \(V_2(tx)/V_2(t)\to |x|^\beta\) and the symbol
estimate \(|V_2^{(n)}(x)|\le C_n(1+V_2(x))\langle x\rangle^{-n}\) fail for
\(V_2(x)=\e^{x^2}\).  The rest of this packet is independent of that missing
step.

\section*{The Model Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[fast exponential first-order model]\label{thm:model}
For \(t>0\), let
\[
    B_t=-\partial_x+W_t(x),
    \qquad W_t(x)=\e^{t^2(x^2-1)}
\]
on \(L^2(\R)\) with maximal domain.  Then \(0\in\rho(B_t)\), and
\[
    \|B_t^{-1}\|\longrightarrow \frac{4}{\pi},
    \qquad t\to+\infty.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The source appendix proves the needed maximal-accretive framework for
operators \(-\partial_x+W\) with \(\Re W\ge0\) and confining real part.  In the
present real positive case \(W_t(x)\to+\infty\) as \(|x|\to\infty\), so
\(B_t\) has compact resolvent and empty spectrum.  Solving the first-order
equation with the \(L^2\) condition at \(+\infty\) gives the inverse kernel
\[
    (B_t^{-1}f)(x)=\int_x^\infty K_t(x,y)f(y)\,\dd y,
    \qquad
    K_t(x,y)=\mathbf 1_{\{x<y\}}
       \exp\!\left(-\int_x^y W_t(s)\,\dd s\right).
\]
Let
\[
    K_0(x,y)=\mathbf 1_{\{-1<x<y<1\}},
\]
and let \(T_0\) be the corresponding integral operator.  Pointwise,
\(K_t(x,y)\to K_0(x,y)\) away from the boundary points \(\pm1\).  Indeed,
on compact subintervals of \((-1,1)\), \(W_t\to0\), while on any interval with
positive distance from \((-1,1)\), \(W_t\to+\infty\) uniformly.

We now show convergence in Hilbert--Schmidt norm.  Fix \(R>1\).  On
the finite square \([-R,R]^2\), dominated convergence gives
\[
    \iint_{[-R,R]^2}|K_t-K_0|^2\,\dd x\,\dd y \to0.
\]
It remains to control the tails.  Since \(W_t(\pm R)=\e^{t^2(R^2-1)}\to+\infty\),
the regions \(x,y>R\) and \(x,y<-R\) satisfy
\[
    \iint_{\{R<x<y\}}K_t(x,y)^2\,\dd x\,\dd y
      \le \frac{1}{2}\int_R^\infty \frac{\dd x}{W_t(x)}\to0,
\]
and, by the same estimate after reflecting \(x\mapsto -x\),
\[
    \iint_{\{x<y<-R\}}K_t(x,y)^2\,\dd x\,\dd y\to0.
\]
For \(x<-R<y<R\), the interval \([x,-R]\) lies where \(W_t\ge W_t(R)\), hence
\[
    K_t(x,y)^2\le \exp\{-2(-R-x)W_t(R)\},
\]
and integration over \(x<-R\) and \(-R<y<R\) gives a bound \(R/W_t(R)\to0\).
The region \(-R<x<R<y\) is identical, and the mixed region \(x<-R<R<y\) is
even smaller.  Therefore \(K_t\to K_0\) in \(L^2(\R^2)\), and
\[
    \|B_t^{-1}-T_0\|\le \|K_t-K_0\|_{L^2(\R^2)}\to0.
\]

It remains to compute \(\|T_0\|\).  Under the translation of \((-1,1)\) to
\((0,2)\), \(T_0\) becomes the Volterra operator \(Vf(s)=\int_s^2 f(r)\,\dd r\).
The non-zero singular values of the Volterra operator on an interval of length
\(L\) are \(2L/((2n+1)\pi)\), \(n=0,1,\ldots\), obtained by differentiating the
eigenvalue equation for \(V^*V\).  With \(L=2\), the largest singular value is
\(4/\pi\).  Hence \(\|B_t^{-1}\|\to4/\pi\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Conditional Consequence for the Source Operator}

\begin{theorem}[conditional real-axis asymptotic]\label{thm:conditional}
Assume Dependency \ref{dep:transfer}.  Then, for
\[
    H=-\partial_x^2+i\e^{x^2},\qquad
    t_a\e^{t_a^2}=2\sqrt a,
\]
one has
\[
    \|(H-a)^{-1}\|=\frac{4}{\pi}\e^{-t_a^2}(1+o(1)),
    \qquad a\to+\infty.
\]
Equivalently, since \(t_a^2=\frac12 W_0(8a)\),
\[
    \|(H-a)^{-1}\|
    =\frac{\sqrt2}{\pi}\sqrt{\frac{W_0(8a)}{a}}\,(1+o(1))
    =\frac{\sqrt2}{\pi}\frac{\sqrt{\log a}}{\sqrt a}\,(1+o(1)).
\]
Moreover, for every fixed \(\eta>0\), the resolvent is conditionally uniformly
bounded in the region
\[
    0\le b<\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\eta\right)\e^{t_a^2}
\]
next to the positive real axis.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The norm asymptotic follows immediately by combining Dependency
\ref{dep:transfer} with Theorem \ref{thm:model}.  The Lambert \(W\) formula is
obtained from
\[
    t_a\e^{t_a^2}=2\sqrt a
    \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
    2t_a^2\e^{2t_a^2}=8a.
\]
For the boundedness region, the triangle inequality gives
\[
    \|(H-a-ib)u\|\ge \|(H-a)u\|-b\|u\|.
\]
The preceding asymptotic says that the lower bound for \(H-a\) is
\[
    \|(H-a)u\|\ge \left(\frac{\pi}{4}\e^{t_a^2}+o(\e^{t_a^2})\right)\|u\|.
\]
Thus any fixed margin below \((\pi/4)\e^{t_a^2}\) gives a uniform resolvent
bound.
\end{proof}

fast-exponential real-axis edge.  Because the packet is conditional, this is
recorded only as a bounded status check, not as a novelty claim for a full
solution.

\section*{Limitations and Human Review Focus}

The proved model theorem does not by itself answer the source question.  The
missing step is to prove Dependency \ref{dep:transfer}.  A reviewer should
focus on whether the Fourier-side localization, partition of unity, and
commutator estimates in the proof of Arnal--Siegl Theorem 4.2 can be replaced
by estimates adapted to \(V_2(x)=\e^{x^2}\).  The obstruction is concrete:
the derivatives of \(\e^{x^2}\) grow like polynomials times \(\e^{x^2}\), so the
source's symbol-class estimates are not available globally.
