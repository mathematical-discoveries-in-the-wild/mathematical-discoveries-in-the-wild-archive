# Literature Status: Garling Sequence Spaces Have No Symmetric Basis

Status: `literature_already_answered`.

Target source: Fernando Albiac, Jose L. Ansorena, and Ben Wallis, "On Garling sequence spaces", arXiv:1703.07772.

Extracted question: Section 7, final question asks whether there exist a weight `w in W` and `1 <= p < infinity` such that the Garling sequence space `g(w,p)` admits a symmetric basis.

Answering source: Fernando Albiac, Jose L. Ansorena, Denny Leung, and Ben Wallis, "Optimality of the rearrangement inequality with applications to Lorentz-type sequence spaces", arXiv:1705.03936.

Why this answers it: the supporting paper explicitly says in its abstract and introduction that it settles a problem raised in arXiv:1703.07772 and proves that Garling sequence spaces have no symmetric basis. In Section 3, Lemma 3.1 proves the canonical basis is not symmetric for any `w in W` and `1 <= p < infinity`; Theorem 3.2(i) then states the full structural conclusion: there is no symmetric basis for `g(w,p)`.

Scope: this is a complete negative answer to the extracted existence question. No subcase of that question remains open in this packet.
