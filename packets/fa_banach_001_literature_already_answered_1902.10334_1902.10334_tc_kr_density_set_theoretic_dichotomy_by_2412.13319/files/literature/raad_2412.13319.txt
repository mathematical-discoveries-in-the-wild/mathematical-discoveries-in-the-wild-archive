1.Introduction
The Lipschitz free space over a metric spaceM, denoted byF(M), is
a Banach space that encodes in its linear structure the metric structure of
M. It is related to many areas of mathematics, including metric geometry
([2]), harmonic analysis ([6]) and machine learning ([10]). We will focus on
its relation to measure theory, exemplified by its construction via the closed
span of Dirac Measures ([8]) and by its relation to the mass transfer prob-
lem ([11] Chapter 3.3). More specifically we will focus on a problem in [3]
regarding a theorem similar to the Riesz–Markov–Kakutani Representation
Theorem.
From [3] Proposition 4.31 we know that ifµis a Borel measure on a
pointed complete metric spaceM, then the mappingLµdefined byLµ(f) =R
f dµis an element ofF(M) ∗∗ if and only if
R

from [3] Proposition 4.4 we know that ifµis also inner-regular orMis
separable thenLµ∈ F(M). Problem 2 in [3] asks if the hypothesis thatµ
is inner-regular is indeed necessary forLµto be an element ofF(M).
