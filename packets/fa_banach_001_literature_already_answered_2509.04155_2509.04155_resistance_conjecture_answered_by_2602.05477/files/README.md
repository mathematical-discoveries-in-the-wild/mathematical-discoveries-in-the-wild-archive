# Resistance Conjecture Status Answered by 2602.05477

Status: `literature_already_answered (resistance conjecture quoted in 2509.04155)`

Source target: Riku Anttila, "An Approach to Sub-Gaussian Heat Kernel Estimates via Analysis on Metric Spaces", arXiv:2509.04155.

Supporting answer: Sylvester Eriksson-Bique, "On the Resistance Conjecture", arXiv:2602.05477.

## Identification

Section 7 of arXiv:2509.04155 discusses the Grigor'yan-Hu-Lau resistance conjecture and states it as Conjecture 4.15 from the 2014 heat-kernel/capacity paper: a strongly local regular Dirichlet form satisfies heat-kernel estimates if and only if it satisfies the Poincare inequality and the upper capacity estimate.

The same section explains that, in the paper's own Theorem 6.4, the cutoff Sobolev condition is replaced by the cutoff energy condition, and that replacing this by the simpler upper capacity estimate is exactly the resistance conjecture.

## Supporting Answer

The later paper arXiv:2602.05477 explicitly states in its abstract that it gives an affirmative answer to the resistance conjecture. In Theorem 1.1 it proves the equivalence between parabolic Harnack, heat-kernel estimates with volume doubling, volume doubling plus elliptic Harnack plus two-sided capacity, and volume doubling plus the Poincare inequality plus the upper capacity bound.

This is the advertised resistance-conjecture replacement of cutoff Sobolev by upper capacity bounds, in the standard volume-doubling formulation. The supporting paper cites arXiv:2509.04155 and notes that Anttila's paper had published a closely related approach and partial results.

## Scope

This packet records status only. It is not a new proof. It does not address the sharper open question isolated in the existing attempt for arXiv:2602.05477: whether upper capacity plus separated-ball lower capacity plus volume doubling imply the Poincare inequality itself.
