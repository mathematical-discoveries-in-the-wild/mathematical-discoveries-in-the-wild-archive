\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Sudeshna Basu and Susmita Seal,
\emph{Ball separation characterization of ball dentability and related
properties}, arXiv:2307.03621, version 4 \cite{BasuSeal2023}.

Definition 4.1 defines semi denting points and their weak-star analogues.
Remark 4.3 observes that if \(x_0^*\in S_{X^*}\) is a semi \(w^*\)-denting
point of \(B_{X^*}\), then \(x_0^*\) is a norm limit of extreme points of
\(B_{X^*}\). The same remark says that it is not known whether every semi
\(w^*\)-denting point must be extreme. In the parsed source this is
\texttt{data/parsed/arxiv\_sources/2307.03621/Ball\_sep\_arxiv.tex}, lines
818--835, and in the PDF it is page 13.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The definition of a semi denting point is deliberately one-sided: for each
\(\varepsilon>0\) there must be a small slice contained in
\(B(x,\varepsilon)\), but the slice need not contain \(x\). Therefore every
norm limit of denting points is automatically semi denting. To disprove the
extreme-point conclusion, it is enough to build a finite-dimensional dual ball
with a non-extreme boundary point that is a norm limit of exposed points.
Finite dimensionality then makes the weak-star and norm topologies coincide.

\section*{Construction}

Work in \(\mathbb R^4\) with coordinates \((s,u,v,t)\). For \(0\le r\le 1\),
put
\[
c(r)=(0,r,r^2,1),\qquad
a=(-1,0,0,1),\qquad b=(1,0,0,1),
\]
and define
\[
F=\operatorname{co}\bigl(\{a,b\}\cup\{c(r):0\le r\le 1\}\bigr),\qquad
K=\operatorname{co}(F\cup(-F)).
\]
The set \(K\) is compact, convex, centrally symmetric, and full dimensional.
Hence \(K\) is the closed unit ball of a four-dimensional real Banach space
\(Y\). Let
\[
p=c(0)=(0,0,0,1).
\]
Since \(p=(a+b)/2\) and \(a\ne b\), \(p\) is not an extreme point of \(K\).
Also \(p\in S_Y\), because the coordinate functional \(t\) satisfies
\(\sup_K t=1\) and \(t(p)=1\).

\begin{lemma}
For every \(r_0\in(0,1]\), the point \(c(r_0)\) is an exposed point of \(K\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(r_0\in(0,1]\) and set
\[
g_{r_0}(s,u,v,t)=2r_0u-v.
\]
On the curve \(c(r)\),
\[
g_{r_0}(c(r))=2r_0r-r^2=r_0^2-(r-r_0)^2\le r_0^2,
\]
with equality only when \(r=r_0\). On the segment \([a,b]\), the value of
\(g_{r_0}\) is \(0<r_0^2\). By linearity, \(g_{r_0}\) has the unique maximum
\(r_0^2\) on \(F\) at \(c(r_0)\).

Let \(M=\sup\{|g_{r_0}(y)|:y\in F\}\), and choose
\(\lambda>0\) so small that \(-1+\lambda M<1\). Consider
\[
\Phi(y)=t(y)+\lambda g_{r_0}(y).
\]
On \(F\), where \(t=1\), the unique maximum of \(\Phi\) occurs at \(c(r_0)\).
On \(-F\), where \(t=-1\), we have \(\Phi(y)\le -1+\lambda M<1\), while
\(\Phi(c(r_0))=1+\lambda r_0^2>1\). Thus \(c(r_0)\) is the unique maximizer of
\(\Phi\) on \(K\), so it is exposed.
\end{proof}

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
There exists a Banach space \(X\) and a point \(p\in S_{X^*}\) such that
\(p\) is a semi \(w^*\)-denting point of \(B_{X^*}\), but \(p\) is not an
extreme point of \(B_{X^*}\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(Y\) be the four-dimensional Banach space whose unit ball is the set
\(K\) constructed above, and let \(X=Y^*\). Since \(Y\) is finite dimensional,
we identify \(X^*\) canonically with \(Y\), and the weak-star topology on
\(X^*=Y\) agrees with the norm topology.

We already observed that \(p=c(0)\in S_Y\) is not extreme in \(K=B_Y\). It
remains to show that \(p\) is semi denting, hence semi \(w^*\)-denting under
the identification \(X^*=Y\).

For each \(n\ge 1\), the point \(c(1/n)\) is exposed by the lemma. In a compact
finite-dimensional convex set, every exposed point is a denting point: if a
linear functional has a unique maximum at \(y\), compactness implies that the
slices
\[
\{z\in K:\phi(z)>\phi(y)-\alpha\}
\]
shrink in norm to \(y\) as \(\alpha\downarrow0\). Hence each \(c(1/n)\) is a
denting point of \(K\).

The sequence \(c(1/n)\) converges to \(p\) in the Euclidean topology, and
therefore in the norm of \(Y\). Given \(\varepsilon>0\), choose \(n\) such that
\(\|c(1/n)-p\|_Y<\varepsilon/2\). Since \(c(1/n)\) is denting, choose a slice
\(S\) of \(K\) containing \(c(1/n)\) with diameter less than
\(\varepsilon/2\). Then
\[
S\subset B_Y(c(1/n),\varepsilon/2)\subset B_Y(p,\varepsilon).
\]
This is exactly the semi denting condition for \(p\). Since weak-star and norm
topologies coincide in finite dimension, \(p\) is a semi \(w^*\)-denting point
of \(B_{X^*}\). But \(p\) is not extreme, as \(p=(a+b)/2\) with \(a\ne b\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof is finite-dimensional and does not rely on unproved external
theorems beyond standard compact-convex facts: exposed points of compact
finite-dimensional convex bodies are denting points, and all Hausdorff vector
topologies on a finite-dimensional space coincide. The construction is also
stable under replacing the continuum \(\{c(r):0\le r\le1\}\) by a sufficiently
rich compact convex curve with exposed points accumulating at \(c(0)\).

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

The main point for review is the exposure argument for \(c(r_0)\) and the
interpretation of semi denting as allowing the small slice to lie near \(p\)
without containing \(p\). If both are accepted, the counterexample directly
answers the question in Remark 4.3 negatively.
