\section*{Status}

This packet gives a partial positive answer to the endpoint question after
Lemma 3.1 in Hytonen--Veraar \cite{HV2008}.  The source proves the estimate
with $\widetilde q>q$ for Banach spaces of cotype $q$, and asks whether one can
take $\widetilde q=q$ when $q\ne 2$.  The result below proves the endpoint for
a natural lattice class: Banach lattices with the Rademacher square-function
equivalence and $q$-concavity.

\section*{Source passage}

The source passage, on PDF page 7, reads that the authors do not know whether
one can take $\widetilde q=q$ in Lemma 3.1(2) if $q\ne 2$, while noting the

\section*{Definitions}

For a finite sequence $(x_n)$ in a Banach space $X$, write
\[
 \|(x_n)\|_{\Rad(X)}
 := \left(\mathbb E\left\|\sum_n r_n x_n\right\|_X^2\right)^{1/2},
\]
where $(r_n)$ are independent Rademacher signs.

Let $X$ be a Banach lattice.  Say that $X$ has square-function constant $K$
if, for every finite sequence $(x_n)$ in $X$,
\[
 K^{-1}\left\|\left(\sum_n |x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\|_X
 \leq \|(x_n)\|_{\Rad(X)}
 \leq
 K\left\|\left(\sum_n |x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\|_X.
\]
We say that $X$ is $q$-concave with constant $M$ if, for all finite positive
families $(u_j)$ in $X$,
\[
 \left(\sum_j \|u_j\|_X^q\right)^{1/q}
 \leq
 M\left\|\left(\sum_j u_j^q\right)^{1/q}\right\|_X.
\]
The same inequality for simple positive $X$-valued functions follows by
approximation.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The source proof loses the endpoint because the general cotype factorization
lands in a Lorentz space.  In a lattice with a square-function model for
Rademacher sums, the endpoint estimate can be checked pointwise in the lattice
variable.  At each point it becomes the elementary scalar inequality
\[
\left\|\left(\sum_n |a_n f_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\|_{L^q(S)}
\leq
\left(\sum_n |a_n|^2 \|f_n\|_{L^q(S)}^2\right)^{1/2},
\]
valid because $q\geq 2$.  The $q$-concavity of the lattice is precisely what
allows the $L^q(S)$ norm of the lattice norms to pass inside the lattice.

\section*{Partial theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let $2\le q<\infty$, let $(S,\Sigma,\mu)$ be a sigma-finite measure space, and
let $X$ be a Banach lattice with square-function Rademacher equivalence
constant $K$ and $q$-concavity constant $M$.  Then there is a constant
$C\le K^2M$ such that for every finite family $(f_n)$ in $L^q(S)$ and every
finite family $(x_n)$ in $X$,
\[
\left\|\sum_n r_n f_n x_n\right\|_{L^q(S;L^2(\Omega;X))}
\leq
C\sup_n\|f_n\|_{L^q(S)}
\left\|\sum_n r_n x_n\right\|_{L^2(\Omega;X)}.
\]
The lattice hypotheses imply that $X$ has cotype $q$, so this is a genuine
subcase of the source question.  Thus the endpoint $\widetilde q=q$ in
Lemma 3.1(2) of \cite{HV2008} holds for this lattice class.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By homogeneity, assume $\sup_n\|f_n\|_{L^q(S)}\leq 1$.  For $s\in S$, the
square-function equivalence gives
\[
\left\|\sum_n r_n f_n(s)x_n\right\|_{L^2(\Omega;X)}
\leq
K\left\|\left(\sum_n |f_n(s)|^2|x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\|_X.
\]
Set
\[
h_s=\left(\sum_n |f_n(s)|^2|x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\in X_+.
\]
Using $q$-concavity, first for simple functions and then by monotone/simple
approximation, we obtain
\[
\left(\int_S \|h_s\|_X^q\,d\mu(s)\right)^{1/q}
\leq
M\left\|\left(\int_S h_s^q\,d\mu(s)\right)^{1/q}\right\|_X.
\]
Pointwise in the lattice variable, the scalar inequality
$L^q(S;\ell_2)\hookrightarrow \ell_2(L^q(S))$ for $q\geq2$ gives
\begin{multline*}
\left(\int_S \left(\sum_n |f_n(s)|^2 |a_n|^2\right)^{q/2}d\mu(s)\right)^{1/q}
\leq
\left(\sum_n |a_n|^2\|f_n\|_{L^q(S)}^2\right)^{1/2}\\
\leq
\left(\sum_n |a_n|^2\right)^{1/2}.
\end{multline*}
Applying this with $a_n=|x_n|$ in the lattice functional calculus yields
\[
\left(\int_S h_s^q\,d\mu(s)\right)^{1/q}
\leq
\left(\sum_n |x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}.
\]
Therefore
\begin{align*}
\left\|\sum_n r_n f_n x_n\right\|_{L^q(S;L^2(\Omega;X))}
&\leq
KM\left\|\left(\sum_n |x_n|^2\right)^{1/2}\right\|_X\\
&\leq
K^2M\left\|\sum_n r_nx_n\right\|_{L^2(\Omega;X)}.
\end{align*}
This is the desired endpoint estimate.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and limitations}

The theorem does not settle the full question for arbitrary Banach spaces of
cotype $q$.  Its hypotheses are lattice hypotheses, not consequences of cotype
alone in the formulation used here.  It does, however, push the endpoint
beyond the explicitly mentioned $L^q$ example in the source: the proof works
for any lattice class where the square-function Rademacher equivalence and
$q$-concavity constants are available.
