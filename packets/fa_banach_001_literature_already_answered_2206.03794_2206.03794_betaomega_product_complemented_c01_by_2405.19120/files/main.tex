\section*{Source Question}

At the end of the Introduction of arXiv:2206.03794, Kakol--Marciszewski--Sobota--Zdomskyy ask whether
\[
        C(\beta\mathbb N\times\beta\mathbb N)
\]
has a complemented infinite-dimensional subspace not isomorphic to any of the
following five spaces:
\[
C(\beta\mathbb N\times\beta\mathbb N),\quad
C(\beta\mathbb N),\quad c_0,\quad
c_0\oplus C(\beta\mathbb N),\quad c_0(C(\beta\mathbb N)).
\]

\section*{Answer}

Plebanek--Rondos--Sobota, arXiv:2405.19120, explicitly revisit this
Alspach--Galego/Candido problem. Their Corollary 1.4 states that
\[
        C(\beta\omega\times\beta\omega)
\]
contains a complemented subspace isomorphic to \(C([0,1]^\kappa)\) for every
cardinal \(1\le \kappa\le\mathfrak c\). Taking \(\kappa=1\), it contains a
complemented copy of \(C[0,1]\).

The space \(C[0,1]\) is not among the five listed isomorphism classes. Four of
the listed spaces are nonseparable because they contain a copy of
\(C(\beta\mathbb N)\cong\ell_\infty\), while \(C[0,1]\) is separable. The
remaining listed space is \(c_0\), and \(C[0,1]\not\cong c_0\) because
\(c_0^*=\ell_1\) is separable whereas \(C[0,1]^*\), the space of finite signed
regular Borel measures on \([0,1]\), is nonseparable.

\section*{Scope}

Thus arXiv:2405.19120 gives a positive answer to the existence question in
arXiv:2206.03794. This does not classify all complemented subspaces of
\(C(\beta\omega\times\beta\omega)\).
