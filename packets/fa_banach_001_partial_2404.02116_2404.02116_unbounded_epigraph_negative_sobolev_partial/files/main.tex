\section*{Status}

This packet gives a partial positive answer to the unbounded-domain question in
Arora--Glueck--Schwenninger \cite{AGS}.  The source proves its domain theorem
for bounded open sets with continuous boundary.  Immediately after the
bounded-domain construction, the source notes that boundedness was used through
compactness of $\overline\Omega$ and asks whether a similar result holds for
unbounded $\Omega$, perhaps under a uniformity assumption on the continuous
boundary.  The theorem below proves the same conclusion for a global epigraph
whose boundary function is uniformly continuous, retaining the source's
generating-cone hypothesis.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:epigraph}
Let $d\geq 2$, let $k\geq 1$, and let $p,q\in(1,\infty)$ be Holder conjugates.
Let $F:\R^{d-1}\to\R$ be uniformly continuous and set
\[
 \Omega=\{(x',t)\in\R^{d-1}\times\R: t>F(x')\}.
\]
Assume that the positive cone in $W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$ is generating.  Then
\[
 \Span\bigl(W^{-k,p}(\Omega)_+\bigr)
\]
is a KB-space, and in particular a Banach lattice, with respect to a norm
equivalent to the usual span norm.  Moreover, the canonical embedding
$L^p(\Omega)\hookrightarrow W^{-k,p}(\Omega)$ has range a lattice ideal in
$\Span(W^{-k,p}(\Omega)_+)$ and is a lattice homomorphism into this span.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
By applying a rigid motion, and by reflecting the last coordinate, the same
statement holds for rigid images of such epigraphs and for corresponding
subgraphs.  The theorem does not address the separate problem of when
$W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)_+$ is generating.
\end{remark}

\section*{The approximation operators}

We use the source's abstract Theorem 2.1 with
$X=W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$, $Z=L^q(\Omega)$, and $J:X\to Z$ the canonical positive
embedding.  Thus it is enough to construct positive operators
$R_n:Z\to X$ such that $R_nJ\to\id_X$ and $JR_n\to\id_Z$ strongly.

Let $E$ denote extension by zero from $\Omega$ to $\R^d$.  Choose a nonnegative
$\rho\in C_c^\infty(\R^d)$ supported in the unit ball, with
$\int_{\R^d}\rho=1$.  Choose $\varepsilon_n\downarrow 0$.  By uniform
continuity of $F$, choose $\eta_n>0$ such that
\[
 |x'-y'|<\eta_n\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
 |F(x')-F(y')|<\varepsilon_n/4.
\]
Set
\[
 r_n=\min(\eta_n,\varepsilon_n/4),
 \qquad
 \rho_n(x)=r_n^{-d}\rho(x/r_n).
\]
For $f\in L^q(\Omega)$ define, on $\R^d$,
\[
 T_n f=\rho_n * \bigl(Ef(\,\cdot-\varepsilon_n e_d)\bigr),
\]
and let $R_nf$ be the restriction of $T_nf$ to $\Omega$.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:inside}
For every $n$ and every $f\in L^q(\Omega)$, the function $T_nf$ vanishes
outside $\Omega$.  Moreover $R_nf\in W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$ and
$R_n:L^q(\Omega)\to W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$ is positive and bounded.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose that $T_nf(x)\ne0$.  Then for some $z\in\Omega$ one has
$|x-(z+\varepsilon_ne_d)|<r_n$.  Hence $|x'-z'|<r_n\leq\eta_n$ and
\[
 x_d>z_d+\varepsilon_n-r_n
    >F(z')+\varepsilon_n-r_n
    \geq F(x')+\varepsilon_n-r_n-\varepsilon_n/4
    \geq F(x')+\varepsilon_n/2.
\]
Thus $x\in\Omega$.  So the zero extension of $R_nf$ is exactly $T_nf$.

Convolution with $\rho_n$ maps $L^q(\R^d)$ boundedly into $W^{k,q}(\R^d)$, with
a constant depending on $n$.  Therefore $T_nf\in W^{k,q}(\R^d)$, and the
preceding paragraph shows that its restriction is supported inside $\Omega$.
For each compact ball cutoff $\chi_m\in C_c^\infty(\R^d)$ with
$\chi_m\to1$, the function $\chi_mT_nf$ is in $C_c^\infty(\Omega)$: its compact
support lies in the closed set $x_d\geq F(x')+\varepsilon_n/2$, hence in
$\Omega$.  The usual cutoff convergence in $W^{k,q}(\R^d)$ gives
$\chi_mT_nf\to T_nf$.  Hence $R_nf\in W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$.  Positivity is
immediate from $\rho_n\geq0$ and from positive zero extension and translation.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:convergence}
The operators above satisfy
\[
 JR_n\to \id_{L^q(\Omega)} \quad\text{strongly on } L^q(\Omega)
\]
and
\[
 R_nJ\to \id_{W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)}
 \quad\text{strongly on } W^{k,q}_0(\Omega).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $f\in L^q(\Omega)$, the zero extension $Ef$ belongs to $L^q(\R^d)$.  Since
$\varepsilon_n\to0$ and $r_n\to0$, translation followed by the standard
approximate identity $\rho_n$ converges to the identity in $L^q(\R^d)$.  The
restriction to $\Omega$ gives $JR_nf\to f$ in $L^q(\Omega)$.

If $u\in W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$, then its zero extension $Eu$ belongs to
$W^{k,q}(\R^d)$ by the definition of $W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$.  Translation and
mollification converge to the identity in $W^{k,q}(\R^d)$.  Since the zero
extension of $R_nJu$ is $T_nu$, Lemma~\ref{lem:inside} yields
$R_nJu\to u$ in $W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:epigraph}}

The space $X=W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)$ is reflexive, since $q\in(1,\infty)$ and it is
a closed subspace of the reflexive Sobolev space defined by zero extension.
By hypothesis, $X_+$ is generating.  The space $Z=L^q(\Omega)$ is a Banach
lattice, and $J:X\to Z$ is positive.  Lemmas~\ref{lem:inside} and
\ref{lem:convergence} provide positive operators $R_n:Z\to X$ for which both
$R_nJ\to\id_X$ and $JR_n\to\id_Z$ strongly, hence also weakly.

The source's Theorem 2.1 therefore applies.  It says that
$\Span(X'_+)$ is a monotonically complete Banach lattice under an equivalent
span norm, that it is a KB-space because $X$ is reflexive, and that
$J'Z'$ is a lattice ideal in $\Span(X'_+)$.  Identifying
$X'=W^{-k,p}(\Omega)$ and $Z'=L^p(\Omega)$ gives precisely the conclusion of
the theorem.

\section*{Scope and verification notes}

The proof only replaces the bounded-domain compactness step in the source.  It
does not prove that $W^{k,q}_0(\Omega)_+$ is generating, and so it leaves the
source's later generating-cone open problem untouched.  It also uses the
global uniformly continuous epigraph structure in an essential way: the single
upward translation is what provides a uniform boundary buffer for all spatial
locations.
