\section*{Source problem}

The source paper is T. Oikhberg, M. A. Taylor, P. Tradacete, and
V. G. Troitsky, \emph{Free Banach lattices}, arXiv:2210.00614.
Remark 9.48 states that for \(r\geq 2\) and \(1\leq p\leq 2\), the moduli
of the \(\ell_r\) basis in \(\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]\) are equivalent to the
\(\ell_s\) basis with \(1/s=1/r+1/2\), and then asks what happens for
\(r,p\in(2,\infty)\).

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(2<r,p<\infty\), and let \((e_k)\) be the canonical basis of
\(\ell_r\). Put
\[
  \frac1s=\frac1r+\frac1p.
\]
Then the sequence \((|\delta_{e_k}|)\) in \(\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]\) is
equivalent to the unit vector basis of \(\ell_s\). Equivalently, there are
constants \(c_{r,p},C_{r,p}>0\) such that for every finitely supported scalar
sequence \((a_k)\),
\[
  c_{r,p}\left(\sum_k |a_k|^s\right)^{1/s}
  \leq
  \left\|\sum_k a_k|\delta_{e_k}|\right\|_{\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]}
  \leq
  C_{r,p}\left(\sum_k |a_k|^s\right)^{1/s}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The free-lattice expression is nonlinear in the variables \(e_k\), but on
the dual side it is exactly the \(p\)-summing norm of a diagonal map.  For
\(a=(a_k)_{k=1}^n\), the function
\[
  f_a=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k|\delta_{e_k}|
\]
evaluated at \(x^*=(b_k)\in \ell_{r'}\) is
\[
  f_a(x^*)=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k |b_k|
  =\|\diag(a_k)b\|_{\ell_1^n}
\]
when \(a_k\geq0\).  Thus the defining norm of
\(\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]\) is the absolutely \(p\)-summing norm of
\(\diag(a_k):\ell_{r'}^n\to\ell_1^n\).  Garling's diagonal theorem then
computes this norm as \(\|a\|_{\ell_s}\), where
\(1/s=1/r+1/p\).

\section*{Proof}

We first record the norm reduction.  Let \(a_1,\dots,a_n\geq0\), and define
\[
  D_a:\ell_{r'}^n\longrightarrow \ell_1^n,\qquad
  D_a(b_1,\dots,b_n)=(a_1b_1,\dots,a_nb_n).
\]
For \(f_a=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k|\delta_{e_k}|\), the functional representation
of \(\FBL^{(p)}[E]\), as used in Proposition 6.1 of the source paper, gives
\[
 \|f_a\|_{\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]}
 =
 \sup
 \left\{
 \left(\sum_{i=1}^m
       \left(\sum_{k=1}^n a_k |x_i^*(e_k)|\right)^p
 \right)^{1/p}
 :
 \sup_{\|x\|_{\ell_r}\leq 1}
 \left(\sum_{i=1}^m |x_i^*(x)|^p\right)^{1/p}\leq1
 \right\}.
\]
Identifying \((\ell_r)^*\) with \(\ell_{r'}\), the last display is precisely
\[
  \pi_p(D_a:\ell_{r'}^n\to\ell_1^n),
\]
the absolutely \(p\)-summing norm of \(D_a\).

We now apply Garling's theorem on diagonal mappings between sequence spaces:
for \(2<r,p<\infty\), equivalently \(1<r'<2<p<\infty\),
\[
  \pi_p(D_a:\ell_{r'}^n\to\ell_1^n)
  \asymp_{r,p}
  \left(\sum_{k=1}^n a_k^s\right)^{1/s},
  \qquad
  \frac1s=\frac1r+\frac1p.
\]
Combining the two displays proves the asserted estimate for non-negative
coefficients.

Finally, the canonical basis of \(\ell_r\) is \(1\)-suppression
unconditional.  Proposition 5.2 of the source paper therefore implies that
\((|\delta_{e_k}|)\) is unconditional in \(\FBL^{(p)}[\ell_r]\), with a
constant depending only on the convention used there.  Hence replacing
\((a_k)\) by \((|a_k|)\) changes the norm by at most a constant independent
of \(n\) and of \(a\).  The same \(\ell_s\) estimate follows for arbitrary
scalar coefficients.

\section*{Verification and limitations}

This resolves the specific \(r,p\in(2,\infty)\) span question in Remark
9.48, assuming the quoted form of Garling's diagonal theorem.  It is still a
partial packet for the source paper as a whole: it does not address the
paper's other open questions on basic sequences, POE-\(p\), LWCG spaces, or
isomorphism of free Banach lattices.

The exponent matches the endpoints suggested by the source paper.  At
\(p=2\) the formula gives \(1/s=1/r+1/2\), the known case.  As
\(p\to\infty\), it tends formally to \(s=r\), matching the AM-space behavior
of \(\FBL^{(\infty)}[\ell_r]\), where unconditional bases have equivalent
moduli.
