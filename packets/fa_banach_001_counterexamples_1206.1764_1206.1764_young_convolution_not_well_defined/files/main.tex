\section{Source conjecture}
Gill, Pantsulaia, and Zachary \cite{GPZ} define a sigma-finite measure
$\lambda_\infty$ on the set $\R_I^\infty=\R^\infty$ and state Young's
inequality for
\[
 (f*g)(x)=\int_{\R_I^\infty} f(y)g(x-y)\,d\lambda_\infty(y).
\]
After the proof of Theorem 5.10, on page 55 of the 65-page arXiv PDF, they
write that the constant one is conjecturally sharp on $\R_I^\infty$.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The counterexample below shows a prior obstruction: the displayed convolution
does not descend to the source's $L^p$ spaces, because changing a function on
a $\lambda_\infty$-null set can change its convolution on a set of positive
measure.  Hence no sharp constant exists for the stated operation.

\section{Definitions from the source}
Set $I=[-1/2,1/2]$, $I_n=\prod_{k>n}I$, and
\[
 D=\R_I^{\prime\infty}
   =\bigcup_{n\geq1}(\R^n\times I_n),
 \qquad N=\R^\infty\setminus D.
\]
Thus $D$ consists of sequences whose coordinates lie in $I$ eventually, and
$N$ consists of sequences having infinitely many coordinates outside $I$.
The source equips $N$ with the discrete topology, includes $\mathcal P(N)$ in
its Borel sigma-algebra, and defines the measure of open sets by taking the
supremum of finite-dimensional compact pieces contained in them.  Consequently
\begin{equation}\label{eq:nullN}
 \lambda_\infty(N)=0.
\end{equation}
Indeed every positive compact piece used in the definition lies in $D$, so
none is contained in the disjoint open set $N$.

Let $I_0=I^\N$.  The normalization in the source is
$\lambda_\infty(I_0)=1$, and the coordinate marginals of
$\lambda_\infty|_{I_0}$ are normalized Lebesgue measure on $I$.
Write $\mu=\lambda_\infty|_{I_0}$.

\section{Proof intuition}
The measure is concentrated on sequences that eventually remain in the tail
box $I$.  Subtraction destroys that property: for two independent tail-box
coordinates, the difference exits $I$ with probability $1/4$.  Across
infinitely many independent coordinates, exits occur infinitely often almost
surely.  Therefore the difference of two typical points of $I_0$ lies in the
null complement $N$.

This turns the null-set indicator $\mathbf1_N$ into a decisive witness.  It is
the zero element of every $L^q$, but the pointwise convolution formula samples
it at typical differences and returns one on $I_0$.

\section{Formal counterexample}
\begin{lemma}\label{lem:difference}
For $\mu\otimes\mu$-almost every $(x,y)\in I_0\times I_0$, one has $x-y\in N$.
Equivalently,
\[
 (\mu\otimes\mu)\{(x,y):x-y\in D\}=0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $k\geq1$ let
\[
 B_k=\{(x,y)\in I_0^2:|x_k-y_k|>1/2\}.
\]
The sets $B_k$ depend on disjoint coordinate pairs and hence are independent.
The two triangles outside the diagonal strip of width $1/2$ in the unit square
$I\times I$ have total area $1/4$, so
$(\mu\otimes\mu)(B_k)=1/4$.

The condition $x-y\in D$ says that only finitely many $B_k$ occur.  For every
$m$,
\[
 (\mu\otimes\mu)\left(\bigcap_{k>m}B_k^c\right)
 =\lim_{M\to\infty}\left(\frac34\right)^{M-m}=0.
\]
Taking the countable union over $m$ proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:counterexample}
The convolution formula in Theorems 5.10 and 5.13 of \cite{GPZ} is not
well-defined on almost-everywhere equivalence classes.  More precisely, for
every admissible exponent triple $p,q,r\in[1,\infty]$, there are measurable
representatives $f$ and $g$ such that
\[
 \|f\|_p=1,\qquad \|g\|_q=0,\qquad \|f*g\|_r\geq1.
\]
Consequently the claimed Young inequality, and hence the conjecture that its
sharp constant is one, is false or ill-posed as stated.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Take
\[
 f=\mathbf1_{I_0},\qquad g=\mathbf1_N.
\]
Both functions are measurable under the source's sigma-algebra.  The
normalization $\lambda_\infty(I_0)=1$ gives $\|f\|_p=1$ for every $p$, while
\eqref{eq:nullN} gives $\|g\|_q=0$ for every $q$, including $q=\infty$ in the
essential-supremum sense.

Lemma \ref{lem:difference} and Fubini give, for $\mu$-almost every $x\in I_0$,
\[
 \mu\{y\in I_0:x-y\in N\}=1.
\]
For those $x$ the source's pointwise formula yields
\[
 (f*g)(x)
 =\int_{\R_I^\infty}\mathbf1_{I_0}(y)\mathbf1_N(x-y)
   \,d\lambda_\infty(y)
 =1.
\]
Thus $\|f*g\|_r\geq1$ for every $r$.  But $g$ and the identically zero
function are equal $\lambda_\infty$-almost everywhere, whereas convolution
with the zero representative is identically zero.  The formula therefore
does not define an operation on $L^p$ equivalence classes, and its asserted
norm bound has right-hand side zero and left-hand side at least one.
\end{proof}

\section{Where the source proof fails}
The proof of Theorem 5.13 changes variables as though
\[
 \int g(x-y)\,d\lambda_\infty(x)=\int g(x)\,d\lambda_\infty(x)
\]
for the relevant $y$.  However, the source itself identifies $\ell_1$ as the
maximal group of admissible translations for $\lambda_\infty$.  A typical
tail-box point is not in $\ell_1$, and Lemma \ref{lem:difference} quantifies
the resulting singular behavior.  Fubini alone cannot supply the missing
translation invariance.

\section{Verification, limitations, and novelty}
The argument is exact and contains no numerical or unproved dependency.  The
finite-coordinate check is the elementary area computation
$\Pr(|X-Y|>1/2)=1/4$ for independent uniform $X,Y\in I$; countable additivity
then gives Lemma \ref{lem:difference}.

The result attacks the convolution and Young inequality exactly as written.
It does not preclude a different construction using a restricted translation
group, a different measure, or an explicit representative convention.  Such
a change would be a reformulation, not a repair of the stated $L^p$ operation.

For novelty, on 19 July 2026 we searched the run's cheap indexes for the arXiv
id and title, then searched the web for the exact title with the phrases
\texttt{convolution}, \texttt{Young}, \texttt{sharp constant},
\texttt{critique}, and \texttt{R\_I\^infty}.  The search found the source and
citing works but no correction or prior counterexample.  This was a bounded,
not exhaustive, search.

\paragraph{Human-review recommendation.}
Review the source's null-complement measure assignment and whether any
unwritten canonical-representative convention was intended.  Under the
published definitions the counterexample is complete; an added convention
would instead expose that the source is defining a different, highly
degenerate operation.
