\begin{abstract}
Problem 7.3 of De Bernardi--Russo--Sezgek--Somaglia asks whether
$\gamma^*(\C(\K))=1$ for all (metrizable) compact spaces $\K$.  We prove
the metrizable case.  The argument combines the paper's zero-dimensional
and perfect-metrizable cases with the Cantor--Bendixson decomposition and
a Dugundji extension operator.
\end{abstract}

\section{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let $\K$ be a compact metrizable space. Then
\[
  \gamma^*(\C(\K))=1 .
\]
Consequently, the parenthesized metrizable version of Problem 7.3 has an
affirmative answer.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We use the following two facts from the source paper. First, if $L$ is a
zero-dimensional compact space, then $\gamma^*(\C(L))=1$. Second, if $L$ is a
perfect compact metric space, then $\C(L)$ is separable and octahedral, hence
the source paper's octahedral theorem gives $\gamma^*(\C(L))=1$.

Let $P$ be the Cantor--Bendixson perfect kernel of $\K$. If $P=\varnothing$,
then $\K$ is compact scattered, hence zero-dimensional, and the first source
fact gives the conclusion.

Assume now that $P\neq\varnothing$. Then $P$ is a perfect compact metric
space, so $\gamma^*(\C(P))=1$. Fix $\varepsilon>0$. Choose numbers
$0<\eta<\alpha<\varepsilon$. Since $\gamma^*(\C(P))=1$, there is a subgroup
$D_P\subseteq \C(P)$ which is $2$-separated and $(1+\eta)$-dense. By the
Dugundji extension theorem, there is a norm-one linear extension operator
\[
  E:\C(P)\longrightarrow \C(\K),\qquad (Eg)|_P=g .
\]
Let
\[
  H=\{h\in \C(\K): h|_P=0\text{ and }h(\K)\subseteq 2\Z\}.
\]
This is a subgroup of $\C(\K)$. Define
\[
  D=E[D_P]+H .
\]
It is also a subgroup. We first check separation. If $Ed+h\in D$ is nonzero
and $d\neq0$, then
\[
  \|Ed+h\|_\infty\geq \|(Ed+h)|_P\|_\infty=\|d\|_\infty\geq 2,
\]
because $h|_P=0$ and $D_P$ is $2$-separated. If $d=0$ and $h\neq0$, then
$h$ takes a nonzero value in $2\Z$, so $\|h\|_\infty\geq2$. Thus $D$ is
$2$-separated.

It remains to show that $D$ is $(1+\varepsilon)$-dense. Let $f\in \C(\K)$.
Choose $d\in D_P$ such that
\[
  \|f|_P-d\|_\infty\leq 1+\eta,
\]
and set $g=f-Ed$. Then $\|g|_P\|_\infty\leq 1+\eta$. Hence the compact set
\[
  A=\{x\in\K: |g(x)|\geq 1+\alpha\}
\]
is disjoint from $P$.

The open subspace $\K\setminus P$ is locally compact, metrizable, and
scattered; in particular it is zero-dimensional. Therefore the compact set
$A\subseteq \K\setminus P$ has a compact clopen neighbourhood $U$ with
$A\subseteq U\subseteq \K\setminus P$. Since $U$ is compact and
zero-dimensional, the even-integer approximation used in the source paper
applies on $U$: take a finite clopen partition of $U$ on which $g$ has
oscillation $<\eta$, and on each piece choose a nearest even integer to a
representative value. This gives $h_U\in \C(U;2\Z)$ with
\[
  \|g|_U-h_U\|_\infty\leq 1+\eta .
\]
Extend $h_U$ by zero outside $U$. Since $U$ is clopen in $\K$ and disjoint
from $P$, the extension $h$ belongs to $H$.

For $x\in U$ we have $|g(x)-h(x)|\leq1+\eta$. For $x\notin U$, the definition
of $A$ gives $|g(x)-h(x)|=|g(x)|<1+\alpha$. Hence
\[
  \|f-(Ed+h)\|_\infty=\|g-h\|_\infty<1+\varepsilon.
\]
Thus $D$ is $(1+\varepsilon)$-dense and $2$-separated. Since
$\varepsilon>0$ was arbitrary, $\gamma^*(\C(\K))\leq1$. The reverse
inequality $\gamma^*(X)\geq1$ holds for every normed space, so
$\gamma^*(\C(\K))=1$.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification notes}

No numerical verification is needed.  The proof reduces the problem to three
standard and cited ingredients:
\begin{itemize}
\item the source paper's result for zero-dimensional compact spaces;
\item the source paper's separable octahedral result, applied to perfect
compact metric spaces;
\item the Dugundji extension theorem for closed subsets of compact metric
spaces.
\end{itemize}
The construction of the subgroup $D=E[D_P]+H$ is explicit enough to verify
the two required inequalities directly: restriction to $P$ gives separation
when the $C(P)$ component is nonzero, and the even-integer subgroup $H$ gives
separation on the scattered remainder.

\begin{remark}
This packet does not claim to settle the nonmetrizable compact case in
Problem 7.3.
\end{remark}
