\section{Source Problem}

The source paper is J. Qi, Q. Li, Z. Yin, Q. Liu, J. Bi, Y. Fu, and Y. Li,
\emph{Generalized geometric constants related to Birkhoff orthogonality in
Banach spaces}, arXiv:2602.13974.
In its final open questions, Problem 2 asks whether one can improve the proof
of the Ficken inner-product criterion by introducing the constant
\[
F(X)=\sup_{t>0}\left\{
\frac{\|x+t y\|-\|t x+y\|}{2}: x,y\in \SX
\right\}.
\]

\section{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be a real normed space with \(\dim X\ge 2\).  Then
\[
0\le F(X)\le 1.
\]
Moreover,
\[
F(X)=0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
X \text{ is an inner product space.}
\]
\end{theorem}

\section{Idea of the Proof}

The expression defining \(F(X)\) is skew in the ordered pair \((x,y)\):
interchanging \(x\) and \(y\) changes its sign.  Hence vanishing of the
supremum is much stronger than it may first look.  It forces equality
\(\|x+t y\|=\|t x+y\|\) for every unit pair and every \(t>0\).  Replacing
\(y\) by \(-y\) supplies the negative values of \(t\), so the usual Ficken
criterion applies.

\section{Proof}

First, \(F(X)\ge 0\), since taking \(x=y\in \SX\) gives value \(0\).  For the
upper bound, for \(x,y\in \SX\) and \(t>0\),
\[
\|x+t y\|\le 1+t,
\qquad
\|t x+y\|\ge |t-1|.
\]
Therefore
\[
\frac{\|x+t y\|-\|t x+y\|}{2}
\le
\frac{1+t-|t-1|}{2}
=\min\{1,t\}\le 1.
\]
Taking the supremum gives \(F(X)\le 1\).

If \(X\) is an inner product space and \(x,y\in \SX\), then
\[
\|x+t y\|^2 = 1+t^2+2t\langle x,y\rangle
= \|t x+y\|^2
\]
for all real \(t\).  Hence \(F(X)=0\).

Conversely, assume \(F(X)=0\).  Fix \(x,y\in \SX\) and \(t>0\).  The definition
of \(F\) gives
\[
\|x+t y\|-\|t x+y\|\le 0.
\]
Applying the same inequality to the pair \((y,x)\) gives
\[
\|y+t x\|-\|t y+x\|\le 0,
\]
or equivalently \(\|t x+y\|\le \|x+t y\|\).  Thus
\[
\|x+t y\|=\|t x+y\|
\qquad (x,y\in \SX,\ t>0).
\]
For \(t<0\), write \(t=-s\) with \(s>0\) and apply the positive-\(s\) equality
to the pair \((x,-y)\):
\[
\|x-sy\|=\|s x-y\|=\|-s x+y\|.
\]
The case \(t=0\) is trivial.  By homogeneity, for every pair of nonzero vectors
\(u,v\) with \(\|u\|=\|v\|\), one has
\[
\|t u+v\|=\|u+t v\|
\qquad\text{for every }t\in\mathbb{R}.
\]
This is exactly Ficken's characterization of inner product spaces, quoted as
Lemma 6 in the source paper.  Hence \(X\) is an inner product space.

\section{Answer to Problem 2}

Problem 2 has an affirmative answer in the following precise sense: the
proposed constant \(F(X)\) improves the skew-equality criterion to a scalar
geometric invariant, and its zero set is exactly the class of inner product
spaces.
