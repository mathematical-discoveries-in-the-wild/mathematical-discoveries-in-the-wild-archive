\section*{Status}

This packet records that published Problem 5 in Motakis--Puglisi--Tolias,
\emph{Algebras of diagonal operators of the form scalar-plus-compact are
Calkin algebras}, arXiv:1711.01340, is already answered affirmatively by
Motakis--Pelczar-Barwacz, \emph{Reflexive Calkin algebras}, arXiv:2401.18037.

\section*{Original Problem}

Problem 5 asks for a Banach space \(X\) with an unconditional basis not
equivalent to the unit vector basis of \(c_0\) such that the unitization of
\(X\) is isomorphic, as a Banach algebra, to a Calkin algebra.

\section*{Supporting Later Result}

The later paper proves the following theorem. If \(U\) has a normalized
\(1\)-unconditional basis and no \(c_0\) asymptotic version, then there is a
Banach space \(\mathfrak X_U\) such that
\[
  \Cal(\mathfrak X_U)
  \cong
  \mathbb C e \oplus U
\]
as Banach algebras, where \(U\) is endowed with coordinate-wise multiplication.
The paper also states explicitly that the earlier Problem 5 is answered by this
theorem.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Problem 5 only asks for one non-\(c_0\) unconditional-basis example. The 2024
theorem gives a large class of such examples: every eligible \(U\) contributes
its coordinate-wise unitization as a Calkin algebra. The easiest witness is
\(U=\ell_2\), whose standard basis is unconditional and whose Banach-space
geometry rules out equivalence to the \(c_0\) basis.

\section*{Verification}

Take \(X=\ell_2\) with its standard orthonormal basis. This basis is normalized
and \(1\)-unconditional. The space \(\ell_2\) is reflexive, whereas \(c_0\) is
not, so the standard basis of \(\ell_2\) is not equivalent to the unit vector
basis of \(c_0\).

Theorem 1.2 of arXiv:2401.18037 applies to \(U=\ell_2\): the paper lists
\(\ell_p\), \(1\leq p<\infty\), among the simplest examples satisfying its
hypotheses and specifically mentions the infinite-dimensional separable Hilbert
space case. Therefore there exists a Banach space \(\mathfrak X_{\ell_2}\)
whose Calkin algebra is isomorphic, as a Banach algebra, to the unitization of
\(\ell_2\) with coordinate-wise multiplication. Thus \(X=\ell_2\) satisfies
Problem 5 of arXiv:1711.01340.

\section*{Limitations}

This is not an original solution produced by the run. It is a
literature-already-answered status packet. It also does not solve the broader
problem posed in arXiv:2401.18037 asking whether every Banach space with a
normalized unconditional basis has its coordinate-wise unitization realized as
a Calkin algebra.
