\section*{Source and Status}

This packet concerns the final open question in Geivison Ribeiro's
\emph{Isometric embeddings of separable Banach spaces into
$(\ell^\infty\setminus c)\cup\{0\}$}, arXiv:2508.18656 \cite{ribeiro2025}.
The question appears on page 9 of the arXiv PDF.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

\section*{Transcription of the Question}

Let \(D\subset \ell^\infty\) be an arbitrary, possibly nonseparable, subspace
with \(D\cap c=\{0\}\), and let \(E\) be a separable Banach space.  Does there
always exist an isometry \(T:E\to\ell^\infty\) such that
\[
        T(E)\cap(D+c)=\{0\}?
\]

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The statement allows \(D\) to be an arbitrary linear subspace, with no
closedness, separability, or quotient-size restriction.  A Hamel-basis
complement to \(c\) is therefore admissible.  For such a complement \(D\), one
has \(D\cap c=\{0\}\), but also \(D+c=\ell^\infty\).  Thus every possible image
of an isometry into \(\ell^\infty\) is already contained in \(D+c\), and a
nonzero image cannot have trivial intersection with \(D+c\).

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
The final open question in \cite{ribeiro2025}, as stated, has a negative
answer.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(c\) denote the subspace of all convergent scalar sequences in
\(\ell^\infty\).  Since \(c\) is a proper linear subspace of \(\ell^\infty\),
the standard Hamel-basis extension argument gives an algebraic complement
\(D\) of \(c\) in \(\ell^\infty\).  Equivalently,
\[
        \ell^\infty = D \oplus c.
\]
In particular \(D\cap c=\{0\}\), so \(D\) satisfies the hypothesis in the
question.  But \(D+c=\ell^\infty\).

Now take the one-dimensional separable Banach space \(E=\K\).  If
\(T:E\to\ell^\infty\) is any isometry, then \(T(E)\) is a nonzero
one-dimensional subspace of \(\ell^\infty\).  Since \(D+c=\ell^\infty\), we
have
\[
        T(E)\cap(D+c) = T(E) \neq \{0\}.
\]
Thus no isometry \(T:E\to\ell^\infty\) can satisfy the desired conclusion for
this \(D\) and this separable \(E\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The same \(D\) blocks every nonzero separable \(E\), not only \(E=\K\).  The
one-dimensional choice is enough to refute the universal quantifier in the
source question.
\end{remark}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet addresses the final arbitrary-\(D\) question only.  It does not
settle the earlier question in the source asking whether, for
finite-dimensional \(D\), one can always choose a closed nonseparable subspace
\(Z\subset \ell^\infty\) containing \(D\) and an isometric copy of a prescribed
separable Banach space while still satisfying \(Z\cap c=\{0\}\).

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof is purely algebraic and uses only the existence of a Hamel complement
to a proper subspace.  No computational verification is needed.

Before promotion, the run indexes were searched for arXiv:2508.18656, the
source title, \(D+c\), and related isometric-embedding phrases.  Bounded web
searches on 2026-06-23 used exact phrases from the open question and the
algebraic-complement obstruction.  No prior run packet or obvious published
answer to this exact question was found.

Human review should mainly check that the intended reading of ``subspace'' in
the final question did not silently require closedness or a proper quotient
image.  Under the literal wording, the algebraic complement is admissible.
