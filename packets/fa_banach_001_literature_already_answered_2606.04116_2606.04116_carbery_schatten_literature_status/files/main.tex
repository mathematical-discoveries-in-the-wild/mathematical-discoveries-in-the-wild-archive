\section*{Question}

In Carbery's notation, under the hypotheses of Theorem 1.3, let \(A_p\) be the
\(\ell_2\)-operator norm of the matrix \((\alpha_{jk}^{p'})\), where
\(p'=p/(p-1)\). He asks whether
\[
\left\|\sum_j T_j\right\|_p
\le
A_p^{1/p'}
\left(\sum_j \|T_j\|_p^p\right)^{1/p}.
\tag{C}
\]
The surrounding text says that this was unknown even in the commutative case
and even when \(p\) is an integer.

\section*{Supporting answer}

The supporting paper explicitly identifies this same question and states that
it answers it affirmatively. The following crop is from page 3 of
arXiv:2606.04116.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/supporting_answer_crop.png}
\end{center}

\begin{theorem}[Chen--Ivanisvili--Madrid--Wang, Theorem 1.1]
Let \(N\ge 2\), \(p\ge 2\), and let \(T_1,\ldots,T_N\in S_p(H)\). Let
\((\alpha_{ij})\) be a symmetric matrix with entries in \([0,1]\) satisfying
the same almost-orthogonality hypotheses as in Carbery's question. Then
\[
\left\|\sum_{j=1}^N T_j\right\|_{S_p}
\le
\|(\alpha_{ij}^{p'})\|_2^{1/p'}
\left(\sum_{j=1}^N \|T_j\|_{S_p}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{claim}
Carbery's Section 3.3 question (3.1) is already answered affirmatively by
arXiv:2606.04116.
\end{claim}

\begin{proof}
The match is direct. Carbery's \(C_p\) and the supporting paper's \(S_p\) are
the Schatten--von Neumann classes with the Schatten \(p\)-norm. Carbery asks
whether estimate (C) holds with \(A_p\) equal to the \(\ell_2\)-operator norm
of \((\alpha_{jk}^{p'})\). The supporting paper says it answers the question
posed by Carbery in ``[2, Section 3.3, (3.1)]'' and proves exactly this
operator-norm estimate for all real \(p\ge 2\), for arbitrary operators in
\(S_p(H)\). The finite-family theorem is the finite form of the question, and
the same paper records the countable-family passage in its concluding
remarks.

Therefore the exact open question shown in the source crop has an explicit
later affirmative answer.
\end{proof}
