\section*{Source and Open Problem}

This packet concerns the space constructed in:

\begin{quote}
Miguel Martin, \emph{A Banach space whose set of norm-attaining
functionals is algebraically trivial}, arXiv:2406.07273.
\end{quote}

The source paper constructs a smooth renorming \(\X\) of \(c_0\) for which
\(\NA(\X,\R)\) contains no non-trivial cone. The paper then asks the following
question on page 11.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

\noindent In words, Open Problem 7 asks whether
\[
  \NA(\X,\ell_2^2)
\]
contains a rank-two operator.

\section*{Candidate Partial Result}

The result below does not solve Open Problem 7. It proves that, for each fixed
two-codimensional kernel, norm-attainment is a nowhere dense condition in the
corresponding finite-dimensional operator fibre.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:fixed-kernel}
Let \(\X\) be Martin's space from arXiv:2406.07273, and let
\(H=\ell_2^2\) be the real two-dimensional Hilbert space. Let \(M\) be a
closed subspace of \(\X\) with \(\codim M=2\), and set
\[
  \calL_M=\{T\in \calL(\X,H): M\subseteq \ker T\}.
\]
Then
\[
  \NA(\X,H)\cap \calL_M
\]
is nowhere dense in \(\calL_M\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
If \(T\in\NA(\X,\ell_2^2)\) has rank two and kernel \(M\), then for every
\(\varepsilon>0\) there is a rank-two operator \(S:\X\to\ell_2^2\) such that
\[
  \ker S=M,\qquad \norm{S-T}<\varepsilon,\qquad
  S\notin\NA(\X,\ell_2^2).
\]
\end{corollary}

\section*{Definitions and Source Facts}

Let \(q:\X\to E:=\X/M\) be the quotient map. Since \(\codim M=2\), the space
\(E\) is two-dimensional. The fibre \(\calL_M\) is naturally isometric to
\(\calL(E,H)\): every \(T\in\calL_M\) has the form \(T=Aq\) for a unique
\(A\in\calL(E,H)\).

We use two facts from Martin's paper.

First, Proposition 2.4 in the source paper implies that
\[
  C_M:=q(B_\X)\cap S_E
\]
contains at most two antipodal pairs. In particular, \(C_M\) is finite.

Second, \(\X\) is smooth. Equivalently, \(\X^*\) is strictly convex. Since
\[
  E^*=(\X/M)^* \cong M^\perp
\]
is a subspace of the strictly convex space \(\X^*\), the dual \(E^*\) is
strictly convex. Therefore the quotient \(E\) is smooth.

\section*{The Finite-Dimensional Lemma}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:fixed-point-nowhere-dense}
Let \(E\) be a smooth real two-dimensional Banach space, let
\(H=\ell_2^2\), and fix \(e\in S_E\). Define
\[
  F_e=\{A\in\calL(E,H): \norm{Ae}=\norm{A}\}.
\]
Then \(F_e\) is closed and nowhere dense in \(\calL(E,H)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Closedness is immediate from continuity of \(A\mapsto \norm{A}\) and
\(A\mapsto \norm{Ae}\). We prove that \(F_e\) has empty interior.

Let \(A\in F_e\) and let \(\delta>0\). If \(A=0\), choose any non-zero
operator \(B:E\to H\) with \(Be=0\) and \(\norm{B}<\delta\). Then
\(B\notin F_e\), so we are done.

Assume now that \(A\neq0\). Put \(y=Ae\), so \(\norm{y}=\norm{A}>0\). Since
\(E\) is smooth, there is a unique \(j\in S_{E^*}\) such that \(j(e)=1\).
Choose \(d\in\ker j\setminus\{0\}\). Then
\[
  \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0}\norm{e+td}=j(d)=0.
\]
Let \(u=y/\norm{y}\in S_H\). Since \(A\) attains its norm at \(e\), we have
\[
  \norm{A(e+td)}\leq \norm{y}\,\norm{e+td}
  \qquad (t\in\R).
\]
Differentiating this inequality at \(t=0\), from the right and from the
left, gives
\[
  \langle u,Ad\rangle=0.
\]

Choose \(B\in\calL(E,H)\) with \(Be=0\) and \(Bd=y\). For \(\alpha>0\), set
\[
  A_\alpha=A+\alpha B.
\]
Then \(A_\alpha e=y\), while
\[
  A_\alpha(e+td)=y+tAd+\alpha t y.
\]
As \(H\) is Hilbert, the derivative at \(t=0\) of
\(\norm{A_\alpha(e+td)}\) is
\[
  \langle u,Ad+\alpha y\rangle
  =
  \alpha\norm{y}>0.
\]
The derivative at \(t=0\) of \(\norm{e+td}\) is \(0\), so for all sufficiently
small positive \(t\),
\[
  \frac{\norm{A_\alpha(e+td)}}{\norm{e+td}}
  >
  \norm{y}
  =
  \norm{A_\alpha e}.
\]
Hence \(e\) is not a norming point for \(A_\alpha\), and so
\(A_\alpha\notin F_e\). Taking \(\alpha>0\) small enough that
\(\norm{\alpha B}<\delta\), we have found an operator outside \(F_e\)
arbitrarily close to \(A\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:fixed-kernel}}

Identify \(\calL_M\) with \(\calL(E,H)\) by \(T=Aq\). We claim that
\[
  \NA(\X,H)\cap\calL_M
  \subseteq
  \{0\}\cup \bigcup_{e\in C_M} F_e.
\]
Indeed, let \(T=Aq\in\NA(\X,H)\cap\calL_M\) be non-zero. Choose
\(x\in S_\X\) with
\[
  \norm{Aq x}=\norm{T}=\norm{A}.
\]
Put \(e=qx\). If \(\norm{e}<1\), then
\[
  \left\|A\frac{e}{\norm{e}}\right\|
  =
  \frac{\norm{A e}}{\norm{e}}
  >
  \norm{A},
\]
which is impossible. Therefore \(\norm{e}=1\), and since \(x\in B_\X\), we
have \(e\in q(B_\X)\cap S_E=C_M\). Also
\[
  \norm{Ae}=\norm{A},
\]
so \(A\in F_e\).

By Martin's Proposition 2.4, the set \(C_M\) is finite. By
Lemma~\ref{lem:fixed-point-nowhere-dense}, each \(F_e\) is closed and nowhere
dense in \(\calL(E,H)\). The singleton \(\{0\}\) is also nowhere dense.
Hence \(\NA(\X,H)\cap \calL_M\) is contained in a finite union of closed
nowhere dense subsets of \(\calL_M\), and is itself nowhere dense.

\section*{Verification Notes}

The key perturbation lemma uses smoothness of \(E\). This is why the special
geometry of Martin's space is relevant: the constructed \(\X\) is smooth, so
\(\X^*\) is strictly convex; strict convexity passes to the subspace
\(M^\perp\cong E^*\), and therefore \(E\) is smooth.

The conclusion is intentionally fixed-kernel. It does not prove that
\(\NA(\X,\ell_2^2)\) has no rank-two operators, nor that such operators are
not dense when kernels are allowed to vary. It only proves that every fixed
two-dimensional quotient has a meagre set of norm-attaining Hilbert-plane
operators.

\section*{Literature and Novelty Check}

The proof uses the source paper's Proposition 2.4 and smoothness properties
of \(\X\). A targeted search on June 13, 2026 for phrases around
\(\NA(X)\) containing non-trivial cones, Open Problem 7, BPBp/RSE tools, and
rank-two operators into \(\ell_2^2\) found Martin's arXiv:2406.07273, KLMW's
arXiv:1905.08272, and later BPBp/RSE papers, but no statement of the
fixed-kernel nowhere-dense consequence above.

Human review should focus on Lemma~\ref{lem:fixed-point-nowhere-dense} and
on the passage from Martin's finite quotient-sphere set \(C_M\) to the
operator fibre \(\calL_M\).
