\section*{Source Question}

Bhattacharyya--Bhowmik--Kumar ask whether Herglotz's representation and
Caratheodory's approximation are equivalent on the polydisc or on the
symmetrized polydisc \cite[p. 19]{BBK}.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

This packet does not solve the full two-domain question.  It proves a
sharp obstruction to the most direct disc-style route from the
Koranyi--Pukanszky Herglotz representation on the polydisc to
Caratheodory approximation.  In fact, the obstruction is stronger than
finite atomicity: every positive Koranyi--Pukanszky determining measure
has Haar marginals along all mixed-sign characters, and hence is
nonatomic.

A further push identifies the exact non-atomic replacement for this failed
atomic mechanism.  On the polydisc, locally uniform approximation by
Cayley rational inner Herglotz functions is equivalent to weak-star density
of the corresponding rational-inner Clark measures in the
Koranyi--Pukanszky determining-measure simplex.

The final section gives a two-way polydisc route: Caratheodory approximation
implies the normalized Koranyi--Pukanszky representation by the same
scaling/Helly mechanism used in the source paper, while the
Koranyi--Pukanszky representation plus the standard Pfister--Rudin--Stout
finite rationalization step yields Caratheodory approximation without using
atomic determining measures.  A separate stress test explains why the same
argument does not yet descend to the symmetrized polydisc.

\section*{Definitions}

For \(d\geq 2\), let \(m_d\) denote Haar probability measure on \(\T^d\).
In the Koranyi--Pukanszky representation for the polydisc, the representing
positive measure \(\mu\) on \(\T^d\) must satisfy the determining moment
condition
\[
  \int_{\T^d} u_1^{n_1}\cdots u_d^{n_d}\,d\mu(u)=0
\]
whenever the multi-index \(n=(n_1,\ldots,n_d)\in\Z^d\) has mixed signs,
that is, whenever \(n\) is neither coordinatewise nonnegative nor
coordinatewise nonpositive.  Write \(\RP(\T^d)\) for the positive finite
measures satisfying this condition.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The one-variable proof has point masses available: finite Blaschke
products correspond, after the inverse Cayley transform, to finite atomic
Herglotz measures.  The polydisc moment condition destroys that mechanism.
If \(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\), then every mixed-sign character
\(u\mapsto u^n\) has all nonzero moments equal to zero under \(\mu\).
Thus its pushforward is Haar measure on \(\T\).  This forces \(\mu\) to
give zero mass to every level set of every mixed-sign character, so in
particular \(\mu\) has no atoms.

\begin{theorem}[Haar marginals]
Let \(d\geq 2\), and let \(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\).  If
\(n=(n_1,\ldots,n_d)\in\Z^d\) has at least one positive coordinate and
at least one negative coordinate, define
\[
  \chi_n:\T^d\to\T,\qquad \chi_n(u)=u_1^{n_1}\cdots u_d^{n_d}.
\]
Then
\[
  (\chi_n)_\#\mu=\mu(\T^d)m_1,
\]
where \(m_1\) is Haar probability measure on \(\T\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\nu=(\chi_n)_\#\mu\).  For every \(k\in\Z\setminus\{0\}\),
the multi-index \(kn\) also has at least one positive and one negative
coordinate.  Therefore the Koranyi--Pukanszky determining condition gives
\[
  \int_\T \zeta^k\,d\nu(\zeta)
  =\int_{\T^d} \chi_n(u)^k\,d\mu(u)
  =\int_{\T^d} u^{kn}\,d\mu(u)=0 .
\]
Hence all nonzero Fourier coefficients of \(\nu\) vanish.  Its zeroth
Fourier coefficient is \(\nu(\T)=\mu(\T^d)\).  By uniqueness of Fourier
coefficients for finite Borel measures on \(\T\),
\[
  \nu=\mu(\T^d)m_1 .
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[No mass on mixed-character level sets]
Let \(d\geq2\), let \(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\), and let \(n\in\Z^d\) have mixed
signs.  Then
\[
  \mu\{u\in\T^d:\chi_n(u)=\lambda\}=0
\]
for every \(\lambda\in\T\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By the theorem, the pushforward \((\chi_n)_\#\mu\) is a scalar multiple of
nonatomic Haar measure.  Therefore each singleton \(\{\lambda\}\subset\T\)
has pushforward mass \(0\), which is exactly the displayed measure.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Diffuse determining measures]
For \(d\geq2\), every positive finite measure in \(\RP(\T^d)\) is
nonatomic.  In particular, \(\RP(\T^d)\) contains no nonzero positive
measure with countable support, and no finite atomic probability measures.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Fix distinct indices \(i\neq j\).  The character
\(\chi(u)=u_i\overline{u_j}\) is mixed-sign.  If \(a\in\T^d\), then
\(\{a\}\subseteq \chi^{-1}\{\chi(a)\}\), and the previous corollary gives
\(\mu(\{a\})=0\).  Thus \(\mu\) has no atoms.  The countable-support and
finite-atomic conclusions follow immediately.
\end{proof}

\section*{The Clark-Measure Replacement}

Let
\[
  \mathfrak H_0(\D^d)=
  \{F\in\Hol(\D^d): \operatorname{Re}F\geq0,\ F(0)=1\}.
\]
Let \(\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) be the set of normalized Cayley rational inner
Herglotz functions
\[
  F=\frac{1+\phi}{1-\phi},
\]
where \(\phi\) is rational inner on \(\D^d\) and \(\phi(0)=0\).  For
\(F\in\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\), let \(\mu_F\in\RP(\T^d)\) denote its unique
Koranyi--Pukanszky representing probability measure.  If
\(F\in\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\), this measure is the Aleksandrov--Clark measure
of the rational inner function \(\phi\) at the parameter \(1\).

\begin{theorem}[Clark density reformulation]
For \(d\geq2\), the following are equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The set \(\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) is dense in
\(\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\) for locally uniform convergence.
\item The set
\[
  \{\mu_F:F\in\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\}
\]
is weak-star dense in the probability measures in \(\RP(\T^d)\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume first that \(\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) is locally uniformly dense in
\(\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\).  Let \(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\) be a probability
measure, and let
\[
  F(z)=\int_{\T^d}\left(2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\right)\,d\mu(u).
\]
Choose \(F_n\in\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) with \(F_n\to F\) locally uniformly,
and write \(\mu_n=\mu_{F_n}\).  The probability measures on the compact
metric space \(\T^d\) are weak-star compact.  If \(\mu_{n_j}\to\nu\)
weak-star along a subsequence, then for each fixed \(z\in\D^d\),
continuity of \(u\mapsto 2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\) gives
\[
  F(z)=\lim_j F_{n_j}(z)
  =\int_{\T^d}\left(2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\right)\,d\nu(u).
\]
The class \(\RP(\T^d)\) is weak-star closed, so \(\nu\in\RP(\T^d)\).
Uniqueness in the Koranyi--Pukanszky representation gives \(\nu=\mu\).
Thus every weak-star cluster point of \((\mu_n)\) is \(\mu\), and hence
\(\mu_n\to\mu\).

Conversely, assume weak-star density of the measures \(\mu_F\).  For
\(F\in\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\), choose \(F_n\in\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) with
\(\mu_{F_n}\to\mu_F\) weak-star.  Since the kernel
\((z,u)\mapsto2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\) is continuous in \(u\) and uniformly
bounded for \(z\) in a fixed compact subset of \(\D^d\), the integral
representations give \(F_n\to F\) uniformly on compact subsets of \(\D^d\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Known positive zones]
The Clark-density condition above holds in every setting where
Caratheodory approximation by rational inner functions is already known.
In particular it holds for the Schur class on the polydisc by the
Pfister--Rudin--Stout approximation theorem, as recalled in
\cite[Sec. 5]{BP} and originally proved in \cite{RudinStout}, and with
finite Taylor-section matching for the Agler class by Bhowmik--Putinar
\cite{BP}.  Since the Agler and Schur classes agree on the bidisc by
Ando's theorem, this gives a complete positive bidisc approximation
statement in the usual Schur, equivalently Herglotz via Cayley transform,
sense.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If \(\theta=(F-1)/(F+1)\) is the Cayley transform of
\(F\in\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\), rational-inner approximation of \(\theta\)
by functions vanishing at the origin gives approximation of \(F\) by
members of \(\mathcal R_0(\D^d)\) after applying the inverse Cayley
transform.  The theorem then gives weak-star density of the corresponding
Clark measures.  The cited approximation theorems provide the stated
classes.
\end{proof}

\section*{A Two-Way Polydisc Route}

We isolate the two assertions involved in the polydisc case.  Let
\(\mathsf{KP}_d\) denote the normalized Koranyi--Pukanszky representation:
every \(F\in\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\) has a representing probability measure
\(\mu_F\in\RP(\T^d)\).  Let \(\mathsf{CA}^0_d\) denote the zero-preserving
Caratheodory approximation statement: every Schur function
\(\theta:\D^d\to\overline\D\) with \(\theta(0)=0\) is a locally uniform
limit of rational inner functions \(\theta_n\) with \(\theta_n(0)=0\).
This normalized form is equivalent to the usual Caratheodory approximation
statement by a disk automorphism.

We shall use the following standard finite rationalization lemma of
Pfister--Rudin--Stout.  If \(g\) is holomorphic on a neighbourhood of
\(\overline{\D^d}\), \(g(0)=0\), and \(\|g\|_{\overline{\D^d}}\leq1\),
then \(g\) is locally uniformly approximable by rational inner functions
vanishing at the origin.  Indeed, after replacing \(g\) by a small dilation
and a polynomial \(p\) with \(p(0)=0\) and \(\|p\|_{\overline{\D^d}}<1\),
one uses the reflected-polynomial formula
\[
  \Phi_N(z)=
  \frac{p(z)+z_1\cdots z_d z^N}
       {1+z_1\cdots z_d p^{*N}(z)},
\]
where \(N=(N,\ldots,N)\) is large and
\[
  p^{*N}(z)=z^N\overline{p(1/\overline z)} .
\]
The denominator is nonzero on \(\D^d\), \(\Phi_N(0)=0\),
\(|\Phi_N|=1\) on \(\T^d\), and \(\Phi_N\to p\) locally uniformly as
\(\min_jN_j\to\infty\).

\begin{theorem}[Polydisc implications]
For every \(d\geq1\), \(\mathsf{CA}^0_d\) implies \(\mathsf{KP}_d\).
Conversely, \(\mathsf{KP}_d\), together with the Pfister--Rudin--Stout
finite rationalization lemma above, implies \(\mathsf{CA}^0_d\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First assume \(\mathsf{CA}^0_d\), and let \(F\in\mathfrak H_0(\D^d)\).
Set
\[
  \theta=\frac{F-1}{F+1}.
\]
Then \(\theta\) is Schur and \(\theta(0)=0\).  Choose rational inner
\(\theta_n\) with \(\theta_n(0)=0\) and \(\theta_n\to\theta\) locally
uniformly.  Pick \(0<r_n<1\) with \(r_n\to1\), slowly enough that
\[
  G_n=\frac{1+r_n\theta_n}{1-r_n\theta_n}
\]
converges to \(F\) locally uniformly.  Each \(G_n\) is holomorphic on a
neighbourhood of \(\overline{\D^d}\), has positive real part, and satisfies
\(G_n(0)=1\).  Hence \(h_n=\operatorname{Re}G_n|_{\T^d}\) is a continuous
positive function with \(\int h_n\,dm_d=1\).  Because \(G_n\) has only
nonnegative Fourier frequencies and \(\overline{G_n}\) has only
nonpositive Fourier frequencies, the measure \(h_n\,dm_d\) annihilates
all mixed-sign characters; that is, \(h_n\,dm_d\in\RP(\T^d)\).  The
Cauchy--Szego formula gives
\[
  G_n(z)=\int_{\T^d}\left(2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\right)h_n(u)\,dm_d(u).
\]
By weak-star compactness of probability measures on \(\T^d\), a subnet of
\(h_n\,dm_d\) converges to a probability measure \(\mu\).  The class
\(\RP(\T^d)\) is weak-star closed, so \(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\), and passing to
the limit in the displayed integral gives the desired representation of
\(F\).  This proves \(\mathsf{KP}_d\).

Conversely assume \(\mathsf{KP}_d\), and let \(\theta\) be Schur with
\(\theta(0)=0\).  Put \(F=(1+\theta)/(1-\theta)\), and let
\(\mu\in\RP(\T^d)\) be its representing probability measure.  Let
\(K_N\) be the product Fejer kernel on \(\T^d\), and set
\(\mu_N=K_N*\mu\).  Then \(\mu_N\) is a positive absolutely continuous
probability measure, \(\mu_N\in\RP(\T^d)\), and \(\mu_N\to\mu\) weak-star.
Write \(d\mu_N=h_N\,dm_d\), and define
\[
  F_N(z)=\int_{\T^d}\left(2S_{\D^d}(z,u)-1\right)h_N(u)\,dm_d(u).
\]
Since \(h_N\) is a trigonometric polynomial, \(F_N\) is a holomorphic
polynomial.  The determining condition gives \(\operatorname{Re}F_N\geq0\),
\(F_N(0)=1\), and \(F_N\to F\) locally uniformly.  Therefore
\[
  \theta_N=\frac{F_N-1}{F_N+1}
\]
is Schur, holomorphic on a neighbourhood of \(\overline{\D^d}\), satisfies
\(\theta_N(0)=0\), and converges to \(\theta\) locally uniformly.  Applying
the Pfister--Rudin--Stout finite rationalization lemma to each \(\theta_N\)
and diagonalizing gives rational inner functions vanishing at the origin
that converge locally uniformly to \(\theta\).  This proves
\(\mathsf{CA}^0_d\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Polydisc status]
For the polydisc, the normalized Koranyi--Pukanszky representation and
zero-preserving Caratheodory approximation are equivalent over the standard
Pfister--Rudin--Stout finite rationalization lemma.  The proof of the
Herglotz-to-Caratheodory direction is necessarily non-atomic for \(d\geq2\):
Fejer smoothing inside \(\RP(\T^d)\) replaces the finite point-mass
approximation used in the disc.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Symmetrized-Polydisc Stress Test}

The preceding proof isolates why the polydisc is tractable: \(\T^d\) is a
compact abelian group, and product Fejer convolution turns an arbitrary
positive \(\RP(\T^d)\)-measure into positive continuous densities while
preserving the mixed-sign annihilation conditions.

For the symmetrized polydisc \(\mathbb G_d=s(\D^d)\), the source paper uses
the Szego kernel
\[
  S_{\mathbb G_d}(s(z),s(w))=\prod_{i,j=1}^d(1-z_i\overline{w_j})^{-1}
\]
and the boundary measure \(\nu=s_\#(|J|^2dm_d)\) on
\(b\mathbb G_d=s(\T^d)\) \cite[p. 19]{BBK}.  Bhowmik--Kumar prove
Caratheodory approximation by rational inner functions on quotient domains
\(\theta(\D^d)\), including the symmetrized polydisc
\cite[Theorem C]{BKquot}.  Their proof pulls functions back to
\(\D^d\), averages to a \(G\)-invariant polynomial, and then applies a
Rudin--Stout/Pfister reflected-polynomial construction before descending
through the quotient map.

This gives a useful one-sided check.  If zero-preserving rational-inner
Caratheodory approximation is available on \(\mathbb G_d\), then the
normalized Szego-kernel Herglotz representation follows by the same
scaling argument as in the first half of the polydisc theorem: approximate
the Cayley transform \((F-1)/(F+1)\), replace each approximant \(\theta_n\)
by \(r_n\theta_n\), set \(G_n=(1+r_n\theta_n)/(1-r_n\theta_n)\), and take a
weak-star limit of the measures
\(\operatorname{Re}G_n\,d\nu\).  Since
\(\operatorname{Re}G_n\in H^2(\mathbb G_d)+\overline{H^2(\mathbb G_d)}\),
these measures satisfy the source paper's determining condition.

The reverse implication is where the polydisc proof does not descend.  A
representing measure \(\eta\in\RP(b\mathbb G_d)\) need not come with an
intrinsic convolution calculus on \(b\mathbb G_d\).  The quotient boundary
is not a compact abelian group; in fact Bhowmik--Kumar point out, using
Morton's theorem, that the symmetrized-polydisc boundary is non-orientable
for even \(d\), so Rudin's compact-group convexity machinery cannot be
used there \cite[p. 10]{BKquot}.  Pulling \(\eta\) back to an
\(S_d\)-invariant measure on \(\T^d\) also does not immediately solve the
problem: Fejer smoothing of such a lift must still descend to positive
measures annihilating
\[
  C(b\mathbb G_d)\cap
  \bigl(H^2(\mathbb G_d)+\overline{H^2(\mathbb G_d)}\bigr)^\perp,
\]
and must interact correctly with the weighted Szego measure
\(s_\#(|J|^2dm_d)\), not only with the quotient Haar pushforward
\(s_\#m_d\).

This obstruction is not merely cosmetic: the naive pullback-then-Fejer
procedure fails already for \(\mathbb G_2\).  Write \(s=z_1+z_2\),
\(p=z_1z_2\), and let
\[
  q(s,p)=\frac{s^2}{p}-1 .
\]
On the distinguished boundary,
\[
  q\circ s=1+z_1\overline z_2+z_2\overline z_1 .
\]
The sign-representation Hardy space of \(\mathbb G_2\) is modelled on
the symmetric pullbacks with weighted boundary measure
\(|z_1-z_2|^2dm_2\).  Put \(a=z_1\overline z_2\).  Then
\[
  |z_1-z_2|^2=2-a-a^{-1}
\]
and
\[
  (q\circ s)|z_1-z_2|^2
  =(1+a+a^{-1})(2-a-a^{-1})
  =a+a^{-1}-a^2-a^{-2}.
\]
All Fourier exponents in the last expression are mixed-sign, so \(q\) is
orthogonal to \(H^2(\mathbb G_2)+\overline{H^2(\mathbb G_2)}\), hence
\(q\in C(b\mathbb G_2)\cap W^\perp\).

Let \(\mathcal F_N\) be product Fejer smoothing on \(\T^2\).  Since
\(\mathcal F_N(a)=\lambda_Na\) and
\(\mathcal F_N(a^{-1})=\lambda_Na^{-1}\), where
\(\lambda_N=(1-\frac1{N+1})^2<1\), the smoothed pullback is
\[
  \mathcal F_N(q\circ s)=1+\lambda_N(a+a^{-1}).
\]
Multiplying again by the Szego weight gives a constant Fourier coefficient
\[
  \widehat{\mathcal F_N(q\circ s)|z_1-z_2|^2}(0,0)
  =2(1-\lambda_N)\neq0 .
\]
Thus \(\mathcal F_N(q\circ s)\) is not orthogonal even to the constant
function in \(H^2(\mathbb G_2)\).  Ordinary torus Fejer smoothing therefore
does not preserve the quotient determining annihilator.

Thus a full symmetrized-polydisc equivalence by this route reduces to a
new quotient-boundary smoothing theorem: every positive determining
measure for the Szego Hardy space of \(\mathbb G_d\) should be a weak-star
limit of positive continuous determining densities whose Cayley transforms
can be rationalized on the quotient.  That theorem is not present in the
available source or quotient-domain literature checked here.  This is the
precise remaining barrier, not the atomic obstruction itself.

\section*{Consequence for the Source Problem}

The result rules out the following direct implication strategy:
starting from the Koranyi--Pukanszky Herglotz representation on \(\D^d\),
approximate an arbitrary determining probability measure by finite convex
combinations of point masses that remain in the determining class, and
then take inverse Cayley transforms to obtain rational inner approximants.
Such finite determining point-mass combinations do not exist for
\(d\geq2\).  More strongly, the representing measures in the
Koranyi--Pukanszky class are forced to be diffuse and to have Haar
pushforwards along all mixed-sign characters.

This is only an obstruction to the atomic proof route, not a disproof of
equivalence.  The Clark-density reformulation above shows exactly what
must replace atoms: rational-inner Clark measures, all of which are
diffuse in the Koranyi--Pukanszky setting.  A non-circular proof of the
Herglotz-to-Caratheodory implication from the integral representation
would amount to proving this Clark-measure density directly from the
structure of \(\RP(\T^d)\).  For the symmetrized polydisc, the analogous
missing ingredient is the quotient-boundary smoothing theorem described
above.

\section*{Verification and Novelty Notes}

The proof uses only the Koranyi--Pukanszky mixed Fourier moment condition
quoted in the source paper, the standard uniqueness theorem for finite
Borel measures on \(\T\), and the quotient-domain Caratheodory theorem of
Bhowmik--Kumar \cite{BKquot}.  A bounded search checked the source paper,
the published arXiv/BLMS metadata, and web queries for the exact phrases
``Herglotz representation'', ``Caratheodory approximation'', ``polydisc'',
``symmetrized polydisc'', and ``Clark measures''.  The Clark-measure
literature is consistent with the obstruction: multivariable Clark measures
are positive RP-measures and are singular for inner functions
\cite{Jacobsson}, while bidisc rational-inner Clark measures are known not
to have point masses \cite{BCS}.  The 2025 rational-approximation paper of
Bhowmik--Putinar \cite{BP} gives a strong positive result for Agler-class
Herglotz functions and, on the bidisc, for all Herglotz functions via
Ando's theorem.  The BLMS page for the source paper also records a 2025
addendum clarifying that one theorem in the 2024 paper was previously
known \cite{BBKadd}.  No later exact solution to the full
polydisc/symmetrized-polydisc equivalence question was found in this
bounded check.  This packet should be reviewed as a strengthened partial
obstruction and as a candidate full polydisc subcase, not as a full
solution of the original two-domain question.
