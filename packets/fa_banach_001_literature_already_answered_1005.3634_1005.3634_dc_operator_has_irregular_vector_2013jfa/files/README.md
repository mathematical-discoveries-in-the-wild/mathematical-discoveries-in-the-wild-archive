# Literature status: distributionally chaotic operators admit irregular vectors

Status: `literature_already_answered`.

Source target: arXiv:1005.3634, F. Bermudez, A. Bonilla, F. Martinez-Gimenez, A. Peris, "Li-Yorke and distributionally chaotic operators", J. Math. Anal. Appl. 373 (2011), 83-93, DOI: `10.1016/j.jmaa.2010.06.011`.

Source question: Problem 1 in Section 4 ("Distributionally chaotic operators"), immediately after Proposition 16, asks whether every distributionally chaotic operator \(T:X\to X\) admits a distributionally irregular vector.

Answer source: N. C. Bernardes Jr., A. Bonilla, V. Muller, A. Peris, "Distributional chaos for linear operators", J. Funct. Anal. 265 (9) (2013), 2143-2163, DOI: `10.1016/j.jfa.2013.06.019`.

The later JFA paper gives Theorem 12, whose equivalent conditions include: \(T\) is distributionally chaotic and \(T\) has a distributionally irregular vector. This exactly answers Problem 1 of the source paper in the affirmative for bounded linear operators on Banach spaces.
