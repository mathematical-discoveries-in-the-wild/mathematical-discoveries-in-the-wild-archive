\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Veronica Dimant, Daniel Galicer, and Ricardo Garcia,
\emph{Geometry of integral polynomials, \(M\)-ideals and unique norm
preserving extensions}, arXiv:1108.3975. After proving that, over the real
scalars, symmetric injective tensor powers never preserve nontrivial
\(M\)-ideals, the authors ask the following.

\begin{quote}
Question 2.7. If \(X\) is a nontrivial \(M\)-ideal in \(Y\), \(X\) and \(Y\)
complex Banach spaces, could
\(\widehat\bigotimes^{k,s}_{\varepsilon_{k,s}}X\) be an \(M\)-ideal in
\(\widehat\bigotimes^{k,s}_{\varepsilon_{k,s}}Y\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Claim}

We prove a partial negative answer: the phenomenon cannot occur for
nontrivial complex \(M\)-summands. Thus any positive example to Question 2.7
would have to be a genuinely non-complemented \(M\)-ideal.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The obstruction is already visible in
\(\C e_1\subset \ell_\infty^2\). The symmetric injective tensor norm can be
read as the supremum norm of \(k\)-homogeneous polynomials on the unit ball of
\((\ell_\infty^2)^*=\ell_1^2\). The mixed monomial \(z^{k-1}w\), with the
right normalizing constant, has norm one. But adding any nonzero pure monomial
\(\delta z^k\) makes the norm strictly larger than one, because at the point
\((|z|,|w|)=((k-1)/k,1/k)\) the mixed monomial is already extremal and the
phase of \(w\) can align the two terms.

The three-ball property for \(M\)-ideals now fails: three pure monomials
\(\omega_j z^k\), with \(\omega_j\) the cubic roots of unity, cannot all be
corrected by one pure monomial \(\lambda z^k\). At least one difference
\(\omega_j-\lambda\) has modulus at least one, forcing a uniform norm jump.

\section*{Finite Obstruction}

We use the standard three-ball characterization of \(M\)-ideals: if \(J\) is
an \(M\)-ideal in \(E\), then for every \(u\in B_E\), every
\(v_1,v_2,v_3\in B_J\), and every \(\eta>0\), there is \(v\in J\) such that
\[
  \|u+v_i-v\|\le 1+\eta,\qquad i=1,2,3.
\]

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:finite}
Let \(k\ge2\), let \(E_k=\symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}\ell_\infty^2\) over
the complex scalars, and let
\[
  J_k=\symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}\C e_1\subset E_k .
\]
Then \(J_k\) is not an \(M\)-ideal in \(E_k\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Under the usual polynomial representation of the symmetric injective tensor
norm,
\[
  \|p\|_{E_k}
  = \sup\{ |p(z,w)|:\ |z|+|w|\le 1\}.
\]
The subspace \(J_k\) consists of the pure monomials \(\lambda z^k\).

Set
\[
  C_k=\frac{k^k}{(k-1)^{k-1}},
  \qquad
  p_0(z,w)=C_k z^{k-1}w .
\]
Since
\[
  \max_{0\le t\le1} t^{k-1}(1-t)
  =\left(\frac{k-1}{k}\right)^{k-1}\frac1k,
\]
we have \(\|p_0\|=1\).

Let
\[
  a_k=\left(\frac{k-1}{k}\right)^k.
\]
For every \(\delta\in\C\), choose
\(|z|=(k-1)/k\) and \(|w|=1/k\), and choose the phase of \(w\) so that
\(C_k z^{k-1}w\) and \(\delta z^k\) have the same argument. Then
\[
  \|p_0+\delta z^k\|\ge 1+a_k|\delta|.
\]

Let \(\omega_1,\omega_2,\omega_3\) be the cubic roots of unity. For every
\(\lambda\in\C\),
\[
  \max_{1\le j\le3}|\omega_j-\lambda|\ge 1,
\]
because the smallest closed disk containing the three cubic roots of unity is
the unit disk centered at the origin. Hence for every \(\lambda\in\C\),
\[
  \max_{1\le j\le3}
  \|p_0+\omega_j z^k-\lambda z^k\|
  \ge 1+a_k .
\]
Taking \(u=p_0\) and \(v_j=\omega_jz^k\in B_{J_k}\), the three-ball property
fails, for instance with any \(0<\eta<a_k\). Therefore \(J_k\) is not an
\(M\)-ideal in \(E_k\).
\end{proof}

\section*{M-Summand Consequence}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:msummand}
Let \(k\ge2\), and let \(X\) and \(Z\) be nonzero complex Banach spaces. Put
\(Y=X\oplus_\infty Z\). Then the canonical subspace
\[
  \symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}X
\]
is not an \(M\)-ideal in
\[
  \symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}Y .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(x_0\in S_X\), \(z_0\in S_Z\), and norming functionals
\(x^*\in S_{X^*}\), \(z^*\in S_{Z^*}\) with
\(x^*(x_0)=z^*(z_0)=1\). Define a norm-one projection
\[
  R:Y=X\oplus_\infty Z\longrightarrow \operatorname{span}\{x_0,z_0\}
\]
by
\[
  R(x,z)=x^*(x)x_0+z^*(z)z_0 .
\]
The range is isometric to \(\ell_\infty^2\), and \(R\) maps \(X\) onto
\(\C x_0\). Since the symmetric injective norm is functorial for contractions,
\(\otimes^{k,s}R\) induces a contraction
\[
  \symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}Y
  \longrightarrow
  \symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}\operatorname{span}\{x_0,z_0\}
\]
which fixes the finite-dimensional tensor subspace generated by \(x_0,z_0\)
and maps \(\symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}X\) into the pure subspace generated
by \(x_0^k\).

Assume, toward a contradiction, that
\(\symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}X\) is an \(M\)-ideal in
\(\symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}Y\). Apply the three-ball property to the
finite obstruction from Lemma~\ref{lem:finite}, transported to
\(\operatorname{span}\{x_0,z_0\}\). Thus \(u\) is the normalized mixed monomial
and \(v_1,v_2,v_3\) are the three pure root-of-unity monomials.

For \(0<\eta<a_k\), the three-ball property would give
\(v\in \symten{k}{\varepsilon_{k,s}}X\) such that
\[
  \|u+v_i-v\|\le 1+\eta,\qquad i=1,2,3.
\]
Applying the contraction \(\otimes^{k,s}R\) gives a pure correction in the
two-dimensional model with the same three inequalities, contradicting
Lemma~\ref{lem:finite}. Therefore the global \(M\)-ideal cannot exist.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only the standard polynomial formula for the symmetric
injective norm on \(\ell_\infty^2\), functoriality of the injective symmetric
tensor norm for contractions, and the three-ball characterization of
\(M\)-ideals. The accompanying script
\texttt{code/verify\_obstruction.py} checks the
monomial normalization and the lower bound
\[
  \|p_0+\delta z^k\|\ge 1+\left(\frac{k-1}{k}\right)^k|\delta|
\]
for several values of \(k\).

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This packet is not a full solution to Question 2.7. It rules out all
nontrivial complex \(M\)-summands, including the basic example
\(\C\subset \ell_\infty^2\). It does not rule out proper non-complemented
complex \(M\)-ideals.

The run indexes were searched for arXiv:1108.3975, integral polynomials,
complex \(M\)-ideals, symmetric injective tensor products, and unique
norm-preserving extensions; no exact prior packet was found. A bounded web
search for the exact question, the paper title, and later complex
\(M\)-ideal/symmetric tensor keywords did not locate a later answer. The
source paper itself proves the real case but explicitly leaves the complex
case open.
