\section*{Source question}

Ben Yaacov and Henson ask in Question 6.8 of \cite{BYH}:

\begin{quote}
Is there any infinite-dimensional \(E\) other than \(\mathbb G\) over which
the isolated types are dense?  Is there any infinite-dimensional \(E\) over
which there are unrealised isolated types?  Specifically, what happens in the
case \(E=\ell_1\), \(E^*=\ell_\infty\), to which Proposition 6.7 does not apply?
\end{quote}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/open_question_crop.png}
\end{center}

This packet answers the second question positively, and does so in the
specific case \(E=\ell_1\).  It does not settle whether isolated types are
dense over \(\ell_1\).

\section*{Background from the source paper}

For a Banach space \(E\), one-types over \(E\) are identified in \cite{BYH}
with convex Katetov functions \(f\in \KC(E)\).  If \(f^*\) is the
Legendre-Fenchel conjugate, then Lemma 4.12 of \cite{BYH} says that a closed
convex function \(h\) on \(B(E^*)\), extended by \(+\infty\) outside \(B(E^*)\),
is the conjugate of some \(f\in \KC(E)\) provided
\[
  h(\lambda)+h(-\lambda)\leq 0 \qquad (\lambda\in B(E^*)).
\]
Lemma 5.2 says that \(f\) is local exactly when \(f^*\) is continuous on
\(B(E^*)\), and Theorem 5.7 says that \(f\) is isolated exactly when it is both
local and \(\bd\)-extreme.  Finally, Lemma 4.14 says that a type is realised in
\(E\) exactly when \(f^*(0)=0\).

\section*{Proof intuition}

The weak-star unit ball of \((\ell_1)^*=\ell_\infty\) is the product cube
\([-1,1]^\N\), whose extreme points are the sign sequences
\(\{-1,1\}^\N\).  Put the odd parity value
\[
  p(\eps)=\eps_1\eps_2\eps_3
\]
on those extreme points, and take its convex envelope \(h\) on the whole cube.
The oddness of \(p\) gives the antipode inequality.  The fact that \(h\) is the
convex envelope of its extreme-point values makes it impossible to raise
\(h\) on the boundary while preserving the antipode inequality, so the
corresponding type is \(\bd\)-extreme.  Since this envelope depends on only
three coordinates, it is weak-star continuous.  At the origin, however, the
four sign vectors of parity \(-1\) average to \(0\), so \(h(0)=-1\); hence the
type is not realised.

\section*{The construction}

Let
\[
  K=[-1,1]^\N
\]
with the product topology, identified with \(B(\ell_\infty)\) in the weak-star
topology \(\sigma(\ell_\infty,\ell_1)\).  Let
\(\Omega=\{-1,1\}^\N\), the set of extreme points of \(K\), and define
\[
  p(\eps)=\eps_1\eps_2\eps_3 \qquad (\eps\in\Omega).
\]
For \(\lambda\in K\), set
\[
  h(\lambda)=
  \inf\left\{
    \int_\Omega p(\eps)\,d\mu(\eps):
    \mu\in\Prob(\Omega),\
    \int_\Omega \eps_n\,d\mu(\eps)=\lambda_n\text{ for every }n
  \right\}.
\]
The set of representing measures is never empty; for example, take the product
measure with one-dimensional means \(\lambda_n\).  Since \(p\) only depends on
the first three coordinates, \(h\) is just the convex envelope on the
three-dimensional cube of
\((s_1,s_2,s_3)\mapsto s_1s_2s_3\) on \(\{-1,1\}^3\).  In particular \(h\) is a
finite continuous convex function on \(K\), depending only on
\(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3\).

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:antipode}
The function \(h\) satisfies \(h(\lambda)+h(-\lambda)\leq 0\) for all
\(\lambda\in K\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\mu\) represent \(\lambda\), and let \(\check\mu\) be its image under
\(\eps\mapsto -\eps\).  Then \(\check\mu\) represents \(-\lambda\), while
\[
  \int p\,d\check\mu=\int p(-\eps)\,d\mu(\eps)=-\int p\,d\mu,
\]
because \(p\) is odd.  Therefore \(h(-\lambda)\leq -\int p\,d\mu\).  Taking the
infimum over all representing \(\mu\) gives \(h(\lambda)\leq -h(-\lambda)\).
\end{proof}

By Lemma~\ref{lem:antipode} and the conjugate criterion of \cite[Lemma
4.12]{BYH}, the function
\[
  f(x)=\sup_{\lambda\in K}\bigl(\lambda(x)-h(\lambda)\bigr)
  \qquad (x\in\ell_1)
\]
belongs to \(\KC(\ell_1)\), and \(f^*=h\).  Thus \(f\) is a one-type over
\(\ell_1\).

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:extreme}
The type \(f\) is \(\bd\)-extreme.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(g\in\KC(\ell_1)\) satisfy \(g\leq f\), and put \(k=g^*\).  Then
\(k\geq h\) on \(K\), and \(k\) satisfies the same antipode inequality
\[
  k(\lambda)+k(-\lambda)\leq 0.
\]
We first show that \(k=h\) on the extreme points \(\Omega\).  If
\(\eps\in\Omega\), then
\[
  k(\eps)\leq -k(-\eps)\leq -h(-\eps)=h(\eps),
\]
because \(p(-\eps)=-p(\eps)\) and \(h\) agrees with \(p\) on \(\Omega\).
Together with \(k\geq h\), this gives \(k(\eps)=h(\eps)\) for all
\(\eps\in\Omega\).

Now fix \(\lambda\in K\).  For every representing measure \(\mu\) on
\(\Omega\), Jensen's inequality for the lower semicontinuous convex function
\(k\) gives
\[
  k(\lambda)\leq \int_\Omega k(\eps)\,d\mu(\eps)
  =\int_\Omega h(\eps)\,d\mu(\eps)
  =\int_\Omega p(\eps)\,d\mu(\eps).
\]
Taking the infimum over all representing measures yields \(k(\lambda)\leq
h(\lambda)\).  Since \(k\geq h\), we have \(k=h\) on all of \(K\), in
particular on \(\bd B(\ell_\infty)\).  This is exactly \(\bd\)-extremality of
\(f\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
There is an isolated one-type over \(\ell_1\) that is not realised in \(\ell_1\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The conjugate \(f^*=h\) is continuous on \(B(\ell_\infty)\), so \(f\) is local
by \cite[Lemma 5.2]{BYH}.  By Lemma~\ref{lem:extreme}, \(f\) is also
\(\bd\)-extreme.  Therefore \(f\) is isolated by \cite[Theorem 5.7]{BYH}.

It remains to see that \(f\) is not realised.  Let \(A\subset\{-1,1\}^3\) be
the four sign triples with product \(-1\).  Their average is \((0,0,0)\), so
the uniform measure on \(A\), extended by any product measure on the remaining
coordinates with mean zero, represents \(0\in K\) and has \(p\)-integral
\(-1\).  Since \(p\geq -1\), this proves \(h(0)=-1\).

By \cite[Lemma 4.14]{BYH}, a one-type over \(E\) is realised in \(E\) if and
only if its conjugate takes value \(0\) at the origin.  Since \(f^*(0)=h(0)=-1\),
the isolated type \(f\) is not realised in \(\ell_1\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and limitations}

The proof is purely analytic.  The script
\texttt{code/parity\_envelope\_check.py} verifies the finite parity fact used
at the origin: the four parity-\(-1\) vertices in \(\{-1,1\}^3\) average to
\((0,0,0)\), and the parity integral there is \(-1\).

This is a full positive answer to the unrealised-isolated-type subquestion in
Question 6.8, including the specific case \(E=\ell_1\).  It does not prove that
the isolated types over \(\ell_1\) are dense, and it does not characterise all
separable Banach spaces \(E\) for which \(\mathbb G[E]\) exists.

\section*{Human review recommendation}

Review as a candidate full solution to the second part of Question 6.8.  The
main point to check is Lemma~\ref{lem:extreme}: the convex-envelope maximality
on the extreme sign sequences is what upgrades the continuous parity envelope
from a convex Katetov type to an isolated type.
