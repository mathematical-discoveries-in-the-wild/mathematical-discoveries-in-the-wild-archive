\section{Source and open question}

Fan, Queff\'elec, and Queff\'elec define the random Furstenberg set
$T=T(\omega)$ by independent selectors
\begin{equation}\label{eq:selectors}
 \mathbb P(n\in T)=\delta_n:=\frac{\log n}{n},\qquad n\geq2.
\end{equation}
In arXiv:2104.08944v2 they prove that $T$ is almost surely $p$-Rider for
$p>4/3$ and then ask whether $T$, like the deterministic Furstenberg set,
is a $\Lambda(p)$-set for every finite $p$~\cite[p.~9]{source}.

\begin{figure}[ht]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\caption{The open question in arXiv:2104.08944v2, PDF page 9, immediately
after Theorem 1.7. The crop retains the full readable text width.}
\end{figure}

For $q>2$, a set $E\subset\mathbb Z$ is a $\Lambda(q)$-set if there is a
constant $C_{q,E}<\infty$ such that every trigonometric polynomial
$f(x)=\sum_{n\in E}a_ne^{2\pi i n x}$ satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lambda-def}
 \|f\|_{L^q(\Torus)}\leq C_{q,E}
 \Big(\sum_{n\in E}|a_n|^2\Big)^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

\section{Proof intuition}

The selector means in \eqref{eq:selectors} are just large enough to give
$|T\cap[1,N]|$ order $(\log N)^2$, but they are small enough that every
\emph{fixed} additive-relation type occurs only finitely often. Indeed, if a
fixed integer relation has largest entry $M$, another entry must be comparable
to $M$. Once the other entries are fixed, this second large entry is
determined. The expected number of such relations is then bounded by a
convergent series of order
$\sum_M (\log M)^{2r-2}/M^2$.

The key bookkeeping point is that a Fourier moment contains repeated indices.
Those repetitions cannot be fed directly into the independence calculation.
Instead, cancel the common multiplicity of each frequency on the two sides of
the moment equation. The remaining distinct support is one of finitely many
random relation cores. The canceled part is an arbitrary common multiset, but
summing it produces exactly a power of the coefficient $\ell^2$ norm. Thus
every even Fourier moment has the required bound.

\section{Main theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $T$ be the random subset of the positive integers defined by the
independent selectors \eqref{eq:selectors}. With probability one, simultaneously
for every finite $q>2$, the set $T$ is a $\Lambda(q)$-set.
Equivalently, almost surely, for every finite $q>2$ there is a random constant
$C_{q,T}<\infty$ for which \eqref{eq:lambda-def} holds for all finitely
supported coefficient families on $T$.
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof}

We first isolate the probabilistic input. A tuple is called injective if all
its entries are distinct.

\begin{lemma}[Only finitely many fixed injective relations]\label{lem:cores}
Fix $r\geq1$ and nonzero integers $c_1,\ldots,c_r$. Almost surely there are
only finitely many injective tuples $(v_1,\ldots,v_r)\in T^r$ satisfying
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fixed-relation}
 c_1v_1+\cdots+c_rv_r=0.
\end{equation}
Moreover, the almost-sure event can be chosen simultaneously for all such
finite integer coefficient vectors.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
There is nothing to prove for a coefficient vector admitting no positive
solution, so suppose a solution is given and put $M=\max_k v_k$. Choose an
index $i$ with $v_i=M$. The relation gives
\[
 |c_i|M\leq\sum_{k\ne i}|c_k|v_k.
\]
Consequently some $j\ne i$ satisfies $v_j\geq\kappa M$, where
$\kappa>0$ depends only on the fixed coefficient vector. There are only
finitely many choices for the ordered pair $(i,j)$.

Write $D(M)=\sum_{n\leq M}\delta_n$. For $M$ large enough,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:selector-bounds}
 D(M)\leq C(1+\log M)^2,
 \qquad
 \delta_M\leq C\frac{1+\log M}{M},
 \qquad
 \sup_{\kappa M\leq n\leq M}\delta_n
 \leq C_{\kappa}\frac{1+\log M}{M}.
\end{equation}
Fix $i,j$, the value $M=v_i$, and all $v_k$ with $k\ne i,j$.
Equation \eqref{eq:fixed-relation} determines $v_j$ uniquely. Restricting to
injective tuples lets us multiply the selector probabilities. Therefore the
expected number $N_c$ of injective solutions is bounded by
\begin{align*}
 \E N_c
 &\leq O_c(1)+
 C_c\sum_{M\geq3}
 \delta_M\frac{1+\log M}{M}D(M)^{r-2} \\
 &\leq O_c(1)+C_c'
 \sum_{M\geq3}\frac{(1+\log M)^{2r-2}}{M^2}<\infty.
\end{align*}
For $r=1$ there are no positive solutions, and for $r=2$ the factor
$D(M)^{r-2}$ is interpreted as $1$.

Thus $N_c<\infty$ almost surely. There are only countably many finite integer
coefficient vectors, so intersecting their probability-one events proves the
simultaneous assertion.
\end{proof}

We now work on the probability-one event supplied by Lemma~\ref{lem:cores}.
Fix an integer $m\geq2$ and a trigonometric polynomial
\[
 f(x)=\sum_{n\in T}a_ne^{2\pi i n x},
 \qquad B:=\sum_{n\in T}|a_n|^2<\infty.
\]
Fourier orthogonality gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:moment-expansion}
 \|f\|_{2m}^{2m}
 =\sum_{x_1+\cdots+x_m=y_1+\cdots+y_m}
 a_{x_1}\cdots a_{x_m}
 \overline{a_{y_1}\cdots a_{y_m}},
\end{equation}
where all indices lie in $T$ and in the finite support of $f$.

For a tuple in \eqref{eq:moment-expansion} and each distinct frequency $v$,
let
\[
 c_v=\#\{i:x_i=v\}-\#\{j:y_j=v\}.
\]
Delete the frequencies with $c_v=0$. If none remain, call the tuple diagonal.
Otherwise the remaining distinct frequencies form an injective reduced core
\begin{equation}\label{eq:core}
 \sum_{v\in V}c_vv=0,
 \qquad 0<|c_v|\leq m,
 \qquad \sum_{v\in V}c_v=0.
\end{equation}
For fixed $m$ there are only finitely many possible coefficient patterns in
\eqref{eq:core}; Lemma~\ref{lem:cores} says that each has only finitely many
realizations in $T$. Hence the total number $K_m(T)$ of nonempty reduced cores
is finite.

Fix one core and put
\[
 s=\sum_{c_v>0}c_v=-\sum_{c_v<0}c_v\leq m.
\]
Every full tuple reducing to this core is obtained by adding a common multiset
of $m-s$ frequencies to its two sides and then ordering the slots. To obtain
an upper bound, represent that multiset by an ordered list
$z_1,\ldots,z_{m-s}\in T$ and allow all slot permutations. The total absolute
contribution of the fixed core to \eqref{eq:moment-expansion} is at most
\begin{align*}
 (m!)^2
 \Big(\prod_{v\in V}|a_v|^{|c_v|}\Big)
 \sum_{z_1,\ldots,z_{m-s}\in T}
 \prod_{\ell=1}^{m-s}|a_{z_\ell}|^2
 &=(m!)^2
 \Big(\prod_{v\in V}|a_v|^{|c_v|}\Big)B^{m-s} \\
 &\leq(m!)^2B^m.
\end{align*}
The last inequality uses $|a_v|\leq B^{1/2}$ and
$\sum_v|c_v|=2s$. The diagonal tuples obey the same estimate with the empty
core. Taking absolute values termwise in \eqref{eq:moment-expansion} therefore
gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:even-bound}
 \|f\|_{2m}^{2m}
 \leq (m!)^2\bigl(1+K_m(T)\bigr)B^m.
\end{equation}
Thus $T$ is a $\Lambda(2m)$-set for every integer $m\geq2$.

Finally, given any finite $q>2$, choose $m$ with $2m\geq q$. Haar measure on
$\Torus$ is normalized, so $\|f\|_q\leq\|f\|_{2m}$. Combining this with
\eqref{eq:even-bound} proves the $\Lambda(q)$ inequality and completes the
proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

\section{Verification notes}

Two tempting shortcuts would be false, and the proof avoids both. First, a
full $2m$-term relation need not occur only finitely often: a fixed nontrivial
core can be padded by arbitrary common frequencies. The proof sums this padding
as $B^{m-s}$. Second, repeated indices cannot be assigned a product of selector
probabilities. Independence is invoked only after the support has been reduced
to distinct core frequencies.

The relation-count estimate is uniform enough for simultaneous use because
there are countably many coefficient vectors. For any fixed moment order only
finitely many of those vectors are relevant. No computational experiment is
an ingredient of the proof.

\section{Novelty search, scope, and limitations}

The run's registry, solution, attempt, and proof-gap indexes were searched for
arXiv:2104.08944, the exact title, random Furstenberg sets, $\Lambda(q)$,
$p$-Rider, quasi-independent relations, and selector means $\log n/n$.
No duplicate was found.

The authors' 2023 follow-up still states that a positive-density subset
$T'\subset T$ is $\Lambda(q)$ for every finite $q$, but that they do not know
whether $T$ itself has this property~\cite[Section 5.2]{followup}. Bounded
arXiv-focused and general web searches on 19 July 2026 used the exact titles,
authors, quoted question, and close selector formulations. They found the 2021
source and 2023 follow-up but no later resolution. This is not an exhaustive
bibliographic guarantee.

The theorem proves the qualitative $\Lambda(q)$ property with an almost-sure
constant depending on $q$ and the realization. It does not provide a useful
deterministic bound on that random constant, and it does not settle whether
$T$ is $4/3$-Rider or whether the deterministic Furstenberg set $S$ is
$4/3$-Rider or $4/3$-Sidon.

\section{Human-review recommendation}

This is a candidate full affirmative answer to the source's whole-set
$\Lambda(q)$ question. The highest-value expert checks are (i) the summation
over a coefficient-dependent comparable pair in Lemma~\ref{lem:cores}; and
(ii) the claim that every moment tuple is overcounted by one reduced core,
an ordered common list, and at most $(m!)^2$ slot permutations.
