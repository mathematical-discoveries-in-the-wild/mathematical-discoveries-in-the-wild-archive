\noindent\textbf{Status.} Full answer, likely valid. The source paper asks for
a condition on an adequate family \(\A\) guaranteeing that \(h_{\A,p}\) does
not contain \(c_0\), equivalently that its canonical basis is boundedly
complete. The answer below gives an exact block-measure criterion.

\section*{Source Question}

The source is Trond A. Abrahamsen, Vegard Lima, and Andre Martiny,
\emph{Delta-points in Banach spaces generated by adequate families},
arXiv:2012.00406. In the final section, after proving their main results on
delta-points and polyhedrality, the authors write:
\begin{quote}
``Note that \(h_{\mathcal A,1}\) and even \(h_{\mathcal A,p}\) contains an
isometric copy of \(c_0\) if there exists an infinite subset
\(E\subseteq\mathbb N\) such that \(|A\cap E|\leq 1\) for all
\(A\in\mathcal A\). We ask:

What is a natural condition on \(\mathcal A\) such that \(h_{\mathcal A,1}\)
\((\)or \(h_{\mathcal A,p})\) does not contain \(c_0\). That is, when does
\(h_{\mathcal A,p}\) have a boundedly complete basis?''
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions}

An adequate family \(\A\) of subsets of \(\N\) contains the empty set and all
singletons, is hereditary, and is compact in the topology of pointwise
convergence. For \(1\leq p<\infty\), \(h_{\A,p}\) is the completion of
\(c_{00}\) under the norm
\[
  \left\|\sum_i a_i e_i\right\|
  =
  \sup_{A\in\A}\left(\sum_{i\in A}|a_i|^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
The canonical basis is normalized and \(1\)-unconditional.

For a finitely supported nonnegative measure \(\mu\) on \(\N\), write
\[
  \|\mu\|_{\A}=\sup_{A\in\A}\mu(A).
\]
A sequence \((\mu_n)\) of finitely supported nonnegative measures is
\emph{successive} if their supports are finite intervals in increasing order,
or more generally finite sets \(I_n=\supp\mu_n\) satisfying
\(\max I_n<\min I_{n+1}\). It is an \(\A\)-bounded normalized block-measure
sequence if
\[
  \|\mu_n\|_{\A}=1\quad(n\in\N)
\]
and
\[
  C(\mu):=\sup_{m\in\N}\sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}\mu_n(A)<\infty.
\]

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The norm of \(h_{\A,p}\) reads the \(p\)-mass of a vector through the sets in
\(\A\). Thus a normalized positive block \(x_n\) determines a normalized
measure \(\mu_n(i)=|x_n(i)|^p\), and the norm of a positive sum of such blocks
is exactly the \(p\)-th root of the accumulated \(\A\)-mass of those measures.

This makes the \(c_0\) obstruction transparent. If the accumulated
\(\A\)-mass of normalized block measures is uniformly bounded, the associated
blocks have uniformly bounded finite sums and are therefore equivalent to the
unit vector basis of \(c_0\). Conversely, any copy of \(c_0\) in a space with a
Schauder basis can be gliding-humped into a block sequence equivalent to the
\(c_0\) basis. Taking coordinatewise absolute values and \(p\)-th powers of
that block sequence gives the required bounded block-measure sequence.

\begin{theorem}[Block-measure criterion]\label{thm:blockmeasure}
Let \(\A\) be an adequate family and let \(1\leq p<\infty\). Then
\(h_{\A,p}\) contains an isomorphic copy of \(c_0\) if and only if there is an
\(\A\)-bounded normalized block-measure sequence.

Equivalently, \(h_{\A,p}\) contains no copy of \(c_0\) if and only if every
successive sequence \((\mu_n)\) of finitely supported nonnegative measures with
\(\|\mu_n\|_{\A}=1\) satisfies
\[
  \sup_{m\in\N}\sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}\mu_n(A)=\infty.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First assume that \((\mu_n)\) is an \(\A\)-bounded normalized block-measure
sequence. Let \(I_n=\supp\mu_n\), and define
\[
  x_n=\sum_{i\in I_n}\mu_n(\{i\})^{1/p}e_i .
\]
Then
\[
  \|x_n\|^p
  =
  \sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{i\in A}\mu_n(\{i\})
  =
  \|\mu_n\|_{\A}=1.
\]

Let \(a_1,\ldots,a_m\) be scalars and put
\[
  M=\max_{1\leq n\leq m}|a_n|.
\]
Since the canonical basis is \(1\)-unconditional, coordinate projections onto
arbitrary sets have norm at most one. Hence, for each \(k\leq m\),
\[
  |a_k|
  =
  \|a_k x_k\|
  =
  \left\|P_{I_k}\sum_{n=1}^{m}a_nx_n\right\|
  \leq
  \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m}a_nx_n\right\|.
\]
Taking the maximum over \(k\) gives the lower \(c_0\) estimate.

For the upper estimate, by the definition of the norm,
\[
 \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m}a_nx_n\right\|^p
 =
 \sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}|a_n|^p\mu_n(A)
 \leq
 M^p \sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}\mu_n(A)
 \leq
 M^p C(\mu).
\]
Thus \((x_n)\) is equivalent to the unit vector basis of \(c_0\), and
\(h_{\A,p}\) contains \(c_0\).

Conversely suppose that \(h_{\A,p}\) contains a subspace isomorphic to
\(c_0\). Let \((u_n)\) be the unit vector basis of \(c_0\) and let
\(T:c_0\to h_{\A,p}\) be an isomorphic embedding. Then \(T u_n\) is a
seminormalized weakly null basic sequence equivalent to the \(c_0\) basis.
Since \(h_{\A,p}\) has a Schauder basis, the standard gliding-hump form of the
Bessaga-Pelczynski selection principle gives a subsequence and a normalized
block basic sequence \((z_n)\), with successive finite supports \(I_n\), which
is equivalent to a subsequence of \((T u_n)\). In particular, there is \(K<\infty\)
such that
\[
  \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m} b_n z_n\right\|
  \leq
  K\max_{1\leq n\leq m}|b_n|
\]
for all finite scalar choices \((b_n)\).

Because the canonical basis of \(h_{\A,p}\) is \(1\)-unconditional and the
supports of the \(z_n\)'s are disjoint,
\[
  \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m}|z_n|\right\|
  =
  \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m}z_n\right\|
  \leq K,
\]
where \(|z_n|\) denotes the coordinatewise absolute value. Define
\[
  \mu_n(\{i\})=|z_n(i)|^p\quad (i\in I_n),
  \qquad
  \mu_n(\{i\})=0\quad (i\notin I_n).
\]
Then
\[
  \|\mu_n\|_{\A}
  =
  \sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{i\in A}|z_n(i)|^p
  =
  \|z_n\|^p=1.
\]
Moreover, for every \(m\),
\[
  \sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}\mu_n(A)
  =
  \left\|\sum_{n=1}^{m}|z_n|\right\|^p
  \leq K^p.
\]
Thus \((\mu_n)\) is an \(\A\)-bounded normalized block-measure sequence.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Answer to the boundedly-complete-basis question]
Let \(\A\) be an adequate family and \(1\leq p<\infty\). The canonical basis
of \(h_{\A,p}\) is boundedly complete if and only if \(\A\) admits no
\(\A\)-bounded normalized block-measure sequence.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By James' classical theorem for unconditional bases, a Banach space with an
unconditional basis contains no copy of \(c_0\) if and only if that basis is
boundedly complete. Apply this to the normalized \(1\)-unconditional basis of
\(h_{\A,p}\) and use Theorem~\ref{thm:blockmeasure}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The source sufficient condition]
If there is an infinite set \(E=\{i_1<i_2<\cdots\}\subseteq\N\) and a constant
\(M<\infty\) such that
\[
  |A\cap E|\leq M\qquad(A\in\A),
\]
then \(h_{\A,p}\) contains an isomorphic copy of \(c_0\). In the case \(M=1\)
this is the isometric-copy observation recorded in the source paper.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Take \(\mu_n=\delta_{i_n}\). Then \(\|\mu_n\|_{\A}=1\) because singletons
belong to \(\A\), and
\[
  \sup_{m}\sup_{A\in\A}\sum_{n=1}^{m}\mu_n(A)
  =
  \sup_m\sup_{A\in\A}|A\cap\{i_1,\ldots,i_m\}|
  \leq M.
\]
Theorem~\ref{thm:blockmeasure} applies.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof is a direct norm computation plus two standard facts about Schauder
bases: the gliding-hump/block selection principle for seminormalized weakly
null sequences, and James' bounded-completeness characterization for
unconditional bases. The source paper itself records that the canonical basis
of \(h_{\A,p}\) is normalized and \(1\)-unconditional.

Local run indexes were searched for arXiv:2012.00406 and the phrases
\emph{adequate family}, \emph{delta-points in Banach spaces generated by
adequate families}, \emph{\(h_{\A,p}\)}, \emph{\(c_0\)}, and
\emph{boundedly complete basis}. No existing packet for this question was
found. Web searches on June 20, 2026 for the exact title, the exact
polyhedral/delta-point question, \emph{\(h_{\A}^{*}\) delta-points adequate
family}, and close variants found the source paper but no later paper giving
this block-measure characterization.

\section*{Limitations}

The theorem answers the first closing question in the source paper, namely the
\(c_0\)/boundedly-complete-basis question. It does not answer the separate
question asking whether \((V)\)- or \((I)\)-polyhedrality of \(h_{\A,1}\)
forces \(h_{\A,1}^{*}\) to fail to have delta-points.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review should focus on the converse implication, specifically the use of the
standard fact that a copy of \(c_0\) in a Banach space with a Schauder basis
has a block basic sequence equivalent to the \(c_0\) basis. After that
selection step, the rest of the argument is an explicit calculation in the
defining norm of \(h_{\A,p}\).
