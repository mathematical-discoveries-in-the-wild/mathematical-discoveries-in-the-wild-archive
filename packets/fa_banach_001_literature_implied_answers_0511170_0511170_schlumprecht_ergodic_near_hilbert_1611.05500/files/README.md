# Literature status: Schlumprecht ergodicity question from 0511170

Status: literature-implied answer for the concrete Schlumprecht-space question; literature-implied partial answer for the broader non-ergodic/`ell_2` conjecture.

Source paper: arXiv:math/0511170, Valentin Ferenczi, *On the number of non permutatively equivalent sequences in a Banach space*.

Supporting paper: arXiv:1611.05500, W. Cuellar Carrera, *Non-ergodic Banach spaces are near Hilbert*.

## Source signals

The source paper states the broad conjecture that every separable Banach space is either isomorphic to `ell_2` or ergodic.

Later, after proving that `E_0` Borel reduces to permutative equivalence on block bases of Schlumprecht's space `S`, the source says that it is still unknown whether `S` is ergodic.

## Later answer

The supporting paper proves that every separable non-near-Hilbert Banach space is ergodic. Equivalently, any non-ergodic separable Banach space must be near Hilbert. This is a partial answer to the source's broad `ell_2` conjecture: the unresolved case is restricted to near-Hilbert spaces.

The supporting paper also proves that the family of Schlumprecht-type spaces `S_{p,r}` and their duals are not near Hilbert, and explicitly concludes that they are ergodic. The original Schlumprecht space is `S_{1,infty}`, so the source paper's concrete question about whether `S` is ergodic has a positive answer by direct specialization.

## Scope notes

This is not a new result. The supporting paper does not prove that `ell_2` is the only non-ergodic separable Banach space; it narrows the open problem to near-Hilbert spaces. It does, however, settle the concrete Schlumprecht-space question from arXiv:math/0511170.
