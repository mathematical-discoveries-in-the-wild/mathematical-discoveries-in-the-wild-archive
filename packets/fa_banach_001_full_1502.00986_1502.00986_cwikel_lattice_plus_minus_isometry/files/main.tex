\section*{Status}

\textbf{Candidate full result, likely valid.}
This packet proves the isometry conjectured in Remark 4.1 of Cwikel's
paper \cite{Cwikel2015}: for a couple of complexified Banach lattices
of measurable functions on the same measure space, Cwikel's continuous
plus-minus norm agrees with Calderon's complex interpolation norm.

The earlier order-continuous endpoint result has been moved into the
packet's \texttt{history/} folder.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Michael Cwikel, ``Some alternative definitions for
the `plus-minus' interpolation spaces
$\left\langle A_0,A_1\right\rangle_\theta$ of Jaak Peetre,''
arXiv:1502.00986v2 \cite{Cwikel2015}.  The relevant statement is Remark
4.1 on page 13.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The remark recalls that, for couples of complexified Banach lattices of
measurable functions on the same measure space, the plus-minus space
$\left\langle A_0,A_1\right\rangle_\theta$ is known to coincide with
Calderon's complex interpolation space $[A_0,A_1]_\theta$ up to
equivalent norms.  It then conjectures that the coincidence is actually
isometric when the plus-minus side is equipped with the continuous norm
$\left\langle A_0,A_1\right\rangle_{\theta,(0)}$.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Cwikel already proves the norm-one inclusion
\[
\norm{x}_{\Cplx}\le \norm{x}_{\PM}.
\]
Thus only the reverse inequality is needed.

The direct Calderon-product proof works immediately for elements whose
pointwise Calderon factorization has bounded logarithmic ratio: put a
small scalar kernel at that logarithmic ratio and the endpoint
plus-minus estimates are dominated by the two Calderon factors.  The
old order-continuous packet removed unbounded logarithmic-ratio tails by
order continuity.

The full proof uses a sharper observation.  Calderon's complex space for
Banach lattices is not the whole upper Calderon product in general; by
Shestakov's theorem it is the closure of $X_0\cap X_1$ in the Calderon
product norm \cite{Shestakov1974,Yuan2014}.  For elements already in
$X_0\cap X_1$, the unbounded ratio tails can be killed without order
continuity: on the positive tail, the $X_1$ norm decays exponentially
while the $X_0$ norm is bounded; placing that tail at the midpoint
$t=K/2$ makes both weighted endpoint norms small.  The negative tail is
symmetric.  Therefore the norm-one estimate first holds on
$X_0\cap X_1$, and then passes to its Calderon-product closure, which is
the complex interpolation space.

\section*{Notation and Background}

Let $X_0$ and $X_1$ be complex Banach lattices of measurable functions
on a common measure space, and let $0<\theta<1$.  We write
$\PM$ for Cwikel's continuous plus-minus space and norm from
\cite[Section 3]{Cwikel2015}.  Thus $x\in\PM$ if
$x=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}u(t)\,dt$ for an admissible locally piecewise
continuous $X_0\cap X_1$-valued function $u$, and
\[
\norm{u}_{J}
=\max_{j=0,1}\sup_{\phi}
\left\|\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{(j-\theta)t}\phi(t)u(t)\,dt
\right\|_{X_j},
\]
where the supremum is over locally piecewise continuous scalar
$\phi$ with $\norm{\phi}_\infty\le 1$.  The plus-minus norm is the
infimum of $\norm{u}_J$ over all such representations of $x$.

We use the Calderon product gauge
\[
\norm{f}_{\Cal}
=\inf M,
\]
where the infimum is over all measurable $b\ge |f|$ and real measurable
$s$ such that
\[
 b e^{-\theta s}\in X_0,\quad
 b e^{(1-\theta)s}\in X_1,\quad
 \norm{b e^{-\theta s}}_{X_0}\le M,\quad
 \norm{b e^{(1-\theta)s}}_{X_1}\le M .
\]
This is the usual Calderon product norm, after balancing the two
endpoint factors.

We shall use two standard facts.  First, Calderon's embedding is
contractive:
\[
\norm{x}_{\Cal}\le \norm{x}_{\Cplx}.
\]
Second, Shestakov's Banach-lattice theorem says that Calderon's complex
interpolation space is the closure of $X_0\cap X_1$ in the Calderon
product norm:
\[
\Cplx=\overline{X_0\cap X_1}^{\ \norm{\cdot}_{\Cal}}.
\]
Yuan's note \cite[p. 2]{Yuan2014} records this classical theorem and
its relation to the inner complex method.

\section*{Two Elementary Plus-Minus Estimates}

\begin{lemma}[One-point representation]
\label{lem:point}
For every $z\in X_0\cap X_1$ and every $a\in\mathbb R$,
\[
\norm{z}_{\PM}
\le
\max\{e^{-\theta a}\norm{z}_{X_0},
      e^{(1-\theta)a}\norm{z}_{X_1}\}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $k_\delta$ be the normalized indicator of
$[-\delta/2,\delta/2]$ and put
$u_\delta(t)=z\,k_\delta(t-a)$.  Then
$\int u_\delta(t)\,dt=z$.  For every test function $\phi$ with
$|\phi|\le1$,
\[
\left\|\int e^{-\theta t}\phi(t)u_\delta(t)\,dt\right\|_{X_0}
\le
C_{0,\delta}e^{-\theta a}\norm{z}_{X_0},
\]
and
\[
\left\|\int e^{(1-\theta)t}\phi(t)u_\delta(t)\,dt\right\|_{X_1}
\le
C_{1,\delta}e^{(1-\theta)a}\norm{z}_{X_1},
\]
where
$C_{0,\delta}=\int e^{-\theta r}k_\delta(r)\,dr$ and
$C_{1,\delta}=\int e^{(1-\theta)r}k_\delta(r)\,dr$.  Both constants
tend to $1$ as $\delta\downarrow0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bounded logarithmic-ratio bands]
\label{lem:finite-band}
Let $g$ be a measurable function.  Suppose that for some measurable
$b\ge |g|$ and some real measurable $s$ taking values in a bounded
interval,
\[
y_0:=b e^{-\theta s}\in X_0,\qquad
y_1:=b e^{(1-\theta)s}\in X_1 .
\]
Then
\[
\norm{g}_{\PM}\le \max\{\norm{y_0}_{X_0},\norm{y_1}_{X_1}\}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First suppose that $s$ takes only finitely many values.  Put
$\alpha=g/b$ on $\{b>0\}$ and $\alpha=0$ on $\{b=0\}$, so
$|\alpha|\le1$ and $\alpha b=g$.  Let $k_\delta$ be as in the previous
lemma and set
\[
u_\delta(t,\omega)=\alpha(\omega)b(\omega)k_\delta(t-s(\omega)).
\]
Because $s$ has finite range, $u_\delta$ is locally piecewise constant
as an $X_0\cap X_1$-valued function and has compact $t$-support.  Also
$\int u_\delta=g$.  For every admissible $\phi$,
\[
\left|\int e^{-\theta t}\phi(t)u_\delta(t)\,dt\right|
\le C_{0,\delta} y_0,
\qquad
\left|\int e^{(1-\theta)t}\phi(t)u_\delta(t)\,dt\right|
\le C_{1,\delta} y_1 .
\]
The lattice property gives the corresponding endpoint norm estimates,
and letting $\delta\downarrow0$ proves the finite-valued case.

If $s$ is merely bounded, choose finite-valued measurable $q$ with
$|q-s|\le\eta$.  Applying the finite-valued case with $q$ in place of
$s$ yields
\[
\norm{g}_{\PM}
\le e^\eta\max\{\norm{y_0}_{X_0},\norm{y_1}_{X_1}\}.
\]
Let $\eta\downarrow0$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Norm-One Estimate on the Intersection}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:intersection}
For every $h\in X_0\cap X_1$,
\[
\norm{h}_{\PM}\le \norm{h}_{\Cal}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix $M>\norm{h}_{\Cal}$.  Choose $b\ge |h|$ and measurable real $s$
such that
\[
y_0=b e^{-\theta s}\in X_0,\qquad
y_1=b e^{(1-\theta)s}\in X_1,
\]
and $\norm{y_0}_{X_0},\norm{y_1}_{X_1}\le M$.
For $K>0$ put $h_K=h\one_{\{|s|\le K\}}$.  Lemma
\ref{lem:finite-band} gives
\[
\norm{h_K}_{\PM}\le M.
\]

We claim that $h_K\to h$ in the plus-minus norm.  On the positive tail
$p_K=h\one_{\{s>K\}}$, we have
\[
\norm{p_K}_{X_0}\le\norm{h}_{X_0},\qquad
\norm{p_K}_{X_1}\le e^{-(1-\theta)K}\norm{y_1}_{X_1}\le e^{-(1-\theta)K}M.
\]
Applying Lemma \ref{lem:point} with $a=K/2$ gives
\[
\norm{p_K}_{\PM}
\le
\max\{e^{-\theta K/2}\norm{h}_{X_0},
      e^{-(1-\theta)K/2}M\}\to0.
\]
On the negative tail $n_K=h\one_{\{s<-K\}}$, we similarly have
\[
\norm{n_K}_{X_0}\le e^{-\theta K}M,\qquad
\norm{n_K}_{X_1}\le \norm{h}_{X_1}.
\]
Lemma \ref{lem:point} with $a=-K/2$ gives
\[
\norm{n_K}_{\PM}
\le
\max\{e^{-\theta K/2}M,
      e^{-(1-\theta)K/2}\norm{h}_{X_1}\}\to0.
\]
Thus $h_K\to h$ in $\PM$.  Since all $h_K$ have plus-minus norm at
most $M$, completeness of Cwikel's continuous plus-minus space
\cite[Section 5]{Cwikel2015} gives $\norm{h}_{\PM}\le M$.  Letting
$M\downarrow\norm{h}_{\Cal}$ proves the lemma.
\end{proof}

\section*{The Full Isometry}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:full}
Let $X_0$ and $X_1$ be complex Banach lattices of measurable functions
on the same measure space.  Then, for every $0<\theta<1$,
\[
\left\langle X_0,X_1\right\rangle_{\theta,(0)}
=
\left[X_0,X_1\right]_\theta
\]
isometrically.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Cwikel's Theorem 4.3 gives the contractive embedding
\[
\norm{x}_{\Cplx}\le \norm{x}_{\PM}.
\]
It remains to prove $\norm{x}_{\PM}\le\norm{x}_{\Cplx}$.

Let $x\in\Cplx$ and fix $M>\norm{x}_{\Cplx}$.  By Calderon's
contractive embedding, $\norm{x}_{\Cal}\le\norm{x}_{\Cplx}$.  By
Shestakov's theorem, choose $h_m\in X_0\cap X_1$ such that
$\norm{h_m-x}_{\Cal}\to0$ and, after discarding finitely many terms,
$\norm{h_m}_{\Cal}\le M$ for all $m$.

Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives
\[
\norm{h_m-h_n}_{\PM}\le\norm{h_m-h_n}_{\Cal}\to0,
\]
so $(h_m)$ is Cauchy in $\PM$.  Let $y$ be its plus-minus limit.  The
contractive embeddings
\[
\PM\hookrightarrow \Cplx\hookrightarrow \Cal
\]
imply that $h_m\to y$ in the Calderon product norm.  Since also
$h_m\to x$ in that norm, $y=x$ as a measurable function.  Therefore
$x\in\PM$, and
\[
\norm{x}_{\PM}\le\limsup_m\norm{h_m}_{\PM}
\le\limsup_m\norm{h_m}_{\Cal}\le M.
\]
Letting $M\downarrow\norm{x}_{\Cplx}$ gives the reverse contractive
embedding.  Together with Cwikel's inequality, this proves equality of
the two norms.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof separates the only delicate non-order-continuous issue from
the lattice-complex interpolation theorem.  The weighted
$\ell_\infty$ model shows why the whole upper Calderon product cannot be
used directly: unbounded logarithmic-ratio tails need not vanish in
sup norm.  Shestakov's theorem supplies exactly the missing regularity:
Calderon's complex space is the closure of $X_0\cap X_1$ inside the
Calderon product.  On $X_0\cap X_1$ the tails are controlled by placing
them at midpoint parameters and using their actual endpoint membership.
