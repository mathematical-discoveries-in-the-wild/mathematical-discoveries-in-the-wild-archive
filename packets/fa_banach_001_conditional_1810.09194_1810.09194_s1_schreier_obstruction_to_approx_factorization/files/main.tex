\section*{Status}

This is a conditional reduction packet.  The proved part is a necessary
condition for the approximate factorization asked about by Mathieu and
Tradacete in Remark 6.11 of \cite{MT}.  The packet does not prove that the
needed counterexample operator exists on $L_p$.

\section*{Source Question}

Mathieu and Tradacete ask the following in Remark 6.11 of
\cite{MT}.  The crop below is from page 16 of the source PDF.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In text form, the first question asks whether every
$T\in\SSing(L_p)$ is approximately $(\ell_r,\ell_s)$-factorizable for
some $r<s$.  The stronger version asks whether, for every $\varepsilon>0$,
one may factor $T$ within $\varepsilon$ through
\[
  \oplusell{X}{r(\varepsilon)}
  \longrightarrow
  \oplusell{Y}{s(\varepsilon)}
\]
for finite-dimensional spaces $X_n,Y_n$ and $r(\varepsilon)<s(\varepsilon)$.

\section*{Definitions}

Let $\mathcal S_1$ denote the first Schreier family:
\[
  \mathcal S_1=\{F\subset \mathbb N:\; F\text{ finite and } |F|\leq \min F\}.
\]
An operator $A:E\to F$ is $\mathcal S_1$-strictly singular if, for every
normalized basic sequence $(x_n)$ in $E$ and every $\eta>0$, there are
$G\in\mathcal S_1$ and $0\ne x\in [x_n]_{n\in G}$ such that
\[
  \|Ax\|\leq \eta\|x\|.
\]
Androulakis, Dodos, Sirotkin, and Troitsky prove the standard properties
we use: the class is norm closed and stable under composition with bounded
operators, and every operator $\ell_r\to\ell_s$ belongs to
$\mathcal S_1(\ell_r,\ell_s)$ when $1<r<s<\infty$ \cite{ADST}.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The source factorization has a hidden rank restriction.  The middle space
changes from an $\ell_r$ geometry to an $\ell_s$ geometry with $r<s$.  On
long disjoint blocks, normalized sums in the domain have size $N^{1/r}$,
while their images in the target have size at most on the order of
$N^{1/s}$.  Since $1/s<1/r$, long Schreier-admissible averages force the
middle map to be $\mathcal S_1$-strictly singular.  Composition and norm
closure then pass this property to every operator obtained by the proposed
approximate factorization.

This explains why finite strict singularity is the wrong obstruction:
arbitrary maps $\ell_r\to\ell_s$ may fail to be finitely strictly singular,
but they still have the first Schreier small-vector property.

\section*{Main Lemma}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:fdd-middle}
Let $1<r<s<\infty$, let $(X_n)$ and $(Y_n)$ be finite-dimensional Banach
spaces, and set
\[
  E=\oplusell{X}{r},\qquad F=\oplusell{Y}{s}.
\]
Then every bounded operator $U:E\to F$ is $\mathcal S_1$-strictly singular.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the finite-dimensional-decomposition version of the standard
gliding-hump proof for operators $\ell_r\to\ell_s$; compare
\cite[Example 2.4]{ADST}.  Let $(x_k)$ be a normalized basic sequence in
$E$ and fix $\eta>0$.  Since $E$ is reflexive and has a finite-dimensional
decomposition, the Bessaga-Pelczynski selection principle and the usual
sliding-hump reduction allow us, after passing to a subsequence and, if
necessary, to a seminormalized difference sequence, to prove the estimate for
a normalized block sequence of the FDD.  Passing to such a block sequence
preserves Schreier admissibility, since the original indices only increase.

Thus suppose $(x_k)$ is a normalized block sequence in $E$.  If
$\inf_k\|Ux_k\|=0$, a singleton Schreier set gives the conclusion.  Otherwise
pass to a subsequence so that $(Ux_k)$ is seminormalized.  A second
gliding-hump argument in the finite-dimensional decomposition of $F$ gives a
block sequence $(y_k)$ in $F$ and a summable positive sequence $(\delta_k)$
such that
\[
  \|Ux_k-y_k\|\leq \delta_k,\qquad \sup_k\|y_k\|=:M<\infty .
\]
For $N\in\mathbb N$ put
\[
  z_N=\sum_{k=N+1}^{2N} x_k .
\]
The set $\{N+1,\ldots,2N\}$ belongs to $\mathcal S_1$.  Since $(x_k)$ is a
normalized block sequence in an $\ell_r$-sum,
\[
  \|z_N\|=N^{1/r}.
\]
Also, using that $(y_k)$ is a block sequence in an $\ell_s$-sum,
\[
  \|Uz_N\|
  \leq \left\|\sum_{k=N+1}^{2N} y_k\right\|
       +\sum_{k=N+1}^{2N}\delta_k
  \leq M N^{1/s}+\sum_{k=N+1}^{2N}\delta_k .
\]
Therefore
\[
  \frac{\|Uz_N\|}{\|z_N\|}
  \leq M N^{1/s-1/r}
      +N^{-1/r}\sum_{k=N+1}^{2N}\delta_k
  \longrightarrow 0.
\]
Choose $N$ large enough that this ratio is at most $\eta$.  This gives the
required Schreier vector.
\end{proof}

\section*{Necessary Condition}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:necessary}
Let $X$ be a Banach space.  Suppose $T\in\mathcal L(X)$ is a norm limit of
operators of the form
\[
  X \xrightarrow{A_\varepsilon}
  \oplusell{X^\varepsilon}{r_\varepsilon}
  \xrightarrow{U_\varepsilon}
  \oplusell{Y^\varepsilon}{s_\varepsilon}
  \xrightarrow{B_\varepsilon}
  X,
\]
where $1<r_\varepsilon<s_\varepsilon<\infty$ and all
$X^\varepsilon_n,Y^\varepsilon_n$ are finite-dimensional.  Then
$T\in\mathcal S_1(X)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma \ref{lem:fdd-middle}, each middle operator $U_\varepsilon$ is
$\mathcal S_1$-strictly singular.  The ideal property of
$\mathcal S_1$-strict singularity implies that
$B_\varepsilon U_\varepsilon A_\varepsilon$ is $\mathcal S_1$-strictly
singular on $X$.  Since $\mathcal S_1(X)$ is norm closed
\cite[Proposition 2.2]{ADST}, the norm limit $T$ is also
$\mathcal S_1$-strictly singular.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:counterexample-reduction}
Fix $1<p<\infty$, $p\ne2$.  If there exists an operator
\[
  T\in \SSing(L_p)\setminus \mathcal S_1(L_p),
\]
then $T$ is not approximately factorizable in the sense of Remark 6.11 of
\cite{MT}, in the reflexive exponent range $1<r<s<\infty$, even allowing the
finite-dimensional-sum formulation.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The proposed factorization would put $T$ in $\mathcal S_1(L_p)$ by Theorem
\ref{thm:necessary}, contradicting the choice of $T$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Conditional Dependencies}

The proof above is complete as a necessary-condition theorem.  To turn the
packet into a negative answer to the source question, one still needs the
following unproved dependency:
\[
  \text{construct } T\in\SSing(L_p)\setminus\mathcal S_1(L_p).
\]
No such operator was proved in this loop.  The packet should therefore remain
classified as a conditional reduction.

The exponent range is also part of the scope: the proof uses reflexivity of
the $\ell_r$-sum and the finite-dimensional-decomposition gliding-hump
reduction for $1<r<s<\infty$.  Endpoint variants such as $r=1$ or
$s=\infty$, if intended, require separate verification.

\section*{Why Finite Strict Singularity Does Not Work}

One might try to replace $\mathcal S_1$ by finite strict singularity.  That
would be too strong.  Androulakis-Dodos-Sirotkin-Troitsky record that, for
$1<r<s<\infty$,
\[
  \FSS(\ell_r,\ell_s)\ne \SSing(\ell_r,\ell_s)=\mathcal L(\ell_r,\ell_s)
\]
\cite[Example 2.5]{ADST}.  Thus the source definition, whose middle leg is an
arbitrary bounded map $\ell_r\to\ell_s$, already permits non-FSS behavior in
the middle.  The correct necessary obstruction supplied here is the weaker
Schreier-rank condition $\mathcal S_1$.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review the two standard reductions used in Lemma \ref{lem:fdd-middle}: the
Bessaga-Pelczynski passage from a basic sequence in an FDD space to a block or
difference block sequence, and the gliding-hump approximation of its image by
a block sequence in the target FDD.  If those are accepted, Theorem
\ref{thm:necessary} follows directly from the closed ideal property of
$\mathcal S_1$-strict singularity.
