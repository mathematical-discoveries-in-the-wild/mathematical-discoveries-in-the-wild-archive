\section*{Source question}

The source paper is Castillo--Defant--Garcia--Perez-Garcia--Suarez,
\emph{Local complementation and the extension of bilinear mappings},
arXiv:1106.5089 \cite{CDGPS}.  In the remarks preceding the
\(c_0\)-conjecture, the authors observe that if a subspace
\(Y\subset c_0\) admits extension of bilinear forms to \(c_0\), then
\(Y\) is Hilbert-Schmidt: every operator \(Y\to \ell_2\) is
\(2\)-summing.  For a block subspace \(Y=c_0(F_n)\), this gives a
uniform constant \(C\) such that, for every \(n\),
\[
        \pi_2(T)\le C\|T\|,\qquad
        T:F_n\longrightarrow \ell_2^n .
\]
They then write that they do not know whether this condition implies that
the finite-dimensional spaces \(F_n\) are uniformly complemented in
finite-dimensional \(\ell_\infty\) spaces.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The \(2\)-summing condition only tests Hilbert-valued operators.  For
\(\ell_1^n\), such operators are controlled uniformly by Grothendieck's
inequality: the Gram matrix of the images of the coordinate vectors is a
positive semidefinite matrix with bounded diagonal, and Grothendieck
converts the Hilbert-vector estimate into the weak \(\ell_2\) norm
appearing in the definition of \(\pi_2\).

Uniform complemented copies inside \(\ell_\infty\), however, would force
uniform finite-dimensional \(L_\infty\) structure.  Equivalently, by
injectivity of \(\ell_\infty^m\), it would give uniformly bounded
absolute projection constants for \(\ell_1^n\).  Those constants grow
like \(\sqrt n\).  Thus the Hilbert-Schmidt test and the
\(L_\infty\)-complementability conclusion separate.

\section*{The counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(F_n=\ell_1^n\).  Then there is a universal constant \(C\) such
that
\[
        \pi_2(T)\le C\|T\|
        \quad\text{for every }n\text{ and every }T:\ell_1^n\to\ell_2^n.
\]
Nevertheless the spaces \(\ell_1^n\) are not uniformly complemented in
finite-dimensional \(\ell_\infty\) spaces.  Hence the finite-dimensional
Hilbert-Schmidt condition in the source remarks does not imply uniform
\(\ell_\infty\)-complementability of the blocks.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
It is enough to prove the \(2\)-summing estimate for operators
\(T:\ell_1^n\to H\), where \(H\) is an arbitrary Hilbert space.  Normalize
\(\|T\|\le 1\), and write \(h_i=T e_i\), so that \(\|h_i\|\le 1\).

We write the scalar computation in the real case.  The complex case is
identical after replacing \(x_j^r\) by \(\overline{x_j^r}\) in the Gram
matrix and signs by unimodular scalars.  Let
\(x^1,\ldots,x^N\in \ell_1^n\).  Put
\[
        a_{ij}=\sum_{r=1}^N x^r_i x^r_j .
\]
The matrix \(A=(a_{ij})\) is positive semidefinite.  By Grothendieck's
inequality in its positive semidefinite form, there is a universal
constant \(K_G\) such that, for all Hilbert vectors \(u_i\) of norm at
most one,
\[
        \sum_{i,j=1}^n a_{ij}\langle u_i,u_j\rangle
        \le
        K_G\sup_{\varepsilon_i=\pm1}
        \sum_{i,j=1}^n a_{ij}\varepsilon_i\varepsilon_j .
\]
Applying this to \(u_i=h_i\) gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
        \sum_{r=1}^N \|Tx^r\|^2
        &= \sum_{i,j=1}^n a_{ij}\langle h_i,h_j\rangle  \\
        &\le
        K_G\sup_{\varepsilon_i=\pm1}
        \sum_{r=1}^N\left|\sum_{i=1}^n \varepsilon_i x_i^r\right|^2  \\
        &\le
        K_G\sup_{\varphi\in B_{(\ell_1^n)^*}}
        \sum_{r=1}^N |\varphi(x^r)|^2 .
\end{aligned}
\]
This is precisely the defining inequality for
\(\pi_2(T)\le \sqrt{K_G}\).  Since \(\ell_2^n\) is a Hilbert space, the
displayed estimate proves the required uniform Hilbert-Schmidt condition.

It remains to show that the blocks are not uniformly complemented in
finite-dimensional \(\ell_\infty\) spaces.  Suppose, to the contrary,
that there were constants \(D,K\), embeddings
\[
        J_n:\ell_1^n\longrightarrow \ell_\infty^{m(n)}
\]
with \(\|J_n\|\|J_n^{-1}\|\le D\), and projections
\[
        P_n:\ell_\infty^{m(n)}\longrightarrow J_n(\ell_1^n)
\]
with \(\|P_n\|\le K\).  Because finite-dimensional
\(\ell_\infty^{m(n)}\) is injective, any isometric copy of \(\ell_1^n\)
inside any Banach space would then be \(DK\)-complemented: extend the
map \(J_n\) from that copy into \(\ell_\infty^{m(n)}\), compose with
\(P_n\), and then with \(J_n^{-1}\).

Thus the absolute projection constants of \(\ell_1^n\) would be
uniformly bounded.  This contradicts the classical Grunbaum--Rutovitz
estimate
\[
        \lambda(\ell_1^n)\asymp \sqrt n,
\]
where \(\lambda(E)\) denotes the absolute projection constant of the
finite-dimensional space \(E\).  Therefore no such uniform complemented
\(\ell_\infty\)-realization exists.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet answers only the finite-dimensional implication asked in the
remarks before the \(c_0\)-conjecture.  It does not construct a subspace
\(Y\subset c_0\) for which all bilinear forms extend to \(c_0\), and it
does not disprove the \(c_0\)-conjecture itself.  It shows that the
Hilbert-Schmidt necessary condition isolated in the source is too weak,
by itself, to force the block complementability conclusion.

\section*{Verification and novelty notes}

The proof uses only two standard external facts: Grothendieck's
inequality and the unbounded absolute projection constants of
\(\ell_1^n\).  A bounded novelty check on 2026-06-29 searched the run
indexes for arXiv:1106.5089 and the keywords ``Hilbert-Schmidt'',
``\(\pi_2\)'', ``uniformly complemented'', and ``\(\ell_1^n\)''; it also
read the two previous local attempts for this paper.  Web searches for
the exact source phrase and close variants did not find an explicit
later answer.  Since the counterexample is a short combination of
classical facts, novelty confidence should be treated as modest: this may
be a folklore observation even if no exact citation was found in the
bounded search.

Recommended human-review focus: check that the source's phrase
``uniformly complemented in \(\ell_\infty^m\)'' is intended in the usual
uniform isomorphic-complemented sense.  Under that standard reading, the
projection-constant argument above is decisive.
