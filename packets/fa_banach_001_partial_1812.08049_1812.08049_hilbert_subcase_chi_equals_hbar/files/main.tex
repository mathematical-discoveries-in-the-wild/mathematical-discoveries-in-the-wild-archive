\paragraph{Status.}
This is a \texttt{partial\_result\_likely\_valid} for a question in
Yaroslavtsev's paper \cite{Yaroslavtsev2018}.  It proves the equality
\(\chi_{p,X}=\hbar_{p,X}\) when \(X\) is a real Hilbert space.  It does not
settle the general UMD Banach-space problem.

\section*{Source Question}

Yaroslavtsev asks whether the equality \(\chi_{p,X}=\hbar_{p,X}\), known for
\(X=\mathbb R\), holds for a general UMD Banach space \(X\).  The paper points
out that a positive answer would imply a linear comparison between the UMD
constant \(\beta_{p,X}\) and the Hilbert-transform norm \(\hbar_{p,X}\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The only geometric input needed for Hilbert spaces is that their norm can be
tested by Gaussian scalarization:
\[
  \mathbb E_\gamma |\langle \gamma,v\rangle|^p = a_p\|v\|^p.
\]
Applying the scalar Hilbert transform after this random scalar projection
shows that the \(H\)-valued Hilbert-transform norm is exactly the scalar one.
On the other hand, both \(\hbar_{p,H}\) and \(\chi_{p,H}\) are at least their
scalar constants because a one-dimensional Hilbert line is an isometric
subspace.  The source paper gives the remaining upper bound
\(\chi_{p,H}\leq \hbar_{p,H}\), so all constants collapse to the scalar value.

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(H\) be a nonzero real Hilbert space and let \(1<p<\infty\).  Then
\[
  \chi_{p,H}=\hbar_{p,H}=\hbar_{p,\mathbb R}.
\]
In particular, the equality problem from \cite{Yaroslavtsev2018} has an
affirmative answer for Hilbert-valued martingales.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write \(c_p=\hbar_{p,\mathbb R}\).  We first prove
\(\hbar_{p,H}=c_p\).

The lower bound \(\hbar_{p,H}\geq c_p\) follows by restricting to functions
whose values lie in a fixed unit line \(\mathbb R e\subset H\).  For the upper
bound it is enough, by density of finite-rank simple functions, to consider
finite-dimensional \(H\).  Let \(e_1,\ldots,e_d\) be an orthonormal basis of
\(H\) and write
\[
  f(t)=\sum_{j=1}^d f_j(t)e_j.
\]
Let \(\gamma_1,\ldots,\gamma_d\) be independent standard real Gaussian
variables, independent of \(t\), and let
\[
  a_p=\mathbb E|\gamma_1|^p.
\]
For every \(v=\sum_j v_j e_j\in H\),
\[
  \mathbb E_\gamma \left|\sum_{j=1}^d \gamma_j v_j\right|^p
  =a_p\|v\|_H^p.
\]
Using Fubini and the scalar Hilbert-transform estimate,
\begin{align*}
  a_p\|\mathcal H_H f\|_{L^p(\mathbb R;H)}^p
  &= \mathbb E_\gamma
     \left\|\mathcal H_{\mathbb R}\left(\sum_{j=1}^d\gamma_j f_j\right)
     \right\|_{L^p(\mathbb R)}^p  \\
  &\leq c_p^p\,\mathbb E_\gamma
     \left\|\sum_{j=1}^d\gamma_j f_j\right\|_{L^p(\mathbb R)}^p \\
  &= a_p c_p^p\|f\|_{L^p(\mathbb R;H)}^p .
\end{align*}
Thus \(\hbar_{p,H}\leq c_p\), and hence \(\hbar_{p,H}=c_p\).

It remains to identify \(\chi_{p,H}\).  Yaroslavtsev proves
\(\chi_{p,X}\leq \hbar_{p,X}\) for every UMD Banach space \(X\)
\cite[Corollary 3.5]{Yaroslavtsev2018}; therefore
\[
  \chi_{p,H}\leq \hbar_{p,H}=c_p.
\]
For the reverse inequality, again restrict the defining inequality for
\(\chi_{p,H}\) to integrands of the form \(\Phi=\phi e\), where \(e\in H\) is
a unit vector and \(\phi\) is real-valued.  Any admissible constant for
\(\chi_{p,H}\) is then admissible for the scalar constant \(\chi_{p,\mathbb R}\).
The source paper records that \(\chi_{p,\mathbb R}=\hbar_{p,\mathbb R}\)
\cite[p.~3 and Remark 3.6]{Yaroslavtsev2018}.  Hence
\[
  \chi_{p,H}\geq \chi_{p,\mathbb R}=\hbar_{p,\mathbb R}=c_p.
\]
Combining the two inequalities gives \(\chi_{p,H}=c_p=\hbar_{p,H}\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Novelty Notes}

The proof is purely analytic; no computational check is needed.  The packet
uses only the source paper's inequalities and the standard Gaussian
scalarization identity for Hilbert spaces.

Local cheap indexes were searched for arXiv:1812.08049, strongly orthogonal
martingales, \(\chi_{p,X}\), \(\hbar_{p,X}\), Hilbert transform, and UMD
keywords.  The only close local item is an earlier attempt on arXiv:1805.03948
about broader Hilbert-transform and Burkholder-function constant problems; it
does not duplicate this Hilbert-space \(\chi=\hbar\) subcase.  Web searches for
exact \(\chi_{p,X}\)/\(\hbar_{p,X}\) phrases found the source paper but no
separate later answer.  The Hilbert-space argument is short and may be
folklore-standard, so novelty confidence is modest.

\section*{Limitations}

This result does not cover non-Hilbert UMD spaces.  In particular it does not
prove the source paper's general equality problem, nor the linear dependence
between \(\beta_{p,X}\) and \(\hbar_{p,X}\) for arbitrary UMD spaces.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Check the finite-rank reduction in the proof of \(\hbar_{p,H}= \hbar_{p,\mathbb
R}\) and the scalar-line lower bound for \(\chi_{p,H}\).  These are the only
nontrivial steps; the remaining inequalities are cited directly from the
source paper.
