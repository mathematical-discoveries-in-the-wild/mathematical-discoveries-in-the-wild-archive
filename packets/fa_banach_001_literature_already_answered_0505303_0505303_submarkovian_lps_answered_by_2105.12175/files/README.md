# Literature status: sub-Markovian Littlewood-Paley-Stein question

Status: `literature_already_answered`.

Original source: Teresa Martinez, Jose L. Torrea, and Quanhua Xu, "Vector-valued Littlewood-Paley-Stein theory for semigroups", arXiv:math/0505303; Adv. Math. 203 (2006), 430--475.

Supporting answer source: Quanhua Xu, "Holomorphic functional calculus and vector-valued Littlewood-Paley-Stein theory for semigroups", arXiv:2105.12175.

## Original question

After Theorems 3.1 and 3.2 (`lusin semigroups` and `lusin type semigroups`) the original paper says that all semigroups considered there are Markovian, `T_t 1 = 1`, and asks whether those two theorems still hold for symmetric sub-Markovian semigroups, i.e. the same hypotheses except Markovianity.

Those two theorems characterize martingale cotype/type by the corresponding vector-valued Littlewood-Paley-Stein inequalities for the Poisson semigroup subordinated to every symmetric diffusion semigroup.

## Answer identification

Xu's later paper explicitly cites the original paper as `LP1`, identifies the submarkovian/Cowling setting as an open problem for the results of `LP1`/`LP2`, and states that its objective is to resolve those problems. Its Theorem 1.1 (`Poisson ML`) proves the cotype and type estimates for the subordinated Poisson semigroup of any strongly continuous semigroup of regular operators on a fixed `L_p(Omega)`. The paper then observes that the markovianity or submarkovianity assumptions are not needed, and neither is symmetry.

Since every symmetric sub-Markovian semigroup is a semigroup of positive contractions, hence of regular contractions on each `L_p`, Theorem 1.1 gives exactly the positive directions asked for in the original sub-Markovian extension of Theorems 3.1 and 3.2. The converse directions remain automatic for a "for every symmetric sub-Markovian semigroup" formulation because the old Markovian symmetric diffusion semigroups are a subclass.

## Scope limitations

This packet records only the literature answer to the sub-Markovian extension of the Poisson-subordinate Theorems 3.1 and 3.2. It does not claim to settle the original paper's separate Problem 1 on weak type `(1,1)`, nor all non-subordinated heat-semigroup variants outside the later paper's hypotheses.
