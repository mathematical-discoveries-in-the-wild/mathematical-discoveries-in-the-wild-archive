{\normalsize Partial packet for arXiv:1612.01364}

\section*{Source and target}

The source is Antonin Dalet and Gilles Lancien,
\emph{Some properties of coarse Lipschitz maps between Banach spaces},
arXiv:1612.01364 \cite{DaletLancien}.  In Section 4 the source introduces
the net extension property (NEP), proves in Proposition 4.4 that NEP implies
the Lipschitz representation property (LRP), and proves in Proposition 4.5
that LRP implies completeness of the quotient normed space
\((CL(X,Y),\|\cdot\|_{CL})\).  The section then leaves open whether this
space is always complete.

\noindent
The target in this packet is the finite-dimensional-domain subcase of the
open completeness question.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be a finite-dimensional Banach space and let \(Y\) be any Banach
space.  Then \((X,Y)\) has the net extension property of Definition 4.1 in
\cite{DaletLancien}.  Consequently, by Propositions 4.4 and 4.5 of
\cite{DaletLancien}, \((CL(X,Y),\|\cdot\|_{CL})\) is a Banach space.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
Thus any counterexample to the source's general conjecture must have
infinite-dimensional domain.  The argument does not address the
infinite-dimensional case.  The source's separate question whether LRP/ANEP
implies NEP is handled in a companion full packet.
\end{remark}

\section*{Proof}

Fix a finite-dimensional Banach space \(X\).  Let \(\M\subset X\) be a
maximal \(1\)-separated set.  Then \(\M\) is a \(1\)-net in \(X\).  Since
\(X\) is finite-dimensional, \(\M\) has bounded local multiplicity: for every
\(R>0\) there is an integer \(N_R\) such that
\[
  \#(\M\cap B(x,R))\le N_R \qquad (x\in X).
\]
Indeed, the balls \(B(m,1/2)\), \(m\in \M\cap B(x,R)\), are disjoint and are
contained in \(B(x,R+1/2)\), so comparison with Lebesgue measure on \(X\)
gives a bound independent of \(x\).

It remains to show that every Lipschitz map \(h:\M\to Y\) admits a
Lipschitz extension to \(X\).  Write \(L=\Lip(h)\).  For \(m\in \M\) define
\[
  \eta_m(x)=(2-\|x-m\|)_+,\qquad
  S(x)=\sum_{m\in \M}\eta_m(x),\qquad
  \lambda_m(x)=\frac{\eta_m(x)}{S(x)}.
\]
The sums are finite because of local multiplicity.  Since \(\M\) is a
\(1\)-net, \(S(x)\ge 1\) for all \(x\).  Also \(S(x)\le 2N_2\).  The functions
\(\lambda_m\) form a Lipschitz partition of unity, supported in \(B(m,2)\).
More precisely, there is a constant \(A_X<\infty\), depending only on the
packing numbers of \(\M\), such that for all \(x,y\in X\),
\[
  \sum_{m\in \M} |\lambda_m(x)-\lambda_m(y)|
  \le A_X\|x-y\| \quad\text{whenever } \|x-y\|\le 1.      \tag{1}
\]
To see this, note that for \(\|x-y\|\le 1\) all indices appearing in either
partition lie in \(B(x,3)\), hence there are at most \(N_3\) of them.  The
maps \(\eta_m\) are \(1\)-Lipschitz, and \(S\) is Lipschitz on such pairs
with constant controlled by \(N_3\), while \(S\ge 1\).

Define the partition extension
\[
  H_0(x)=\sum_{m\in \M}\lambda_m(x)h(m).
\]
We first check that \(H_0\) is Lipschitz.  Suppose \(\|x-y\|\le 1\), and
choose \(p\in \M\) with \(\|x-p\|\le 1\).  Since
\(\sum_m(\lambda_m(x)-\lambda_m(y))=0\), we may subtract \(h(p)\) and get
\[
  H_0(x)-H_0(y)
  = \sum_m(\lambda_m(x)-\lambda_m(y))(h(m)-h(p)).
\]
For every index in this finite sum, \(m\) is within distance \(4\) of \(p\).
Thus, using (1),
\[
  \|H_0(x)-H_0(y)\| \le 4A_XL\|x-y\|.
\]
For \(\|x-y\|>1\), choose \(p,q\in \M\) with \(\|x-p\|\le 1\) and
\(\|y-q\|\le 1\).  The support condition gives
\(\|H_0(x)-h(p)\|\le 3L\) and \(\|H_0(y)-h(q)\|\le 3L\), while
\(\|h(p)-h(q)\|\le L(\|x-y\|+2)\).  Hence
\[
  \|H_0(x)-H_0(y)\|\le L(\|x-y\|+8)\le 9L\|x-y\|.
\]
So \(H_0\) is Lipschitz, with constant bounded by a constant depending only
on \(X\) times \(L\).

The map \(H_0\) need not agree with \(h\) on \(\M\), but the error is
uniformly bounded.  If \(m\in \M\), then \(H_0(m)\) is a convex combination
of values \(h(n)\) with \(\|n-m\|<2\).  Hence
\[
  e(m):=h(m)-H_0(m) \quad\text{satisfies}\quad \|e(m)\|\le 2L.
\]
Correct this error with disjoint bumps.  Put
\[
  \beta_m(x)=(1-2\|x-m\|)_+,\qquad
  E(x)=\sum_{m\in \M}\beta_m(x)e(m).
\]
The supports of the positive parts of the functions \(\beta_m\) are
disjoint, since \(\M\) is \(1\)-separated.  On each support \(E\) is
\(4L\)-Lipschitz.  If two points lie in different supports, the line segment
between them leaves the first support before entering the second, and the
corresponding bump value is zero at the exit and entry points; the same
\(4L\) bound follows by applying the one-support estimate to the two
subsegments.  Thus \(E\) is globally Lipschitz.

Finally set \(H=H_0+E\).  Then \(H:X\to Y\) is Lipschitz and, for every
\(m\in \M\),
\[
  H(m)=H_0(m)+e(m)=h(m),
\]
because \(\beta_m(m)=1\) and \(\beta_n(m)=0\) for \(n\ne m\).  Therefore
\((\M,X,Y)\) has the Lipschitz extension property.  Since \(\M\) is a net in
\(X\), the pair \((X,Y)\) has NEP.  Proposition 4.4 of
\cite{DaletLancien} gives LRP, and Proposition 4.5 then gives completeness of
\((CL(X,Y),\|\cdot\|_{CL})\).

\section*{Verification and limitations}

The exact arXiv id and the core phrases \texttt{coarse Lipschitz},
\texttt{CL(X,Y) completeness}, \texttt{net extension property}, and
\texttt{LRP} were checked in the run indexes before packaging.  No previous
packet for arXiv:1612.01364 was found.  Nearby coarse-Lipschitz entries in
the run concern different permanence or renorming questions and do not cover
this finite-dimensional NEP argument.

A related prior run artifact,
\texttt{1506.05391\_finite\_dimensional\_net\_extensions}, records the same
finite-dimensional net-extension mechanism for Naor's Rosendal-net question,
using later literature.  Thus this packet should not be read as claiming
novelty of the finite-dimensional extension lemma itself.  The target-level
contribution here is the application of NEP to Dalet--Lancien's completeness
problem through their Propositions 4.4 and 4.5.

This packet is a partial result only.  It does not construct the conjectured
counterexample and it does not settle completeness for infinite-dimensional
domains.  The adjacent LRP/ANEP-versus-NEP converse question is treated in
the companion full packet for arXiv:1612.01364.

\section*{Human-review recommendation}

Likely valid partial result.  Review should check the partition-of-unity
extension estimate, especially the disjoint-bump correction step.  The
deduction from NEP to completeness is exactly the source's
Proposition 4.4 followed by Proposition 4.5.
