\section*{Status}

This packet gives a counterexample to removing the \(K\)-convexity hypothesis
from the reverse one-variable Steinhaus decoupling estimate in
Carando--Marceca--Sevilla-Peris \cite{CMS2020}. It shows that the estimate
fails for the non-\(K\)-convex space \(c_0\).

\section*{Source Question}

The source paper proves that, if \(X\) is \(K\)-convex and \(p>1\), then for
every \(m\)-homogeneous tetrahedral polynomial \(P:\C^n\to X\), with
associated symmetric \(m\)-linear map \(M\),
\[
\sqrt m\big(\E\|M(w',w,\ldots,w)\|^p\big)^{1/p}
\le C_{p,X}\big(\E\|P(w)\|^p\big)^{1/p},
\]
where \(w,w'\) are independent Steinhaus vectors. The authors then ask whether
the \(K\)-convexity assumption is necessary.

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
For \(X=c_0\), and for every \(p\ge 1\), there is no constant \(C_p\) such
that
\[
\sqrt m\big(\E\|M(w',w,\ldots,w)\|_{c_0}^p\big)^{1/p}
\le C_p\big(\E\|P(w)\|_{c_0}^p\big)^{1/p}
\]
for all \(m,n\) and all \(m\)-homogeneous tetrahedral polynomials
\(P:\C^n\to c_0\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(m\) and \(n\). Let \(\Pcal_m[n]\) denote the family of \(m\)-element
subsets of \([n]=\{1,\ldots,n\}\). We identify the finite-dimensional space
\(\ell_\infty(\Pcal_m[n])\) isometrically with a coordinate subspace of
\(c_0\). Let \((e_A)_{A\in\Pcal_m[n]}\) be its canonical basis and define
\[
  P(z)=\sum_{A\in\Pcal_m[n]} e_A z_A,
  \qquad z_A=\prod_{j\in A} z_j .
\]
For every Steinhaus vector \(w\in\T^n\),
\[
  \|P(w)\|_\infty=\sup_{A\in\Pcal_m[n]} |w_A|=1.
\]
Hence \((\E\|P(w)\|_\infty^p)^{1/p}=1\).

Let \(M\) be the symmetric \(m\)-linear map associated to \(P\). For a fixed
coordinate \(A\), symmetrization gives
\[
  M_A(z^{(1)},\ldots,z^{(m)})
  =\frac1{m!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_m}
      \prod_{r=1}^m z^{(r)}_{a_{\sigma(r)}},
  \qquad A=\{a_1,\ldots,a_m\}.
\]
Substituting \((z^{(1)},z^{(2)},\ldots,z^{(m)})=(w',w,\ldots,w)\) yields
\[
  M_A(w',w,\ldots,w)
  =\frac1m\sum_{j\in A} w'_j\prod_{\substack{i\in A\\ i\ne j}}w_i.
\]
Since \(|w_i|=1\), if \(u_j=w'_j/w_j\), then
\[
  |M_A(w',w,\ldots,w)|
  =\frac1m\left|\sum_{j\in A}u_j\right|.
\]
The random variables \(u_j\) are independent Steinhaus variables.

Let
\[
  \Gamma=\{e^{i\theta}:|\theta|\le \pi/3\}.
\]
Then \(\mathbb P(u_j\in\Gamma)=1/3\), and if \(u_j\in\Gamma\) then
\(\operatorname{Re}u_j\ge 1/2\). Choose \(n=n(m)\) large enough that, with
probability at least \(1/2\), at least \(m\) of the variables
\(u_1,\ldots,u_n\) lie in \(\Gamma\). This is possible because the number of
such variables has binomial distribution \(\mathrm{Bin}(n,1/3)\), whose mean
tends to infinity with \(n\).

On this event, choose an \(m\)-set \(A\) among those good indices. Then
\[
  \left|\sum_{j\in A}u_j\right|
  \ge \operatorname{Re}\sum_{j\in A}u_j
  \ge \frac m2,
\]
and therefore \(\|M(w',w,\ldots,w)\|_\infty\ge 1/2\). Consequently
\[
  \big(\E\|M(w',w,\ldots,w)\|_\infty^p\big)^{1/p}
  \ge \frac12\,2^{-1/p}.
\]

If the displayed reverse one-variable decoupling estimate held in \(c_0\)
with a constant \(C_p\) independent of \(m\) and \(n\), applying it to these
polynomials would give
\[
  2^{-1-1/p}\sqrt m \le C_p
\]
for every \(m\), a contradiction. Thus no such estimate can hold for \(c_0\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope}

The counterexample shows that the \(K\)-convexity assumption cannot simply be
discarded. It does not decide the finer finite-cotype weakening discussed in
the source remark, since \(c_0\) has trivial cotype.

\section*{Verification Notes}

No computer verification is needed. The proof has two checks: the symmetric
multilinear coordinate formula and the elementary binomial arc-counting
argument for independent Steinhaus ratios \(w'_j/w_j\).

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a short counterexample packet. The only substantive mathematical
point is that the supremum over all \(m\)-sets \(A\) lets a large number of
variables expose an almost-aligned Steinhaus block, while the original
polynomial has constant supremum norm \(1\).
