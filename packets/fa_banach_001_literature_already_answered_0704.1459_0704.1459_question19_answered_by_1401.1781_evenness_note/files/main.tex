\section*{Status}

This packet is classified as \emph{literature already answered}.  The exact
Ferenczi--Galego Question~19 from \cite{FG}, asking whether there exists a
Banach space with exactly $\omega$ complex structures, is answered positively
by Cuellar-Carrera \cite[Corollary~9]{CC}.  The remaining material is preserved
as an adjacent strengthening: the countably-many-complex-structure examples of
Cuellar-Carrera may be chosen to be even in the sense of Ferenczi--Galego.

\section*{Source Question}

Ferenczi--Galego ask on page 16 of \cite{FG}:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

\noindent In text form: Does there exist a Banach space with exactly
$\omega$ complex structures?

\section*{Supporting Literature Evidence}

Cuellar-Carrera proves that the spaces $\X_\gamma(\C)$ have exactly
$\omega$ complex structures for every limit ordinal
$\omega^2 \leq \gamma < \omega_1$; the same page also proves that the
real hyperplanes of $\X_{\omega_1}(\C)$ have no complex structure, using
the Ferenczi--Galego lifting criterion.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\textwidth]{figures/supporting_cuellar_crop.png}

\section*{Definitions}

A complex structure on a real Banach space $X$ is a bounded real-linear
operator $J\colon X\to X$ such that $J^2=-\Id_X$, considered up to
real-linear conjugacy.  Following \cite{FG}, an infinite-dimensional
real Banach space is \emph{even} if it admits a complex structure and
its hyperplanes do not admit a complex structure.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Cuellar-Carrera's construction is rigid enough that every real-linear
operator on the large transfinite space has a diagonal part modulo
strictly singular operators.  If an operator squares to $-\Id$ modulo
strictly singular operators, the diagonal part itself squares exactly to
$-\Id$.  This is precisely the lifting property that Ferenczi--Galego
identify with absence of complex structures on hyperplanes.

The proof below uses the same extension trick as Cuellar-Carrera's
Corollary~9: extend an operator on the separable initial interval
$\X_\gamma(\C)$ to $\X_{\omega_1}(\C)$ by putting the standard
multiplication by $i$ on the complementary interval.  The diagonal
lifting on the large space then restricts back to a diagonal lifting on
$\X_\gamma(\C)$.

\begin{theorem}
Let $\gamma$ be a limit ordinal with $\omega^2\leq \gamma<\omega_1$.
Let $\X_\gamma(\C)$ be Cuellar-Carrera's space, viewed as a real Banach
space.  Then $\X_\gamma(\C)$ is even and has exactly $\omega$ complex
structures up to isomorphism.  In particular, there is a separable even
real Banach space with exactly countably infinitely many complex
structures.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Cuellar-Carrera's Corollary~9 \cite{CC} gives exactly $\omega$ complex
structures on $\X_\gamma(\C)$ for every limit ordinal
$\omega^2\leq\gamma<\omega_1$.  Since $\gamma$ is countable, this space
is separable.

It remains to prove evenness.  The space already has a complex structure,
namely the original scalar multiplication by $i$.  By
Ferenczi--Galego's lifting criterion \cite[Proposition~13]{FG}, it is
enough to show that the ideal of real-linear strictly singular
operators on $\X_\gamma(\C)$ has the lifting property: whenever
$T\colon \X_\gamma(\C)\to\X_\gamma(\C)$ is real-linear and
$T^2+\Id$ is strictly singular, there exists a strictly singular
operator $S$ such that $(T-S)^2=-\Id$.

Put $I=[0,\gamma)$ and regard $\X_\gamma(\C)=\X_I(\C)$ as the
1-complemented initial-interval subspace of $\X_{\omega_1}(\C)$, with
coordinate projections $P_I$ and $P^I=\Id-P_I$.  Given such a $T$, define
a real-linear operator on $\X_{\omega_1}(\C)$ by
\[
        \widetilde T = T P_I + i P^I .
\]
Then
\[
        \widetilde T^2+\Id = (T^2+\Id)P_I,
\]
so $\widetilde T^2+\Id$ is strictly singular on $\X_{\omega_1}(\C)$.

Cuellar-Carrera's diagonal construction, Proposition~3, and Remark~7 in
\cite{CC} imply that the diagonal part $D_{\widetilde T}$ is bounded,
that $D_{\widetilde T}^2=-\Id$, and that
$\widetilde T-D_{\widetilde T}$ is strictly singular.  For completeness,
the last assertion is exactly the final step in the proof of
\cite[Theorem~2]{CC}: once the diagonal part is bounded, the relevant
strictly singular perturbation proposition
of \cite{CC} applies because the difference tends to zero on every
rapidly increasing sequence.  Remark~7 supplies the exact-square
conclusion $D_{\widetilde T}^2=-\Id$ under the weaker hypothesis
$\widetilde T^2+\Id\in\SSing$.

The operator $D_{\widetilde T}$ is diagonal with respect to the
transfinite basis, and the initial interval $I=[0,\gamma)$ is invariant
under such diagonal operators.  Hence
\[
        D = D_{\widetilde T}\!\restriction_{\X_\gamma(\C)}
\]
is a real-linear operator on $\X_\gamma(\C)$ with $D^2=-\Id$.  Moreover
\[
        T-D = (\widetilde T-D_{\widetilde T})
              \!\restriction_{\X_\gamma(\C)}
\]
is strictly singular.  Taking $S=T-D$ gives $(T-S)^2=D^2=-\Id$, so the
strictly singular ideal on $\X_\gamma(\C)$ has the lifting property.

Ferenczi--Galego's criterion therefore says that the hyperplanes of
$\X_\gamma(\C)$ do not admit complex structures.  Thus
$\X_\gamma(\C)$ is even.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The exact Question~19 is not claimed as a new run solution: it is
explicitly answered by Cuellar-Carrera's Corollary~9.  The packet's
claim is the strengthened parity conclusion for the same separable
examples.  The proof uses only the operator decomposition and
strictly-singular lifting mechanism already present in \cite{CC} and
\cite{FG}; no numerical or computational verification is involved.

\section*{Novelty and Scope}

Searches were made in the local run indexes and source corpus for
\texttt{0704.1459}, ``even countably many complex structures'',
``countable infinite number of complex structures'', and related
phrases.  Web searches for exact phrases such as ``even countably many
complex structures'' and ``exactly countably many complex structures
even Banach space'' did not reveal a statement of the strengthened
parity conclusion.  The novelty confidence is therefore moderate: the
argument is a direct transfer from Cuellar-Carrera's machinery, and may
be folklore to specialists, but it is not the same as the already-known
answer to Question~19.

This packet does not settle Ferenczi--Galego's other questions about
direct sums of arbitrary even spaces or projection-total incomparability.
