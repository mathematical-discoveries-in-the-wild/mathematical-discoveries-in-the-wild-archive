# Literature-Already-Answered: Orlicz Counterexamples to Guarded-Fraisse Questions

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source question: Cuth--de Rancourt--Doucha, arXiv:2408.12755, Section 7 asks whether every guarded or cofinally Fraisse Banach space is omega-categorical (`quest:GFImpliesOmegaCat`) and whether every guarded or cofinally Fraisse Banach space has closed age (`quest:closedAge`). The same section also asks for new infinite-dimensional examples of guarded/cofinally Fraisse Banach spaces.

Answering paper: de Rancourt--Fakhoury, arXiv:2604.02080, `Isometric rigidity and Fraisse properties of Orlicz sequence spaces`.

Answer recorded here: the questions have negative answers, even for cofinally Fraisse spaces. For every `p > 3`, with `M(t)=t^p e^{t-1}`, the Orlicz sequence space `ell_M` is cofinally Fraisse, hence guarded Fraisse, but it is not oligomorphic/omega-categorical and has non-closed age. The same theorem also records that `ell_M` contains no isomorphic copy of `ell_2`.

Files:
- `main.tex` / `solution_packet.pdf`: compact provenance and implication note.
- `source_paper.pdf`: original arXiv:2408.12755 source.
- `answer_paper_2604.02080.pdf`: later answering paper.

Scope note: this packet does not settle the source paper's specific subquestions about `L_p(L_q(L_r))` or `L_p(E)`. It settles the general omega-categoricity and closed-age questions by explicit counterexample, and supplies new Orlicz-space examples for the general "new guarded/cofinally Fraisse examples" request.
