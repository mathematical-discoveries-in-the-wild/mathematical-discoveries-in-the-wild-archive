\begin{abstract}
This packet upgrades the previous partial packet for Problem 3.19 of
Rozendaal--Veraar, \emph{Fourier multiplier theorems involving type and
cotype}.  It is still not a full intrinsic classification of all Banach pairs
\((X,Y)\), but it adds several substantial results: the scalar classification,
endpoint failures for every nonzero Banach pair, a corrected reading of
Theorem 3.21, an endpoint correction to the positive-kernel statements, a
closed-graph uniformization principle, monotonicity by fractional integration,
an exact bilinear \(\gam\)-Pitt criterion, a target cotype-index obstruction,
and a complete classification for standard infinite-dimensional
\(L^u\to L^v\) and \(\ell^u\to\ell^v\) pairs.  The arbitrary Banach-pair
classification remains open.
\end{abstract}

\section{Source problem and status}

The source paper is Jan Rozendaal and Mark Veraar, \emph{Fourier multiplier
theorems involving type and cotype}, arXiv:1605.09340; the arXiv page records
the journal version in \emph{J. Fourier Anal. Appl.} 24 (2018), 583--619
\cite{RV}.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/problem_3_19_crop.png}
\end{center}

Problem 3.19 asks for a classification of Banach spaces \(X,Y\) for which, for
\[
  1\le p\le 2\le q\le\infty,\qquad
  \frac1r=\frac1p-\frac1q,\qquad r\in(1,\infty],
\]
every \(X\)-strongly measurable symbol
\[
  m:\R^d\setminus\{0\}\to\cL(X,Y)
\]
whose weighted range
\[
  \{\abs{\xi}^{d/r}m(\xi):\xi\ne0\}
\]
is \(\gam\)-bounded defines a bounded Fourier multiplier
\[
  T_m:L^p(\R^d;X)\to L^q(\R^d;Y).
\]
We write this property as \(\Pprop^d_{p,q}(X,Y)\), or simply
\(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\).

\paragraph{Status.}
This packet is a \emph{strong partial result}.  It gives complete answers in
several important regimes, especially the scalar case, all endpoint faces, and
the \(L^u\to L^v\) scale.  It does not give a familiar intrinsic classification
for arbitrary Banach pairs.

\section{Scalar and endpoint classification}

\begin{theorem}[Scalar classification]\label{thm:scalar}
For \(X=Y=\C\), the property \(\Pprop_{p,q}(\C,\C)\) holds if and only if
\[
  1<p\le2\le q<\infty.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For \(1<p\le2\le q<\infty\), scalar \(\gam\)-boundedness is uniform
boundedness, so
\[
  \abs{m(\xi)}\le C\abs{\xi}^{-d/r}.
\]
The function \(\abs{\xi}^{-d/r}\) belongs to weak \(L^r\), and Hormander's
weak-\(L^r\) multiplier theorem gives \(L^p\to L^q\) boundedness
\cite{Hormander}.  The case \(p=q=2\) is Plancherel.

Let \(p=1\) and \(2\le q<\infty\).  The symbol
\[
  a_q(\xi)=\abs{\xi}^{-d/q'}
\]
has weighted range bounded.  Its inverse Fourier transform is a nonzero
multiple of \(\abs{x}^{-d/q}\).  Testing \(T_{a_q}\) on a nonnegative
approximate identity \(\eta_\varepsilon\) gives, on
\(2\varepsilon\le\abs{x}\le1/2\),
\[
  \abs{T_{a_q}\eta_\varepsilon(x)}\gtrsim \abs{x}^{-d/q},
\]
and therefore
\[
  \norm{T_{a_q}\eta_\varepsilon}_{L^q}^q
  \gtrsim\int_{2\varepsilon\le\abs{x}\le1/2}\abs{x}^{-d}\,dx
  \simeq \log(1/\varepsilon)\to\infty.
\]
The \(q=\infty\) face follows by duality from the real even multiplier
\(\abs{\xi}^{-d/p}\): boundedness \(L^p\to L^\infty\) would imply boundedness
\(L^1\to L^{p'}\), already contradicted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Endpoint obstruction for nonzero pairs]\label{thm:endpoints}
Let \(X,Y\ne0\).  Then
\[
  \Pprop_{1,q}(X,Y)\quad(2\le q<\infty)
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  \Pprop_{p,\infty}(X,Y)\quad(1<p\le2)
\]
both fail.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(x_0\in X\), \(x^*\in X^*\), \(y_0\in Y\), and \(y^*\in Y^*\) with
\(x^*(x_0)=y^*(y_0)=1\).  If \(a\) is one of the scalar counterexample symbols,
put
\[
  m(\xi)x=a(\xi)x^*(x)y_0.
\]
The weighted family is \(\gam\)-bounded because, for \(\abs{\lambda_j}\le1\),
\[
\Big(\mathbb E\norm{\sum_j\gamma_j\lambda_jx^*(u_j)y_0}_Y^2\Big)^{1/2}
\le \norm{y_0}\norm{x^*}
\Big(\mathbb E\norm{\sum_j\gamma_ju_j}_X^2\Big)^{1/2}.
\]
Testing on \(f=hx_0\) and applying \(y^*\) recovers the bad scalar estimate.
\end{proof}

\section{Corrections to Theorem 3.21}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/theorem_3_21_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source's Theorem 3.21 includes \(p=1\).  This cannot be correct, already
for \(X=Y=\C\), by Theorem~\ref{thm:scalar}.  The proof imports the critical
embedding
\[
  \dot H^{d/p-d/2}_p(\R^d;X)\hookrightarrow \gam(\R^d;X).
\]
At \(p=1\) and \(X=\C\), this would imply the false strong endpoint Sobolev
embedding \(\dot H^{d/2}_1\hookrightarrow L^2\).  Thus the issue is not a
normalization artifact; it is the classical failure of strong endpoint
fractional integration.

There is also a target-space wording issue.  The source assumes that \(Y\) is
continuously embedded in a \(q\)-concave Banach lattice \(Y_0\).  The proof
obtains an estimate for the image \(J_YT_mf\) in \(Y_0\).  To recover an
estimate in \(Y\), \(J_Y\) must be bounded below, i.e. an isomorphism onto a
closed subspace.  Mere injectivity is insufficient.

\begin{proposition}[Mere target injection is insufficient]\label{prop:injection}
There is a bounded injective map \(J:\ell^v\to\ell^2\) for each \(v>2\), but
\(\Pprop_{2,2}(\C,\ell^v)\) fails.  Hence Theorem 3.21 cannot be read with
``continuous embedding'' meaning only bounded injective map.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The map \(J(y_1,y_2,\ldots)=(2^{-1}y_1,2^{-2}y_2,\ldots)\) is bounded and
injective from \(\ell^v\) to \(\ell^2\).  If this sufficed, the Hilbert target
would give \(\Pprop_{2,2}(\C,\ell^v)\).  But Theorem~\ref{thm:target-v} below,
with \(q=2<v\), gives a contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Corrected lattice theorem]\label{thm:corrected321}
Let
\[
  1<p\le2\le q<\infty,\qquad \frac1r=\frac1p-\frac1q.
\]
Assume \(X\) is isomorphic to a complemented subspace of a \(p\)-convex Banach
lattice \(X_0\) with finite cotype, and \(Y\) is isomorphic to a closed subspace
of a \(q\)-concave Banach lattice \(Y_0\).  If
\[
  \{\abs{\xi}^{d/r}m(\xi):\xi\ne0\}
\]
is \(\gam\)-bounded, then
\[
  T_m:L^p(\R^d;X)\to L^q(\R^d;Y)
\]
is bounded.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For \(X_0,Y_0\), the valid lattice embeddings are
\[
 \dotH^{d/p-d/2}_p(\R^d;X_0)\hookrightarrow\gam(\R^d;X_0),
 \qquad
 \gam(\R^d;Y_0)\hookrightarrow\dotH^{d/q-d/2}_q(\R^d;Y_0),
\]
for \(p>1\).  The source proof then applies with
\[
  m_1(\xi)=\abs{\xi}^{d/2-d/p},\qquad
  m_2(\xi)=\abs{\xi}^{d/r}m(\xi)m_1(\xi),
\]
and the \(\gam\)-multiplier theorem.  For subspaces, use
\(\widetilde m(\xi)=J_Ym(\xi)P_X\), where \(P_X\) is a bounded projection onto
the copy of \(X\) and \(J_Y:Y\to Y_0\) is bounded below.  The \(Y_0\)-estimate
then transfers back to \(Y\).
\end{proof}

\section{Another endpoint correction}

The source's Theorem 3.24 and Corollary 3.25 also include the face \(p=1<q\).
The same endpoint obstruction invalidates that face.

\begin{proposition}[Positive-kernel endpoint failure]\label{prop:positivekernel}
Let \(1<q<\infty\), \(s=d(1-1/q)\), and let \(K\in C_c^\infty(\R^d)\) be
nonnegative with \(\int K=1\).  Convolution by \(K\) is not bounded
\[
  \dot H^s_1(\R^d)\to L^q(\R^d).
\]
Equivalently, the positive symbol \(m_s(\xi)=\abs{\xi}^{-s}\widehat K(\xi)\)
does not define a bounded map \(L^1\to L^q\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(I_s\) be the Riesz potential, with kernel comparable to
\(\abs{x}^{s-d}=\abs{x}^{-d/q}\).  For a nonnegative approximate identity
\(\eta\), \(f=I_s\eta\) belongs to \(\dot H^s_1\) with controlled norm.  But
\[
  K*f=I_s(K*\eta).
\]
Since \(K*\eta\) is compactly supported and has integral one, its Riesz
potential has asymptotic size \(c\abs{x}^{-d/q}\) at infinity, hence is not in
\(L^q\).
\end{proof}

\section{Uniformization and exact criterion}

For the rest of the packet assume
\[
  1<p\le2\le q<\infty,\qquad a=d\Big(\frac1p-\frac1q\Big)<d.
\]
Let \(\Gcal(X,Y)\) be the Banach space of essentially \(\gam\)-bounded
strongly measurable maps \(M:\R^d\to\cL(X,Y)\), with norm the essential
\(\gam\)-bound of the range.

\begin{theorem}[Closed-graph uniformization]\label{thm:uniformization}
If \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) holds qualitatively, then there is \(C\) such that
for every \(M\in\Gcal(X,Y)\),
\[
  \norm{T_{\abs{\cdot}^{-a}M}}_{\cL(L^p(\R^d;X),L^q(\R^d;Y))}
  \le C\norm{M}_{\Gcal}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The map
\[
  \Phi:\Gcal(X,Y)\to\cL(L^p(\R^d;X),L^q(\R^d;Y)),\qquad
  \Phi(M)=T_{\abs{\cdot}^{-a}M},
\]
is everywhere defined by the qualitative property.  If \(M_n\to M\) in
\(\Gcal\) and \(\Phi(M_n)\to S\) in operator norm, then for finite-rank
Schwartz \(f\) the functions \(\Phi(M_n)f\) converge in distributions to
\(\Phi(M)f\), because \(a<d\) and \(\widehat f\) is rapidly decreasing.  They
also converge in distributions to \(Sf\).  Hence \(S=\Phi(M)\) on a dense
subspace, so the graph is closed.  The closed graph theorem gives the bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Monotonicity]\label{thm:monotonicity}
If
\[
  1<p\le p_0\le2\le q_0\le q<\infty
\]
and \(\Pprop_{p_0,q_0}(X,Y)\) holds, then \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) holds.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Put
\[
  \alpha=d\Big(\frac1p-\frac1{p_0}\Big),\qquad
  \beta=d\Big(\frac1{q_0}-\frac1q\Big).
\]
For a symbol \(m\) at \((p,q)\), set \(n(\xi)=\abs{\xi}^{\alpha+\beta}m(\xi)\).
The weighted families for \(m\) at \((p,q)\) and \(n\) at \((p_0,q_0)\) are
identical.  Since
\[
  T_m=I_\beta T_n I_\alpha,
\]
and vector-valued Hardy--Littlewood--Sobolev estimates for the positive Riesz
kernels hold for every Banach space, the result follows.
\end{proof}

For finite-rank Schwartz \(f:\R^d\to X\) and \(g:\R^d\to Y^*\), define
\[
 \Pi_a^\gam(f,g)=
 \sup_{\norm M_{\Gcal}\le1}
 \left|\int_{\R^d}\abs{\xi}^{-a}
 \ip{M(\xi)\widehat f(\xi)}{\widehat g(-\xi)}\,d\xi\right|.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Exact bilinear \(\gam\)-Pitt criterion]\label{thm:pitt}
For nonzero \(X,Y\), \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) holds if and only if there is
\(C\) such that
\[
  \Pi_a^\gam(f,g)
  \le C\norm f_{L^p(\R^d;X)}\norm g_{L^{q'}(\R^d;Y^*)}
\]
for all finite-rank Schwartz \(f,g\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The forward implication is Theorem~\ref{thm:uniformization} and Fourier-side
duality.  Conversely, for \(M\in\Gcal(X,Y)\) and finite-rank Schwartz \(f\), the
inverse transform \(h=T_{\abs{\cdot}^{-a}M}f\) is a continuous \(Y\)-valued
function.  The displayed bilinear inequality bounds the pairing of \(h\) with
dense finite-rank \(L^{q'}(Y^*)\) test functions.  The standard Bochner
norming argument gives
\[
  \norm h_{L^q(Y)}\le C\norm M_{\Gcal}\norm f_{L^p(X)}.
\]
Density gives the multiplier extension.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The criterion is exact but not an intrinsic classification in familiar Banach
space terms.  This is why the packet remains partial for the original Problem
3.19.
\end{remark}

\section{Frequency-translation obstructions}

Fix nonzero \(k,\phi\in C_c^\infty(\R^d)\), set \(\psi=\widehat k\), and put
\(h=k*\phi\ne0\).  Choose translations \(b_1,\ldots,b_n\) so far apart that the
relevant translates of \(\phi\) and \(h\) are disjoint.  Since \(\psi\) is
Schwartz,
\[
  \sup_{\xi\ne0}\abs{\xi}^a\abs{\psi(\xi)}<\infty.
\]
The phases \(e^{\pm2\pi i b_j\cdot\xi}\) make separated inputs add coherently
after multiplication in frequency.

\begin{theorem}[Domain obstruction]\label{thm:domain-u}
Let \(1\le u<p\), let \(Y\ne0\), and suppose \(X\) contains uniformly
complemented copies of \(\ell^u_n\).  Then \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) fails.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
It suffices to use the isometric model \(X=\ell^u\).  Let \(e_j\) be the first
basis vectors and define
\[
  R_\theta x=n^{-1/u'}\sum_{j=1}^n\theta_j e_j^*(x),\qquad \theta\in\T^n.
\]
Because \(u<p\le2\), \(u<2\), and the family \(\{R_\theta\}\subset\cL(\ell^u,\C)\)
is uniformly \(\gam\)-bounded.  Fix \(y_0\in Y\), \(\norm{y_0}=1\), and set
\[
  M_n(\xi)x=n^{-1/u'}\sum_{j=1}^n e^{2\pi i b_j\cdot\xi}e_j^*(x)y_0,\qquad
  m_n(\xi)=\psi(\xi)M_n(\xi).
\]
The weighted \(\gam\)-bounds are uniform.  For
\[
  f_n(t)=\sum_{j=1}^n\phi(t-b_j)e_j,
\]
disjointness gives \(\norm{f_n}_{L^p(\ell^u)}=n^{1/p}\norm{\phi}_p\), while
phase cancellation gives
\[
  T_{m_n}f_n=n^{1/u}h\,y_0.
\]
Uniformization would force \(n^{1/u}\lesssim n^{1/p}\), impossible when
\(u<p\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Target obstruction]\label{thm:target-v}
Let \(q<v<\infty\), let \(X\ne0\), and suppose \(Y\) contains uniformly
isomorphic copies of \(\ell^v_n\).  Then \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) fails.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose embeddings \(J_n:\ell^v_n\to Y\) with uniformly bounded distortion.
Fix \(x_0\in X\), \(x^*\in X^*\), \(x^*(x_0)=1\).  For \(\theta\in\T^n\), define
\[
  S_\theta z=n^{-1/v}zJ_n\Big(\sum_{j=1}^n\theta_je_j\Big).
\]
The family \(\{S_\theta\}\subset\cL(\C,Y)\) is uniformly \(\gam\)-bounded, using
the type \(2\) behavior of fixed \(L^v\)-geometry for \(v>2\).  Put
\[
  M_n(\xi)x=n^{-1/v}x^*(x)J_n\Big(\sum_{j=1}^n e^{-2\pi i b_j\cdot\xi}e_j\Big),
  \qquad m_n=\psi M_n.
\]
For \(f=\phi x_0\),
\[
  T_{m_n}f(t)=n^{-1/v}J_n\Big(\sum_{j=1}^n h(t-b_j)e_j\Big),
\]
so disjointness gives
\[
  \norm{T_{m_n}f}_{L^q(Y)}\gtrsim n^{1/q-1/v}\norm h_q\to\infty,
\]
while \(\norm f_{L^p(X)}\) is fixed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[\(\ell^\infty\) target obstruction]\label{thm:target-infty}
If \(X\ne0\) and \(Y\) contains \(\ell^\infty_n\) uniformly, then
\(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) fails for every finite \(q\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Use the preceding construction with
\[
  c_n=(1+\log(n+1))^{-1/2}
\]
in place of \(n^{-1/v}\).  The Gaussian maximal inequality gives a uniform
\(\gam\)-bound for the phase family into \(\ell^\infty_n\), while the output
norm grows like
\[
  \frac{n^{1/q}}{\sqrt{\log(n+1)}}.
\]
\end{proof}

Let
\[
  q_Y=\inf\{s\in[2,\infty):Y\text{ has cotype }s\},
\]
with \(q_Y=\infty\) if \(Y\) has no finite cotype.

\begin{corollary}[Target cotype-index obstruction]\label{cor:cotype}
If \(X\ne0\) and \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) holds, then \(q_Y\le q\).  In particular
\(Y\) must have finite cotype.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By the Maurey--Pisier finite representability theorem, if \(q_Y>q\), then
\(\ell^v_n\) for some \(v>q\), or \(\ell^\infty_n\), is uniformly finitely
representable in \(Y\).  Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:target-v} or
Theorem~\ref{thm:target-infty}.
\end{proof}

\section{Complete \(L^u\to L^v\) classification}

\begin{theorem}[Infinite-dimensional Lebesgue pairs]\label{thm:LuLv}
Let \(1\le u,v<\infty\), and let \(L^u(S)\), \(L^v(T)\) be
infinite-dimensional Lebesgue spaces over measure spaces admitting arbitrarily
many pairwise disjoint sets of finite positive measure.  For
\[
  1<p\le2\le q<\infty,
\]
one has
\[
  \Pprop_{p,q}(L^u(S),L^v(T))
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  p\le u\ \text{ and }\ v\le q.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
If \(p\le u\), then \(L^u\) is \(p\)-convex and has finite cotype.  If
\(v\le q\), then \(L^v\) is \(q\)-concave.  The positive direction follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:corrected321}.  If \(u<p\), disjoint normalized indicators
span \(1\)-complemented \(\ell^u_n\)'s in \(L^u\), so Theorem~\ref{thm:domain-u}
applies.  If \(v>q\), disjoint normalized indicators give uniformly
isomorphic \(\ell^v_n\)'s in \(L^v\), so Theorem~\ref{thm:target-v} applies.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Sequence spaces]\label{cor:sequence}
For \(1\le u,v<\infty\),
\[
  \Pprop_{p,q}(\ell^u,\ell^v)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  p\le u\ \text{ and }\ v\le q.
\]
In particular,
\[
  \Pprop_{p,q}(\ell^u,\ell^u)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  p\le u\le q.
\]
\end{corollary}

\section{Remaining open core}

The results above strongly constrain the answer and solve many concrete
regimes, but they do not finish the original problem for arbitrary Banach
pairs.  The main remaining task is to turn the exact
\(\gam\)-Pitt criterion of Theorem~\ref{thm:pitt} into a classification in
standard Banach-space invariants.

\begin{problem}
Classify the pairs \((X,Y)\) for which the bilinear criterion in
Theorem~\ref{thm:pitt} holds using familiar invariants such as type, cotype,
Fourier type, \(K\)-convexity, or local finite representability.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}
Decide whether \(\Pprop_{p,q}(X,Y)\) forces type \(p\) of \(X\) or cotype
\(q\) of \(Y\) at the exact endpoint without the extra assumptions in known
rank-one Pitt converse arguments.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}
Classify \(\Pprop_{p,q}(L^\infty(S),Y)\) for finite-cotype targets \(Y\).  The
corrected lattice theorem does not apply because \(L^\infty\) lacks finite
cotype.
\end{problem}

\section{Novelty and verification notes}

The source paper, local run index, and web/arXiv searches through June 18, 2026
were checked for Problem 3.19, Theorem 3.21, errata/corrigenda, weighted
\(\gam\)-bounded multiplier ranges, and \(L^u\to L^v\) or
\(\ell^u\to\ell^v\) formulations.  No official correction to the \(p=1\)
endpoint and no matching published \(L^u\to L^v\) cross-pair classification
were located.  This is bounded evidence, not an exhaustive novelty proof.

Human review should focus on:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item the closed-graph argument for \(\Gcal(X,Y)\);
\item the closed-range interpretation of the target embedding in Theorem 3.21;
\item the positive-kernel endpoint counterexample;
\item the uniform \(\gam\)-bounds in the frequency-translation constructions;
\item the finite-representability step in Corollary~\ref{cor:cotype}.
\end{enumerate}
