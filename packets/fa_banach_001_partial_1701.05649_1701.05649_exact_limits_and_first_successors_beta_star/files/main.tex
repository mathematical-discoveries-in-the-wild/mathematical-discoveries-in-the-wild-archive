\section*{Difference and Potential Ranks}

For disjoint $\bPi^0_{\xi+1}$ sets $A,B$, let $\alpha_\xi(A,B)$ be the
least length of a decreasing continuous sequence of $\bPi^0_\xi$ sets whose
alternating difference contains $A$ and is disjoint from $B$.  For a Baire
class $\xi$ function $f$, $\alpha_\xi(f)$ is the supremum of these separation
lengths over the rational level pairs $\{f\leq p\},\{f\geq q\}$.

Let $\alpha^*_\xi(f)$ be the minimum ordinary separation rank over the same
admissible Polish refinements used to define $\beta^*_\xi(f)$.  Elekes, Kiss,
and Vidny\'anszky prove the exact inequalities
\begin{equation}\label{eq:comparison}
  \alpha^*_\xi(f)\leq\alpha_\xi(f)
  \leq 2\alpha^*_\xi(f),
\end{equation}
where multiplication is ordinal multiplication from the left
\cite[Theorem 5.17]{EKV}.

We use the classical strictness theorem for the Hausdorff difference
hierarchy: on Cantor space, for every $1\leq\xi<\omega_1$ and every nonzero
countable $\theta$, there is a $K\in\bDelta^0_{\xi+1}$ whose least
continuous alternating-difference length over $\bPi^0_\xi$ is exactly
$\theta$; see \cite[Section 22]{Kechris}.  This is the main external
descriptive-set-theoretic input.

\begin{lemma}[Characteristic functions]\label{lem:characteristic}
For every admissible Polish topology $\sigma$ and every set $H$ for which
$\chi_H$ is Baire class one in $\sigma$,
\[
  \alpha_\sigma(\chi_H)=\beta_\sigma(\chi_H).
\]
Consequently $\alpha^*_\xi(\chi_H)=\beta^*_\xi(\chi_H)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For the only nontrivial rational level pairs of $\chi_H$, the two separated
sets are $H^c$ and $H$.  Their separation derivative on a closed set $F$ is
\[
 \overline{F\cap H}^{\,\sigma}\cap
 \overline{F\cap H^c}^{\,\sigma}.
\]
For every $0<\varepsilon\leq1$, this is also exactly the oscillation
derivative $D_\sigma(\chi_H,\varepsilon,F)$.  For $\varepsilon>1$ the latter
is empty.  The transfinite iterates, their ranks, and then the minima over
admissible topologies therefore agree.
\end{proof}

\section*{Exact Realization Theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $(X,\tau)$ be an uncountable Polish space and let
$2\leq\xi<\omega_1$.  Suppose that $\theta$ is either
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a nonzero countable limit ordinal, or
  \item $\lambda+1$, where $\lambda$ is a nonzero countable limit ordinal.
\end{enumerate}
Then there is a set $A\subseteq X$ such that $\chi_A\in\B_\xi(X,\tau)$ and
\[
  \beta^*_\xi(\chi_A)=\theta.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Every uncountable Polish space contains a closed subset $P$ homeomorphic to
Cantor space.  Work first in $P$.  By strictness of the difference hierarchy,
choose $K\in\bDelta^0_{\xi+1}(P)$ of exact $\bPi^0_\xi(P)$-difference length
$\theta$, and put $H=P\setminus K$.

For $f=\chi_H$ the only nontrivial separation problem is the ordered pair
$(K,H)$.  A set separating $K$ from $H=P\setminus K$ must equal $K$.
Therefore the orientation is fixed and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:alphaxi-exact}
  \alpha_{\xi,P}(f)=\theta.
\end{equation}
Since $H,K\in\bDelta^0_{\xi+1}(P)$, the characteristic function $f$ is of
Baire class at most $\xi$.

Set
\[
 r=\beta^*_{\xi,P}(f)=\alpha^*_{\xi,P}(f),
\]
using Lemma \ref{lem:characteristic}.  Equations
\eqref{eq:comparison} and \eqref{eq:alphaxi-exact} give
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sandwich}
  r\leq\theta\leq2r.
\end{equation}

If $\theta$ is a limit and $r<\theta$, then
\[
  2r<r+\omega\leq\theta.
\]
This contradicts $\theta\leq2r$ in \eqref{eq:sandwich}.  Hence $r=\theta$.

Now let $\theta=\lambda+1$, with $\lambda$ a nonzero limit.  If
$r<\lambda+1$, then $r\leq\lambda$, so
\[
  2r\leq2\lambda=\lambda<\lambda+1=\theta,
\]
again contradicting \eqref{eq:sandwich}.  The identity $2\lambda=\lambda$
follows from $2\eta<\eta+\omega\leq\lambda$ for every $\eta<\lambda$ and
continuity of ordinal multiplication in its right argument.  Thus in both
cases
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rank-on-P}
  \beta^*_{\xi,P}(\chi_H)=\theta.
\end{equation}

Finally extend $\chi_H$ by zero from $P$ to $X$, equivalently regard
$H\subseteq P\subseteq X$ and use $g=\chi_H$ on $X$.  Lemma 3.5 of \cite{LNT}
gives
\[
 \theta=\beta^*_{\xi,P}(g|_P)
 \leq\beta^*_{\xi,X}(g)
 \leq1+\beta^*_{\xi,P}(g|_P)=1+\theta.
\]
If $\theta$ is a nonzero limit, $1+\theta=\theta$.  If
$\theta=\lambda+1$, then
$1+\theta=(1+\lambda)+1=\lambda+1=\theta$.  Hence the two bounds agree.
The same lemma ensures that the zero extension is Baire class $\xi$ on $X$,
and the proof is complete.
\end{proof}

\section*{Why the Argument Stops at the Next Finite Tail}

For $\theta=\lambda+n$ with $n\geq2$, a hypothetical strict drop to
$r=\lambda+n-1$ is compatible with the comparison theorem because
\[
  2r=\lambda+2n-2\geq\lambda+n=\theta.
\]
Thus exact difference length alone no longer prevents a lower potential
rank.  A full proof would need a calibrated witness that simultaneously has
an admissible upper topology of rank $\lambda+n$ and a stronger
refinement-resistant lower invariant.  A one-point successor gadget is
destroyed by making the point clopen, while a tested finite rooted-tree
calibration did not attain the required sharp factor two.  Neither route is
promoted as a theorem.
