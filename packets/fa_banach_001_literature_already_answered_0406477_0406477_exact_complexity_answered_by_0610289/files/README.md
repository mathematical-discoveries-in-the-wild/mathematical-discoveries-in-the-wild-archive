# Exact complexity of separable Banach-space isomorphism

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source/open-problem paper: Valentin Ferenczi and Eloi Medina Galego,
*Some equivalence relations which are Borel reducible to isomorphism between
separable Banach spaces*, arXiv:math/0406477.

Supporting answer paper: Valentin Ferenczi, Alain Louveau, and Christian
Rosendal, *The complexity of classifying separable Banach spaces up to
isomorphism*, arXiv:math/0610289.

## Identification

Remark 5.4 of arXiv:math/0406477 says that the exact complexity of isomorphism
between separable Banach spaces, and even of Banach spaces with Schauder bases,
was quite open, and suggests that it could be `Sigma^1_1`-complete.

The later paper arXiv:math/0610289 proves exactly this for separable Banach
spaces: its abstract and main theorem state that isomorphism between separable
Banach spaces is a complete analytic equivalence relation, equivalently
`Sigma^1_1`-complete under Borel reducibility.  The same paper also proves
that permutative equivalence between Schauder bases is complete analytic, and
the reduction used for the Banach-space theorem constructs reflexive spaces
with suppression unconditional bases.

## Scope

This packet records an already-known literature answer to the exact-complexity
question in Remark 5.4.  It does not solve the distinct open problems in
Remark 5.2 and Conjecture 5.3 about ergodicity of `ell_p` for `p>2`, the
broader non-ergodicity conjecture, or block-subspace dichotomies for arbitrary
unconditional bases.
