\section*{Source Question}

Klep, \v{S}trekelj, and Zalar compare compact affine sections of the
copositive and completely positive cones and obtain a dimension-free lower
bound for the ratio of their volume radii \cite{KSZ}.  In Remark 1.5(1), on
pages 4--5, they ask whether the analogous largeness persists inside the
max-norm unit ball $B_{n,\max}$:

The displayed source passage writes the relevant volume as
$c^{\dim\Sym_n}\Vol(B_{n,\max})$ and is embedded in a discussion of volume
radii.  We therefore formalize the substantive question by asking whether
the base $c$, rather than the raw probability $c^{\dim\Sym_n}$, stays bounded
away from zero.  (For raw probability, $\CP_n\subseteq\mathcal{NN}_n$ already
gives an immediate negative answer.)

\section*{Definitions and Answer}

Let $\Sym_n$ be the real vector space of symmetric $n\times n$ matrices,
$d_n=n(n+1)/2$, and
\[
 B_{n,\max}=\{A=[a_{ij}]\in\Sym_n:\max_{i,j}|a_{ij}|\leq1\}.
\]
For a measurable cone $K\subseteq\Sym_n$, define its normalized max-cube
volume radius by
\[
 \rho_n(K)=
 \left(\frac{\Vol(K\cap B_{n,\max})}{\Vol(B_{n,\max})}\right)^{1/d_n}.
\]
This ratio is independent of whether Lebesgue measure is expressed in the
independent upper-triangular coordinates or in orthonormal Frobenius
coordinates.

Write $\CP_n$ for the completely positive cone and $\PSD_n$ for the
positive-semidefinite cone.  Let
\[
 v_k=\Vol\{x\in\mathbb R^k:\lVert x\rVert_2\leq1\}
     =\frac{\pi^{k/2}}{\Gamma(k/2+1)}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Negative answer in the max cube]\label{thm:main}
For every $n\geq2$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:explicit}
 \rho_n(\CP_n)\leq\rho_n(\PSD_n)
 \leq\frac12
 \left[
   \left(\frac{2}{n+1}\right)^n
   \prod_{k=1}^{n-1}v_k
 \right]^{1/d_n}.
\end{equation}
Consequently,
\[
                  \rho_n(\CP_n)=O(n^{-1/2})\longrightarrow0.
\]
Thus the max-norm ball makes the difference visible: completely positive
matrices do not occupy a dimension-free normalized volume radius there.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The key point is that the max cube interacts very rigidly with positive
semidefiniteness.  Once the diagonal $(t_1,\ldots,t_n)$ is fixed, every PSD
matrix in the cube is uniquely a diagonally scaled correlation matrix
$D^{1/2}CD^{1/2}$.  The Jacobian contributes only
$\prod_i t_i^{(n-1)/2}$.  It remains to estimate the volume of the correlation
elliptope.

Build a correlation matrix one row and column at a time.  Above a leading
block $C$, the new correlation vector lies in the Schur-complement ellipsoid
$x^TC^{-1}x\leq1$, whose volume is $v_k\sqrt{\det C}\leq v_k$.
Iteration gives a product of Euclidean ball volumes.  That product has log
volume $-(n^2/4)\log n+O(n^2)$, which becomes a factor of order
$n^{-1/2}$ after taking the $d_n$-th root.

\section*{Proof}

Let
\[
 \E_n=\{C\in\PSD_n:\diag(C)=(1,\ldots,1)\}
\]
be the correlation elliptope, viewed in its $n(n-1)/2$ off-diagonal
coordinates, and put $V_n=\Vol(\E_n)$.

\begin{lemma}[Elliptope recursion]\label{lem:elliptope}
For $n\geq2$,
\[
                       V_n\leq\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}v_k.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Set $V_1=1$.  Apart from a null boundary, a matrix in $\E_n$ has a
positive-definite leading block and can be written
\[
                 \begin{pmatrix}C&x\\x^T&1\end{pmatrix},
                 \qquad C\in\operatorname{int}(\E_{n-1}).
\]
By the Schur complement, the allowed fiber is
$\{x:x^TC^{-1}x\leq1\}$.  Its volume is
$v_{n-1}\sqrt{\det C}$.  Hadamard's determinant inequality gives
$0\leq\det C\leq1$, because $C$ is PSD with unit diagonal.  Fubini's theorem
therefore yields
\[
 V_n=v_{n-1}\int_{\E_{n-1}}\sqrt{\det C}\,dC
     \leq v_{n-1}V_{n-1}.
\]
Iteration proves the claim.
\end{proof}

We next compute the PSD volume in the max cube.  Use the independent entries
$a_{ii}$ and $a_{ij}$, $i<j$, as coordinates.  The resulting volume ratios
are the same as for the paper's Frobenius Lebesgue measure, since the latter
merely multiplies every off-diagonal coordinate by $\sqrt2$ in both numerator
and denominator.

If $A\in\PSD_n\cap B_{n,\max}$, then $t_i=a_{ii}\in[0,1]$.
Outside the null set on which some $t_i=0$, put $D=\diag(t_1,\ldots,t_n)$
and
\[
                         C=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}.
\]
Then $C\in\E_n$.  Conversely, every $t\in(0,1]^n$ and $C\in\E_n$ gives
$A=D^{1/2}CD^{1/2}\in\PSD_n\cap B_{n,\max}$: indeed
$|c_{ij}|\leq1$ for a correlation matrix, so
$|a_{ij}|\leq\sqrt{t_it_j}\leq1$.

For fixed $t$, the map on off-diagonal coordinates is
$c_{ij}\mapsto a_{ij}=\sqrt{t_it_j}\,c_{ij}$.  Its Jacobian is
\[
 \prod_{i<j}\sqrt{t_it_j}
       =\prod_{i=1}^n t_i^{(n-1)/2}.
\]
Consequently,
\begin{align}
 \Vol(\PSD_n\cap B_{n,\max})
 &=V_n\prod_{i=1}^n\int_0^1t_i^{(n-1)/2}\,dt_i\notag\\
 &=V_n\left(\frac{2}{n+1}\right)^n.\label{eq:psdvolume}
\end{align}
In these coordinates $\Vol(B_{n,\max})=2^{d_n}$.  Since
$\CP_n\subseteq\PSD_n$, equation \eqref{eq:psdvolume} and Lemma
\ref{lem:elliptope} give \eqref{eq:explicit}.

It remains only to read its asymptotics.  The elementary ball-volume bound
\[
                         v_k\leq\left(\frac{2\pi e}{k}\right)^{k/2}
\]
implies
\begin{align*}
 \log\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}v_k
 &\leq\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}k\bigl(\log(2\pi e)-\log k\bigr)\\
 &=-\frac14n^2\log n+O(n^2),
\end{align*}
where one uses
$\sum_{k<n}k\log k=\frac12n^2\log n-\frac14n^2+O(n\log n)$.
Because $d_n=\frac12n^2+O(n)$ and
$n\log(2/(n+1))=O(n\log n)$, taking logarithms in
\eqref{eq:explicit} gives
\[
                \log\rho_n(\CP_n)\leq-\frac12\log n+O(1).
\]
This proves Theorem \ref{thm:main}. \qed

\section*{Scope and Novelty Check}

The theorem settles the max-cube question negatively in the normalized
volume-radius sense.  It does not compute the sharp constant or the exact
asymptotic of $\rho_n(\CP_n)$; it only supplies the decisive vanishing upper
bound.  The separate source question about precise constants in the authors'
special affine sections is unaffected.

Bounded searches through July 22, 2026 used arXiv:2305.16224, the exact source
title, and combinations of ``max norm,'' ``completely positive matrix,''
``volume,'' and ``correlation elliptope.''  No later paper stating this answer
or this reduction was found.  The argument is short once the PSD/correlation
change of variables is made, so novelty confidence is moderate and specialist
review is recommended.
