\section*{Source question}

The source is Yves Cornulier and Romain Tessera, \emph{On the vanishing of reduced
1-cohomology for Banach representations}, arXiv:1711.05566.  On PDF page 19,
Questions 4.6 and 4.7 ask whether Property $\APfd$ holds for arbitrary locally
compact groups, and more concretely:

\begin{quote}
Let $G$ be a locally compact group and $V$ an almost periodic Banach
$G$-module. Consider
$b\in \Zone(G,V)\setminus \overline{\Bone(G,V)}$. Does there exist a
$G$-submodule of finite codimension $W\subset V$ such that the image of $b$ in
$\Zone(G,V/W)$ is unbounded?
\end{quote}

\section*{Result}

The following gives a positive answer for the standard class of almost periodic
modules which can be approximated by equivariant finite-dimensional projections.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $G$ be a locally compact group and let $V$ be an almost periodic Banach
$G$-module.  Suppose that there is a net $(P_i)$ of bounded linear projections
on $V$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item each $P_i$ commutes with the $G$-action;
\item each range $F_i=P_iV$ is finite-dimensional;
\item $P_i v\to v$ in norm for every $v\in V$.
\end{enumerate}
If $b\in \Zone(G,V)$ is not an almost coboundary, then for some $i$ the
projected cocycle $P_i b\in \Zone(G,F_i)$ is unbounded.  Consequently, with
$W_i=\ker P_i$, the image of $b$ in $\Zone(G,V/W_i)$ is unbounded and $W_i$ is a
finite-codimensional $G$-submodule.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof intuition}

The obstruction in Question 4.7 is whether a nonzero reduced cohomology class in
an almost periodic module must already be visible at finite dimension.  If
finite-dimensional equivariant projections approximate the identity, then the
answer is yes.  A bounded finite-dimensional affine action with precompact
linear part has a fixed point, so every bounded projected cocycle is an actual
coboundary.  If all finite-dimensional projections were bounded, their transfer
vectors would give almost fixed points for the original affine action, because
the discarded part $(I-P_i)b$ is uniformly small on compact subsets of $G$.

\section*{Proof}

We first record the elementary finite-dimensional fact used in the argument.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:bounded-fd}
Let $F$ be a finite-dimensional Banach $G$-module whose linear action has
precompact image in $\mathrm{GL}(F)$.  If $c\in \Zone(G,F)$ is bounded, then
$c$ is a coboundary.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\alpha(g)x=\pi(g)x+c(g)$ be the corresponding affine action on $F$.  Since
$c(G)$ is bounded and $\pi(G)$ is precompact, $\alpha(G)$ has compact closure in
the finite-dimensional affine group.  This closure is a compact group of affine
maps.  Averaging any point of $F$ over this compact group with Haar probability
measure gives a fixed point $x_0$.  Thus
\[
  \pi(g)x_0+c(g)=x_0
\]
for all $g\in G$, i.e. $c(g)=x_0-\pi(g)x_0$.
\end{proof}

We prove Theorem \ref{thm:main} by contraposition.  Assume that every $P_i b$ is
bounded.  By Lemma \ref{lem:bounded-fd}, for each $i$ there exists $x_i\in F_i$
such that
\[
  P_i b(g)=x_i-\pi(g)x_i\qquad(g\in G).
\]
Fix a compact subset $K\subset G$ and $\varepsilon>0$.  Since $b$ is continuous,
$b(K)$ is a compact subset of $V$.  The pointwise convergence $P_i\to I$ and the
uniform boundedness principle imply that the net $(P_i)$ is uniformly bounded;
hence $P_i\to I$ uniformly on compact subsets of $V$.  Therefore, for some $i$,
\[
  \sup_{g\in K}\|(I-P_i)b(g)\|<\varepsilon.
\]
For this $i$ and every $g\in K$,
\[
  \pi(g)x_i+b(g)-x_i
  = b(g)-P_i b(g)
  = (I-P_i)b(g),
\]
so $x_i$ is an $\varepsilon$-almost fixed point for the affine action associated
to $b$ on the compact set $K$.  Since $K$ and $\varepsilon$ were arbitrary, $b$
is an almost coboundary.  This contradicts the hypothesis.  Hence some $P_i b$
is unbounded.  Since $P_i$ is equivariant, $W_i=\ker P_i$ is a closed
$G$-submodule of finite codimension and $V/W_i$ identifies with $F_i$.
\qed

\section*{Consequences}

\begin{corollary}
Let $V$ be an almost periodic Banach $G$-module with an equivariant
finite-dimensional decomposition: $V$ is the closed span of finite-dimensional
$G$-submodules $(E_n)$ and the coordinate partial-sum projections
$P_N:\overline{\bigoplus E_n}\to E_1+\cdots+E_N$ are uniformly bounded and
converge strongly to the identity.  Then Question 4.7 has a positive answer for
cocycles into $V$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The partial-sum projections satisfy the hypotheses of Theorem \ref{thm:main}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
This includes the usual $c_0$- and $\ell_p$-sums, $1\le p<\infty$, of
finite-dimensional $G$-modules, with the coordinatewise action.  Thus a
counterexample to Question 4.7 cannot be obtained merely by taking a diagonal
direct sum of finite-dimensional almost periodic representations with the
standard coordinate projections.
\end{remark}

\section*{Limitations and novelty check}

This packet does not prove Property $\APfd$ for all locally compact groups.  The
extra hypothesis is exactly a controlled finite-dimensional approximation of
the identity by equivariant projections.  General Banach representations of
compact groups need not come equipped with such projections, even though
finite-dimensional submodules are dense in the almost periodic part.

Run-local cheap indexes were searched for arXiv:1711.05566 and for the terms
\texttt{WAPFD}, \texttt{WAPAP}, \texttt{APFD}, and \texttt{almost periodic
Banach}.  No prior run packet for this target was found.  A targeted web search
for the exact WAPFD/WAPAP/APFD wording did not reveal an obvious later answer
to Questions 4.6 or 4.7.
