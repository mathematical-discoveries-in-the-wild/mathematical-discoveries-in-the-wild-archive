\section*{Source and Question}

The source paper is A. K. Karn and D. P. Sinha, \emph{Compactness and an approximation property related to an operator ideal}, arXiv:1207.1947. In Section 4 the authors define the $\ell_\infty$-extension property for an operator ideal $(\cA,\alpha)$: whenever
\[
T\in \cA(X,\linf(\Gamma)),
\]
there should exist
\[
\widetilde T\in \cA(\linf(\Ball_{X^*}),\linf(\Gamma))
\]
such that $T=\widetilde T i_X$ and $\alpha(\widetilde T)=\alpha(T)$, where
$i_X:X\to \linf(\Ball_{X^*})$ is the Alouglu embedding.

Near the end of the paper, after Corollary 6.6, the authors write:
\[
\text{``we do not know whether there is an injective operator ideal that fails the }
\ell_\infty\text{-extension property.''}
\]

\section*{Result}

The answer is affirmative. The operator ideal of separable-range operators is injective, but it fails the $\ell_\infty$-extension property.

\section*{Definitions}

Let $\cX(E,F)$ denote the class of bounded linear operators $T:E\to F$ for which $\overline{T(E)}$ is separable, equipped with the ordinary operator norm. This is a closed Banach operator ideal: sums and ideal compositions preserve separability of the closed range, and norm limits have range contained in the closed linear span of countably many separable ranges.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The $\ell_\infty$-extension property would extend every separable-range map into an $\ell_\infty$ space while keeping the extension's range separable. Apply this to the canonical embedding of $\czero$ into its Alouglu envelope. Any separable-range extension has a separable closed range containing the copy of $\czero$. Sobczyk's theorem then complements that copy of $\czero$ inside the separable range. Composing the projection with the hypothetical extension gives a projection from the Alouglu envelope onto $\czero$. But a Banach space complemented in its Alouglu envelope is injective; this would force $\czero$ to be injective, contradicting Phillips' theorem.

\begin{lemma}
The ideal $\cX$ of separable-range operators is injective.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $J:F\to G$ be an isomorphic isometry and let $T:E\to F$ be bounded. If $T$ has separable range, then $JT$ has separable range. Conversely, if $JT$ has separable range, then $J(T(E))$ is separable, and applying $J^{-1}$ on the subspace $J(F)$ shows that $T(E)$ is separable. Thus $T\in\cX(E,F)$ if and only if $JT\in\cX(E,G)$, which is precisely injectivity of the ideal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
The injective Banach operator ideal $\cX$ does not have the $\ell_\infty$-extension property.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $X=\czero$ and identify $X^*=\ellone$. Write
\[
i=i_X:\czero\longrightarrow \linf(\Ball_{\ellone})
\]
for the Alouglu embedding. Since $\czero$ is separable, $i$ has separable range, so
\[
i\in \cX(\czero,\linf(\Ball_{\ellone})).
\]

Suppose, toward a contradiction, that $\cX$ had the $\ell_\infty$-extension property. Then there would be an operator
\[
S\in \cX(\linf(\Ball_{\ellone}),\linf(\Ball_{\ellone}))
\]
such that
\[
S i = i.
\]
The range of $S$ is separable; let
\[
E=\overline{S(\linf(\Ball_{\ellone}))}.
\]
Then $E$ is a separable Banach subspace of $\linf(\Ball_{\ellone})$, and $i(\czero)\subset E$ because $S i=i$.

By Sobczyk's theorem, every copy of $\czero$ inside a separable Banach space is complemented. Hence there is a bounded projection
\[
P:E\to i(\czero).
\]
Now $PS$, viewed as an operator from $\linf(\Ball_{\ellone})$ into $\linf(\Ball_{\ellone})$, is a bounded projection onto $i(\czero)$: its range is contained in $i(\czero)$, and for every $x\in\czero$,
\[
PS(i x)=P(i x)=i x.
\]
Thus $i(\czero)$ is complemented in its Alouglu envelope $\linf(\Ball_{\ellone})$.

We next recall the standard consequence. If a Banach space $Y$ is complemented in $\linf(\Ball_{Y^*})$ through its Alouglu embedding, then $Y$ is injective. Indeed, for any isometric embedding $Y\subset Z$, injectivity of the $\linf$ space extends the Alouglu embedding $i_Y:Y\to \linf(\Ball_{Y^*})$ to a bounded operator $R:Z\to \linf(\Ball_{Y^*})$; composing $R$ with the projection onto $i_Y(Y)$ and then with $i_Y^{-1}$ extends the identity on $Y$ to a projection from $Z$ onto $Y$.

Therefore the preceding paragraph would make $\czero$ injective. This is impossible: by Phillips' theorem, $\czero$ is not complemented in $\linf$. Equivalently, $\czero$ is not an injective Banach space.

The contradiction shows that no such separable-range extension $S$ exists. In particular, the norm-preserving extension demanded by the $\ell_\infty$-extension property cannot exist. Hence $\cX$ is an injective operator ideal failing the $\ell_\infty$-extension property.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses only standard Banach-space facts: Sobczyk's theorem and Phillips' non-complementability theorem for $\czero\subset\linf$. The contradiction rules out any separable-range extension of the Alouglu embedding $i_{\czero}$, so it is stronger than a failure of norm preservation.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a likely valid counterexample. The main points to check are: (1) the separable-range class is accepted as a Banach operator ideal with the operator norm; (2) Sobczyk's theorem applies to the separable closed range $E$; and (3) complementability in the Alouglu envelope indeed implies injectivity, hence contradicts Phillips' theorem for $\czero$.
