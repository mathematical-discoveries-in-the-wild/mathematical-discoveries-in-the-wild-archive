\section{Source Question}

The source paper is

\begin{quote}
P. M. Berna, O. Blasco, G. Garrigos, E. Hernandez, and T. Oikhberg,
\emph{Embeddings and Lebesgue-type inequalities for the greedy algorithm in
Banach spaces}, arXiv:1707.07513.

In Section 7 the paper asks:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Question 2.} Characterize the systems for which
\[
   \max\{L_N,L_N^*\}\lesssim \log(N+1).
\]
Concerning Question 2,
\[
   \OT_N(D,D^*)\lesssim \log(N+1)

gives a sufficient condition, but the authors do not know whether it is

\section{Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:counterexample}
There is a seminormalized complete biorthogonal system
\((\mathcal B,\mathcal B^*)\) in a Banach space \(X\) such that
\[
   \max\{L_N(\mathcal B,X),L_N(\mathcal B^*,\widehat X)\}
      \le C\log(N+1),\qquad N\ge 2,
\]
but
\[
   \OT_N(D,D^*) \ge c(\log N)^2,\qquad N\ge 2.
\]
Consequently, the sufficient condition
\(\OT_N(D,D^*)\lesssim \log(N+1)\) is not necessary for the logarithmic
Lebesgue estimate in Question 2.
\end{theorem}

\section{Idea of the Proof}

The construction puts two Lorentz sequence spaces next to each other.  One has
fundamental function \(\eta(n)\simeq \sqrt{n\log n}\), and the other has the
reciprocal fundamental function \(\xi(n)\simeq \sqrt{n/\log n}\), so that
\(\eta(n)\xi(n)=n\).  The \(\ell_\infty\)-direct sum makes the lower democracy
function small, of size \(\xi\), but leaves the upper democracy function large,
of size \(\eta\).  The same happens in the dual because the two fundamental
functions are reciprocal.  Thus both primal and dual democracy ratios are only
logarithmic, and unconditionality gives \(L_N,L_N^*=O(\log N)\).

On the other hand \(D(N)\) and \(D^*(N)\) both dominate \(\eta(N)\).  Since
\[
   \sum_{j\le N} \frac{\eta(j)}{j}\Delta\eta(j)
        \asymp \sum_{j\le N}\frac{\log j}{j}
        \asymp (\log N)^2,
\]
the \(\OT_N\)-quantity is quadratically logarithmic.  This separates the
sufficient condition from the desired conclusion.

\section{Construction}

Set, for \(n\ge 1\),
\[
   \lambda_n=2+\log n,\qquad
   \eta(n)=\sqrt{n\lambda_n},\qquad
   \xi(n)=\sqrt{\frac{n}{\lambda_n}},
\]
and put \(\eta(0)=\xi(0)=0\).  The functions
\(x\mapsto \sqrt{x(2+\log x)}\) and \(x\mapsto \sqrt{x/(2+\log x)}\) are
increasing and concave on \([1,\infty)\), hence the sequences \(\eta\) and
\(\xi\) are concave weights.  They satisfy
\[
   \eta(n)\xi(n)=n,\qquad \frac{\eta(n)}{\xi(n)}=\lambda_n.
\]

For a concave weight \(\omega\), let \(d(\omega)\) denote the discrete Lorentz
space \(\ell^1_{\widehat \omega}\) used in the source paper:
\[
   \|a\|_{d(\omega)}=\sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_j^*\,\Delta\omega(j),
\]
where \(a^*\) is the nonincreasing rearrangement.  The canonical unit-vector
basis is 1-symmetric and has fundamental function exactly \(\omega\):
\[
   \|{\bf 1}_A\|_{d(\omega)}=\omega(|A|).
\]
By the duality theorem for these Lorentz spaces recorded in the source paper,
the closed coordinate span of the dual is the Marcinkiewicz space
\(m(\omega')\), where \(\omega'(n)=n/\omega(n)\).  A direct calculation gives
\[
   \|{\bf 1}_A\|_{m(\omega')}=\omega'(|A|).
\]

Now define
\[
   X=d(\eta)\oplus_\infty d(\xi),
\]
and let \(\mathcal B\) be the concatenated coordinate basis.  This is a
seminormalized complete 1-unconditional basis.  Its closed dual coordinate
span is
\[
   \widehat X=d(\eta)^*\oplus_1 d(\xi)^* .
\]

\section{Democracy Estimates}

If \(A\) has \(k\) coordinates in the \(d(\eta)\)-summand and \(\ell\)
coordinates in the \(d(\xi)\)-summand, then
\[
   \|{\bf 1}_A\|_X=\max\{\eta(k),\xi(\ell)\},
\]
with the convention that \(\eta(0)=\xi(0)=0\).  Since \(\eta\ge \xi\), the
upper democracy function is exactly
\[
   D(N)=\eta(N).
\]
The lower democracy function satisfies
\[
   d(N)=\inf_{k+\ell=N}\max\{\eta(k),\xi(\ell)\}\asymp \xi(N).
\]
Indeed, the upper bound is obtained by taking all \(N\) coordinates from the
\(d(\xi)\)-summand.  For the lower bound, either \(k\ge N/2\), when
\(\eta(k)\gtrsim \eta(N)\ge \xi(N)\), or \(\ell\ge N/2\), when
\(\xi(\ell)\gtrsim \xi(N)\).

In the dual coordinate span, the same split gives
\[
   \|{\bf 1}_A^*\|_{\widehat X}
      = \xi(k)+\eta(\ell),
\]
because \(\eta'= \xi\) and \(\xi'=\eta\).  Hence
\[
   D^*(N)=\sup_{k+\ell=N}\{\xi(k)+\eta(\ell)\}\asymp \eta(N),
\]
and
\[
   d^*(N)=\inf_{k+\ell=N}\{\xi(k)+\eta(\ell)\}\asymp \xi(N).
\]
Consequently the primal and dual democracy ratios obey
\[
   \mu_N\asymp \frac{D(N)}{d(N)}\lesssim \lambda_N\lesssim \log(N+1),
   \qquad
   \mu_N^*\asymp \frac{D^*(N)}{d^*(N)}\lesssim \log(N+1).
\]

\section{Lebesgue Constants}

We use the following standard Konyagin--Temlyakov estimate, included for
completeness in the 1-unconditional case.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:unconditional-lebesgue}
Let \((e_n)\) be a 1-unconditional basis with superdemocracy ratio
\[
  \mu_N=\sup_{\substack{|A|=|B|\le N\\ \eps,\delta}}
  \frac{\|{\bf 1}_{\eps A}\|}{\|{\bf 1}_{\delta B}\|}.
\]
Then \(L_N\le 1+\mu_N\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\Gamma\) be a greedy set of size \(N\) for \(x\), and let
\(z\) be supported on a set \(B\) with \(|B|\le N\).  Put
\[
   A=B\setminus \Gamma,\qquad C=\Gamma\setminus B.
\]
Then \(|A|\le |C|\).  Since
\[
   x-P_\Gamma x=P_{(B\cup\Gamma)^c}(x-z)+P_Ax,
\]
1-unconditionality gives
\[
   \|P_{(B\cup\Gamma)^c}(x-z)\|\le \|x-z\|.
\]
Let \(\alpha=\min_{n\in \Gamma}|e_n^*(x)|\).  The greedy choice gives
\(|e_n^*(x)|\le \alpha\) for \(n\in A\), while
\(|e_n^*(x-z)|=|e_n^*(x)|\ge \alpha\) for \(n\in C\).  By lattice
monotonicity and democracy, choosing \(C_0\subset C\) with
\(|C_0|=|A|\) when \(A\ne\emptyset\),
\[
   \|P_Ax\|\le \alpha\|{\bf 1}_A\|
       \le \mu_N\alpha\|{\bf 1}_{C_0}\|
       \le \mu_N\alpha\|{\bf 1}_C\|
       \le \mu_N\|P_C(x-z)\|
       \le \mu_N\|x-z\|.
\]
Taking the infimum over all such \(z\) proves the claim.
\end{proof}

Applying Lemma~\ref{lem:unconditional-lebesgue} to \(\mathcal B\) and to its
dual coordinate basis gives
\[
   L_N\lesssim \log(N+1),\qquad L_N^*\lesssim \log(N+1).
\]

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$\OT_N$}{OTN} Lower Bound}

Let \(H(N)=D^*(N)\).  From the previous section,
\[
   D(N)=\eta(N),\qquad H(N)\ge \eta(N).
\]
Also \(w_j=\eta(j)/j=\sqrt{\lambda_j/j}\) is decreasing.  Abel summation
therefore yields
\[
   T_N(D,D^*)=\sum_{j=1}^N w_j\,\Delta H(j)
      \ge \sum_{j=1}^N w_j\,\Delta\eta(j)
      =T_N(\eta,\eta).
\]
The other order is even more immediate:
\[
   T_N(D^*,D)=\sum_{j=1}^N \frac{H(j)}{j}\Delta\eta(j)
      \ge T_N(\eta,\eta).
\]
Thus \(\OT_N(D,D^*)\ge T_N(\eta,\eta)\).

It remains to estimate \(T_N(\eta,\eta)\).  Since
\[
   \eta(j)^2=j(2+\log j),
\]
the discrete difference satisfies
\[
   \Delta(\eta^2)(j)\asymp 2+\log j,\qquad j\ge 2.
\]
Moreover
\[
   \frac12\Delta(\eta^2)(j)
      \le \eta(j)\Delta\eta(j)
      \le \Delta(\eta^2)(j).
\]
Consequently
\[
   T_N(\eta,\eta)
      =\sum_{j=1}^N\frac{\eta(j)}{j}\Delta\eta(j)
      \asymp \sum_{j=2}^N\frac{2+\log j}{j}
      \asymp (\log N)^2.
\]
This proves Theorem~\ref{thm:counterexample}.

\section{Verification and Novelty Notes}

The construction is deterministic and uses no numerical computation.  The
source-paper ingredients used above are the definitions of \(D,D^*,T_N,\OT_N\),
the discrete Lorentz spaces \(\ell^1_{\widehat\omega}\), and the duality
\((\ell^1_{\widehat\omega})^*=m(\omega')\) for doubling weights with
\(\omega(n)/n\to0\).

Cheap run indexes were searched for arXiv:1707.07513, the title, greedy
Lebesgue constants, \(T_N(D,D^*)\), \(\OT_N(D,D^*)\), and democracy keywords.
The only nearby indexed results concern different greedy-basis questions.
External web/arXiv searches for exact \(\OT_N(D,D^*)\) phrases and the source
question found related greedy-basis literature but no answer to this necessity
question.  The novelty confidence is therefore medium-high, subject to expert
review of nearby greedy-basis folklore.

\section{Limitations and Human Review}

This is not a characterization of all systems satisfying Question 2.  It only
shows that the sufficient condition \(\OT_N(D,D^*)\lesssim\log(N+1)\) is not
necessary.  It also does not address Question 1: the present example still has
\(\OT_N(D,D^*)\lesssim L_N\log(N+1)\), so it is compatible with the authors'
observed behavior for Question 1.

Recommended human checks:
\begin{itemize}
  \item verify the dual coordinate-span calculation for the two Lorentz
  spaces and the \(\oplus_\infty/\oplus_1\) direct sum;
  \item verify Lemma~\ref{lem:unconditional-lebesgue} under the exact
  convention for \(L_N\) used in the source paper;
  \item check that no later paper has already recorded this direct-sum
  counterexample to the necessity of the \(\OT_N\)-condition.
\end{itemize}
