\section{Source question}

The question appears in C\'ecilia Lancien's thesis \cite{Lancien},
Section 9.16.3, page 148.  With $\gamma=(1\,2\,\cdots\,p)$ and Cayley distance
$d(\sigma,\tau)=p-\cyc(\sigma^{-1}\tau)$, it asks whether there are universal
constants $\theta,\kappa$ such that
\[
d(\id,\alpha)+d(\alpha,\gamma)=d(\id,\gamma)+2\delta
\]
implies the existence of a geodesic permutation $\alpha'$ satisfying
\[
d(\alpha,\alpha')=2\delta',\qquad \delta'\leq\theta\delta,
\]
with a choice map $\alpha\mapsto\alpha'$ having fibres at most
$p^{\kappa\delta}$.  The exact source passage is reproduced below.

\includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The interval $[\id,\gamma]$ is the set of noncrossing permutations in the
cyclic order $1,\ldots,p$; see, for example, Hall \cite[Theorem 3.1]{Hall}.

\section{Idea of the proof}

Keep the defect equal to one while making the obstruction larger.  On
$4m$ cyclically ordered points, take two families of $m$ nested chords.  Every
chord in the first family crosses every chord in the second, so their
intersection graph is $K_{m,m}$.  The corresponding fixed-point-free
involution has genus one for every $m$.

If a permutation is changed by $r$ transpositions, the relative permutation
is supported on at most $2r$ points.  Consequently at least $2m-2r$ of the
original chord transpositions remain literal cycles.  A noncrossing
permutation cannot retain chords from both sides of the $K_{m,m}$ crossing
pattern, hence it retains at most $m$.  Therefore $r\geq m/2$, although the
defect never exceeds one.

\section{Counterexample family}

For $m\geq1$, put $p=4m$, let $\gamma=(1\,2\,\cdots\,4m)$, and define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:family}
\alpha_m=
\prod_{i=1}^{m}(i,3m+1-i)
\prod_{j=1}^{m}(m+j,4m+1-j).
\end{equation}
The factors in \eqref{eq:family} are pairwise disjoint.  Call the first $m$
transpositions the $A$-family and the last $m$ the $B$-family.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
For every $m\geq1$, the permutation $\alpha_m\in\mathfrak S_{4m}$ has defect
$\delta=1$ relative to the canonical full cycle $\gamma$.  Moreover,
\[
\min_{\pi\in[\id,\gamma]}d(\alpha_m,\pi)
\geq \left\lceil\frac m2\right\rceil.
\]
In particular, no universal constants $\theta,\kappa$ with the property asked
for in the source question exist.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $\alpha_m$ is a product of $2m$ disjoint transpositions,
$\cyc(\alpha_m)=2m$.  Direct evaluation gives the disjoint-cycle
decomposition
\begin{align}\label{eq:complement}
\alpha_m\gamma={}&
\prod_{i=1}^{m-1}(i,3m-i)\,
(m,4m,3m,2m)\,
\prod_{j=1}^{m-1}(m+j,4m-j).
\end{align}
Thus $\cyc(\alpha_m\gamma)=2m-1$.  Since $\alpha_m$ is an involution,
$(\gamma^{-1}\alpha_m)^{-1}=\alpha_m\gamma$, and hence
$\cyc(\gamma^{-1}\alpha_m)=2m-1$.  It follows that
\[
\cyc(\alpha_m)+\cyc(\gamma^{-1}\alpha_m)
=4m-1=(4m)+1-2,
\]
which is exactly defect $\delta=1$.

Now fix $\pi\in[\id,\gamma]$ and write
$r=d(\alpha_m,\pi)$ and $\tau=\alpha_m^{-1}\pi$.  If the nontrivial cycles
of $\tau$ have lengths $\ell_1,\ldots,\ell_s$, then
\[
r=\sum_{q=1}^{s}(\ell_q-1),\qquad
|\operatorname{supp}(\tau)|=\sum_{q=1}^{s}\ell_q\leq2r,
\]
because $s\leq r$.  Every point belongs to exactly one transposition cycle of
$\alpha_m$.  Hence at most $2r$ of its $2m$ transposition cycles meet the
support of $\tau$.  If neither endpoint of such a transposition meets that
support, then $\pi=\alpha_m\tau$ agrees with $\alpha_m$ at both endpoints, so
that transposition remains a cycle of $\pi$.  Therefore $\pi$ contains at
least $2m-2r$ of the displayed transposition cycles.

Within each of the $A$- and $B$-families the chords are nested.  For every
$1\leq i,j\leq m$, however, their four endpoints occur in the order
\[
i<m+j<3m+1-i<4m+1-j,
\]
so the $i$th $A$-chord crosses the $j$th $B$-chord.  A geodesic permutation
$\pi\in[\id,\gamma]$ is noncrossing and therefore cannot contain a retained
$A$-transposition and a retained $B$-transposition simultaneously.  It
contains at most $m$ retained transpositions in total.  Consequently
\[
2m-2r\leq m,
\]
which gives $r\geq m/2$ and thus the stated integer lower bound.

Finally, if the source conclusion held, then for this defect-one family it
would give $d(\alpha_m,\alpha'_m)=2\delta'_m\leq2\theta$.  Choosing an integer
$m>4\theta$ contradicts the lower bound.  The proposed fibre estimate is
therefore moot: the required bounded-distance choice does not exist.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification}

The accompanying script \texttt{code/verify\_family.py} verifies
\eqref{eq:complement}, the defect, and all crossing assertions for
$1\leq m\leq50$.  It also exhaustively enumerates the two smallest cases:
for $m=1$ the nearest geodesic distance is $1$, and for $m=2$ it is $2$.
These computations only guard against indexing errors; Theorem
\ref{thm:main} is entirely symbolic.

\section{Scope and novelty check}

This disproves the exact full-cycle mapping question on page 148.  It is
strictly stronger than the separate two-cycle obstruction previously found
in this run.  A bounded search on 19 July 2026 used the exact source wording
and combinations of \emph{distance to noncrossing permutations}, \emph{genus
one permutation}, and \emph{Cayley distance}.  We also inspected
arXiv:1306.4628, arXiv:1502.07674, and arXiv:2110.00176.  No source explicitly
answering Lancien's question or stating this family was found.  Novelty is
therefore plausible but provisional pending expert bibliographic review.

\section{Human-review recommendation}
