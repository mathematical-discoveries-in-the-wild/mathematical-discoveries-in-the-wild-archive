# 2408.07827 — self-similarity forces uniformly block-natural centralizer generation

Status: `partial_result_likely_valid`; `human_review_needed`.

Source paper: W. Corrêa, S. Dantas, and D. L. Rodríguez-Vidanes,
*Twisted Hilbert spaces defined by bi-Lipschitz maps*, arXiv:2408.07827.

## Result

The source conjectures that, up to isomorphism, the Kalton–Peck space
`Z_2` is the only nontrivial self-similar twisted Hilbert space. It proves
this only for extensions generated by the scalar coordinate maps `Z(phi)`.

This packet proves a structural reduction for the full conjecture:

> Every self-similar exact sequence
> `0 -> ell_2 -> X -> ell_2 -> 0` is equivalent, as an exact sequence, to
> one generated by an exact `ell_infinity`-centralizer
> `omega:c_00 -> ell_2` with `omega(e_n)=0`.

It also proves a uniform strengthening. There is one constant, independent of
the normalized block basis `(u_n)`, for the canonical defect

`T_U omega(x) - omega(T_U x) + sum x_n omega(u_n)`.

Consequently the scalar indicator

`H(p) = <omega(sqrt(p)), sqrt(p)>`

satisfies a dimension-free approximate Shannon chain rule under every ordered
refinement `p_n -> (p_n q_(n,k))`.

Thus no genuinely non-centralizer quasilinear extension can be a
counterexample to the conjecture. The remaining problem is to classify the
self-similar centralizers on `ell_2`, equivalently to classify the resulting
ordered indicator cocycle without assuming permutation symmetry.

## Proof mechanism

The proof has two independent Baire-category upgrades.

1. Self-similarity for the signed singleton block bases
   `(epsilon_n e_n)` says that every sign-diagonal conjugate of a normalized
   quasilinear representative differs from it by a linear map plus a bounded
   map. Baire category makes the bounded errors uniform over the Cantor sign
   group. Averaging then produces an equivalent representative that commutes
   exactly with all sign diagonals. After the centralizer estimate is proved,
   a final bounded averaging over the full complex torus gives a fully exact
   representative in the complex case.

2. For each multiplier `a` in the unit ball of `ell_infinity`, use the
   two-coordinate block basis
   `u_n = a_n e_(2n-1) + sqrt(1-|a_n|^2)e_(2n)`.
   Self-similarity and exactness show that the commutator
   `a omega - omega a` is bounded for each `a`. A second Baire argument makes
   those bounds uniform in `a`, which is precisely the centralizer estimate.

3. A gliding-hump paste makes the singleton-subsequence constants uniform:
   otherwise arbitrarily bad finite commutators could be shifted into
   disjoint domain/range windows of one increasing injection. The same paste
   then gives one canonical-defect constant for all normalized block bases.
   Pairing the defect with the block sum yields the ordered chain rule.

## Scope and reviewer focus

This is a substantial reduction, not a full proof of the uniqueness
conjecture. It does not show that the resulting centralizer is symmetric or
equivalent to a scalar Kalton–Peck centralizer. Standard entropy-stability
theorems require approximate symmetry; the rule proved here is ordered because
successive block bases do not implement arbitrary coordinate permutations.

The most important points for expert review are:

- closure of the sets of diagonal conjugacies having bounded error at most
  `m`, where weak compactness of `ell_2` is used;
- the invariant-mean averaging of the associated possibly unbounded linear
  corrections on `c_00`;
- the relative diagonalization of the correction for a block basis; and
- the two gliding-hump pastes that uniformize increasing injections and then
  arbitrary block bases; and
- the second Baire argument, whose sublevel sets need not be closed but whose
  closures enlarge the bound by only the quasilinearity constant.
