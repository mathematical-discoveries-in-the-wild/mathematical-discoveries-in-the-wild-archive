# 1104.1451 countable tightness of P(K) and Maharam type

Status: `literature_already_answered` (relative-consistency negative answer under Jensen's `diamond`).

Source question: Krupski--Plebanek, arXiv:1104.1451, Section 4, asks whether countable tightness of `P(K)` implies that every measure in `P(K)` has countable Maharam type.

Supporting answer: Koszmider--Silber, arXiv:2312.02750, Theorem 1, assumes `diamond` and constructs a compact Hausdorff space `K` such that `P(K)` has countable tightness in the weak-star topology but `K` carries a Radon probability measure of uncountable Maharam type.

Scope: this rules out the ZFC implication relative to the consistency of `diamond`; it is not an absolute ZFC construction. The supporting paper notes that the additional set-theoretic assumption is necessary in the consistency sense, citing Fremlin's consistency result that such spaces do not exist.
