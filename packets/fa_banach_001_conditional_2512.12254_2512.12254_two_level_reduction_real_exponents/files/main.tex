\section*{Status}

This is a conditional reduction, not a full solution.  It proves that for
real exponents \(q>2\), every positive-support constrained extremizer has at
most two positive coefficient levels.  Therefore Conjecture 4.8 of
Brazitikos--Pandis is reduced, for \(q>5\), to a two-level Gamma inequality
stated below.  The earlier lane-5 packet already proves the conjecture for
\(0<q\le 5\).

\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Silouanos Brazitikos and Christos Pandis, \emph{Sharp
inequalities for symmetric polynomials, Hunter's conjecture, and moments of
exponential random variables}, arXiv:2512.12254.  Its Conjecture 4.8 asks:
if \(q>0\), \(a_i\ge0\), \(\sum_i a_i^2=1\), and \(X_i\) are independent
standard exponential random variables, must
\[
  \E(a_1X_1+\cdots+a_nX_n)^q
  \ge
  \min_{1\le m\le n}\rho(m,q),
  \qquad
  \rho(m,q)=
  \E\left(\frac{X_1+\cdots+X_m}{\sqrt m}\right)^q ?
\]

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

For non-integer \(q\), the moment is no longer a complete homogeneous
symmetric polynomial, but it is still a divided difference.  More precisely,
after dividing by \(\Gamma(q+1)\), the moment equals the divided difference of
\(x^{q+n-1}\) at the coefficient nodes.  The Lagrange multiplier equations
therefore have the same structural consequence as in the integer proof:
three distinct positive coefficient values would force two adjacent secant
slopes of a divided-difference gradient to be equal.  That is impossible
because divided differences of \(x^{q+n-1}\) of the relevant order are
strictly increasing in each node when \(q>2\).

Thus any remaining minimizer has only two coefficient levels.  The full
conjecture is then equivalent to checking a one-variable family
\[
  \frac{\E(xG_r+G_s)^q}{(rx^2+s)^{q/2}},
\]
where \(G_r,G_s\) are independent Gamma variables of integer shapes.  The
packet isolates this as the exact remaining dependency.

\section*{Divided-Difference Preliminaries}

For positive nodes \(b_1,\ldots,b_N\), including repeated nodes by the usual
Hermite convention, write
\[
  [b_1,\ldots,b_N]f
\]
for the divided difference of order \(N-1\).  We use two standard facts:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If \(f\in C^{N-1}\) and the nodes are positive, then repeated-node
divided differences are continuous limits of ordinary divided differences.
\item If \(f^{(N)}>0\) on \((0,\infty)\), then the divided difference
\([b_1,\ldots,b_N]f\) is strictly increasing in each node, unless the
corresponding variation is trivial.  This follows, for example, from the
Hermite--Genocchi formula.
\end{enumerate}

For \(q>0\) and \(a_i>0\), set
\[
  H_{n,q}(a_1,\ldots,a_n)
  =\frac{1}{\Gamma(q+1)}
    \E(a_1X_1+\cdots+a_nX_n)^q .
\]
The hypoexponential moment formula gives
\[
  H_{n,q}(a_1,\ldots,a_n)
  =[a_1,\ldots,a_n]\, f_{n,q},
  \qquad
  f_{n,q}(x)=x^{q+n-1}.
\]
Equivalently, this follows first for distinct \(a_i\) by partial fractions
and then for repeated nodes by continuity.

\section*{Extremizers Have At Most Two Levels}

\begin{lemma}
Let \(q>2\).  Fix a positive coordinate support of size \(n\).  Every
relative-interior local extremum of \(H_{n,q}\) under
\(\sum_{i=1}^n a_i^2=1\) has at most two distinct coefficient values.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(f=f_{n,q}\).  The derivative of a divided difference with respect to a
node is the divided difference with that node repeated:
\[
  \partial_i H_{n,q}(a)
  =[a_1,\ldots,a_i,a_i,\ldots,a_n]f .
\]
For \(i\ne j\), subtracting these two repeated-node divided differences gives
\[
  \frac{\partial_i H_{n,q}(a)-\partial_j H_{n,q}(a)}{a_i-a_j}
  =[a_1,\ldots,a_n,a_i,a_j]f . \tag{1}
\]
This identity is first checked for distinct nodes from the Newton divided
difference recursion and then extended to repeated nodes by continuity.

At a relative-interior constrained extremum there is a Lagrange multiplier
\(\lambda\) such that
\[
  \partial_i H_{n,q}(a)=2\lambda a_i,\qquad i=1,\ldots,n.
\]
Hence the quotient in (1) is equal to \(2\lambda\) for every pair of distinct
coefficient values.

Suppose, toward a contradiction, that three positive coefficient values
\(x<y<z\) occur.  Let \(Q_{uv}=[a_1,\ldots,a_n,u,v]f\).  The multiset defining
\(Q_{yz}\) is obtained from the multiset defining \(Q_{xy}\) by replacing one
extra copy of \(x\) with one extra copy of \(z\), leaving all other nodes the
same after sorting.  Since
\[
  f^{(n+2)}(t)
  =(q+n-1)(q+n-2)\cdots(q-2)t^{q-3}>0
\]
for \(t>0\), divided-difference monotonicity gives \(Q_{yz}>Q_{xy}\).
But the Lagrange equations give \(Q_{yz}=2\lambda=Q_{xy}\), a contradiction.
Thus at most two positive coefficient values can occur.
\end{proof}

\section*{Conditional Dependencies}

The remaining dependency is the following scalar two-level inequality.

\begin{lemma}[Two-level Gamma inequality, unproved]\label{lem:two-level}
Let \(q>5\), let \(r,s\ge1\) be integers, and let \(G_r,G_s\) be independent
Gamma random variables with shapes \(r,s\) and unit scale.  Then for every
\(x\ge0\),
\[
  \frac{\E(xG_r+G_s)^q}{(rx^2+s)^{q/2}}
  \ge
  \min_{1\le m\le r+s}\rho(m,q).
\]
\end{lemma}

This packet does not prove Lemma~\ref{lem:two-level}.  The numerical checks in
the code folder found no violation, but those checks are not proof.

\begin{theorem}[Conditional completion of Conjecture 4.8 for \(q>5\)]
Assume Lemma~\ref{lem:two-level}.  Then Conjecture 4.8 of arXiv:2512.12254
holds for every real \(q>5\).  Together with the earlier lane-5
\(0<q\le5\) packet, this would prove the conjecture for all \(q>0\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(q>5\), \(n\), and \(a_i\ge0\) with \(\sum_i a_i^2=1\).  By compactness,
the moment has a global minimizer on the positive orthant of the sphere.
Remove zero coordinates and work on its positive support, of size \(d\le n\).
By the preceding lemma, the positive coefficients of the minimizer have at
most two distinct values.  If there is only one value, the minimizer is an
equal-support vector and gives exactly \(\rho(d,q)\), which is at least
\(\min_{1\le m\le n}\rho(m,q)\).

If there are two values, write them as \(\alpha\) repeated \(r\) times and
\(\beta\) repeated \(s\) times, with \(r+s=d\).  If \(\beta>0\), set
\(x=\alpha/\beta\).  Since \(r\alpha^2+s\beta^2=1\),
\[
  \E(\alpha G_r+\beta G_s)^q
  =
  \frac{\E(xG_r+G_s)^q}{(rx^2+s)^{q/2}} .
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:two-level} gives this quantity at least
\(\min_{1\le m\le d}\rho(m,q)\), and this is at least the weaker ambient
lower bound \(\min_{1\le m\le n}\rho(m,q)\).  The cases \(x=0\) or
\(\beta=0\) are boundary equal-support cases and are included by continuity.
This proves the conditional claim.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The code folder contains two non-proof checks.

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{pair\_splitting\_scan.py} checks exact Gamma--Dirichlet quadrature slices
with one or two background coefficient blocks plus a split pair.  In the
recorded runs, no off-center pair-splitting minima were found.
\item \texttt{exact\_vector\_search.py} screens arbitrary vectors using the exact
hypoexponential divided-difference formula and validates the best candidates
with high-precision arithmetic.  The validated candidates found in the run
collapsed back to equal supports or stayed above the conjectured threshold.
\end{itemize}

These checks support the two-level dependency but do not establish it.

\section*{Novelty and Limitations}

Local run indexes on 2026-07-18 showed the previous lane-5 \(0<q\le5\)
partial packet and a serious full-push attempt for arXiv:2512.12254, but no
packet recording this divided-difference two-level reduction.  The adjacent
later paper arXiv:2602.03058, already recorded in the earlier packet, covers
the related Schur-concavity range \(0\le q\le4\), not this conditional
reduction.

No fresh external web search was performed for this conditional packet.  The
novelty confidence is therefore local-run confidence, not a literature-wide
claim.
