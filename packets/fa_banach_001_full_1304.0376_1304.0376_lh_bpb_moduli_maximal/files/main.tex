\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Chica--Kadets--Martin--Moreno-Pulido--Rambla-Barreno,
\emph{Bishop-Phelps-Bollobas moduli of a Banach space}, arXiv:1304.0376.
It proves maximal Bishop-Phelps-Bollobas moduli for many spaces, including
unital complex \(C^*\)-algebras with nontrivial center, and then asks whether
the same is true for \(L(H)\). The paper notes that its centralizer and
M-ideal criteria do not apply to \(L(H)\).

This packet gives a positive answer for every complex Hilbert space
\(H\) with \(\dim H\ge2\).

\section*{Definitions}

For a Banach space \(X\), write
\[
  \Pi(X)=\{(y,y^*)\in S_X\times S_{X^*}: y^*(y)=1\}.
\]
The Bishop-Phelps-Bollobas modulus \(\Phi_X(\delta)\) is the least radius
which approximates every pair \((x,x^*)\in B_X\times B_{X^*}\) satisfying
\(\re x^*(x)>1-\delta\) by some element of \(\Pi(X)\). The spherical
modulus \(\Phi_X^S(\delta)\) is defined in the same way with
\((x,x^*)\in S_X\times S_{X^*}\).

The source paper proves the universal upper bound
\[
  \Phi_X^S(\delta)\leq \Phi_X(\delta)\leq \sqrt{2\delta}
  \qquad (0<\delta<2).
\]
Therefore, to prove maximality it suffices to prove the matching lower
bound for \(\Phi_X^S\).

For unit vectors \(\xi,\eta\in H\), write
\[
  \omega_{\eta,\xi}(a)=\langle a\xi,\eta\rangle
  \qquad (a\in B(H)).
\]
When \(\eta=\xi\), write \(\omega_\xi=\omega_{\xi,\xi}\).

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(H\) be a complex Hilbert space with \(\dim H\ge2\), and let
\(X=L(H)=B(H)\) with the operator norm. Then
\[
  \Phi_X(\delta)=\Phi_X^S(\delta)=\sqrt{2\delta}
  \qquad (0<\delta\leq 1/2).
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

We use two elementary Hilbert-operator facts. For \(T\in B(H)\), let
\(a_2(T)\) denote the second approximation number,
\[
  a_2(T)=\inf\{\norm{T-F}: \rank F<2\}.
\]
In finite dimension this is the second singular value.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:simple-top}
Let \(0<\eps\le1\), let \(p\) be a rank-one orthogonal projection on \(H\),
and put
\[
  x=(1-\eps)I+\eps p .
\]
If \(y\in B(H)\), \(\norm{y}=1\), and \(\norm{x-y}<\eps\), then
\[
  a_2(y)<1.
\]
Consequently, \(1\) is a simple top singular value of \(y\): the spectral
projection of \(|y|=(y^*y)^{1/2}\) corresponding to the eigenvalue \(1\) is
rank one.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since \(x-p=(1-\eps)(I-p)\), we have
\[
  a_2(x)\leq \norm{x-p}=1-\eps .
\]
The approximation numbers are Lipschitz in the operator norm, hence
\[
  a_2(y)\leq a_2(x)+\norm{x-y}<1-\eps+\eps=1.
\]
As \(\norm{y}=1\), the strict inequality \(a_2(y)<1\) puts the norm of
\(y\) above the second approximation number. By the Hilbert-space min-max
characterization of approximation numbers, the singular values of \(y\)
above \(a_2(y)\) form a one-dimensional top singular space. Hence the
singular value \(1\) is attained and has multiplicity one.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:norming-rank-one}
Let \(y\in B(H)\) satisfy \(\norm{y}=1\) and \(a_2(y)<1\). If
\(\varphi\in B(H)^*\), \(\norm{\varphi}=1\), and \(\varphi(y)=1\), then
\(\varphi\) is a rank-one vector functional:
\[
  \varphi(a)=\omega_{\eta,\xi}(a)=\langle a\xi,\eta\rangle
  \qquad(a\in B(H))
\]
for unit vectors \(\xi,\eta\in H\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:simple-top}'s conclusion applied to \(y\), the spectral
projection \(e\) of \(|y|\) for the eigenvalue \(1\) has rank one. Let
\(e=\xi\otimes\xi\), \(\norm{\xi}=1\). Since \(a_2(y)<1\), there is
\(0\leq \beta<1\) such that
\[
  y^*y\leq e+\beta^2(I-e).
\]

Let \(|\varphi|\) be the right absolute value of \(\varphi\) in the polar
decomposition of functionals on the \(C^*\)-algebra \(B(H)\), so that the
C*-Cauchy-Schwarz inequality gives
\[
  1=|\varphi(y)|^2\leq |\varphi|(y^*y)\leq 1.
\]
Thus \(|\varphi|(y^*y)=1\). From the displayed bound on \(y^*y\),
\[
  1=|\varphi|(y^*y)\leq |\varphi|(e)+\beta^2|\varphi|(I-e)
  =\beta^2+(1-\beta^2)|\varphi|(e).
\]
Since \(\beta<1\), this forces \(|\varphi|(e)=1\), hence
\(|\varphi|(I-e)=0\). By Cauchy-Schwarz, \(|\varphi|\) vanishes on the
off-corner terms \((I-e)B(H)\) and \(B(H)(I-e)\). Therefore
\[
  |\varphi|(a)=|\varphi|(eae)=\langle a\xi,\xi\rangle
  \qquad(a\in B(H)).
\]

Again by C*-Cauchy-Schwarz,
\[
  |\varphi(a)|^2\leq |\varphi|(a^*a)=\norm{a\xi}^2 .
\]
So \(\varphi(a)\) depends only on the vector \(a\xi\), and defines a
norm-one functional on \(H\). Hence there is a unit vector \(\eta\in H\)
such that
\[
  \varphi(a)=\langle a\xi,\eta\rangle
  \qquad(a\in B(H)).
\]
The equality \(\varphi(y)=1\) then gives
\(\langle y\xi,\eta\rangle=1\), so necessarily \(\eta=y\xi\). In particular,
\(\varphi\) is rank one.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:bad-pair}
Let \(0<\eps\le1\). Choose orthonormal vectors \(\xi,\zeta\in H\), let
\(p=\xi\otimes\xi\), and put
\[
  x=(1-\eps)I+\eps p,\qquad
  \rho=(1-\eps/2)\omega_\xi+(\eps/2)\omega_\zeta .
\]
If \((y,\varphi)\in\Pi(B(H))\), then
\[
  \max\{\norm{x-y},\norm{\rho-\varphi}\}\geq\eps .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume \(\norm{x-y}<\eps\). By Lemma~\ref{lem:simple-top}, \(a_2(y)<1\).
By Lemma~\ref{lem:norming-rank-one}, the norming functional \(\varphi\) is
a rank-one vector functional.

The functional \(\rho\) is normal and is represented by the positive
trace-class operator
\[
  (1-\eps/2)\,\xi\otimes\xi+(\eps/2)\,\zeta\otimes\zeta,
\]
whose singular values are \(1-\eps/2,\eps/2,0,\ldots\). A rank-one
norm-one vector functional is represented by a trace-class operator with
singular values \(1,0,0,\ldots\). Since both functionals are normal, their
distance as elements of \(B(H)^*\) is the trace norm of the difference.
The Ky Fan--Mirsky singular-value inequality therefore gives
\[
  \norm{\rho-\varphi}
  \geq \left|(1-\eps/2)-1\right|+\left|\eps/2-0\right|
  =\eps .
\]
Thus whenever \(\norm{x-y}<\eps\), one has
\(\norm{\rho-\varphi}\geq\eps\). The lemma follows.
\end{proof}

We now prove the theorem. Fix \(0<\delta\leq1/2\), choose
\(0<\gamma<\delta\), and set \(\eps=\sqrt{2\gamma}\). Then
\(0<\eps\le1\). With \(x\) and \(\rho\) as in Lemma~\ref{lem:bad-pair},
we have
\[
  \norm{x}=1,\qquad \norm{\rho}=1,
\]
and, since \(\zeta\perp\xi\),
\[
  \rho(x)
  =(1-\eps/2)\cdot 1+(\eps/2)(1-\eps)
  =1-\eps^2/2
  =1-\gamma
  >1-\delta .
\]
Thus \((x,\rho)\in S_X\times S_{X^*}\) is an admissible spherical test pair
for \(\Phi_X^S(\delta)\). Lemma~\ref{lem:bad-pair} says that its
\(d_\infty\)-distance from \(\Pi(X)\) is at least \(\eps=\sqrt{2\gamma}\).
Hence
\[
  \Phi_X^S(\delta)\geq\sqrt{2\gamma}.
\]
Letting \(\gamma\uparrow\delta\), we get
\[
  \Phi_X^S(\delta)\geq\sqrt{2\delta}.
\]
The universal upper bound gives
\[
  \Phi_X^S(\delta)\leq\Phi_X(\delta)\leq\sqrt{2\delta}.
\]
Therefore both moduli equal \(\sqrt{2\delta}\).

\section*{What Changed From the \(M_2\) Packet}

The earlier \(M_2(\mathbb C)\) packet used
\(\operatorname{diag}(1-\eps,1)\). In higher dimension, the direct analogue
\(\operatorname{diag}(1-\eps,1,\ldots,1)\) has a large top singular face,
so norming functionals can spread over that face.

The present proof uses the reversed witness
\[
  (1-\eps)I+\eps p,
\]
whose top singular value is simple and separated from all other singular
values by exactly \(\eps\). This forces every sufficiently close
norm-attaining contraction to have a rank-one norming functional, reducing
the problem to the same trace-norm distance calculation as in the \(M_2\)
case.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

The proof depends on two standard facts that are worth checking carefully:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If \(a_2(y)<\norm{y}=1\), then \(1\) is a simple top singular value
of \(y\).
\item A norm-one functional \(\varphi\in B(H)^*\) with \(\varphi(y)=1\)
must be a vector functional when the top singular value of \(y\) is simple.
\end{enumerate}

Both facts are proved above, but they are the right places for a reviewer to
look first.
