\section*{Status}

This is a literature-status packet, not a new proof packet.  The two rigid
operator questions asked by Costakis, Manoussos and Parissis in
\cite[Question 2.22, p. 12]{CMP} are already answered negatively by Mazet and
Saias \cite[pp. 2--3]{MS}.

\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is G. Costakis, A. Manoussos and I. Parissis,
\emph{Recurrent Linear Operators}, arXiv:1301.1812.  In Question 2.22 on page
12 of the arXiv PDF, after proving that uniformly rigid operators are
invertible, the authors ask:

\begin{quote}
whether every rigid operator is invertible.  Failing that, is it true that if
$T$ is rigid and invertible then $T^{-1}$ is also rigid?
\end{quote}

\section*{Supporting Answer}

Mazet and Saias \cite{MS} explicitly identify the Costakis--Manoussos--Parissis
questions.  Their numbering refers to Question 2.20, while the local arXiv PDF
crop above labels the same passage Question 2.22.  On page 2 they restate:

\begin{quote}
Question 1. Are all rigid operators invertible?

Question 2. For every rigid and invertible operator, is its inverse rigid?
\end{quote}

They then state that the answer to both questions is negative and prove the
following theorem: for every $r\in[0,1]$, there exists a rigid block-diagonal
operator with spectrum
\[
  \{\lambda\in\C: r\leq |\lambda|\leq 1\}.
\]
For $r=0$ the operator is not invertible, giving a negative answer to the
first question.  For $0<r<1$, the operator is invertible, but the spectrum of
its inverse is
\[
  \{\lambda\in\C: 1\leq |\lambda|\leq r^{-1}\},
\]
so the inverse is not rigid because a rigid operator has spectral radius at
most one.  Mazet and Saias state this conclusion for the second question on
the top of page 3.

\section*{Scope}

This answers both parts of the source question.  It does not merely solve a
subcase: the source first asks whether all rigid operators are invertible, and
Mazet--Saias give a rigid non-invertible operator.  The source's fallback asks
whether an invertible rigid operator has rigid inverse, and Mazet--Saias give
invertible rigid operators whose inverses are not rigid.

\section*{Search Evidence}

The local run indexes were searched for \texttt{1301.1812}, \texttt{rigid
invertible}, \texttt{non-invertible rigid}, \texttt{Recurrent Linear
Operators}, and close variants.  Web/arXiv searches for the exact source
question and for \texttt{On the spectrum of rigid operators Mazet Saias}
located arXiv:2101.03751.  A later contextual paper, Saavedra and Stadlbauer
\cite[p. 2]{SS}, also records that Mazet and Saias constructed rigid operators
which are either non-invertible or have non-rigid inverse, providing negative
answers to two questions of Costakis--Manoussos--Parissis.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

No new mathematical claim is promoted here.  Human review should treat the
target as closed by the Mazet--Saias literature answer and use this packet to
avoid reworking the same source question.
