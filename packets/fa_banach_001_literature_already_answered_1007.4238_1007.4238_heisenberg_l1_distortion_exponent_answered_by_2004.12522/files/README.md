# Literature Answer: Heisenberg Ball `L_1` Distortion Exponent

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source/open-problem paper: Tim Austin, Assaf Naor, and Romain Tessera, *Sharp quantitative nonembeddability of the Heisenberg group into superreflexive Banach spaces*, arXiv:1007.4238.

Supporting answer paper: Assaf Naor and Robert Young, *Foliated corona decompositions*, arXiv:2004.12522.

## Question Identified

In Section 1.1, "Bi-Lipschitz distortion of balls", Austin--Naor--Tessera recall the Cheeger--Kleiner--Naor lower bound

```tex
c_{L_1}(B_R) \gtrsim (\log R)^c
```

for some universal `c > 0`, where `B_R` is the word ball of radius `R` in the 3-dimensional discrete Heisenberg group. They then state that evaluating the supremum over all such exponents `c` remains an important open problem.

## Answer Located

Naor--Young, *Foliated corona decompositions*, Theorem 1.6, gives the sharp asymptotic evaluation

```tex
c_{\ell_1}(B_n) \asymp (\log n)^{1/4}
```

for every integer `n >= 2`, where `B_n` is the word ball in the discrete 3-dimensional Heisenberg group. The paper explicitly says that Theorem 1.6 answers the asymptotic evaluation question for `c_{\ell_1}(B_n)` asked in the Cheeger--Kleiner--Naor/Lafforgue--Naor line of references, and notes that the earlier best lower bound was only `(log n)^\delta` for a small universal `\delta`.

Therefore the supremum exponent in the source question is exactly `1/4`.

## Scope Notes

This packet concerns the 3-dimensional discrete Heisenberg group used in arXiv:1007.4238. Higher-dimensional Heisenberg groups have a different `L_1` distortion exponent: Naor--Young's earlier 2017/2018 vertical-versus-horizontal result gives order `sqrt(log n)` for the 5-dimensional case. The source target is the 3-dimensional group generated by `a,b` with central commutator, so Theorem 1.6 of arXiv:2004.12522 is the decisive answer.
