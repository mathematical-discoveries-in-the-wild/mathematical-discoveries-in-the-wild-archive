\section*{Source and Open Problem}

The source is Rasila--Talponen, \emph{On Quasihyperbolic Geodesics in Banach Spaces}, arXiv:1301.0900 \cite{RT2014}.  The target signal is Conjecture 2.5 on page 7:

\begin{quote}
We conjecture that a strictly convex Banach space is reflexive if and only if each of its open half spaces is geodesic in the quasihyperbolic metric.
\end{quote}

The same page notes, without proof, that the preceding \(c_0\) half-space example can be modified so that the ambient Banach space is strictly convex.  The crop below records both the conjecture and that omitted-proof remark.

\section*{Result Status}

This packet proves the strict-convex \(c_0\) subcase announced in the source remark.  It is a partial result relative to Conjecture 2.5, not a proof of the full conjecture.  In particular, the argument uses the coordinate structure of \(c_0\) and does not show that every nonreflexive strictly convex Banach space has a non-geodesic half-space.

\section*{Definitions}

For a domain \(\Omega\) in a Banach space \(X\), the quasihyperbolic length of a rectifiable curve \(\gamma\) is
\[
  \ell_k(\gamma)=\int \frac{\|d\gamma\|}{\dist(\gamma,\partial\Omega)}.
\]
The domain is geodesic if every two points in it can be joined by a curve attaining the corresponding infimum.

Let \(w_n=2^{-n}\).  On \(c_0\) define
\[
  \N(x)^2=\|x\|_\infty^2+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}w_n|x_n|^2.
\]
This norm is equivalent to the usual \(c_0\)-norm and is strictly convex, because the positive quadratic summand is strictly convex and is added to a convex function.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The original \(c_0\) proof with the sup norm raises many far coordinates of an alleged geodesic.  Raising coordinates does not increase the sup-norm speed, but it increases the functional \(f\), hence increases the distance to the boundary of the half-space and lowers quasihyperbolic length.

The strict convex perturbation adds a weighted Hilbert term.  A single far-coordinate bump can increase this norm only by a quantity proportional to \(2^{-m}\), while it increases the denominator by a quantity proportional to \(f_m\).  Taking \(m\) with \(f_m/2^{-m}\) very large makes the denominator improvement dominate.  Thus every alleged geodesic can be shortened.

\begin{lemma}[one-coordinate speed estimate]\label{lem:speed}
Let \(\gamma:[0,1]\to c_0\) be Lipschitz for \(\|\cdot\|_\infty\), parametrized with constant sup-metric speed \(S>0\).  Fix \(m\).  Suppose \(\Gamma\) is obtained from \(\gamma\) by changing only the \(m\)-th coordinate, and the new \(m\)-th coordinate is also \(S\)-Lipschitz.  Then, for almost every \(t\),
\[
  \md_{\N}\Gamma(t)\le \md_{\N}\gamma(t)+S w_m.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The metric derivative exists almost everywhere for Lipschitz curves.  At almost every \(t\), the scalar derivatives of the relevant coordinate functions also exist.  Since \(\gamma\) has constant sup-metric speed \(S\), the modified curve has sup-metric derivative at most \(S\).  All weighted square-coordinate contributions agree with those of \(\gamma\), except possibly the \(m\)-th one, where the new scalar derivative has absolute value at most \(S\).  Hence
\[
  \md_{\N}\Gamma(t)^2\le \md_{\N}\gamma(t)^2+w_mS^2.
\]
Also \(\md_{\N}\gamma(t)\ge \md_{\infty}\gamma(t)=S\) almost everywhere.  Therefore
\[
  \md_{\N}\Gamma(t)
  \le \sqrt{\md_{\N}\gamma(t)^2+w_mS^2}
  \le \md_{\N}\gamma(t)+S w_m,
\]
using \(0<w_m\le 1\). 
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
There are an equivalent strictly convex norm \(\N\) on \(c_0\) and a continuous linear functional \(f\in (c_0,\N)^*\) such that the open half-space
\[
  \Omega=\{x\in c_0: f(x)>0\}
\]
is not geodesic in the quasihyperbolic metric induced by \(\N\).  More precisely, no two distinct points of \(\Omega\) are joined by a quasihyperbolic geodesic.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
  f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}f_nx_n,
\]
where \(f_n>0\), \(\sum_n f_n=1\), and \(f_n/w_n\) is unbounded.  For instance, after normalization one may take \(f_n=n^{-2}\).  Since \(\N(x)\ge \|x\|_\infty\), the functional \(f\) is continuous.  For \(x\in\Omega\),
\[
  \dist_{\N}(x,\partial\Omega)=\frac{f(x)}{\|f\|_{\N,*}},
\]
so the constant factor \(\|f\|_{\N,*}\) is irrelevant for comparing quasihyperbolic lengths.  It is enough to compare
\[
  L_f(\sigma)=\int_0^1\frac{\md_{\N}\sigma(t)}{f(\sigma(t))}\,dt.
\]

Assume toward a contradiction that a quasihyperbolic geodesic \(\gamma\) joins distinct points \(x,y\in\Omega\).  Reparametrize \(\gamma\) on \([0,1]\) by constant \(\|\cdot\|_\infty\)-arclength speed \(S>0\).  The image of \(\gamma\) is compact in \(\Omega\), so
\[
  a:=\min_t f(\gamma(t))>0,\qquad A:=\max_t f(\gamma(t))<\infty,
\]
and
\[
  C:=\int_0^1\frac{dt}{f(\gamma(t))}<\infty.
\]
Compactness of \(\gamma([0,1])\subset c_0\) gives uniform tail decay.  Choose \(m\) so large that
\[
  \sup_{t\in[0,1]}|\gamma_m(t)|<S/16,\qquad |x_m|<S/16,\qquad |y_m|<S/16,
\]
and also
\[
  \frac{f_m}{w_m}>\frac{16(A+S)^2C}{S}.
\]

Set
\[
  \alpha(t)=\min(x_m,y_m)+S\min(t,1-t)
\]
and define \(\Gamma\) by
\[
  \Gamma_n(t)=\gamma_n(t)\quad(n\ne m),\qquad
  \Gamma_m(t)=\max\{\gamma_m(t),\alpha(t)\}.
\]
Then \(\Gamma(0)=x\), \(\Gamma(1)=y\), and \(\Gamma(t)\in c_0\).  Moreover \(q(t):=\Gamma_m(t)-\gamma_m(t)\ge 0\), so \(\Gamma(t)\in\Omega\).  On \(I=[1/4,3/4]\), the tail choice yields
\[
  q(t)\ge S/8.
\]
Also \(q(t)\le S\) for all \(t\), after increasing \(m\) if necessary within the same tail condition.

By Lemma \ref{lem:speed},
\[
\begin{aligned}
L_f(\Gamma)-L_f(\gamma)
&\le
\int_0^1 \md_{\N}\gamma(t)
\left(\frac{1}{f(\gamma(t))+f_mq(t)}-\frac{1}{f(\gamma(t))}\right)\,dt
   +S w_m C .
\end{aligned}
\]
The first integrand is nonpositive everywhere.  On \(I\), using \(\md_{\N}\gamma(t)\ge S\), \(q(t)\ge S/8\), and \(f(\gamma(t))+f_mq(t)\le A+S\), it is at most
\[
  -\frac{S\cdot f_m(S/8)}{(A+S)^2}.
\]
Since \(|I|=1/2\), we get
\[
  L_f(\Gamma)-L_f(\gamma)
  \le S w_m C-\frac{f_mS^2}{16(A+S)^2}<0
\]
by the choice of \(m\).  Thus \(\Gamma\) has strictly smaller quasihyperbolic length than \(\gamma\), contradicting the assumption that \(\gamma\) was geodesic.

The endpoints \(x\ne y\) were arbitrary.  Hence no distinct pair of points in \(\Omega\) is joined by a quasihyperbolic geodesic.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof uses no numerical search.  The only analytic inputs beyond the source definitions are standard facts about metric derivatives of Lipschitz curves and the elementary formula
\(\dist(x,\ker f)=|f(x)|/\|f\|\) for a continuous nonzero linear functional.

The key inequality to review is Lemma \ref{lem:speed}; it is the place where the strict convex perturbation is controlled.  The argument changes a single far coordinate, so the extra Hilbert-square contribution is \(w_mS^2\), while the half-space denominator gain is \(f_mq(t)\) on a fixed middle interval.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This is not a full solution of Conjecture 2.5.  The proof depends on the coordinate lattice structure of \(c_0\) and on choosing \(f_m/2^{-m}\) unbounded.  It does not address arbitrary nonreflexive strictly convex Banach spaces.

Novelty confidence is intentionally modest: the source paper itself states the strict-convex modification without proof.  The contribution of this packet is to make that omitted argument explicit and reviewable.  I searched the run indexes for \texttt{1301.0900}, quasihyperbolic half-spaces, and the strict-convex/reflexive keywords, and checked later quasihyperbolic follow-up material including Rasila--Talponen--Zhang \cite{RTZ2017}.  I found no later local packet or obvious later paper settling the full conjecture; the 2017 follow-up still records a broader reflexivity-converse problem.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a partial packet.  The result is compatible with, but does not settle, Rasila--Talponen Conjecture 2.5.  The main review focus should be the metric-derivative estimate under the one-coordinate max modification and the strict inequality in the final length comparison.
