\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is S. V. Dzhenzher and V. Zh. Sakbaev, \emph{The law of large numbers for discrete generalized quantum channels}, arXiv:2504.10033.
Theorem~1 of the source proves, for \(1\le p\le2\), that if \(A,A_1,A_2,\ldots\) are bounded i.i.d. random pre-channel generators on \(\calT_p\), then
\[
  e^{A_1t/n}\cdots e^{A_nt/n}
  \longrightarrow
  e^{(\E A)t}
\]
almost surely in the strong operator topology of \(\calT_2\), uniformly for \(t\) in compact intervals.
Immediately after the theorem, the paper states:

\begin{quote}
It is interesting to obtain the LLN in SOT of \(\calT_p\) not \(\calT_2\).
This leads us to the following conjecture.

Under the assumptions of Theorem~1,
\[
e^{A_1t/n}\cdots e^{A_nt/n}
\]
converges almost surely in SOT of \(\calT_p\) to \(e^{\E A t}\) uniformly for \(t\) in any segment.
\end{quote}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The conjecture looks like it should require the geometry of \(\calT_p\), especially at the endpoint \(p=1\).  It does not.  The scaling \(t/n\) makes every factor a first-order perturbation of the identity, and a coarse block argument removes the noncommutativity.

Split the \(n\) factors into \(m\) consecutive blocks.  Inside one block, whose total time length is about \(t/m\), the product satisfies
\[
  \prod_{\text{block}} e^{tA_j/n}
  =
  I+\frac{t}{n}\sum_{\text{block}} A_j + O(m^{-2})
\]
uniformly in \(n\).  For each fixed block partition, the ordinary Banach-valued strong law gives
\[
  \frac1n\sum_{\text{block}} A_j \longrightarrow \frac1m \E A
\]
almost surely in the operator norm of \(\calL(\calT_p)\).  Thus every block is asymptotic to
\[
  I+\frac{t}{m}\E A+O(m^{-2}),
\]
or equivalently to \(e^{t\E A/m}\) up to \(O(m^{-2})\).  Multiplying the \(m\) blocks gives an \(O(m^{-1})\) total error, and then \(m\to\infty\).  This proves operator-norm convergence and therefore the desired strong operator convergence.

\section*{Banach-Algebra Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Random exponential product LLN in a Banach algebra]\label{thm:banach-algebra}
Let \(\calB\) be a unital Banach algebra.  Let
\[
  A,A_1,A_2,\ldots
\]
be i.i.d. \(\calB\)-valued random variables with
\[
  M:=\operatorname*{ess\,sup}\norm{A}<\infty,
  \qquad
  \bar A:=\E A.
\]
Assume \(A\) is Bochner measurable.  Then for every \(T>0\),
\[
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{
    e^{tA_1/n}\cdots e^{tA_n/n}-e^{t\bar A}
  }
  \longrightarrow 0
\]
almost surely.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The random variable \(A\) is bounded and Bochner measurable, so it takes values in a separable closed subspace of \(\calB\).  The Banach-valued strong law of large numbers gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:slln}
  \frac1N\sum_{j=1}^N A_j \longrightarrow \bar A
  \qquad\text{almost surely in }\calB.
\end{equation}
Work on an event of probability one where \eqref{eq:slln} holds.

We first record a deterministic estimate.  If \(b_1,\ldots,b_L\in\calB\), \(\norm{b_j}\le M\), and \(0\le hL\le T\), then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:block-first-order}
  \norm{
    e^{hb_1}\cdots e^{hb_L}
    -
    I-h\sum_{j=1}^L b_j
  }
  \le C_{M,T}(Lh)^2 .
\end{equation}
Indeed, write \(e^{hb_j}=I+u_j\).  Then \(\norm{u_j}\le C_M h\), and the product expansion gives
\[
  \norm{\prod_{j=1}^L(I+u_j)-I-\sum_{j=1}^L u_j}
  \le
  \exp\left(\sum_{j=1}^L\norm{u_j}\right)-1-\sum_{j=1}^L\norm{u_j}
  \le C_{M,T}(Lh)^2.
\]
Also \(\sum_j\norm{u_j-hb_j}\le C_M Lh^2\le C_{M,T}(Lh)^2\), after increasing \(C_{M,T}\).  This proves \eqref{eq:block-first-order}.

Fix \(m\in\N\).  Put \(N_r(n)=\lfloor rn/m\rfloor\), \(r=0,\ldots,m\), and let
\[
  B_{n,r}(t)
  :=
  e^{tA_{N_{r-1}(n)+1}/n}
  \cdots
  e^{tA_{N_r(n)}/n}
\]
be the \(r\)-th consecutive block, with the convention that an empty block is \(I\).  From \eqref{eq:slln},
\[
  \frac1n\sum_{j=N_{r-1}(n)+1}^{N_r(n)} A_j
  =
  \frac{N_r(n)}{n}\frac1{N_r(n)}\sum_{j=1}^{N_r(n)}A_j
  -
  \frac{N_{r-1}(n)}{n}\frac1{N_{r-1}(n)}\sum_{j=1}^{N_{r-1}(n)}A_j
  \longrightarrow
  \frac1m\bar A
\]
for each fixed \(r=1,\ldots,m\), with the obvious interpretation for \(N_{r-1}(n)=0\).
Using \eqref{eq:block-first-order}, uniformly for \(0\le t\le T\),
\begin{align*}
  \limsup_{n\to\infty}
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{B_{n,r}(t)-e^{t\bar A/m}}
  &\le
  \limsup_{n\to\infty}
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{
    B_{n,r}(t)-I-\frac{t}{n}\sum_{j=N_{r-1}(n)+1}^{N_r(n)}A_j
  } \\
  &\quad+
  \limsup_{n\to\infty}
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{
    \frac{t}{n}\sum_{j=N_{r-1}(n)+1}^{N_r(n)}A_j-\frac{t}{m}\bar A
  } \\
  &\quad+
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{I+\frac{t}{m}\bar A-e^{t\bar A/m}} \\
  &\le \frac{C_{M,T}}{m^2}.
\end{align*}
The middle term tends to zero uniformly in \(t\in[0,T]\), because it is \(t\) times a norm-convergent operator.

The full product is the product of these \(m\) blocks.  The block norms and the norms of \(e^{t\bar A/m}\) are bounded by constants depending only on \(M\) and \(T\).  A telescoping estimate therefore gives
\[
  \limsup_{n\to\infty}
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \norm{
    e^{tA_1/n}\cdots e^{tA_n/n}
    -
    \left(e^{t\bar A/m}\right)^m
  }
  \le \frac{C_{M,T}}{m}.
\]
Since \(\left(e^{t\bar A/m}\right)^m=e^{t\bar A}\), and \(m\) was arbitrary, the desired convergence follows by letting \(m\to\infty\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequence For The Source Conjecture}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:schatten}
Let \(1\le p\le2\).  In the setting of arXiv:2504.10033, if
\[
  A,A_1,A_2,\ldots:\Omega\to\calL(\calT_p)
\]
are bounded i.i.d. random pre-channel generators, then for every \(T>0\),
\[
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \left\|
    e^{A_1t/n}\cdots e^{A_nt/n}-e^{(\E A)t}
  \right\|_{\calL(\calT_p)}
  \longrightarrow0
\]
almost surely.  Consequently, for every \(x\in\calT_p\),
\[
  \sup_{0\le t\le T}
  \left\|
    \left(e^{A_1t/n}\cdots e^{A_nt/n}-e^{(\E A)t}\right)x
  \right\|_p
  \longrightarrow0
\]
almost surely.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:banach-algebra} to the unital Banach algebra
\(\calB=\calL(\calT_p)\).  The source assumes the random pre-channel generator is bounded, and its discrete probability-space hypothesis ensures measurability; the expectation used in the source is the Bochner expectation in \(\calL(\calT_p)\).  Operator-norm convergence immediately implies convergence in the strong operator topology of \(\calT_p\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The Banach-algebra theorem is not special to Schatten classes.  It applies to bounded i.i.d. generators in \(\calL(X)\) for any Banach space \(X\), provided the operator-valued random variable is Bochner measurable.  The source conjecture only asks for \(X=\calT_p\), \(1\le p\le2\).
\end{remark}
