\section*{Source question}

The source paper asks the following final problem.

\begin{quote}
Let \(X\) be a Banach space with a convex block homogeneous basis
\((e_i)\). Define
\[
    d_1=e_1,\qquad d_{i+1}=e_{i+1}-e_i\quad (i\geq 1).
\]
Is \(X\), endowed with the basis \((d_i)\), submultiplicative?
\end{quote}

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Problem 7 of arXiv:1711.01340 has a negative answer as printed.  In
\(\ell_2\), the usual unit vector basis is convex block homogeneous, but
the difference sequence
\[
    d_1=e_1,\qquad d_{i+1}=e_{i+1}-e_i
\]
does not satisfy any uniform submultiplicative estimate for finite
coordinate vectors.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The usual unit vector basis of \(\ell_2\) is convex block homogeneous:
every normalized block sequence is orthonormal, hence isometric to the
original basis.  In particular, every normalized convex block sequence
has the same property.

For a finitely supported scalar sequence \(a=(a_i)\), with \(a_i=0\)
after the support, one has
\[
 \left\|\sum_i a_i d_i\right\|_2^2
   =\sum_i |a_i-a_{i+1}|^2 .
\]
Fix \(N\geq 2\) and set
\[
a_i =
\begin{cases}
i/\sqrt N, & 1\leq i\leq N,\\
(2N-i)/\sqrt N, & N<i\leq 2N,\\
0, & i>2N .
\end{cases}
\]
Then
\[
 \left\|\sum_i a_i d_i\right\|_2^2
   = \frac{2N-1}{N}<2 .
\]
On the other hand, applying the same coordinate vector in both factors
would force control of
\[
 \left\|\sum_i a_i^2 d_i\right\|_2^2
  = \sum_i |a_i^2-a_{i+1}^2|^2 .
\]
Already the increasing side gives
\[
 \left\|\sum_i a_i^2 d_i\right\|_2^2
 \geq \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{i=1}^{N-1}\bigl((i+1)^2-i^2\bigr)^2
 = \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{i=1}^{N-1}(2i+1)^2 ,
\]
which tends to infinity as \(N\to\infty\).

Thus no constant \(C\) can satisfy
\[
 \left\|\sum_i a_i b_i d_i\right\|_2
 \leq C
 \left\|\sum_i a_i d_i\right\|_2
 \left\|\sum_i b_i d_i\right\|_2
\]
for all finitely supported coefficient vectors \(a,b\), since taking
\(b=a\) gives a bounded right-hand side and an unbounded left-hand side.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The argument answers the literal printed formulation.  If the intended
question silently assumes that \((d_i)\) is already a Schauder basis, or
imposes another additional hypothesis excluding the classical Hilbert
basis, this packet should be read as a wording counterexample rather than
as a solution of that strengthened variant.
\end{remark}
