\section*{Source and Question}

This packet concerns Question 5.6 from:

\begin{quote}\raggedright
S. Garcia-Ferreira and A. C. Hernandez-Soto,
\emph{Ramsey Property and Block Oscillation Stability on Normalized
Sequences in Banach Spaces}, arXiv:2009.03247.
\end{quote}

In the notation of the source paper, if $(x_i)$ is a normalized basic
sequence and $s\in FIN^*$, then
\[
  \Xblock(s)=
  \frac{\sum_{i\in s}x_i}{\norm{\sum_{i\in s}x_i}} .
\]
For a barrier $\cB$, the paper defines $\cB$-spreading models as the
single-barrier case of its block asymptotic models. On page 26 the authors
ask:

\begin{quote}
Question 5.6. Is every $\cB$-spreading model of $T$ isomorphic to
$\ell_1$? where $\cB$ is an arbitrary barrier.
\end{quote}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The obstacle in Question 5.6 is that the source paper proves a
$\cB$-spreading model is a spreading sequence, but the cited theorem about
Tsirelson space applies to spreading models actually generated by sequences
in $T$. The missing bridge is small: a barrier always contains an infinite
successive chain $s_1<s_2<\cdots$. Along such a chain the normalized block
vectors $\Xblock(s_n)$ form a normalized block sequence in the original
space, and the defining estimate for the $\cB$-spreading model is exactly
the ordinary Brunel--Sucheston estimate for this block sequence.

\section*{A Bridge Lemma}

\begin{lemma}
Let $\cB$ be a barrier on $\N$, let $(x_i)$ be a normalized basic sequence
in a Banach space $X$, and let $(y_i)$ be a $\cB$-spreading model of
$(x_i)$ in the sense of Garcia-Ferreira and Hernandez-Soto. Then there is
a normalized block sequence $(z_n)$ of $(x_i)$ such that $(z_n)$ generates
$(y_i)$ as an ordinary spreading model.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the barrier property, we may recursively choose an infinite chain
\[
  s_1<s_2<\cdots,\qquad s_n\in\cB .
\]
Indeed, after $s_1,\ldots,s_n$ have been chosen, apply the barrier
property to the infinite tail above $\max s_n$ and take the initial
segment supplied by $\cB$. Put
\[
  z_n=\Xblock(s_n) .
\]
Then $(z_n)$ is a normalized block sequence of the basic sequence $(x_i)$.

Let $(\varepsilon_m)$ be a null error sequence witnessing that $(y_i)$ is a
$\cB$-spreading model. Since $s_1<s_2<\cdots$, we have
$\min s_n\to\infty$. Define
\[
  \delta_n=\sup_{m\ge n}\varepsilon_{\min s_m}.
\]
Then $\delta_n\to0$.

Fix $k\in\N$, indices $k\le n_1<\cdots<n_k$, and scalars
$(a_i)_{i=1}^k\in[-1,1]^k$. Since $s_1<s_2<\cdots$ and $\min s_{n_1}\ge
n_1\ge k$, the family
\[
  \{s_{n_1},\ldots,s_{n_k}\}
\]
belongs to $Bl^k(\cB\upharpoonright_{\N/(k-1)})$, the admissible block
family appearing in the source definition. Therefore
\[
  \left|
    \norm{\sum_{i=1}^k a_i z_{n_i}}_X
    -
    \norm{\sum_{i=1}^k a_i y_i}
  \right|
  =
  \left|
    \norm{\sum_{i=1}^k a_i\Xblock(s_{n_i})}_X
    -
    \norm{\sum_{i=1}^k a_i y_i}
  \right|
  < \varepsilon_{\min s_{n_1}}
  \le \delta_{n_1}.
\]
This is precisely the usual spreading-model convergence condition for
$(z_n)$ with spreading model $(y_i)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}
Every $\cB$-spreading model of Tsirelson space $T$ is isomorphic to
$\ell_1$, for every barrier $\cB$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $(y_i)$ be a $\cB$-spreading model of $T$. By the lemma, $(y_i)$ is an
ordinary spreading model generated by a normalized block sequence in $T$.
The source paper recalls the standard theorem of Odell
\cite[Theorem 3.5(3)]{OdellStability} that every spreading model of
Tsirelson space $T$ is isomorphic to $\ell_1$. Hence $(y_i)$ is isomorphic
to the unit vector basis of $\ell_1$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof uses only the source definitions, the elementary recursive
selection of a successive chain inside a barrier, and Odell's theorem on
spreading models of Tsirelson space. The bridge lemma is insensitive to the
particular structure of $T$; for any Banach space $X$, every
$\cB$-spreading model of a normalized basic sequence in $X$ is an ordinary
spreading model of a normalized block sequence in $X$.

Before promotion, the run indexes were searched for the arXiv id
\texttt{2009.03247}, the title, \texttt{B-spreading model},
\texttt{block asymptotic model}, and \texttt{Tsirelson}. Web searches for
exact phrases around
\[
  \text{``Is every B-spreading model of T isomorphic to ell\_1''}
\]
and nearby variants found no explicit later answer. The proof should
therefore be reviewed as a short direct observation using a known theorem,
not as an independent reproving of the Tsirelson spreading-model theorem.
