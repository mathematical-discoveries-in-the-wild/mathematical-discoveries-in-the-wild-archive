\section*{Source and question}

The source paper is Charles-Philippe Diez, \emph{Free Stein kernels and moment maps},
arXiv:2410.02470. After recalling the Bercovici--Voiculescu failure of the
ordinary free Cramer theorem, the source asks whether a weak version might hold
inside the class of one-dimensional free Gibbs measures with strictly
\(C^2\) convex potentials:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=.96\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In the notation of the source, the conjecture says that if
\(\mu=\nu_\phi\) and \(\nu=\nu_\psi\) for strictly \(C^2\) convex
potentials \(\phi,\psi:\R\to\R\), and if \(\mu\boxplusfree\nu\) is
semicircular, then both \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) are semicircular.

\section*{Proof intuition}

Free additive convolution is linearized by the \(R\)-transform. The centered
semicircle of variance \(1\) has \(R(z)=z\). Thus, if one can realize two
nonsemicircular free Gibbs laws with
\[
  R_+(z)=\frac12z+a z^2,\qquad
  R_-(z)=\frac12z-a z^2,
\]
then their free convolution is exactly semicircular, while the nonzero third
free cumulants \(\pm a\) prevent either factor from being semicircular.

The only analytic issue is whether these tiny cubic cumulant perturbations are
free Gibbs laws for strictly convex \(C^2\) potentials. For small \(a\), they
are one-cut algebraic perturbations of the variance-\(1/2\) semicircle. Their
Hilbert transforms are \(C^1\)-close to the semicircular Hilbert transform, so
the associated equilibrium potentials are strictly convex after a harmless
extension outside the support.

\section*{The counterexample}

We use the convention that the Cauchy transform is
\[
  G_\mu(z)=\int_\R\frac{1}{z-x}\,\dd\mu(x),
  \qquad z\in\C^+,
\]
and that \(R_\mu\) is determined near zero by
\[
  z=\frac1{G_\mu(z)}+R_\mu(G_\mu(z)).
\]

\begin{lemma}[Small cubic one-cut perturbations]\label{lem:onecut}
For all sufficiently small real \(a\), there is a compactly supported
probability measure \(\mu_a\) whose \(R\)-transform is
\[
  R_a(w)=\frac12 w+a w^2.
\]
For \(a\ne0\), this measure is not semicircular. Moreover, for \(|a|\) small
the measure \(\mu_a\) has one interval of support, a positive real-analytic
density in the interior, square-root edge behavior, and boundary Cauchy
transform depending \(C^1\)-continuously on \(a\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Consider
\[
  K_a(w):=\frac1w+\frac12 w+a w^2.
\]
At \(a=0\), the inverse branch of \(K_0\) at infinity is the Cauchy transform
of the centered semicircle of variance \(1/2\). The two finite real branch
points are simple, coming from \(K_0'(w)=0\), and the corresponding density is
the usual one-cut square-root density.

For \(|a|\) small, the critical equation
\[
  K_a'(w)=-\frac1{w^2}+\frac12+2aw=0
\]
has two simple real critical points perturbing the two semicircular critical
points; the remaining critical point is far away and does not affect the
inverse branch normalized by \(G_a(z)\sim 1/z\) at infinity. The standard
algebraic one-cut perturbation argument gives a branch \(G_a\) solving
\[
  z=\frac1{G_a(z)}+\frac12 G_a(z)+aG_a(z)^2
\]
which maps \(\C^+\) into \(\C^-\) and satisfies \(zG_a(z)\to1\) at infinity.
By the Herglotz representation theorem, \(G_a\) is the Cauchy transform of a
compactly supported probability measure \(\mu_a\). Stieltjes inversion gives a
positive real-analytic one-cut density with square-root edges, and the
construction is analytic in \(a\) after rescaling the perturbed support to a
fixed interval.

The displayed identity is precisely the defining relation for
\(R_{\mu_a}(w)=w/2+aw^2\). If \(a\ne0\), the third free cumulant of \(\mu_a\)
is \(a\), so \(\mu_a\) is not semicircular.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Convex Gibbs realization]\label{lem:gibbs}
For all sufficiently small real \(a\), the law \(\mu_a\) from
Lemma~\ref{lem:onecut} is a free Gibbs/equilibrium measure
\(\nu_{\Phi_a}\) for some strictly \(C^2\) convex potential
\(\Phi_a:\R\to\R\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \([\alpha_a,\beta_a]\) be the support of \(\mu_a\), and write
\[
  H_a(x):=\PV\int_{\alpha_a}^{\beta_a}\frac{1}{x-y}\,\dd\mu_a(y)
       =\operatorname{Re}G_a(x+i0),
  \qquad x\in(\alpha_a,\beta_a).
\]
For \(a=0\), \(\mu_0\) is the variance-\(1/2\) semicircle and
\[
  2H_0(x)=2x
\]
on its support. By the \(C^1\)-continuous one-cut perturbation in
Lemma~\ref{lem:onecut}, after taking \(|a|\) small enough we have
\[
  \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\bigl(2H_a(x)\bigr)\ge 1
  \qquad\hbox{for }x\in(\alpha_a,\beta_a).
\]
Choose a \(C^1\) extension \(W_a:\R\to\R\) of \(2H_a\) such that
\[
  W_a'(x)\ge \frac12\quad\hbox{for all }x\in\R,
\]
and such that outside a compact neighborhood of the support the primitive grows
quadratically fast enough. Define
\[
  \Phi_a(x):=\int_0^x W_a(t)\,\dd t + C_a.
\]
Then \(\Phi_a\in C^2(\R)\) is strictly convex.

On the support of \(\mu_a\), the Euler-Lagrange equation for logarithmic
energy is
\[
  \Phi_a'(x)=2\,\PV\int\frac{1}{x-y}\,\dd\mu_a(y).
\]
Equivalently,
\[
  \Phi_a(x)-2\int\log|x-y|\,\dd\mu_a(y)
\]
is constant on \(\supp\mu_a\). By choosing the convex extension outside the
support sufficiently steep, the same expression is at least that constant off
the support. Hence \(\mu_a\) is the logarithmic equilibrium measure associated
with \(\Phi_a\). Strict convexity of \(\Phi_a\) gives uniqueness, so in the
source's notation \(\mu_a=\nu_{\Phi_a}\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
The weak free Cramer conjecture from arXiv:2410.02470 is false. There are
strictly \(C^2\) convex potentials \(\Phi_+\) and \(\Phi_-\) such that
\(\nu_{\Phi_+}\boxplusfree\nu_{\Phi_-}\) is semicircular, while neither
\(\nu_{\Phi_+}\) nor \(\nu_{\Phi_-}\) is semicircular.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(a\ne0\) sufficiently small for Lemmas~\ref{lem:onecut} and
\ref{lem:gibbs} to apply, and put
\[
  \mu_+=\mu_a,\qquad \mu_-=\mu_{-a}.
\]
By Lemma~\ref{lem:gibbs}, \(\mu_\pm\) are free Gibbs measures associated with
strictly \(C^2\) convex potentials. Their \(R\)-transforms satisfy
\[
  R_{\mu_+}(z)+R_{\mu_-}(z)
  =
  \left(\frac12z+a z^2\right)
  +
  \left(\frac12z-a z^2\right)
  =
  z.
\]
Since \(R\)-transforms add under free convolution, \(\mu_+\boxplusfree\mu_-\)
has \(R\)-transform \(z\), hence is the centered semicircular law of variance
\(1\). But the third free cumulants of \(\mu_+\) and \(\mu_-\) are \(a\) and
\(-a\), respectively, so neither factor is semicircular.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes}

The algebraic sanity check in
\texttt{code/verify\_cubic\_perturbation.py} numerically solves
\[
  aG^3+\frac12G^2-zG+1=0
\]
for a small value of \(a\), samples the boundary density, and estimates the
convexity margin of \(2\operatorname{Re}G(x+i0)\) on the support. This is not
a substitute for the analytic one-cut perturbation lemma, but it checks the
intended branch and the size of the convexity buffer.

\section*{Limitations and novelty check}

The packet is scoped only to the one-dimensional weak free Cramer conjecture
as stated in the source. It does not address the later multidimensional
free-transport conjectures or the proposed non-commutative Bakry-Emery
curvature criterion.

The bounded novelty check searched the run indexes for arXiv:2410.02470,
``Free Stein kernel'', ``free Cramer'', ``free Gibbs'', and ``semicircular''.
Web searches for the exact phrase ``Weak free Cramer's conjecture'' and for
``free Gibbs Cramer's theorem free convolution semicircular'' found the source
and the classical Bercovici--Voiculescu failure of free Cramer, but no public
answer to the strictly convex free-Gibbs variant in this bounded search.
