\section*{Status}

This is a likely valid partial result for the open problem in Lev--Tselishchev
\cite{LTGabor}.  It does not solve the arbitrary countable-index case.  It
isolates a necessary condition for any counterexample to the expected
nonexistence theorem.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper studies Gabor systems
\[
  \mathcal G(g;\LambdaSet)=\{e_s\tau_t g:(t,s)\in\LambdaSet\},\qquad
  (e_s\tau_t g)(x)=g(x-t)e^{2\pi i sx},
\]
in \(L^p(\R)\), \(p\ne2\).  For \(1<p<2\), Theorem 2.4 of
\cite{LTGabor} proves nonexistence of unconditional Gabor bases and
unconditional Schauder frames when \(\LambdaSet\subset \TT\times\R\) for a
uniformly discrete, more generally bounded-density, time set \(\TT\).  The
paper then leaves open the corresponding nonexistence question with no a
priori condition on \(\LambdaSet\) or \(g\).

\section*{Result}

For a countable \(\LambdaSet\subset\R^2\), write
\[
  \TT=\{t\in\R:\text{ there is }s\in\R\text{ with }(t,s)\in\LambdaSet\},
  \qquad
  \SSS_t=\{s:(t,s)\in\LambdaSet\}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Square-overlap obstruction]
\label{thm:square}
Let \(1<p<2\), let \(0\ne g\in L^p(\R)\), and let
\(\LambdaSet\subset\R^2\) be countable.  Suppose that
\[
  H_2(x):=\left(\sum_{t\in\TT}|g(x-t)|^2\right)^{1/2}
\]
is finite on a set of positive Lebesgue measure.  Then no choice of
coefficient functionals \(g^*_{t,s}\in (L^p(\R))^*\) can make
\[
  \{(e_s\tau_tg,g^*_{t,s})\}_{(t,s)\in\LambdaSet}
\]
an unconditional Schauder frame for \(L^p(\R)\).  Consequently no Gabor system
\(\mathcal G(g;\LambdaSet)\) satisfying this square-overlap hypothesis is an
unconditional basis for \(L^p(\R)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:necessary}
If an unconditional Gabor Schauder frame exists in \(L^p(\R)\), \(1<p<2\),
then for its time projection \(\TT\) one must have
\[
  \sum_{t\in\TT}|g(x-t)|^2=\infty
\]
almost everywhere on every measurable set of positive measure.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The proof keeps the coefficient-estimate half of the source argument and
changes only the final obstruction.  Unconditionality and cotype \(2\) give a
uniform bound from \(L^p(\R)\) into local \(L^2\)-valued coefficient functions
\((u_t f)_{t\in\TT}\).  If the translated copies of the window are
square-summable on a positive-measure set, then the frame expansion gives the
pointwise estimate
\[
  |f(x)|\le H_2(x)\left(\sum_t |u_t f(x)|^2\right)^{1/2}.
\]
On a subset where \(H_2\) is bounded, this would make the inclusion
\(L^p(E)\hookrightarrow L^2(E)\) bounded.  Since \(1<p<2\), that is impossible
on any positive-measure Lebesgue set.

\section*{Proof}

We use two standard ingredients that are also used in Sections 3 and 5 of
\cite{LTGabor}: unconditional Schauder frames have a uniform unconditional
constant, and \(L^p(\R)\), \(1<p<2\), has cotype \(2\).  We also use the
nonuniform Bessel estimate for exponentials stated as Lemma 3.7 in
\cite{LTGabor}.

\begin{lemma}[Block coefficient estimate]
\label{lem:block}
Assume that
\(\{(e_s\tau_tg,g^*_{t,s})\}_{(t,s)\in\LambdaSet}\) is a
\(K\)-unconditional Schauder frame for \(L^p(\R)\), \(1<p<2\), and normalize
\(\norm{g}_p=1\).  There are \(\delta>0\) and \(C<\infty\) such that, for
\[
  \SSS_{t,l}=\SSS_t\cap[l\delta,(l+1)\delta),\qquad l\in\mathbb Z,
\]
every \(f\in L^p(\R)\) satisfies
\[
 \left(\sum_{t\in\TT}\sum_{l\in\mathbb Z}
   \left(\sum_{s\in\SSS_{t,l}} |g^*_{t,s}(f)|\right)^2
 \right)^{1/2}
 \le C\norm{f}_p .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(\delta>0\) so small that
\[
  \norm{(e_a-1)g}_p<1/2,\qquad |a|<\delta,
\]
which is possible by dominated convergence.  For finite collections of
time-frequency blocks, set \(c_{t,s}=g^*_{t,s}(f)\) and
\[
  y_{t,l}=\sum_{s\in\SSS_{t,l}}
    |c_{t,s}|e^{-2\pi i st}e_s\tau_tg .
\]
The unconditional frame estimate, applied with scalar multipliers of modulus
at most one, gives
\[
  \mathbb E\norm{\sum_{t,l}\varepsilon_{t,l}y_{t,l}}_p
  \le K\norm{f}_p .
\]
Cotype \(2\) therefore yields
\[
  \left(\sum_{t,l}\norm{y_{t,l}}_p^2\right)^{1/2}
  \le C_pK\norm{f}_p .
\]
If \(\SSS_{t,l}\ne\varnothing\), choose \(s_{t,l}\in\SSS_{t,l}\).  For
\(s\in\SSS_{t,l}\),
\[
 \norm{e^{-2\pi i st}e_s\tau_tg
      -e^{-2\pi i s_{t,l}t}e_{s_{t,l}}\tau_tg}_p
 =\norm{(e_{s-s_{t,l}}-1)g}_p<1/2.
\]
By the triangle inequality,
\[
  \norm{y_{t,l}}_p
  \ge \frac12\sum_{s\in\SSS_{t,l}} |c_{t,s}|.
\]
Combining the last two displays and passing from finite subcollections to the
countable family proves the estimate.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Local \(L^2\) analysis estimate]
\label{lem:local}
Under the hypotheses of Lemma \ref{lem:block}, for each \(t\in\TT\) define
\[
  (u_t f)(x)=\sum_{s\in\SSS_t}g^*_{t,s}(f)e_s(x).
\]
Then the series converges unconditionally in \(L^2(I)\) for every unit interval
\(I\), and there is \(C_I<\infty\), independent of \(f\), such that
\[
  \sum_{t\in\TT}\norm{u_tf}_{L^2(I)}^2\le C_I\norm{f}_p^2 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Apply the nonuniform exponential Bessel estimate of \cite[Lemma 3.7]{LTGabor}
to each frequency set \(\SSS_t\), using the same \(\delta\) as in Lemma
\ref{lem:block}.  For every unit interval \(I\),
\[
  \norm{u_tf}_{L^2(I)}
  \le C(\delta)
  \left(\sum_{l\in\mathbb Z}
    \left(\sum_{s\in\SSS_{t,l}} |g^*_{t,s}(f)|\right)^2
  \right)^{1/2}.
\]
Squaring, summing in \(t\), and invoking Lemma \ref{lem:block} gives the
claim.
\end{proof}

We now prove Theorem \ref{thm:square}.  Suppose that an unconditional
Schauder frame as above exists.  Since \(H_2<\infty\) on a set of positive
measure, there are a bounded interval \(I\), a measurable set \(E\subset I\)
with \(m(E)>0\), and a finite constant \(M\) such that \(H_2(x)\le M\) for
almost every \(x\in E\).

Let \(f\in L^p(\R)\) be supported on \(E\).  The unconditional frame expansion
may be grouped first by time:
\[
  f=\sum_{t\in\TT}\sum_{s\in\SSS_t}g^*_{t,s}(f)e_s\tau_tg .
\]
For each fixed \(t\), the inner subseries agrees locally a.e. with
\[
  g(x-t)(u_tf)(x).
\]
By Lemma \ref{lem:local}, \((u_tf(x))_{t\in\TT}\in\ell^2(\TT)\) for a.e.
\(x\in I\).  On \(E\), the vector \((g(x-t))_{t\in\TT}\) is also in
\(\ell^2(\TT)\).  Hence the grouped time series is absolutely convergent for
a.e. \(x\in E\).  Passing to pointwise convergent subsequences of the
unconditional partial sums identifies its value with \(f(x)\), and Cauchy's
inequality gives
\[
  |f(x)|
  \le \sum_{t\in\TT}|g(x-t)|\,|u_tf(x)|
  \le H_2(x)\left(\sum_{t\in\TT}|u_tf(x)|^2\right)^{1/2}.
\]
Consequently Lemma \ref{lem:local} gives
\[
  \norm{f}_{L^2(E)}^2
  \le M^2\int_I\sum_{t\in\TT}|u_tf(x)|^2\,dx
  \le M^2 C_I\norm{f}_p^2 .
\]
Thus every \(L^p\)-function supported on \(E\) would belong to \(L^2(E)\), with
a uniform bound \(\norm{f}_{2}\lesssim\norm{f}_{p}\).  This is impossible for
\(1<p<2\) on a positive-measure Lebesgue set: choose measurable subsets
\(E_n\subset E\) with \(0<m(E_n)\to0\), and put
\[
  f_n=m(E_n)^{-1/p}{\bf 1}_{E_n}.
\]
Then \(\norm{f_n}_p=1\) but
\(\norm{f_n}_2=m(E_n)^{1/2-1/p}\to\infty\).  The contradiction proves the
theorem.  The assertion about unconditional bases follows because an
unconditional basis induces an unconditional Schauder frame with its coordinate
functionals.

\section*{Relation to the Source Theorem}

If \(\TT\) has bounded density, then for every bounded interval \(I\),
\[
  \int_I\sum_{t\in\TT}|g(x-t)|^p\,dx<\infty.
\]
Hence \((g(x-t))_{t\in\TT}\in\ell^p(\TT)\subset\ell^2(\TT)\) for a.e.
\(x\in I\).  Theorem \ref{thm:square} therefore recovers the \(1<p<2\)
bounded-density frame nonexistence theorem of \cite{LTGabor}.  The present
formulation additionally covers arbitrary countable time projections whenever
their translated-window square overlap is finite on a set of positive measure.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This packet does not rule out the remaining dense-overlap case
\[
  \sum_{t\in\TT}|g(x-t)|^2=\infty\quad\text{a.e.}
\]
That is the exact surviving obstruction after this argument.  The source paper
contains the coefficient estimates used above but states the \(1<p<2\)
nonexistence theorem under bounded-density hypotheses.  A bounded search of
the run indexes and web literature for the exact Gabor \(L^p\) open problem,
the source arXiv id, and the related exponential-frame paper
\cite{LTExp} found no later full solution of the arbitrary countable Gabor
case.  Novelty confidence is therefore moderate: the result is best viewed as
an extracted and sharpened partial obstruction from the source method, not as
an independent full solution.
