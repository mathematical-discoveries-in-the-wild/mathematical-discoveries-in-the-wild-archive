\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Sofiya Ostrovska and Mikhail I. Ostrovskii,
\emph{Distortion in the finite determination result for embeddings of locally
finite metric spaces into Banach spaces}, arXiv:1510.05974.  It defines
\(D(X)\le \alpha\) to mean that whenever all finite subsets of a locally finite
metric space \(M\) embed into \(X\) with distortion at most \(C\), the whole
space \(M\) embeds into \(X\) with distortion at most \(\alpha C\).  Its final
problem asks:

\begin{quote}
Problem 5.1. Do there exist Banach spaces \(X\) with \(D(X)>1^+\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
For every \(1\le p<\infty\),
\[
  D(L_p[0,1])=1.
\]
Equivalently, if \(M\) is locally finite and all finite subsets of \(M\)
embed into \(L_p[0,1]\) with distortion at most \(C\), then \(M\) itself
embeds into \(L_p[0,1]\) with distortion at most \(C\).
\end{theorem}

This is a partial negative answer to the source problem: the classical
nonatomic spaces \(L_p[0,1]\), \(1\le p<\infty\), cannot witness a strict
finite-determination gap.  The existence of some other Banach space
\(X\) with \(D(X)>1^+\) remains open.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The usual finite-determination compactness argument first embeds \(M\) into
an ultrapower of \(L_p[0,1]\) with the same distortion \(C\).  The point is
that, for classical \(L_p[0,1]\), this ultrapower detour loses no metric
information: ultrapowers of \(L_p\)-spaces are again \(L_p\)-spaces, and the
countable image of a locally finite metric space spans only a separable
subspace.  That separable subspace is contained in a separable \(L_p\)-space,
and every separable \(L_p\)-space over a finite measure algebra embeds
linearly isometrically into \(L_p[0,1]\).  Composing the ultrapower embedding
with this linear isometry returns the metric copy to \(L_p[0,1]\) without
changing the distortion.

\section*{Proof}

Let \(X=L_p[0,1]\), \(1\le p<\infty\).  Let \(M\) be locally finite and suppose
that all finite subsets of \(M\) admit bilipschitz embeddings into \(X\) with
distortion at most \(C\).  Fix a base point \(o\in M\).  Since \(M\) is locally
finite, \(M=\bigcup_{n\ge 1} B(o,n)\) is countable.

Let \(\mathcal F\) be the directed set of finite subsets of \(M\) containing
\(o\), and choose an ultrafilter \(\U\) on \(\mathcal F\) containing every tail
\[
  \{F\in\mathcal F: E\subseteq F\},
\]
where \(E\subseteq M\) is finite.  For each \(F\in\mathcal F\), choose an
embedding \(f_F:F\to X\), translate it so that \(f_F(o)=0\), and rescale it so
that
\[
  d(u,v)\le \|f_F(u)-f_F(v)\|_X \le C d(u,v)
  \quad (u,v\in F).
\]
For \(x\notin F\), set \(f_F(x)=0\).  For each fixed \(x\in M\), the family
\((f_F(x))_{F\in\mathcal F}\) is bounded, because on the \(\U\)-large set
\(\{F:x\in F\}\) its norm is at most \(C d(x,o)\), and elsewhere it is zero.
Hence
\[
  f(x)=[(f_F(x))_{F\in\mathcal F}]_{\U}
\]
defines a map \(f:M\to X^\U\).  For any \(x,y\in M\), the set of indices
\(F\) containing \(\{o,x,y\}\) is \(\U\)-large, so
\[
  d(x,y)\le \|f(x)-f(y)\|_{X^\U}\le C d(x,y).        \tag{1}
\]

It remains to bring this countable subset of \(X^\U\) back into \(X\)
isometrically.  By the standard Kakutani--Heinrich representation theorem for
ultraproducts of \(L_p\)-spaces, \(X^\U\) is itself an \(L_p\)-space over a
finite measure algebra.  Let
\[
  E=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\, f(M)\subseteq X^\U .
\]
This is separable.  In an \(L_p(\Omega,\Sigma,\mu)\) representation of
\(X^\U\), choose a countable dense subset of \(E\).  The sigma-algebra
generated by these countably many functions is countably generated, and the
corresponding closed sublattice \(L_p(\Sigma_0)\) is a separable \(L_p\)-space
containing \(E\).

Every separable \(L_p\)-space over a finite measure algebra decomposes as the
\(p\)-sum of a nonatomic part and at most countably many atoms.  Such a space
embeds linearly isometrically into \(L_p[0,1]\): realize the nonatomic part on
a subinterval and realize the countably many atoms on pairwise disjoint
intervals of the appropriate measures.  Thus there is a linear isometry
\[
  J:L_p(\Sigma_0)\longrightarrow L_p[0,1].
\]
Composing \(f\) with \(J\) and using (1) gives
\[
  d(x,y)\le \|Jf(x)-Jf(y)\|_{L_p[0,1]}\le C d(x,y)
  \quad (x,y\in M).
\]
Therefore \(M\) embeds into \(L_p[0,1]\) with distortion at most \(C\).  Since
the definition has \(\alpha\ge 1\), this proves \(D(L_p[0,1])=1\).

\section*{Verification and Limitations}

The proof uses only standard facts about \(L_p\)-spaces: ultraproducts of
\(L_p\)-spaces are \(L_p\)-spaces, and separable finite-measure \(L_p\)-spaces
embed linearly isometrically into \(L_p[0,1]\).  No computational verification
is involved.

This does not solve Problem 5.1 in full.  It rules out the classical nonatomic
\(L_p[0,1]\) spaces as witnesses for a strict gap, but it does not address
arbitrary Banach spaces, nor spaces such as \(\ell_p\) where the source paper
already proves \(D\le 1^+\) by a different argument.

\section*{Novelty Check}

The local run indexes contained a previous unfinished attempt for arXiv:1510.05974
but no packet for this \(L_p[0,1]\) subcase.  Searches of the local source
corpus and web queries combining \(D(X)>1^+\), \(L_p\), finite determination,
and locally finite metric spaces did not reveal an explicit later answer.
Catrina--Ostrovskii's 2023 paper
improves the general finite-determination constant to \(3+\eps\) and restates
the \(1+\eps\) problem, but does not record the constant-one pullback for
classical \(L_p[0,1]\).
