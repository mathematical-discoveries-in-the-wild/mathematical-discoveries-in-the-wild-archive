\section*{Status}

This is a \texttt{literature\_implied\_answer (partial subcase)} packet. It
does not claim a new proof of Pisier's general analyticity conjecture. It
records that a later theorem of Arhancet \cite{Arhancet2018} gives a stronger

\cite{Arhancet2014}.

\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper recalls Pisier's conjecture as follows. If $X$ is a
K-convex Banach space and $(T_t)_{t\ge 0}$ is a weak-star continuous semigroup
of selfadjoint contractive operators on $L^\infty(\Omega)$, where $\Omega$ is
a measure space, then the conjecture predicts that analyticity of the scalar
$L^p(\Omega)$ semigroup is preserved after tensoring with $\Id_X$ on the
Bochner space $\Lp(\Omega,X)$.

The source paper gives partial positive results for Fourier multipliers on

partial route.

\section*{Supporting Literature}

The supporting paper arXiv:1807.00875 introduces the following condition:
a Banach space $X$ is $K'$-convex if it is isomorphic to a Banach space $E$
with an operator space structure such that the vector-valued Schatten space
$S^p(E)$ is K-convex. In operator-space language, $E$ is OK-convex. The paper
notes that every $K'$-convex space is K-convex, and conjectures that the two
classes coincide.

Its Corollary 14 gives the locally compact abelian Fourier-multiplier subcase.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The source conjecture asks whether tensoring by $\Id_X$ preserves bounded
analyticity for selfadjoint contractive semigroups when $X$ is K-convex. The
2018 theorem proves this conclusion for a structured but substantial subcase:
the underlying measure space is a second-countable locally compact abelian
group, the semigroup consists of Fourier multipliers, and $X$ satisfies the
stronger $K'$-convex/OK-convex realization hypothesis. Because $K'$-convexity
implies K-convexity, this is a genuine subcase of the source conjecture, not a
change of target.

\begin{theorem}
Let $G$ be a second-countable abelian locally compact group, let
$1<p<\infty$, and let $X$ be a $K'$-convex Banach space. Suppose
$(T_t)_{t\ge 0}$ is a weak-star continuous semigroup of selfadjoint
contractive Fourier multipliers on $L^\infty(G)$. Then
$(T_t\otimes \Id_X)_{t\ge 0}$ is a strongly continuous bounded analytic
semigroup of contractions on $\Lp(G,X)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is exactly Corollary 14 of Arhancet \cite{Arhancet2018}. The hypotheses
there are: $G$ is second countable, abelian, and locally compact;
$(T_t)_{t\ge0}$ is weak-star continuous and consists of selfadjoint
contractive Fourier multipliers on $L^\infty(G)$; $X$ is $K'$-convex; and
$1<p<\infty$. The conclusion is precisely that the tensorized semigroup
$(T_t\otimes \Id_X)_{t\ge0}$ induces a strongly continuous bounded analytic
semigroup of contractions on the Bochner space $\Lp(G,X)$.

Definition 12 and the following paragraph in \cite{Arhancet2018} explain that
$K'$-convexity means that $X$ is isomorphic to a Banach space with an
OK-convex operator space structure, and that such spaces are K-convex. Hence
the theorem is a positive instance of Pisier's K-convex analyticity
prediction, restricted to this Fourier-multiplier/locally compact abelian
setting.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Limitations}

No computational verification is involved. The verification is the direct
identification between the source conjecture and the supporting corollary:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $L^\infty(G)$ is a scalar commutative von Neumann algebra attached to
  the Haar-measure space $G$;
  \item Fourier multipliers in Corollary 14 are a subclass of the selfadjoint
  contractive operators appearing in the source conjecture;
  \item $K'$-convexity is a sufficient extra hypothesis implying K-convexity;
  \item the conclusion is exactly bounded analyticity after tensoring with
  $\Id_X$.
\end{itemize}

The result does not solve Pisier's general conjecture for arbitrary measure
spaces, arbitrary selfadjoint contractive semigroups, or all K-convex target
spaces. The supporting paper explicitly keeps the equality of K-convex and
$K'$-convex spaces as a conjecture.
