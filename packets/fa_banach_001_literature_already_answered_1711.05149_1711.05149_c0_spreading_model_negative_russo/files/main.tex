\section*{Source question}

Hajek, Kania, and Russo's paper \emph{Symmetrically separated sequences in
the unit sphere of a Banach space} asks the following question about
spreading models and the symmetric Kottman constant.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\caption{Problem 5.11 in arXiv:1711.05149, page 17.}
\end{figure}

The question is whether
\[
  X \hbox{ admits a spreading model isomorphic to } \cnull
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \Ks(X)=2.
\]

\section*{Proof intuition}

The source problem is tempting because \(\cnull\) itself has symmetrically
\(2\)-separated unit vectors built from long initial-support patterns.  A
spreading model, however, preserves fixed finite patterns asymptotically; it
does not automatically transfer constructions whose support length grows with
the index.  Russo makes this obstruction concrete with the original
Tsirelson spaces.  Their spreading models are all isomorphic to \(\cnull\),
but the asymptotic \(\cnull\) structure still imposes an upper bound on
ordinary separated sequences.

\section*{The literature answer}

\begin{theorem}
Problem 5.11 of \cite{HKR} has a negative answer.  More precisely, for every
\(\varepsilon\in(0,1)\) there is a Banach space \(X\) such that every spreading
model of \(X\) is isomorphic to \(\cnull\), but \(\Ks(X)<2\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Russo's Theorem B \cite{Russo} states that for every \(\varepsilon>0\) there
exists a Banach space \(X\), all of whose spreading models are isomorphic to
\(\cnull\), such that \(K(X)\leq 1+\varepsilon\).  Since symmetric
\(\sigma\)-separation implies ordinary \(\sigma\)-separation, one always has
\[
  \Ks(X)\leq K(X).
\]
Choosing \(\varepsilon\in(0,1)\) gives
\[
  \Ks(X)\leq K(X)\leq 1+\varepsilon<2,
\]
while every spreading model of \(X\) is isomorphic to \(\cnull\).  This is
exactly the negation of the conclusion asked in Problem 5.11.

For a concrete family, Russo's Theorem 4.2 computes the constants of the
original Tsirelson space \(T^*_\theta\):
\[
  K(T^*_\theta)=\Ks(T^*_\theta)=
  \begin{cases}
    2, & 0<\theta\leq 1/2,\\
    1/\theta, & 1/2<\theta<1.
  \end{cases}
\]
The same section recalls that every spreading model of \(T^*_\theta\) is
isomorphic to \(\cnull\).  Hence any \(\theta\in(1/2,1)\) gives
\(\Ks(T^*_\theta)=1/\theta<2\), and \(\theta\) close to \(1\) gives Russo's
arbitrarily small upper bound \(K(T^*_\theta)\leq 1+\varepsilon\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes and limitations}

This packet is a literature-status identification, not a new proof.  The
supporting paper explicitly cites the source problem as \cite[Problem
5.11]{HKR} and states that the answer is negative.  The inequality
\(\Ks(X)\leq K(X)\) is immediate from the definitions: symmetric separation
includes separation for differences.

The result answers only the \(\cnull\) spreading-model question, Problem 5.11.
It does not settle the broader Problem 5.12 in \cite{HKR}, which asks whether
\(\Ks(X)\geq \Ks(Z)\) for arbitrary spreading models \(Z\) of \(X\).

The run lightweight indexes were searched for arXiv:1711.05149, the title,
and the key phrases ``spreading model'', ``\(c_0\)'', and ``Kottman'' before
promotion.  No existing exact packet or attempt for this question was found.
The exact supporting source was found by primary-source arXiv/web search for
Kottman constants and \(c_0\) spreading models.
