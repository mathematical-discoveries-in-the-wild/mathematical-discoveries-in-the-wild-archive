\section{Source question and metadata}

The source is V.~Runde, \emph{Factorization of completely bounded maps through
reflexive operator spaces with applications to weak almost periodicity},
J. Math. Anal. Appl. \textbf{385} (2012), 477--484,
arXiv:1104.3812, DOI 10.1016/j.jmaa.2011.06.073 \cite{Runde}.
On PDF page 11 it asks:
\begin{quote}
Let $(M,\Gamma)$ be a Hopf--von Neumann algebra.  Is $\WAP(M_*)$ a
$C^*$-subalgebra of $M$?
\end{quote}
Here is the question as it appears in the source.

The source's Corollary 2.3 states, under injectivity of $M$ and $N$, that the
weak-slice class $\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$ is a bimodule over the spatial minimal
tensor product.  The result below shows that injectivity is not needed for
this particular corollary.

\section{Definitions}

Let $M,N$ be von Neumann algebras with their canonical preduals.  For
$u\in M\bar\otimes N$, define its left slice map
\[
 T_u:M_*\longrightarrow N,
 \qquad T_u(f)=(f\otimes\id)(u).
\]
Set
\[
 \WC(M\bar\otimes N)
 =\{u\in M\bar\otimes N:T_u\text{ is weakly compact}\}.
\]
The spatial minimal $C^*$-tensor product $M\otimes_{\min}N$ is viewed through
its canonical isometric inclusion in $M\bar\otimes N$.

For $a\in M$, let $L_a(m)=am$ and $R_a(m)=ma$.  These maps are normal, and
their predual maps will be denoted
\[
 L_{a,*}(f)=f\circ L_a,
 \qquad R_{a,*}(f)=f\circ R_a.
\]
We use the same $L_b,R_b$ notation for multiplication on $N$.

\section{Proof intuition}

Multiplication by an elementary tensor does not combine two uncontrolled
weakly compact maps.  It merely precomposes the old slice map with normal
multiplication on $M_*$ and postcomposes it with multiplication on $N$.
Thus weak compactness survives for algebraic tensors.  Norm density of the
algebraic tensor product then gives the full minimal tensor product because
weakly compact operators are norm closed.

For a Hopf coproduct, this produces a canonical multiplier algebra
$A_\Gamma=\Gamma^{-1}(M\otimes_{\min}M)$.  What remains open is precisely the
case where both coproducts have weakly compact slice maps but neither lies in
the minimal tensor product.

\section{The partial theorem}

\begin{theorem}[injectivity-free minimal-tensor bimodule theorem]
For arbitrary von Neumann algebras $M,N$,
\[
 (M\otimes_{\min}N)\,\WC(M\bar\otimes N)
 \subseteq\WC(M\bar\otimes N)
 \quad\text{and}\quad
 \WC(M\bar\otimes N)\,(M\otimes_{\min}N)
 \subseteq\WC(M\bar\otimes N).
\]
In particular, $\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$ is a bimodule over
$M\otimes_{\min}N$.  No injectivity assumption on $M$ or $N$ is required.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First observe that $u\mapsto T_u$ is contractive from $M\bar\otimes N$ into
$\mathcal B(M_*,N)$.  Since weakly compact operators form a norm-closed
operator ideal, $\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$ is a norm-closed linear subspace.

Fix $u\in\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$ and $a\in M$, $b\in N$.  Directly on algebraic
tensors, and hence on all of $M\bar\otimes N$ by normality, one has
\begin{align}
 T_{u(a\otimes b)}
   &=R_b\circ T_u\circ R_{a,*}, \label{eq:right}\\
 T_{(a\otimes b)u}
   &=L_b\circ T_u\circ L_{a,*}. \label{eq:left}
\end{align}
Indeed, if $u=\sum_jm_j\otimes n_j$, then for $f\in M_*$,
\begin{align*}
 T_{u(a\otimes b)}(f)
 &=\sum_j f(m_ja)n_jb
 =R_bT_u(R_{a,*}f),\\
 T_{(a\otimes b)u}(f)
 &=\sum_j f(am_j)bn_j
 =L_bT_u(L_{a,*}f).
\end{align*}
All four multiplication maps in \eqref{eq:right}--\eqref{eq:left} are bounded.
The operator-ideal property of weakly compact maps therefore implies
$u(a\otimes b),(a\otimes b)u\in\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$.

By linearity the same conclusion holds for every
$z\in M\odot N$.  Now take $z\in M\otimes_{\min}N$ and choose
$z_k\in M\odot N$ with $\lVert z_k-z\rVert\to0$.  Multiplication in
$M\bar\otimes N$ gives
\[
 \lVert uz_k-uz\rVert\leq\lVert u\rVert\lVert z_k-z\rVert\to0,
 \qquad
 \lVert z_ku-zu\rVert\to0.
\]
The norm-closedness noted in the first paragraph yields
$uz,zu\in\WC(M\bar\otimes N)$.
\end{proof}

For completeness, the norm-closedness invoked above can be seen from the
standard bidual criterion: if weakly compact $S_k:X\to Y$ converge to $S$ in
operator norm, then $S_k^{**}\to S^{**}$ in norm; since
$S_k^{**}(X^{**})\subseteq Y$ and the canonical copy of $Y$ is norm closed in
$Y^{**}$, one has $S^{**}(X^{**})\subseteq Y$, so $S$ is weakly compact.

\begin{corollary}[Hopf--von Neumann consequence]
Let $(M,\Gamma)$ be any Hopf--von Neumann algebra and put
\[
 A_\Gamma=\{y\in M:\Gamma(y)\in M\otimes_{\min}M\}.
\]
Then $A_\Gamma$ is a unital $C^*$-subalgebra of $M$, and
$\WAP(M_*)$ is an $A_\Gamma$-bimodule.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Since $\Gamma$ is a unital $*$-homomorphism and
$M\otimes_{\min}M$ is a norm-closed $C^*$-subalgebra of
$M\bar\otimes M$, its inverse image $A_\Gamma$ is a unital
$C^*$-subalgebra.  By Proposition 3.2 of the source \cite{Runde},
\[
 x\in\WAP(M_*)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
 \Gamma(x)\in\WC(M\bar\otimes M).
\]
If $x\in\WAP(M_*)$ and $y\in A_\Gamma$, then the theorem and multiplicativity
of $\Gamma$ give
\[
 \Gamma(xy)=\Gamma(x)\Gamma(y)\in\WC(M\bar\otimes M),
 \qquad
 \Gamma(yx)=\Gamma(y)\Gamma(x)\in\WC(M\bar\otimes M).
\]
Another application of the displayed equivalence proves
$xy,yx\in\WAP(M_*)$.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification notes}

The proof uses only four standard facts: multiplication by a fixed von Neumann
algebra element is normal; bounded pre- and post-composition preserve weak
compactness; weakly compact operators are norm closed; and
$M\odot N$ is norm dense in $M\otimes_{\min}N$.  The two slice identities
\eqref{eq:right}--\eqref{eq:left} have been checked in both orientations.
No computational experiment is relevant or used.

An adversarial audit is included as \texttt{verification\_report.md}.  The
recommended human check is to compare the slice conventions in
\eqref{eq:right}--\eqref{eq:left} with source equation (1).

\section{Limitations and novelty status}

The original $C^*$-algebra question remains open: the theorem does not apply
to a product of two arbitrary WAP elements unless one coproduct lies in the
minimal tensor product.  For noninjective $M$ no identification of $A_\Gamma$
with $\operatorname{CAP}(M_*)$ is asserted.

Cheap run indexes and bounded web searches on 19 July 2026 found no exact
version of the injectivity-free theorem.  Runde states Corollary 2.3 with
injectivity.  Daws's later treatment \cite{Daws} still describes the Hopf WAP
algebra question as unresolved; its counterexample concerns the unconstrained
two-fold weak-slice class and does not supply a Hopf coproduct counterexample.
The result is therefore marked \emph{likely valid partial result}, not
literature-certified novel.
