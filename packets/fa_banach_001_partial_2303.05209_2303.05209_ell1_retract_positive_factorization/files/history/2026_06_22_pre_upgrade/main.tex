\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Timur Oikhberg,
\emph{Geometry of unit balls of free Banach lattices, and its applications},
arXiv:2303.05209.  The relevant discussion appears in Section 3, on pages
12--13 of the arXiv PDF.  The paper defines positive factorization through a
free Banach lattice and then asks whether the canonical factorization
\[
X \longrightarrow \FBL[X] \longrightarrow X
\]
for an arbitrary Banach lattice can always be made with a positive first map.

\section*{Result Type}

This packet gives a \textbf{partial result}.  It proves the desired positive
factorization for Banach lattices that are positive retracts of an atomic
\(\ell_1(I)\).  It does not settle the arbitrary Banach-lattice question.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

For \(\ell_1(I)\), the positive coordinates give an explicit lifting into the
free Banach lattice: send the \(i\)-th unit vector to the positive part of the
\(i\)-th free generator.  This is linear on \(\ell_1(I)\), positive, and
contractive because the series is absolutely summable in the free-lattice norm.
The canonical lattice homomorphism back to \(\ell_1(I)\) sends that positive
part to the original positive atom.  If \(X\) is a positive retract of
\(\ell_1(I)\), functoriality of the free Banach lattice transfers this lifting
from \(\ell_1(I)\) to \(X\).

\section*{Definitions}

For a Banach space \(E\), write \(\iota_E:E\to \FBL[E]\) for the canonical
isometric embedding.  If \(X\) is a Banach lattice, let
\[
\rho_X:\FBL[X]\longrightarrow X
\]
be the contractive lattice homomorphism extending the identity map on \(X\).
Thus \(\rho_X\iota_X=\Id_X\).  If \(T:E\to F\) is a bounded operator between
Banach spaces, \(\FBL[T]:\FBL[E]\to\FBL[F]\) denotes the induced lattice
homomorphism satisfying
\[
\FBL[T]\iota_E(e)=\iota_F(Te).
\]
This is the standard universal property of free Banach lattices
\cite{Oikhberg2023,ART2018}.

A Banach lattice \(X\) is called a positive retract of \(\ell_1(I)\) here if
there are positive bounded linear maps
\[
J:X\to \ell_1(I),
\qquad
Q:\ell_1(I)\to X,
\qquad
QJ=\Id_X.
\]

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:ellone}
For every index set \(I\), \(\ell_1(I)\) has a \(1\)-positive factorization
through \(\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \((e_i)_{i\in I}\) be the canonical positive unit vectors of
\(\ell_1(I)\), and let
\[
\rho:\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\to \ell_1(I)
\]
be the lattice homomorphism extending the identity map on \(\ell_1(I)\).
For \(a=(a_i)\in\ell_1(I)\), define
\[
S a=\sum_{i\in I} a_i\,\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+.
\]
The sum is the norm limit of its finite partial sums.  Indeed,
\(\norm{\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+}\leq
\norm{\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)}=1\), so the net over finite subsets of \(I\)
is absolutely Cauchy and
\[
\norm{S a}\leq \sum_{i\in I}|a_i|=\norm{a}_{\ell_1(I)}.
\]
Thus \(S:\ell_1(I)\to\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\) is a contraction.  It is positive
because nonnegative coordinates are sent to a norm-convergent sum of positive
free-lattice elements.

Since \(\rho\) is a lattice homomorphism,
\[
\rho\big(\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+\big)
=\rho\big(\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)\big)^+
=e_i^+
=e_i .
\]
By linearity and continuity, \(\rho S a=a\) for every \(a\in\ell_1(I)\).
Hence \(\ell_1(I)\) positively factors through \(\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\) with
\(\norm{S}\norm{\rho}\leq 1\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:retract}
Let \(X\) be a Banach lattice.  Suppose that \(X\) is a positive retract of
\(\ell_1(I)\): there are positive bounded linear maps
\[
J:X\to\ell_1(I),
\qquad
Q:\ell_1(I)\to X,
\qquad
QJ=\Id_X.
\]
Then \(X\) positively factors through \(\FBL[X]\).  More precisely, there are
positive maps
\[
U:X\to\FBL[X],
\qquad
V:\FBL[X]\to X,
\]
such that \(VU=\Id_X\) and
\[
\norm{U}\norm{V}\leq \norm{J}\norm{Q}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(S:\ell_1(I)\to\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\) be the positive contraction from
Lemma \ref{lem:ellone}.  Let
\[
\FBL[Q]:\FBL[\ell_1(I)]\to\FBL[X]
\]
be the lattice homomorphism induced by \(Q\), and let
\[
V=\rho_X:\FBL[X]\to X.
\]
Define
\[
U=\FBL[Q]\,S\,J:X\to\FBL[X].
\]
The map \(U\) is positive because \(J\) and \(S\) are positive and
\(\FBL[Q]\) is a lattice homomorphism.  Also
\[
\norm{U}\leq \norm{\FBL[Q]}\norm{S}\norm{J}\leq \norm{Q}\norm{J},
\qquad
\norm{V}\leq 1.
\]

It remains to check that \(VU=\Id_X\).  Let \(x\in X\), and put
\(a=Jx=(a_i)_{i\in I}\in\ell_1(I)\).  Using the norm-convergent expansion in
Lemma \ref{lem:ellone},
\[
VUx
=\rho_X\FBL[Q]\left(\sum_{i\in I} a_i
  \iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+\right).
\]
Since \(\FBL[Q]\) and \(\rho_X\) are lattice homomorphisms,
\[
\rho_X\FBL[Q]\big(\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+\big)
=\rho_X\big(\iota_X(Qe_i)^+\big)
=Qe_i,
\]
where the last equality uses \(Qe_i\geq 0\), because \(Q\) is positive.
Therefore
\[
VUx=\sum_{i\in I}a_i Qe_i=Q(Jx)=x.
\]
So \(X\) has the claimed positive factorization through \(\FBL[X]\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Every finite-dimensional Banach lattice positively factors through its free
Banach lattice.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
A finite-dimensional Banach lattice is lattice isomorphic to
\(\mathbb{R}^n\) with coordinatewise order.  Let \(J\) be the positive
coordinate map into \(\ell_1^n\), and let \(Q\) reconstruct the corresponding
linear combination of the positive atoms in \(X\).  Then \(J\) and \(Q\) are
positive bounded maps and \(QJ=\Id_X\).  Theorem \ref{thm:retract} applies.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The only infinite-sum point is in Lemma \ref{lem:ellone}.  It is controlled by
\[
\sum_i |a_i|\,\norm{\iota_{\ell_1(I)}(e_i)^+}
\leq \sum_i |a_i|,
\]
so the partial sums converge in norm even for arbitrary index sets.  The
identity \(VU=\Id_X\) is checked first on this absolutely convergent expansion
and then follows by continuity.  No numerical computation is used.

The result also combines with Corollary 3.6 of the source paper: if such an
\(X\) has the \(\mu\)-approximation property, then it has the
\(\mu\norm{J}\norm{Q}\)-positive approximation property.  That consequence is
not the main claim here; the packet records the positive factorization itself.

\section*{Limitations And Novelty Check}

This packet does not solve the source paper's general question.  The proof
requires a positive retraction through an atomic \(\ell_1(I)\).  It gives no
answer for arbitrary Banach lattices, nonatomic \(L_1\)-spaces, \(c_0\), or
Banach lattices where no such atomic positive lifting is known.

Before packaging, the run's lightweight indexes were searched for
arXiv:2303.05209, the title, positive factorization, AP/PAP, and free Banach
lattice phrases.  No exact duplicate packet or prior attempt was found.  A
bounded web search on June 14, 2026 for exact phrases around positive
factorization through \(\FBL[X]\) did not locate a later paper explicitly
answering the question.  This should be treated as a candidate partial result
for human review, not as a full literature-status claim.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

The proof is short and appears likely valid.  The main points to check are the
definition of \(S\) on \(\ell_1(I)\), the use of the positive part of each free
generator, and the transfer step using positivity of \(Q\):
\[
\rho_X\FBL[Q](\iota(e_i)^+)=Qe_i.
\]
