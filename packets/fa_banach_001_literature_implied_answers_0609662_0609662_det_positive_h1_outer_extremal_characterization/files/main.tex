\section*{Status}

This is a literature-implied partial answer to a question in Blecher--Labuschagne,
\emph{Applications of the Fuglede-Kadison determinant: Szego's theorem and outers
for noncommutative \(H^p\)}, arXiv:math/0609662.  The source paper asks on page
14:
\begin{quote}
Is there a characterization of outers in \(H^1\) in terms of extremals, as in
the deLeeuw--Rudin theorem?
\end{quote}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The later paper by Bekjan and Xu,
\emph{Riesz and Szego type factorizations for noncommutative Hardy spaces},
arXiv:0705.1947, gives such a characterization in the determinant-positive
subcase.

\section*{Supporting Theorem}

Let \(A\) be a finite subdiagonal algebra in a finite von Neumann algebra
\(\M\), let \(H^p(A)\) be the associated noncommutative Hardy space, let
\(\PhiE:\M\to \D=A\cap A^*\) be the conditional expectation, and let \(\De\)
denote the Fuglede--Kadison determinant.

Bekjan--Xu Corollary 4.11 states that if \(0<p\leq \infty\) and
\(h\in H^p(A)\) satisfies \(\De(h)>0\), then \(h\) is outer if and only if for
every \(x\in H^p(A)\) with \(|x|=|h|\) one has
\[
  \De(\PhiE(x)) \leq \De(\PhiE(h)).
\]

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{figures/supporting_answer_crop.png}
\end{center}

Taking \(p=1\) gives a determinant-extremal characterization of strongly outer
\(H^1\) functions: among analytic competitors with the same modulus, the outer
element is exactly the one whose diagonal expectation has maximal
Fuglede--Kadison determinant.

\section*{Identification and Scope}

This answers the Blecher--Labuschagne question for the determinant-positive
part of \(H^1\).  The relation is an agent-identified implication rather than
an explicitly named resolution in Bekjan--Xu: the supporting paper cites and
extends the Blecher--Labuschagne outer theory, and Corollary 4.11 specializes
directly to the \(p=1\) extremal characterization requested by the source.

The packet is not a full answer to all nearby open points.  It does not remove
the hypothesis \(\De(h)>0\), does not handle determinant-zero outers, does not
give a literal deLeeuw--Rudin extreme-point theorem, and does not settle the
separate question whether every outer element of \(H^1\) is the square of an
outer element of \(H^2\).

\section*{Search Notes}

The local parsed arXiv corpus was searched for the exact square-of-outer phrase,
for the deLeeuw--Rudin/extremal wording, and for determinant/outer variants.
The exact square question reappears in a Blecher--Labuschagne survey
arXiv:math/0611879, but no later full closure was found in the local corpus.
ArXiv web searches for the same determinant and noncommutative Hardy keywords
identified Bekjan--Xu arXiv:0705.1947 as the relevant later source.
