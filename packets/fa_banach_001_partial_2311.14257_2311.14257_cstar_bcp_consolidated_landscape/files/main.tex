\section*{Status After Second Consolidation}

This packet is a consolidated review packet, not a claimed full arbitrary
\(C^*\)-classification.  It preserves all lane-19 packets and attempts under
\texttt{history/}.  The present state is much sharper than the first
consolidation:

\paragraph{Non-duplication marker.}
The qualitative BCP side is already settled in full Banach-space generality:
for every nonzero real or complex normed space \(X\),
\[
        X\text{ has BCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        X\text{ has no countable capture}.
\]
Consequently every \(C^*\)-algebra has BCP exactly when its underlying
Banach space has no countable capture.  The remaining problem is not to
reprove this equivalence, but to turn it into intrinsic \(C^*\)-structure and
to decide whether \(C^*\)-algebra BCP always upgrades to UBCP.

\begin{itemize}
\item For every nonzero real or complex normed space,
\[
        X\text{ fails BCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        X\text{ has countable capture}.
\]
Thus the qualitative BCP side is exactly understood in Banach-space language.

\item For \(C^*\)-algebras, BCP forces a countable primitive \(\pi\)-base, a
faithful state, an essential ideal generated by a countable BCP blocker, and
an essential \(\sigma\)-unital hereditary support built from the blocker
squares.

\item Every separable \(C^*\)-algebra has UBCP.  More generally, countable
products of separable \(C^*\)-algebras have UBCP, while uncountable products
of nonzero separable factors fail BCP.

\item The complete Type I classification is known:
\[
\begin{aligned}
A\text{ Type I has BCP}
&\Longleftrightarrow A\text{ has UBCP}\\
&\Longleftrightarrow
\Prim(A)\text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-base and every irreducible}\\
&\qquad\text{representation of the largest Fell ideal } \operatorname{Fell}(A)
\text{ is separable.}
\end{aligned}
\]

\item Continuous-trace separable-fibre algebras, their unitizations, and their
finite-dimensional quotients are classified by the same countable
\(\pi\)-base condition on the continuous-trace primitive part.

\item Primitive topology alone is not enough.  Simple \(\mathrm{II}_1\)
factors, nonseparable \(\Kcal(H)\), uncountable tensor products, reduced group
algebras of uncountable groups, graph/CCR reflection examples, and
corona/reduced-product examples supply failures beyond primitive topology.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Consolidated Theorem Map}

\begin{theorem}[lane-19 consolidated facts]
The following promoted packets are part of the current review landscape.
\begin{enumerate}
\item A nonzero normed space fails BCP if and only if it has countable capture:
for every countable \(C\subset X\), some \(u\in S_X\) satisfies
\[
        \dist(c,\mathbb F u)=\|c\| \qquad(c\in C).
\]

\item If a \(C^*\)-algebra \(A\) has BCP, then \(\Prim(A)\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-base.  Also \(A\) admits a faithful state, a countable BCP blocker
generates an essential ideal, and the positive element
\[
        h=\sum_n 2^{-n-2}
        \frac{c_n^*c_n+c_nc_n^*}{1+\|c_n\|^2}
\]
associated to any countable blocker has essential hereditary support
\(\overline{hAh}\).

\item If \(A\) contains a separable essential ideal, then \(A\) has UBCP.
This explains many positive nonseparable examples, but it is not a necessary
condition.

\item If \(A\) is Type I, then \(A\) has BCP iff it has UBCP iff
\(\Prim(A)\) has a countable \(\pi\)-base and the largest Fell ideal has only
separable irreducible representations.

\item If \(I\) is continuous trace with separable nonzero elementary fibres,
then \(I\), its minimal unitization, and every finite-dimensional quotient
extension \(0\to I\to A\to F\to0\) satisfy
\[
        A\text{ has BCP}
        \Longleftrightarrow
        A\text{ has UBCP}
        \Longleftrightarrow
        \Prim(I)\text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-base}.
\]

\item If \(H\) is nonseparable and \(F\subseteq\mathcal B(H)\) is finite
dimensional, then \(\Kcal(H)+F\) fails BCP.

\item If \((A_\gamma)\) is a family of separable \(C^*\)-algebras, then
\[
        \prod_\gamma A_\gamma
\]
has BCP, equivalently UBCP, iff only countably many factors are nonzero.

\item If \(G\) is a discrete group, then
\[
        C^*_r(G)\text{ has BCP}
        \Longleftrightarrow
        C^*_r(G)\text{ has UBCP}
        \Longleftrightarrow
        G\text{ is countable}.
\]

\item Product quotients, ideal reduced products under disjoint refinement,
sigma-unital multiplier coronas, stabilized Calkin-type coronas, and Hilbert
module block coronas fail BCP by diagonal or annular escape criteria.

\item Infinite-dimensional countably saturated Banach spaces, hence
countably saturated \(C^*\)-algebras, have countable capture and fail BCP.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\section*{False Bridges Removed}

Several plausible routes were tested and refuted.  These negative lessons are
important because they prevent circular or impossible future proofs.

\paragraph{Separable support-net bridge.}
The endpoint-free split interval \(K\) has a countable \(\pi\)-base, so
\(C(K)\) has UBCP by the source commutative theorem.  But every nonempty open
subset of \(K\) contains a Sorgenfrey interval, hence no nonzero principal
ideal \(C_0(\operatorname{coz} e)\) is separable.  Therefore pure-state
countable \(\pi\)-base cannot imply a separable right-support net.

\paragraph{Sequence-quotient reflection.}
For \(A=\Kcal(\ell_2)\), a countable dense \(C\subset S_A\) is a BCP witness.
If \(p_n\) are rank-one projections onto an orthonormal basis and
\(v=[(p_n)]\in \ell_\infty(A)/c_0(A)\), then
\[
        \|\Delta(a)-\lambda v\|
        =
        \max\{\|a\|,|\lambda|\}
        \qquad(a\in A).
\]
Thus \(\Delta(C)\) is captured in the sequence quotient, but no unit vector
of \(A\) captures \(C\).  Bare quotient capture therefore cannot reflect back
to \(A\).

\paragraph{Transition pi-base alone.}
The pure transition functional space
\[
        T(A)=\operatorname{ext}(B_{A^*})
\]
calibrates the commutative and von Neumann boundary cases.  However, a
transition-open patch is not automatically an operator-norm support patch.
To get UBCP one needs localizable coefficient devices, not only a topological
\(\pi\)-base in \(T(A)\).

\section*{Proof Intuition Map}

The current landscape is organized by three positive/negative mechanisms.

\paragraph{Local coefficient models.}
The positive examples have countably many local models: scalar Urysohn bumps
in \(C(K)\), localizable fibre-nets in continuous fields, finite-rank compact
charts in continuous trace/Fell/type-I algebras, or countable dense reservoirs
inside separable essential ideals.  These models amplify a small local
coefficient into a fixed UBCP gap.

\paragraph{Visibility maps and fresh directions.}
Negative non-Type-I examples often admit contractive maps or automorphisms
that preserve old centres while killing or flipping a new unit direction:
subgroup expectations in reduced group algebras, two-evaluation tensor
directions, graph sign-reflections, or coordinate deletion in products.
These produce countable capture and hence failure of BCP.

\paragraph{Diagonal and annular escape.}
Quotients and coronas allow countably many centres to be assigned to disjoint
tails or annuli where they nearly realize quotient norm.  A global quotient
element glues local escape pieces.  This explains product quotients, reduced
products, sigma-unital coronas, and Hilbert-module block coronas.

\section*{Where the Full Classification Is Missing}

The remaining wall is now quite specific.  A possible counterexample to
\[
        \text{BCP}\Rightarrow\text{UBCP}
\]
would have to be a non-Type-I, nonseparable \(C^*\)-algebra with:

\begin{itemize}
\item countable primitive \(\pi\)-base;
\item a faithful state;
\item an essential ideal generated by a countable BCP blocker;
\item no separable essential ideal;
\item an essential \(\sigma\)-unital hereditary support generated by its
countable blocker, but no extracted countable local coefficient model;
\item no Type I/Fell separable local model;
\item no visible reduced group expectation, tensor/product coordinate,
graph sign-reflection, countable saturation, von Neumann atomless projection,
or corona/diagonal tail mechanism.
\end{itemize}

This is why the next route should be structural:
\[
\begin{array}{c}
\text{BCP and no countable local coefficient model}\\
\Downarrow\\
\text{one of the known countable-capture mechanisms}\\
\Downarrow\\
\text{failure of BCP.}
\end{array}
\]
If this dichotomy is proved, it gives the desired implication
BCP\(\Rightarrow\)UBCP and likely the right noncommutative analogue of the
commutative \(\pi\)-base theorem.
