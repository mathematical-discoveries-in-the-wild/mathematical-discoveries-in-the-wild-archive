\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when its pure state space, i.e.
\(K\), has a countable \(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative
version:

\includegraphics[width=0.92\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

\section*{Statement}

Let \(A\) be a continuous \(C_0(X)\)-algebra over a locally compact Hausdorff
space \(X\).  We write \(A_x\) for the fiber at \(x\), and \(a(x)\in A_x\) for
the image of \(a\in A\).  We use the standard fiber norm formula
\[
        \|a\|=\sup_{x\in X}\|a(x)\|.
\]

\begin{definition}
Let \(0<\delta<1\).  We say that \(A\) has a countable localizable
\(\delta\)-fiber-net if there is a countable family
\(\Sigma=\{\sigma_m:m\ge1\}\) of bounded sections, \(\sigma_m(x)\in A_x\), such
that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(\sup_x\|\sigma_m(x)\|\le1\) for every \(m\);
    \item for every \(h\in C_c(X)\), the section \(x\mapsto h(x)\sigma_m(x)\)
    belongs to \(A\);
    \item for every \(a\in A\) and every \(m\), the map
    \[
        (x,s)\mapsto \|a(x)-s\sigma_m(x)\|
    \]
    is continuous on \(X\times[0,4]\);
    \item for every \(x\in X\) and every \(u\in S_{A_x}\), there is \(m\) such
    that \(\|\sigma_m(x)-u\|<\delta\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

This hypothesis is designed to include the trivial bundle \(C_0(X,B)\) with
separable nonzero fiber \(B\): take constant bounded sections from a countable
dense subset of \(S_B\), then localize them by multiplying by functions in
\(C_c(X)\).

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:fibernet}
Let \(A\) be a continuous \(C_0(X)\)-algebra with \(A_x\ne0\) for every
\(x\in X\).  Assume that \(A\) has a countable localizable
\(1/64\)-fiber-net.  Then the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(A\) has BCP;
    \item \(A\) has UBCP;
    \item \(X\) has a countable \(\pi\)-basis.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The previous continuous-field obstruction shows that BCP forces the base to
have a countable \(\pi\)-basis.  For the converse, the localizable fiber-net
provides the missing noncommutative ingredient: a countable list of fiber
directions.  Near a point where \(a\in S_A\) is large, choose a net section
\(\sigma_m\) that approximates the normalized fiber value \(a(x_0)/\|a(x_0)\|\).
The center is then a localized section \(4h_j\sigma_m\), where \(h_j\) is a
scalar bump supported in a sufficiently small \(\pi\)-basic set.  Convexity in
the bump value \(h_j(x)\in[0,1]\) gives a fixed radius estimate.

\section*{Proof}

We first recall the negative half, proved in the companion packet
\cite{lane19base}.  For convenience, we include the exact form needed here.

\begin{lemma}[base obstruction]
\label{lem:base}
Let \(A\) be a continuous \(C_0(X)\)-algebra with \(A_x\ne0\) for all \(x\).
If \(A\) has BCP, then \(X\) has a countable \(\pi\)-basis.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Given countably many candidate centers \(a_n\), the sets
\[
        U_{n,k}=\{x:\|a_n(x)\|>\|a_n\|-1/k\}
\]
are open and nonempty whenever \(a_n\ne0\).  If \(X\) has no countable
\(\pi\)-basis, choose a nonempty open set \(W\) containing none of the nonempty
\(U_{n,k}\).  Since all fibers are nonzero, choose \(b\in A\) with
\(\|b\|=1\) and \(b(x)=0\) outside \(W\): take \(a\) with \(a(x_0)\ne0\) for
some \(x_0\in W\), multiply by a scalar \(C_c\)-bump supported in \(W\), and
normalize.  For each \(n,k\), pick \(x_{n,k}\in U_{n,k}\setminus W\).  Then
\[
        \|b-a_n\|\ge \|a_n(x_{n,k})\|>\|a_n\|-1/k,
\]
and letting \(k\to\infty\) gives \(\|b-a_n\|\ge\|a_n\|\).  Thus no countable
family of balls \(B(a_n,\|a_n\|)\) covers the unit sphere.
\end{proof}

It remains to prove that a countable \(\pi\)-basis gives UBCP under the
fiber-net hypothesis.  Fix a countable \(\pi\)-basis \((V_j)\).  By local
compactness and regularity, choose for each \(j\) a function \(h_j\in C_c(X)\)
such that
\[
        0\le h_j\le1,\qquad \|h_j\|_\infty=1,\qquad \supp h_j\subset V_j.
\]

Let \(\delta=1/64\).  For \(j,m\ge1\), put
\[
        c_{j,m}=4h_j\sigma_m\in A.
\]
Keep only those pairs \((j,m)\) for which \(\|c_{j,m}\|>15/4\).  We claim that
the open balls
\[
        B(c_{j,m},7/2)
\]
over this retained countable family cover \(S_A\).  Since each retained center
has norm \(>15/4\), these balls are all disjoint from the open ball
\(B(0,1/4)\).  Thus this will prove UBCP.

Let \(a\in S_A\).  Choose \(x_0\in X\) with
\[
        \alpha:=\|a(x_0)\|>3/4.
\]
Let \(u=a(x_0)/\alpha\in S_{A_{x_0}}\).  By the fiber-net property, choose
\(m\) such that
\[
        \|\sigma_m(x_0)-u\|<\delta.
\]
Write \(w=\sigma_m(x_0)\).  For \(0\le s\le1\), the function
\[
        s\mapsto \|\alpha u-4s w\|
\]
is convex, hence bounded by the maximum of its endpoint values.  At \(s=0\)
the value is \(\alpha<1\).  At \(s=1\),
\[
        \|\alpha u-4w\|
        \le
        \alpha\|u-w\|+(4-\alpha)\|w\|
        \le
        \alpha\delta+4-\alpha
        =
        4-\alpha(1-\delta).
\]
Since \(\alpha>3/4\) and \(\delta=1/64\), this is strictly less than \(7/2\).
Therefore
\[
        \sup_{0\le s\le1}\|a(x_0)-4s\sigma_m(x_0)\|<7/2.
\]

By the continuity assumption on \(\sigma_m\), and compactness of \([0,1]\),
there is a neighborhood \(U\) of \(x_0\) such that
\[
        \sup_{0\le s\le1}\|a(x)-4s\sigma_m(x)\|<7/2
        \qquad (x\in U).
\]
Also \(\|\sigma_m(x_0)\|>1-\delta\), so after shrinking \(U\) we may assume
\[
        \|\sigma_m(x)\|>1-2\delta
        \qquad (x\in U).
\]
Choose \(j\) with \(V_j\subset U\).

For \(x\in\supp h_j\), put \(s=h_j(x)\).  Since \(\supp h_j\subset U\), the
previous estimate gives
\[
        \|a(x)-c_{j,m}(x)\|
        =
        \|a(x)-4h_j(x)\sigma_m(x)\|
        <7/2.
\]
For \(x\notin\supp h_j\), we have \(c_{j,m}(x)=0\), so
\[
        \|a(x)-c_{j,m}(x)\|=\|a(x)\|\le1<7/2.
\]
Thus \(\|a-c_{j,m}\|<7/2\).

Finally, because \(h_j\) attains the value \(1\) at some point of \(V_j\subset
U\), the lower bound on \(\|\sigma_m(x)\|\) on \(U\) gives
\[
        \|c_{j,m}\|\ge 4(1-2\delta)=31/8>15/4.
\]
Hence \((j,m)\) is one of the retained pairs, and \(a\in B(c_{j,m},7/2)\).
This proves that \(A\) has UBCP.

Combining this UBCP implication with Lemma~\ref{lem:base}, and the trivial
implication UBCP \(\Rightarrow\) BCP, proves Theorem~\ref{thm:fibernet}.

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

The theorem is a partial answer to the source question, not a full
characterization of arbitrary \(C^*\)-algebras.  It applies to continuous
fields whose fibers admit a countable family of localizable directions.  It
does not cover arbitrary upper-semicontinuous fields, nor C*-algebras with no
useful central \(C_0(X)\)-structure.  The point of the theorem is that it
isolates a positive noncommutative replacement for the scalar bump directions
used in the commutative proof.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

The main checks are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item the definition of a localizable fiber-net, especially that it really
    includes constant bounded directions in \(C_0(X,B)\);
    \item the compact-parameter continuity step for
    \((x,s)\mapsto\|a(x)-4s\sigma_m(x)\|\);
    \item the constants \(1/64\), \(7/2\), and \(15/4\), which ensure both
    coverage and uniform separation from the origin.
\end{itemize}
