\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[reflection-capture criterion]
\label{thm:criterion}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra and let \((u_v)_{v\in V}\subset S_A\), where
\(V\) is uncountable.  Suppose:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item for every countable \(C\subset A\), there is a countable
          \(S\subset V\) such that \(C\subset C^*(u_s:s\in S)\);
    \item for every \(v\in V\), there is an isometric automorphism
          \(\alpha_v\in\operatorname{Aut}(A)\) satisfying
          \[
                \alpha_v(u_v)=-u_v,\qquad
                \alpha_v(u_w)=u_w \quad(w\ne v).
          \]
\end{enumerate}
Then \(A\) has countable capture.  Consequently \(A\) fails BCP.
\end{theorem}

Here countable capture means that for every countable \(C\subset A\) there is
\(u\in S_A\) such that
\[
        \dist(c,\C u)=\|c\|\qquad(c\in C).
\]
The packet
\[
\texttt{2311.14257\_countable\_capture\_characterization}
\]
records that countable capture implies failure of BCP.

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:graph}
Let \(G=(V,E)\) be an uncountable graph and let \(B(G)\) be Farah's graph
CCR algebra generated by self-adjoint unitaries \((u_v)_{v\in V}\) with
\[
        u_vu_w=u_wu_v \quad\text{if } \{v,w\}\notin E,
        \qquad
        u_vu_w=-u_wu_v \quad\text{if } \{v,w\}\in E .
\]
Then \(B(G)\) has countable capture and fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:farah}
The simple nuclear AM algebra of density continuum constructed by Farah in
\cite{FarahCCR} has countable capture and fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The missing mechanism in the arbitrary simple case is an exact way to add one
new direction over a countable old subalgebra.  For graph CCR algebras the
new direction does not have to commute tensorially with the old algebra.  It
is enough that an automorphism fixes the old algebra and flips the new
generator.

Indeed, if \(\alpha(c)=c\) and \(\alpha(u)=-u\), then
\[
        \|c-\lambda u\|=\|\alpha(c-\lambda u)\|=\|c+\lambda u\|.
\]
Since \(c\) is the average of \(c-\lambda u\) and \(c+\lambda u\), convexity
forces
\[
        \|c\|\le \|c-\lambda u\|
\]
for every scalar \(\lambda\).  This is exactly the countable-capture
distance inequality.

\section*{Proof of the Criterion}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:criterion}]
Let \(C\subset A\) be countable.  By hypothesis, choose a countable
\(S\subset V\) such that \(C\subset C^*(u_s:s\in S)\).  Since \(V\) is
uncountable, choose \(v_0\in V\setminus S\), and set \(u=u_{v_0}\).

The automorphism \(\alpha=\alpha_{v_0}\) fixes every generator \(u_s\) with
\(s\in S\).  Therefore it fixes every element of \(C^*(u_s:s\in S)\), and
in particular
\[
        \alpha(c)=c\qquad(c\in C).
\]
It also satisfies \(\alpha(u)=-u\).

Fix \(c\in C\) and \(\lambda\in\C\).  Since \(\alpha\) is isometric,
\[
        \|c-\lambda u\|
        =
        \|\alpha(c-\lambda u)\|
        =
        \|c+\lambda u\|.
\]
But
\[
        c=\frac12(c-\lambda u)+\frac12(c+\lambda u),
\]
and hence
\[
        \|c\|
        \le
        \frac12\|c-\lambda u\|+\frac12\|c+\lambda u\|
        =
        \|c-\lambda u\|.
\]
Taking the infimum over \(\lambda\in\C\) gives
\[
        \dist(c,\C u)\ge \|c\|.
\]
The reverse inequality is obtained by taking \(\lambda=0\).  Thus
\(\dist(c,\C u)=\|c\|\) for every \(c\in C\).  This proves countable
capture.  Countable capture implies failure of BCP by the previously
packaged countable-capture obstruction.
\end{proof}

\section*{Graph CCR Algebras}

We recall only the features of Farah's construction needed here.  For a graph
\(G=(V,E)\), Farah defines \(B(G)\) as the universal \(C^*\)-algebra
generated by self-adjoint unitaries \((u_v)_{v\in V}\) satisfying the
commutation/anticommutation relations displayed in
Corollary~\ref{cor:graph}.  Farah proves that \(B(G)\) is well-defined for
every graph \(G\), and that for an infinite graph its character density is
\(|V|\) \cite[Lemma 1.1]{FarahCCR}.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:support}
If \(G=(V,E)\) is a graph and \(C\subset B(G)\) is countable, then there is
a countable \(S\subset V\) such that
\[
        C\subset C^*(u_s:s\in S).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Farah's construction realizes \(B(G)\) as the direct limit of the algebras
\(B(G_0)\), where \(G_0\) ranges over finite induced subgraphs of \(G\).
Thus the union of the finite-vertex subalgebras
\[
        C^*(u_s:s\in F),\qquad F\subset V\text{ finite},
\]
is dense in \(B(G)\).

For each \(c\in C\), choose a sequence of finite-vertex algebra elements
converging to \(c\).  Let \(S_c\) be the union of all finite vertex sets used
in this sequence.  Then \(S_c\) is countable and
\[
        c\in C^*(u_s:s\in S_c).
\]
Putting \(S=\bigcup_{c\in C}S_c\), we obtain a countable set of vertices
with \(C\subset C^*(u_s:s\in S)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:flip}
For each \(v\in V\), the assignment
\[
        u_v\mapsto -u_v,\qquad u_w\mapsto u_w\quad(w\ne v)
\]
extends to an automorphism of \(B(G)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The modified family of self-adjoint unitaries satisfies exactly the same
relations as the original family: multiplying one generator by \(-1\) does
not change whether it commutes or anticommutes with any other generator.
By the universal property of \(B(G)\), the assignment therefore induces a
unital \(*\)-homomorphism \(B(G)\to B(G)\).  Applying the same assignment
twice gives the identity on generators, hence the homomorphism is an
automorphism and is isometric.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:graph}]
For an uncountable graph \(G=(V,E)\), Lemma~\ref{lem:support} verifies the
countable-support hypothesis in Theorem~\ref{thm:criterion}, and
Lemma~\ref{lem:flip} verifies the reflection hypothesis.  The theorem gives
countable capture, hence failure of BCP.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:farah}]
Farah constructs an independent dense family \(\mathcal A\subseteq 2^{\mathbb
N}\) of cardinality \(2^{\aleph_0}\) and studies the bipartite graph
\[
        G(\mathbb N,\mathcal A).
\]
He proves that \(B(G(\mathbb N,\mathcal A))\) is simple, nuclear, unital, AM,
has unique trace, has density continuum, and has irreducible representations
on both a separable and a nonseparable Hilbert space
\cite[Theorem 1 and Lemmas 1.4, 1.6, 1.8]{FarahCCR}.  Since the vertex set
\(\mathbb N\cup\mathcal A\) is uncountable, Corollary~\ref{cor:graph}
applies and proves that this simple nuclear density-continuum algebra has
countable capture and fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes and Limitations}

The proof is exact: no approximate centrality, saturation, or limiting
compactness is used after choosing the unused generator.  The only structural
ingredients are countable support and a sign-flip automorphism.

This substantially narrows the live simple density-continuum wall because it
settles a canonical nonseparable simple nuclear example with a faithful
separable representation.  It does not settle arbitrary simple nonseparable
\(C^*\)-algebras.  A remaining example would have to lack this reflection
symmetry, as well as the previously packaged tensor, product, group,
von Neumann, primitive-topological, corona, and saturation mechanisms.
