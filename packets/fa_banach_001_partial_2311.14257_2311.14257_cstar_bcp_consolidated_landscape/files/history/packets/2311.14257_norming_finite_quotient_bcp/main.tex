\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when its pure state space, i.e.
\(K\), has a countable \(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative
version:

\section*{Statement}

The scalar-unitization packet handles one multiplier direction over a
continuous-trace ideal.  This packet gives the finite-dimensional quotient
version under the natural hypothesis that the continuous-trace ideal still
uniformly norms the extension.

\begin{definition}
Let \(I\) be a closed ideal of a \(C^*\)-algebra \(A\).  We say that \(I\) is
\(\gamma\)-norming in \(A\), where \(0<\gamma\le1\), if
\[
        \sup\{\|\pi(a)\|:\pi\in\widehat I\}\ge \gamma\|a\|
        \qquad(a\in A).
\]
Equivalently, the primitive quotients coming from \(\Prim(I)\) see at least a
fixed fraction \(\gamma\) of every norm in \(A\).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[norming finite-dimensional quotient criterion]
\label{thm:main}
Let
\[
        0\longrightarrow I\longrightarrow A
        \stackrel{q}{\longrightarrow} F\longrightarrow0
\]
be an exact sequence of \(C^*\)-algebras.  Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(F\) is finite dimensional;
    \item \(I\) is continuous trace, and every elementary fibre of \(I\) is
          \(\Kcal(H)\) for a separable nonzero Hilbert space \(H\);
    \item \(\Prim(I)\) has a countable \(\pi\)-basis;
    \item \(I\) is \(\gamma\)-norming in \(A\) for some \(\gamma>0\).
\end{enumerate}
Then \(A\) has UBCP.

Consequently, within this norming finite-quotient class,
\[
        A\text{ has BCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        A\text{ has UBCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        \Prim(I)\text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-basis}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Choose a bounded linear section \(\sigma:F\to A\).  Every \(a\in A\) can be
written
\[
        a=\sigma(q(a))+k,\qquad k\in I.
\]
The old compact-bump proof controls the compact section \(k\), but the lifted
finite-dimensional quotient part \(\sigma(q(a))\) acts locally as an arbitrary
bounded operator \(B(P)\), not as a compact operator.

The matrix-block replacement for the scalar spike is simple.  At a point where
\(\|\sigma(q(a))(P)+k(P)\|\) is bounded below, approximate \(k(P)\) by a
finite-rank operator \(T\).  Then choose a rank-one operator \(R\) aligned with
a vector where \(B(P)+T\) almost attains its norm.  For a large fixed \(M\),
\[
        \|B(P)+T+MR\|\approx M+\|B(P)+T\|,
\]
whereas along the bump path
\[
        \|k(P)-s(T+MR)\|\lesssim M\qquad(0\le s\le1).
\]
Thus the finite-dimensional quotient part supplies the same extra margin that
the scalar phase supplied in the unitization proof.

\section*{Proof}

We first record the two elementary lemmas used in the covering construction.

\begin{lemma}[rank-one ballast]
\label{lem:ballast}
Let \(H\) be a separable nonzero Hilbert space, let \(D\in\mathcal B(H)\), let
\(M>0\), and let \(\varepsilon>0\).  There is a norm-one rank-one operator
\(R\) belonging to a fixed countable norm-dense family of rank-one operators
such that
\[
        \|D+MR\|\ge M+\|D\|-\varepsilon .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose a unit vector \(\xi\) with
\(\|D\xi\|>\|D\|-\varepsilon/3\).  If \(D\xi\ne0\), put
\(v=D\xi/\|D\xi\|\); otherwise take any unit vector \(v\).  The rank-one
operator \(R_0\eta=\langle \eta,\xi\rangle v\) satisfies
\[
        \|(D+MR_0)\xi\|\ge M+\|D\|-\varepsilon/3.
\]
Rank-one norm-one operators are norm separable when \(H\) is separable: take
initial and final unit vectors from a fixed countable dense subset of the unit
sphere.  Choose \(R\) in that countable family with
\(\|R-R_0\|<\varepsilon/(3M)\).  Then
\[
        \|D+MR\|\ge M+\|D\|-\varepsilon .
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[charted bump families]
\label{lem:charts}
If \(I\) is continuous trace with separable fibres and \(\Prim(I)\) has a
countable \(\pi\)-basis, then there are countably many charts
\[
        \Phi_n:I_{O_n}\longrightarrow C_0(O_n,\Kcal(H_n))
\]
with separable nonzero \(H_n\), such that \((O_n)\) is a \(\pi\)-basis of
\(\Prim(I)\).  For each \(n\), there is a countable family
\(\mathcal B_n\subset C_c(O_n)\), \(0\le h\le1\), \(\|h\|=1\), whose supports
are cofinal in the nonempty open subsets of \(O_n\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the standard local triviality and bump selection for continuous-trace
algebras, as used in the earlier continuous-trace packet.  The spaces \(O_n\)
are locally compact Hausdorff, so local regularity gives the scalar bumps.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
The implication UBCP \(\Rightarrow\) BCP is immediate.  Conversely, if \(A\)
has BCP, the primitive \(\pi\)-base obstruction gives a countable
\(\pi\)-basis for \(\Prim(A)\), hence also for the open subspace \(\Prim(I)\).
Since \(F\) is finite dimensional, the finite quotient part of \(\Prim(A)\)
does not affect countability of a \(\pi\)-basis.  It remains to prove UBCP
from the four stated assumptions.

Choose a bounded linear section \(\sigma:F\to A\), and put
\[
        C=\max\{1,\|\sigma\|\}.
\]
Let
\[
        M=C+2.
\]
Choose \(0<\varepsilon<1\) so small that
\[
        (2C+5)\varepsilon<\gamma/2.
\]
Because \(F\) is finite dimensional, the unit ball of \(F\) has a countable
\(\varepsilon\)-net \(Z\).

Fix the chart and bump families from Lemma~\ref{lem:charts}.  In each chart
choose:
\begin{itemize}
\item a countable norm-dense set \(\mathcal T_n\) of finite-rank operators in
      \(\Kcal(H_n)\);
\item a countable norm-dense set \(\mathcal R_n\) of norm-one rank-one
      operators on \(H_n\).
\end{itemize}
For every
\[
        z\in Z,\quad n,\quad h\in\mathcal B_n,\quad
        T\in\mathcal T_n,\quad R\in\mathcal R_n
\]
form
\[
        c(z,n,h,T,R)
        =
        \sigma(z)+\Phi_n^{-1}\bigl(h(T+MR)\bigr)\in A.
\]
Retain only those countably many centers satisfying
\[
        \|c(z,n,h,T,R)\|\ge M+\gamma/2-(C+3)\varepsilon.
\]
For each retained center use the common radius
\[
        r=M+(C+2)\varepsilon.
\]
The choice of \(\varepsilon\) gives
\[
        M+\gamma/2-(C+3)\varepsilon-r
        =
        \gamma/2-(2C+5)\varepsilon>0,
\]
so the retained balls are uniformly off the origin.

We claim that these balls cover \(S_A\).  Let \(a\in S_A\).  Put
\[
        y=q(a)\in F,\qquad k=a-\sigma(y)\in I.
\]
Then \(\|y\|\le1\), so choose \(z\in Z\) with \(\|y-z\|<\varepsilon\), and set
\[
        b=\sigma(z),\qquad e=\sigma(y-z).
\]
Thus \(\|e\|\le C\varepsilon\) and
\[
        a=b+k+e.
\]

By the \(\gamma\)-norming hypothesis, the open set
\[
        W=\{P\in\Prim(I):\|a(P)\|>\gamma/2\}
\]
is nonempty.  Choose a chart \(O_n\subset W\), and choose \(P_0\in O_n\).
Write \(k_n(P)=\Phi_n(k|_{I_{O_n}})(P)\).  Choose
\[
        T\in\mathcal T_n
        \qquad\text{with}\qquad
        \|T-k_n(P_0)\|<\varepsilon.
\]
By norm-continuity of \(k_n\), choose a nonempty open neighbourhood
\[
        N\subset O_n\cap W
\]
of \(P_0\) such that
\[
        \|k_n(P)-k_n(P_0)\|<\varepsilon\qquad(P\in N).
\]
Choose \(h\in\mathcal B_n\) with \(\supp h\subset N\).  Since \(h\) has
compact support and norm one, there is \(P_1\in\supp h\) such that
\(h(P_1)=1\).

Let \(B(P_1)\in\mathcal B(H_n)\) denote the local operator by which \(b\) acts
in the irreducible representation over \(P_1\).  Since
\[
        a(P_1)=B(P_1)+k_n(P_1)+e(P_1)
\]
and \(P_1\in W\), we have
\[
        \|B(P_1)+k_n(P_1)\|>\gamma/2-C\varepsilon.
\]
Also \(\|k_n(P_1)-T\|<2\varepsilon\), whence
\[
        D:=B(P_1)+T
        \quad\text{satisfies}\quad
        \|D\|>\gamma/2-(C+2)\varepsilon.
\]
By Lemma~\ref{lem:ballast}, choose \(R\in\mathcal R_n\) such that
\[
        \|D+MR\|\ge M+\|D\|-\varepsilon
        >
        M+\gamma/2-(C+3)\varepsilon.
\]
For the center \(c=c(z,n,h,T,R)\), the local value at \(P_1\) is
\[
        B(P_1)+T+MR.
\]
Therefore \(c\) is one of the retained centers.

It remains to show that \(a\in B(c,r)\).  Let \(P\in\supp h\), and put
\(s=h(P)\).  In the chart,
\[
        a(P)-c(P)
        =
        e(P)+k_n(P)-s(T+MR).
\]
Since \(P\in N\),
\[
        \|k_n(P)-T\|<2\varepsilon.
\]
Also
\[
        \|T\|\le \|k_n(P_0)\|+\varepsilon
        \le \|a(P_0)\|+\|\sigma(y)(P_0)\|+\varepsilon
        \le 1+C+\varepsilon<M.
\]
Thus
\[
 \begin{aligned}
        \|a(P)-c(P)\|
        &\le C\varepsilon+2\varepsilon
             +\|(1-s)T-sMR\| \\
        &\le C\varepsilon+2\varepsilon
             +(1-s)\|T\|+sM \\
        &< M+(C+2)\varepsilon
        =r .
 \end{aligned}
\]
If \(P\in\Prim(I)\setminus\supp h\), then the compact bump vanishes at \(P\),
and
\[
        \|a(P)-c(P)\|=\|e(P)+k(P)\|
        \le C\varepsilon+\|a(P)\|+\|\sigma(y)(P)\|
        \le C\varepsilon+1+C<r,
\]
because \(M=C+2\) and \(\varepsilon<1\).  Finally, on primitive quotients
coming from \(F=A/I\), the ideal bump vanishes and
\[
        \|q(a-c)\|=\|y-z\|<\varepsilon<r.
\]
Taking the supremum over primitive quotients gives \(\|a-c\|<r\).  Hence the
retained countable family of balls covers \(S_A\), and the uniform gap proved
above gives UBCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This is a finite-dimensional quotient theorem, but not yet the arbitrary
finite-dimensional quotient theorem.  The new assumption is that the
continuous-trace ideal is uniformly norming in the extension.  That hypothesis
is exactly what lets the matrix-ballast argument find a local ideal point
where the element has definite size.

The remaining case is the non-norming quotient-dominant case: elements whose
norm is seen almost entirely by the finite quotient primitive points while the
continuous-trace ideal sees only a small part.  The next plausible route is to
show that this quotient-dominant part splits off as a finite-dimensional
direct summand, or to build a separate quotient-point covering mechanism.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

The main point to check is Lemma~\ref{lem:ballast} and its use at the point
\(P_1\) where the bump equals one.  Notice that no norm-continuity of the
lifted finite-dimensional quotient field is used: the lifted operator
\(B(P)\) cancels in the distance estimate, and it is used only at the single
point \(P_1\) to lower-bound the center norm.

Reviewers should also check the constants involving \(C=\max(1,\|\sigma\|)\),
\(\gamma\), \(M=C+2\), and \(\varepsilon\), and the outside-support estimate.
