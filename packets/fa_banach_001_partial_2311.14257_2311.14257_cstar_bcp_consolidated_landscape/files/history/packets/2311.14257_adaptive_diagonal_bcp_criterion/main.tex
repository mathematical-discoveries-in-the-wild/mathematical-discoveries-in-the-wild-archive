\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Several previous packets use the same diagonal escape.  A proposed center is
nearly normed on a fresh coordinate, block, annulus, or two-sided corner.  One
then puts a unit vector in that coordinate pointing away from the local image
of the center, and glues all chosen coordinates into one global unit vector.
The global vector stays outside every ball \(B(x_i,\|x_i\|)\).

This packet records the exact form of that mechanism.  The primitive ideal
space cannot see it: a \(\mathrm{II}_1\) factor has one-point primitive ideal
space, but its diffuse finite projection structure supplies adaptive
two-sided corners.

\section*{The Abstract Criterion}

For normed spaces \(E_k\), write
\[
        \ell_\infty(E_k)=\{(u_k): \sup_k\|u_k\|<\infty\}.
\]

\begin{definition}
Let \(X\) be a normed space.  We say that \(X\) has the adaptive exact
diagonal property if for every sequence \((x_i)_{i\ge1}\subset X\) there are:
\begin{itemize}
\item a map \(\pi:\N\to\N\) such that every fiber \(\pi^{-1}(\{i\})\) is
infinite;
\item nonzero normed spaces \(E_k\);
\item contractive linear maps \(R_k:X\to E_k\);
\item a linear isometry \(\Gamma:\ell_\infty(E_k)\to X\);
\item a null sequence \(\eta_k\downarrow0\);
\end{itemize}
such that
\[
        R_k\Gamma((u_j)_{j\ge1})=u_k
        \quad\text{for every }(u_j)\in\ell_\infty(E_j)
\]
and
\[
        \|R_k x_{\pi(k)}\|>\|x_{\pi(k)}\|-\eta_k,\qquad k\ge1.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[one-coordinate escape]
\label{lem:one-coordinate}
Let \(E\ne\{0\}\) be a normed space and let \(y\in E\).  There is
\(u\in S_E\) such that
\[
        \|u-y\|\ge \|y\|.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If \(y=0\), take any \(u\in S_E\).  If \(y\ne0\), choose
\(f\in S_{E^*}\) with \(f(y)=\|y\|\).  Choose \(v\in S_E\) with
\(|f(v)|>0\), and multiply \(v\) by a scalar of modulus one, in the complex
case, or change its sign, in the real case, so that the resulting
\(u\in S_E\) satisfies \(\operatorname{Re}f(u)\le0\).  Then
\[
        \|u-y\|
        \ge
        \operatorname{Re} f(y-u)
        =
        \|y\|-\operatorname{Re}f(u)
        \ge
        \|y\|.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:abstract-diagonal}
If a normed space \(X\) has the adaptive exact diagonal property, then \(X\)
fails BCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We use the equivalent form of BCP saying that \(S_X\) is covered by countably
many open balls \(B(x_i,\|x_i\|)\).

Let \((x_i)\) be an arbitrary countable family in \(X\).  Choose adaptive
diagonal data for this sequence.  For each \(k\), apply
Lemma~\ref{lem:one-coordinate} to \(E_k\) and
\[
        y_k=R_k x_{\pi(k)}
\]
to choose \(u_k\in S_{E_k}\) such that
\[
        \|u_k-R_kx_{\pi(k)}\|\ge \|R_kx_{\pi(k)}\|.
\]
Put
\[
        z=\Gamma((u_k)_{k\ge1}).
\]
Since \(\Gamma\) is an isometry and \(\sup_k\|u_k\|=1\), we have \(z\in S_X\).

Fix \(i\).  Along the infinite set \(\{k:\pi(k)=i\}\),
\[
        \|z-x_i\|
        \ge
        \|R_k(z-x_i)\|
        =
        \|u_k-R_kx_i\|
        \ge
        \|R_kx_i\|
        >
        \|x_i\|-\eta_k .
\]
Letting \(k\to\infty\) through that fiber gives
\[
        \|z-x_i\|\ge \|x_i\|.
\]
Thus \(z\) lies outside every open ball \(B(x_i,\|x_i\|)\).  Since the
countable family of centers was arbitrary, \(X\) fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{C*-Algebra Corner Form}

\begin{definition}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  We say that \(A\) has the adaptive exact
corner property if for every sequence \((a_i)\subset A\) there are
\(\pi:\N\to\N\) with infinite fibers, a null sequence \(\eta_k\downarrow0\),
and pairwise orthogonal projections \(p_k,q_k\in A\) such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item each corner \(q_kAp_k\) is nonzero;
\item there is a linear isometry
\[
        \Gamma:\ell_\infty(q_kAp_k)\to A
\]
that satisfies
\[
        q_k\Gamma((u_j))p_k=u_k;
\]
\item the centers are nearly normed on the assigned corners:
\[
        \|q_ka_{\pi(k)}p_k\|>\|a_{\pi(k)}\|-\eta_k,\qquad k\ge1.
\]
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:cstar-corner}
Every \(C^*\)-algebra with the adaptive exact corner property fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:abstract-diagonal} with
\[
        E_k=q_kAp_k,\qquad R_k(a)=q_kap_k,
\]
and the gluing map \(\Gamma\) from the definition.
\end{proof}

\section*{Von Neumann Corner Gluing}

The exact gluing condition is automatic in von Neumann algebras once the
left and right support projections are orthogonal.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:vn-gluing}
Let \(\M\) be a von Neumann algebra.  Let \((p_k)\) and \((q_k)\) be pairwise
orthogonal sequences of projections in \(\M\).  For every bounded sequence
\[
        u_k\in q_k\M p_k
\]
there is a strong-operator convergent sum
\[
        u=\sum_{k=1}^\infty u_k\in\M
\]
with
\[
        \|u\|=\sup_k\|u_k\|,
        \qquad
        q_kup_k=u_k .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Represent \(\M\) on a Hilbert space \(H\).  For finite \(F\subset\N\),
\[
        \left\|\sum_{k\in F}u_k\right\|\le \sup_k\|u_k\|,
\]
because the initial projections \(p_k\) and final projections \(q_k\) are
orthogonal.  More explicitly, for \(\xi\in H\),
\[
        \left\|\sum_{k\in F}u_k\xi\right\|^2
        =
        \sum_{k\in F}\|u_kp_k\xi\|^2
        \le
        \big(\sup_k\|u_k\|\big)^2\sum_{k\in F}\|p_k\xi\|^2
        \le
        \big(\sup_k\|u_k\|\big)^2\|\xi\|^2 .
\]
The same estimate on tails shows strong convergence of the net of finite
partial sums, and the adjoint sums converge strongly as well.  Since \(\M\)
is strongly closed, the limit belongs to \(\M\).  The equality
\(q_kup_k=u_k\) is immediate from orthogonality, and it gives the reverse
norm inequality.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:vn-corner}
Let \(\M\) be a von Neumann algebra.  Suppose that for every sequence
\((x_i)\subset\M\) there are \(\pi:\N\to\N\) with infinite fibers, a null
sequence \(\eta_k\downarrow0\), and pairwise orthogonal projections
\((p_k)\), \((q_k)\) in \(\M\) such that \(q_k\M p_k\ne\{0\}\) and
\[
        \|q_kx_{\pi(k)}p_k\|>\|x_{\pi(k)}\|-\eta_k .
\]
Then \(\M\) fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Lemma~\ref{lem:vn-gluing} verifies the gluing hypothesis in
Corollary~\ref{cor:cstar-corner}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Exact and Asymptotic Diagonal Examples}

The atomless von Neumann proof in Huang--Kudaybergenov--Liu constructs exactly
the projections required in Corollary~\ref{cor:vn-corner}: after passing to a
\(\sigma\)-finite corner when necessary, their decreasing projection
construction produces pairwise orthogonal differences \(p_k\) and \(q_k\)
with
\[
        \|q_kx_{\pi(k)}p_k\|
        >
        \|x_{\pi(k)}\|-\eta_k .
\]
Thus the source theorem that nontrivial atomless von Neumann algebras fail
BCP is an exact instance of the adaptive corner criterion.

The reduced-product, sigma-unital corona, and Hilbert-module corona packets
are asymptotic versions of the same idea rather than literal instances of
Definition 1.  For a reduced product
\[
        \ell_\infty(F_n)/c_\cI(F_n),
\]
the proof chooses disjoint positive sets and glues one specifically chosen
escape vector on each set.  However arbitrary quotient classes on those sets
do not provide a canonical \(\ell_\infty\)-sum retraction back into the global
quotient: changing representatives on countably many disjoint positive sets
need not produce a difference in \(c_\cI(F_n)\).  For multiplier coronas the
same issue appears analytically: compact or ideal terms vanish only along the
chosen tail blocks.  The natural next strengthening is therefore an
``approximate adaptive diagonal'' criterion, with exact retractions replaced
by tail-vanishing errors.

\section*{Verification and Search Bounds}

The run indexes were searched for arXiv:2311.14257 together with BCP,
diagonal obstruction, orthogonal corners, von Neumann atomless, reduced
products, Hilbert-module block criterion, and sigma-unital coronas.  Existing
packets contained concrete diagonal arguments but no abstract Banach-space
criterion isolating the exact common mechanism.

The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:abstract-diagonal} is formal.  The von Neumann
application uses only standard strong-operator summation over orthogonal
corners.  The source paper is cited only to identify its atomless von Neumann
argument as an instance of the criterion; this packet is not a new proof of
the source paper's full von Neumann classification.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

Check whether the adaptive exact diagonal property is stated at the right
level of generality for future packets.  The immediate proof is short; the
important review question is how far the exact property can be weakened to
cover multiplier coronas and Hilbert-module coronas without redoing each
diagonal argument separately.
