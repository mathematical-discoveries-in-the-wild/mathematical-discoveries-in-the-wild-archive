\section*{Proof Intuition}

In a quotient \(X=Y/J\), the useful local norming maps are often defined on
the ambient space \(Y\), not on the quotient.  For example, in a multiplier
corona one tests a multiplier on a far-out annulus; adding an element of the
ideal changes the annulus, but by a quantity that tends to zero along the
tail.  The same phenomenon appears in reduced products and Hilbert-module
coronas.

The criterion below isolates exactly what the diagonal proof needs.  Each
center is assigned infinitely many local tests.  The assigned tests nearly
norm that center.  The ideal \(J\) vanishes along those tests.  Finally, one
global quotient unit has prescribed local behavior, chosen to point away from
the corresponding local center.  These four properties force the quotient
unit to avoid every ball \(B(\xi_i,\|\xi_i\|)\).

\section*{Criterion}

Let \(Y\) be a normed space, let \(J\subseteq Y\) be a closed subspace, and
write
\[
        q:Y\longrightarrow X:=Y/J
\]
for the quotient map.

\begin{definition}
\label{def:asymptotic-diagonal}
The quotient \(X=Y/J\) has the asymptotic diagonal escape property if for
every sequence \((y_i)_{i\ge1}\subset Y\) there are:
\begin{itemize}
\item a map \(\pi:\N\to\N\) such that every fiber \(\pi^{-1}(\{i\})\) is
infinite;
\item normed spaces \(E_k\);
\item contractive linear maps \(L_k:Y\to E_k\);
\item a null sequence \(\eta_k\downarrow0\);
\item vectors \(u_k\in E_k\);
\item a vector \(y\in Y\) with \(q(y)\in S_X\);
\end{itemize}
such that the following hold.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Assigned near-norming.}
\[
        \|L_k y_{\pi(k)}\|
        \ge
        \|q(y_{\pi(k)})\|-\eta_k,\qquad k\ge1.
\]

\item \emph{Ideal vanishing along fibers.}  For every \(c\in J\) and every
\(i\ge1\),
\[
        \lim_{\substack{k\to\infty\\ \pi(k)=i}}\|L_kc\|=0.
\]

\item \emph{Asymptotic gluing.}
\[
        \|L_ky-u_k\|\le \eta_k,\qquad k\ge1.
\]

\item \emph{Local escape.}
\[
        \|u_k-L_k y_{\pi(k)}\|
        \ge
        \|L_k y_{\pi(k)}\|-\eta_k,\qquad k\ge1.
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:asymptotic-diagonal}
If \(X=Y/J\) has the asymptotic diagonal escape property, then \(X\) fails
BCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We use the standard equivalent form of BCP: if \(X\) has BCP, then \(S_X\) is
covered by countably many open balls \(B(\xi_i,\|\xi_i\|)\).

Let \((\xi_i)_{i\ge1}\subset X\) be an arbitrary countable family of centers,
and choose representatives \(y_i\in Y\) with \(q(y_i)=\xi_i\).  Apply the
asymptotic diagonal escape property to \((y_i)\), obtaining the data from
Definition~\ref{def:asymptotic-diagonal}.  Put
\[
        \zeta=q(y)\in S_X.
\]

Fix \(i\ge1\).  We prove that
\[
        \|\zeta-\xi_i\|\ge \|\xi_i\|.
\]
Let \(c\in J\).  For each \(k\) with \(\pi(k)=i\), contractivity gives
\[
        \|y-y_i-c\|
        \ge
        \|L_k(y-y_i-c)\|.
\]
Using asymptotic gluing, local escape, and assigned near-norming,
\begin{align*}
        \|L_k(y-y_i-c)\|
        &\ge
        \|u_k-L_ky_i\|-\|L_ky-u_k\|-\|L_kc\|\\
        &\ge
        \|L_ky_i\|-2\eta_k-\|L_kc\|\\
        &\ge
        \|\xi_i\|-3\eta_k-\|L_kc\|.
\end{align*}
Letting \(k\to\infty\) through the infinite fiber \(\pi^{-1}(\{i\})\), and
using ideal vanishing, gives
\[
        \|y-y_i-c\|\ge \|\xi_i\|.
\]
Since \(c\in J\) was arbitrary,
\[
        \|\zeta-\xi_i\|
        =
        \inf_{c\in J}\|y-y_i-c\|
        \ge
        \|\xi_i\|.
\]
Thus \(\zeta\in S_X\) is outside every open ball
\[
        B(\xi_i,\|\xi_i\|).
\]
The countable family of centers was arbitrary, so \(X\) fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{How to Produce the Local Escape Vectors}

The criterion separates the formal diagonal argument from the local
construction.  In most applications \(u_k\) is obtained by a Hahn--Banach
phase or sign choice.

\begin{lemma}[local sign escape]
\label{lem:local-sign}
Let \(E\ne\{0\}\) be a real or complex normed space and let \(v\in E\).  There
is \(u\in S_E\) such that
\[
        \|u-v\|\ge \|v\|.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If \(v=0\), choose any \(u\in S_E\).  If \(v\ne0\), choose \(f\in S_{E^*}\)
with \(f(v)=\|v\|\).  Choose \(w\in S_E\) with \(|f(w)|>0\), and multiply
\(w\) by a scalar of modulus one in the complex case, or change sign in the
real case, so that \(u\in S_E\) satisfies \(\operatorname{Re}f(u)\le0\).
Then
\[
        \|u-v\|\ge \operatorname{Re}f(v-u)\ge \|v\|.
\]
\end{proof}

\section*{Relation to the Exact Criterion}

The exact adaptive diagonal criterion from the previous packet is the special
case \(J=\{0\}\) where the local maps \(L_k\) are genuine quotient maps
\(R_k:X\to E_k\), the gluing is exact, and \(\eta_k=0\).  In particular, if
one has a linear isometry
\[
        \Gamma:\ell_\infty(E_k)\to X,\qquad R_k\Gamma((u_j))=u_k,
\]
and the assigned coordinates nearly norm the centers, then choosing local
sign escapes \(u_k\) and setting \(y=\Gamma((u_k))\) verifies
Definition~\ref{def:asymptotic-diagonal}.

\section*{How the Earlier Diagonal Packets Fit}

This section is a guide for applying the criterion; it is not a replacement
for the full proofs in the earlier packets.

\subsection*{Sigma-Unital Multiplier Coronas}

For a nonunital \(\sigma\)-unital \(C^*\)-algebra \(A\), take
\[
        Y=\M(A),\qquad J=A,\qquad X=\M(A)/A.
\]
With an increasing approximate unit \((e_n)\) satisfying
\(e_me_n=e_n\) for \(m\ge n\), the local tests are
\[
        L_k(T)=Tg_k,\qquad g_k=e_{m_k}-e_{n_k},
\]
where the annuli \(g_k\) are chosen far out.  Finite annulus recovery gives
assigned near-norming:
\[
        \|T_{\pi(k)}g_k\|\approx \|q(T_{\pi(k)})\|.
\]
For \(C\in A\), tail convergence gives
\[
        \|Cg_k\|\to0,
\]
which is ideal vanishing.  The local escape vectors are phased annuli
\(u_k=\lambda_kg_k\), chosen by the sign trick against
\(T_{\pi(k)}g_k\).  The global multiplier
\[
        z=\sum_k\lambda_kh_k
\]
over larger orthogonal annuli satisfies \(zg_k=u_k\), and an auxiliary
subsequence of annuli verifies \(\|q(z)\|=1\).  These are exactly the
ingredients of Definition~\ref{def:asymptotic-diagonal}.

\subsection*{Hilbert-Module Coronas}

For a Hilbert module \(E\) with a BCP block system, take
\[
        Y=\mathcal L(E),\qquad J=\mathcal K(E),\qquad
        X=\mathcal L(E)/\mathcal K(E).
\]
The local tests are
\[
        L_k(T)=Te_{F_k},
\]
where \(e_{F_k}\) is a fresh finite block projection.  Tail norm recovery
gives assigned near-norming, compact-vanishing gives ideal vanishing, fresh
range escape supplies local \(u_k=v_k\), and strict gluing supplies the global
operator \(V\) with \(Ve_{F_k}=v_k\).  The previous Hilbert-module packet is
therefore an instance of the quotient criterion.

\subsection*{Reduced Products}

For reduced products
\[
        X=\ell_\infty(E_n)/c_\cI(E_n),
\]
the ambient space is \(Y=\ell_\infty(E_n)\) and \(J=c_\cI(E_n)\).  Given
centers, the proof chooses disjoint \(\cI\)-positive sets \(T_k\) on which
the assigned center is nearly normed.  The local maps are restrictions
\[
        L_k((a_n))
        =
        (a_n)_{n\in T_k}
        +c_{\cI|T_k}(E_n:n\in T_k)
\]
viewed in the local reduced quotient
\[
        \ell_\infty(E_n:n\in T_k)/c_{\cI|T_k}(E_n:n\in T_k).
\]
Thus elements of \(J\) vanish exactly under every \(L_k\).  The glued
sequence is built by putting the locally escaping representatives on the
disjoint sets \(T_k\) and zero elsewhere; the reduced-product norm formula
checks that the resulting quotient class is a unit.  This is the same
asymptotic diagonal pattern in coordinate form.
