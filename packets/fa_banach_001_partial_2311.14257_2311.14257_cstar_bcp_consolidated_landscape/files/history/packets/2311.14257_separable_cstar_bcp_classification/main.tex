\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:separable-ubcp}
Every separable normed space has UBCP.  Consequently every separable
\(C^*\)-algebra has UBCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:separable-classification}
If \(A\) is a separable \(C^*\)-algebra, then the following hold:
\[
        A \text{ has UBCP},\qquad
        A \text{ has BCP},\qquad
        \Prim(A) \text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-base}.
\]
In particular, the countably generated \(C^*\)-algebra case of the
2311.14257 question is settled positively.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The proof separates into two familiar facts.  First, separable normed spaces
have a countable dense set in the unit sphere.  If \(d\) is close to a unit
vector \(x\), then \(x\) lies in the ball centered at \(2d\) of radius
\(3/2\); this ball avoids the origin because its center has norm \(2\).  This
is a uniform ball cover of the unit sphere.

Second, if \(A\) is a separable \(C^*\)-algebra, then \(\Prim(A)\) is second
countable.  A countable dense set in \(A\), together with rational thresholds,
gives a countable base by primitive near-norm sets
\[
        \{P\in\Prim(A):\|a+P\|>t\}.
\]
Thus the primitive pi-base condition from the previous obstruction packet is
automatic in the separable case.

\section*{Proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:separable-normed-ubcp}
Every separable normed space \(X\) has UBCP.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If \(X=\{0\}\), the unit sphere is empty and there is nothing to prove.  Assume
\(X\ne\{0\}\).  Let \(D\subseteq S_X\) be countable and \(1/4\)-dense in
\(S_X\).  For each \(d\in D\), put
\[
        c_d=2d.
\]
Then \(\|c_d\|=2\), so the open ball \(B(c_d,3/2)\) avoids the origin.  If
\(x\in S_X\), choose \(d\in D\) with \(\|x-d\|<1/4\).  Then
\[
        \|x-c_d\|
        =
        \|x-2d\|
        \le
        \|x-d\|+\|d\|
        <
        \frac14+1
        <
        \frac32.
\]
Hence
\[
        S_X\subseteq \bigcup_{d\in D} B(2d,3/2),
\]
a countable equal-radius cover by balls off the origin.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:separable-ubcp}]
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:separable-normed-ubcp} to the underlying normed space of
the separable \(C^*\)-algebra.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:prim-second-countable}
If \(A\) is a separable \(C^*\)-algebra, then \(\Prim(A)\) is second
countable.  In particular it has a countable \(\pi\)-base.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(D\) be a countable dense subset of \(A\), and let
\[
        \mathcal B=
        \bigl\{
        U(d,q): d\in D,\ q\in\mathbb Q,\ q>0
        \bigr\},
\]
where
\[
        U(d,q)=\{P\in\Prim(A):\|d+P\|>q\}.
\]
Each \(U(d,q)\) is open: as in the primitive pi-base obstruction packet,
\[
        U(d,q)=\{P: (d^*d-q^2)_+\notin P\}.
\]

We show that \(\mathcal B\) is a base.  Let \(U_I=\{P:I\not\subseteq P\}\)
be a basic open set corresponding to a nonzero closed two-sided ideal \(I\),
and let \(P_0\in U_I\).  Choose \(b\in I\) with \(b\notin P_0\).  Replacing
\(b\) by \(b^*b\) and rescaling if necessary, assume \(b\ge0\) and
\(\|b+P_0\|>0\).  Choose \(0<\eta<\|b+P_0\|/3\), and choose \(d\in D\) with
\[
        \|d-b\|<\eta.
\]
Pick a rational \(q\) with
\[
        \eta<q<\|b+P_0\|-\eta.
\]
Then \(P_0\in U(d,q)\), because
\[
        \|d+P_0\|\ge \|b+P_0\|-\eta>q.
\]
If \(P\notin U_I\), then \(I\subseteq P\), so \(b\in P\), and hence
\[
        \|d+P\|\le \|d-b\|<\eta<q.
\]
Thus \(U(d,q)\subseteq U_I\).  Therefore \(\mathcal B\) refines every basic
open neighborhood, so it is a countable base for \(\Prim(A)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:separable-classification}]
The UBCP assertion is Theorem~\ref{thm:separable-ubcp}, and BCP follows
immediately from UBCP.  Lemma~\ref{lem:prim-second-countable} gives a
countable \(\pi\)-base for \(\Prim(A)\).

Finally, a countably generated \(C^*\)-algebra is separable: the
\(*\)-polynomials with coefficients in \(\mathbb Q+i\mathbb Q\) in a countable
generating set form a countable dense subset of the generated algebra.  Hence
the countably generated case follows from the separable case.
\end{proof}

\section*{Relation to the General Problem}

This packet settles the separable/countably generated subcase, but it also
explains why the remaining problem is genuinely nonseparable.  The previous
primitive pi-base obstruction says that BCP forces \(\Prim(A)\) to have a
countable \(\pi\)-base for every \(C^*\)-algebra.  In separable algebras this
condition is automatic, and the Banach-space separability argument supplies
UBCP.

For nonseparable algebras, neither side is automatic.  Commutative examples
show that nonseparable algebras can still have BCP when the underlying
topological space has a countable \(\pi\)-base, while the primitive pi-base
obstruction rules out many large ideal lattices.  Thus the remaining frontier
is the nonseparable, countable-\(\pi\)-base case.
