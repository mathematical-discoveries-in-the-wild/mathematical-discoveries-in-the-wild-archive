\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Definitions}

Let \(X\) be a nonzero normed space over \(\F\in\{\mathbb R,\mathbb C\}\).
We say that \(X\) has the \emph{countable-capture property} if for every
countable set \(C\subset X\) there are a normed space \(Y_C\), a contraction
\[
        E_C:X\to Y_C,
\]
and \(u_C\in S_X\) such that
\[
        E_Cu_C=0,
        \qquad
        \|E_Cc\|=\|c\|\quad(c\in C).
\]
The previous packet
\[
\texttt{2311.14257\_countable\_capture\_bcp\_obstruction}
\]
proved that this property forces failure of BCP.

For \(x\in X\setminus\{0\}\), write
\[
        J(x)=\{f\in X^*: \|f\|=1,\ f(x)=\|x\|\}
\]
for the norming functionals of \(x\).

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[exact Banach-space characterization]
\label{thm:banach}
For a nonzero normed space \(X\), the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(X\) has the countable-capture property.
    \item For every countable \(C\subset X\), there exists \(u\in S_X\) such
          that
          \[
                \dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|\qquad(c\in C).
          \]
    \item For every countable \(C\subset X\setminus\{0\}\), there are
          functionals \(f_c\in J(c)\), \(c\in C\), such that
          \[
                \bigcap_{c\in C}\ker f_c
          \]
          contains a nonzero vector.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[primitive-space source of countable capture]
\label{thm:primitive-capture}
Let \(A\) be a nonzero \(C^*\)-algebra.  If \(\Prim(A)\) has no countable
\(\pi\)-base, then the underlying Banach space of \(A\) has the
countable-capture property.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[commutative exact classification]
\label{cor:commutative}
Let \(K\) be a nonempty locally compact Hausdorff space.  Then \(C_0(K)\) has
the countable-capture property if and only if \(K\) has no countable
\(\pi\)-base.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Hilbert spaces]
\label{cor:hilbert}
A nonzero Hilbert space \(H\) has the countable-capture property if and only
if \(H\) is nonseparable.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[reduced group C*-algebras]
\label{cor:reduced-group}
For a discrete group \(G\), \(\Cr(G)\) has the countable-capture property if
and only if \(G\) is uncountable.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The contraction in the countable-capture definition is not mysterious.  If a
contraction kills \(u\), it factors through the quotient \(X/\F u\).  Since
the quotient map is the largest contractive map killing \(u\), exact norm
preservation of \(c\) is equivalent to
\[
        \|c+\F u\|_{X/\F u}=\|c\|,
\]
that is, \(\dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|\).  Hahn-Banach then says the same thing in
dual language: each \(c\) has a norming functional vanishing on the same
fresh direction \(u\).

For \(C^*\)-algebras with no countable primitive \(\pi\)-base, the fresh
direction comes from an open ideal.  Given countably many centres \(a_n\),
form the countable family of primitive near-norm sets
\[
        \{P:\|a_n+P\|>\|a_n\|-\varepsilon\}.
\]
Since these sets are not a \(\pi\)-base, there is a nonempty open set \(V\)
containing none of them.  A norm-one element \(u\) in the ideal for \(V\)
vanishes in primitive quotients outside \(V\), while every \(a_n\) is
arbitrarily near normed in such quotients.  Hence
\[
        \dist(a_n,\C u)=\|a_n\|
\]
for all \(n\).

\section*{Proof of the Banach-Space Characterization}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:banach}]
Suppose first that \(X\) has countable capture, and fix a countable set
\(C\subset X\).  Choose \(E:X\to Y\) and \(u\in S_X\) as in the definition.
Let \(q:X\to X/\F u\) be the quotient map.  Since \(E(u)=0\), the map \(E\)
factors through \(q\): there is a contraction \(\widetilde E:X/\F u\to Y\)
such that \(E=\widetilde E q\).  Therefore, for every \(c\in C\),
\[
        \|c\|=\|Ec\|\le \|q(c)\|\le \|c\|.
\]
Thus \(\|q(c)\|=\|c\|\), which is exactly
\(\dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|\).  This proves (1) implies (2).

Conversely, if (2) holds, take \(Y=X/\F u\) and \(E=q\), the quotient map.
Then \(q\) is a contraction, \(q(u)=0\), and
\[
        \|q(c)\|=\dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|
\]
for every \(c\in C\).  Hence \(X\) has countable capture.

It remains to compare (2) and (3).  Assume (2), and fix a countable
\(C\subset X\setminus\{0\}\).  Choose \(u\in S_X\) such that
\(\dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|\) for all \(c\in C\).  For each \(c\), the quotient
element \(q(c)\in X/\F u\) has norm \(\|c\|\).  By Hahn-Banach, choose a
norm-one functional \(\varphi_c\) on \(X/\F u\) such that
\[
        \varphi_c(q(c))=\|q(c)\|=\|c\|.
\]
Then \(f_c=\varphi_c q\) belongs to \(J(c)\), and \(f_c(u)=0\).  Thus
\[
        u\in \bigcap_{c\in C}\ker f_c.
\]

Conversely, assume (3).  Given countable \(C\subset X\), discard the zero
vectors.  Choose \(f_c\in J(c)\) with a nonzero common kernel vector \(v\),
and put \(u=v/\|v\|\).  For \(c\ne0\) and every scalar \(\lambda\),
\[
        \|c-\lambda u\|
        \ge
        |f_c(c-\lambda u)|
        =
        \|c\|.
\]
Taking the infimum over \(\lambda\) gives
\(\dist(c,\F u)\ge\|c\|\), while the reverse inequality follows by taking
\(\lambda=0\).  Hence \(\dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|\) for all \(c\in C\), so (2)
holds.
\end{proof}

\section*{Primitive-Ideal Capture}

We use two standard facts.  First, open subsets of \(\Prim(A)\) correspond to
closed two-sided ideals: if \(I\triangleleft A\), then
\[
        U_I=\{P\in\Prim(A): I\not\subseteq P\}
\]
is open, and every open set arises this way.  Second,
\[
        \|a\|=\sup_{P\in\Prim(A)}\|a+P\|.
\]

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:near-norm-open}
For \(a\in A\) and \(t>0\), the set
\[
        U(a,t)=\{P\in\Prim(A):\|a+P\|>t\}
\]
is open in \(\Prim(A)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The element \(b=(a^*a-t^2)_+\) satisfies
\[
        b+P=((a+P)^*(a+P)-t^2)_+
\]
in \(A/P\).  Hence \(b\notin P\) if and only if \(\|a+P\|>t\).  Thus
\[
        U(a,t)=\{P:b\notin P\}=U_{\overline{AbA}},
\]
which is open.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:primitive-capture}]
Let \(C=\{a_1,a_2,\ldots\}\subset A\) be countable.  For every \(n\) with
\(r_n=\|a_n\|>0\), and every integer \(m\) with \(1/m<r_n\), set
\[
        U_{n,m}
        =
        \{P\in\Prim(A):\|a_n+P\|>r_n-1/m\}.
\]
By Lemma~\ref{lem:near-norm-open}, these are nonempty open sets, and the
whole family is countable.  Since \(\Prim(A)\) has no countable \(\pi\)-base,
there is a nonempty open set \(V\subseteq\Prim(A)\) such that no \(U_{n,m}\)
is contained in \(V\).

Let \(I\triangleleft A\) be the nonzero ideal with \(V=U_I\).  Choose
\(u\in I\) with \(\|u\|=1\).  Fix \(n\).  If \(a_n=0\), then
\(\dist(a_n,\C u)=0=\|a_n\|\).  If \(r_n>0\), then for all sufficiently large
\(m\) we may choose
\[
        P_{n,m}\in U_{n,m}\setminus V.
\]
Because \(P_{n,m}\notin V=U_I\), we have \(I\subseteq P_{n,m}\), hence
\(u\in P_{n,m}\).  Therefore for every scalar \(\lambda\),
\[
        \|a_n-\lambda u\|
        \ge
        \|a_n-\lambda u+P_{n,m}\|
        =
        \|a_n+P_{n,m}\|
        >
        r_n-\frac1m.
\]
Letting \(m\to\infty\) gives
\[
        \|a_n-\lambda u\|\ge r_n
        \qquad(\lambda\in\C).
\]
Thus \(\dist(a_n,\C u)\ge r_n\), and equality follows by taking
\(\lambda=0\).  Theorem~\ref{thm:banach} now gives countable capture.
\end{proof}

\section*{Model Classifications}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:commutative}]
For \(A=C_0(K)\), the primitive ideal space is homeomorphic to \(K\).  If
\(K\) has no countable \(\pi\)-base, Theorem~\ref{thm:primitive-capture}
gives countable capture.

Conversely, if \(K\) has a countable \(\pi\)-base, then \(C_0(K)\) has UBCP
by the commutative \(C_0(K)\) theorem of Liu--Liu--Lu--Zheng.  Since
countable capture implies failure of BCP, \(C_0(K)\) cannot have countable
capture.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:hilbert}]
If \(H\) is nonseparable and \(C\subset H\) is countable, the closed linear
span \(L=\overline{\operatorname{span}}C\) is separable and hence proper.
Choose \(u\in S_H\cap L^\perp\).  Then for every \(c\in C\),
\[
        \dist(c,\F u)=\|c\|.
\]
So \(H\) has countable capture by Theorem~\ref{thm:banach}.

If \(H\) is separable, then \(H\) has UBCP because every separable normed
space has UBCP: choose a countable dense subset \((d_j)\) of \(S_H\) and use
the balls \(B(2d_j,3/2)\).  Since countable capture forces failure of BCP,
separable \(H\) cannot have countable capture.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:reduced-group}]
If \(G\) is countable, then \(\Cr(G)\) is separable, hence has UBCP, so it
does not have countable capture.

If \(G\) is uncountable and \(C\subset \Cr(G)\) is countable, the reduced
group packet shows that \(C\subset \Cr(H)\) for some countable subgroup
\(H\le G\).  Choose \(g\in G\setminus H\).  The canonical subgroup
expectation
\[
        E_H:\Cr(G)\to \Cr(H)
\]
fixes \(\Cr(H)\) and kills \(\lambda_g\).  Thus \(E_H\) and
\(u=\lambda_g\) witness countable capture for \(C\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Remaining Wall}

This packet classifies the countable-capture mechanism itself in Banach-space
terms and identifies broad exact model classes.  It also upgrades the
primitive \(\pi\)-base obstruction: if the primitive space has no countable
\(\pi\)-base, then the obstruction is literally countable capture.

It does not classify all \(C^*\)-algebras with countable capture.  The hard
remaining region is where \(\Prim(A)\) already has a countable \(\pi\)-base.
This includes simple nonseparable algebras, atomless von Neumann factors, and
corona/reduced-product examples.  In that region the obstruction must come
from an internal norm-visibility mechanism, such as subgroup expectations,
annular diagonal maps, or von Neumann corner decompositions, rather than from
the topology of \(\Prim(A)\).
