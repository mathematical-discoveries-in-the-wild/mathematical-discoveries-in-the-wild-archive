\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when its pure state space, i.e.
\(K\), has a countable \(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative
version:

\section*{Statement}

The previous continuous-trace packet proves the positive theorem when the
whole algebra is locally a continuous field of compact operators.  The
obstruction to extending that proof is that an element of a larger algebra may
act on a continuous-trace ideal only as a multiplier.  The following criterion
isolates exactly the extra local regularity that is needed.

\begin{definition}
\label{def:chart}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  A \emph{compact-norming chart} for \(A\)
consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item a nonempty open subset \(O\subset \Prim(A)\), with corresponding
    ideal \(I_O\);
    \item a separable nonzero Hilbert space \(H_O\);
    \item a \(C_0(O)\)-isomorphism
    \[
        \Phi_O:I_O\longrightarrow C_0(O,\Kcal(H_O));
    \]
    \item for every \(a\in A\), a bounded norm-continuous function
    \[
        \widehat a_O:O\longrightarrow \Kcal(H_O)
    \]
    such that \(\widehat b_O=\Phi_O(b)\) for \(b\in I_O\), and
    \[
        \|a+P\|=\|\widehat a_O(P)\|\qquad(P\in O).
    \]
\end{enumerate}
Here \(a+P\) denotes the image of \(a\) in the primitive quotient associated
with \(P\).
\end{definition}

We say that \(A\) has a \emph{countable compact-norming chart \(\pi\)-basis}
if there are compact-norming charts \((O_n,\Phi_n,H_n)_{n\ge1}\) such that
\((O_n)\) is a \(\pi\)-basis of \(\Prim(A)\).

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  Assume that \(A\) has a countable
compact-norming chart \(\pi\)-basis.  Then \(A\) has UBCP.  Consequently, for
every \(C^*\)-algebra satisfying this chart hypothesis, the following are
equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(A\) has BCP;
    \item \(A\) has UBCP;
    \item \(\Prim(A)\) has a countable \(\pi\)-basis.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The chart hypothesis says that on countably many primitive-open patches, every
ambient element of \(A\) is not merely a multiplier of a continuous-trace
ideal: it is represented by a norm-continuous compact-operator section.  That
is exactly what the continuous-trace bump proof needs.

Given \(a\in S_A\), the set where \(\|a+P\|>3/4\) is open.  Choose a chart
\(O_n\) inside this set.  In that chart, \(\widehat a_n\) is a continuous
compact-operator field.  At one point \(P_0\), choose a countable net direction
\(T\in \Kcal(H_n)\) close to the normalized compact operator
\(\widehat a_n(P_0)/\|\widehat a_n(P_0)\|\).  A scalar bump \(h\) supported
inside the chart gives the center \(\Phi_n^{-1}(4hT)\).  The same convexity
estimate as in the continuous-trace proof gives a fixed radius \(7/2\), while
all centers have norm \(4\).

\section*{Proof}

We use the general primitive-space obstruction proved in the companion packet
\cite{lane19primitive}.

\begin{lemma}[primitive \(\pi\)-base obstruction]
\label{lem:primitive}
If a \(C^*\)-algebra \(B\) has BCP, then \(\Prim(B)\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[countable bump families inside the charts]
\label{lem:bumps}
For each chart \(O_n\), there is a countable family
\(\mathcal B_n\subset C_c(O_n)\) such that \(0\le h\le1\),
\(\|h\|_\infty=1\), and for every nonempty open \(W\subset O_n\), some
\(h\in\mathcal B_n\) satisfies \(\supp h\subset W\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since the sets \(O_m\) form a \(\pi\)-basis of \(\Prim(A)\), the subfamily
\[
        \{O_m:O_m\subset O_n\}
\]
is a \(\pi\)-basis for \(O_n\).  The chart identifies \(O_n\) with the
primitive space of the continuous-trace algebra \(I_{O_n}\), hence \(O_n\) is
locally compact Hausdorff.  For each \(m\) with \(O_m\subset O_n\), choose
\[
        h_{n,m}\in C_c(O_n),\qquad
        0\le h_{n,m}\le1,\qquad
        \|h_{n,m}\|_\infty=1,\qquad
        \supp h_{n,m}\subset O_m.
\]
Local compactness and regularity give such a bump.  The countable family of
all \(h_{n,m}\)'s has the required cofinal support property.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
For each \(n\), choose a countable \(1/64\)-net
\[
        D_n\subset S_{\Kcal(H_n)}
\]
in the unit sphere of \(\Kcal(H_n)\).  This is possible because \(H_n\) is
separable.  For \(h\in\mathcal B_n\) and \(T\in D_n\), define
\[
        c(n,h,T)=\Phi_n^{-1}(4hT)\in I_{O_n}\subset A.
\]
The family of all such centers is countable, and
\[
        \|c(n,h,T)\|=4.
\]
We claim that the balls \(B(c(n,h,T),7/2)\) cover \(S_A\).  Since all centers
have norm \(4\), these balls avoid \(B(0,1/2)\), and the claim proves UBCP.

Let \(a\in S_A\).  The function \(P\mapsto \|a+P\|\) on \(\Prim(A)\) is lower
semicontinuous, so
\[
        W_a=\{P\in\Prim(A):\|a+P\|>3/4\}
\]
is nonempty open.  Choose \(n\) with \(O_n\subset W_a\), and choose
\(P_0\in O_n\).  Put
\[
        \alpha=\|\widehat a_n(P_0)\|>3/4,\qquad
        u=\widehat a_n(P_0)/\alpha\in S_{\Kcal(H_n)}.
\]
Choose \(T\in D_n\) with \(\|T-u\|<1/64\).

For \(0\le s\le1\), the map
\[
        s\mapsto \|\alpha u-4sT\|
\]
is convex.  At \(s=0\) the value is \(\alpha\le1\), while at \(s=1\),
\[
        \|\alpha u-4T\|
        \le \alpha\|u-T\|+(4-\alpha)\|T\|
        < 4-\alpha(1-1/64)
        < 7/2.
\]
Hence
\[
        \sup_{0\le s\le1}\|\widehat a_n(P_0)-4sT\|<7/2.
\]
By norm-continuity of \(\widehat a_n\), there is a nonempty open neighborhood
\(N\subset O_n\) of \(P_0\) such that
\[
        \sup_{0\le s\le1}\|\widehat a_n(P)-4sT\|<7/2
        \qquad(P\in N).
\]
Choose \(h\in\mathcal B_n\) with \(\supp h\subset N\), and set
\[
        c=c(n,h,T).
\]

For \(P\in\supp h\), using \(s=h(P)\) gives
\[
        \|a+P-(c+P)\|
        =
        \|\widehat a_n(P)-4h(P)T\|
        <7/2.
\]
For \(P\notin\supp h\), the image of \(c\) in the primitive quotient at \(P\)
is zero: if \(P\notin O_n\), this is because \(c\in I_{O_n}\subset P\); if
\(P\in O_n\setminus\supp h\), it follows from the displayed section
\(4hT\).  Thus
\[
        \|a+P-(c+P)\|=\|a+P\|\le1<7/2.
\]
Taking the supremum over primitive quotients gives \(\|a-c\|<7/2\).
Therefore \(a\) is covered by one of the countably many fixed-radius balls.

This proves UBCP.  The implication UBCP \(\Rightarrow\) BCP is immediate, and
Lemma~\ref{lem:primitive} gives BCP \(\Rightarrow\) countable \(\pi\)-basis
for every \(C^*\)-algebra.  Under the chart hypothesis, the three conditions
are therefore equivalent.
\end{proof}

\section*{Why This Breaks the Previous Wall}

The previous failed route tried to use continuous-trace ideals inside a larger
type-I algebra.  The failure mode was precise: an ambient element may act on
such an ideal only as a multiplier, for example \(B(H)\) acting on \(\Kcal(H)\).
The present criterion adds exactly the missing hypothesis, namely that on the
selected patches the ambient element is represented by norm-continuous compact
operators.  Under that hypothesis, the bump-center proof goes through
unchanged and does not need a global central \(C_0(X)\)-algebra structure.

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This is a partial criterion, not a full arbitrary \(C^*\)-classification.  It
applies to any class where one can verify the compact-norming chart
\(\pi\)-basis condition.  It recovers the continuous-trace/separable-fibre
packet by taking local trivial continuous-trace charts.  It also explains why
unitizations or multiplier algebras with infinite-dimensional fibres remain
hard: scalar identity directions are not compact local sections, so they do
not satisfy Definition~\ref{def:chart} without extra arguments.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

The main point to check is Definition~\ref{def:chart}: it is intentionally
stronger than merely having continuous-trace ideals.  Once the chart
representatives \(\widehat a_n\) are available for all \(a\in A\), the proof
is the same fixed-constant bump argument as in the continuous-trace packet.
Reviewers should also check the use of lower semicontinuity of
\(P\mapsto\|a+P\|\) and the fact that \(c\in I_{O_n}\) vanishes in primitive
quotients outside \(O_n\).
