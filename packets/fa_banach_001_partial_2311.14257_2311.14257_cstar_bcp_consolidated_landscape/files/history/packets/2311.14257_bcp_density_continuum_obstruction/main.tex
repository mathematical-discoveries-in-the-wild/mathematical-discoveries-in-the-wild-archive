\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:banach-density}
Let \(X\) be a complex Banach space.  If \(X\) has BCP, then
\[
        |X|\le |\C^{\mathbb N}|=\mathfrak c.
\]
In particular \(\dens(X)\le\mathfrak c\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:cstar-density}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  If \(\dens(A)>\mathfrak c\), then \(A\)
fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

A BCP cover has countably many centres \(c_n\) and radii \(r_n<\|c_n\|\).
Choose one norming functional \(f_n\) for each nonzero centre.  If the
functionals failed to separate points, a nonzero common-kernel vector \(u\)
could be normalized.  Since \(f_n(u)=0\), every centre would be at distance at
least \(\|c_n\|\) from \(u\), so \(u\) would escape all balls in the cover.
Therefore the chosen norming functionals separate points, and \(X\) embeds
linearly into \(\C^{\mathbb N}\).  This forces cardinality at most continuum.

\section*{Proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:banach-density}]
Suppose \(X\) has BCP.  Use the equivalent open-ball formulation.  Then there
are centres \(c_n\in X\) and radii \(r_n>0\) such that
\[
        S_X\subseteq \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B^\circ(c_n,r_n),
        \qquad
        0\notin B^\circ(c_n,r_n)
\]
for every \(n\).  Thus \(r_n\le\|c_n\|\), and no centre relevant to the cover
is zero.

For each \(n\), choose \(f_n\in X^*\) with
\[
        \|f_n\|=1,\qquad f_n(c_n)=\|c_n\|,
\]
using Hahn--Banach.

We claim that \(\{f_n:n\in\mathbb N\}\) separates points of \(X\).  If not,
there is a nonzero \(v\in X\) such that \(f_n(v)=0\) for every \(n\).  Put
\(u=v/\|v\|\in S_X\).  Then, for every \(n\),
\[
        \|u-c_n\|
        \ge |f_n(u-c_n)|
        =
        |-\|c_n\||
        =
        \|c_n\|
        \ge
        r_n.
\]
Thus \(u\) belongs to none of the open covering balls, a contradiction.

Therefore the linear map
\[
        T:X\to\C^{\mathbb N},
        \qquad
        T(x)=(f_n(x))_{n=1}^{\infty},
\]
is injective.  Since \(|\C^{\mathbb N}|=\mathfrak c\), we get
\(|X|\le\mathfrak c\).  The density character of a metric space is bounded
above by its cardinality, so \(\dens(X)\le\mathfrak c\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:cstar-density}]
A \(C^*\)-algebra is a complex Banach space under its \(C^*\)-norm.  If it had
BCP, Theorem~\ref{thm:banach-density} would give density at most
\(\mathfrak c\).  Hence density strictly larger than \(\mathfrak c\) forces
failure of BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope for the C*-Classification}

This obstruction is independent of primitive ideal theory.  It applies to all
Banach spaces, hence to all \(C^*\)-algebras.  In the C*-classification
program for arXiv:2311.14257, it removes all algebras of density greater than
the continuum from the positive side.

It does not settle the remaining density-\(\mathfrak c\) simple
nonseparable case.  The countable product \(\ell_\infty(\mathcal O_2)\) has
density \(\mathfrak c\) and UBCP by the product dichotomy packet, so the
cardinal bound is sharp even among nonseparable non-Type-I \(C^*\)-algebras.
