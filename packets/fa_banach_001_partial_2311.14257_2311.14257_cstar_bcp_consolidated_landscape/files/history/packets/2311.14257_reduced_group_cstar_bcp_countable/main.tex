\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(G\) be a discrete group.  Then
\[
        \Cr(G)\text{ has BCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        \Cr(G)\text{ has UBCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        G\text{ is countable}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The positive direction is separability.  If \(G\) is countable, then the group
algebra with rational complex coefficients is countable and dense in
\(\Cr(G)\), so \(\Cr(G)\) has UBCP.

For the negative direction, countably many centres cannot see an uncountable
group.  Each centre belongs to the reduced algebra of a countable subgroup.
After collecting countably many centres, choose one countable subgroup
\(H\le G\) that contains all of them.  Pick \(g\in G\setminus H\).  The
canonical conditional expectation
\[
        E_H:\Cr(G)\to \Cr(H)
\]
fixes the centres and kills \(\lambda_g\).  Hence the unitary \(\lambda_g\)
is at distance at least \(\|a_n\|\) from every centre \(a_n\), so it escapes
all BCP balls.

\section*{Proof}

We use the standard equivalent BCP test: a normed space \(X\) has BCP if and
only if there are countably many centres \(x_n\in X\) such that
\[
        S_X\subseteq\bigcup_n B(x_n,\|x_n\|).
\]

\begin{lemma}[countable support subgroups]
\label{lem:countable-subgroup}
For every \(a\in\Cr(G)\), there is a countable subgroup \(H\le G\) such that
\[
        a\in \Cr(H)\subseteq\Cr(G).
\]
Consequently, every countable subset of \(\Cr(G)\) is contained in
\(\Cr(H)\) for some countable subgroup \(H\le G\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The algebraic group algebra \(\mathbb C[G]\) is dense in \(\Cr(G)\).  Choose
a sequence \(f_m\in\mathbb C[G]\) such that \(f_m\to a\) in norm.  Let
\[
        S=\bigcup_m \operatorname{supp}(f_m),
\]
a countable subset of \(G\), and let \(H=\langle S\rangle\) be the subgroup
generated by \(S\).  Then \(H\) is countable, each \(f_m\) belongs to
\(\mathbb C[H]\), and therefore \(a\in\Cr(H)\) under the canonical inclusion
\(\Cr(H)\subseteq\Cr(G)\).

For countably many elements, take the subgroup generated by the union of the
countably many subgroups just obtained.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[subgroup expectation]
\label{lem:expectation}
If \(H\le G\), there is a contractive conditional expectation
\[
        E_H:\Cr(G)\to\Cr(H)
\]
such that
\[
        E_H(\lambda_s)=
        \begin{cases}
        \lambda_s,&s\in H,\\
        0,&s\notin H,
        \end{cases}
\]
for the left regular unitaries \(\lambda_s\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On \(\ell^2(G)\), identify \(\ell^2(H)\) with the closed span of
\(\{\delta_h:h\in H\}\).  The Fourier-coefficient map displayed above is the
standard reduced subgroup expectation.  It is completely positive and
contractive because it is implemented by compression onto the coset
decomposition of \(\ell^2(G)\), or equivalently by the canonical
coefficient-preserving expectation on the reduced crossed-section
completion.  On the dense algebra \(\mathbb C[G]\) it has exactly the stated
formula, so it extends to \(\Cr(G)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
If \(G\) is countable, then \(\Cr(G)\) is separable.  Every separable normed
space has UBCP: choose a countable dense subset \((d_j)\) of the unit sphere
and use the balls \(B(2d_j,3/2)\).  Thus \(\Cr(G)\) has UBCP, hence BCP.

Assume now that \(G\) is uncountable, and let \((a_n)\) be any countable
family in \(\Cr(G)\).  By Lemma~\ref{lem:countable-subgroup}, there is a
countable subgroup \(H\le G\) such that \(a_n\in\Cr(H)\) for every \(n\).
Choose \(g\in G\setminus H\), and put
\[
        u=\lambda_g\in\Cr(G).
\]
Then \(u\) is unitary, so \(\|u\|=1\).  By Lemma~\ref{lem:expectation},
\[
        E_H(u)=0,\qquad E_H(a_n)=a_n.
\]
Therefore, for every \(n\),
\[
        \|u-a_n\|
        \ge
        \|E_H(u-a_n)\|
        =
        \|-a_n\|
        =
        \|a_n\|.
\]
Thus \(u\in S_{\Cr(G)}\) lies outside every open ball
\[
        B(a_n,\|a_n\|).
\]
Since the countable family of centres was arbitrary, \(\Cr(G)\) fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This theorem gives a complete answer for reduced group \(C^*\)-algebras of
discrete groups.  It is a non-Type-I visibility theorem: the bad direction is
detected by a canonical conditional expectation onto the countable subgroup
generated by the proposed centres.

For uncountable groups whose reduced \(C^*\)-algebras are simple, this gives
simple one-point-primitive non-Type-I \(C^*\)-algebras without BCP, independent
of the von Neumann factor obstruction.  The result does not classify arbitrary
non-Type-I \(C^*\)-algebras; it identifies one robust mechanism by which
nonseparability is visible in the ambient norm.
