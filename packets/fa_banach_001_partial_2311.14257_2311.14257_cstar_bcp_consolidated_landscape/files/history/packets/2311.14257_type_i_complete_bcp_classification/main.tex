\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Definitions}

A normed space \(X\) has the ball-covering property, or BCP, if its unit
sphere is covered by countably many open balls none of which contains the
origin.  Equivalently, \(X\) has BCP if and only if there are countably many
centres \(x_n\in X\) such that
\[
        S_X\subseteq\bigcup_n B(x_n,\|x_n\|).
\]
It has UBCP if the balls can be chosen with a common positive gap from the
origin.

For an open set \(O\subseteq\Prim(A)\), let \(A_O\) denote the corresponding
closed two-sided ideal.  For a Type I algebra \(A\), write \(\Fell(A)\) for
the largest Fell ideal.

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[nonseparable elementary open patches obstruct BCP]
\label{thm:patch}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  Suppose there is a nonempty open set
\(O\subseteq\Prim(A)\) such that
\[
        A_O\cong C_0(O,\Kcal(H))
\]
for a nonseparable Hilbert space \(H\).  Then \(A\) fails BCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[complete Type I criterion]
\label{thm:typei}
Let \(A\) be a Type I \(C^*\)-algebra.  Then the following are equivalent:
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
\textup{(i)}   & A\text{ has BCP};\\
\textup{(ii)}  & A\text{ has UBCP};\\
\textup{(iii)} & \Prim(A)\text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-basis and every}\\
               & \text{irreducible representation of }\Fell(A)
                 \text{ is separable.}
\end{array}
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The positive half was already proved in the essential-Fell packet: a Type I
algebra has an essential largest Fell ideal, and if the irreducible
representations on that Fell ideal are separable, countably many atomic
multiplier charts cover the sphere uniformly.

The missing half is to show that nonseparable Fell fibres really are visible
to BCP.  The key is local.  On a trivial patch
\[
        C_0(O,\Kcal(H))\triangleleft A
\]
with \(H\) nonseparable, choose a scalar bump \(h\) with compact support
\(S\subset O\).  A countable list of centres acts on this ideal as bounded
strict-continuous \(B(H)\)-fields \(T_n(P)\).  Over the compact set \(S\),
strict continuity makes each orbit \(P\mapsto T_n(P)\xi\) norm-compact.
Therefore one can build a separable subspace \(L\subset H\) reducing all
operators \(T_n(P)\), \(P\in S\), while still norming each field on \(S\).

Put a rank-one projection \(R\) in \(L^\perp\), and set
\[
        p(P)=h(P)R.
\]
On \(S\), the difference \(p(P)-T_n(P)\) has the untouched block
\(-T_n(P)|_L\).  Outside \(S\), \(p\) vanishes.  Thus \(p\) is a unit-sphere
element escaping all BCP balls.

\section*{The Local Obstruction}

\begin{lemma}[separable reducing subspace for compact strict families]
\label{lem:strict-reducing}
Let \(S\) be a compact Hausdorff space, let \(H\) be a Hilbert space, and for
each \(n\ge1\) let
\[
        P\longmapsto T_n(P)\in\Bcal(H)
\]
be a bounded field such that both \(P\mapsto T_n(P)\xi\) and
\(P\mapsto T_n(P)^*\xi\) are norm-continuous for every \(\xi\in H\).
Then there is a separable closed subspace \(L\subseteq H\) such that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(L\) reduces every \(T_n(P)\), for all \(n\) and \(P\in S\);
    \item
          \[
             \sup_{P\in S}\|T_n(P)|_L\|
             =
             \sup_{P\in S}\|T_n(P)\|
             \qquad(n\ge1).
          \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put
\[
        \alpha_n=\sup_{P\in S}\|T_n(P)\|.
\]
For each \(n,k\), choose \(P_{n,k}\in S\) and a unit vector
\(\xi_{n,k}\in H\) such that
\[
        \|T_n(P_{n,k})\xi_{n,k}\|>\alpha_n-1/k
\]
when \(\alpha_n>0\); if \(\alpha_n=0\), choose arbitrary unit vectors.

We construct increasing separable subspaces \(L_m\).  Let \(L_0\) be the
closed span of the countable set \(\{\xi_{n,k}:n,k\ge1\}\).  Given \(L_m\),
choose a countable dense subset \(D_m\) of its unit ball.  For each
\(\xi\in D_m\) and each \(n\), the sets
\[
        \{T_n(P)\xi:P\in S\},
        \qquad
        \{T_n(P)^*\xi:P\in S\}
\]
are compact metric subsets of \(H\), hence separable.  Let \(L_{m+1}\) be the
closed span of \(L_m\) together with countable dense subsets of all these
compact image sets, over all \(n\) and \(\xi\in D_m\).

Set
\[
        L=\overline{\bigcup_{m=0}^\infty L_m}.
\]
Then \(L\) is separable.  The construction and boundedness of the fields show
that \(T_n(P)L\subseteq L\) and \(T_n(P)^*L\subseteq L\) for every \(n,P\):
first this holds on each dense set \(D_m\), then by norm approximation on
\(L_m\), and finally by closure.  Hence \(L\) reduces every \(T_n(P)\).

Since each \(\xi_{n,k}\) lies in \(L\),
\[
        \sup_{P\in S}\|T_n(P)|_L\|
        \ge
        \sup_k\|T_n(P_{n,k})\xi_{n,k}\|
        =
        \alpha_n.
\]
The reverse inequality is automatic, so equality holds.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:patch}]
Identify \(A_O\) with \(C_0(O,\Kcal(H))\).  Let \((a_n)\) be any countable
family of centres in \(A\).  Choose \(h\in C_c(O)\) with
\[
        0\le h\le1,\qquad \|h\|=1,
\]
and put \(S=\supp h\), a nonempty compact subset of \(O\).

The action of \(A\) on the ideal \(A_O\) gives, for each \(a_n\), a bounded
strict-continuous multiplier field
\[
        P\longmapsto T_n(P)\in\Bcal(H)
        \qquad(P\in O).
\]
On the compact set \(S\), these fields satisfy the hypotheses of
Lemma~\ref{lem:strict-reducing}.  Choose the corresponding separable reducing
subspace \(L\).  Since \(H\) is nonseparable, \(L^\perp\ne\{0\}\).  Pick a
unit vector \(\eta\in L^\perp\), let \(R\) be the rank-one projection onto
\(\C\eta\), and define
\[
        p(P)=h(P)R
        \qquad(P\in O).
\]
Then \(p\in C_c(O,\Kcal(H))\subseteq A_O\subseteq A\), and \(\|p\|=1\).

Fix \(n\).  Put
\[
        \alpha_n=\sup_{P\in S}\|T_n(P)\|,
        \qquad
        \beta_n=\sup_{P\in\Prim(A)\setminus S}\|a_n+P\|.
\]
Here, on \(O\), \(\|a_n+P\|=\|T_n(P)\|\).  Since the \(C^*\)-norm is the
supremum over primitive quotients,
\[
        \|a_n\|=\max\{\alpha_n,\beta_n\}.
\]

For \(P\notin S\), the element \(p\) vanishes in the primitive quotient at
\(P\), so
\[
        \|p+P-(a_n+P)\|=\|a_n+P\|.
\]
Thus the contribution from \(\Prim(A)\setminus S\) is at least \(\beta_n\).

For \(P\in S\), the subspace \(L\) reduces \(T_n(P)\), while \(R\) acts on
\(L^\perp\).  Therefore
\[
        h(P)R-T_n(P)
        =
        (-T_n(P)|_L)\oplus
        (h(P)R-T_n(P)|_{L^\perp})
\]
relative to \(H=L\oplus L^\perp\).  Hence
\[
        \|h(P)R-T_n(P)\|\ge\|T_n(P)|_L\|.
\]
Taking the supremum over \(P\in S\) and using
Lemma~\ref{lem:strict-reducing} gives a contribution at least \(\alpha_n\).
Combining the two regions,
\[
        \|p-a_n\|\ge\max\{\alpha_n,\beta_n\}=\|a_n\|.
\]

Thus the unit-sphere element \(p\) is outside every open ball
\(B(a_n,\|a_n\|)\).  Since the countable family of centres was arbitrary,
\(A\) fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{The Type I Classification}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:typei}]
First suppose \(A\) has BCP.  The primitive \(\pi\)-base obstruction from the
earlier lane-19 packet gives a countable \(\pi\)-basis for \(\Prim(A)\).
We claim that every irreducible representation of \(\Fell(A)\) is separable.
If not, choose a nonseparable irreducible representation in the Fell ideal.
Fell spectra are locally Hausdorff, and on Hausdorff open patches Fell
algebras are continuous trace \cite{CHR}.  Local triviality of continuous
trace algebras gives a nonempty open set
\[
        O\subseteq\Prim(\Fell(A))\subseteq\Prim(A)
\]
such that
\[
        A_O=\Fell(A)_O\cong C_0(O,\Kcal(H))
\]
for a nonseparable Hilbert space \(H\).  Theorem~\ref{thm:patch} would then
force \(A\) to fail BCP, a contradiction.  Thus condition (iii) is necessary.

The implication UBCP \(\Rightarrow\) BCP is immediate.

Conversely, assume (iii).  The previous Type I packet proved that every Type
I algebra has an essential largest Fell ideal \(\Fell(A)\).  Since all
irreducible representations of \(\Fell(A)\) are separable, the
essential-Fell theorem \cite{lane19essential} applies: the countable
\(\pi\)-basis of \(\Prim(A)\) refines to countably many pi-dense trivial
continuous-trace patches with separable fibres, and the atomic multiplier
chart construction gives UBCP for \(A\).  Hence (iii) implies (ii).

The three conditions are therefore equivalent.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This packet gives a complete answer for Type I \(C^*\)-algebras.  It also
subsumes the one-point nonseparable compact-operator obstruction: if
\(\Kcal(H)\subseteq A\subseteq\Bcal(H)\) and \(H\) is nonseparable, then
\(\Prim(A)\) has an open elementary nonseparable patch, so \(A\) fails BCP.

The arbitrary \(C^*\)-classification remains open.  Outside Type I, separable
non-Type-I algebras still have UBCP, while nonseparable non-Type-I algebras
can fail by atomless von Neumann or diagonal quotient mechanisms.  The
remaining problem is to determine exactly when those non-Type-I mechanisms
are visible to the ambient \(C^*\)-norm.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

Reviewers should check:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Lemma~\ref{lem:strict-reducing}, especially the use of strict
          continuity to keep the reducing subspace separable over a compact
          support;
    \item the split between the compact support \(S\) and its complement in
          the proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:patch};
    \item the passage from a nonseparable irreducible representation of
          \(\Fell(A)\) to an open trivial continuous-trace patch;
    \item the handoff to the earlier essential-Fell positive theorem.
\end{enumerate}
