\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Definitions}

A normed space \(X\) has the ball-covering property, or BCP, if its unit
sphere is covered by countably many open balls none of which contains the
origin.  Equivalently, \(X\) has BCP if and only if there are countably many
centres \(x_n\in X\) such that
\[
        S_X\subseteq\bigcup_n B(x_n,\|x_n\|).
\]
Indeed, any open ball not containing the origin has radius strictly smaller
than the norm of its centre, and enlarging it to radius \(\|x_n\|\) preserves
the open-ball escape test.

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[intermediate nonseparable compact obstruction]
\label{thm:main}
Let \(H\) be a nonseparable Hilbert space.  Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra
such that
\[
        \Kcal(H)\subseteq A\subseteq\Bcal(H).
\]
Then \(A\) fails BCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[faithful nonseparable Type I representation obstruction]
\label{cor:faithful}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra with a faithful irreducible representation
\(\pi:A\to\Bcal(H)\) on a nonseparable Hilbert space.  If
\(\Kcal(H)\subseteq\pi(A)\), then \(A\) fails BCP.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The finite-dimensional packet proved failure for algebras of the form
\(\Kcal(H)+F\) by using the fact that compact perturbations have separable
range.  For an arbitrary intermediate algebra, the centres can contain
general bounded multiplier directions, so the compact-range argument no
longer applies directly.

The replacement is to make a separable reducing subspace for the given
countable list of centres.  For each centre \(a_n\), choose countably many
unit vectors that almost norm \(a_n\).  Close their span under all words in
the operators \(a_n\) and \(a_n^*\).  The resulting subspace \(L\) is
separable, reduces every \(a_n\), and still carries the full norm of every
\(a_n\).

Since \(H\) is nonseparable, \(L^\perp\ne0\).  A rank-one projection \(p\)
inside \(L^\perp\) belongs to \(\Kcal(H)\subseteq A\).  In the decomposition
\[
        H=L\oplus L^\perp,
\]
the operator \(p-a_n\) contains the untouched block \(-a_n|_L\), whose norm
is exactly \(\|a_n\|\).  Thus \(p\) escapes all balls
\(B(a_n,\|a_n\|)\).

\section*{Proof}

\begin{lemma}[separable norming reducing subspace]
\label{lem:reducing}
Let \(H\) be a Hilbert space, and let \((T_n)_{n\ge1}\subseteq\Bcal(H)\) be a
countable family.  There is a separable closed subspace \(L\subseteq H\) such
that \(L\) reduces every \(T_n\) and
\[
        \|T_n|_L\|=\|T_n\|
        \qquad(n\ge1).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For each pair \(n,k\ge1\), choose a unit vector \(x_{n,k}\in H\) satisfying
\[
        \|T_nx_{n,k}\|>\|T_n\|-1/k
\]
when \(T_n\ne0\); if \(T_n=0\), choose any unit vector.

Let \(\mathcal W\) be the countable set of all finite words in the alphabet
\[
        \{T_n,T_n^*:n\ge1\},
\]
including the empty word.  Define
\[
        L=
        \overline{\operatorname{span}}
        \{W x_{n,k}: W\in\mathcal W,\ n,k\ge1\}.
\]
This is separable, because it is generated by countably many vectors.
Moreover, \(L\) is invariant under every \(T_j\) and every \(T_j^*\), since
left multiplication by \(T_j\) or \(T_j^*\) just adds one letter to a word.
Thus \(L\) reduces every \(T_j\).

Finally, each \(x_{n,k}\) belongs to \(L\), so
\[
        \|T_n|_L\|\ge \sup_k \|T_nx_{n,k}\|=\|T_n\|.
\]
The reverse inequality is automatic.  Hence \(\|T_n|_L\|=\|T_n\|\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
Let \((a_n)\) be an arbitrary countable family of centres in \(A\).  Apply
Lemma~\ref{lem:reducing} to the same operators regarded as elements of
\(\Bcal(H)\).  We obtain a separable closed subspace \(L\subseteq H\) that
reduces every \(a_n\) and satisfies
\[
        \|a_n|_L\|=\|a_n\|
        \qquad(n\ge1).
\]
Since \(H\) is nonseparable and \(L\) is separable, \(L^\perp\ne\{0\}\).
Choose a unit vector \(\xi\in L^\perp\), and let \(p\) be the rank-one
projection onto \(\C\xi\).  Then
\[
        p\in \Kcal(H)\subseteq A,
        \qquad
        \|p\|=1.
\]

Because \(L\) reduces every \(a_n\), relative to
\[
        H=L\oplus L^\perp
\]
we have
\[
        a_n=a_n|_L\oplus a_n|_{L^\perp},
        \qquad
        p=0\oplus p.
\]
Therefore
\[
        p-a_n=(-a_n|_L)\oplus(p-a_n|_{L^\perp}),
\]
and hence
\[
        \|p-a_n\|
        =
        \max\{\|a_n|_L\|,\|p-a_n|_{L^\perp}\|\}
        \ge
        \|a_n|_L\|
        =
        \|a_n\|.
\]
Thus \(p\in S_A\) is outside every open ball \(B(a_n,\|a_n\|)\).

Since the countable family \((a_n)\) was arbitrary, no countable collection of
BCP balls can cover the unit sphere of \(A\).  Hence \(A\) fails BCP.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:faithful}]
Identify \(A\) isometrically with its faithful image \(\pi(A)\).  Since
\(\pi(A)\) is an intermediate \(C^*\)-algebra between \(\Kcal(H)\) and
\(\Bcal(H)\), Theorem~\ref{thm:main} applies.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This theorem strengthens the earlier packet on
\(\Kcal(H)+F\), where \(F\) was finite dimensional.  It shows that no
intermediate multiplier algebra between \(\Kcal(H)\) and \(\Bcal(H)\) can
repair BCP when \(H\) is nonseparable.

The theorem does not by itself complete the Type I classification.  If a
general Type I algebra has a nonseparable irreducible representation, that
representation may occur only as a quotient representation.  Since BCP is not
stable under quotients in general, failure of BCP for the represented quotient
does not automatically imply failure for the original algebra.  The remaining
visibility problem is to identify when such quotient-level nonseparable
fibres can be lifted into an ambient diagonal escape.
