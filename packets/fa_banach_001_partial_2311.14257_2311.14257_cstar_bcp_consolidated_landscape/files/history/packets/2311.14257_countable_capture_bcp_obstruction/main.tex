\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[countable-capture obstruction]
\label{thm:capture}
Let \(X\) be a normed space.  Suppose that for every countable set
\(C\subset X\) there are a normed space \(Y_C\), a contraction
\[
        E_C:X\to Y_C,
\]
and an element \(u_C\in S_X\) such that
\[
        E_C u_C=0
        \qquad\hbox{and}\qquad
        \|E_C c\|=\|c\|\quad(c\in C).
\]
Then \(X\) fails the ball-covering property.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[C*-algebra visibility form]
\label{cor:cstar}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  Assume that for every countable
\(C\subset A\) there are a contraction \(E_C:A\to Y_C\) into a normed space
and a unit vector \(u_C\in A\) such that \(E_Cu_C=0\) and
\(\|E_Cc\|=\|c\|\) for all \(c\in C\).  Then \(A\) fails BCP.

In particular, the conclusion holds if the maps \(E_C\) can always be chosen
as contractive projections or conditional expectations onto subspaces or
subalgebras which norm the proposed centres and whose kernels contain a unit
vector.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

BCP is countable, so one is allowed to inspect the proposed centres before
building an escaping unit vector.  The hypothesis gives a contractive
``shadow'' map which keeps the norms of all proposed centres but sends a fresh
unit vector to zero.  Applying the shadow map to \(u_C-x_n\) forces
\[
        \|u_C-x_n\|\ge \|E_C(u_C-x_n)\|=\|E_Cx_n\|=\|x_n\|.
\]
Thus the unit vector \(u_C\) lies outside every ball \(B(x_n,\|x_n\|)\).

\section*{Proof}

We use the standard equivalent BCP test for normed spaces: \(X\) has BCP if
and only if there are countably many centres \(x_n\in X\) such that
\[
        S_X\subseteq \bigcup_n B(x_n,\|x_n\|),
\]
where \(B(x,r)\) denotes the open ball of radius \(r\) around \(x\).  This is
the form used throughout the source paper and the run packets for
arXiv:2311.14257.

Assume the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:capture}, and let
\((x_n)\subset X\) be an arbitrary countable family of proposed centres.
Apply the countable-capture hypothesis to
\[
        C=\{x_n:n\ge 1\}.
\]
We obtain a contraction \(E_C:X\to Y_C\) and a unit vector \(u_C\in S_X\) such
that \(E_Cu_C=0\) and \(\|E_Cx_n\|=\|x_n\|\) for every \(n\).  Therefore,
for each \(n\),
\[
        \|u_C-x_n\|
        \ge
        \|E_C(u_C-x_n)\|
        =
        \|-E_Cx_n\|
        =
        \|x_n\|.
\]
Hence \(u_C\notin B(x_n,\|x_n\|)\) for every \(n\).  Since the countable
family \((x_n)\) was arbitrary, no countable BCP cover exists.  Thus \(X\)
fails BCP.

Corollary~\ref{cor:cstar} is the same theorem applied to the underlying
Banach space of \(A\).

\section*{Relation to the Reduced Group C*-Algebra Packet}

The partial packet
\[
\texttt{2311.14257\_reduced\_group\_cstar\_bcp\_countable}
\]
is an exact instance of this criterion.  If \(G\) is an uncountable discrete
group and \((a_n)\subset \Cr(G)\), each \(a_n\) belongs to \(\Cr(H)\) for one
common countable subgroup \(H\le G\).  Choose \(g\in G\setminus H\).  The
canonical subgroup expectation
\[
        E_H:\Cr(G)\to \Cr(H)
\]
fixes every \(a_n\) and kills the fresh unitary \(\lambda_g\).  Thus
Theorem~\ref{thm:capture} yields failure of BCP for \(\Cr(G)\).

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This is a negative criterion, not a classification of all non-Type-I
\(C^*\)-algebras.  It explains what a future broad non-Type-I obstruction
must supply: after countably many proposed centres are known, one needs a
contractive map that preserves their norms while annihilating a fresh unit
vector.

The condition is not automatic for non-Type-I algebras.  Separable
\(C^*\)-algebras have UBCP, and the source paper gives positive non-Type-I
von Neumann algebra cases.  Thus an arbitrary non-Type-I classification must
distinguish between norm-visible countable-capture mechanisms and genuinely
positive nonseparable behaviour.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

Reviewers should check:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item the use of the standard BCP equivalent formulation
          \(S_X\subseteq\bigcup_n B(x_n,\|x_n\|)\);
    \item that equality \(\|E_Cc\|=\|c\|\) for the proposed centres is enough;
    \item the C*-algebra specialization, especially when \(E_C\) is supplied
          by conditional expectations or quotient/capture maps.
\end{enumerate}
