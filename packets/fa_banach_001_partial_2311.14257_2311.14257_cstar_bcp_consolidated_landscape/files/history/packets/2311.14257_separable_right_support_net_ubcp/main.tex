\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative
unital \(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a
countable \(\pi\)-basis, it asks for a noncommutative \(C^*\)-algebra
characterization:

\section*{Statement}

Recall that a normed space has UBCP if its unit sphere is covered by
countably many open balls whose radii are bounded and which all avoid a fixed
neighborhood of the origin.

\begin{definition}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  A sequence \((e_n)\) of positive
contractions in \(A\) is a \emph{separable right-support net} if
\[
        R_n:=\cl{A e_n}
\]
is separable for every \(n\), and
\[
        \|a\|=\sup_n \|a e_n\|
        \qquad(a\in A).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[right-support net criterion]
\label{thm:main}
If a \(C^*\)-algebra \(A\) admits a separable right-support net, then \(A\)
has UBCP.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The proof abstracts the useful part of the separable-essential-ideal
argument.  A positive contraction \(e\) gives a universal contraction
\(1-2e\) in the unitization.  If \(a\in S_A\) is almost normed by right
multiplication with \(e\), then the piece \(ae\) can be used as a countable
center, provided the right ideal \(\cl{Ae}\) is separable.  The center is
just a nearby dense point \(d\), scaled to \(2d\).  The estimate
\[
        \|a-2d\|
        \le
        \|a(1-2e)\|+2\|ae-d\|
\]
gives a fixed radius, independent of \(a\).

\section*{Proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
For each \(n\), choose a countable dense subset \(D_n\) of the closed unit
ball of \(R_n=\cl{Ae_n}\).  Let
\[
        D_n^0=\{d\in D_n:\|d\|>13/16\}.
\]
For every \(d\in \bigcup_n D_n^0\), use the center \(2d\) and the fixed
radius \(9/8\).

This is a countable family.  Its radii are bounded, and every retained center
has norm larger than \(13/8\), so all balls avoid the open ball \(B(0,1/2)\).
It remains to prove that these balls cover \(S_A\).

Fix \(a\in S_A\).  Since \((e_n)\) is norming on the right, choose \(n\) such
that
\[
        \|ae_n\|>7/8.
\]
The element \(ae_n\) lies in the closed unit ball of \(R_n\).  Choose
\(d\in D_n\) with
\[
        \|d-ae_n\|<1/16.
\]
Then
\[
        \|d\|>7/8-1/16=13/16,
\]
so \(d\in D_n^0\).

In the unitization of \(A\), the element \(1-2e_n\) is a contraction because
\(e_n\) is a positive contraction.  Hence
\[
\begin{aligned}
        \|a-2d\|
        &\le \|a-2ae_n\|+2\|ae_n-d\|\\
        &=   \|a(1-2e_n)\|+2\|ae_n-d\|\\
        &<  1+1/8
        =   9/8.
\end{aligned}
\]
Thus \(a\in B(2d,9/8)\).  Since \(a\in S_A\) was arbitrary, the displayed
countable family is a UBCP cover.
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequences}

\begin{corollary}[separable essential ideals]
If \(A\) contains a separable essential closed two-sided ideal, then \(A\)
has UBCP.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Let \(I\triangleleft A\) be separable and essential.  Choose a sequential
positive contractive approximate identity \((e_n)\) for \(I\).  Essentiality
gives \(\|ae_n\|\to\|a\|\) for every \(a\in A\), and
\(\cl{Ae_n}\subset I\) is separable.  Theorem~\ref{thm:main} applies.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[countable products]
Let \((A_n)\) be a sequence of separable \(C^*\)-algebras.  Then
\(\prod_n A_n\) has UBCP.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Let \(e_n\) be the central projection onto the \(n\)-th coordinate.  Then
\(\cl{(\prod_k A_k)e_n}\cong A_n\) is separable, and
\[
        \|a\|=\sup_n \|ae_n\|
        \qquad(a\in \prod_k A_k).
\]
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Relation to the Pure-State Push}

This packet does not prove a pure-state \(\pi\)-base classification.  It
clarifies what such a proof would need to produce.  If a countable
\(\pi\)-base in the pure state space could be converted into positive
contractions \(e_n\) whose right ideals \(\cl{Ae_n}\) are separable and which
right-norm every element of \(A\), then Theorem~\ref{thm:main} would
immediately give UBCP.

Pure-state excision is a plausible source of such \(e_n\)'s: it can create
positive contractions whose high-level pure-state support lies in a prescribed
weak-star neighborhood.  The missing point is directional and quantitative:
pure-state topology by itself has not yet been shown to force the separability
of the corresponding right ideals, nor a countable set of local directions
inside the excised corners.

\section*{Limitations}

The theorem is a positive criterion, not an if-and-only-if classification.
The hypothesis is stronger than the existence of a countable BCP blocker.  In
particular, an ideal generated by countably many elements inside a larger
\(C^*\)-algebra can be nonseparable; a rank-one projection in
\(\mathcal B(H)\), for nonseparable \(H\), generates \(\mathcal K(H)\) as an
ideal.  The present theorem requires separability of the right-support pieces
\(\cl{Ae_n}\), not merely countable generation of an ideal.

\section*{Human Review Focus}

Reviewers should check:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item that the norming condition \(\|a\|=\sup_n\|ae_n\|\) is used only to
          choose \(n\) with \(\|ae_n\|>7/8\);
    \item that the dense set in the closed unit ball of \(\cl{Ae_n}\) supplies
          a countable choice of centers;
    \item that \(\|1-2e_n\|\le1\) in the unitization for positive
          contractions \(e_n\);
    \item the constants \(7/8\), \(1/16\), \(13/16\), and \(9/8\);
    \item that the corollaries are genuinely special cases of the right
          support hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}
