\section*{Source Problem}

The source paper is Jinghao Huang, Karimbergen Kudaybergenov, and Rui Liu,
\emph{The ball-covering property of von Neumann algebras and noncommutative
symmetric spaces}, arXiv:2311.14257.  After proving that a commutative unital
\(C^*\)-algebra \(C(K)\) has BCP/UBCP exactly when \(K\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis, the authors ask for a noncommutative version:

\section*{Definitions}

\begin{definition}
A \(C^*\)-algebra \(A\) has the ball-covering property, or BCP, if the unit
sphere of \(A\) is covered by countably many open balls none of which contains
the origin.  It has the uniform ball-covering property, or UBCP, if the balls
can be chosen with a common positive gap from the origin.
\end{definition}

For an open set \(O\subset\Prim(A)\), let \(A_O\) denote the corresponding
closed two-sided ideal of \(A\).

\section*{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[atomic multiplier chart criterion]
\label{thm:atomic}
Let \(A\) be a \(C^*\)-algebra.  Assume that \(\Prim(A)\) has a countable
\(\pi\)-basis \((O_n)\) such that every patch is trivial continuous trace,
that is,
\[
        A_{O_n}\cong C_0(O_n,\Kcal(H_n))
\]
for a separable nonzero Hilbert space \(H_n\).  Then \(A\) has UBCP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Fell algebras with separable irreducible representations]
\label{cor:fell}
Let \(A\) be a Fell \(C^*\)-algebra all of whose irreducible representations
act on separable Hilbert spaces.  Then
\[
        A\text{ has BCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        A\text{ has UBCP}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        \Prim(A)\text{ has a countable }\pi\text{-basis}.
\]
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The earlier compact-chart packet required every ambient element of \(A\) to
be represented on a chart by a norm-continuous compact-operator field.  That
is stronger than necessary.  On a continuous-trace patch
\[
        A_O\cong C_0(O,\Kcal(H)),
\]
an ambient element \(a\in A\) acts as a multiplier, hence as a bounded
strict-continuous \(B(H)\)-field \(P\mapsto a(P)\).  The whole field need not
be norm-continuous, but every fixed matrix coefficient
\[
        P\longmapsto \langle a(P)\xi,\eta\rangle
\]
is continuous.

Given \(a\in S_A\), choose a primitive point where \(\|a(P)\|>3/4\).  On a
continuous-trace patch around that point, choose unit vectors \(\xi,\eta\)
from a countable dense set so that
\[
        \operatorname{Re}\langle a(P)\xi,\eta\rangle>\theta
\]
for a fixed \(\theta>0\).  By coefficient continuity this remains true on a
smaller open set.  A scalar bump \(h\) supported there gives a centre
\[
        c=Mh(\eta\otimes\xi^*).
\]
The operator estimate below says that \(a\) stays within a radius \(\rho<M\)
of this centre at every primitive quotient.  Since \(\|c\|=M\), the balls are
uniformly off the origin.

\section*{Proof}

\begin{lemma}[rank-one coefficient estimate]
\label{lem:rankone}
Fix \(0<\theta<1\).  There are constants \(M>1\) and \(\rho<M\) such that the
following holds for every Hilbert space \(H\).  If \(T\in B(H)\) satisfies
\(\|T\|\le1\), if \(\xi,\eta\in H\) are unit vectors, and if
\[
        \operatorname{Re}\langle T\xi,\eta\rangle\ge\theta,
\]
then, for the rank-one operator \(R u=\langle u,\xi\rangle\eta\),
\[
        \sup_{0\le t\le M}\|T-tR\|\le \rho.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(0<\varepsilon<1\) so small that
\[
        2\theta(1-\varepsilon)-2\sqrt{\varepsilon}\ge\theta.
\]
Put \(\delta=\theta/8\).  We first show that for all sufficiently large
\(t\),
\[
        \|T-tR\|\le t-2\delta.
\]

Let \(x\in H\) be a unit vector, and write
\[
        x=\lambda\xi+v,
        \qquad
        v\perp\xi,
        \qquad
        s=|\lambda|.
\]
Then \(\|v\|=(1-s^2)^{1/2}\).  Since \(\|T\|\le1\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \|(T-tR)x\|^2
 &= \|Tx\|^2+t^2s^2
    -2t\,\operatorname{Re}\bigl(\overline{\lambda}\langle Tx,\eta\rangle\bigr)\\
 &\le 1+t^2s^2
    -2t\theta s^2+2t s(1-s^2)^{1/2}.
\end{aligned}
\]
If \(s^2\le1-\varepsilon\), then
\[
        \|(T-tR)x\|^2\le t^2(1-\varepsilon)+2t+1,
\]
which is at most \((t-2\delta)^2\) for all sufficiently large \(t\).
If \(s^2>1-\varepsilon\), then the choice of \(\varepsilon\) gives
\[
        -2\theta s^2+2s(1-s^2)^{1/2}\le -\theta,
\]
and therefore
\[
        \|(T-tR)x\|^2\le t^2-\theta t+1.
\]
This is also at most \((t-2\delta)^2\) for all sufficiently large \(t\).  Thus
there is \(t_0\) such that \(\|T-tR\|\le t-2\delta\) whenever \(t\ge t_0\).

Choose \(M>t_0+1+2\delta\), and set \(\rho=M-\delta\).  If \(0\le t<t_0\),
then
\[
        \|T-tR\|\le 1+t<t_0+1<M-2\delta<\rho.
\]
If \(t_0\le t\le M\), then
\[
        \|T-tR\|\le t-2\delta\le M-2\delta<\rho.
\]
Hence \(\sup_{0\le t\le M}\|T-tR\|\le\rho<M\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[countable atomic bump data]
\label{lem:data}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:atomic}, one can choose, for each
chart \(O_n\):
\begin{enumerate}
    \item an isomorphism
          \[
             \Phi_n:A_{O_n}\longrightarrow C_0(O_n,\Kcal(H_n));
          \]
    \item a countable dense set \(D_n\) in the unit sphere of \(H_n\);
    \item a countable family \(\mathcal B_n\subset C_c(O_n)\), with
          \(0\le h\le1\) and \(\|h\|=1\), such that every nonempty open
          subset of \(O_n\) contains the support of some \(h\in\mathcal B_n\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Hilbert spaces \(H_n\) are separable, so the sets \(D_n\) exist.  The
continuous-trace trivializations are part of the hypothesis, after refining
the countable \(\pi\)-basis if necessary.  Since \(O_n\) is locally compact
Hausdorff and the \(O_m\)'s form a \(\pi\)-basis, local regularity gives
countably many compactly supported scalar bumps with cofinal supports.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:atomic}]
Fix \(\theta=1/2\), and let \(M\) and \(\rho<M\) be the constants supplied by
Lemma~\ref{lem:rankone}.  Put
\[
        r=\frac{M+\rho}{2},
\]
so that \(\rho<r<M\).  For every chart \(O_n\), every
\(h\in\mathcal B_n\), and every \(\xi,\eta\in D_n\), define
\[
        c(n,h,\xi,\eta)
        =
        \Phi_n^{-1}\bigl(Mh(\eta\otimes\xi^*)\bigr)
        \in A_{O_n}\subset A.
\]
These centres are countable and have norm \(M\).  We claim that the balls
\[
        B(c(n,h,\xi,\eta),r)
\]
cover \(S_A\).  Since \(r<M\), this proves UBCP.

Let \(a\in S_A\).  The set
\[
        W=\{P\in\Prim(A):\|a+P\|>3/4\}
\]
is nonempty and open.  Choose a chart \(O_n\subset W\), and then choose
\(P_0\in O_n\).  Let \(T_{P_0}\in B(H_n)\) be the operator representing the
multiplier action of \(a\) on the fibre over \(P_0\).  Then
\[
        \|T_{P_0}\|=\|a+P_0\|>3/4.
\]
Choose unit vectors \(\xi_0,\eta_0\in H_n\) such that
\[
        \operatorname{Re}\langle T_{P_0}\xi_0,\eta_0\rangle>2/3.
\]
After approximating \(\xi_0,\eta_0\) by vectors in the dense set \(D_n\), we
may choose \(\xi,\eta\in D_n\) with
\[
        \operatorname{Re}\langle T_{P_0}\xi,\eta\rangle>1/2.
\]

The canonical action of \(A\) on \(A_{O_n}\cong C_0(O_n,\Kcal(H_n))\) is a
multiplier field.  In this trivialization, multiplier fields are bounded
strict-continuous \(B(H_n)\)-valued fields; hence the scalar coefficient
\[
        P\longmapsto
        \operatorname{Re}\langle T_P\xi,\eta\rangle
\]
is continuous.  Therefore there is a nonempty open neighbourhood
\[
        N\subset O_n
\]
of \(P_0\) such that
\[
        \operatorname{Re}\langle T_P\xi,\eta\rangle\ge1/2
        \qquad(P\in N).
\]
Choose \(h\in\mathcal B_n\) with \(\supp h\subset N\), and set
\[
        c=c(n,h,\xi,\eta).
\]

If \(P\in\supp h\), then in the fibre over \(P\),
\[
        c(P)=Mh(P)(\eta\otimes\xi^*),
\]
and Lemma~\ref{lem:rankone}, applied with \(t=Mh(P)\), gives
\[
        \|a+P-(c+P)\|\le\rho<r.
\]
If \(P\in O_n\setminus\supp h\), then \(c(P)=0\), so
\[
        \|a+P-(c+P)\|=\|a+P\|\le1<r,
\]
after increasing \(M\), if necessary, so that \(\rho>1\).  If
\(P\notin O_n\), then \(c\in A_{O_n}\subset P\), and again
\[
        \|a+P-(c+P)\|=\|a+P\|\le1<r.
\]
Taking the supremum over primitive quotients gives \(\|a-c\|<r\).  Thus
the displayed countable family of balls covers \(S_A\), and \(A\) has UBCP.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:fell}]
If \(A\) has BCP, the primitive \(\pi\)-base obstruction from the earlier
lane-19 packet gives a countable \(\pi\)-basis for \(\Prim(A)\).  UBCP always
implies BCP.

It remains to prove that a Fell algebra with separable irreducible
representations and countable primitive \(\pi\)-basis satisfies the hypothesis
of Theorem~\ref{thm:atomic}.  Fell spectra are locally Hausdorff.  Given a
nonempty open set \(U\subset\Prim(A)\), choose a nonempty open Hausdorff
subset \(V\subset U\).  The ideal \(A_V\) is Fell with Hausdorff spectrum,
hence has continuous trace.  Local triviality gives a nonempty open
\[
        O\subset V
\]
such that
\[
        A_O\cong C_0(O,\Kcal(H))
\]
for the Hilbert space of the irreducible representations over that patch.
By assumption \(H\) is separable.  Now start from any countable \(\pi\)-basis
\(\mathcal P\) of \(\Prim(A)\), and retain those members of \(\mathcal P\)
which are contained in one of the continuous-trace trivialization patches just
constructed.  The preceding paragraph shows that this retained subfamily is
still a countable \(\pi\)-basis, and each retained member inherits the
corresponding separable continuous-trace trivialization.  Thus the hypotheses
of Theorem~\ref{thm:atomic} hold.
Therefore \(A\) has UBCP.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This packet settles the positive Fell case under separability of irreducible
representations, but it does not claim a full arbitrary \(C^*\)-classification.
The remaining type-I question is whether countable primitive \(\pi\)-base
forces enough continuous-trace open patches outside the Fell class, or whether
there are non-Fell CCR/type-I algebras with countable primitive \(\pi\)-base
and separable irreducible representations that still fail BCP.

The separability assumption on irreducible representations is also real for
this proof: it supplies the countable rank-one directions.  Earlier lane-19
packets show that nonseparable compact-operator fibres can fail BCP even with
one-point primitive spectrum.
