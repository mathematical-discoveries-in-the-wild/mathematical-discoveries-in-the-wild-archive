## Goal

Break the wall left by the continuous-trace packet:

> Ambient type-I elements may act on continuous-trace ideals only as
> multipliers, so the compact-section bump proof does not automatically apply.

Two routes were tested:

1. prove a Banach-space/C*-extension permanence theorem for UBCP;
2. replace permanence by a local chart condition that rules out the multiplier
   obstruction directly.

## Extension Route

The tempting statement was:

> If `0 -> Y -> X -> Z -> 0` is exact and `Y`, `Z` have UBCP, then `X` has
> UBCP.

The naive product-of-covers proof runs into a real cancellation problem. If
one chooses centers of the form `lift(z_i) + y_j`, the norm of this sum is not
controlled from below by the norms of both components in an arbitrary Banach
extension. The quotient component gives one lower bound, and the subspace
component gives another only when a projection or special norm geometry is
available. The final covering radius is roughly the sum of quotient and
subspace errors, and the available lower bound on the center can be smaller
than that sum.

This does not disprove extension permanence, but it blocks the straightforward
route. Quotient instability already shows one must be careful: there are
UBCP C*-algebras with quotients failing BCP.

## Promoted Criterion

The successful replacement is the packet

`solutions/partial/2311.14257_local_compact_chart_bcp_criterion/`.

It proves:

> If `A` has a countable primitive pi-basis of local charts where every ambient
> element is represented by a bounded norm-continuous compact-operator section
> over a separable Hilbert fibre, then `A` has UBCP.

This is exactly the condition that removes the multiplier obstruction. The
proof is the same fixed-radius bump construction as in the continuous-trace
case, but it no longer requires a global `C_0(X)`-algebra structure.

## Remaining Wall

To push toward the full type-I classification, one must now show one of:

1. a broad type-I/Fell/CCR family satisfies the compact-norming chart
   hypothesis;
2. an atomic-multiplier replacement covers the cases where local sections take
   values in `B(H)` rather than `K(H)`;
3. extension permanence holds under a C*-specific hypothesis strong enough for
   type-I composition series;
4. a counterexample such as a unitized or multiplier continuous-trace algebra
   has countable primitive pi-base but fails BCP.

The most concrete next target is unitizations or finite-dimensional quotients
of continuous-trace algebras: they are just beyond the compact-chart criterion
because scalar identity directions are not compact local sections.
