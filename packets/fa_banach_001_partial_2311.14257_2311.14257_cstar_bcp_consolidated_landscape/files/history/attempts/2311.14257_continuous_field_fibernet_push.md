# Attempt: arXiv:2311.14257 continuous-field fiber-net route

status: promoted partial result

target: Question 3 in Huang--Kudaybergenov--Liu, asking for a
noncommutative characterization of C*-algebras with BCP/UBCP.

## Candidate theorem

Let `A` be a continuous `C_0(X)`-algebra over a locally compact Hausdorff
space `X`, all fibers nonzero.  Assume `A` admits a countable family
`Sigma={sigma_m}` of bounded localizable contraction sections such that for
every `x in X` and every `u in S_{A_x}`, some `m` satisfies
`||sigma_m(x)-u||<1/64`.

Then the following are equivalent:

1. `A` has BCP;
2. `A` has UBCP;
3. `X` has a countable pi-basis.

The negative implication `(1) -> (3)` is the previous continuous-field
obstruction.  The positive implication `(3) -> (2)` is the new route.

## Localizable section hypothesis

The bounded sections must be localizable because for noncompact `X` the
constant fiber directions in a trivial bundle `C_0(X,B)` usually do not lie in
`C_0(X,B)`.  The right abstraction is:

- `sigma_m(x) in A_x` and `sup_x ||sigma_m(x)|| <= 1`;
- for every `h in C_c(X)`, `h sigma_m` is an element of `A`;
- for every `a in A`, the map `(x,t) -> ||a(x)-t sigma_m(x)||` is continuous
  on `X x [0,4]`.

This covers the trivial bundle `C_0(X,B)` with separable fiber `B`: take
constant bounded sections from a countable dense subset of `S_B`.

## Proof idea for the positive direction

Let `(V_j)` be a countable pi-basis and choose scalar bumps `h_j` with
`supp h_j subset V_j`, `0 <= h_j <= 1`, and `||h_j||=1`.
Use centers
`c_{j,m}=4 h_j sigma_m`, but keep only those with norm `>15/4`, and use fixed
radius `7/2`.

For `a in S_A`, choose `x0` with `alpha=||a(x0)||>3/4` and
`u=a(x0)/alpha`.  Pick `sigma_m` with `||sigma_m(x0)-u||<delta`, where
`delta=1/64`.  At `x0`, convexity in `s in [0,1]` gives

`sup_s ||alpha u - 4s sigma_m(x0)|| < 7/2`.

Continuity of `(x,s) -> ||a(x)-4s sigma_m(x)||` gives a neighborhood `U` of
`x0` where the same inequality remains true.  Shrink `U` also so that
`||sigma_m(x)||>1-2delta` on `U`.  Choose `V_j subset U`.  Then for
`x in supp h_j`, put `s=h_j(x)` and get
`||a(x)-c_{j,m}(x)||<7/2`; outside the support the distance is `||a(x)||<=1`.
The center has norm at least `4(1-2delta)>15/4`, so the fixed-radius balls are
uniformly off the origin.
