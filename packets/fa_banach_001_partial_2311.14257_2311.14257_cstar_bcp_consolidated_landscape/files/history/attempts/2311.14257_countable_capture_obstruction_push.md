## Result

The reduced-group proof abstracts to a simple Banach-space criterion.

Let `X` be a normed space.  Suppose that for every countable set
`C subset X` there are:

- a normed space `Y`;
- a contraction `E:X -> Y`;
- an element `u in S_X`;

such that

```text
E(u)=0
and
||E(c)|| = ||c|| for every c in C.
```

Then `X` fails BCP.

Indeed, for any countable proposed centers `x_n`, choose such `(E,u)`.
Then

```text
||u-x_n|| >= ||E(u-x_n)|| = ||E(x_n)|| = ||x_n||.
```

So `u` escapes every ball `B(x_n,||x_n||)`.

## Why This Matters

This is the exact visibility mechanism behind the uncountable reduced group
C*-algebra theorem.  It also explains what a future arbitrary non-Type-I
negative theorem must supply: a countable capture map that preserves the
norms of proposed centers while killing a fresh unit.

It does not classify all non-Type-I C*-algebras, because positive examples
such as separable C*-algebras and separably represented atomic von Neumann
algebras cannot have this obstruction.
