## Routes Tested

### Direct dimension/counting

Works above continuum and cannot improve by cardinality alone, because
positive examples exist at density continuum.

### Approximate central sequence / separable absorption

This is not enough by itself.  An approximate fresh direction or an element in
an ultrapower gives eventual escape from each fixed ball, but a countable
cover can chase a sequence with different balls.  To prove failure of BCP one
needs either an exact realization in the algebra, a countably saturated
realization principle, or a direct single-element diagonal construction.

### Countable separating functionals

Any positive density-continuum example must admit a countable point-separating
family of norming functionals attached to some BCP cover.  I do not currently
see a C*-theoretic contradiction with simplicity.  This is the key obstacle to
turning the density obstruction into a full simple-core theorem.
