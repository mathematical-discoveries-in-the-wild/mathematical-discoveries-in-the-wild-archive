## Direct Finite-Series Induction

The first idea was to prove an extension permanence theorem:

```text
0 -> I -> A -> B -> 0,
I and B positive => A positive.
```

The naive proof still runs into the old cancellation problem.  A quotient
UBCP cover does not give a small norm-net unless the quotient is separable or
finite dimensional.  For nonseparable continuous-trace quotients with only a
countable pi-base, quotient balls can have large radii.  Lifting such centers
and adding ideal centers gives a radius roughly equal to a quotient error plus
an ideal error, while the C*-norm of the center is only a maximum over
primitive layers.  This is not enough for a uniform off-origin gap.

The finite-dimensional quotient theorem escapes exactly because finite
dimensionality supplies small quotient nets.  A general finite composition
series will need a sharper layer-lifting or atomic multiplier mechanism.

## Successful Detour: FDI Fell Algebras

The local compact-chart criterion suggested a better next theorem.

For a Fell algebra, the spectrum is locally Hausdorff.  On a Hausdorff open
patch, the corresponding ideal is again Fell with Hausdorff spectrum, hence is
continuous trace.  If all irreducible representations are finite-dimensional,
then the local continuous-trace fibres are finite matrix algebras.  On finite
matrix fibres, multiplier strict continuity and norm continuity coincide.

Therefore every ambient element restricts on such a patch to a bounded
norm-continuous compact-operator section.  In other words, finite-dimensional
irreducible-representation Fell algebras satisfy the compact-norming chart
hypothesis from the earlier packet.

## What Remains

This is not the full Fell theorem.  The remaining hard case is Fell/type-I
with infinite-dimensional separable irreducible representations.  There the
local Hausdorff patches are continuous trace with compact-operator fibres
`K(H)`, but an ambient element may restrict as a multiplier field in `B(H)`.
Pointwise compactness/strict continuity need not give norm-continuous compact
sections.  That strict-versus-norm gap is now the precise next obstruction.
