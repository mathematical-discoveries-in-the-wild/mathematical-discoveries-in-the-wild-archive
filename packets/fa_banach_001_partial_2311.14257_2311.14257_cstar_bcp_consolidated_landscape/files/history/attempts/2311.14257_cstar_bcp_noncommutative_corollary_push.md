`A=C_0(K)` has BCP/UBCP iff `K` has a countable pi-basis.

## Result Found

For trivial continuous C*-bundles the base-space obstruction is exactly the
commutative one.

Let `K` be locally compact Hausdorff and `X` a nonzero Banach space.

1. If `C_0(K,X)` has BCP, then `K` has a countable pi-basis.
2. If `K` has a countable pi-basis and `X` has BCP, then `C_0(K,X)` has BCP.
3. If `K` has a countable pi-basis and `X` is separable, then `C_0(K,X)` has
   UBCP.

Consequently, for every nonzero separable C*-algebra `A`,
`C_0(K,A)` has BCP/UBCP iff `K` has a countable pi-basis. This gives
noncommutative examples such as `C_0(K,M_n)` and `C_0(K,K(H))`.

## Proof Sketch

Negative direction: given countably many centers `F_n` in `C_0(K,X)`, use the
near-norm cozero sets
`U_{n,k}={t: ||F_n(t)||>||F_n||-1/k}`. If `K` has no countable pi-basis, choose
a nonempty open `W` containing none of these sets. A norm-one bump supported in
`W` then stays at distance at least `||F_n||` from every center because each
center is almost norm-attaining somewhere outside `W`.

Positive direction: choose a countable pi-basis and bump functions `h_j` with
`supp h_j` inside basis elements. If the fiber has BCP, scale its BCP centers
to norm bigger than two. For `f in S_{C_0(K,X)}`, choose a point where `f` is
near norm one, choose a fiber center covering the normalized value, and then
choose a bump supported in a neighborhood where `f` changes little. Convexity
of `s -> ||x-sy||` on `[0,1]` controls the transition region.

For separable `X`, take a countable dense set in `S_X` and fixed centers
`4u_m` with radius `7/2` to get UBCP.
