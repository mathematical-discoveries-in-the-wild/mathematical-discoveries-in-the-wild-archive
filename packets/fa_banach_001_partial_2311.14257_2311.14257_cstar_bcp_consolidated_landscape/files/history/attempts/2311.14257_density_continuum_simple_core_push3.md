## New Promoted Result

This push found a new exact mechanism between literal tensor coordinates and
arbitrary simple algebras:

```text

```

If a C*-algebra has an uncountable generating family with countable support
for countable subsets, and each unused generator can be sign-flipped by an
automorphism fixing all the others, then the algebra has countable capture
and fails BCP.

The proof is one line once the unused generator is chosen: if `alpha(c)=c`
and `alpha(u)=-u`, then

```text
||c - lambda u|| = ||alpha(c - lambda u)|| = ||c + lambda u||,
```

and since `c` is the average of `c-lambda u` and `c+lambda u`, convexity
gives `||c|| <= ||c-lambda u||`.

## Application

Farah's graph/CCR algebra `B(G)` satisfies the hypotheses whenever the graph
has uncountably many vertices. Therefore every uncountable graph/CCR algebra
has countable capture and fails BCP.

In particular, Farah's simple nuclear AM algebra of density continuum, with
irreducible representations on both separable and nonseparable Hilbert
spaces, fails BCP.

This closes a major stress test for the remaining wall: simple, nuclear,
separably represented, density-continuum examples are not automatically
positive.
