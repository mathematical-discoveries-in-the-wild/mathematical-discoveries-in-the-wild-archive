## Key Realization

The atomic multiplier chart proof does not require the chart family to be a
literal pi-basis for all of `Prim(A)`.  It only needs to be pi-dense: every
nonempty primitive-open set contains one atomic chart.

Indeed, for `a` in the unit sphere,

```text
W_a = {P in Prim(A): ||a+P|| > 3/4}
```

is nonempty open.  The proof only needs one chart inside `W_a`.

- essential Fell ideal + separable irreps + countable primitive pi-base

- the positive mechanism is purely primitive-topological plus atomic local
  multiplier patches.

## Remaining Wall

essential.  If the complement has nonempty primitive interior, the problem
reduces to that quotient/open piece.  Thus a plausible full positive
classification may proceed by peeling off essential Fell layers, while the
negative side should come from:

1. nonseparable compact fibres;
2. atomless/non-type-I primitive quotients;
3. diagonal quotient/corona tails;
4. type-I spaces with no pi-dense Fell part and no separability fallback.
