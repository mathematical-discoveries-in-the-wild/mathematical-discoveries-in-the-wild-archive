## Breakthrough

The FDI proof used that multiplier fields over matrix fibres are
norm-continuous.  That is too strong.  For a continuous-trace patch

```text
I_O ~= C_0(O, K(H))
```

an ambient element `a in A` acts as a multiplier, hence as a bounded
strict-continuous `B(H)`-field.  The full field may not be norm-continuous, but
fixed matrix coefficients are continuous:

```text
P |-> <a(P) xi, eta>.
```

This is enough.  If `||a(P0)|| > 3/4`, choose countable dense unit vectors
`xi, eta` so that `Re <a(P0) xi, eta> > theta`.  By coefficient continuity this
persists on a smaller open set.  A scalar bump `h` supported there and the
rank-one operator `R = eta tensor xi*` give centers

```text
c = M h R.
```

The key operator estimate says that for fixed `theta>0`, there are `M` and
`rho<M` such that whenever `A` is a contraction and

```text
Re <A xi, eta> >= theta,
```

then

```text
sup_{0 <= t <= M} ||A - t R|| <= rho.
```

Thus the ball `B(M h R, rho)` covers `a`, and the center norm is `M`.

## What Remains

This likely handles the broad Fell/separable-irrep positive side.  The next
frontier is type-I beyond Fell: primitive spaces may have countable pi-base but
may not contain a pi-basis of continuous-trace open patches.  A full type-I
classification needs either:

1. a proof that the relevant type-I classes have enough Fell/continuous-trace
   patches;
2. a new patch theorem for non-Fell CCR points; or
3. a counterexample showing that countable primitive pi-base plus separable
   irreps is still insufficient outside Fell.
