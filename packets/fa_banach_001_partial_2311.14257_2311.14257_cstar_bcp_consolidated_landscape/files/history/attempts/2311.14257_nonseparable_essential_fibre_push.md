## Goal

Push the missing nonseparable-fibre direction.  The finite-dimensional packet
proved that `K(H)+F` fails BCP for nonseparable `H`; the question is whether
arbitrary intermediate multiplier algebras

```text
K(H) subset A subset B(H)
```

also fail BCP.

## Key Observation

Yes.

Let `(a_n)` be countably many centers in an intermediate C*-algebra `A`.
For each `a_n`, choose countably many unit vectors that almost norm `a_n`.
Let `L` be the closed reducing subspace generated by these vectors under all
words in the countable set `{a_n,a_n^*: n in N}`.

Then:

- `L` is separable;
- `L` reduces every `a_n`;
- the compression `a_n|_L` has the same norm as `a_n`.

Since `H` is nonseparable, choose a unit vector `xi in L^\perp`, and let `p`
be the rank-one projection onto `C xi`.  Then `p in K(H) subset A`, `||p||=1`,
and, relative to `H=L direct_sum L^\perp`,

```text
p - a_n = (-a_n|_L) direct_sum (p - a_n|_{L^\perp}).
```

Therefore

```text
||p-a_n|| >= ||a_n|_L|| = ||a_n||
```

for every `n`.  Thus no countable family of open balls `B(a_n,||a_n||)` can
cover the unit sphere.

## Consequence

Every C*-algebra with a faithful irreducible representation on a nonseparable
Hilbert space whose image contains the compacts fails BCP.  Equivalently,
every intermediate C*-algebra between `K(H)` and `B(H)` fails BCP when `H` is
nonseparable.

## Remaining Wall

This does not yet prove that every Type I algebra with some nonseparable
irreducible representation fails BCP, because such a representation may be a
quotient representation of `A`, and BCP is not quotient stable.  The theorem
does prove the faithful one-fibre visibility case.
