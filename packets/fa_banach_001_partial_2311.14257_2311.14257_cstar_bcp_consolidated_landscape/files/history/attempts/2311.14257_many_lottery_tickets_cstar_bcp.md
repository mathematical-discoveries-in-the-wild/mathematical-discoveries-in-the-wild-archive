## Ticket That Paid Out

The nonseparable compact-operator fibre gives a clean obstruction, and the
obstruction survives every finite-dimensional extension inside
\(\mathcal B(H)\).

For every nonzero Hilbert space \(H\) and every finite-dimensional
\(C^*\)-subalgebra \(F\subseteq\mathcal B(H)\),
\[
  \mathcal K(H)+F \text{ has BCP}
  \Longleftrightarrow
  H \text{ is separable}
  \Longleftrightarrow
  \mathcal K(H)+F \text{ has UBCP}.
\]

## Mechanism

Given countably many compact centers \(T_n\), each \(T_n\) and \(T_n^*\) has
separable range.  The closed span of all these ranges is separable, so in a
nonseparable \(H\) choose a unit vector \(\xi\) orthogonal to it.  Let \(p\) be
the rank-one projection onto \(\mathbb C\xi\).  Then \(T_np=pT_n=0\), hence
\[
  \|p-T_n\|=\max(1,\|T_n\|)\ge \|T_n\|.
\]
Thus \(p\) escapes every canonical BCP ball.

For centers \(f_n+T_n\) in \(\mathcal K(H)+F\), decompose the finite
dimensional representation of \(F\) into matrix blocks with multiplicity
spaces.  One multiplicity space is nonseparable.  Choose a slice over a vector
orthogonal to the compact supports.  The complement still contains infinitely
many copies of the same \(F\)-matrix block, so compact perturbations cannot
shrink the center norm there.  A rank-one projection in the chosen slice
escapes every center.

## Consequences

- The separable-fibre hypothesis in the continuous-trace positive packet is
  necessary.
- Primitive topology alone is far from sufficient: \(\mathcal K(H)\) has
  one-point primitive ideal space but fails BCP for nonseparable \(H\).
- The obstruction survives arbitrary finite-dimensional extensions
  \(\mathcal K(H)+F\), giving unital examples when \(F\) contains the identity.
- This directly answers the earlier "unitization/finite-dimensional
  extension" target for the nonseparable compact-operator fibre.
