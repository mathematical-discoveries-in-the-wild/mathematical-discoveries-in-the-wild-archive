## Promoted During This Push

1. `2311.14257_uncountable_tensor_product_capture`

   If `(A_gamma)_{gamma in Gamma}` is a family of unital C*-algebras with
   uncountably many nontrivial factors, then the unital minimal infinite
   tensor product has countable capture and fails BCP.  In particular,
   `tensor_Gamma M_2` for uncountable `Gamma` is a simple one-point-primitive
   C*-algebra failing BCP.

2. `2311.14257_von_neumann_capture_classification`

   For von Neumann algebras, countable capture is equivalent to failure of
   BCP, and hence equivalent by the source theorem to not being atomic and
   separably represented.

3. `2311.14257_relative_two_evaluation_capture`

   A relative two-evaluation direction over every separable subalgebra implies
   countable capture and hence failure of BCP.  This abstracts the fresh
   coordinate/tensor-factor argument into a reusable criterion.

4. `2311.14257_countable_products_separable_cstar_ubcp`

   Products of separable C*-algebras are now classified: the product has BCP,
   equivalently UBCP, iff at most countably many factors are nonzero.  In
   particular `ell_infty(O_2)` is a nonseparable non-Type-I C*-algebra with
   UBCP, while uncountable products of nonzero separable factors fail BCP by
   countable capture.

5. `2311.14257_bcp_density_continuum_obstruction`

   Every Banach space with BCP has cardinality and density at most the
   continuum.  Hence every C*-algebra of density strictly larger than the
   continuum fails BCP.  This is independent of primitive ideal theory and
   narrows the remaining simple nonseparable wall to density exactly
   continuum.

6. `2311.14257_countably_saturated_banach_capture`

   Every infinite-dimensional countably norm-saturated Banach space has
   countable capture and fails BCP.  In particular, every infinite-dimensional
   countably saturated C*-algebra, and more generally every C*-algebra
   realizing the relevant countable degree-one norm types, fails BCP.

7. `2311.14257_graph_ccr_reflection_capture`

   If an uncountable generating family has countable support for countable
   subsets and each unused generator can be sign-flipped by an automorphism
   fixing the old generators, then the algebra has countable capture and
   fails BCP.  Hence every uncountable Farah graph/CCR algebra fails BCP; in
   particular Farah's simple nuclear AM density-continuum example with
   irreducible representations on both separable and nonseparable Hilbert
   spaces fails BCP.

8. `2311.14257_bcp_countable_capture_exact_equivalence`

   For every real or complex normed space, BCP failure is equivalent to
   countable capture.  The reverse implication is the scalar-rescaling trick:
   if a countable set blocks capture, then
   `dist(c, F u)<||c||` gives `||c-lambda u||<||c||`; dividing by nonzero
   `lambda` and approximating `lambda^{-1}` by rational scalars yields a
   countable BCP cover.

## Main Tested Conjecture

The natural conjecture after the countable-capture characterization was:

```text
For C*-algebras, failure of BCP is equivalent to countable capture.
```

This is now proved for all normed spaces, hence for all C*-algebras.  The
following regions remain important because they give structural ways to
produce or block countable capture:

- commutative C*-algebras `C_0(K)`;
- Hilbert spaces as operator-space model objects;
- reduced group C*-algebras;
- von Neumann algebras;
- C*-algebras with `Prim(A)` lacking countable pi-base;
- uncountable unital infinite tensor products of nontrivial factors;
- algebras satisfying the relative two-evaluation criterion over every
  separable subalgebra;
- the previously packaged diagonal/corona/reduced-product settings, modulo
  their exact or asymptotic capture criteria.
- all C*-algebras of density strictly larger than the continuum.
- countably saturated or countably degree-one saturated infinite-dimensional
  C*-algebras.
- uncountable graph/CCR algebras with generator sign-flip automorphisms,
  including Farah's simple nuclear AM density-continuum example.

There is also a large positive family outside the Type I/separable-looking
regime:

- products of separable C*-algebras: BCP/UBCP iff only countably many factors
  are nonzero.

## Remaining Wall After Exact BCP/Capture

The exact BCP side is now closed in capture language:

```text
A has BCP  <=>  A does not have countable capture.
```

The unresolved region is therefore no longer whether the listed negative
mechanisms imply BCP failure; they do, because they produce countable capture.
The remaining classification problem is structural and quantitative:

```text
Which C*-algebras fail countable capture, and when does that positive BCP
case upgrade to UBCP?
```

The hardest test case is now an arbitrary simple nonseparable C*-algebra of
density exactly continuum.  The
known examples we can control fail BCP by countable capture:

- II_1 factors: singular-state capture;
- uncountable reduced group C*-algebras: subgroup expectation capture;
- uncountable UHF/CAR tensor products: fresh tensor-factor capture.
- Farah graph/CCR algebras: reflection/sign-flip capture.

Before the exact equivalence, the missing negative lemma was something like:

```text
For every separable subalgebra B of a nonseparable simple C*-algebra A,
there is u in S_A such that dist(b, C u)=||b|| for all b in B.
```

Equivalently, every separable subalgebra would have a common Birkhoff-James
orthogonal direction in `A`.

The relative two-evaluation packet proves this lemma under the
stronger condition that the new direction admits two contractive evaluations
which agree isometrically on the old separable algebra.

The saturation packet proves the same countable-capture conclusion for
countably saturated objects without any C*-structure: finite centre sets
always admit a common Birkhoff-James orthogonal direction, and saturation
realizes the resulting countable type.

## Routes Tried

### Fresh Tensor Coordinate

Works for literal uncountable tensor products.  Countable centers lie in a
countable subproduct, and an unused factor supplies a self-adjoint contraction
with endpoint states `rho_+(v)=1`, `rho_-(v)=-1`.

Barrier: arbitrary simple nonseparable algebras need not have visible tensor
coordinates or relative commutants for separable subalgebras.

### Von Neumann Singular States

Works for atomless von Neumann algebras.  The source singular-state norming
lemma plus a common projection killed by countably many singular states gives
common-kernel norming functionals.

Barrier: arbitrary C*-algebras do not have enough projections or a comparable
singular-state projection calculus.

### Primitive Topology

Works exactly when `Prim(A)` has no countable pi-base.

Barrier: simple algebras have one-point primitive space, so primitive topology
is silent.

### Product Dichotomy

Products of separable C*-algebras are now completely controlled.  Countable
products are positive: a countable coordinate/direction net with centers
supported in one coordinate covers the unit sphere by balls of radius `3/2`
centered at norm `2`.

Uncountable products are negative by countable capture.  For any countable
old center set, each center has at most one coordinate whose deletion lowers
its norm.  Avoid the countable union of exceptional coordinates and put the
escaping unit vector there.

This blocks any attempted classification by nonseparability or non-Type-I
behavior alone.  The obstruction must detect genuinely uncountable fresh
directions or a comparable capture mechanism.

### Density-Continuum Obstruction

Any Banach space with BCP has cardinality at most continuum.  Indeed, choose
one norming functional for each centre in a countable BCP cover.  If these
functionals had a nonzero common kernel vector, its normalization would escape
every ball.  Hence they separate points and embed the space into `C^N`.

This proves every C*-algebra of density greater than continuum fails BCP.
The result is sharp at the level of nonseparable non-Type-I algebras because
`ell_infty(O_2)` has density continuum and UBCP.

### Saturation Obstruction

In any infinite-dimensional Banach space, every finite set of centres has a
common capture direction: choose finitely many norming functionals and take a
unit vector in their common kernel.  Countable norm-saturation realizes the
countable type

```text
||u|| = 1 and ||c_j - lambda u|| >= ||c_j||
```

for all centres `c_j` and rational complex `lambda`.  Thus countably
saturated Banach spaces and countably degree-one saturated C*-algebras have
countable capture and fail BCP.

### Reflection Capture

Works for uncountable graph/CCR algebras.  A countable set of old centres is
contained in the subalgebra generated by countably many graph generators.  An
unused generator `u` admits an automorphism fixing the old subalgebra and
sending `u` to `-u`.  Therefore

```text
||c - lambda u|| = ||c + lambda u||
```

and convexity of `c = ((c-lambda u)+(c+lambda u))/2` gives the countable
capture inequality.  This covers Farah's simple nuclear AM algebra of density
continuum with irreducible representations on both separable and nonseparable
Hilbert spaces.

Barrier: arbitrary simple nonseparable algebras need not have a countable
support presentation or sign-flip automorphisms over separable subalgebras.

## Current Best Plan

The next plausible push is quantitative.  Since BCP is exactly failure of
countable capture, UBCP should correspond to a uniform version of capture
failure: a countable set of blockers with a uniform scalar-rescaled gap and
bounded radii.  The right target is a theorem of the form:

```text
For C*-algebras, every BCP witness can be replaced by a uniformly separated
and bounded witness; equivalently BCP implies UBCP.
```

All positive C*-classes packaged so far have UBCP, but there is not yet a
general proof that arbitrary C*-algebra BCP implies UBCP.
