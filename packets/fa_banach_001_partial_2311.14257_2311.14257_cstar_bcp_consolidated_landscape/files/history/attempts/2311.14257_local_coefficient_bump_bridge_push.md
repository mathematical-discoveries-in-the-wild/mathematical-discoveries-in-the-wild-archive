Try to replace the false sequence-quotient reflection theorem with a stronger

```text
C*-algebra BCP => C*-algebra UBCP.
```

The immediate input is the counterexample

## What the Stronger Invariant Has to Do

The commutative proof works because a countable pi-base in `K` gives
countably many scalar bumps `h_j`.  If `f in S_{C(K)}` is almost constant on
the support of `h_j`, then a fixed center such as `4 alpha h_j` covers `f`
with a fixed gap.  Crucially, the principal ideal `C_0(coz(h_j))` may be
nonseparable; separability of the whole local ideal is irrelevant.

So the noncommutative replacement cannot be:

```text
separable right-support net.
```

The split-interval counterexample already kills that.  The replacement must
look more like:

```text
countably many local coefficient devices
which are invisible off their patch and carry a countable local model on
their patch.
```

This includes the known positive cases:

- commutative `C(K)`: scalar Urysohn bumps;
- continuous fields with countable fiber-net: localized fiber directions;
- continuous trace/Fell/type-I separable-fibre charts: compact finite-rank
  local models;
- separable essential ideals: a countable dense reservoir.

## First Lottery Ticket: Transition Pi-Base Alone

Let

```text
T(A)=ext(B_{A*})
```

be the pure transition functional space, modulo phases if desired.  It
calibrates the two source boundary cases:

- for `C(K)`, `T(C(K))` has a countable pi-base iff `K` does;
- for von Neumann algebras, `T(M)` countable pi-base forces `M` atomic and
  separably represented.

It is tempting to conjecture:

```text
T(A) countable pi-base => A has UBCP.
```

The push did not find a proof.  The problem is that a transition-open patch is
not an operator-norm support patch.  If `b in A` is large on one transition
functional in a patch, there is generally no reason for `b` to be invisible
on all pure transition functionals outside the patch.

In the commutative case, phase-saturated supports of scalar functions are
honest open subsets of `K`.  In continuous-trace/type-I cases, compact
rank-one elements give honest local operator models.  In genuinely non-Type-I
irreducible representations, a literal rank-one bump cannot exist: an
irreducible C*-subalgebra of `B(H)` containing one nonzero compact contains
`K(H)`.  Thus transition pi-base alone is missing the operator-norm
localization needed for a UBCP estimate.

## Second Lottery Ticket: Pure-State Excision

Pure-state excision says that a pure state can be excised against a finite
set: for a pure state `phi` and finite `F subset A`, one can find positive
contractions `e` with

```text
||e a e - phi(a) e^2|| small        (a in F).
```

This looks like a noncommutative scalar bump.  The obstruction is that the
excisor depends on the finite set `F`, hence on the element being covered.
UBCP needs countably many centers fixed in advance.  Excision supplies
finite, element-dependent localization; it does not by itself produce a
countable preselected bump basis.

This is exactly why the separable case is easy: a countable dense set lets
one diagonalize the dependence on `F`.  Without a separable or finite-rank
local model, excision does not give a fixed countable cover.

## Third Lottery Ticket: Quantitative BCP Coefficient Sets

Let `C` be a countable BCP blocker, so for every `u in S_A` some `c in C`
satisfies

```text
dist(c, C u) < ||c||.
```

For a fixed nonzero `c`, write `J(c)` for its norming functionals.  The strict
inequality above is equivalent to saying that no norming functional of `c`
vanishes on `u`.  By weak-star compactness of `J(c)`, this gives a positive
threshold:

```text
min_{f in J(c)} |f(u)| > 0.
```

Thus the unit sphere is covered by the countable coefficient sets

```text
E(c,k) = {u in S_A : |f(u)| >= 1/k for every f in J(c)}.
```

This is a useful reformulation, but it still does not yield UBCP.  The
threshold `1/k` can go to zero.  In Hilbert geometry, a large cap around
`R e` only forces the coefficient of `e` to be bigger than about `1/(2R)`;
no bounded family of centers associated only to `e` covers that cap with a
fixed UBCP gap.  C*-polar decomposition or spectral truncation has the same
loss: normalizing the center divides away exactly the tiny coefficient that
made the strict BCP inequality true.

So the coefficient-set reformulation isolates the missing amplification:

```text
tiny strict coefficient
  + local bump/model structure
  -> fixed UBCP gap.
```

Without the local bump/model structure, the amplification is false.

## Why the Compact Reflection Counterexample Matters Here

For `A=K(ell_2)`, every countable bounded set is captured in the sequence
quotient by a weakly-null rank-one projection sequence.  Nevertheless
`K(ell_2)` is separable and has UBCP.

So the obstruction is not:

```text
asymptotic capture exists.
```

The obstruction would have to be:

```text
asymptotic capture exists and there is no countable local model that turns
the escaping sequence into actual bounded centers.
```

In `K(ell_2)`, the escaping rank-one projections themselves reveal the local
compact model, and separability gives bounded centers.  In a hypothetical
BCP-not-UBCP algebra, one would need quotient escape without any such
localizable model.

## Surviving Route

The viable theorem shape is now a dichotomy, not a direct reflection theorem.

For a C*-algebra with BCP, prove either:

1. **local model side:** the countable BCP blocker produces countably many
   local coefficient devices with a fixed UBCP gap; or
2. **escape side:** failure of such devices yields one of the known capture
   mechanisms, hence failure of BCP.

This is consistent with all known packets:

- primitive space with no countable pi-base gives countable capture;
- atomless/nonseparably represented von Neumann pieces give countable capture;
- nonseparable compact fibres give countable capture;
- products, coronas, and reduced products give diagonal/annular capture;
- separable, commutative pi-base, continuous-field fiber-net, and type-I
  separable-fibre regimes give local models and UBCP.

## Current Verdict

This push did not prove the full BCP=>UBCP implication.  It also did not find
a new counterexample to the source problem.

It did rule out two tempting shortcuts:

1. transition pi-base alone is not enough unless it is strengthened to a
   localizable/operator-norm bump basis;
2. pure-state excision is not enough unless the local patch has a countable
   finite-dimensional or separable model so the excisors can be preselected.

The next best push is therefore structural:

```text
Show that a C*-algebra with BCP and no local coefficient model must contain
one of the known countable-capture mechanisms.
```

Equivalently, the remaining wall is not reflection from `A_infty`; it is the
existence/nonexistence of local coefficient models inside arbitrary
non-Type-I, nonseparable C*-algebras with countable primitive pi-base.
