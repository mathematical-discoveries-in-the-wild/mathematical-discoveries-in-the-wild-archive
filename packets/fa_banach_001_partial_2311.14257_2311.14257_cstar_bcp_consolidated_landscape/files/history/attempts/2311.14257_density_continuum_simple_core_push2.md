## New Promoted Result

The push found a general saturation obstruction:

```text
Every infinite-dimensional countably norm-saturated Banach space has
countable capture and fails BCP.
```

The proof is short but powerful.  For finitely many centres, choose norming
functionals and take a unit vector in their finite-codimensional common
kernel.  Hence every finite part of the countable-capture type is satisfiable.
Countable saturation realizes the whole countable type.

## Routes Tested

### Separable faithful representation

BCP gives a countable total family of functionals, which suggests a
separable-representation shadow.  However, this does not by itself imply
separability or BCP/UBCP.  A cyclic GNS representation of a nonseparable
algebra can still be nonseparable, and countable total functionals need not
give norm control strong enough for a cover.

### Irreducible representation and Kadison transitivity

For a faithful irreducible representation on a nonseparable Hilbert space,
one would like to annihilate the countably many vector functionals used to
norm a countable centre set.  Kadison transitivity gives exact control on
finite-dimensional subspaces, but not on a whole separable subspace.  The
finite-control obstruction is exactly what countable saturation fixes in
ultrapower/corona-like settings.

### Saturation

This route works and was promoted.  It is a robust negative mechanism at
density continuum, but not automatic for arbitrary simple C*-algebras.

## Refined Remaining Wall

A possible positive simple counterexample must now satisfy all of the
following:

- density exactly continuum;
- not covered by primitive topology, product/tensor/group/von Neumann/corona
  mechanisms;
- no relative two-evaluation direction over every separable subalgebra;
- not countably saturated and not countably degree-one saturated in the
  relevant norm-type sense;
- has a countable BCP centre geometry strong enough to cover the unit sphere
  but weak enough to avoid forcing countable capture.

## Best Next Attacks

1. **Exactify approximate central directions.**  In nonseparable LM/AF or

2. **Disprove simple positive geometry.**  Assume a simple nonseparable
   density-continuum algebra has a BCP centre set.  Try to prove the induced
   countable norming-slice geometry yields a product-like or quotient-like
   decomposition, contradicting simplicity.

3. **Construct a simple positive example.**  Look for a simple nonseparable
   subalgebra of an atomic separably represented positive algebra whose BCP
   cover can be inherited or rebuilt internally.  The obstruction is that BCP
   is not hereditary, so this requires an explicit centre construction.
