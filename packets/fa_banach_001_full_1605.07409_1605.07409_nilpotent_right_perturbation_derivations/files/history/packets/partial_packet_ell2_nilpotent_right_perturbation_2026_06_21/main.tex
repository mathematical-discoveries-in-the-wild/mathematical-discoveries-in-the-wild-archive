\section*{Source question}

The source paper asks in Section 6 whether reasonable sufficient conditions
exist on a pair \((A,B)\) inducing hypercyclic generalized derivations
\(\tau_{A,B}=L_A-R_B\) on separable Banach ideals.

\section*{Historical partial theorem}

Let \(X=\ell_2\), let \(W_\lambda\) be \(\lambda\) times the backward shift for
some \(\lambda>1\), and let \(N\in \mathcal L(X)\) be nonzero and nilpotent.
Then, for every separable admissible Banach ideal \(J\subset \mathcal L(X)\),
the generalized derivation
\[
  \tau_{W_\lambda,N}=L_{W_\lambda}-R_N
\]
satisfies the Hypercyclicity Criterion.

\section*{Promotion}

The full packet proves a stronger abstract criterion: \(W_\lambda\) may be
replaced by any operator \(A\) with a right inverse \(F\), \(AF=I\), \(r(F)<1\),
and a dense subspace eventually killed by powers of \(A\).  This historical
subcase is retained only to document the partial-to-full progression.
