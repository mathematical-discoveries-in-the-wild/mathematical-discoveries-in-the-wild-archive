\section*{Open Problem Extract}

For $-1\le \gamma<0$, the source paper defines
\[
\|u\|_{\BV(\gamma)}
  :=\|\nabla u\|_{\mathcal M}
  +\sup_{\lambda>0}\lambda\,\nu_\gamma(E_{\lambda,\gamma}[u])
\]
and the corresponding subspace $\Wone(\gamma)$ of $\Wone$. The paper asks:

\begin{quote}
``Are these spaces normable in the range $\gamma\in[-1,0)$?''
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions}

For $u\in L^1_{\mathrm{loc}}(\R^N)$, set
\[
E_{\lambda,\gamma}[u]
=\left\{(x,y)\in\R^N\times\R^N:x\ne y,\ 
\frac{|u(x)-u(y)|}{|x-y|^{1+\gamma}}>\lambda\right\},
\]
and
\[
d\nu_\gamma(x,y)=|x-y|^{\gamma-N}\,dx\,dy.
\]
For $-1<\gamma<0$, define
\[
\Sg(u):=\sup_{\lambda>0}\lambda\,\nu_\gamma(E_{\lambda,\gamma}[u]),
\qquad
\qg(u):=\|\nabla u\|_{L^1(\R^N)}+\Sg(u)
\]
on $\Wone(\gamma)$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The paper constructs Cantor-type functions $u_m$ for which the weak-type term grows like $m$ while the ordinary $W^{1,1}$ size stays controlled. The extra observation is that these same $u_m$ are not exotic single atoms: they are convex averages of $2^m$ one-scale transition atoms. Each one-scale atom has uniformly bounded $\qg$ because a single Lipschitz transition layer has uniformly bounded weak endpoint functional when $-1<\gamma<0$.

If the quasi-norm topology came from an equivalent norm, then the convex hull of the quasi-unit ball would have to be quasi-bounded. The functions $u_m$ give convex averages of uniformly bounded atoms whose $\qg$-size tends to infinity. This contradicts normability.

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let $N\ge 1$ and $-1<\gamma<0$. The quasi-normed spaces $\Wone(\gamma)$ and $\BV(\gamma)$ defined in Section 7.1 of Brezis--Seeger--Van Schaftingen--Yung are not normable, in the sense that their natural quasi-norm topologies are not induced by any equivalent norm.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

We first recall a standard criterion. Let $(X,q)$ be a quasi-normed vector space. If the topology of $q$ is induced by an equivalent norm, then the convex hull of the $q$-unit ball is $q$-bounded. Indeed, if $a\|x\|\le q(x)\le b\|x\|$, every convex combination of points satisfying $q(x)\le1$ has norm at most $a^{-1}$ and therefore $q$-size at most $ba^{-1}$.

It is enough to contradict this criterion in $\Wone(\gamma)$; the conclusion for $\BV(\gamma)$ follows because $\Wone(\gamma)$ is a linear subspace of $\BV(\gamma)$ and the restricted quasi-norm is the same.

Let
\[
\rho=2^{-1/(1+\gamma)}\in(0,1/2).
\]
The source paper constructs smooth increasing functions $g_m$ on $\R$, with $g_m=0$ to the left of $0$ and $g_m=1$ to the right of $1$, by the self-similar recursion
\[
g_{m+1}(t)=\frac12 g_m(t/\rho)+\frac12 g_m\!\left(1-\frac{1-t}{\rho}\right).
\]
Equivalently, if $\mathcal I_m$ is the family of the $2^m$ basic intervals in the $m$th step of the symmetric Cantor construction with ratio $\rho$, and $a_I$ is the left endpoint of $I$, then
\[
g_m(t)=2^{-m}\sum_{I\in\mathcal I_m} g_0\!\left(\frac{t-a_I}{\rho^m}\right).
\]

For $N=1$ set
\[
\phi_I(t)=g_0\!\left(\frac{t-a_I}{\rho^m}\right).
\]
For $N>1$, use the same tensor cutoff as the source paper: choose a fixed $\eta\in C_c^\infty(\R^N)$, equal to one on the relevant unit box, and set
\[
\Phi_I(x)=16\,\phi_I(x_1)\eta(x).
\]
For $N=1$ this formula is read with $\eta\equiv 1$ and the harmless factor $16$ omitted. Then the source functions used in the lower-bound construction have the form
\[
u_m=2^{-m}\sum_{I\in\mathcal I_m}\Phi_I .
\]

\begin{lemma}
There is a constant $A=A(N,\gamma,g_0,\eta)$ such that
\[
\qg(\Phi_I)\le A
\]
for every level $m$ and every $I\in\mathcal I_m$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The $L^1$ gradient bound is uniform: the $x_1$ derivative has size $\rho^{-m}$ on a strip of width $\rho^m$, while all cutoff-derivative terms are supported in a fixed compact set and are uniformly bounded.

For the weak endpoint term, write $r=\rho^m$. A single atom has only one transition layer of width comparable to $r$. Split pairs $(x,y)$ into $|x-y|\le r$ and $|x-y|>r$.

For $|x-y|\le r$, Lipschitz control gives
\[
|\Phi_I(x)-\Phi_I(y)|\lesssim r^{-1}|x-y|,
\]
so membership in $E_{\lambda,\gamma}[\Phi_I]$ implies
\[
|x-y|\gtrsim(\lambda r)^{1/|\gamma|}.
\]
The transition layer has measure $O(r)$ in the normal direction, and integrating the kernel $|x-y|^{\gamma-N}$ over the admissible annuli gives
\[
\nu_\gamma(E_{\lambda,\gamma}[\Phi_I]\cap\{|x-y|\le r\})\lesssim \lambda^{-1}.
\]

For $|x-y|>r$, the atom behaves like a bounded jump across one Lipschitz layer. The set of points within distance $h$ of that layer has measure $O(h)$ in the normal direction; integrating $h^\gamma\,dh$ up to the largest scale allowed by
\[
1\gtrsim \lambda h^{1+\gamma}
\]
again gives a bound $\lesssim \lambda^{-1}$. The cutoff region contributes the same estimate with constants depending only on the fixed cutoff. Hence
\[
\lambda\,\nu_\gamma(E_{\lambda,\gamma}[\Phi_I])\le C
\]
uniformly in $I,m,\lambda$, proving the lemma.
\end{proof}

On the other hand, the lower-bound construction in Section 6 of the source paper proves that these multiscale averages satisfy
\[
\nu_\gamma(E_{1,\gamma}[u_m])\ge c_{N,\gamma}m-C_{N,\gamma},
\]
after the same tensor cutoff in higher dimensions. Therefore
\[
\qg(u_m)\ge c_{N,\gamma}m-C_{N,\gamma}.
\]

Now normalize the atoms by $A$: each $A^{-1}\Phi_I$ belongs to the $\qg$-unit ball, while
\[
A^{-1}u_m
=2^{-m}\sum_{I\in\mathcal I_m} A^{-1}\Phi_I
\]
belongs to the convex hull of that unit ball. But
\[
\qg(A^{-1}u_m)=A^{-1}\qg(u_m)\to\infty.
\]
Thus the convex hull of the $\qg$-unit ball is not $\qg$-bounded. By the criterion at the start of the proof, $\Wone(\gamma)$ is not normable for $-1<\gamma<0$.

If $\BV(\gamma)$ were normable, then its linear subspace $\Wone(\gamma)$ with the inherited topology would be normable, contradicting what we just proved. Hence $\BV(\gamma)$ is not normable either.
\qed

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This proves a negative answer to the source paper's normability question throughout the open interval $-1<\gamma<0$ and for every dimension $N\ge1$.

The endpoint $\gamma=-1$ is not settled here. The reason is structural: the uniform one-scale atom bound used above becomes logarithmically unstable at $\gamma=-1$, exactly the endpoint where the source paper separates special cases in its generic-failure section.
