\section*{Status}

This packet gives a sharp classification theorem for a precise subproblem
suggested by Micheli and Glaun\`es~\cite{MG}.  No nondegenerate fixed,
continuously differentiable, translation-invariant positive-definite
matrix kernel can make landmark Hamiltonian dynamics exactly covariant
under all spatial dilations.  The obstruction persists even if each
dilation is allowed its own positive scalar rescaling of time and
momenta.  In the paper's \(C_0^1\)-RKHS class, the only exactly covariant
kernel is the zero kernel.  Conversely, every constant positive-semidefinite
matrix kernel has exact dilation covariance, so the classification is tight;
these and only these examples are degenerate.

The source conclusion states a broader program rather than a formal
conjecture, so this is classified as a substantial partial result.  It
settles the fixed stationary-kernel version of exact scale covariance,
but not multiscale families, approximate invariance, or the other
geometric questions in the conclusion.

\section*{Source Direction}

Micheli and Glaun\`es develop matrix-valued reproducing kernels for
landmark and diffeomorphic shape deformation.  For a translation-invariant
kernel \(K(x,y)=\kappa(x-y)\), their landmark Hamiltonian is
\[
 H(p,q)=\frac12\sum_{a,b=1}^{N}
 p_a^{\mathsf T}\kappa(q^a-q^b)p_b,
\]
and the first Hamilton equation is
\begin{equation}
 \dot q^a=\sum_{b=1}^{N}\kappa(q^a-q^b)p_b.
 \label{eq:first-hamilton}
\end{equation}
Their conclusion observes that all kernels considered there contain a
length constant and that the resulting landmark and diffeomorphism
dynamics are not scale invariant.  It then identifies application-adapted
kernel design and the relation between kernels and geodesic shape
properties as future work:

\section*{Definition and Proof Intuition}

Fix the two-landmark system.  We call it \emph{exactly dilation covariant}
if for every \(\lambda>0\) there are numbers \(A_\lambda,C_\lambda>0\)
such that every solution \((q,p)\) is transformed into another solution
of the same, fixed-kernel Hamiltonian system by
\begin{equation}
 Q^a(t)=\lambda q^a(C_\lambda t),
 \qquad
 P_a(t)=A_\lambda p_a(C_\lambda t).
 \label{eq:covariance}
\end{equation}
This definition is deliberately permissive: both time and all momenta
may be renormalized by arbitrary dilation-dependent scalars.

At time zero, choose positions \((x,0)\) and momenta \((0,v)\).
Equation~\eqref{eq:first-hamilton} computes the first landmark's velocity
using the single cross-kernel value \(\kappa(x)v\).  Applying the same
equation to the dilated trajectory compares this with
\(\kappa(\lambda x)v\), forcing
\[
 \kappa(\lambda x)=h_\lambda\kappa(x)
 \quad\text{for all }x,
 \qquad
 h_\lambda=\frac{\lambda C_\lambda}{A_\lambda}.
\]
Positive definiteness prevents a nonzero stationary kernel from having
\(\kappa(0)=0\).  Evaluation at \(x=0\) therefore forces
\(h_\lambda=1\).  Sending \(\lambda\) to zero and using continuity then
makes \(\kappa\) constant.  Constant kernels move every landmark through
the same finite-dimensional velocity subspace and have singular
multi-landmark Gram matrices.

\section*{Main Obstruction}

We use the standard matrix-valued positive-definiteness convention:
\[
 \sum_{i,j=1}^{m}u_i^{\mathsf T}
 \kappa(x_i-x_j)u_j\geq0
\]
for all finite choices of \(x_i,u_i\in\R^d\).  It implies
\(\kappa(-x)=\kappa(x)^{\mathsf T}\) and
\(\kappa(0)\) is positive semidefinite.

\begin{lemma}[A stationary positive-definite kernel cannot vanish at zero]
\label{lem:zero}
If \(\kappa:\R^d\to\R^{d\times d}\) is positive definite and
\(\kappa(0)=0\), then \(\kappa\equiv0\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(x,u,v\in\R^d\), positive semidefiniteness of the scalar
\(2\times2\) compression of the block Gram matrix at \(x\) and \(0\)
gives the kernel Cauchy--Schwarz inequality
\[
 \left|u^{\mathsf T}\kappa(x)v\right|^2
 \leq
 \bigl(u^{\mathsf T}\kappa(0)u\bigr)
 \bigl(v^{\mathsf T}\kappa(0)v\bigr).
\]
If \(\kappa(0)=0\), the right side vanishes for every \(u,v\).
Thus every matrix coefficient of \(\kappa(x)\) is zero, for every \(x\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exact dilation covariance obstruction]
\label{thm:main}
Let \(K(x,y)=\kappa(x-y)\) be a nonzero, continuously differentiable,
translation-invariant positive-definite matrix-valued kernel on
\(\R^d\).  Suppose the two-landmark Hamiltonian system associated to
\(K\) is exactly dilation covariant in the sense of
\eqref{eq:covariance}.  Then
\[
 \kappa(x)=\kappa(0)\qquad(x\in\R^d).
\]
Consequently, for every \(N\geq2\), the \(Nd\times Nd\) landmark block
Gram matrix is singular.  In particular, \(K\) cannot induce a
nondegenerate Riemannian metric on the space of \(N\) distinct
landmarks.  Conversely, every constant positive-semidefinite matrix
kernel is exactly dilation covariant.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(\lambda>0\).  Take arbitrary \(x,v\in\R^d\) and a solution with
initial data
\[
 q^1(0)=x,\quad q^2(0)=0,\qquad
 p_1(0)=0,\quad p_2(0)=v.
\]
The first Hamilton equation~\eqref{eq:first-hamilton} gives
\[
 \dot q^1(0)=\kappa(x)v.
\]
Differentiate the transformed position in~\eqref{eq:covariance}:
\[
 \dot Q^1(0)=\lambda C_\lambda\kappa(x)v.
\]
On the other hand, because \((Q,P)\) is a solution of the same system,
its initial data and~\eqref{eq:first-hamilton} give
\[
 \dot Q^1(0)
 =\kappa(Q^1(0)-Q^2(0))P_2(0)
 =A_\lambda\kappa(\lambda x)v.
\]
Since \(v\) is arbitrary,
\begin{equation}
 \kappa(\lambda x)=h_\lambda\kappa(x)
 \quad\text{for all }x\in\R^d,
 \qquad
 h_\lambda:=\frac{\lambda C_\lambda}{A_\lambda}>0.
 \label{eq:homogeneity}
\end{equation}

Lemma~\ref{lem:zero} and the assumption \(K\neq0\) imply
\(\kappa(0)\neq0\).  Setting \(x=0\) in
\eqref{eq:homogeneity} yields
\((1-h_\lambda)\kappa(0)=0\), hence \(h_\lambda=1\).
Therefore
\[
 \kappa(\lambda x)=\kappa(x)
 \qquad(\lambda>0,\ x\in\R^d).
\]
For fixed \(x\), let \(\lambda\downarrow0\).  Continuity at the origin
gives \(\kappa(x)=\kappa(0)\).

For an \(N\)-landmark configuration, the block Gram matrix is now
\[
 G_N=(\one_N\one_N^{\mathsf T})\kron\kappa(0).
\]
Its rank is at most \(d\), whereas its size is \(Nd\).  It is therefore
singular for every \(N\geq2\).  The landmark metric tensor in the source
paper is the inverse of this block Gram matrix, so no nondegenerate
metric results.

For the converse, let \(\kappa(x)=B\), where \(B\) is positive
semidefinite.  Then the Hamiltonian equations reduce to
\[
 \dot q^a=B\sum_{b=1}^{N}p_b,
 \qquad \dot p_a=0.
\]
For every \(\lambda>0\), taking \(C_\lambda=1\) and
\(A_\lambda=\lambda\) shows directly that
\(Q(t)=\lambda q(t)\), \(P(t)=\lambda p(t)\) is again a solution.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The \(C_0^1\)-RKHS class has only the zero kernel]
\label{cor:c0}
Assume, as in the source paper, that \(K\) is the reproducing kernel of
a Hilbert space \(V\) continuously embedded in
\(C_0^1(\R^d,\R^d)\).  If the fixed-kernel landmark dynamics are exactly
dilation covariant, then \(K=0\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If \(K\neq0\), Theorem~\ref{thm:main} makes
\(\kappa(x)=\kappa(0)\).  For every \(y,a\in\R^d\), the kernel section
\[
 x\longmapsto K(x,y)a=\kappa(0)a
\]
belongs to \(V\), hence lies in \(C_0^1\) and must vanish at infinity.
A constant function with that property is zero.  Thus
\(\kappa(0)a=0\) for every \(a\), contradicting \(K\neq0\).
\end{proof}

\section*{What the Result Says About Kernel Design}

The sharp classification shows that the obstruction is caused by the
conjunction of four requirements:
a single fixed kernel, stationarity, ordinary positive definiteness with
a finite value at the origin, and exact covariance under all dilations.
It is not merely a defect of Gaussian or Bessel examples.  Any kernel in
this class would have to be homogeneous of degree zero; regularity at the
origin collapses degree-zero homogeneity to constancy, and constancy
collapses the landmark metric.

Thus exact scale robustness requires changing at least one ingredient.
Viable directions include a family
\(\kappa_\sigma\) with a length parameter transformed as
\(\sigma\mapsto\lambda\sigma\), a dynamical scale variable, a
configuration-dependent normalization, or a singular/conditionally
positive-definite construction outside the source's RKHS assumptions.
The theorem does not distinguish which enlargement is most appropriate
for an application.

\begin{remark}[Why a scale family escapes]
If \(\kappa_\sigma(x)=\phi(x/\sigma)\), then
\(\kappa_{\lambda\sigma}(\lambda x)=\kappa_\sigma(x)\).
This is covariance of a \emph{family}: the dilation changes the kernel
parameter.  Theorem~\ref{thm:main} concerns covariance of the dynamics
generated by one fixed kernel, exactly the setting in which a built-in
length constant breaks scale invariance.
\end{remark}

\section*{Verification}

The necessary direction is analytic and uses only the first Hamilton
equation; the converse follows by writing the constant-kernel equations
explicitly.  The accompanying dependency-free Python script performs three deterministic
sanity checks:
\begin{enumerate}
\item exact rational row reduction confirms that
  \((\one_N\one_N^{\mathsf T})\kron\kappa(0)\) has rank \(2\), not
  \(2N\), for a positive-definite \(2\times2\) choice of
  \(\kappa(0)\) and \(N=2,3,4\);
\item a Gaussian example has dilation ratio \(1\) at the origin but
  \(e^{-3}\) at unit distance when \(\lambda=2\), so it cannot satisfy
  \eqref{eq:homogeneity} with one scalar \(h_\lambda\);
\item a two-point scalar block with zero diagonal and nonzero cross term
  has quadratic values of both signs, illustrating
  Lemma~\ref{lem:zero}.
\end{enumerate}
The exact command and captured output are recorded in
\texttt{verification.md}.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This packet does not solve the source's full program of designing
application-specific kernels or relating their tails and regularity to
conjugate points and generic geodesic geometry.  It also does not rule
out approximate scale invariance, invariance over a bounded range, or
the model enlargements listed above.

Before packaging, the run's cheap title, identifier, and keyword indexes
were checked for earlier treatment of arXiv:1308.5739.  Bounded web
searches on 2026-07-22 for the exact title and combinations of
\emph{LDDMM}, \emph{landmark Hamiltonian}, \emph{scale invariant},
\emph{dilation covariance}, and \emph{homogeneous positive-definite
kernel} found the source paper and general kernel or computer-vision
material, but no prior statement of this fixed-kernel obstruction.
This is evidence of novelty, not an exhaustive literature review.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a likely valid substantial partial result.  The critical points
are the quantifiers in the covariance definition, the two-landmark
velocity comparison leading to~\eqref{eq:homogeneity}, the use of
positive definiteness at the origin, and the distinction between a fixed
kernel and a scale-indexed family.  The argument requires no claim about
the second Hamilton equation beyond existence of the flow and covariance
of the full solution.
