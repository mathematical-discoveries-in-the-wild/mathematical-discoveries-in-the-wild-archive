# Roe and uniform Roe rigidity questions answered in later literature

Status: literature_already_answered

Source paper: Bruno M. Braga, Yeong Chyuan Chung, and Kang Li, "Coarse
Baum-Connes conjecture and rigidity for Roe algebras", arXiv:1907.10237,
Journal of Functional Analysis 279 (2020), 108728.

Supporting papers:

- Diego Martinez and Federico Vigolo, "C*-Rigidity of bounded geometry metric
  spaces", arXiv:2501.03128, Publications mathematiques de l'IHES 141 (2025),
  333-348.
- Alessandro Vignati, "The rigidity problem for uniform Roe algebras",
  arXiv:2602.01998 (2026).

## Identification

On page 18, Question 5.5 of the source asks whether Osajda's finitely generated
group containing a large-girth expander is Roe rigid. Martinez--Vigolo's
Theorem A proves the general statement that any uniformly locally finite
metric spaces with isomorphic Roe algebras are coarsely equivalent. A Cayley
graph of a finitely generated group is uniformly locally finite, so the answer
is yes. The supporting paper cites the source as reference [6] while describing
the same C*-rigidity program.

On the same page, Question 5.8 asks whether every uniformly locally finite
metric space coarsely embedding into Hilbert space is uniform Roe bijectively
rigid. Vignati's Theorem A proves, without geometric assumptions, that
isomorphic uniform Roe algebras force bijective coarse equivalence. Hence the
answer is again yes, and the Hilbert-space embedding assumption is unnecessary.

## Scope and provenance

This packet records later literature answers, not new mathematics produced by
the run. The first supporting paper explicitly cites the source and solves the
general Roe-rigidity problem containing Question 5.5. The second paper presents
its theorem as the ultimate solution of the uniform Roe rigidity problem;
Question 5.8 is an immediate special case.
