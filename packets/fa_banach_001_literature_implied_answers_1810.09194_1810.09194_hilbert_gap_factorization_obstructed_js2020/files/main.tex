\section*{Status}

This is a literature-implied partial-subcase answer.  It records that a
later theorem of Johnson and Schechtman rules out the Hilbert-gap versions of
Mathieu--Tradacete Remark 6.11.  It is not a full answer to the all-exponent
approximate factorization question.

\section*{Source Question}

Mathieu and Tradacete ask in Remark 6.11 of \cite{MT} whether every
$T\in\SSing(\Lp)$, $1<p<\infty$ and $p\ne2$, is approximately
$(\ell_r,\ell_s)$-factorizable for some $r<s$.  They also ask the stronger
finite-dimensional-sum version.

\section*{Supporting Literature}

Let $\GammaTwo(L_p)$ denote the ideal of operators on $L_p$ that factor
through a Hilbert space, and let $\overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p)$ be its norm
closure.  Johnson and Schechtman prove in \cite{JS2020}, Remark 6, that their
large closed ideals in $L(L_p)$ do not contain the strictly singular ideal.
In the introduction they explicitly draw the consequence that
$\overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p)$ does not contain all strictly singular operators
on $L_p$, for every $1<p\ne2<\infty$.

Thus, for every $1<p\ne2<\infty$, there exists
\[
  S\in\SSing(L_p)\setminus \overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p).
\]

\section*{Identification}

If $1<p<2$ and an operator $T$ is approximately $(\ell_p,\ell_2)$-factorizable,
then $T$ is a norm limit of operators
\[
  L_p \xrightarrow{A_\varepsilon} \ell_p
      \xrightarrow{B_\varepsilon} \ell_2
      \xrightarrow{C_\varepsilon} L_p .
\]
Each approximant factors through the Hilbert space $\ell_2$, namely as
$C_\varepsilon(B_\varepsilon A_\varepsilon)$.  Hence
$T\in\overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p)$.

Similarly, if $2<p<\infty$ and $T$ is approximately
$(\ell_2,\ell_p)$-factorizable, then every approximant
\[
  L_p \xrightarrow{A_\varepsilon} \ell_2
      \xrightarrow{B_\varepsilon} \ell_p
      \xrightarrow{C_\varepsilon} L_p
\]
factors through $\ell_2$, so again $T\in\overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p)$.

Combining this elementary ideal observation with Johnson--Schechtman gives:

\[
\begin{array}{ll}
1<p<2: & \text{some }S\in\SSing(L_p)\text{ is not approximately }
        (\ell_p,\ell_2)\text{-factorizable},\\[3pt]
2<p<\infty: & \text{some }S\in\SSing(L_p)\text{ is not approximately }
        (\ell_2,\ell_p)\text{-factorizable}.
\end{array}
\]

\section*{Scope and Remaining Frontier}

This does not disprove the full assertion in Remark 6.11.  A strictly singular
operator outside $\overline{\GammaTwo}(L_p)$ might still be approximately
$(\ell_r,\ell_s)$-factorizable for a non-Hilbert pair $r<s$ with neither
exponent equal to $2$.  The result only shows that the natural Hilbert-gap
upgrade of the DFJP/Johnson factorization machinery cannot solve the problem
for all strictly singular operators.

The supporting authors were not, in this paper, explicitly answering
Mathieu--Tradacete Remark 6.11.  The relation above is an agent-identified
implication from their closed-ideal theorem.
