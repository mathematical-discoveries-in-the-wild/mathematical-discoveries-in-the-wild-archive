# Literature answer packet: Webster operator compact maps are not cb-norm closed

Status: literature already answered, negative answer to one final question in the source paper.

Source/open-problem paper: Javier Alejandro Chavez-Dominguez, Veronica Dimant, and Daniel Galicer, *Operator p-compact mappings*, arXiv:1809.07741.

Question addressed: in the final section, the paper recalls Webster's problem asking whether the space of operator compact maps must be closed in the completely bounded norm topology. The source reformulates it as whether `kappa_infty^o` and `||.||_cb` are equivalent on `K_infty^o`.

Supporting answer paper: Javier Alejandro Chavez-Dominguez, Veronica Dimant, and Daniel Galicer, *Revisiting Operator p-Compact Mappings*, arXiv:2405.18571.

Answer: negative. The later paper states in the introduction and again after its multiplication example that the case `p=infty` gives a negative answer to Webster's question: a two-sided multiplication map on `S_infty` is not operator compact but is the cb-norm limit of finite-rank truncations, which are operator compact.

Scope: this packet records the answer to Webster's cb-norm-closedness problem from `1809.07741`. It does not claim that the tensor-norm association questions `(/d_p^o, K_p^o)` or the general surjective-hull question are answered here.
