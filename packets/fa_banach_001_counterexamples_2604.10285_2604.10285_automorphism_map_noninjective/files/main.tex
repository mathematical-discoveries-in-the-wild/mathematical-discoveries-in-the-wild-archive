\begin{center}
{\Large A counterexample to injectivity of the automorphism-to-homeomorphism map}\\[4pt]
{\large arXiv:2604.10285, Remark 6.16}\\[6pt]
\today
\end{center}

\section*{Source metadata}

The source paper is M.H.M. Rashid, \emph{Operator Algebras of
Bourgain Delbaen Spaces: Realization, Rigidity, and Ideal Structure},
arXiv:2604.10285.  Remark 6.16, on page 23 of the source PDF, asks
about the automorphism group of \(\Lop(\mathfrak X_{C(K)})\) and the
natural map from automorphisms to homeomorphisms of \(K\).

\section*{Source question}

The relevant source remark is reproduced below.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In particular, the source asks whether the natural map
\[
    \operatorname{Aut}\bigl(\Lop(\mathfrak X_{C(K)})\bigr)
    \longrightarrow \Homeo(K), \qquad \Phi\longmapsto h,
\]
obtained from the induced action on
\(\Cal(\mathfrak X_{C(K)})\cong C(K)\), might be injective.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The kernel of this map is already large at the elementary level.  If an
invertible operator \(U\) is a compact perturbation of the identity, then
conjugation by \(U\) is invisible in the Calkin algebra: modulo compact
operators, \(U\) and \(U^{-1}\) are both just the identity.  Nevertheless,
conjugation by such a \(U\) need not be the identity automorphism of the
full operator algebra.  Taking \(U=I+P\) for a nonzero finite-rank
projection \(P\) gives an explicit witness.

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be any Banach space with \(\dim X\geq 2\).  The quotient-induced
map from automorphisms of \(\Lop(X)\) to automorphisms of the Calkin
algebra is not injective.  Consequently, for every space
\(\mathfrak X_{C(K)}\) in the source paper, the stated map
\[
    \operatorname{Aut}\bigl(\Lop(\mathfrak X_{C(K)})\bigr)
    \longrightarrow \Homeo(K)
\]
is not injective.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(x\in X\) and \(f\in X^*\) with \(f(x)=1\), and set
\[
    Pz=f(z)x \qquad (z\in X).
\]
Then \(P\) is a rank-one projection.  The operator \(U=I+P\) is invertible,
with inverse \(U^{-1}=I-\frac12P\).  Hence
\[
    \Phi(T)=UTU^{-1} \qquad (T\in\Lop(X))
\]
defines an automorphism of the Banach algebra \(\Lop(X)\).

This automorphism induces the identity on the Calkin algebra.  Indeed,
\(U-I=P\) is finite-rank, hence compact, so in the quotient map
\(q:\Lop(X)\to \Cal(X)=\Lop(X)/\K(X)\) we have \(q(U)=q(I)\) and
\(q(U^{-1})=q(I)\).  Therefore, for every \(T\in \Lop(X)\),
\[
    q(\Phi(T))=q(U)q(T)q(U^{-1})=q(T).
\]

It remains only to check that \(\Phi\) is not the identity automorphism.
Pick \(0\neq y\in \ker f\), and define the rank-one operator
\[
    Tz=f(z)y \qquad (z\in X).
\]
Then \(PT=0\), while \(TP=T\).  Hence
\[
    \Phi(T)=(I+P)T\Bigl(I-\frac12P\Bigr)
    =(I+P)\Bigl(T-\frac12TP\Bigr)
    =(I+P)\frac12T
    =\frac12T\neq T.
\]
Thus \(\Phi\neq \operatorname{id}_{\Lop(X)}\), but \(\Phi\) has the same
induced Calkin-algebra action as the identity automorphism.

For \(X=\mathfrak X_{C(K)}\), the source paper identifies
\(\Cal(X)\) with \(C(K)\).  Since \(\Phi\) induces the identity on
\(\Cal(X)\), it induces the identity automorphism of \(C(K)\), and hence
the identity homeomorphism of \(K\) under the Banach-Stone/Gelfand
identification used in the source paper.  Therefore the automorphisms
\(\operatorname{id}_{\Lop(X)}\) and \(\Phi\) are distinct but have the
same image in \(\Homeo(K)\).  The map is not injective.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The automorphism constructed above is inner.  Thus the argument answers
the literal injectivity question in Remark 6.16, but it does not decide
whether there is an outer automorphism in the kernel.  If the intended
problem is the induced map after quotienting by inner automorphisms, that
is a strictly stronger formulation and is not settled here.
\end{remark}

\section*{Verification and novelty check}

No computational verification is needed; the proof is the finite-rank
calculation above.  The open-question crop is from page 23 of the source
PDF.  Local duplicate checks over the run registry, solution index,
attempt index, proof-gap index, and active claims found nearby Calkin
algebra packets but no packet for arXiv:2604.10285 or this
automorphism-kernel question.

A bounded web search on 2026-06-14 for exact and close phrases such as
\[
\texttt{"Aut(\textbackslash mathcal\{L\}(\textbackslash mathfrak\{X\}\_\{C(K)\}))" "Homeo(K)"}
\]
and
\[
\texttt{"Operator Algebras of Bourgain" "Is it injective"}
\]
found no prior answer to the exact Remark 6.16 injectivity question.
Novelty confidence is therefore moderate: the obstruction is elementary,
so it may be folklore, but it was not found in the bounded search.
