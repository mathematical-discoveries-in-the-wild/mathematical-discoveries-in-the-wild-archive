\section*{Source Problem}

Laitila, Nieminen, Saksman and Tylli ask whether their rigidity theorems for
composition operators on one Hardy space have analogues for
$C_\phi\in\mathcal L(H^p,H^q)$ when $p\ne q$.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source paper notes that, in the case $q<p$, compactness of
$C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ is equivalent to
\[
        m(E_\phi)=0,
\]
where
\[
        E_\phi=\{e^{it}\in\T:|\phi(e^{it})|=1\}.
\]
It cites Riedl, Jarchow and Smith for the boundedness/compactness theory
between different Hardy exponents.

\section*{Result}

This packet records the corresponding strict-singularity rigidity in the
``integrability-losing'' range $q<p$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The noncompact case $q<p$ is exactly the case in which the symbol has a
positive-measure boundary contact set.  The source paper already shows that
positive boundary contact forces a lacunary sequence of monomials
$z^{n_k}$ whose images $\phi^{n_k}$ dominate an $\ell^2$ basis in every
finite Hardy norm.  Paley's theorem says that the original lacunary monomials
are also an $\ell^2$ basis in every $H^p$.  Combining these two facts gives an
$\ell^2$ subspace of the domain on which $C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ is bounded below.
Thus noncompactness is incompatible with strict singularity.

\begin{theorem}
Let $1\le q<p<\infty$, and let $\phi:\D\to\D$ be analytic.  Then the following
conditions are equivalent for the composition operator $C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $C_\phi$ is compact;
    \item $C_\phi$ is strictly singular;
    \item $C_\phi$ is $\ell^2$-singular;
    \item $m(E_\phi)=0$.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, if $C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ is noncompact, then it fixes an
isomorphic copy of $\ell^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $q<p$ and the circle has normalized measure, the inclusion
$H^p\subset H^q$ is continuous.  Littlewood subordination gives boundedness of
$C_\phi$ on $H^p$, so $C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ is bounded for every analytic
self-map $\phi$.

Compact operators are strictly singular, and strictly singular operators are
$\ell^2$-singular.  Thus
\[
        (1)\Rightarrow (2)\Rightarrow (3).
\]

The source paper records the known compactness criterion for $q<p$: one has
\[
        C_\phi\in\K(H^p,H^q)
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        m(E_\phi)=0.
\]
Hence $(4)\Rightarrow(1)$, and it remains only to show that failure of (4)
implies failure of (3).

Assume $m(E_\phi)>0$.  The lacunary-power argument in Proposition 3.2 of the
source paper applies with the target exponent $q$.  Therefore there is an
increasing lacunary sequence of integers
\[
        0\le n_1<n_2<\cdots
\]
and a constant $A>0$ such that
\[
        \left\|\sum_k c_k \phi^{n_k}\right\|_{H^q}
        \ge A\|(c_k)\|_{\ell^2}
\]
for every finitely supported scalar sequence $(c_k)$.  For completeness, the
mechanism is as follows.  Lemma 3.1 of the source paper first gives an
$H^2$ lower estimate for a subsequence of the powers $\phi^n$ on the
positive-measure contact set.  If $q\ge2$, the normalized-measure inequality
$\|f\|_q\ge\|f\|_2$ gives the desired lower bound.  If $1\le q<2$, the
source proof uses the $H^1$--BMOA duality estimate from Proposition 3.2 to get
an $H^1$ lower bound, and then $\|f\|_q\ge\|f\|_1$ gives the $H^q$ lower
bound.

By passing to a further subsequence if necessary, the same sequence
$(n_k)$ may be assumed lacunary.  Paley's theorem for lacunary monomials gives
a constant $B\ge1$ such that
\[
        B^{-1}\|(c_k)\|_{\ell^2}
        \le
        \left\|\sum_k c_k z^{n_k}\right\|_{H^p}
        \le
        B\|(c_k)\|_{\ell^2}
\]
for every finitely supported $(c_k)$.  Put
\[
        M=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\{z^{n_k}:k\ge1\}\subset H^p.
\]
Then $M$ is isomorphic to $\ell^2$, and for every finitely supported
$(c_k)$,
\[
 \left\|C_\phi\left(\sum_k c_k z^{n_k}\right)\right\|_{H^q}
 =
 \left\|\sum_k c_k \phi^{n_k}\right\|_{H^q}
 \ge A\|(c_k)\|_{\ell^2}
 \ge \frac{A}{B}
       \left\|\sum_k c_k z^{n_k}\right\|_{H^p}.
\]
By density, the same lower estimate holds on all of $M$.  Hence $C_\phi$ is
bounded below on a subspace of $H^p$ isomorphic to $\ell^2$, so $C_\phi$ is
not $\ell^2$-singular.  This proves $(3)\Rightarrow(4)$ by contrapositive,
and all four conditions are equivalent.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
For $1\le q<p<\infty$, every noncompact composition operator
$C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ is non-strictly singular.  In fact it fixes a copy of
$\ell^2$.
\end{corollary}

\section*{Scope and Novelty Check}

This is a partial answer to Problem 4.3(1) of the source paper.  It settles
the finite-exponent range $q<p$, where compactness is governed by the boundary
contact condition.  It does not address the opposite range $p<q$, where
boundedness itself is a stronger Carleson-type condition and the source's
lacunary monomial span does not by itself give a bounded-below map from an
$\ell^p$ domain model into an $\ell^q$ image model.

Cheap run indexes were searched for \texttt{1607.00113}, the source title,
\texttt{H\^{}p,H\^{}q}, \texttt{Hardy}, \texttt{composition operator}, and
\texttt{strict singularity}.  No exact prior local packet for this
cross-exponent partial result was found.  Bounded web checks for
\texttt{strictly singular composition operators H\^{}p H\^{}q},
\texttt{composition operators between Hardy spaces strict singular}, and
related phrases found the source paper and adjacent weighted-composition
literature, but no later result explicitly recording this $q<p$ implication.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review should focus on the reuse of Proposition 3.2 of the source paper with
the target exponent renamed to $q$, and on the cited compactness criterion
for $C_\phi:H^p\to H^q$ in the case $q<p$.  The rest is a direct Paley
lacunary-subspace argument.
