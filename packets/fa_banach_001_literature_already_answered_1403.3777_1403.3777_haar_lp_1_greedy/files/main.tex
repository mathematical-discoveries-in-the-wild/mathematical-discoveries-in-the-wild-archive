\section*{Source Open Problem}

The original open problem appears in Dilworth, Kutzarova, Odell,
Schlumprecht, and Zsak, \emph{Renorming spaces with greedy bases},
arXiv:1403.3777, Section 5, Problem 14. The paper asks whether, for
every \(1<p<\infty\), there is an equivalent norm on \(L_p[0,1]\) for
which the Haar basis is \(1\)-greedy.

\section*{Status}

The answer is affirmative, and this was already known to the authors of
the later supporting paper. This packet is therefore a literature-status
record, not an original proof and not a new literature completion by the
pipeline. The later arXiv paper explicitly identifies the Albiac--
Wojtaszczyk question and states that it resolves it:

\begin{quote}
F. Albiac, J. L. Ansorena, M. Berasategui, and P. M. Berna,
\emph{Isometric Renormings for Greedy Bases in Banach Spaces, with
applications to the Haar system in \(L_p[0,1]\), \(1<p<\infty\)},
arXiv:2603.21733.
\end{quote}

That paper proves that for every \(1<p<\infty\), the space \(L_p[0,1]\)
can be equivalently renormed so that the \(L_p\)-normalized Haar system
is isometrically greedy. In the standard terminology of greedy bases,
``isometrically greedy'' means greedy with constant \(1\). Therefore the
supporting paper settles Problem 14 exactly, and explicitly knows it is
settling the earlier Albiac--Wojtaszczyk problem.

\section*{Proof Chain}

Let \(\mathcal H_p\) denote the \(L_p\)-normalized Haar system in
\(L_p[0,1]\).

\begin{claim}
For every \(1<p<\infty\), \(L_p[0,1]\) admits an equivalent norm for
which \(\mathcal H_p\) is \(1\)-greedy.
\end{claim}

\begin{proof}
The supporting paper proves a general renorming theorem: if a greedy
basis has fundamental function equivalent to \(m^\alpha\), with
\(0<\alpha<1\), then the ambient Banach space admits an equivalent norm
under which that basis is isometrically greedy.

For the Haar system, Temlyakov's theorem gives that \(\mathcal H_p\) is
a greedy basis of \(L_p[0,1]\) for \(1<p<\infty\). Its fundamental
function is equivalent to \(m^{1/p}\). Since \(1<p<\infty\), we have
\(0<1/p<1\). Applying the general renorming theorem with
\(\alpha=1/p\) gives an equivalent norm on \(L_p[0,1]\) under which
\(\mathcal H_p\) is isometrically greedy, hence \(1\)-greedy.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

No numerical computation is needed for this packet. Verification is by
exact statement matching:

\begin{itemize}
\item The original problem asks for an equivalent norm on \(L_p[0,1]\)
making the Haar basis \(1\)-greedy for all \(1<p<\infty\).
\item The supporting theorem gives an equivalent norm on \(L_p[0,1]\)
making the normalized Haar system isometrically greedy for all
\(1<p<\infty\).
\item ``Isometrically greedy'' is the constant-one version of greedy,
so the theorem gives precisely the requested property.
\end{itemize}
