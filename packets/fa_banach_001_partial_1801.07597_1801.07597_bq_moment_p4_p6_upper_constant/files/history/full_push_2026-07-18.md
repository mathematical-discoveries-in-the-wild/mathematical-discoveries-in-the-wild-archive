# Full-push history note: beyond p=4 and p=6

Status: no promoted strengthening.

This note records the follow-up push after the packet proving the finite-dimensional
upper constants for p=4 and p=6 in Question 5 of Eskenazis--Nayar--Tkocz,
arXiv:1801.07597.  It is intentionally kept inside the existing partial packet
so that the run does not accumulate multiple partial packets for the same
result.

## What was checked

The iid reduction in the source paper asks us to maximise

```text
E |sum_i a_i Y_i|^p,  sum_i a_i^2 = 1,
```

where the Y_i are iid with density proportional to exp(-|x|^q), q>2.  Writing
x_i=a_i^2, the p=2m moment is a symmetric polynomial on the simplex.  The p=4
and p=6 packet proves Schur concavity of this polynomial.

The push recomputed the polynomial and Schur derivative tests for even moments
p=8,10,12,16,20.  Random interior tests and direct optimisation over many
simplex samples found no counterexample; the equal point remained the largest
sampled value, and the sampled Schur derivative brackets were negative.

For p=8, the normalised moment polynomial is

```text
F_8 =
  105
  + (315 - 210 u + 35 u^2) s_2^2
  + (-630 + 210 u) s_2
  + (840 - 420 u + 28 v) s_3
  + (-630 + 420 u - 28 v - 35 u^2 + w) s_4,
```

where u=E Y^4/(E Y^2)^2, v=E Y^6/(E Y^2)^3, w=E Y^8/(E Y^2)^4, and
s_k=sum_i x_i^k.  A possible proof route is to bound the Schur derivative by
two one-variable quadratics in t=x_i+x_j.  The endpoint inequalities reduce to
the same type of Gaussian moment deficits used in the packet:

```text
w < 7 v,
3 w < 35 u^2.
```

These follow from the source paper's Lemma 11 with r=6, h(t)=t^(4/3), and
with r=4, h(t)=t^2, respectively.  However, the quadratic monotonicity step
also needs additional gamma-moment inequalities, for example

```text
2 w - 35 v + 105 u >= 0,
3 w - 42 v + 210 u - 35 u^2 >= 0.
```

The inequalities are numerically positive for q>2 and vanish at q=2, but a
clean proof was not completed.  Therefore p=8 was not added to the theorem.

## Later-literature route checked

The 2025 paper of Andreas Malliaris, "Monotonicity of functionals associated to
product measures via their Fourier transform and applications" (arXiv:2504.06172,
published in Mathematika 2025), gives broad Schur monotonicity criteria for
product measures using positivity and log-convexity/log-concavity of the Fourier
transform of the density.

This does not currently close the present q>2 exponential-power case.  The
criterion assumes a positive Fourier transform.  For phi(x)=c exp(-|x|^q) with
q>2 this fails: by Schoenberg's positive-definiteness theorem, exp(-|x|^q) is
not positive definite on R for q>2, hence its Fourier transform cannot be a
nonnegative measure.  Numerically, for example, the unnormalised Fourier
transform of exp(-x^4) with the exp(-2 pi i t x) convention is already negative
near t=0.56.

## Current verdict

The full result still looks plausible, and the p=8 route is now more promising
than a naive obstruction narrative.  The missing component is a rigorous proof
of the required gamma-moment inequalities (or a different global argument for
Schur concavity).  Until that is supplied, the durable claim should remain the
p=4 and p=6 partial theorem in the main packet.
