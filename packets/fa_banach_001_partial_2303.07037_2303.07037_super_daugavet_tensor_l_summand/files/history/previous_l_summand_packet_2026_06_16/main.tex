\emph{Unconditional bases and Daugavet renormings}, arXiv:2303.07037.

\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

The sentence reads: ``We do not know whether super Daugavet points pass to the

\section*{Partial Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be Banach spaces over \(\K\).  Suppose \(x_0\in S_X\) is
a super Daugavet point.  Suppose \(y_0\in S_Y\) spans a one-dimensional
L-summand of \(Y\), i.e. there is a Banach space \(Z\) such that
\[
   Y = \K y_0 \oplus_1 Z
\]
isometrically.  Then \(x_0\otimes y_0\) is a super Daugavet point of
\(X\otpi Y\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:examples}
If \(x_0\) is a super Daugavet point in \(X\), then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item for every set \(\Gamma\) and every \(\gamma\in\Gamma\),
      \(x_0\otimes e_\gamma\) is a super Daugavet point of
      \(X\otpi \ell_1(\Gamma)\);
\item if \(A\) is an atom of finite positive measure in a measure space
      \((\Omega,\Sigma,\mu)\), then
      \(x_0\otimes \mu(A)^{-1}\mathbf 1_A\) is a super Daugavet point of
      \(X\otpi L_1(\mu)\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

\section*{Idea Of The Proof}

The key point is that the projective tensor product distributes isometrically
over an \(\ell_1\)-sum.  If \(Y=\K y_0\oplus_1 Z\), then
\[
   X\otpi Y \cong X \oplus_1 (X\otpi Z),
\]
and \(x_0\otimes y_0\) becomes \((x_0,0)\).  Thus the problem reduces to a
simple stability fact: a super Daugavet point remains super Daugavet after
embedding it as the first coordinate of an \(\ell_1\)-sum.

This only treats tensor factors with a one-dimensional L-summand.  It does not
settle the source paper's general question for arbitrary \(y_0\in S_Y\).

\section*{Proof}

We use the following standard net characterization, included for completeness.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:net}
Let \(x\in S_X\).  Then \(x\) is a super Daugavet point if and only if, for
every \(u\in B_X\), there is a net \((u_\alpha)\subset B_X\) such that
\[
   u_\alpha \xrightarrow{w} u
   \qquad\text{and}\qquad
   \|x-u_\alpha\|\longrightarrow 2 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If \(x\) is a super Daugavet point, order pairs \((U,\varepsilon)\), where
\(U\) is a weak neighbourhood of \(u\) in \(B_X\) and \(\varepsilon>0\), by
reverse inclusion and decreasing \(\varepsilon\).  For each pair choose
\(u_{U,\varepsilon}\in U\) with
\(\|x-u_{U,\varepsilon}\|>2-\varepsilon\).  This gives the desired net.

Conversely, let \(W\) be a non-empty relatively weakly open subset of \(B_X\)
and choose \(u\in W\).  A net as above is eventually in \(W\), so
\(\sup_{w\in W}\|x-w\|=2\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:l1sum}
Let \(x_0\) be a super Daugavet point in \(X\), and let \(Z\) be any Banach
space.  Then \((x_0,0)\) is a super Daugavet point in \(X\oplus_1 Z\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Take \((u,v)\in B_{X\oplus_1 Z}\), and put \(s=\|v\|\) and \(r=1-s\).  Then
\(\|u\|\le r\).

If \(r=0\), then \(u=0\), and the constant net \((0,v)\) already satisfies
\[
   \|(x_0,0)-(0,v)\|=\|x_0\|+\|v\|=2.
\]

Assume now that \(r>0\).  By Lemma~\ref{lem:net}, choose a net
\((p_\alpha)\subset B_X\) such that
\[
   p_\alpha\xrightarrow{w} u/r
   \qquad\text{and}\qquad
   \|x_0-p_\alpha\|\to2.
\]
Set \(w_\alpha=(rp_\alpha,v)\).  Then
\[
   \|rp_\alpha\|+\|v\|\le r+s=1,
\]
so \(w_\alpha\in B_{X\oplus_1 Z}\), and clearly
\(w_\alpha\xrightarrow{w}(u,v)\).
Moreover,
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \|(x_0,0)-w_\alpha\|
   &= \|x_0-rp_\alpha\|+s  \\
   &\ge \|x_0-p_\alpha\|-(1-r)\|p_\alpha\|+s \\
   &\ge \|x_0-p_\alpha\|.
\end{aligned}
\]
The reverse inequality \(\|(x_0,0)-w_\alpha\|\le2\) is automatic because both
points lie in the unit ball.  Hence the distances tend to \(2\), and
Lemma~\ref{lem:net} proves that \((x_0,0)\) is super Daugavet.
\end{proof}

We now prove Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.  The projective tensor product is
isometrically additive over \(\ell_1\)-sums:
\[
  X\otpi(\K y_0\oplus_1 Z)
  \cong (X\otpi \K y_0)\oplus_1(X\otpi Z)
  \cong X\oplus_1(X\otpi Z).
\]
Under this canonical isometry, \(x_0\otimes y_0\) corresponds to
\((x_0,0)\).  The conclusion follows directly from Lemma~\ref{lem:l1sum}.
\(\square\)

The corollary follows because each coordinate line in \(\ell_1(\Gamma)\) is
an L-summand, and a finite positive atom \(A\) gives the isometric
decomposition
\[
   L_1(\mu)
   = \K\,\mu(A)^{-1}\mathbf 1_A
     \oplus_1
     L_1(\mu|_{\Omega\setminus A}).
\]

\section*{Scope And Limitations}

This is a partial positive answer to the tensor-transfer uncertainty in the
source paper.  It covers tensor factors with an L-summand coordinate, including
\(\ell_1(\Gamma)\) coordinates and atoms of \(L_1(\mu)\).  It does not prove
that \(x_0\otimes y_0\) is super Daugavet for an arbitrary Banach space \(Y\)
and arbitrary \(y_0\in S_Y\).

This packet also does not address the later renorming question in the source
paper asking whether \(\ell_1\) can be renormed to contain a super Daugavet
point.
