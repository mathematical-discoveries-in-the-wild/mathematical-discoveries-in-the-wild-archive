Problem 6.1 asks whether every subspace of a Banach space with a norming
M-basis again admits a norming M-basis.

## Result

Let `X_rho` be the HRST space from Theorem A of the source paper, namely the
closed linear span of the coordinate functions `f_alpha` in `C(K_rho)`.
For every set `A subset omega_1`, the coordinate-generated subspace

```text
X_A = closure span{f_alpha : alpha in A} subset X_rho
```

admits a `1`-norming M-basis. More precisely,

```text
{ f_alpha ; mu_alpha|_{X_A} }_{alpha in A}
```

is a `1`-norming M-basis of `X_A`.

## Proof Idea

The HRST norming proof for `X_rho` rests on the dense family `F_rho` of finite
sets in `K_rho`. For each finite `F in F_rho`, the point evaluation
`delta_F` agrees on `X_rho` with the finite sum of coordinate measures
`sum_{alpha in F} mu_alpha`.

After restricting to the coordinate subspace generated by `A`, the same
identity simply becomes

```text
delta_F|_{X_A} = sum_{alpha in F cap A} mu_alpha|_{X_A}.
```

Thus the evaluations on the dense finite part of `K_rho` are still contained
in the finite coordinate span indexed by `A`, so that span remains
`1`-norming.

## Scope And Limitations

- This does not solve Problem 6.1 for arbitrary subspaces of `X_rho` or of a
  general norming-M-basis space.
- It rules out the most direct counterexample search inside `X_rho`: a bad
  subspace cannot be merely the closed span of a subset of the canonical HRST
  coordinates.
- The result is specific to the HRST coordinate model and uses the finite-set
  density of `F_rho`.
