\section*{Status and source question}

\noindent\textbf{Status.} Candidate counterexample, likely valid pending human review.

This packet concerns the $p$-Schatten boundedness question in G.~Lee and
M.~Lein, \emph{A proof of $\Sp^2$-boundedness for magnetic
pseudodifferential super operators via matrix representations with respect to
Parseval frames}, arXiv:2405.19964, published in \emph{Monatshefte f\"ur
Mathematik} \textbf{206} (2025), 597--627 \cite{LeeLein}.

On page 3 the source asks for conditions under which a magnetic
pseudodifferential super operator is bounded on the trace class and, more
generally, on $\Sp^p$ for $1\leq p\leq\infty$:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

Theorem 5.3 proves that every order-zero symbol in $S^0_{\rho,0}$ gives a
bounded operator on $\Sp^2$.  Remark 5.4 (pages 29--30) singles out the hoped
endpoint extensions under the same hypotheses, which would yield all other
exponents by interpolation:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.96\textwidth]{figures/same_hypotheses_remark_crop.png}
\end{center}

The result below gives a sharp negative answer to that same-hypotheses
extension.  It does not attempt to classify the additional symbol conditions
that do imply $\Sp^p$ boundedness.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

When the magnetic field is zero and a super symbol depends only on the two
position variables, its super Weyl quantization multiplies operator kernels:
\[
   K(x,y)\longmapsto F(x,y)K(x,y).
\]
Thus a smooth function of the difference $x-y$ produces a continuous Toeplitz
Schur multiplier.  We construct a sign sequence $m:\Z\to\{-1,1\}$ whose
Fourier multiplier is unbounded on every $L^p(\T)$ except $L^2(\T)$, and
smoothly interpolate the values $m(n)$ by a function $\phi\in C^\infty(\R)$
with every derivative bounded.  The symbol
\[
   F(x_L,\xi_L,x_R,\xi_R)=\phi(x_L-x_R)
\]
then belongs to every order-zero class $S^0_{\rho,0}$.

Small, disjoint translates of one test function embed matrices into operators
on $L^2(\R)$.  On this embedded copy, multiplication by $\phi(x-y)$ is exactly
the Toeplitz Schur multiplier $(a_{ij})\mapsto(m(i-j)a_{ij})$.  Fourier--Schur
transference now transfers the bad $L^p(\T)$ multiplier behavior to every
Schatten class.  The one exceptional exponent is $p=2$, where any bounded
entrywise multiplier is automatically bounded.

\section*{Construction of one multiplier bad at every $p\ne2$}

We normalize Haar measure on $\T$ and write
\[
   D_N(t)=\sum_{k=1}^N e^{ikt}.
\]

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:universal-sign}
There is a real sequence $m:\Z\to\{-1,1\}$ such that the Fourier multiplier
\[
  T_m\!\left(\sum_k \widehat f(k)e^{ikt}\right)
   =\sum_k m(k)\widehat f(k)e^{ikt}
\]
does not extend boundedly to $L^p(\T)$ for any
$p\in[1,\infty]$, $p\ne2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix $q>2$.  Khintchine's inequality, applied pointwise in $t$ and then
integrated, shows that for every $N$ there are signs
$\varepsilon_1,\ldots,\varepsilon_N$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:random-sign}
  \norm{\sum_{k=1}^N\varepsilon_k e^{ikt}}_{L^q(\T)}
  \leq C_q N^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
On the other hand, $|D_N(t)|\geq cN$ when $|t|\leq c/N$, and hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dirichlet}
  \norm{D_N}_{L^q(\T)}\geq c_q N^{1-1/q}.
\end{equation}

Enumerate the rational numbers in $(2,\infty)$, repeating every rational
infinitely many times.  For the $j$th term $q_j$, choose a fresh finite
interval $I_j\subset\Z$, with length $N_j$ tending to infinity sufficiently
fast, and choose signs on $I_j$ satisfying \eqref{eq:random-sign} for $q_j$.
Define $m$ to be those signs on the pairwise disjoint intervals $I_j$, and put
$m(k)=1$ elsewhere.

For a block $I_j$ define
\[
  f_j(t)=\sum_{k\in I_j}m(k)e^{ikt}.
\]
Up to multiplication by a character, \eqref{eq:random-sign} gives
$\norm{f_j}_{q_j}\leq C_{q_j}N_j^{1/2}$, whereas $m(k)^2=1$ and
\eqref{eq:dirichlet} give
\[
  \norm{T_m f_j}_{q_j}\geq c_{q_j}N_j^{1-1/q_j}.
\]
The norm ratio is at least a constant times
$N_j^{1/2-1/q_j}$.  The lengths can be chosen so that, along the infinitely
many blocks assigned to each rational $q>2$, this ratio tends to infinity.
Thus $T_m$ is unbounded on every rational $L^q(\T)$ with $q>2$.

Since $|m|=1$, $T_m$ is an isometry on $L^2$.  If it were bounded on some
$L^p$ with $p>2$, interpolation with $L^2$ would make it bounded on every
$L^q$ with $2<q<p$, contradicting a rational exponent in this interval.
For $1<p<2$, boundedness would imply boundedness of the adjoint multiplier
on $L^{p'}$; because $m$ is real, the adjoint has the same symbol, contradicting
the case $p'>2$.  Boundedness on $L^1$ or $L^\infty$, together with duality or
interpolation with $L^2$, likewise implies boundedness at some finite exponent
different from $2$.  Therefore no such bounded extension exists.
\end{proof}

\section*{The counterexample}

Choose $\eta\in C_c^\infty((-1/4,1/4))$, real-valued, with
$0\leq\eta\leq1$ and $\eta=1$ on $[-1/8,1/8]$, and let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:phi}
  \phi(t)=\sum_{n\in\Z}m(n)\eta(t-n).
\end{equation}
The supports in this sum are disjoint.  Consequently $\phi$ is real-valued,
bounded, smooth, and every derivative of $\phi$ is bounded.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:counterexample}
Let $d=1$, $B=0$, and $A=0$.  The real super symbol
\begin{equation}\label{eq:F}
  F(x_L,\xi_L,x_R,\xi_R)=\phi(x_L-x_R)
\end{equation}
belongs to $S^0_{\rho,0}(\R^4)$ and to the double class
$S^{0,0}_{\rho,0}(\R^4)$ for every $0\leq\rho\leq1$.  Its non-magnetic super
Weyl quantization $\operatorname{Op}^0(F)$ is bounded on
$\Sp^2(L^2(\R))$, but it has no bounded extension on
$\Sp^p(L^2(\R))$ for any $p\in[1,\infty]$, $p\ne2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
All position derivatives of $F$ are bounded because all derivatives of
$\phi$ are bounded, while every derivative in $\xi_L$ or $\xi_R$ vanishes.
This is precisely the required order-zero estimate for both stated
H\"ormander classes, for every $\rho\in[0,1]$.

For a symbol independent of the two momentum variables, the non-magnetic
super Weyl quantization is joint position functional calculus.  Hence, on
smooth finite-rank operators $X$ with integral kernel $K_X$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kernel-action}
  K_{\operatorname{Op}^0(F)X}(x,y)=\phi(x-y)K_X(x,y).
\end{equation}
This also follows first for tensor symbols from the identity
\[
  F(x_L,x_R)=f_L(x_L)f_R(x_R)
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \operatorname{Op}^0(F)X=f_L(Q)Xf_R(Q),
\]
and then from the distributional super Weyl formula.

Choose a real unit vector $h\in C_c^\infty((-1/32,1/32))$ and set
$h_j(x)=h(x-j)$ for $j\in\Z$.  These vectors have pairwise disjoint supports,
so the map $U:\ell^2(\Z)\to L^2(\R)$, $Ue_j=h_j$, is an isometry.  If
$a=(a_{ij})$ is a finite matrix, then $UaU^*$ has kernel
\[
  K_a(x,y)=\sum_{i,j}a_{ij}h_i(x)\overline{h_j(y)}.
\]
For $(x,y)\in\operatorname{supp}(h_i)\times\operatorname{supp}(h_j)$ we have
$|(x-y)-(i-j)|<1/16$.  The plateau and disjoint-support properties of
$\eta$ in \eqref{eq:phi} therefore imply
\[
   \phi(x-y)=m(i-j).
\]
Combining this identity with \eqref{eq:kernel-action} gives the exact
intertwining relation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intertwine}
  \operatorname{Op}^0(F)(UaU^*)
    =U\bigl(m(i-j)a_{ij}\bigr)_{i,j\in\Z}U^*.
\end{equation}

For finite $p$, $a\mapsto UaU^*$ preserves the Schatten $p$-norm.  If
$\operatorname{Op}^0(F)$ were bounded on $\Sp^p(L^2(\R))$, relation
\eqref{eq:intertwine} would give a uniformly bounded Toeplitz Schur
multiplier
\[
   S_m:(a_{ij})\longmapsto(m(i-j)a_{ij})
\]
on $\Sp^p(\ell^2(\Z))$.  The Fourier-to-Schur transference inequality of
Neuwirth and Ricard \cite[Theorem~2.1]{NeuwirthRicard} says, for the amenable
group $\Z$, that the $L^p(\T)$ Fourier multiplier norm of $m$ is no larger
than this Toeplitz Schur multiplier norm.  Lemma~\ref{lem:universal-sign}
therefore rules out boundedness for every finite $p\ne2$.

At $p=\infty$, boundedness together with the automatic $\Sp^2$ bound would,
by interpolation of Schatten classes, give boundedness on every
$\Sp^q$, $2<q<\infty$, which has just been ruled out.  Finally,
$\operatorname{Op}^0(F)$ is bounded on $\Sp^2$ directly from
\eqref{eq:kernel-action}: under the Hilbert--Schmidt kernel identification it
is multiplication by the bounded function $\phi(x-y)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification, failed route, and scope}

The first attempted positive route decomposed the source paper's rapidly
off-diagonal four-index frame matrix into shifts.  For fixed shifts, however,
the remaining coefficient array is an arbitrary Schur multiplier; a bounded
array need not be bounded on $\Sp^p$ when $p\ne2$.  This obstruction led to
the construction above.  An intermediate endpoint-only construction used the
triangular projection.  The final sign-block construction upgrades that route
to one symbol failing simultaneously at every exponent $p\ne2$.

The finite script \texttt{code/check\_block\_multiplier.py} samples the block
mechanism.  With $q=4$, 128 trials, and a $16384$-point quadrature grid, the
observed Dirichlet-to-random-sign norm ratio increased from $1.70$ at $N=16$
to $3.14$ at $N=256$.  This is only a sanity check; the proof is the
Khintchine and Dirichlet-kernel estimate above.

The theorem disproves a universal $S^0_{\rho,0}\Rightarrow\Sp^p$ boundedness
statement for every $p\ne2$, already in the zero-field case.  It does not
exclude positive results for smaller symbol classes.  Indeed, Cornean and
Thorn \cite{CorneanThorn} prove later sufficient conditions involving
integrable weights (Proposition 5.2) and a completed projective tensor-product
symbol class (Proposition 5.5).  Those stronger subclasses do not contain the
general order-zero conclusion refuted here.

\section*{Novelty check and human-review recommendation}

The four lightweight run indexes were searched for arXiv:2405.19964, its title,
``magnetic pseudodifferential super operator'', ``Schatten'', ``trace class'',
``Schur multiplier'', and close variants; no duplicate was found.  Bounded web
searches used the exact source title and combinations of ``order zero'',
``Schatten $p$'', ``Schur multiplier'', and the authors' names.  They found the
2025 published source and the March 2026 follow-up arXiv:2603.26455, but no
paper stating this counterexample or the sharp failure for all $p\ne2$.

The recommended human review is to check two convention-sensitive points:
(i) equation \eqref{eq:kernel-action} under Lee--Lein's right-action convention,
and (ii) the application of Neuwirth--Ricard Theorem 2.1 to the full Toeplitz
set for $\Gamma=\Z$.  The remainder is elementary harmonic analysis and an
isometric finite-matrix compression.
