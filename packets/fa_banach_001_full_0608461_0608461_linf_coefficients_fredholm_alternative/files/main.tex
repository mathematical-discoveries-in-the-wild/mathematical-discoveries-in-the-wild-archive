\section*{Question Restated}

In the notation of \cite{KmitRecke2007}, Theorem 1 assumes
\(\ga\ge 1\), \(a,d\in \Linfty(0,1)\), \(b,c\in BV(0,1)\), and the
nonresonance condition
\[
  |r_0r_1|\ne
  \exp\left(\int_0^1(a(x)+d(x))\,dx\right).
\]
It then proves that \(A+B:V^\ga(r_0,r_1)\to W^\ga\) is Fredholm of
index zero and gives the Fredholm alternative range description via the
homogeneous adjoint problem. Remark 14 asks whether the \(BV\) assumption on
\(b,c\) can be weakened to \(b,c\in \Linfty(0,1)\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Kmit--Recke prove Fredholmness by applying their abstract lemma to
\(C=BA^{-1}\): it suffices to show that \(C^2\) is compact on \(W^\ga\).
The compactness criterion requires uniform smallness of translated Fourier
modes. All parts of their proof already use only \(L^\infty\) bounds except one
high-frequency term, where \(c\in BV\) is used to integrate by parts and obtain
a \(1/(1+|k|)\) decay factor.

That much decay is stronger than the compactness argument needs. It is enough
that the troublesome coefficient tends to zero as \(|k|\to\infty\). After one
application of \(A^{-1}\), the diagonal solution factors
\(e^{ik\om x+\al(x)}u_k(x)\) and \(e^{-ik\om x-\de(x)}v_k(x)\) are uniformly
bounded in \(H^1(0,1)\), after normalization by
\(\|f_k\|_2+\|g_k\|_2\). Since bounded \(H^1\)-sets are compact in \(C[0,1]\),
oscillatory integrals against an arbitrary \(L^\infty\) coefficient satisfy a
uniform Riemann--Lebesgue lemma on exactly the needed family. This replaces the
\(BV\)-based \(1/(1+|k|)\) estimate by a decay factor \(\mu_k\to 0\), and the
same finite-tail argument proves compactness of \(C^2\).

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(\ga\ge 1\), \(a,b,c,d\in \Linfty(0,1)\), and assume
\[
  |r_0r_1|\ne
  \exp\left(\int_0^1(a(x)+d(x))\,dx\right).
\]
Then, in the spaces and notation of \cite{KmitRecke2007},
\[
  A+B:V^\ga(r_0,r_1)\longrightarrow W^\ga
\]
is Fredholm of index zero. Moreover,
\[
  \im(A+B)=
  \left\{(f,g)\in W^\ga:
  \int_0^{2\pi/\om}\int_0^1(fu+gv)\,dx\,dt=0
  \text{ for all }(u,v)\in\ker(\widetilde A+\widetilde B)\right\}.
\]
Thus all assertions of Theorem 1 of \cite{KmitRecke2007} remain true when
the hypothesis \(b,c\in BV(0,1)\) is replaced by \(b,c\in \Linfty(0,1)\).
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

We follow the notation of \cite{KmitRecke2007}. Put
\[
  \al(x)=\int_0^x a(y)\,dy,\qquad
  \de(x)=\int_0^x d(y)\,dy,
\]
and
\[
  \Delta_k=e^{ik\om+\de(1)}-r_0r_1e^{-ik\om-\al(1)}.
\]
The nonresonance condition gives \(\inf_k|\Delta_k|>0\), exactly as in
\cite[(3.8)]{KmitRecke2007}.

\begin{lemma}[uniform oscillation on \(H^1\)-bounded sets]
\label{lem:uniform-rl}
Let \(h\in\Linfty(0,1)\) and \(R>0\). Then
\[
  \eta_m(h,R):=
  \sup_{\|q\|_{H^1(0,1)}\le R}\;
  \sup_{0\le x\le 1}
  \left|\int_0^x e^{im\om y}h(y)q(y)\,dy\right|
  \longrightarrow 0
\]
as \(|m|\to\infty\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The closed \(H^1\)-ball of radius \(R\) is compact in \(C[0,1]\). Given
\(\eps>0\), choose a finite \(C[0,1]\)-net \(q_1,\ldots,q_N\) for this ball
with mesh \(\eps/(3\|h\|_\infty+1)\). For each fixed \(q_j\), the function
\(h q_j\) lies in \(L^1(0,1)\), and the indefinite Riemann--Lebesgue lemma
gives
\[
  \sup_{0\le x\le 1}
  \left|\int_0^x e^{im\om y}h(y)q_j(y)\,dy\right|\to 0 .
\]
For completeness, this indefinite version follows by approximating
\(h q_j\) in \(L^1\) by a step function: step functions have uniformly
\(O(|m|^{-1})\) oscillatory primitives, and the \(L^1\)-error bounds the
primitive error uniformly in \(x\). Taking the maximum over the finite net
and then using the mesh bound proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[the \(BV\) estimate has an \(L^\infty\) replacement]
\label{lem:mode-decay}
Let \((u_k,v_k)=A^{-1}(f_k,g_k)\) be the modewise solution given by
\cite[(3.6)]{KmitRecke2007}, and set
\[
  E_k=\|f_k\|_{L^2(0,1)}+\|g_k\|_{L^2(0,1)}.
\]
If \(b,c\in\Linfty(0,1)\), then there is a sequence \(\mu_k\to 0\) as
\(|k|\to\infty\), depending only on the coefficients and boundary constants,
such that, for every \(x\in[0,1]\),
\[
\left|
\int_0^x e^{-ik\om y-\de(y)}c(y)u_k(y)\,dy
\right|
\le \mu_k E_k,
\]
\[
\left|
\int_0^x e^{ik\om y+\al(y)}b(y)v_k(y)\,dy
\right|
\le \mu_k E_k,
\]
and
\[
  |w_k(bv_k,cu_k)|\le \mu_k E_k,
\]
where \(w_k\) is the functional in \cite[(3.7)]{KmitRecke2007}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Define
\[
  q_k(x):=e^{ik\om x+\al(x)}u_k(x),\qquad
  s_k(x):=e^{-ik\om x-\de(x)}v_k(x).
\]
From the explicit diagonal formula \cite[(3.6)]{KmitRecke2007},
\[
  q'_k(x)=e^{ik\om x+\al(x)}f_k(x),\qquad
  s'_k(x)=e^{-ik\om x-\de(x)}g_k(x),
\]
and the initial constants \(q_k(0)\), \(s_k(0)\) are bounded by
\(C E_k\), using the uniform lower bound on \(|\Delta_k|\). Hence
\[
  \|q_k\|_{H^1(0,1)}+\|s_k\|_{H^1(0,1)}\le C E_k .
\]
If \(E_k=0\), the desired estimates are trivial. Otherwise apply
Lemma \ref{lem:uniform-rl} to the \(H^1\)-bounded functions \(q_k/E_k\) and
\(s_k/E_k\). Since
\[
  \int_0^x e^{-ik\om y-\de(y)}c(y)u_k(y)\,dy
  =
  \int_0^x e^{-2ik\om y}
  c(y)e^{-\al(y)-\de(y)}q_k(y)\,dy
\]
and
\[
  \int_0^x e^{ik\om y+\al(y)}b(y)v_k(y)\,dy
  =
  \int_0^x e^{2ik\om y}
  b(y)e^{\al(y)+\de(y)}s_k(y)\,dy,
\]
both displayed integrals are bounded by \(o_{|k|\to\infty}(1)E_k\),
uniformly in \(x\). The estimate for \(w_k(bv_k,cu_k)\) follows from the same
two global integrals at \(x=1\) and the bounded scalar factors in the
definition of \(w_k\).
\end{proof}

Now let \(N\subset W^\ga\) be bounded and put
\[
  M=(BA^{-1})^2N .
\]
As in Section 4 of \cite{KmitRecke2007}, it is enough to verify the
componentwise compactness criterion of \cite[Lemma 2]{KmitRecke2007}. Uniform
boundedness of \(M\) follows from boundedness of \(B\) on \(W^\ga\) and of
\(A^{-1}:W^\ga\to V^\ga(r_0,r_1)\), both valid under \(L^\infty\) coefficient
assumptions.

For the shift-continuity condition, repeat the proof of
\cite[Section 4]{KmitRecke2007}. The first shift term is estimated by
\[
  C\xi^2
\]
using only \(b,c\in\Linfty\) and the uniform mode estimate
\cite[(3.9)]{KmitRecke2007}. The third shift term tends to zero by \(L^2\)
continuity in the mean of \(x\mapsto b(x)e^{\de(x)}\), again requiring only
\(b\in\Linfty\). The identical statements with \(b\) and \(c\) interchanged
handle the \(v\)-component.

It remains only to replace the estimate of the middle term
\cite[(4.10)]{KmitRecke2007}. By Lemma \ref{lem:mode-decay} and
\(\inf_k|\Delta_k|>0\), the expression inside the last absolute value in
\cite[(4.10)]{KmitRecke2007} is bounded by \(\mu_kE_k\), with
\(\mu_k\to 0\). Therefore that middle contribution is at most
\[
  C\sum_{k\in\Z}(1+k^2)^\ga
  |e^{ik\om(\xi+\tau)}-1|^2\mu_k^2
  \left(\|f_k\|_{L^2(0,1)}^2+\|g_k\|_{L^2(0,1)}^2\right),
\]
where \(C\) is independent of \((f,g)\in N\), \(\xi\), and \(\tau\). Since
\(N\) is bounded in \(W^\ga\), given \(\eps>0\) choose \(K\) so large that
\(\sup_{|k|>K}\mu_k^2\) makes the tail \(<\eps/2\), using
\(|e^{i\theta}-1|\le 2\). The finite sum over \(|k|\le K\) tends to zero as
\(\xi,\tau\to0\), uniformly for \((f,g)\in N\), because there are only
finitely many factors \(e^{ik\om(\xi+\tau)}-1\) and the weighted mode norms
are uniformly bounded on \(N\). Thus the middle term also tends to zero
uniformly.

The compactness criterion gives that \((BA^{-1})^2\) is compact on \(W^\ga\).
Kmit--Recke's abstract Lemma 11, which states that \(I+C\) is Fredholm when
\(C^2\) is compact, applied to \(C=BA^{-1}\), shows that \(I+BA^{-1}\) is
Fredholm. Therefore \(A+B\) is Fredholm.

It remains to record that the index-zero and range-identification arguments in
Section 5 of \cite{KmitRecke2007} do not use derivatives of \(b\) or \(c\).
They use the adjoint identity for \(L^\infty\) coefficients, the Fredholmness
just proved, and the standard finite-dimensional equality between the number
of solutions of a modewise \(2\times2\) linear boundary-value ODE and the
number of solutions of its adjoint boundary-value problem. These arguments
therefore carry over verbatim to \(b,c\in\Linfty(0,1)\). Hence \(A+B\) has
index zero and the asserted Fredholm alternative range description follows.
\(\square\)

the source paper identifies the published version and DOI \cite{KmitRecke2007}.
Kmit's 2007 follow-up paper on the whole Sobolev scale treats \(L^\infty\)

packet is promoted as a new candidate proof, not as a literature-status
identification. Novelty confidence should still be treated as bounded rather
than exhaustive.
