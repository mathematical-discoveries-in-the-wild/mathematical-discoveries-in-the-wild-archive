\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Sauli Lindberg, \emph{A note on the Jacobian problem of
Coifman, Lions, Meyer and Semmes}, arXiv:2208.13576.  In Corollary 1.11 the
paper proves that, for the planar Jacobian quadratic operator
\[
  Q\omega=|\mathcal S\omega|^2-|\omega|^2:
  L^2(\C,\C)\to \mathcal H^1(\C),
\]
the associated dual unit ball is not strictly convex:
\[
  \ext(\B_{X_Q^*})\subsetneq \Sph_{X_Q^*}.
\]
Immediately afterward, the author writes that it is unclear whether the
analogous statement holds for the Gateaux derivative
\[
  (\omega,\gamma)\mapsto Q'_\omega\gamma:
  L^2(\C,\C)^2\to\mathcal H^1(\C).
\]

The source also proves the block self-adjoint modification identity:
\[
  \widetilde T_b(f,g)=(T_b^*g,T_bf),
  \qquad
  \|\widetilde T_b\|_{H\oplus_2 H\to H\oplus_2H}
  =
  \|T_b\|_{H\to H},
\]
and identifies this modification with the derivative:
\[
  \langle \widetilde T_b(f,g),(f,g)\rangle
  =
  \langle b,Q'_f g\rangle .
\]

\section*{Claimed Partial Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(Q:H\to X^*\) be a quadratic operator in the source paper's framework,
generated by \(T_b:H\to H\), and let \(\widetilde Q:H\oplus_2 H\to X^*\) be
the self-adjoint block modification
\[
  \langle b,\widetilde Q(f,g)\rangle
  =
  \langle \widetilde T_b(f,g),(f,g)\rangle,
  \qquad
  \widetilde T_b(f,g)=(T_b^*g,T_bf).
\]
Then the coefficient-space norm induced by \(\widetilde Q\) is identical to
the coefficient-space norm induced by \(Q\):
\[
  \|b\|_{X_{\widetilde Q}}=\|b\|_{X_Q}
  \qquad (b\in X).
\]
Consequently, \(X_{\widetilde Q}=X_Q\) isometrically by the identity map, and
the dual unit balls \(\B_{X_{\widetilde Q}^*}\) and \(\B_{X_Q^*}\) have the
same extreme points.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

By definition in the source framework,
\[
  \|b\|_{X_Q}
  =
  \sup_{\|\omega\|_H=1}\langle b,Q\omega\rangle
  =
  \|T_b\|_{H\to H}.
\]
For the modified operator, the source's self-adjoint block construction gives
\[
  \langle b,\widetilde Q(f,g)\rangle
  =
  \langle \widetilde T_b(f,g),(f,g)\rangle
\]
on \(H\oplus_2 H\), and \(\widetilde T_b\) is self-adjoint.  Therefore the
induced norm is its numerical radius, which equals its operator norm:
\[
  \|b\|_{X_{\widetilde Q}}
  =
  \sup_{\|(f,g)\|_{H\oplus_2H}=1}
  \langle \widetilde T_b(f,g),(f,g)\rangle
  =
  \|\widetilde T_b\|_{H\oplus_2 H\to H\oplus_2H}.
\]
The source lemma gives
\[
  \|\widetilde T_b\|_{H\oplus_2H\to H\oplus_2H}
  =
  \|T_b\|_{H\to H}.
\]
Combining the three displayed identities yields
\[
  \|b\|_{X_{\widetilde Q}}=\|T_b\|=\|b\|_{X_Q}.
\]
Thus the identity map is an isometry from \(X_Q\) onto \(X_{\widetilde Q}\).
The dual identity is also an isometry, so the dual unit balls are literally
the same convex body in \(X^*\).  Their extreme points and strict convexity
properties are therefore identical.

\section*{Derivative Consequence}

\begin{corollary}
For the planar Jacobian in arXiv:2208.13576, let \(\widetilde Q\) denote the
Gateaux derivative operator
\[
  \widetilde Q(\omega,\gamma)=Q'_\omega\gamma:
  L^2(\C,\C)^2\to\mathcal H^1(\C).
\]
Then
\[
  \ext(\B_{X_{\widetilde Q}^*})\subsetneq \Sph_{X_{\widetilde Q}^*}.
\]
In particular, the analogue of Corollary 1.11 for the Gateaux derivative
holds.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The theorem identifies \(X_{\widetilde Q}\) isometrically with \(X_Q\).  Hence
\[
  \ext(\B_{X_{\widetilde Q}^*})=\ext(\B_{X_Q^*}),
  \qquad
  \Sph_{X_{\widetilde Q}^*}=\Sph_{X_Q^*}.
\]
The source paper's Corollary 1.11 gives
\(\ext(\B_{X_Q^*})\subsetneq \Sph_{X_Q^*}\) for the planar Jacobian.  The same
proper inclusion follows for \(X_{\widetilde Q}^*\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This answers only the strict-convexity geometry remark for the Gateaux
derivative.  It does not prove \(Q(\A)=\Sph_{X_Q^*}\), does not solve
Question 1.6, and does not settle the CLMS/Iwaniec Jacobian surjectivity
problem.

Cheap duplicate checks were run against the run registry, solution, attempt,
and proof-gap indexes for arXiv:2208.13576 and for the core phrases

and ``\(X_Q\)''.  No existing packet for this observation was found.  Web
exact-phrase checks found the source paper but no direct later answer to this
derivative strict-convexity remark.
