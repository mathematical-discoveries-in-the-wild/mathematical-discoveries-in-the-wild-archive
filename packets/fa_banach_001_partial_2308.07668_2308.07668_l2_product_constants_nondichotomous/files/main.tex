\section*{Status}

This is a partial solution packet for arXiv:2308.07668, ``On Ramsey-type properties of the distance in nonseparable spheres'' by Piotr Koszmider.  It resolves the case \(p=2\) of the question, asked after Proposition 55, concerning the exact values of the constants \(c_n\), \(c\), and whether the unit sphere of \(L_p(\{0,1\}^{\kappaCard})\) is dichotomous.

\section*{Definitions and Source Notation}

Let \(\mu\) be the product probability measure on \(\{0,1\}^{\kappaCard}\), where \(\kappaCard\leq 2^\omega\) is uncountable.  In Proposition 55 of the source paper, for \(A\in[\kappaCard]^n\), the map \(T_A\) sends a function \(F\in L_p(\{0,1\}^n)\) to the function on \(\{0,1\}^{\kappaCard}\) depending on the coordinates in \(A\) through the increasing enumeration of \(A\).  The constant \(c_n\) is the supremum of
\[
 \|T_A(F)-T_B(F)\|_{L_p}
\]
over all unit vectors \(F\in L_p(\{0,1\}^n)\) and pairs \(A,B\in[\kappaCard]^n\) whose common coordinates occur in the same positions.  Finally \(c=\sup_n c_n\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

For \(p=2\), the expression defining \(c_n\) is a Hilbert-space variance computation.  Two copies \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) share precisely the coordinates in \(A\cap B\).  Conditional on the shared coordinates, the two remaining parts are independent copies of the same conditional random variable.  Therefore their covariance is the square of a conditional expectation, hence nonnegative.  The squared distance is consequently at most \(2\|F\|_2^2\).  Disjoint \(A\) and \(B\), together with a mean-zero norm-one \(F\), attain equality.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:l2constant}
For every \(n\geq 1\), the constant \(c_n\) from Proposition 55 of the source paper satisfies \(c_n=\sqrt 2\) when \(p=2\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(n\geq 1\), \(F\in L_2(\{0,1\}^n)\) with \(\|F\|_2=1\), and admissible \(A,B\in[\kappaCard]^n\).  Let \(I\subseteq\{1,\ldots,n\}\) be the set of positions corresponding to \(A\cap B\).  Equivalently, the random variables \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) have the joint distribution of \(F(X)\) and \(F(Y)\), where \(X,Y\in\{0,1\}^n\) are uniform, \(X_i=Y_i\) for \(i\in I\), and all non-shared coordinates are conditionally independent.

Let \(\mathcal G\) be the sigma-algebra generated by the shared coordinates.  Conditional on \(\mathcal G\), \(F(X)\) and \(F(Y)\) are independent copies with the same conditional mean \(\mathbb E(F(X)\mid\mathcal G)\).  Hence
\[
 \mathbb E(F(X)F(Y)\mid\mathcal G)
 = \mathbb E(F(X)\mid\mathcal G)\mathbb E(F(Y)\mid\mathcal G)
 = \mathbb E(F(X)\mid\mathcal G)^2.
\]
Thus
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \|T_A(F)-T_B(F)\|_2^2
 &= \mathbb E|F(X)-F(Y)|^2 \\
 &= 2\mathbb E|F(X)|^2 - 2\mathbb E(F(X)F(Y)) \\
 &= 2 - 2\mathbb E\bigl(\mathbb E(F(X)\mid\mathcal G)^2\bigr) \\
 &\leq 2.
\end{aligned}
\]
This proves \(c_n\leq \sqrt2\).

For the reverse inequality, choose disjoint \(A,B\in[\kappaCard]^n\), possible because \(\kappaCard\) is infinite and \(n\) is finite.  Let \(F(x)=1\) if \(x_1=1\) and \(F(x)=-1\) if \(x_1=0\).  Then \(\|F\|_2=1\) and \(\mathbb E F=0\).  Since \(A\cap B=\varnothing\), \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) are independent mean-zero norm-one random variables, so
\[
 \|T_A(F)-T_B(F)\|_2^2
 = \|T_A(F)\|_2^2+\|T_B(F)\|_2^2
   -2\mathbb E(T_A(F)T_B(F))
 =2.
\]
Therefore \(c_n\geq\sqrt2\), and equality follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:l2subcase}
For \(p=2\) in Proposition 55 of the source paper,
\[
 c_n=\sqrt2\quad(n\geq1),\qquad c=\sqrt2.
\]
Moreover, for uncountable \(\kappaCard\leq 2^\omega\), the unit sphere of \(L_2(\{0,1\}^{\kappaCard},\mu)\) is almost dichotomous but not dichotomous.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The formula for \(c_n\) is Lemma \ref{lem:l2constant}, and \(c=\sup_n c_n=\sqrt2\).

It remains to record the dichotomy status in the same subcase.  The Walsh characters indexed by finite subsets of \(\kappaCard\) form an orthonormal basis of \(L_2(\{0,1\}^{\kappaCard},\mu)\).  Since \(|[\kappaCard]^{<\omega}|=\kappaCard\), this Hilbert space is linearly isometric to \(\ell_2(\kappaCard)\).  Proposition 54 of the source paper, applied with \(p=2\), states that
\[
 \Kplus(S_{\ell_2(\kappaCard)})=[0,\sqrt2),
 \qquad
 \SigmaSet(S_{\ell_2(\kappaCard)})=(\sqrt2,2].
\]
Consequently \(S_{L_2(\mu)}\) is almost dichotomous and not dichotomous.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof of \(c_n\leq\sqrt2\) is a direct conditional-expectation identity.  Equality is attained by disjoint coordinate sets and a Rademacher function on one coordinate.  The auxiliary script in the packet's \texttt{code/} directory builds the finite quadratic forms for \(1\leq n\leq5\) and confirms that the largest squared distance is \(2\) for every overlap pattern; this is only a finite sanity check and is not used as evidence in place of the proof.

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This packet does not determine \(c_n\), \(c\), or dichotomy for \(p\neq2\).  The non-dichotomy statement for \(p=2\) is obtained by identifying \(L_2(\mu)\) with \(\ell_2(\kappaCard)\) and invoking the source paper's earlier Hilbert-space result.  The packet's direct computation is the exact value of the product-measure constants \(c_n\) in the Hilbertian case.
