\section*{Open question in the source}

On page 3 of arXiv:0810.5705, Pisier states the non-commutative
Khintchine inequality
\[
 |||x|||_q \le \beta_q
\left(\int\left\|\sum_k r_k(t)x_k\right\|_q^q\,dt\right)^{1/q},
\tag{0.1}
\]
where, for \(1\le q\le 2\),
\[
|||x|||_q =
\inf_{x_k=a_k+b_k}
\left\{
\left\|\left(\sum a_k^*a_k\right)^{1/2}\right\|_q+
\left\|\left(\sum b_kb_k^*\right)^{1/2}\right\|_q
\right\}.
\]
The paper proves an extrapolation result down to \(p\ge 1\), but then says
that the argument was not available for \(0<p<1\), because Step 3 was missing,
and explicitly leaves open the validity of \((0.1)\) for \(0<q<1\).

The same obstruction is identified later in the source after the proof of
Theorem 1.1. Pisier uses Junge--Parcet to justify estimate \((1.6)\) when
\(p\ge 1\), but says that it is not known whether \((1.6)\) still holds for
\(0<p<1\).

\section*{Supporting answer}

The abstract of arXiv:1412.0222 states that the paper gives a proof of the
Khintchine inequalities in non-commutative \(L_p\)-spaces for every
\(0<p<1\). Its introduction explicitly refers to the earlier paper
arXiv:0810.5705 as reference [25], says that the \(q<1\) case remained open
there, and states that the goal of the paper is to give an explicit estimate
and proof for \(q<1\).

The decisive formal statement is Corollary 2.2 of arXiv:1412.0222:
\[
K_q \Rightarrow K_p \quad \text{remains valid for every } 0<p<1.
\]
The same corollary immediately adds that, in particular, the non-commutative
Khintchine inequality holds for every \(0<q<1\).

\begin{claim}
The main open \(0<q<1\) case of the non-commutative Khintchine inequality
\((0.1)\) in arXiv:0810.5705 is already answered by arXiv:1412.0222.
\end{claim}

\begin{proof}
The identification is direct. The source asks for the validity of the
non-commutative Khintchine inequality \((0.1)\) below exponent \(1\), and
locates the missing step in the non-commutative Holder-type estimate needed
for extrapolation. The supporting paper explicitly cites the source, says
that exactly the \(q<1\) case remained open there, and then proves the
required extrapolation for \(0<p<1\). Corollary 2.2 states the resulting
validity of the non-commutative Khintchine inequality for all \(0<q<1\).

Therefore the extracted source question has a separate later literature
answer.
\end{proof}

\section*{Classification and scope}

This packet belongs in
\texttt{solutions/literature\_already\_answered/}. The supporting paper knows
it is answering the missing \(q<1\) case from the source paper and cites that
source. This is a retrieval/status result, not a new proof by the run.

The recorded answer is limited to the main non-commutative Khintchine
inequality \((0.1)\) for \(0<q<1\), in the Rademacher setting and the
generalized settings covered by the later theorem. This packet does not claim
to settle the source paper's distinct questions about best possible sizes of
operator subspaces, \(\sigma(q)\)-sets versus \(\sigma(q)_{cb}\)-sets, or
other side questions.
