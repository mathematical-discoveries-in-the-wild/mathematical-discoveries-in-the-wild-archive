\section*{Source Question}

In the Introduction of arXiv:1003.4261, Naor recalls the
Cheeger--Kleiner--Naor lower bound that embeddings of the
$n\times n\times n$ grid in the discrete 3-dimensional Heisenberg group into
$L_1$ incur distortion at least $(\log n)^c$ for some universal $c>0$. He then
states that evaluating the supremum of such exponents remains an important
open question, with $1/2$ being the largest possible value allowed by the known
$\sqrt{\log n}$ upper bound.

\section*{Answer}

Naor--Young, arXiv:2004.12522, \emph{Foliated corona decompositions}, explicitly
identifies the asymptotic evaluation of
$c_{\ell_1}(\mathcal B_n)$ as a question asked in several earlier sources,
including Naor's ICM article \cite{Nao10}. Their Theorem 1.6 proves the sharp
asymptotic
\[
        c_{\ell_1}(\mathcal B_n)\asymp \sqrt[4]{\log n}
        \qquad (n\ge 2),
\]
where $\mathcal B_n$ is the word ball in the discrete 3-dimensional Heisenberg
group. Hence the exponent asked for in arXiv:1003.4261 is exactly $1/4$.

\section*{Scope}

This is a literature-status answer for the Heisenberg distortion exponent
question only. The later question in arXiv:1003.4261 about invariant metrics on
cyclic groups and the dependence on the Abelian group exponent is separate and
is not settled by this note.
