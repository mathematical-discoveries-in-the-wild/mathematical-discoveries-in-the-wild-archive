\section*{Status}

This packet gives a counterexample to Question 6.1 of Borodulin-Nadzieja,
Farkas, Jachimek, and Pelczar-Barwacz, arXiv:2404.01733.  The question asks
whether, for every hereditary finite cover $\mathcal F$,
\[
  \overline{\operatorname{span}}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))=X_{\mathcal F}^*
  \quad\text{or even}\quad
  \overline{\operatorname{conv}}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))=B(X_{\mathcal F}^*)
\]
in norm.

\section*{Source Question}

The source defines
\[
  W(\mathcal H)=\{\sigma\in\{\pm1,0\}^{\mathbb N}:
  \operatorname{supp}(\sigma)\in\mathcal H\}
  \subseteq B(X_{\mathcal F}^*)
\]
for $\mathcal H\subseteq\overline{\mathcal F}$ and records that
$W(\mathcal H)$ is weak-star norming when $\mathcal F\subseteq\mathcal H$.

hulls already give the whole dual or its unit ball.

\section*{Counterexample}

Let $\Omega$ be the disjoint union of finite blocks $I_k$ with
$m_k=|I_k|\to\infty$.  Define
\[
  \mathcal F=\{E\in[\Omega]^{<\infty}: |E\cap I_k|\leq 1
  \text{ for every }k\}.
\]
After identifying $\Omega$ with $\mathbb N$, this is an element of FHC: it is
hereditary and covers $\Omega$.

\begin{theorem}
For this $\mathcal F$,
\[
  \overline{\operatorname{span}}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))
  \ne X_{\mathcal F}^*.
\]
Consequently,
\[
  \overline{\operatorname{conv}}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))
  \ne B(X_{\mathcal F}^*).
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The family $\mathcal F$ allows only one point per block.  Thus a selector-sign
functional in $W(\overline{\mathcal F})$ has at most one nonzero coordinate in
each block.  A finite linear combination of $r$ such functionals has at most
$r$ nonzero coordinates in each block.  The dual unit ball nevertheless
contains vectors that spread mass uniformly over every block.  Since the block
sizes tend to infinity, no uniformly bounded number of atoms can approximate
these uniform block measures in $\ell_1$ on every block at once.

\section*{Proof}

For $x\in c_{00}(\Omega)$,
\[
  \|x\|_{\mathcal F}
  =\sup_{E\in\mathcal F}\sum_{\omega\in E}|x(\omega)|
  =\sum_{k=1}^\infty \|x|_{I_k}\|_\infty.
\]
Indeed, a finite selector may choose one coordinate of maximum absolute value
from each nonzero block of $x$, and no selector can do better inside a block.
Therefore
\[
  X_{\mathcal F}
  =
  \left(\bigoplus_{k=1}^\infty \ell_\infty^{m_k}\right)_{\ell_1}.
\]
Since the summands are finite-dimensional,
\[
  X_{\mathcal F}^*
  =
  \left(\bigoplus_{k=1}^\infty \ell_1^{m_k}\right)_{\ell_\infty},
  \qquad
  \|\alpha\|=\sup_k \|\alpha|_{I_k}\|_1.
\]

The closure $\overline{\mathcal F}$ consists exactly of all selectors
$A\subseteq\Omega$ with $|A\cap I_k|\leq1$ for every $k$, finite or infinite.
Hence every $\sigma\in W(\overline{\mathcal F})$ has at most one nonzero
coordinate in each block.

Define $u\in X_{\mathcal F}^*$ by
\[
  u(\omega)=\frac{1}{m_k}\quad(\omega\in I_k).
\]
Then
\[
  \|u\|=\sup_k \sum_{\omega\in I_k}\frac1{m_k}=1,
\]
so $u\in B(X_{\mathcal F}^*)$.

Let $y=\sum_{j=1}^r a_j\sigma_j$ be any finite linear combination of elements
of $W(\overline{\mathcal F})$.  For every block $I_k$, the support of
$y|_{I_k}$ has cardinality at most $r$, because each $\sigma_j$ contributes to
at most one coordinate in that block.  Choose $k$ with $m_k>2r$.  Then
\[
  \|u|_{I_k}-y|_{I_k}\|_1
  \geq
  \sum_{\omega\in I_k\setminus \operatorname{supp}(y|_{I_k})}
  \frac1{m_k}
  \geq \frac{m_k-r}{m_k}
  >\frac12.
\]
Thus
\[
  \|u-y\|_{X_{\mathcal F}^*}
  =\sup_k\|u|_{I_k}-y|_{I_k}\|_1
  >\frac12.
\]
This holds for every algebraic finite linear combination $y$, so $u$ is not in
the norm closure of $\operatorname{span}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))$.

Since
\[
  \operatorname{conv}(W(\overline{\mathcal F}))
  \subseteq
  \operatorname{span}(W(\overline{\mathcal F})),
\]
the same $u$ is not in the norm-closed convex hull.  This proves both negative
assertions.

\section*{Verification Notes}

The argument uses only the definitions from the source paper and standard
duality for $\ell_1$ sums.  The obstruction is uniform in the number of terms
of a proposed approximation: each finite combination fixes a finite support
bound per block, and a larger block defeats that approximation by at least
$1/2$ in the dual norm.

\section*{Limitations}

This counterexample refutes the general assertions in Question 6.1.  It does
not address whether compact hereditary families, or other restricted subclasses
of combinatorial families, satisfy the norm-closed span or convex-hull
property.  It also does not independently settle the lambda-property for
arbitrary $X_{\mathcal F}^*$.
