\section*{Source question}
Chung and Du ask on page 56 of \emph{Ideal structure of $\ell^p$ uniform
Roe algebras} whether a uniformly locally finite coarse space $(X,\E)$
without property A must admit a non-trivial ghost in $B_u^p(X)$ for some
$p\in\{0\}\cup[1,\infty]$ \cite{ChungDu}.  Section 6 has already imposed
unitality, and their definition of a coarse structure includes every finite
subset of $X\times X$.

\section*{Identification and proof idea}
Property A is quantified one entourage at a time.  If it fails, one
entourage $E_0$ already witnesses the failure.  Retain only the coarse
structure generated by $E_0$, the diagonal, and the mandatory finite
entourages.  This smaller structure is countably generated and still fails
property A.  A direct path-metric construction turns it into a uniformly
discrete bounded-geometry metric space without changing its coarse
structure.  Roe--Willett then produce a non-compact Hilbert-space ghost.
The smaller uniform Roe algebra embeds in the original one, while ghostness
and non-compactness do not depend on which of the two coarse structures is
being viewed.

\section*{A metric core for one entourage}
\begin{lemma}[single-entourage metric core]\label{lem:core}
Let $(X,\E)$ be a countable unital uniformly locally finite coarse space.  If
$(X,\E)$ does not have property A, there is a sub-coarse structure
$\J\subseteq\E$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $(X,\J)$ is unital, connected, countably generated, and uniformly
locally finite;
\item $(X,\J)$ does not have property A;
\item there is a uniformly discrete bounded-geometry metric $d$ on $X$ whose
bounded coarse structure is exactly $\J$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Negating the definition of property A gives an entourage $E_0\in\E$ and a
number $\varepsilon_0>0$ for which no controlled support entourage in $\E$
provides the required property-A witness.  Let $\J$ be the coarse structure
generated by $E_0$ and $\Delta_X$.  It is a substructure of $\E$, is unital,
and is connected because, under the conventions of \cite{ChungDu}, every
finite subset of $X\times X$ belongs to every coarse structure.  It inherits
uniform local finiteness from $\E$.  It still fails property A at
$(E_0,\varepsilon_0)$: a witness supported in an entourage of $\J$ would also
be a witness supported in an entourage of $\E$.

Because $X\times X$ is countable, the mandatory finite entourages form a
countable family.  Taking all finite coarse operations on that family,
$E_0$, and $\Delta_X$ gives a countable cofinal family in $\J$.  Choose an
increasing symmetric cofinal sequence $(F_n)_{n\geq1}$ in $\J$, containing
$\Delta_X$, whose union is $X\times X$.  For $x\ne y$ put
\[
 w(x,y)=\min\{n:(x,y)\in F_n\},
\]
and set $w(x,x)=0$.  Define $d(x,y)$ as the infimum of the sums of the edge
weights along all finite paths from $x$ to $y$.  The weights off the diagonal
are positive integers, so the infimum is attained, $d$ is a metric, and
$d(x,y)\geq1$ for $x\ne y$.

For each $n$, $F_n\subseteq\{d\leq n\}$.  Conversely, if $d(x,y)\leq R$ and
$N=\lceil R\rceil$, a minimizing path uses at most $N$ positive-weight edges,
each lying in $F_N$; hence
\[
 \{(x,y):d(x,y)\leq R\}\subseteq F_N^{\circ N}\in\J.
\]
Cofinality of $(F_n)$ now shows that the bounded coarse structure of $d$ is
exactly $\J$.  Finally, uniform local finiteness of $\J$ uniformly bounds the
cardinality of every $d$-ball of fixed radius, so $d$ has bounded geometry.
\end{proof}

\section*{Full answer}
\begin{theorem}\label{thm:answer}
Let $(X,\E)$ be a countable unital uniformly locally finite coarse space.  If
$(X,\E)$ does not have property A, then $B_u^2(X,\E)$ contains a non-trivial
ghost.  Equivalently, it contains a ghost operator that is not compact on
$\ell^2(X)$.  Thus the source existence question is affirmative, always with
$p=2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:core} and write $\J=\E_d$.  Since property A depends only
on the coarse structure, the bounded-geometry metric space $(X,d)$ does not
have property A.  Roe and Willett's Theorem~1.3 therefore supplies a
non-compact ghost
\[
 T\in C_u^*(X,d)=B_u^2(X,\J) \cite{RoeWillett}.
\]
Because $\J\subseteq\E$, every finite-$\J$-propagation operator has
finite $\E$-propagation.  Taking closures in the same operator norm on
$\mathcal B(\ell^2(X))$ gives
\[
 B_u^2(X,\J)\subseteq B_u^2(X,\E).
\]
The assertion that the matrix coefficients of $T$ vanish at infinity is
independent of the coarse structure, and compactness is intrinsic to
$\mathcal B(\ell^2(X))$.  Hence the same $T$, viewed in $B_u^2(X,\E)$, is a
non-compact---therefore non-trivial---ghost.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope, provenance, and verification}
The result answers the source's existential question for every space in its
Section~6 setting.  It does \emph{not} show the corresponding assertion for
each fixed $p\in(1,\infty)\setminus\{2\}$; those exponent-specific cases are
outside the claim.

This packet is classified as a literature-implied answer rather than as a
new full solution.  Roe--Willett prove the decisive ghost-existence theorem
for metric spaces, while Lemma~\ref{lem:core} identifies a metric sub-coarse
structure on which their theorem applies.  Their paper predates the source
question and therefore does not explicitly claim this general-coarse
consequence.  The source paper itself cites Roe--Willett only for the metric
special case.

Checks performed:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item The source question was verified in arXiv:2606.11586v1, p.~56, and
Roe--Willett Theorem~1.3 in arXiv:1303.6532v1, p.~2.
\item The substructure argument uses the same property-A definition and the
same matrix definition of a ghost as the source.
\item The metric construction proves equality of coarse structures, avoiding
any invariance issue under a merely abstract coarse equivalence.
\item No computational or numerical dependency is present.
\item On 22 July 2026, the run's cheap indexes and bounded arXiv searches for
the exact source id and combinations of ``uniformly locally finite coarse
space'', ``property A'', ``noncompact ghost'', ``countably generated'', and
``metric'' found no explicit publication of this general reduction.
\end{itemize}
