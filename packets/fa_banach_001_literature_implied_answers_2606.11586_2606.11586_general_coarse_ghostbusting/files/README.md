# General coarse ghostbusting from a one-entourage metric core

Status: `literature_implied_answer (full scope), likely valid, needs human review`

Source question: Yeong Chyuan Chung and Xinhui Du, *Ideal structure of
$\ell^p$ uniform Roe algebras*, arXiv:2606.11586v1, page 56.  The paper asks
whether every unital uniformly locally finite coarse space without property A
has a non-trivial ghost in $B_u^p(X)$ for some
$p\in\{0\}\cup[1,\infty]$.

Answer: yes, always with $p=2$.  Failure of property A is witnessed by one
entourage.  The sub-coarse structure generated by that entourage and the
unital/minimal entourages is connected, countably generated, uniformly locally
finite, and still fails property A.  It is therefore the bounded coarse
structure of a uniformly discrete bounded-geometry metric.  Roe--Willett's
Theorem 1.3 supplies a non-compact ghost for this metric core, and inclusion of
the smaller uniform Roe algebra into the original one preserves both the
matrix ghost condition and non-compactness.

This is filed as a literature-implied answer rather than a new full-solution
claim because the decisive existence theorem is Roe--Willett's known metric
theorem (arXiv:1303.6532).  The full general-coarse conclusion comes from the
agent-identified single-entourage reduction; the supporting authors could not
have identified the 2026 question explicitly.
