\section{Source question}

Kilian, Maschke, Mironchenko, and Wirth study a non-autonomous
port-Hamiltonian family associated with two systems separated by a moving
interface \cite{KMMW}.  In the abstract, and again in Section VI, they state
that the general conditions under which the family generates an evolution
family remain open.  Their assumptions require the path
$l\colon[0,\tau]\to(a,b)$ to be continuously differentiable, but place no
bound on the interface speed.

The relevant source statement is reproduced below from PDF page 1.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.97\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source proves Kato stability under assumptions (A1)--(A2).  The result
below shows that these assumptions cannot by themselves yield a classical
evolution family on the natural operator domains.  A subcharacteristic speed
condition is already necessary in the constant unit medium.

\section{Definitions and normalization}

Let $X=L^2([-2,2],\R^2)$, let
\[
 P_1=\begin{bmatrix}0&-1\\-1&0\end{bmatrix},
 \qquad \mathcal Q^- =\mathcal Q^+=I_2,
\]
and take the moving interface $l(t)=2t$ for $0\le t\le\tau$, with
$0<\tau<1/6$.  Away from $z=l(t)$ the source equation becomes
\begin{equation}
 x_t=P_1x_z.
\end{equation}
Set $r=0$ in the source interface relation.  Since its interface flow is the
common trace of the second component, every classical solution must satisfy
\begin{equation}
 x_2(t,l(t)^-)=x_2(t,l(t)^+)=0.
\end{equation}

At the outer endpoints impose $x_2(t,-2)=x_2(t,2)=0$.  In the source's
boundary variables this is obtained from
\[
 W_B=\begin{bmatrix}1&0&0&0\\0&0&0&1\end{bmatrix},
 \qquad
 W_B\begin{bmatrix}f_\partial\\e_\partial\end{bmatrix}=0.
\]
Indeed, the two selected equations are $f_{\partial,1}=0$ and
$e_{\partial,2}=0$, which are equivalent to the two endpoint conditions.
For
\[
 \Sigma=\begin{bmatrix}0&I_2\\I_2&0\end{bmatrix}
\]
we have $\operatorname{rank}W_B=2$ and
$W_B\Sigma W_B^{\mathsf T}=0$.  Thus (A2) holds.  The unit Hamiltonian
densities satisfy (A1).  Consequently the source's Kato-stability corollary
applies to this family.

By a \emph{classical evolution family on the natural domains} we mean an
evolution family $U(t,s)$ for which every $x_s\in D(A(s))$ gives, at least on
a right neighborhood of $s$, a classical solution
$x(t)=U(t,s)x_s$ of $\dot x(t)=A(t)x(t)$ satisfying the source trace
conditions.  This is the notion ruled out below.

\section{Proof intuition}

The matrix $P_1$ has characteristic speeds $+1$ and $-1$.  The interface
moves to the right with speed $2$, so it overtakes both characteristic
families on its right.  Therefore both traces at the right side of the
interface are determined by the initial datum; the interface cannot prescribe
a relation between them.  We choose a smooth datum for which those two
outgoing traces immediately differ.  The source condition nevertheless
requires them to be equal, giving an exact contradiction.

\section{The counterexample}

\begin{theorem}[Supersonic obstruction]
For the family just defined, every frozen operator satisfies the source
hypotheses (A1)--(A2), and the family is Kato-stable by the source result.
Nevertheless, there exists $x_0\in C_c^\infty((-2,2),\R^2)\cap D(A(0))$
for which
\[
 \dot x(t)=A(t)x(t),\qquad x(0)=x_0,
\]
has no classical solution on any interval $[0,\varepsilon]$ with
$\varepsilon>0$.  Hence the family cannot generate a classical evolution
family on its natural domains.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose $\eta\in C_c^\infty((-1,1))$ with $\eta=1$ on
$[-1/2,1/2]$, and put
\begin{equation}
 x_0(z)=\frac{z\eta(z)}2\binom{1}{1}.
\end{equation}
This datum is smooth and compactly supported, satisfies both outer boundary
conditions, and has $x_{0,2}(0)=0$.  It therefore belongs to $D(A(0))$.

Introduce the characteristic variables
\[
 u=x_1+x_2,\qquad v=x_1-x_2.
\]
Equation (1) diagonalizes to
\begin{equation}
 u_t+u_z=0,\qquad v_t-v_z=0.
\end{equation}
For the datum (3),
\begin{equation}
 u_0(z)=z\eta(z),\qquad v_0(z)=0.
\end{equation}

Assume for contradiction that a classical solution exists.  Fix sufficiently
small $t>0$ and consider the right trace at $(t,l(t))=(t,2t)$.  The backward
$u$-characteristic through this point is
\[
 z_u(s)=2t-(t-s)=t+s,
\]
and for $0\le s<t$ it satisfies
\[
 z_u(s)-l(s)=t-s>0.
\]
Thus it stays strictly in the right subsystem and reaches time zero at
$z_u(0)=t$.  Similarly, the backward $v$-characteristic is
\[
 z_v(s)=2t+(t-s)=3t-s,
\]
with
\[
 z_v(s)-l(s)=3(t-s)>0,
\]
and it reaches time zero at $z_v(0)=3t$.  Neither trace can be affected by
the moving interface or by an outer boundary.  The standard characteristic
identity for (4), valid also for the $H^1$ traces of a classical solution,
therefore gives
\begin{equation}
 u(t,2t^+)=u_0(t),\qquad v(t,2t^+)=v_0(3t).
\end{equation}
For $0<t<1/6$, both $t$ and $3t$ lie in $(-1/2,1/2)$, so (5)--(6) yield
\[
 u(t,2t^+)=t,\qquad v(t,2t^+)=0.
\]
Consequently
\[
 x_2(t,2t^+)=\frac{u(t,2t^+)-v(t,2t^+)}2=\frac t2\ne0,
\]
contradicting the source interface condition (2).  No classical solution
exists for this natural-domain datum.
\end{proof}

\section{What the theorem establishes}

The example meets the source's structural and boundary hypotheses exactly;
in particular it is not the non-Kato-stable example already given in the
source.  It proves that Kato stability of the frozen family does not prevent a
moving interface from overdetermining outgoing characteristic traces.  In the
normalized unit medium, an all-data classical generation theorem must at
least exclude rightward speeds $l'>1$.  Reflecting the construction excludes
leftward speeds $l'<-1$ as well.

This packet does not prove sufficiency when $|l'|<1$, and it does not rule out
weaker evolution notions that do not furnish classical solutions for every
datum in $D(A(s))$.
