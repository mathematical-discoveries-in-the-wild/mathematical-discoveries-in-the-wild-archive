\section*{Source and target}

The source paper is Fouad Naderi,
\emph{C*-algebraic approach to fixed point theory generalizes Baggett's theorem
to groups with discrete reduced duals}, arXiv:1612.08286, later published in
J. Math. Anal. Appl. 461 (2018), 451--460 \cite{Naderi1612}.
Theorem 3.1 of the source proves:

\begin{quote}
If the reduced Fourier--Stieltjes algebra \(\Br(G)=\Cr(G)^*\) of a second
countable locally compact group \(G\) has the \weakstar{} fixed point property,
then \(G\) is compact.
\end{quote}

On page 9, the paper asks whether the second countability hypothesis can be
removed:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.94\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Question 5.} Can one prove Theorem 3.1 for weak* fpp without using the
second countability assumption on \(G\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(G\) be an arbitrary locally compact group. If
\(\Br(G)=\Cr(G)^*\), with its canonical \(\Cr(G)\)-weak\(^*\) topology, has the
weak\(^*\) fixed point property for nonexpansive mappings, then \(G\) is
compact.
\end{theorem}

Together with Naderi's compact-group converse \cite[Proposition 3.3]{Naderi1612},
this gives the countability-free equivalence:
\[
        G \text{ compact}
        \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
        \Br(G) \text{ has weak}^{*}\text{ fpp}.
\]
In fact the compact direction gives the stronger weak\(^*\) fpp for left
reversible semigroups.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The second countability in Naderi's proof is used to make \(\Cr(G)\) separable
and then invoke the separable C*-algebra machinery.  Instead of trying to make
the whole group separable, pass to a separable quotient where noncompactness is
still visible.

Every noncompact locally compact group has a noncompact \(\sigma\)-compact open
subgroup \(H\).  By the Kakutani--Kodaira theorem, \(H\) has a compact normal
subgroup \(N\) such that \(H/N\) is second countable; since \(N\) is compact and
\(H\) is noncompact, \(H/N\) is still noncompact.  The weak\(^*\) fixed point
property of \(\Br(G)\) passes first to \(\Br(H)\) by the open-subgroup
compression \(\Cr(G)\to\Cr(H)\), and then to \(\Br(H/N)\) by the quotient map
\(\Cr(H)\to\Cr(H/N)\).  Naderi's second-countable theorem applied to \(H/N\)
then forces \(H/N\) compact, a contradiction.

\section*{Proof}

We first record two elementary permanence facts.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:dual-subspace}
Let \(A\) and \(B\) be Banach spaces.  Suppose
\(J:B^*\to A^*\) is a weak\(^*\)-to-weak\(^*\) continuous linear isometry.  If
\(A^*\) has the weak\(^*\) fixed point property, then \(B^*\) has the weak\(^*\)
fixed point property.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(K\subset B^*\) be nonempty, convex, and weak\(^*\) compact, and let
\(T:K\to K\) be nonexpansive.  Then \(J(K)\) is a nonempty convex weak\(^*\)
compact subset of \(A^*\).  Define \(\widetilde T:J(K)\to J(K)\) by
\(\widetilde T(Jx)=J(Tx)\).  Since \(J\) is an isometry, \(\widetilde T\) is
nonexpansive.  A fixed point \(Jx\) of \(\widetilde T\) gives \(Tx=x\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:permanence}
Let \(G\) be a locally compact group.

\begin{enumerate}
\item If \(H\) is an open subgroup of \(G\), then weak\(^*\) fpp of \(\Br(G)\)
implies weak\(^*\) fpp of \(\Br(H)\).
\item If \(N\) is a compact normal subgroup of \(G\), then weak\(^*\) fpp of
\(\Br(G)\) implies weak\(^*\) fpp of \(\Br(G/N)\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For (1), identify \(L^2(H)\) with the subspace of \(L^2(G)\) consisting of
functions supported on \(H\), and let \(P\) be the orthogonal projection from
\(L^2(G)\) onto this subspace.  For \(f\in C_c(G)\), compression gives
\[
        P\lambda_G(f)|_{L^2(H)}=\lambda_H(f|_H).
\]
Hence compression extends by continuity to a contractive projection
\[
        E_H:\Cr(G)\longrightarrow \Cr(H)
\]
which is the identity on the canonical copy of \(\Cr(H)\).  Therefore
\(E_H^*:\Br(H)\to\Br(G)\) is a weak\(^*\)-continuous isometry.  Lemma
\ref{lem:dual-subspace} applies.

For (2), write \(q:G\to G/N\) for the quotient map and normalize Haar measure
on the compact group \(N\).  The averaging map
\[
        Q:C_c(G)\to C_c(G/N),\qquad
        Qf(gN)=\int_N f(gn)\,dn
\]
is a convolution \(*\)-homomorphism.  It is compatible with the left regular
representations: \(\lambda_{G/N}(Qf)\) is obtained by compressing
\(\lambda_G(f)\) to the \(N\)-invariant subspace of \(L^2(G)\), identified with
\(L^2(G/N)\).  Thus \(\|\lambda_{G/N}(Qf)\|\le \|\lambda_G(f)\|\), and \(Q\)
extends to a surjective \(*\)-homomorphism
\[
        \Cr(G)\longrightarrow \Cr(G/N).
\]
Surjectivity follows already on \(C_c\): if \(\varphi\in C_c(G/N)\), then
\(\varphi\circ q\in C_c(G)\) and \(Q(\varphi\circ q)=\varphi\).  The adjoint is
a weak\(^*\)-continuous isometry \(\Br(G/N)\to\Br(G)\), so Lemma
\ref{lem:dual-subspace} applies again.
\end{proof}

We also need the following standard topological reduction.

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:topological-reduction}
Every noncompact locally compact group \(G\) has a noncompact second countable
locally compact quotient of an open subgroup.  More precisely, there are an
open subgroup \(H\leq G\) and a compact normal subgroup \(N\triangleleft H\)
such that \(H\) is noncompact and \(\sigma\)-compact, while \(H/N\) is
second countable and noncompact.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose a symmetric relatively compact open neighbourhood \(U\) of the identity,
and let \(H_0\) be the subgroup generated by \(U\).  Then \(H_0\) is open and
\(\sigma\)-compact.  If \(H_0\) is noncompact, set \(H=H_0\).  If \(H_0\) is
compact, then \(G/H_0\) must be infinite; otherwise \(G\) would be a finite
union of compact cosets.  Choose representatives \(g_1,g_2,\ldots\) of
distinct cosets and let \(H\) be the subgroup generated by
\(H_0\cup\{g_1,g_2,\ldots\}\).  This \(H\) is open, since it contains \(H_0\);
it is \(\sigma\)-compact, because it is a countable union of finite products
involving the compact set \(H_0\) and finitely many of the \(g_i\)'s; and it is
noncompact, because its image in the discrete space \(G/H_0\) is infinite.

Thus we have a noncompact \(\sigma\)-compact open subgroup \(H\).  The
Kakutani--Kodaira theorem \cite[Theorem 8.7]{HewittRossI} gives a compact
normal subgroup \(N\triangleleft H\) such that \(H/N\) is metrizable.  Since
\(H\) is \(\sigma\)-compact, the locally compact metrizable group \(H/N\) is
second countable.  Finally, \(H/N\) is noncompact: if it were compact, finitely
many translates of a compact identity neighbourhood in \(H\), multiplied by
the compact subgroup \(N\), would cover \(H\), forcing \(H\) itself compact.
\end{proof}

We now prove Theorem \ref{thm:main}.  Suppose, toward a contradiction, that
\(G\) is noncompact and \(\Br(G)\) has weak\(^*\) fpp.  By Lemma
\ref{lem:topological-reduction}, choose \(H\leq G\) open and
\(N\triangleleft H\) compact with \(H/N\) second countable and noncompact.
Lemma \ref{lem:permanence}(1) gives weak\(^*\) fpp for \(\Br(H)\), and Lemma
\ref{lem:permanence}(2) then gives weak\(^*\) fpp for \(\Br(H/N)\).  Applying
Naderi's Theorem 3.1 \cite[Theorem 3.1]{Naderi1612} to the second countable
locally compact group \(H/N\), we conclude that \(H/N\) is compact.  This
contradicts Lemma \ref{lem:topological-reduction}.  Therefore \(G\) must be
compact.

\section*{Verification and novelty notes}

The proof is non-computational.  The decisive checks are:

\begin{itemize}
\item the open-subgroup map is a contractive projection
\(\Cr(G)\to\Cr(H)\), hence gives a weak\(^*\)-continuous isometric embedding
\(\Br(H)\hookrightarrow \Br(G)\);
\item compact normal quotients give a contractive surjection
\(\Cr(H)\to\Cr(H/N)\), hence a weak\(^*\)-continuous isometric embedding
\(\Br(H/N)\hookrightarrow \Br(H)\);
\item weak\(^*\) fpp passes to such isometric weak\(^*\)-embedded dual
subspaces by Lemma \ref{lem:dual-subspace};
\item the topological reduction preserves noncompactness.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Limitations and human-review recommendation}

This packet answers Question 5 only.  It does not address the broader BGC
Questions 1--4 on page 9 of the source paper.

Human review should focus on the two functoriality steps for reduced group
C*-algebras.  The open-subgroup compression and compact-normal quotient maps
are standard, but they are exactly where a hidden topological convention would
matter.
