# Literature status: q-Araki-Woods two-dimensional factoriality gap

Status: literature already answered.

Source paper: arXiv:1703.01317, *Complete metric approximation property for q-Araki-Woods algebras*.

Later answer paper: arXiv:2301.08619, *Full solution of the factoriality question for q-Araki-Woods von Neumann algebras via conjugate variables*.

## Question identified

In the introduction of arXiv:1703.01317, page 2, Avsec--Brannan--Wasilewski note that the remaining case for the factoriality and non-injectivity questions is the case of q-Araki-Woods algebras built from a two-dimensional Hilbert space.

This was the queue signal extracted from source line 140 of `CMAP_for_q-Araki-Feb28.tex`.

## Resolution by later literature

arXiv:2301.08619 states in its abstract that q-Araki-Woods von Neumann algebras with at least two generators are factorial in full generality, and that the same paper also establishes non-injectivity and determines the type.

The theorem-level statement on page 2 says that for every `q in (-1,1)`, every real Hilbert space of dimension at least 2, and every orthogonal one-parameter group, the q-Araki-Woods von Neumann algebra is a non-injective factor, with type determined by the generator spectrum. Theorem 2.5 on page 9 separately records non-injectivity for dimension at least 2.

Thus the two-dimensional gap flagged in arXiv:1703.01317 is already closed by later literature. This is not a new proof or counterexample from the run.

## Scope notes

The status here covers the factoriality and non-injectivity gap for q-Araki-Woods algebras of underlying Hilbert-space dimension at least 2, including the two-dimensional case singled out in arXiv:1703.01317.

The neighboring conjecture in arXiv:1703.01317 about type-invariance and an isometric/bijective transference principle for radial multipliers is not settled by this packet.
