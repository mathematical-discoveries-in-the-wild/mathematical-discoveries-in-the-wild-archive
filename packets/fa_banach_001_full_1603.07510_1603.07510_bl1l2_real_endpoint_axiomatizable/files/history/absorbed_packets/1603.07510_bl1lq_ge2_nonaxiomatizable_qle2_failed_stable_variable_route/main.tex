\section*{Status}

This packet records a partial negative result related to Raynaud's final
question in \cite{Raynaud2016}. It does not answer the unrestricted question
whether \(\BL_1L_q\) is axiomatizable as a Banach-space class for
\(1<q\le2\). It proves that the natural fiber-dimension-restricted variants
\(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\), \(n\ge2\), are not axiomatizable in that range.

\section*{Source question}

Raynaud summarizes the known positive cases for the Banach-space
axiomatizability of mixed-norm classes and then asks:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=.92\textwidth]{figures/question_6_17_crop.png}
\end{center}

\noindent
Question 6.17 of \cite{Raynaud2016} asks whether
\[
\BL_1L_q
\]
is axiomatizable in the language of Banach spaces for \(1<q\le2\).
Earlier in the same section Raynaud proves positive results for
\(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\) when \(q>2\) and \(n\ge3\). The theorem below shows that
no analogous positive theorem is possible for \(1<q\le2\), even with any fixed
fiber dimension lower bound \(n\ge2\).

\section*{Idea of the proof}

For \(1<q\le2\), the finite-dimensional space \(\ell_q^n\) embeds
isometrically into an \(L_1\)-space by \(q\)-stable random variables. Hence
\(L_1(\ell_q^n)\) and \(L_1\) are mutually finitely representable: \(L_1\)
is a one-coordinate subspace of \(L_1(\ell_q^n)\), while
\(L_1(\ell_q^n)\) embeds into \(L_1\) after applying the stable embedding
fiberwise. Heinrich's ultrapower theorem then gives isometric ultrapowers of
the two spaces.

If the class \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\) were axiomatizable after forgetting the
lattice structure, it would be closed under ultrapowers and ultraroots. Since
\(L_1(\ell_q^n)\) belongs to the class, the ultrapower equivalence would force
\(L_1\) itself to belong to the class. But this is impossible: the dual of a
nonzero \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\)-space contains a 1-complemented copy of
\(\ell_{q'}^n\), while \(L_1^*=L_\infty\) is 1-injective and cannot contain a
1-complemented non-injective \(\ell_{q'}^n\).

\begin{lemma}[stable finite-dimensional embedding]
Let \(1<q\le2\) and \(n\ge2\). Then \(\ell_q^n\) embeds linearly isometrically
into an \(L_1\)-space.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\xi_1,\ldots,\xi_n\) be independent symmetric \(q\)-stable random
variables normalized so that \(\mathbb E|\xi_1|=1\). This first moment is
finite because \(q>1\). By stability,
\[
\sum_{j=1}^n a_j\xi_j
\]
has the same distribution as
\[
\left(\sum_{j=1}^n |a_j|^q\right)^{1/q}\xi_1 .
\]
Therefore
\[
\mathbb E\left|\sum_{j=1}^n a_j\xi_j\right|
=
\left(\sum_{j=1}^n |a_j|^q\right)^{1/q}.
\]
The map \(a=(a_j)\mapsto \sum_j a_j\xi_j\) is the desired isometric embedding
of \(\ell_q^n\) into \(L_1\). For \(q=2\), this is the Gaussian special case.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
Let \(1<q<\infty\) and \(n\ge2\). The Banach space \(L_1[0,1]\) is not
linearly isometric to a member of \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We use a standard Banach-space invariant. The dual of an \(L_1\)-space is an
\(L_\infty\)-space and is 1-injective. Moreover, every 1-complemented subspace
of a 1-injective space is again 1-injective.

By contrast, let \(Y\) be a nonzero member of \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\), represented
as a band in a mixed-norm lattice \(L_1(\Omega;L_q(\Omega_\omega))\). The
condition \(\ge n\) says that every nonzero base-band contains \(n\) disjoint
fiber directions with the same base support, spanning a lattice copy of
\(\ell_q^n\). Passing to the dual representation gives a contractive
coordinate projection from \(Y^*\) onto the corresponding \(n\)-dimensional
fiber dual \(\ell_{q'}^n\). Thus \(Y^*\) contains a 1-complemented subspace
isometric to \(\ell_{q'}^n\), where \(1/q+1/q'=1\).

For \(1<q<\infty\), the finite-dimensional space \(\ell_{q'}^n\) is not
1-injective unless \(q'=\infty\), equivalently \(q=1\). Since here \(q>1\),
this is impossible in the dual of an \(L_1\)-space. Therefore
\(L_1[0,1]\) cannot be linearly isometric to a member of
\(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[negative fiber-dimension result]
Let \(1<q\le2\) and \(n\ge2\). The class of Banach spaces linearly isometric to
members of \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\) is not axiomatizable in the Banach-space
language.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Put
\[
X=L_1[0,1],
\qquad
Y=L_1([0,1];\ell_q^n).
\]
The space \(Y\) is a member of \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\): it is the mixed-norm
space with constant \(n\)-dimensional fiber \(\ell_q^n\).

The spaces \(X\) and \(Y\) are finitely equivalent. Indeed, \(X\) embeds
isometrically into \(Y\) as the first coordinate. Conversely, by the stable
embedding lemma there is an isometry \(J:\ell_q^n\to L_1[0,1]\). Applying \(J\)
fiberwise gives an isometric embedding
\[
L_1([0,1];\ell_q^n)
\longrightarrow
L_1([0,1]\times[0,1]),
\]
and the latter is linearly isometric to \(L_1[0,1]\) as a Banach space.
Thus each space is finitely representable in the other with constant one.

By Heinrich's ultrapower characterization of finite equivalence, there are
ultrafilters \(\U,\mathcal V\) such that
\[
X_\U \cong Y_\mathcal V
\]
linearly isometrically. Suppose, for contradiction, that the Banach-space
class generated by \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\) were axiomatizable. Such a class is
closed under ultrapowers and ultraroots. Since \(Y\) belongs to the class,
\(Y_\mathcal V\) belongs to the class. But \(Y_\mathcal V\cong X_\U\), so
\(X\) is an ultraroot of a member of the class. Closure under ultraroots would
imply \(X\) itself belongs to the class, contradicting the preceding lemma.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

The theorem does not settle Raynaud's Question 6.17 for the unrestricted class
\(\BL_1L_q\). The obstruction uses \(L_1[0,1]\), which is itself in
\(\BL_1L_q\) through a one-dimensional fiber representation. Thus it only
rules out axiomatizability of the stronger subclasses that require every
nonzero base-band to have fiber dimension at least \(n\ge2\).

crossing-Hilbert-exponent range:

\section*{Human review recommendation}

Review as a partial negative result adjacent to Question 6.17. The main proof
points to check are the exclusion lemma for \(L_1[0,1]\), especially the
assertion that nonzero \(\BL_1L_q^{\ge n}\)-members have duals with a
1-complemented \(\ell_{q'}^n\), and the use of Heinrich's finite-equivalence
to ultrapower-isometry theorem.
