# Literature-implied answer to Conjecture 2 of arXiv:math/0007058

Status: `literature_implied_answer (full classification)`

Source paper: S. J. Montgomery-Smith and E. M. Semenov, *Embeddings of rearrangement invariant spaces that are not strictly singular*, arXiv:math/0007058, Positivity 4 (2000), 397-404.

Source question: Conjecture 2 asks whether, for a rearrangement invariant space \(E\) on \([0,1]\), the natural embedding \(E\hookrightarrow L_1\) is not strictly singular if and only if the Rodin-Semenov space \(G=L^{\exp x^2}_0\) embeds continuously into \(E\).

Supporting record: Hernández, Ruiz, and Sanchiz, *Disjointly strictly singular inclusions between variable Lebesgue spaces*, arXiv:2406.14175, Introduction, PDF page 1, records as known that for symmetric/rearrangement invariant spaces \(E(\mu)\) on finite measures the inclusion \(E(\mu)\hookrightarrow L_1(\mu)\) is strictly singular iff \(L^{\exp x^2}_0\) is not included in \(E(\mu)\). It cites Hernández--Novikov--Semenov, *Strictly singular embeddings between rearrangement invariant spaces*, Positivity 7 (2003), 119-124, and Astashkin--Hernández--Semenov, *Strictly singular inclusions of rearrangement invariant spaces and Rademacher spaces*, Studia Math. 193 (2009), 269-283.

Interpretation: this is placed under `literature_implied_answers` rather than `literature_already_answered` because the accessible supporting arXiv paper records the exact classification as known, but does not explicitly say it is resolving Montgomery-Smith--Semenov Conjecture 2.
