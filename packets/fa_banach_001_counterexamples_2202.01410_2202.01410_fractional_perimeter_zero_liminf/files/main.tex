\section*{Source metadata and status}

The source is H.~Brezis, A.~Seeger, J.~Van Schaftingen, and P.-L.~Yung,
\emph{Sobolev spaces revisited}, arXiv:2202.01410v1 (2022).  The open
questions occur on page 20, in Section 6.  The local source is
\texttt{source\_paper.pdf}.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Claimed status: counterexample, likely valid.}
For every dimension $n\geq1$ and every $-1<\gamma<0$, both negative-$\gamma$
questions quoted below have negative answers.  The argument does not address
$\gamma\leq-1$ or $\gamma>0$.

\section*{The source questions}

For $\gamma\in\R$, put
\[
  \dif\nu_\gamma(x,h)=|h|^{\gamma-n}\dif x\dif h,
  \qquad
  \cE_{\lambda,1+\gamma}[u]
  =\left\{(x,h):
  \frac{|u(x+h)-u(x)|}{|h|^{1+\gamma}}>\lambda\right\}.
\]
The source asks, for $\gamma<0$:
\begin{quote}
If $u\in L^1_{\mathrm{loc}}$ and
\[
 \liminf_{\lambda\to0^+}
 \lambda\nu_\gamma(\cE_{\lambda,1+\gamma}[u])<\infty,
\]
must $u\in\BV$?  If $u\in\BV$, must $\|u\|_{\BV}$ be bounded by a
multiple, depending only on $n$ and $\gamma$, of this liminf?
\end{quote}

\end{center}

\section*{Proof intuition}

Write $s=-\gamma$.  On the interval $-1<\gamma<0$ one has $0<s<1$.
For a characteristic function $u=\mathbf1_A$, the superlevel indicator is
bounded by $|u(x+h)-u(x)|$.  Consequently the questioned quantity is at most
$\lambda$ times the fractional $W^{s,1}$ seminorm of $u$, and therefore tends
to zero whenever $A$ has finite fractional $s$-perimeter.

This observation supplies both counterexamples.  A cube has positive finite
classical perimeter and finite fractional perimeter, so it destroys the
proposed lower bound for the BV norm.  For the membership question, take a
bounded union of infinitely many disjoint intervals whose lengths decay so
fast that the sum of their fractional perimeters is finite.  It has infinitely
many classical boundary points and hence infinite variation.  Taking its
Cartesian product with a cube gives the same construction in every dimension.

\section*{Counterexample theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $n\geq1$ and $-1<\gamma<0$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item There is a compactly supported $u\in L^1(\R^n)\setminus\BV(\R^n)$ for
which
\[
  \lim_{\lambda\to0^+}
  \lambda\nu_\gamma(\cE_{\lambda,1+\gamma}[u])=0.
\]
In particular, finiteness of the liminf does not imply membership in $\BV$.
\item There is a nonzero compactly supported $v\in\BV(\R^n)$ with
\[
  \lim_{\lambda\to0^+}
  \lambda\nu_\gamma(\cE_{\lambda,1+\gamma}[v])=0.
\]
Thus no finite constant $C(n,\gamma)$ can bound $\|v\|_{\BV}$ by the
questioned liminf for all $v\in\BV$.
\end{enumerate}
Both $u$ and $v$ may be chosen to be characteristic functions of bounded
measurable sets.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

Set $s=-\gamma\in(0,1)$ and use the homogeneous fractional seminorm
\[
 [f]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R^n)}
 :=\int_{\R^n}\int_{\R^n}
 \frac{|f(x+h)-f(x)|}{|h|^{n+s}}\dif x\dif h.
\]

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:vanish}
If $A\subset\R^n$ is measurable and
$\mathbf1_A\in\dot W^{s,1}(\R^n)$, then
\[
  0\leq
  \lambda\nu_{-s}(\cE_{\lambda,1-s}[\mathbf1_A])
  \leq \lambda[\mathbf1_A]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R^n)}
\]
for every $\lambda>0$.  In particular, the expression tends to zero as
$\lambda\to0^+$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The difference of two values of $\mathbf1_A$ is either zero or one.  Hence
\[
 \mathbf1_{\cE_{\lambda,1-s}[\mathbf1_A]}(x,h)
 \leq |\mathbf1_A(x+h)-\mathbf1_A(x)|.
\]
Since $\dif\nu_{-s}=|h|^{-n-s}\dif x\dif h$, integration and multiplication
by $\lambda$ give the assertion.
\end{proof}

We first construct the non-BV set.  In one dimension let
\[
 I_k=\left(2^{-k},\,2^{-k}+2^{-2k-2}\right),\qquad
 E=\bigcup_{k=1}^{\infty}I_k.
\]
The intervals are pairwise disjoint.  If $I$ is an interval of length $\ell$,
a direct integration gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:interval}
 [\mathbf1_I]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R)}
 =\frac{4}{s(1-s)}\ell^{1-s}.
\end{equation}
Because $\mathbf1_E=\sum_k\mathbf1_{I_k}$ pointwise and the intervals are
disjoint, the triangle inequality and Tonelli's theorem give
\[
 [\mathbf1_E]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R)}
 \leq\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}[\mathbf1_{I_k}]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R)}
 =\frac4{s(1-s)}\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}
   2^{-(2k+2)(1-s)}<\infty.
\]
On the other hand, $\mathbf1_E$ has infinite variation.  Indeed, for every
$m$ one can choose pairwise disjoint neighborhoods of $I_1,\ldots,I_m$ and a
function $\phi_m\in C_c^1(\R)$, $|\phi_m|\leq1$, supported in their union,
such that $\phi_m$ equals $-1$ at the left endpoint and $1$ at the right
endpoint of each of these intervals.  Therefore
\[
 \int_E\phi_m'(t)\dif t=2m.
\]
The variational definition of $\BV$ now shows that
$\mathbf1_E\notin\BV(\R)$.

For $n>1$, let $Q=(0,1)^{n-1}$ and $A=E\times Q$; for $n=1$, simply set
$A=E$.  We verify that $A$ still has finite fractional perimeter.  With
$h=(t,z)\in\R\times\R^{n-1}$,
\[
 |A\mathbin\triangle(A-h)|
 \leq |Q|\,|E\mathbin\triangle(E-t)|
       +|E|\,|Q\mathbin\triangle(Q-z)|.
\]
Integrating this estimate against $|h|^{-n-s}$ and first integrating in $z$
in the first term and in $t$ in the second gives
\[
 [\mathbf1_A]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R^n)}
 \leq C_{n,s}\left(
 |Q|[\mathbf1_E]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R)}
 +|E|[\mathbf1_Q]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R^{n-1})}
 \right)<\infty.
\]
For completeness, the last seminorm is finite because
$|Q\mathbin\triangle(Q-z)|\leq C_n\min\{|z|,1\}$; its integral against
$|z|^{-(n-1)-s}$ is integrable both at zero (since $s<1$) and at infinity
(since $s>0$).

The set $A$ has infinite classical perimeter.  To see this directly, fix
$0\leq\psi\in C_c^1(Q)$ with $\int_Q\psi>0$ and $\|\psi\|_\infty\leq1$.
The vector fields
\[
 \Phi_m(x_1,x')=(\phi_m(x_1)\psi(x'),0,\ldots,0)
\]
have supremum norm at most one, while
\[
 \int_A\operatorname{div}\Phi_m
 =2m\int_Q\psi\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
Thus $u=\mathbf1_A$ lies in $L^1(\R^n)\setminus\BV(\R^n)$.  Lemma
\ref{lem:vanish} proves part (1).

For part (2), take $B=(0,1)^n$ and $v=\mathbf1_B$.  This is a nonconstant BV
function and $\|v\|_{\BV}>0$.  Moreover,
\[
 |B\mathbin\triangle(B-h)|\leq C_n\min\{|h|,1\},
\]
so exactly the same radial integration shows that
$[v]_{\dot W^{s,1}(\R^n)}<\infty$.  Lemma \ref{lem:vanish} yields a zero
limit, proving part (2). \qed

\section*{Verification notes}

The proof uses only nonnegative integrals and exact inequalities.  The main
checks are:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\gamma=-s$ makes the measure kernel exactly $|h|^{-n-s}$;
\item $1+\gamma=1-s>0$ is not needed for the domination in Lemma
  \ref{lem:vanish}, but $0<s<1$ is essential for the existence of nonconstant
  characteristic functions with finite fractional seminorm;
\item the interval series converges for every $s<1$ and its classical
  variation is infinite;
\item the product estimate proves the claim in every dimension without an
  appeal to a slicing theorem.
\end{itemize}
No numerical or computer-assisted step is used.

\section*{Scope, novelty check, and review recommendation}

This packet completely answers both quoted questions negatively on the whole
parameter interval $-1<\gamma<0$, for every $n\geq1$.  It does not settle the
remaining ranges.  In particular, the source notes that the membership/lower
bound statement at $\gamma=-1$ is known to behave differently; the present
fractional-perimeter construction becomes critical there.

The run indexes were searched for arXiv:2202.01410, the title, the exact
liminf questions, ``fractional perimeter'', and close $BV$ variants.  They
contained no prior packet for this result.  Bounded web searches on July 21,
2026 used the exact functional notation, the source title and quoted question,
and combinations of ``fractional perimeter'', ``liminf'', ``BV'', and
``negative gamma''.  They found the two source papers but no later paper
stating this counterexample.  Novelty confidence is moderate: the mechanism
is elementary and may be folklore even though no matching written answer was
found.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Human review recommendation.}  Review as a likely valid
counterexample.  The decisive line is Lemma \ref{lem:vanish}; reviewers should
also confirm that the source intended the questions parameter-by-parameter,
as its surrounding discussion indicates.
