\section*{Source Question}

The source paper is Tao Mei, Eric Ricard, and Quanhua Xu,
\emph{A H\"ormander-Mikhlin multiplier theory for free groups and amalgamated
free products of von Neumann algebras}, arXiv:2103.04368.  In the introduction,
the authors write that a better understanding of non-radial multipliers appears
needed for fundamental open questions, including construction of Schauder bases
for the free group reduced \(C^\ast\)-algebras and the corresponding
noncommutative \(L_p\)-spaces.

\section*{Supporting Theorem}

The relevant supporting paper is M. Junge and Z.-J. Ruan,
\emph{Approximation properties for noncommutative \(L_p\)-spaces associated
with discrete groups}, Duke Math. J. 117 (2003), 313--341.
Its abstract states, in particular, that if \(G\) is a countable discrete group
with the Haagerup--Kraus approximation property and \(VN(G)\) has QWEP, then
for \(1<p<\infty\), \(L_p(VN(G))\) is a \(\mathcal{COL}_p\)-space and has a
completely bounded Schauder basis.

\section*{Identification}

Let \(G\) be a countable free group, either \(F_n\) with \(2\le n<\infty\) or
\(F_\infty\).  Free groups have the Haagerup--Kraus approximation property
(indeed they are weakly amenable, as in Haagerup's free-group approximation
work).  They are also residually finite, hence hyperlinear.  The source paper
itself records, citing R\u{a}dulescu, that \(VN(\Gamma)\) is QWEP iff
\(\Gamma\) is hyperlinear.

Therefore the hypotheses of Junge--Ruan apply to every countable free group.
Consequently, for every \(1<p<\infty\),
\[
  L_p(VN(F_n)) \quad\text{and}\quad L_p(VN(F_\infty))
\]
have completely bounded Schauder bases.

\section*{Scope Limitations}

This only answers the ordinary Banach-space existence question for the
finite-\(p\) noncommutative \(L_p\)-spaces associated with free groups.  It
does not answer the reduced \(C^\ast_r(F_n)\) basis problem, the endpoint
\(p=\infty\), or whether the canonical group elements or a multiplier-adapted
ordering form a Schauder basis.  The supporting paper predates the source and
does not explicitly cite this footnote; the relation is an agent-identified
implication.
