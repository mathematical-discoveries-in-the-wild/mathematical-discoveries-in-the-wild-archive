\section*{Source And Target}

Jochen Glueck, \emph{Increasing sequences in ordered Banach spaces -- new
theorems and open problems}, arXiv:2409.19768.

The paper gives an explicit non-normal cone on \(\ell^1\), notes that the
Schur property only controls sequences, and says it is unclear whether every
increasing weakly convergent net for that cone is norm convergent. It then
poses the broader Dini-normal open problem.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(X=\ell^1(\mathbb N_0)\), where \(\mathbb N_0=\{0,1,2,\ldots\}\), and
write \(x=(x_0,u)\) with \(u=(x_k)_{k\geq1}\in \ell^1(\mathbb N)\).  Endow
\(X\) with the cone
\[
  X_+ =
  \left\{x\in \ell^1(\mathbb N_0):
    x_0\geq \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{|x_k|}{2^k}
  \right\}.
\]
Then there is an increasing net \((y_i)\) in \(X_+\) which converges weakly
in \(X\) but does not converge in norm.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

For \(u=(u_k)_{k\geq1}\in\ell^1(\mathbb N)\), put
\[
  \|u\|_w=\sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{|u_k|}{2^k}.
\]
Thus \((a,u)\in X_+\) if and only if \(a\geq \|u\|_w\), and
\[
  (a,u)\leq (b,v)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  b-a\geq \|v-u\|_w .
\]

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:annihilator}
Let \(F\subset \ell^\infty(\mathbb N)\) be finite and let \(n\geq1\).  There
is \(u=u_{n,F}\in\ell^1(\mathbb N)\) such that
\[
  \|u\|_1=1,\qquad
  \operatorname{supp}u\subseteq\{k\geq n\},\qquad
  \varphi(u)=0\quad(\varphi\in F).
\]
Consequently \(\|u\|_w\leq 2^{-n}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose \(r>|F|\) distinct integers \(k_1,\ldots,k_r\geq n\).  The homogeneous
system
\[
  \sum_{j=1}^r c_j\varphi(k_j)=0\qquad(\varphi\in F)
\]
has a nonzero real solution \(c=(c_1,\ldots,c_r)\).  Define \(u\) by
supporting it on the \(k_j\)'s with coefficients \(c_j\), then normalize so
that \(\|u\|_1=1\).  Since \(2^{-k_j}\leq2^{-n}\), we have
\(\|u\|_w\leq2^{-n}\|u\|_1=2^{-n}\).
\end{proof}

Let \(D\) be the set of pairs \(i=(n,F)\), where \(n\geq1\) and
\(F\subset\ell^\infty(\mathbb N)\) is finite.  Define \(i=(n,F)\preceq
j=(m,G)\) if either \(i=j\), or \(m\geq n+2\) and \(F\subseteq G\).  This is a
directed order: given two indices, take the union of the finite sets and a
large enough integer.

For each \(i=(n,F)\), choose \(u_i=u_{n,F}\) as in
Lemma~\ref{lem:annihilator}, and set
\[
  a_n=4-2^{1-n},\qquad y_i=(a_n,u_i)\in X.
\]
Since \(a_n\geq2\) and \(\|u_i\|_w\leq2^{-n}\leq1/2\), each \(y_i\) is in
\(X_+\).

We claim that \((y_i)\) is increasing.  If \(i=(n,F)\prec j=(m,G)\), then
\(m\geq n+2\).  Hence
\[
  \|u_j-u_i\|_w\leq \|u_i\|_w+\|u_j\|_w\leq 2^{-n}+2^{-m},
\]
while
\[
  a_m-a_n=2(2^{-n}-2^{-m}).
\]
Since \(m\geq n+2\), one has \(2^{-m}\leq 2^{-n}/4\), and therefore
\[
  2(2^{-n}-2^{-m})\geq 2^{-n}+2^{-m}.
\]
Thus \(y_j-y_i\in X_+\), proving monotonicity.

We next prove weak convergence.  Let \(\psi\in X'=\ell^\infty(\mathbb N_0)\).
Write \(\psi=(\beta,\varphi)\), where \(\beta\in\mathbb R\) acts on the
zeroth coordinate and \(\varphi\in\ell^\infty(\mathbb N)\) acts on the tail.
For all sufficiently large indices in the directed order, the finite set
component contains \(\varphi\), so \(\varphi(u_i)=0\).  Also \(a_n\to4\) along
the directed set.  Hence
\[
  \psi(y_i)=\beta a_n+\varphi(u_i)\longrightarrow 4\beta.
\]
This is exactly weak convergence to \((4,0)\in X\).

Finally,
\[
  \|y_i-(4,0)\|_1
  = |a_n-4|+\|u_i\|_1
  = 2^{1-n}+1
\]
for every \(i=(n,F)\).  The net therefore does not converge to \((4,0)\) in
norm.  Since the weak limit is unique, it does not converge in norm at all.
\qed

\section*{Scope And Limitations}

This answers the concrete uncertainty about the explicit cone in Example 6.1
of the source paper: the Schur property does not rescue Dini's theorem for
weakly convergent nets in that ordered \(\ell^1\) space.

It is not a full solution of Open Problem 6.2.  The construction uses the
special tail geometry of the cone
\[
  x_0\geq \sum_{k\geq1}|x_k|2^{-k}
\]
and the ability to annihilate finitely many \(\ell^\infty\) functionals on
arbitrarily far tails.
