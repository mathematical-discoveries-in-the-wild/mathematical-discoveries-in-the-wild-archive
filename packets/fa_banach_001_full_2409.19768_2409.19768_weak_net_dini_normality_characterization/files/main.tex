\large Candidate full solution packet for Open Problem 6.2 in Gl\"uck}

\begin{abstract}
Let $X$ be a real ordered Banach space with closed pointed cone $X_+$.
We prove that $X_+$ is normal if and only if every increasing weakly
convergent net in $X$ is norm convergent.  In fact, it is enough to assume
this only for norm-bounded nets.  More precisely, if $X_+$ is non-normal,
then there are $u\in X_+$ and a norm-bounded increasing net $(y_i)$ in the
order interval $[0,u]$ such that $y_i\rightharpoonup u$ while
$\norm{y_i-u}\to 1$.  This gives an affirmative answer to Open Problem 6.2
of Gl\"uck and also settles the bounded-net variant mentioned there.

We also audit the supplied partial construction for the explicit non-normal
cone on $\ell^1(\mathbb N_0)$.  That construction is correct; it is a concrete
special case of the general phenomenon proved here.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem and result}

Throughout, an \emph{ordered Banach space} means a real Banach space $X$
endowed with a closed pointed cone $X_+$.  The induced order is
$x\leq y$ if $y-x\in X_+$.  Recall that $X_+$ is \emph{normal} if there is
$M\geq 0$ such that
\[
  0\leq x\leq y \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \norm{x}\leq M\norm{y}.
\]

Gl\"uck asked whether normality follows from the property that every
increasing weakly convergent net is norm convergent
\cite[Open Problem~6.2]{Glueck2025}.  He also noted that the answer might
conceivably change if one assumes, in addition, that the net is norm
bounded.  The following theorem answers both questions.

\begin{theorem}[Weak-net Dini characterization]\label{thm:main}
Let $X$ be an ordered Banach space.  The following assertions are
equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item The cone $X_+$ is normal.
  \item Every increasing weakly convergent net in $X$ converges in norm.
  \item Every norm-bounded increasing weakly convergent net in $X$
        converges in norm.
\end{enumerate}
If $X_+$ is non-normal, the counterexample to \textup{(iii)} can be chosen
in the following strong form: there exist $u\in X_+$ and an increasing net
$(y_i)\subseteq[0,u]$ such that
\[
  y_i\rightharpoonup u,
  \qquad
  \sup_i\norm{y_i}<\infty,
  \qquad
  \norm{y_i-u}\longrightarrow 1.
\]
\end{theorem}

The implication (i)$\Rightarrow$(ii) is the classical ordered-space version
of Dini's theorem; see, for example, Schaefer--Wolff
\cite[Section~V.4.3]{SchaeferWolff1999} and Gl\"uck
\cite[Corollary~4.2]{Glueck2025}.  The content here is the converse,
including the boundedness conclusion.

\section{Proof intuition}

The forward direction is the usual Dini compactness argument: normality makes
the positive dual cone uniformly norming on positive vectors, and weak-star
compactness of the positive dual unit ball turns pointwise decay of
$f(x-x_i)$ into uniform norm decay of $x-x_i$.

For the converse, non-normality gives an unbounded order interval
$[-u,u]$.  The main observation is that no finite family of functionals can
capture this unboundedness: after passing to the principal order ideal
generated by $u$, boundedness on a finite-codimensional slice would force the
order-ideal inclusion to be bounded everywhere, contradicting the unbounded
interval.  Thus one can choose very large vectors in $[-u,u]$ annihilating
any prescribed finite set of functionals.  Scaling these vectors down creates
a decreasing positive weakly null net whose norms stay close to one; subtracting
it from $u$ gives the required increasing weakly convergent, norm-bounded net
that does not converge in norm.

\section{Audit of the supplied \texorpdfstring{$\ell^1$}{l1} construction}

Consider $X=\ell^1(\mathbb N_0)$ with the cone
\begin{equation}\label{eq:l1cone}
 X_+
 =\left\{x\in\ell^1(\mathbb N_0):
 x_0\geq\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{|x_k|}{2^k}\right\}.
\end{equation}
The source paper records that this cone is closed, generating, and
non-normal, and asks specifically whether it nevertheless satisfies the
net version of Dini's theorem \cite[Example~6.1 and Open
Problem~6.2]{Glueck2025}.

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:audit}
The supplied partial proof constructs a valid increasing weakly convergent
net in \eqref{eq:l1cone} which is not norm convergent.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Write a vector as $(a,u)$ with $u\in\ell^1(\mathbb N)$, and set
\[
  q(u)=\sum_{k\geq1}2^{-k}|u_k|.
\]
Then
\[
 (a,u)\leq(b,v) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad b-a\geq q(v-u).
\]
The supplied argument uses the directed set of pairs $(n,F)$, where $F$ is
a finite subset of $\ell^\infty(\mathbb N)$, and chooses
$u_{n,F}\in\ell^1(\mathbb N)$ satisfying
\[
 \norm{u_{n,F}}_1=1,
 \qquad
 \supp u_{n,F}\subseteq\{k\geq n\},
 \qquad
 \varphi(u_{n,F})=0\quad(\varphi\in F).
\]
Such a vector exists because a homogeneous system with $|F|$ equations and
more than $|F|$ unknowns has a nonzero solution.  Its support condition
gives $q(u_{n,F})\leq2^{-n}$.

The order on the index set is defined so that a strict increase from
$(n,F)$ to $(m,G)$ forces $m\geq n+2$ and $F\subseteq G$.  For
\[
 a_n=4-2^{1-n},
 \qquad
 y_{n,F}=(a_n,u_{n,F}),
\]
the key estimate is
\[
 q(u_{m,G}-u_{n,F})
 \leq 2^{-n}+2^{-m}
 \leq 2(2^{-n}-2^{-m})
 =a_m-a_n,
\]
where the middle inequality follows from $m\geq n+2$.  Thus the net is
increasing.  Given any $\varphi\in\ell^\infty(\mathbb N)$, every sufficiently
large finite-set component contains $\varphi$, so
$\varphi(u_{n,F})=0$ eventually.  Since $a_n\to4$ along the directed set,
$y_{n,F}\rightharpoonup(4,0)$.  Finally,
\[
 \norm{y_{n,F}-(4,0)}_1=1+2^{1-n},
\]
so norm convergence fails.  Moreover, $0\leq y_{n,F}\leq4e_0$, so this
particular counterexample is both order bounded and norm bounded.  All uses
of the directed order, including transitivity, directedness, and eventual
containment of a prescribed functional, are valid.
\end{proof}

Thus the partial result completely settles the concrete $\ell^1$ question.
The remaining task is to remove the special weighted-tail geometry.  The
next two sections do so.

\section{A principal order ideal and its finite-codimensional slices}

Assume for this section that $u\in X_+$.  Put
\[
 A_u=[-u,u]
 =\{x\in X:-u\leq x\leq u\}
\]
and define the principal order ideal
\[
 E_u=\bigcup_{r>0}rA_u
 =\{x\in X:\text{for some }r>0,\ -ru\leq x\leq ru\}.
\]
The Minkowski functional of $A_u$ is
\[
 p_u(x)=\inf\{r>0:x\in rA_u\},\qquad x\in E_u.
\]
The set $A_u$ is absolutely convex and norm closed.

\begin{lemma}[The order-unit norm and the inclusion map]\label{lem:closed-inclusion}
The function $p_u$ is a norm on $E_u$, its closed unit ball is exactly
$A_u$, and the canonical inclusion
\[
 J_u:(E_u,p_u)\longrightarrow (X,\norm{\cdot}),\qquad J_u x=x,
\]
has closed graph.  If $A_u$ is norm unbounded, then $J_u$ is unbounded.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The only nonstandard point in the Minkowski-functional argument is
positive definiteness.  If $p_u(x)=0$, then $x\in\varepsilon A_u$ for every
$\varepsilon>0$, hence
\[
 -\varepsilon u\leq x\leq\varepsilon u\qquad(\varepsilon>0).
\]
Letting $\varepsilon\downarrow0$ and using norm closedness of $X_+$ gives
$x\in X_+$ and $-x\in X_+$.  Pointedness yields $x=0$.

Certainly $A_u\subseteq\{p_u\leq1\}$.  Conversely, if $p_u(x)\leq1$, then
$x\in(1+1/n)A_u$ for every $n$, so $x/(1+1/n)\in A_u$.  Norm closedness of
$A_u$ gives $x\in A_u$.  Thus $A_u$ is the closed $p_u$-unit ball.

To prove that $J_u$ is closed, suppose that $x_n\to x$ in $p_u$ and
$x_n\to y$ in the norm of $X$.  For each $\varepsilon>0$, eventually
$x_n-x\in\varepsilon A_u$.  Since $\varepsilon A_u$ is norm closed,
$y-x\in\varepsilon A_u$.  This holds for every $\varepsilon>0$, and the
positive-definiteness argument above gives $y=x$.  Finally, if $A_u$ is
norm unbounded, then the image under $J_u$ of the $p_u$-unit ball is norm
unbounded, so $J_u$ is unbounded.
\end{proof}

The following lemma is the central device.  It says that the unboundedness
of an order interval cannot be concentrated in finitely many weak
directions.

\begin{lemma}[Finite-codimensional slice lemma]\label{lem:slice}
Suppose that $A_u=[-u,u]$ is norm unbounded.  For every finite set
$F\subseteq X'$ the set
\[
 A_u\cap F^\perp,
 \qquad
 F^\perp:=\bigcap_{f\in F}\ker f,
\]
is norm unbounded.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let
\[
 M=E_u\cap F^\perp.
\]
Assume for a contradiction that $A_u\cap M$ is norm bounded, say by $K$.
Since $A_u$ is the $p_u$-unit ball, the restriction $J_u|_M$ is bounded:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bounded-on-M}
  \norm{m}\leq Kp_u(m)\qquad(m\in M).
\end{equation}

We first show that $M$ is closed in $(E_u,p_u)$.  Let $m_n\in M$ and
$m_n\to m$ in $p_u$.  By \eqref{eq:bounded-on-M}, $(m_n)$ is norm Cauchy,
so it converges in $X$ to some $y$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:closed-inclusion}
gives $y=m$.  Since every $f\in F$ is norm continuous,
$f(m)=\lim_n f(m_n)=0$; hence $m\in M$.

The algebraic codimension of $M$ in $E_u$ is finite.  Because $M$ is
$p_u$-closed, there is a finite-dimensional subspace $N\subseteq E_u$ such
that
\[
 E_u=M\oplus N
\]
topologically.  Equivalently, there is $c>0$ such that the unique
decomposition $x=m+n$ satisfies
\[
 p_u(m)+p_u(n)\leq c\,p_u(x).
\]
The inclusion $J_u$ is bounded on $M$ by \eqref{eq:bounded-on-M}, and it is
bounded on the finite-dimensional space $N$.  Therefore $J_u$ is bounded on
all of $E_u$, contradicting Lemma~\ref{lem:closed-inclusion}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:exact-radius}
Under the assumptions of Lemma~\ref{lem:slice}, for every finite
$F\subseteq X'$ and every $t>0$ there is $x\in A_u\cap F^\perp$ with
$\norm{x}=t$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Choose $a\in A_u\cap F^\perp$ with $\norm{a}\geq t$ and put
$x=(t/\norm{a})a$.  The set $A_u\cap F^\perp$ is balanced.
\end{proof}

\section{Proof of the characterization theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
\emph{(i)$\Rightarrow$(ii).}
We include the standard Dini argument for completeness.  Let
$(x_i)_{i\in I}$ be increasing and suppose that $x_i\rightharpoonup x$.
For each fixed $i$, the weak closedness of $X_+$ gives $x_i\leq x$.

Normality of $X_+$ implies that the dual cone $X'_+$ is generating.  By the
uniform decomposition theorem, there is $M\geq1$ such that every
$f\in X'$ can be written as $f=g-h$, where $g,h\in X'_+$ and
\[
 \norm{g},\norm{h}\leq M\norm{f}.
\]
Consequently, with
\[
 K=X'_+\cap\{f\in X':\norm{f}\leq1\},
\]
we have the norming estimate
\begin{equation}\label{eq:positive-norming}
  \sup_{f\in K}f(v)\geq M^{-1}\norm{v}
  \qquad(v\in X_+).
\end{equation}
Indeed, for $v\neq0$ choose $f\in X'$ with $\norm{f}=1$ and
$f(v)=\norm{v}$, decompose $f=g-h$, and use
$\norm{v}=g(v)-h(v)\leq g(v)$.

The set $K$ is weak-star compact.  Given $\varepsilon>0$, define
\[
 U_i=\{f\in K:f(x-x_i)<\varepsilon/M\}.
\]
The sets $U_i$ are weak-star open in $K$ and cover $K$, since for each
positive $f$ the nonnegative scalar net $f(x-x_i)$ tends to zero.
Compactness gives indices $i_1,\ldots,i_n$ with
$K=U_{i_1}\cup\cdots\cup U_{i_n}$.  Choose $i_0$ dominating all $i_k$.
Then monotonicity gives $K=U_{i_0}$, and \eqref{eq:positive-norming}
yields $\norm{x-x_i}<\varepsilon$ for all $i\geq i_0$.  Hence
$x_i\to x$ in norm.

The implication (ii)$\Rightarrow$(iii) is immediate.

\emph{Contrapositive of (iii)$\Rightarrow$(i).}
Assume that $X_+$ is non-normal.  A standard characterization of normal
cones says that some order interval $[0,u]$, with $u\in X_+$, is norm
unbounded; see \cite[Theorem~2.40]{AliprantisTourky2007}.  Hence
$A_u=[-u,u]$ is norm unbounded.

Set
\[
 t_n=4^n(1+\norm{u})\qquad(n\in\N).
\]
Let $D$ consist of all pairs $i=(n,F)$, where $n\in\N$ and
$F\subseteq X'$ is finite.  Define
\[
 (n,F)\preceq(m,G)
 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
 (n,F)=(m,G)
 \ \text{or}\ 
 \bigl(m\geq n+1\ \text{and}\ F\subseteq G\bigr).
\]
This is a directed order: two indices are dominated by an index whose
finite-set component is their union and whose integer component is larger
than both.

For every $i=(n,F)$, Corollary~\ref{cor:exact-radius} gives
$x_i\in A_u\cap F^\perp$ such that
\[
 \norm{x_i}=t_n.
\]
Define
\[
 z_i=\frac{2u+x_i}{t_n}.
\]
Since $-u\leq x_i\leq u$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:z-bounds}
  \frac{u}{t_n}\leq z_i\leq\frac{3u}{t_n}.
\end{equation}
In particular, $0\leq z_i\leq u$, because $t_n\geq4$.
If $i=(n,F)\prec j=(m,G)$, then $t_m\geq4t_n$, and
\[
 z_j\leq\frac{3u}{t_m}
 \leq\frac{u}{t_n}
 \leq z_i.
\]
Thus $(z_i)$ is decreasing.

Fix $f\in X'$.  Once the finite-set component contains $f$, we have
$f(x_i)=0$.  Therefore, eventually,
\[
 f(z_i)=\frac{2f(u)}{t_n}\longrightarrow0.
\]
Hence $z_i\rightharpoonup0$.
On the other hand, the reverse triangle inequality and $\norm{x_i}=t_n$
give
\begin{equation}\label{eq:norm-z}
  1-\frac{2\norm{u}}{t_n}
  \leq \norm{z_i}
  \leq 1+\frac{2\norm{u}}{t_n}.
\end{equation}
The integer component tends to infinity along $D$, so
$\norm{z_i}\to1$.  In particular, $(z_i)$ is norm bounded but not norm
null.

Finally put
\[
 y_i=u-z_i.
\]
By \eqref{eq:z-bounds}, $0\leq y_i\leq u$; since $(z_i)$ is decreasing,
$(y_i)$ is increasing.  Moreover,
\[
 y_i\rightharpoonup u,
 \qquad
 \norm{y_i-u}=\norm{z_i}\longrightarrow1.
\]
The estimate \eqref{eq:norm-z} also gives
\[
 \sup_i\norm{y_i}
 \leq \norm{u}+1+\frac{2\norm{u}}{t_1}<\infty.
\]
Thus (iii) fails whenever the cone is non-normal, completing the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why a net is essential]
The finite-set component of the directed index set forces the chosen vector
to annihilate each prescribed functional eventually.  In a space with the
Schur property, such as $\ell^1$, no analogous weakly null normalized
\emph{sequence} exists.  The construction uses the full weak neighborhood
filter and therefore genuinely exploits nets.
\end{remark}

\section{The explicit cone on \texorpdfstring{$\ell^1$}{l1}}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:l1}
For the cone \eqref{eq:l1cone}, there is a norm-bounded increasing net
$(y_i)\subseteq[0,e_0]$ such that
\[
 y_i\rightharpoonup e_0
 \quad\text{but}\quad
 \norm{y_i-e_0}_1\not\longrightarrow0.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For each $k\geq1$, the vector
\[
 w_k=\frac12(e_0+2^k e_k)
\]
satisfies $0\leq w_k\leq e_0$, while
$\norm{w_k}_1=\frac12+2^{k-1}\to\infty$.  Thus $[0,e_0]$ is norm
unbounded.  Apply the counterexample construction in
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} with $u=e_0$.
\end{proof}

The supplied construction is more explicit than this corollary: it gives
closed formulas for the scalar buffer and uses weighted-tail vectors of
$\ell^1$-norm one.  The general proof shows that this is not a peculiarity
of that cone; every non-normal closed cone admits a bounded weak-net Dini
failure.

\section{Literature check and novelty status}

The literature search was carried out through June 21, 2026.  It included
exact-phrase searches for Open Problem 6.2, searches for combinations of
``weakly convergent increasing net'', ``normal cone'', and ``Dini'', forward
citation checks for Gl\"uck's article, and inspection of the principal older
references on monotone nets and ordered-space Dini theorems.

Gl\"uck's paper was published in 2025 and explicitly leaves the question
open \cite{Glueck2025}.  McArthur's classical paper studies convergence of
order-bounded monotone nets and regularity of cones
\cite{McArthur1970}, while Wong and Ng study the sequence version of a Dini
property \cite{WongNg1975}.  The standard references prove the forward
implication from normality to norm convergence of weakly convergent
monotone nets, but the searches did not locate the converse proved in
Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.  The only forward citation to Gl\"uck's article that
was located was unrelated to this open problem.

Accordingly, the theorem above appears to be a full answer not already
present in the sources located.  This is a literature-search conclusion,
not a claim that an unindexed or differently phrased prior proof cannot
exist.

For this packet upgrade, the local duplicate/status check found only the
earlier explicit-cone partial packet and two unpromoted attempts for
arXiv:2409.19768.  No fresh internet search was run during incorporation;
the search paragraph above records the supplied report's stated search bounds
and should be independently checked by a human reviewer before any external
novelty claim.
