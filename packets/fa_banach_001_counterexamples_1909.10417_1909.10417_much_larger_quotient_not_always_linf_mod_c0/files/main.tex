\section*{Source and target}

The source is Miek Messerschmidt, \emph{A family of quotient maps of
$\ell^\infty$ that do not admit uniformly continuous right inverses},
arXiv:1909.10417 \cite{messerschmidt}.  For ideals $\I,\J$ on $\N$,
the source writes $\I\Subset\J$ when $\I\subsetneq\J$ and there are
nonempty pairwise disjoint sets $(D_j)\subseteq\I$ such that
$\bigcup_{j\in K}D_j\in \J\setminus\I$ for every infinite
$K\subseteq\N$.  It proves that if $\I\Subset\J$, then the quotient
map
\[
   \J c_0\longrightarrow \J c_0/\I c_0
\]
has no uniformly continuous right inverse.

After proving abundance of much-larger inclusions, the paper remarks
that one could conjecture that $\J c_0/\I c_0$ is always isomorphic to
$\ell_\infty/c_0$.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

This packet gives a counterexample to that conjectural remark.

\section*{Definitions}

For an ideal $\J$ on $\N$,
\[
   \J c_0=\{x\in \ell_\infty:
   \{n\in\N: |x_n|\ge \varepsilon\}\in\J
   \text{ for every }\varepsilon>0\}.
\]
For $\J=\Fin$ this is the usual $c_0$.

A Banach space is Grothendieck if every weak-star convergent sequence in
the dual is weakly convergent.  We use the classical facts that
$\ell_\infty$ is Grothendieck, quotients and complemented subspaces of
Grothendieck spaces are Grothendieck, and $c_0$ is not Grothendieck.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The source's much-larger relation only requires one countable family of
small pieces whose infinite unions become large.  We put that witness
inside a single infinite block $D$.  Separately, we add infinitely many
other infinite blocks $A_m$ to the larger ideal, but only finitely many
at a time.  Those $A_m$ blocks survive in the quotient as a block
coordinate system: ultrafilter limits on the blocks define a bounded
projection from $\J c_0/c_0$ onto a copy of $c_0$.  Since
$\ell_\infty/c_0$ is Grothendieck and hence cannot contain complemented
$c_0$, the quotient cannot be isomorphic to $\ell_\infty/c_0$.

\begin{theorem}
There is an ideal $\J$ on $\N$ such that $\Fin\Subset\J$, but
$\J c_0/c_0$ is not isomorphic to $\ell_\infty/c_0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Partition $\N$ into pairwise disjoint infinite sets
\[
   \N=D\sqcup A_1\sqcup A_2\sqcup\cdots .
\]
Define
\[
   \J=\{S\subseteq\N:\text{ for some }n,\ 
       S\setminus D\subseteq A_1\cup\cdots\cup A_n\}.
\]
This is a proper ideal: it is hereditary, closed under finite unions,
contains all finite sets, and does not contain $\bigcup_{m\ge1}A_m$.

We first verify $\Fin\Subset\J$.  Choose pairwise disjoint singletons
$D_j=\{d_j\}$ inside $D$.  Each $D_j$ belongs to $\Fin$.  If
$K\subseteq\N$ is infinite, then $\bigcup_{j\in K}D_j$ is an infinite
subset of $D$, hence belongs to $\J\setminus\Fin$.  Thus
$\Fin\Subset\J$.

It remains to show that $\J c_0/c_0$ cannot be isomorphic to
$\ell_\infty/c_0$.  For each $m$, fix a free ultrafilter on $A_m$ and
let
\[
   \varphi_m:\ell_\infty(A_m)\to\mathbb R
\]
be the corresponding ultrafilter-limit functional.  Then
$\|\varphi_m\|=1$, $\varphi_m(1_{A_m})=1$, and
$\varphi_m$ vanishes on $c_0(A_m)$.

Define a bounded linear operator $R:\J c_0\to \ell_\infty$ by
\[
   (Rx)(n)=
   \begin{cases}
   \varphi_m(x|_{A_m}), & n\in A_m,\\
   0, & n\in D.
   \end{cases}
\]
We claim that $R(\J c_0)\subseteq \J c_0$.  Let $x\in\J c_0$ and
write $b_m=\varphi_m(x|_{A_m})$.  If $|b_m|\ge\varepsilon$, then
$\sup_{n\in A_m}|x_n|\ge\varepsilon$.  Hence
\[
   \{m: |b_m|\ge\varepsilon\}
\]
is finite, because the set
$\{n:|x_n|\ge\varepsilon\}$ belongs to $\J$ and therefore meets only
finitely many of the blocks $A_m$ outside $D$.  Thus $(b_m)\in c_0$,
so $Rx\in\J c_0$.

Moreover, $R$ annihilates $c_0$: if $x\in c_0$, then
$x|_{A_m}\in c_0(A_m)$ for every $m$, and therefore
$\varphi_m(x|_{A_m})=0$.  Thus $R$ descends to a bounded operator
\[
   \overline R:\J c_0/c_0\longrightarrow \J c_0/c_0 .
\]
It is a projection.  Indeed, if $x$ is already constant with value
$b_m$ on each block $A_m$ and zero on $D$, then
$\varphi_m(x|_{A_m})=b_m$.

The range of $\overline R$ is isometric to $c_0$.  For
$a=(a_m)\in c_0$, let $x_a\in\J c_0$ be zero on $D$ and equal to
$a_m$ on $A_m$.  The map
\[
   T:c_0\to \J c_0/c_0,\qquad T(a)=[x_a]
\]
has range equal to $\operatorname{ran}\overline R$.  Its quotient norm
is exactly $\|a\|_\infty$: the upper bound is clear by taking the zero
element of $c_0$, while for any $z\in c_0$ and any $m$, the set $A_m$
is infinite, so points in $A_m$ can be chosen with $|z_n|$ arbitrarily
small; hence
\[
   \|x_a-z\|_\infty\ge |a_m|-\delta
\]
for every $m$ and every $\delta>0$.  Taking the supremum over $m$ and
then $\delta\downarrow0$ gives
$\operatorname{dist}(x_a,c_0)\ge\|a\|_\infty$.  Therefore
$T$ is an isometry onto the complemented range of $\overline R$.

Consequently $\J c_0/c_0$ contains a complemented subspace isomorphic
to $c_0$.

On the other hand, $\ell_\infty/c_0$ is Grothendieck.  Indeed,
$\ell_\infty$ is Grothendieck by Grothendieck's theorem, and quotients
of Grothendieck spaces are Grothendieck.  Complemented subspaces of
Grothendieck spaces are also Grothendieck.  But $c_0$ is not
Grothendieck: the unit vector basis of $\ell_1=(c_0)^*$ is weak-star
null on $c_0$, but it is not weakly null, since the constant-one
functional in $\ell_\infty=(\ell_1)^*$ takes value $1$ on every basis
vector.

Thus $\ell_\infty/c_0$ cannot contain a complemented subspace
isomorphic to $c_0$.  Since $\J c_0/c_0$ does contain such a subspace,
$\J c_0/c_0$ is not isomorphic to $\ell_\infty/c_0$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and limitations}

The construction is entirely in ZFC and uses no special set-theoretic
axiom.  It refutes the source's conjectural isomorphism remark for
arbitrary ideals with $\I\Subset\J$.  It does not address whether the
specific classical examples in the source, such as the density-zero,
Banach-density-zero, tile-density-zero, or reciprocal-series ideals,
have quotients isomorphic to $\ell_\infty/c_0$.

Bounded novelty checks were performed on 2026-06-20 against the local
run indexes, local parsed arXiv corpus, and web searches for the exact
conjectural phrase and close variants.  The searches found the source
arXiv page and no prior answer to this specific conjectural remark.
