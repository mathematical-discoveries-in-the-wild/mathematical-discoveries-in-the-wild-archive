# The ccc Valdivia Dichotomy Is Refuted under Diamond

Status: `literature_already_answered (conditional negative answer)`

## Source conjecture

Claudia Correa and Daniel V. Tausk, *Nontrivial twisted sums of c_0 and
C(K)*, arXiv:1505.06727, Journal of Functional Analysis 270 (2016),
842--853.

The unnumbered conjecture on arXiv PDF page 10 asks whether every nonempty
ccc Valdivia compact space either has a G_delta point or admits a nontrivial
convergent sequence in the complement of a dense Sigma-subset.

## Explicit later counterexample

The same authors' separate paper, *Small Valdivia compacta and trees*,
arXiv:1512.02610, Studia Mathematica 235 (2016), 117--135, explicitly says
that it constructs a counterexample to this conjecture under the Diamond
axiom. Theorem 4.1, on arXiv PDF pages 10--11, gives a Valdivia compact space
of weight omega_1 that has ccc, has no G_delta points, and has no nontrivial
convergent sequence in the complement of a dense Sigma-subset.

This is an author-explicit literature answer, not a new mathematical result.
Its scope is conditional: it refutes the conjecture under Diamond, and hence
precludes a ZFC proof (assuming the usual consistency background), but it
does not furnish a ZFC counterexample. The supporting paper also states that
the twisted-sum problem for its counterexample remains open.
