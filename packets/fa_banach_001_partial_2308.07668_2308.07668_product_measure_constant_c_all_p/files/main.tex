This is a partial solution packet for arXiv:2308.07668, ``On Ramsey-type properties of the distance in nonseparable spheres'' by Piotr Koszmider.  It extends the earlier packet for the Hilbertian case \(p=2\).  The packet proves the exact value of \(c=\sup_n c_n\) for every \(1<p<\infty\), proves the exact finite values of \(c_n\) for \(p\geq2\), and gives asymptotically sharp finite bounds for \(1<p<2\).

\section*{Source Notation}

Let \(\mu\) be the product probability measure on \(\{0,1\}^{\kappa}\), where \(\kappa\leq2^\omega\) is uncountable.  In Proposition 55 of the source paper, for \(A\in[\kappa]^n\), the map \(T_A\) sends a function \(F\in L_p(\{0,1\}^n)\) to the function on \(\{0,1\}^{\kappa}\) depending on the coordinates in \(A\).  The constant \(c_n\) is the supremum of
\[
 \|T_A(F)-T_B(F)\|_{L_p}
\]
over all unit vectors \(F\in L_p(\{0,1\}^n)\) and all admissible pairs \(A,B\in[\kappa]^n\) whose common coordinates occur in the same positions.  Finally \(c=\sup_n c_n\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Once \(A\) and \(B\) are fixed, the only probabilistic structure that matters is this: after conditioning on the coordinates shared by \(A\) and \(B\), the two random variables \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) become iid copies of the same finite random variable.  Thus the constants are governed by the sharp ratio between \(\E|U-V|^p\) and \(\E|U|^p\) for iid real random variables \(U,V\).  For \(1<p\leq2\), the real line has \(p\)-negative type, giving the bound \(2\).  For \(p\geq2\), translating to the \(p\)-center and using the monotonicity inequality for \(t\mapsto |t|^{p-2}t\) gives the bound \(2^{p-1}\).  Rare spikes attain the first bound in the limit; Rademacher variables attain the second bound at every finite level.

\begin{lemma}[iid moment bound]\label{lem:iid}
Let \(1<p<\infty\), and let \(U,V\) be iid real random variables with finite \(p\)-th moment.  Then
\[
 \E|U-V|^p \leq
 \begin{cases}
 2\,\E|U|^p, & 1<p\leq2,\\
 2^{p-1}\,\E|U|^p, & 2\leq p<\infty.
 \end{cases}
\]
Both constants are sharp in the following sense: the first is approached by rare spikes, and the second is attained by a symmetric two-point Rademacher variable.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(1<p\leq2\), use the standard negative-type representation
\[
 |t|^p=C_p\int_0^\infty (1-\cos(st))s^{-1-p}\,ds,\qquad C_p>0.
\]
Hence the kernel \(|x-y|^p\) is conditionally negative definite on \(\R\).  Applying this to the signed measure \(\nu=\mathcal L(U)-\delta_0\), which has total mass zero, gives
\[
 0\geq\iint |x-y|^p\,d\nu(x)d\nu(y)
   = \E|U-V|^p-2\E|U|^p.
\]

Now suppose \(p\geq2\), and write \(\phi(t)=|t|^{p-2}t\).  For all real \(x,y\),
\[
 |x-y|^p\leq 2^{p-2}(x-y)(\phi(x)-\phi(y)).
\]
Indeed, after separating the cases \(xy\geq0\) and \(xy<0\), this follows respectively from
\[
 |a-b|^{p-1}\leq a^{p-1}-b^{p-1}\quad(a\geq b\geq0)
\]
and
\[
 (a+b)^{p-1}\leq2^{p-2}(a^{p-1}+b^{p-1})\quad(a,b\geq0).
\]
Let \(a\) be a minimizer of \(t\mapsto\E|U-t|^p\), and put \(Z=U-a\).  Then \(\E\phi(Z)=0\), and \(\E|Z|^p\leq\E|U|^p\).  If \(Z'\) is an independent copy of \(Z\), then
\[
\begin{aligned}
\E|U-V|^p
&=\E|Z-Z'|^p \\
&\leq 2^{p-2}\E\bigl[(Z-Z')(\phi(Z)-\phi(Z'))\bigr]\\
&=2^{p-1}\bigl(\E|Z|^p-\E Z\,\E\phi(Z)\bigr)\\
&=2^{p-1}\E|Z|^p
\leq 2^{p-1}\E|U|^p.
\end{aligned}
\]
Sharpness for \(1<p\leq2\) follows by taking \(U_N=N^{1/p}\) with probability \(1/N\) and \(U_N=0\) otherwise; then \(\E|U_N|^p=1\) and \(\E|U_N-U_N'|^p=2(1-1/N)\to2\).  Sharpness for \(p\geq2\) follows from \(U=\pm1\) with equal probabilities, for which \(\E|U-U'|^p=2^{p-1}\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:upper}
For the constants \(c_n\) of Proposition 55 in the source paper,
\[
 c_n^p \leq
 \begin{cases}
 2, & 1<p\leq2,\\
 2^{p-1}, & 2\leq p<\infty.
 \end{cases}
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(F\in L_p(\{0,1\}^n)\) with \(\|F\|_p=1\), and fix an admissible pair \(A,B\).  Condition on the coordinates belonging to \(A\cap B\).  Given those common coordinates, \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) are iid copies of the conditional distribution of \(F\) over the remaining coordinates.  Lemma \ref{lem:iid}, applied conditionally and then averaged over the common coordinates, gives the stated upper bounds.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:c}
For Proposition 55 of the source paper,
\[
 c=\sup_n c_n =
 \begin{cases}
 2^{1/p}, & 1<p\leq2,\\
 2^{1-1/p}, & 2\leq p<\infty.
 \end{cases}
\]
Moreover, if \(p\geq2\), then \(c_n=2^{1-1/p}\) for every \(n\geq1\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The upper bounds follow immediately from Proposition \ref{prop:upper}.

For \(p\geq2\), choose disjoint \(A,B\in[\kappa]^n\) and let \(F\) be a Rademacher function depending on one coordinate of \(\{0,1\}^n\).  Then \(T_A(F)\) and \(T_B(F)\) are independent Rademacher variables, so
\[
 \|T_A(F)-T_B(F)\|_p^p=2^{p-1}.
\]
Thus \(c_n^p=2^{p-1}\) for every \(n\).

It remains only to prove the lower bound for \(c\) when \(1<p<2\).  Let \(N=2^n\), choose disjoint \(A,B\), and let \(F\) take the value \(a=(N/2)^{1/p}\) on one atom of \(\{0,1\}^n\), the value \(-a\) on another atom, and \(0\) on all remaining atoms.  Then \(\|F\|_p=1\).  For independent copies \(U,V\) of \(F\),
\[
\E|U-V|^p
 = \frac{2(2a)^p+4(N-2)a^p}{N^2}
 = 2-\frac{4-2^p}{N}.
\]
Therefore \(c_n^p\geq 2-(4-2^p)/2^n\), and letting \(n\to\infty\) gives \(c^p\geq2\).  The upper bound gives \(c^p\leq2\), so \(c=2^{1/p}\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Finite \(c_n\) for \(1<p<2\)}

The proof above gives
\[
 2-\frac{4-2^p}{2^n}\leq c_n^p\leq2
\]
for \(1<p<2\).  Numerical optimization strongly suggests that the lower bound is the exact value for every \(n\), but this packet does not claim that finite extremal inequality.  The unresolved finite statement is:
\[
 \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{i,j=1}^N |x_i-x_j|^p
 \leq
 \left(2-\frac{4-2^p}{N}\right)\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N |x_i|^p
\]
for \(N=2^n\), \(1<p<2\), and all real \(x_1,\ldots,x_N\).  Equality would be attained by two opposite nonzero entries and \(N-2\) zero entries.

\section*{Scope and Limitations}

This packet answers the exact value of \(c\), and it answers the exact finite constants \(c_n\) for \(p\geq2\).  It does not prove the exact finite formula for \(c_n\) when \(1<p<2\), and it does not resolve whether \(S_{L_p(\mu)}\) is dichotomous for \(p\neq2\).
