\section*{Status}

This packet records a likely-valid counterexample to the second question in
Question 3.2 of Ciaci's paper \emph{On the transfinite symmetric strong
diameter two property} \cite{Ciaci2023}.  It gives a compact Hausdorff, hence
$T_4$ locally compact, space $K$ with $\dens(K)>\aleph_0$ such that
$C_0(K)=C(K)$ fails $\ASSD_{\dens(K)}$.  Thus the proposed density-only
positive statement for $\ASSD$ is false.

The packet does not answer the first question, namely whether $C_0(X)$ must
fail $\SSD_{\cellularity(X)^+}$ for every $T_4$ locally compact space $X$.

\section*{Source question}

The source asks whether Theorem 3.1 can be rewritten using only one cardinal
function:

\begin{quote}
Let $X$ be a $T_4$ locally compact space. Is it true that $C_0(X)$ fails the
$\SSD_{\cellularity(X)^+}$? Is it true that $C_0(X)$ enjoys the
$\ASSD_{\dens(X)}$, whenever $\dens(X)>\aleph_0$?
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions used}

All Banach spaces are real, as in the source paper.  For an infinite cardinal
$\kappa$, a Banach space $E$ has $\ASSD_{\kappa}$ if, for every set
$A\subset S_{E^*}$ with $|A|<\kappa$, there are a set $B\subset S_E$ norming
$A$ and a vector $y\in S_E$ such that $B\pm y\subset S_E$.  Here $B$ norms
$A$ means that for every $a^*\in A$ and every $0<r<1$ there is $b\in B$ with
$a^*(b)\ge r$.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

A strictly positive probability measure blocks the exact symmetric
$1$-ASSD2P requirement.  If $B\pm y$ stays in the unit sphere of $C(K)$, then
for every $f\in B$ the pointwise inequality
\[
        |f(t)|+|y(t)|\le 1
\]
holds.  A full-support measure $\mu$ sees every nonzero continuous $y$, so
$\int |y|\,d\mu>0$.  Consequently every such $f$ satisfies
\[
        \mu(f)\le 1-\int |y|\,d\mu < 1,
\]
and $B$ cannot norm the singleton $\{\mu\}$.

\section*{The counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
Let $\Gamma$ be a set with $|\Gamma|>\cardc=2^{\aleph_0}$, and put
$K=[0,1]^\Gamma$ with the product topology.  Then $K$ is a $T_4$ locally
compact space with $\dens(K)>\aleph_0$, but $C_0(K)=C(K)$ fails
$\ASSD_{\dens(K)}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The space $K$ is compact Hausdorff by Tychonoff's theorem.  In particular it
is normal and locally compact, and $C_0(K)=C(K)$.

First, $K$ is nonseparable.  Let $D\subset K$ be countable.  For each
$\gamma\in\Gamma$, define
\[
        v_\gamma=(x(\gamma))_{x\in D}\in [0,1]^D .
\]
Since $D$ is countable, $|[0,1]^D|=\cardc$.  Since $|\Gamma|>\cardc$, there
are distinct $\gamma,\eta\in\Gamma$ such that $v_\gamma=v_\eta$.  Hence
$x(\gamma)=x(\eta)$ for every $x\in D$.  Thus $D$ is contained in the closed
proper subset
\[
        F_{\gamma,\eta}=\{z\in K: z(\gamma)=z(\eta)\}.
\]
The set is proper because points with different $\gamma$ and $\eta$
coordinates exist.  Therefore $D$ is not dense in $K$.  No countable subset of
$K$ is dense, so $\dens(K)>\aleph_0$.

Let $\mu$ be the product of Lebesgue probability measures on the factors
$[0,1]$.  This regular Borel probability measure has full support: every
nonempty basic open cylinder restricts only finitely many coordinates and has
positive product measure.

Suppose, for contradiction, that $C(K)$ has $\ASSD_{\dens(K)}$.  Since
$\dens(K)>\aleph_0$, the singleton $A=\{\mu\}\subset S_{C(K)^*}$ has
cardinality $<\dens(K)$.  Hence there are $B\subset S_{C(K)}$ norming $A$ and
$y\in S_{C(K)}$ such that $B\pm y\subset S_{C(K)}$.

Fix $f\in B$ and $t\in K$.  Because both $f+y$ and $f-y$ are in the unit
sphere of $C(K)$, their sup norms are at most one.  Therefore
\[
        |f(t)+y(t)|\le 1,\qquad |f(t)-y(t)|\le 1.
\]
For real scalars $a,b$,
\[
        \max\{|a+b|,|a-b|\}=|a|+|b|.
\]
Applying this with $a=f(t)$ and $b=y(t)$ yields
\[
        |f(t)|+|y(t)|\le 1.
\]
In particular $f(t)\le |f(t)|\le 1-|y(t)|$ for every $t\in K$.  Hence
\[
        \mu(f)=\int_K f\,d\mu
        \le \int_K (1-|y|)\,d\mu
        =1-\int_K |y|\,d\mu .
\]
Since $y\in S_{C(K)}$, it is not identically zero.  Choose $t_0$ with
$|y(t_0)|>0$.  By continuity, $|y|\ge\delta>0$ on some nonempty open
neighborhood $U$ of $t_0$.  Full support gives $\mu(U)>0$, so
$\int_K |y|\,d\mu\ge \delta\mu(U)>0$.

Thus there is a constant $q<1$ such that $\mu(f)\le q$ for all $f\in B$.
This contradicts the assertion that $B$ norms $\{\mu\}$.  Therefore
$C(K)$ fails $\ASSD_{\dens(K)}$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Human review recommendation}

Review as a likely-valid counterexample to the second half of Question 3.2.
The key points to check are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item the source definition of $\ASSD_{\kappa}$ requires testing all
$A\subset S_{C(K)^*}$ of cardinality $<\kappa$, so the singleton
$\{\mu\}$ is admissible when $\dens(K)>\aleph_0$;
\item the product Lebesgue measure on $[0,1]^\Gamma$ is regular and strictly
positive on nonempty open subsets;
\item the scalar identity
$\max\{|a+b|,|a-b|\}=|a|+|b|$ is the only place where the real-scalar
convention is used.
\end{itemize}
