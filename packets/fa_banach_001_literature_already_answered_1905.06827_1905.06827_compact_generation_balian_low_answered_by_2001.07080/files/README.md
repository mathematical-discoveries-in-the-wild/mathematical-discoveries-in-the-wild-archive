# Compact-generation question answered by arXiv:2001.07080

Status: `literature_already_answered`

## Source question

Ulrik Enstad, *The Balian--Low theorem for locally compact abelian groups
and vector bundles*, arXiv:1905.06827.

In the introduction, on arXiv PDF page 3, the paper asks whether the
compact-generation hypothesis can be dropped from the Kaniuth--Kutyniok
zero theorem: for a second-countable locally compact abelian group with
noncompact identity component, must every continuous Zak transform have a
zero for every lattice?

## Explicit later answer

Ulrik Enstad, Mads S. Jakobsen, Franz Luef, and Tron Omland,
*Deformations and Balian--Low theorems for Gabor frames on the adeles*,
arXiv:2001.07080, answers yes.

The supporting paper says explicitly on arXiv PDF page 4 that it removes the
compact-generation assumption, and states this as Theorem 1.7.  The full
result is Theorem 5.8 on arXiv PDF page 24: for every locally compact abelian
group `G` and every lattice `Lambda`, the identity component of `G` is
noncompact if and only if every continuous `Lambda`-quasiperiodic function
has a zero; in particular every continuous Zak transform has a zero.

This completely answers the source question and is stronger than its
second-countable formulation.  The supporting authors explicitly use
Theorem 5.5 of arXiv:1905.06827 in their Proposition 5.7, so this is an
author-known answer, not a new implication discovered in this run.
