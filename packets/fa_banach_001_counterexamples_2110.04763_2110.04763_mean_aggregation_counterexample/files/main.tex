\section{Source and target}

Attias and Kontorovich study fat-shattering dimension under pointwise
aggregation of real-valued function classes \cite{AK}.  Their Theorem 3 gives
a dimension-free upper bound for Lipschitz aggregations of bounded affine
classes.  In Section 5, page 15 of the arXiv PDF, they state Conjecture 11:
for \(R_i\)-bounded affine classes \(F_i\) and an aggregation rule \(G\) that
is \(1\)-Lipschitz in supremum norm,
\begin{equation}
 \fat_\gamma(G(F_1,\ldots,F_k))
 \geq
 \frac{C\Log(k)}{\gamma^2}\sum_{i=1}^k R_i^2,
 \qquad
 0<\gamma<\min_i R_i,
 \label{eq:conjecture}
\end{equation}
where \(C>0\) is universal.  The same wording appears in the final JMLR
version as Conjecture 11, equation (29).

\section{Definitions}

Let \(H\) be a real Hilbert space and let \(B_H=\{x\in H:\|x\|\leq1\}\).
For \(R>0\), write
\[
 \A_R(H)
 =
 \bigl\{x\mapsto\langle w,x\rangle+b:
       w\in H,\ \|w\|\leq R,\ |b|\leq R\bigr\}.
\]
This is precisely the equal-radius instance of the bounded affine classes
used in the source paper (and includes its finite-dimensional setting).

A finite set \(\{x_1,\ldots,x_m\}\) is \(\gamma\)-shattered by a function
class \(F\) if there are thresholds \(r_1,\ldots,r_m\) such that for every
sign vector \(\varepsilon\in\{-1,1\}^m\) there is \(f_\varepsilon\in F\)
with
\[
 \varepsilon_i\bigl(f_\varepsilon(x_i)-r_i\bigr)\geq\gamma
 \quad (i=1,\ldots,m).
\]
The largest possible \(m\) is \(\fat_\gamma(F)\).

For \(k\geq1\), let
\[
 M_k(z_1,\ldots,z_k)=\frac1k\sum_{j=1}^k z_j
\]
be the arithmetic-mean aggregation.

\section{Proof intuition}

The proposed lower bound charges a term of order \(R_i^2\Log(k)\) for every
input class, regardless of how the aggregation uses those inputs.  Averaging
identical convex affine classes does not create any new functions: averaging
their parameters stays in the same parameter ball, and every original
function is recovered by putting it in all \(k\) coordinates.  Thus the
aggregate class is independent of \(k\).

For completeness, its fat dimension is bounded without invoking any external
entropy theorem.  Average the shattering inequalities over random signs.
The arbitrary threshold vector cancels in expectation, while the Hilbert
norm and intercept contributions are each at most \(R\sqrt m\).  This forces
\(m\leq4R^2/\gamma^2\), so the conjectured \(k\Log(k)\) growth is impossible.

\section{Counterexample}

\begin{lemma}[Fat dimension of a bounded affine class]
\label{lem:fat}
For every real Hilbert space \(H\), \(R>0\), and \(\gamma>0\),
\[
 \fat_\gamma(\A_R(H))\leq \frac{4R^2}{\gamma^2}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose \(x_1,\ldots,x_m\in B_H\) are \(\gamma\)-shattered with thresholds
\(r_1,\ldots,r_m\).  For each sign vector
\(\varepsilon\in\{-1,1\}^m\), choose
\(f_\varepsilon(x)=\langle w_\varepsilon,x\rangle+b_\varepsilon\in\A_R(H)\)
witnessing its labeling.  Summing the \(m\) shattering inequalities gives
\[
 m\gamma
 \leq
 \sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i
 \bigl(f_\varepsilon(x_i)-r_i\bigr)
 \leq
 \sup_{\substack{\|w\|\leq R\\|b|\leq R}}
 \sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i
 \bigl(\langle w,x_i\rangle+b-r_i\bigr).
\]
The supremum on the right is exactly
\[
 R\left\|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i x_i\right\|
 +R\left|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i\right|
 -\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i r_i.
\]
Now take expectation over independent uniform signs.  The last term has
expectation zero.  Therefore
\begin{align*}
 m\gamma
 &\leq
 R\,\mathbb E\left\|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i x_i\right\|
 +R\,\mathbb E\left|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i\right|\\
 &\leq
 R\left(\mathbb E\left\|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i x_i\right\|^2\right)^{1/2}
 +R\left(\mathbb E\left|\sum_{i=1}^m\varepsilon_i\right|^2\right)^{1/2}\\
 &=
 R\left(\sum_{i=1}^m\|x_i\|^2\right)^{1/2}+R\sqrt m
 \leq 2R\sqrt m.
\end{align*}
For \(m>0\), division by \(\sqrt m\,\gamma\) and squaring yield the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Arithmetic-mean counterexample]
\label{thm:counterexample}
For every \(k\geq1\),
\[
 M_k(\A_R(H),\ldots,\A_R(H))=\A_R(H),
\]
and \(M_k:\mathbb R^k\to\mathbb R\) has optimal Lipschitz constant \(1\)
with respect to the supremum norm.  Consequently, no universal
\(C>0\) can make \eqref{eq:conjecture} hold for all \(k\), all bounded
affine classes, and all \(1\)-Lipschitz aggregation rules.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For \(z,z'\in\mathbb R^k\),
\[
 |M_k(z)-M_k(z')|
 \leq \frac1k\sum_{j=1}^k|z_j-z_j'|
 \leq \|z-z'\|_\infty.
\]
Equality occurs when every coordinate difference is the same nonzero number,
so the optimal Lipschitz constant is exactly \(1\).

If \(f_j(x)=\langle w_j,x\rangle+b_j\in\A_R(H)\), then
\[
 M_k(f_1,\ldots,f_k)(x)
 =
 \left\langle\frac1k\sum_{j=1}^k w_j,x\right\rangle
 +\frac1k\sum_{j=1}^k b_j.
\]
The averaged parameters still satisfy
\[
 \left\|\frac1k\sum_j w_j\right\|\leq R,
 \qquad
 \left|\frac1k\sum_j b_j\right|\leq R.
\]
This proves
\(M_k(\A_R(H),\ldots,\A_R(H))\subseteq\A_R(H)\).
Conversely, every \(f\in\A_R(H)\) is the mean of the diagonal tuple
\((f,\ldots,f)\), proving equality.

Now set \(F_i=\A_R(H)\) and \(R_i=R\) for every \(i\).  Lemma
\ref{lem:fat} gives
\[
 \fat_\gamma(M_k(F_1,\ldots,F_k))
 =
 \fat_\gamma(\A_R(H))
 \leq \frac{4R^2}{\gamma^2}.
\]
But \eqref{eq:conjecture} would give
\[
 \fat_\gamma(M_k(F_1,\ldots,F_k))
 \geq
 \frac{Ck\Log(k)R^2}{\gamma^2}.
\]
Taking, for example, \(\gamma=R/2\), the first bound is at most \(16\),
whereas the second is \(4Ck\Log(k)\), which exceeds \(16\) for all
sufficiently large \(k\).  This contradicts every proposed universal
\(C>0\).
\end{proof}

\section{Interpretation and limitations}

The counterexample uses one of the paper's own canonical aggregation rules:
the mean.  It is symmetric, depends on every coordinate, is
shift-commuting, and has exact Lipschitz constant one.  Thus the obstruction
is not an artifact of a constant map or of an aggregation that ignores most
inputs.

The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The phrase ``Theorem 3 is tight up
to constants'' may have been intended existentially (some aggregation
attains the upper bound) or specifically for \(G_{\max}\).  The displayed
Conjecture 11, however, states the lower bound for an aggregation rule \(G\)
under only the \(1\)-Lipschitz hypothesis.  The arithmetic mean disproves
that universal formulation.  It says nothing against the max-specific lower
bound, which remains outside this packet.

\section{Verification and novelty check}

The proof has no computational or conditional dependency.  The two
substantive checks are the exact class identity under averaging and the
random-sign estimate in Lemma \ref{lem:fat}; both are written out above.

Before promotion, the run's registry and lightweight indexes were searched
for arXiv:2110.04763, the exact title, and the core bounded-affine aggregation
terms.  No duplicate result appeared.  The current arXiv source and the
official 2024 JMLR version were inspected; both retain Conjecture 11 in the
form shown in Figure \ref{fig:source}.  Bounded exact-phrase web searches for
the title, ``Conjecture 11,'' ``1-Lipschitz'' aggregation, and the displayed
lower bound found no correction, erratum, or later paper recording this
counterexample.  This is not an exhaustive novelty guarantee.  Because the
argument is elementary and may identify an unintended quantifier, novelty
confidence is moderate; confidence in the literal mathematical
counterexample is high.

\section{Human-review recommendation}

The recommended review is to confirm the intended quantifier in Conjecture
11.  If the printed universal reading is intended, Theorem
\ref{thm:counterexample} is a complete counterexample.  If the intended
claim is max-specific or merely existential sharpness of Theorem 3, the
source statement should be narrowed and this packet should be classified as
a statement-level counterexample rather than a refutation of that intended
variant.
