\paragraph{Source problem.}
Let \(\C\subset\R_+^2\) be a Cantor dust with uniform dissection
ratio \(1/4\).  Does
\[
  \bigl(e^\lambda B\times e^\mu B\bigr)_{(\lambda,\mu)\in\C}

\section*{Definitions and the characterization used}

Here a uniform four-corner Cantor dust means the usual attractor obtained
from an axis-parallel square \(Q_\varnothing\subset(0,\infty)^2\) by retaining,
inside every square, its four corner subsquares of side one quarter of the
parent side.  Write \(L>0\) for the side of \(Q_\varnothing\).  At level
\(m\), the dust is contained in \(4^m\) axis-parallel squares \(Q_w\),
\(w\in\{0,1,2,3\}^m\), each of side \(L4^{-m}\).

We use Theorem 2.2 of Bayart--Costa--Menet
\cite{BayartCostaMenet}.  In the case \(d=2\), it says that the family
\[
  \bigl(e^{\lambda(1)}B\times e^{\lambda(2)}B\bigr)_{\lambda\in\Lambda}
\]
on \(c_0\times c_0\) has a common hypercyclic vector if and only if the
following condition holds.  For every \(\tau>0\), every \(N\geq1\), and
every compact \(K\subset\Lambda\), there are
\[
  N\leq n_1<n_1+N\leq n_2<\cdots<n_{q-1}+N\leq n_q
\]
and tags \(\lambda_k\in(0,\infty)^2\) such that
\begin{align}
 K&\subset\bigcup_{k=1}^q
 \prod_{i=1}^2
 \left[\lambda_k(i)-\frac{\tau}{n_k},\lambda_k(i)\right],
 \label{eq:cover}\\
 \lambda_{k+1}(i)n_{k+1}-\lambda_k(i)n_k&\geq N
 \qquad(i=1,2;\ 1\leq k<q).
 \label{eq:ordered}
\end{align}
The tags need not themselves belong to \(\Lambda\).

\section*{Proof intuition}

A level-\(m\) listing of all \(4^m\) dust squares has a logarithmic
accumulation of jumps: each generation contributes a bounded amount of
variation.  This is exactly why using one fixed resolution loses at the
endpoint.

The remedy is to use many short blocks at gradually increasing resolutions.
At resolution \(m\), cover only a symbolic portion comparable to
\(\tau/m\).  The accumulated jump variation within such a block is then
uniformly bounded, so the times required by \eqref{eq:ordered} grow by at
most a fixed factor.  Moving from \(m\) to \(m+s\), for one fixed integer
\(s\), supplies exactly that fixed-factor room.  The portions covered are
comparable to
\[
  \frac{\tau}{m_0},\quad
  \frac{\tau}{m_0+s},\quad
  \frac{\tau}{m_0+2s},\ \ldots.
\]
Their sum diverges.  Consequently, after finitely many blocks the whole
symbolic Cantor dust is covered.  This converts the unavoidable jumps into a
harmonic cost instead of trying to eliminate them.

\section*{Two estimates}

Order the words in \(\{0,1,2,3\}^m\) lexicographically.  This order is
consistent under refinement: every level-\(\ell\) cylinder is a consecutive
block of \(4^{m-\ell}\) level-\(m\) cylinders.  Let \(u_w\) be the
upper-right corner of \(Q_w\).

\begin{lemma}[Variation of a consecutive dust block]\label{lem:variation}
Let \(w_r,\ldots,w_{r+q-1}\) be \(q\) consecutive level-\(m\) words.  Then
\[
 \sum_{j=r}^{r+q-2}\|u_{w_{j+1}}-u_{w_j}\|_\infty
 \leq 4L\,m\frac{q}{4^m}+\frac{8L}{3}.
\]
If one prepends one arbitrary tag from \(Q_\varnothing\), the right-hand
side may be replaced by
\[
 4L\,m\frac{q}{4^m}+\frac{11L}{3}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For two consecutive level-\(m\) words, let \(h\) be the length of their
common prefix.  Their tags lie in the same level-\(h\) square, and hence
their distance is at most \(L4^{-h}\).  Charge this jump to every level
\(\ell=h+1,\ldots,m\), with charge \(L4^{-(\ell-1)}\).

For a fixed \(\ell\), a consecutive block of \(q\) level-\(m\) words crosses
at most
\[
  \frac{q}{4^{m-\ell}}+2
\]
boundaries of level-\(\ell\) blocks.  Summing the charges gives
\begin{align*}
 \sum_{j=r}^{r+q-2}\|u_{w_{j+1}}-u_{w_j}\|_\infty
 &\leq
 \sum_{\ell=1}^m L4^{-(\ell-1)}
 \left(\frac{q}{4^{m-\ell}}+2\right)\\
 &\leq 4L\,m\frac{q}{4^m}+\frac{8L}{3}.
\end{align*}
All tags lie in the initial square, so prepending one tag adds at most
\(L\) in the sup norm.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Scheduling tags]\label{lem:schedule}
Suppose all coordinates of the tags \(\lambda_0,\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_q\)
are at least \(a>0\).  Starting from an integer \(n_0\), define \(n_j\) to
be the least integer satisfying
\[
 n_j\geq n_{j-1}+N,\qquad
 n_j\lambda_j(i)\geq n_{j-1}\lambda_{j-1}(i)+N
 \quad(i=1,2).
\]
There is a constant \(A=A(a,N)\geq1\) such that
\[
 n_q\leq
 \left(n_0+Aq\right)
 \exp\left(A\sum_{j=1}^q
 \|\lambda_j-\lambda_{j-1}\|_\infty\right).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put \(d_j=\|\lambda_j-\lambda_{j-1}\|_\infty\).  The definition and the
lower coordinate bound give, for a sufficiently large
\(A=A(a,N)\),
\[
 n_j\leq n_{j-1}+A(1+n_{j-1}d_j)
       =(1+Ad_j)n_{j-1}+A.
\]
Iterating this affine inequality and using
\(\prod_j(1+Ad_j)\leq\exp(A\sum_jd_j)\) yields the claim.
\end{proof}

\section*{Main result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let \(\C\subset\R_+^2\) be a uniform axis-parallel four-corner Cantor dust
with dissection ratio \(1/4\).  Then
\[
 \bigcap_{(\lambda,\mu)\in\C}
 \HC(e^\lambda B\times e^\mu B)\neq\varnothing
\]
on \(c_0\times c_0\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(\tau>0\) and \(N\geq1\).  It is enough to verify
\eqref{eq:cover}--\eqref{eq:ordered} for all of \(\C\), rather than for one
compact subset.  Replacing \(\tau\) by a smaller number only strengthens the
required cover, so put
\[
 \varepsilon=\min\{\tau,L\},\qquad
 \theta=\frac{\varepsilon}{L}\in(0,1].
\]
All coordinates of all level tags are bounded below by some \(a>0\).
Let \(A=A(a,N)\) be supplied by Lemma~\ref{lem:schedule}, and set
\[
 V_*=\frac{23L}{3},\qquad P=e^{AV_*},\qquad
 \eta=\frac1{4P}.
\]
Choose an integer \(s\geq1\) so that
\[
 4^s\geq2P.
\]
Choose \(m_0\) sufficiently large that
\begin{equation}
 N\leq\eta\theta4^{m_0},\qquad
 m_0\geq4PA,\qquad
 \frac{\theta4^{m_0}}{m_0}\geq2.
\label{eq:m0}
\end{equation}
We construct finitely many consecutive symbolic blocks.  At stage \(j\),
use resolution
\[
 m_j=m_0+js.
\]
The endpoint of every earlier stage is a level-\(m_j\) grid point.  If
\(R_j\) level-\(m_j\) cylinders remain, take the next
\begin{equation}
 q_j=\min\left\{
 R_j,\left\lfloor\frac{\theta4^{m_j}}{m_j}\right\rfloor
 \right\}
\label{eq:qj}
\end{equation}
cylinders in lexicographic order, and tag them by their upper-right corners.

We schedule the tags consecutively using Lemma~\ref{lem:schedule}.  For the
very first tag, use a dummy preceding tag equal to it and dummy time \(N\);
the resulting first actual time is at least \(2N\), which is harmless.
At every later stage, the preceding tag and time are the last ones from the
previous stage.

We claim inductively that, at the start of a stage of resolution \(m\), its
incoming time \(n_{\rm in}\) satisfies
\begin{equation}
 n_{\rm in}\leq\eta\theta4^m.
\label{eq:invariant}
\end{equation}
This holds initially by \eqref{eq:m0}.  From \eqref{eq:qj} and
\(\theta\leq1\),
\[
 m\frac{q_j}{4^m}\leq\theta\leq1.
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:variation}, including the incoming transition, therefore
bounds the tag variation during this stage by
\[
 4L+\frac{11L}{3}=V_*.
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:schedule} and \(m\geq m_0\geq4PA\) give
\begin{align}
 n_{\rm out}
 &\leq P(n_{\rm in}+Aq_j)\\
 &\leq
 P\eta\theta4^m+
 PA\frac{\theta4^m}{m}
 \leq \frac12\theta4^m.
\label{eq:out}
\end{align}
In particular, every time used during this stage satisfies
\[
 n_k\leq\theta4^m.
\]
Since its cylinder square has side \(L4^{-m}\), we have
\[
 L4^{-m}\leq\frac{\varepsilon}{n_k}\leq\frac{\tau}{n_k}.
\]
Thus the rectangle in \eqref{eq:cover}, with tag equal to the upper-right
corner, contains the whole part of \(\C\) in that cylinder.

Moreover, \eqref{eq:out} and \(4^s\geq2P\) imply
\[
 n_{\rm out}\leq\frac12\theta4^m
 \leq\eta\theta4^{m+s},
\]
which proves the invariant for the next stage.  By construction of the
times, their gaps are at least \(N\) and \eqref{eq:ordered} holds.

It remains to prove that only finitely many stages are needed.  If a stage
does not finish the cover, then \eqref{eq:m0} and \eqref{eq:qj} imply
\[
 \frac{q_j}{4^{m_j}}
 \geq\frac{\theta}{2m_j}.
\]
But
\[
 \sum_{j=0}^{\infty}\frac{\theta}{2(m_0+js)}=\infty.
\]
Hence the successive symbolic portions cannot all fit inside a total
symbolic interval of length \(1\).  Some finite stage must take all remaining
cylinders.  The resulting finite concatenation covers \(\C\) and satisfies
\eqref{eq:cover}--\eqref{eq:ordered}.  Theorem 2.2 of
Bayart--Costa--Menet now gives the required common hypercyclic vector.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
The same conclusion holds with \(c_0\) replaced by \(\ell_p\) for any
\(1\leq p<\infty\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The characterization used above holds verbatim for \(\ell_p\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item The proof uses only the exact necessary-and-sufficient characterization
  in Theorem 2.2 of \cite{BayartCostaMenet}; no unproved dynamical criterion
  is introduced.
\item The critical accounting is explicit: a block of symbolic length
  \(q/4^m\) costs at most \(O(1+m q/4^m)\) tag variation.
  Choosing \(q/4^m\asymp\theta/m\) makes this cost uniform.
\item The stage-time invariant is checked in \eqref{eq:invariant}--\eqref{eq:out}.
  The fixed resolution increment \(s\) absorbs the uniform multiplicative
  scheduling cost, and the harmonic divergence proves finite termination.
\item Exploratory code in \texttt{code/finite\_traversal\_probe.cpp} implements
  a different greedy Hilbert-prefix schedule.  For \(\tau=0.1\), it advanced
  through \(0.877709756\) of symbolic mass after \(10^9\) pieces.  This was a
  stress test that motivated the block proof; it is not used as evidence for
  any formal step.
\end{itemize}
