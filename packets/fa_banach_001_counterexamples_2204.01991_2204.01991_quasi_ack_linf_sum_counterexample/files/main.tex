\title{A Counterexample to $\ell_\infty$-Stability of Quasi-$\ACK$ Structure}
\author{Research report}
\date{June 23, 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Choi and Jung asked whether arbitrary $\ell_\infty$-sums of Banach spaces
with quasi-$\ACK$ structure again have quasi-$\ACK$ structure, and whether
the analogous assertion holds for property quasi-$\beta$.  We first verify
the supplied partial result: a uniform bound below one on the active
$\rho$-values upgrades each quasi-$\ACK$ witness to an ordinary
$\ACK_r$ witness, after which the standard coordinate argument applies.
We then give a countable real counterexample to the unrestricted question.
The summands are two-dimensional regular-polygon spaces $X_n$, each having
property $\beta$ (indeed $\ACK_{\cos(\pi/m_n)}$ structure), where
$m_n\to\infty$.  Nevertheless,
\[
   Z=\Bigl(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty X_n\Bigr)_{\ell_\infty}
\]
does not have quasi-$\ACK$ structure.  The proof identifies every extreme
point of $B_{Z^*}$ with an extreme functional in an ultrafilter fiber.  Free
ultrafilters see the Euclidean limit of the polygonal norms.  A local
consequence of the quasi-$\ACK$ axioms is then incompatible with the round
geometry of that limit fiber.  Consequently, arbitrary $\ell_\infty$-sum
stability fails for both quasi-$\ACK$ structure and property quasi-$\beta$
over the real field.
\end{abstract}

\section{The question and the conclusion}

In Remarks 3.4 and 4.8 of Choi--Jung~\cite{ChoiJung}, the following question
is left open: if every member of a family $(X_i)_{i\in I}$ has quasi-$\ACK$
structure, must $(\bigoplus_{i\in I}X_i)_{\ell_\infty}$ have quasi-$\ACK$
structure?  The paper also asks the same question for property quasi-$\beta$.
Finite $\ell_\infty$-sums cause no difficulty, since they are finite
$c_0$-sums; the issue is genuinely infinite.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The answer for arbitrary families is negative.

\begin{theorem}[Main result]\label{thm:main}
There is a sequence $(X_n)_{n\ge1}$ of two-dimensional real Banach spaces
such that every $X_n$ has property $\beta$, but
\[
   \Bigl(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty X_n\Bigr)_{\ell_\infty}
\]
does not have quasi-$\ACK$ structure.  Hence neither quasi-$\ACK$ structure
nor property quasi-$\beta$ is stable under arbitrary $\ell_\infty$-sums.
\end{theorem}

The proof is self-contained apart from standard forms of Krein--Milman and
Milman's converse.  Since the argument appears not to have been published in
the literature located for this report, it should be regarded as a new,
unrefereed proof and checked independently before citation.

\section{Definitions}

All spaces in the counterexample are real.  A set $\Gamma\subset B_{X^*}$
is \emph{$1$-norming} if
\[
   \|x\|=\sup_{x^*\in\Gamma}|x^*(x)|\qquad(x\in X).
\]
For a subset $A$ of a vector space, $\aco(A)$ denotes its absolutely convex
hull.

\begin{definition}[Choi--Jung]\label{def:qack}
A Banach space $X$ has \emph{quasi-$\ACK$ structure} if there are a
$1$-norming set $\Gamma\subset B_{X^*}$ and a function
$\rho:\Gamma\to[0,1)$ such that, for every
$e^*\in\Ext(B_{X^*})$, there is a set $\Gamma_{e^*}\subset\Gamma$ with
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item $e^*\in\overline{\T\Gamma_{e^*}}^{w^*}$;
\item $\sup_{x^*\in\Gamma_{e^*}}\rho(x^*)<1$;
\item for every $\eps>0$ and every nonempty relatively weak-star open
      $U\subset\Gamma_{e^*}$, there are a nonempty $V\subset U$,
      $x_1^*\in V$, $e\in S_X$, and $F\in\Lin(X)$ such that
      \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)$'$},leftmargin=2.6em]
      \item $\|F(e)\|=\|F\|=1$;
      \item $x_1^*(F(e))=1$;
      \item $F^*x_1^*=x_1^*$;
      \item with
      \[
      V_1=\bigl\{x^*\in\Gamma:\
      \|F^*x^*\|+(1-\eps)\|x^*-F^*x^*\|\le1\bigr\},
      \]
      one has $|x^*(F(e))|\le\rho(x_1^*)$ for every
      $x^*\in\Gamma\setminus V_1$;
      \item $\dist(F^*x^*,\aco(\{0\}\cup V))<\eps$ for every
      $x^*\in\Gamma$;
      \item $|v^*(e)-1|\le\eps$ for every $v^*\in V$.
      \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
Here $\T=\{-1,1\}$ in the real case.
\end{definition}

We use only the standard fact that property $\beta$ implies quasi-$\ACK$
structure; in fact it gives $\ACK_\rho$ structure for a fixed
$\rho<1$~\cite{CascalesEtAl,ChoiJung}.

\section{Audit of the supplied partial result}

The conditional theorem in the supplied packet is correct.  We record it in
a slightly streamlined form.

\begin{proposition}[Uniform active bound]\label{prop:uniform}
Suppose that $X_i$ has quasi-$\ACK$ data
$(\Gamma_i,\rho_i,(\Gamma_{i,e^*})_{e^*})$, and put
\[
 \Delta_i=
 \left(\bigcup_{e^*\in\Ext(B_{X_i^*})}\Gamma_{i,e^*}\right)\setminus\{0\}.
\]
If
\[
  r:=\sup_i\sup_{x^*\in\Delta_i}\rho_i(x^*)<1,
\]
then $(\bigoplus_iX_i)_{\ell_\infty}$ has $\ACK_r$ structure.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We first check the only nonstandard step.  For one space $X$, let
$\Delta$ be the corresponding active union.  Removing $0$ does not affect
the weak-star approximation of a norm-one extreme point.  Thus every
$e^*\in\Ext(B_{X^*})$ lies in
$\overline{\T\Delta}^{w^*}$.  Krein--Milman gives
\[
 B_{X^*}
 =\overline{\operatorname{co}\Ext(B_{X^*})}^{w^*}
 \subset \overline{\aco(\Delta)}^{w^*}\subset B_{X^*}.
\]
Consequently $\Delta$ is $1$-norming.

Let $U$ be a nonempty relatively weak-star open subset of $\Delta$ and pick
$u^*\in U$.  Some $e_0^*$ satisfies
$u^*\in\Gamma_{e_0^*}$.  Then
$U\cap\Gamma_{e_0^*}$ is nonempty and relatively weak-star open in
$\Gamma_{e_0^*}$.  Apply the local quasi-$\ACK$ axioms there.  Restricting
the ambient norming set from $\Gamma$ to $\Delta$ preserves all six
conditions, while condition (iv)$'$ improves to the constant estimate
$\rho(x_1^*)\le r$.  Hence $X$ has $\ACK_r$ structure with norming set
$\Delta$.

Apply this to every $X_i$.  The set
\[
  \Delta=\bigcup_i P_i^*(\Delta_i)
\]
is $1$-norming for the $\ell_\infty$-sum.  Given a relatively weak-star
open subset of $\Delta$, choose one of its points and work in that coordinate.
Extend the resulting vector and operator by zero in all other coordinates.
For a functional supported in another coordinate, both its value on $F(e)$
and its image under $F^*$ are zero, so conditions (iv)$'$ and (v)$'$ are
immediate.  The remaining conditions are exactly those in the chosen
summand.  This proves $\ACK_r$ structure.
\end{proof}

The second paragraph is also an immediate application of
Choi--Jung's Proposition 4.9 once the first paragraph has upgraded every
summand to $\ACK_r$.  Thus the supplied result is valid; the missing
uniformity is precisely where its proof cannot apply.  The counterexample
below has natural constants tending to one.

\section{Regular-polygon summands}

Choose strictly increasing integers $m_n\to\infty$, with $m_n\ge4$, and put
\[
  h_n=\frac{\pi}{m_n},\qquad
  u_{n,k}=(\cos(kh_n),\sin(kh_n))
  \quad(0\le k<m_n).
\]
On $\R^2$ define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:polygon-norm}
  \|x\|_n=\max_{0\le k<m_n}|\ip{x}{u_{n,k}}|,
  \qquad X_n=(\R^2,\|\cdot\|_n).
\end{equation}
We identify Euclidean vectors and Euclidean linear functionals.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:polygon}
For every $n$ and $x\in\R^2$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:norm-comparison}
  \cos(h_n/2)|x|_2\le\|x\|_n\le |x|_2.
\end{equation}
Moreover, $X_n$ has property $\beta$.  One may use the pairs
\[
 (u_{n,k},u_{n,k})\qquad(0\le k<m_n)
\]
and the parameter
\[
  \rho_n=\cos h_n<1.
\]
In particular, every $X_n$ has quasi-$\ACK$ structure, while
$\rho_n\uparrow1$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The lines spanned by the $u_{n,k}$ divide the projective unit circle into
arcs of angular length $h_n$.  A Euclidean unit vector is within angle
$h_n/2$ of one of these lines, proving \eqref{eq:norm-comparison}.

The polar of the unit ball in \eqref{eq:polygon-norm} is
\[
  B_{X_n^*}=\operatorname{co}\{\pm u_{n,k}:0\le k<m_n\},
\]
a regular $2m_n$-gon; in particular each $u_{n,k}$ has dual norm one.
Also $\|u_{n,k}\|_n=1$ and
$\ip{u_{n,k}}{u_{n,k}}=1$.  If $j\ne k$, then the angular separation of
the corresponding unoriented lines is at least $h_n$, whence
\[
 |\ip{u_{n,j}}{u_{n,k}}|\le\cos h_n.
\]
The definition of the norm says that the functionals are $1$-norming.
These are exactly the property-$\beta$ requirements.
\end{proof}

Set
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Z}
   Z=\Bigl(\bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty X_n\Bigr)_{\ell_\infty}.
\end{equation}
By \eqref{eq:norm-comparison}, a member $z=(z_n)$ of $Z$ is a uniformly
Euclidean-bounded sequence.

\section{Ultrafilter fibers and the extreme points of $B_{Z^*}$}

Let $\beta\N$ be the Stone--\v{C}ech compactification of $\N$, viewed as the
space of ultrafilters.  For $p\in\beta\N$, define the fiber
\[
 X_p=
 \begin{cases}
   X_n,&p=n\text{ is principal},\\
   H:=(\R^2,|\cdot|_2),&p\text{ is free}.
 \end{cases}
\]
For $z=(z_n)\in Z$, let
\[
  Q_pz=\lim_{n\to p}z_n\in\R^2,
\]
the coordinatewise ultralimit.  If $p=n$, this is simply $z_n$.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:fiber-quotient}
For every $p\in\beta\N$, $Q_p:Z\to X_p$ is a norm-one quotient map, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fiber-norm-limit}
  \|Q_pz\|_{X_p}=\lim_{n\to p}\|z_n\|_n\qquad(z\in Z).
\end{equation}
For a free $p$, the constant-sequence map
\[
  J:H\longrightarrow Z,\qquad Jx=(x,x,\ldots),
\]
is an isometry and satisfies $Q_pJ=I_H$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The norms $\|\cdot\|_n$ converge uniformly to $|\cdot|_2$ on the Euclidean
unit ball by \eqref{eq:norm-comparison}.  Since every $z\in Z$ is uniformly
Euclidean bounded,
\[
   \bigl|\|z_n\|_n-|z_n|_2\bigr|\longrightarrow0.
\]
For a free $p$, continuity of the Euclidean norm therefore gives
\[
 \lim_{n\to p}\|z_n\|_n
 =\lim_{n\to p}|z_n|_2
 =\left|\lim_{n\to p}z_n\right|_2.
\]
The principal case is immediate, proving \eqref{eq:fiber-norm-limit} and
$\|Q_p\|\le1$.

If $p=n$ is principal, the injection supported in coordinate $n$ is an
isometric right inverse.  If $p$ is free, then
\[
  \|Jx\|=\sup_n\|x\|_n=|x|_2,
\]
because $\|x\|_n\le|x|_2$ and $\|x\|_n\to|x|_2$.  Thus $J$ is an
isometric right inverse of $Q_p$.  Hence every $Q_p$ is a norm-one quotient.
\end{proof}

The next proposition is the structural core of the argument.

\begin{proposition}[Extreme-point formula]\label{prop:extreme}
One has
\begin{equation}\label{eq:extreme-formula}
 \Ext(B_{Z^*})
 =\bigl\{Q_p^*v^*:p\in\beta\N,
          \ v^*\in\Ext(B_{X_p^*})\bigr\}.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For $A\subset\N$, let $P_A$ be the coordinate projection onto $A$.  Since
\[
 Z=P_AZ\oplus_\infty P_{A^c}Z,
\]
we have the canonical $\ell_1$-decomposition
\[
 Z^*=(P_AZ)^*\oplus_1(P_{A^c}Z)^*.
\]
If $\phi\in\Ext(B_{Z^*})$, then for every $A$ exactly one of the two
components of $\phi$ is zero.  Equivalently, $\phi$ is supported on exactly
one of $A$ and $A^c$, where ``supported on $A$'' means
$\phi=\phi P_A$.  The collection
\[
   p_\phi=\{A\subset\N:\phi=\phi P_A\}
\]
is an ultrafilter: it chooses exactly one of $A,A^c$, is upward closed, and
is closed under finite intersections.

Put $p=p_\phi$.  If $Q_pz=0$, then for every $\eta>0$ the set
\[
 A_\eta=\{n:\|z_n\|_n<\eta\}
\]
belongs to $p$, by \eqref{eq:fiber-norm-limit}.  Since $\phi$ is supported
on $A_\eta$,
\[
  |\phi(z)|=|\phi(P_{A_\eta}z)|\le\eta.
\]
Thus $\phi$ annihilates $\ker Q_p$ and factors uniquely as
$\phi=Q_p^*v^*$ for some $v^*\in B_{X_p^*}$.  Since $Q_p$ is a quotient,
$\|v^*\|=\|\phi\|=1$.  If $v^*$ were not extreme, its nontrivial midpoint
decomposition would pull back to one of $\phi$; hence $v^*$ is extreme.
This proves the inclusion from left to right in \eqref{eq:extreme-formula}.

Conversely, let $v^*\in\Ext(B_{X_p^*})$ and suppose
\[
 Q_p^*v^*=\frac{\psi_1+\psi_2}{2},\qquad \psi_1,\psi_2\in B_{Z^*}.
\]
Fix $A\in p$ and decompose $\psi_j=\psi_{j,A}+\psi_{j,A^c}$.  The average
has norm one and is entirely supported on $A$.  Therefore
\[
 1\le\frac{\|\psi_{1,A}\|+\|\psi_{2,A}\|}{2}
 \le1-\frac{\|\psi_{1,A^c}\|+\|\psi_{2,A^c}\|}{2}\le1.
\]
It follows that both $A^c$-components vanish.  Since this holds for every
$A\in p$, the preceding kernel argument shows that each $\psi_j$ factors
through $Q_p$, say $\psi_j=Q_p^*v_j^*$ with $v_j^*\in B_{X_p^*}$.
Extremality of $v^*$ gives $v_1^*=v_2^*=v^*$, proving the converse.
\end{proof}

For a principal fiber, $\Ext(B_{X_n^*})=\{\pm u_{n,k}\}$; for a free fiber,
$\Ext(B_{H^*})$ is the Euclidean unit circle.

\section{A localization inequality forced by quasi-$\ACK$}

The following consequence of the axioms is useful independently of the
particular polygonal construction.

\begin{lemma}[Localization inequality]\label{lem:localization}
Let $X$ have a $1$-norming set $\Gamma\subset B_{X^*}$ and a function
$\rho:\Gamma\to[0,1)$.  Suppose that, for some $0<\eps<1$, nonempty
$V\subset\Gamma$, $x_1^*\in V$, $e\in S_X$, and $F\in\Lin(X)$, conditions
\textup{(i)$'$}--\textup{(v)$'$} of Definition~\ref{def:qack} hold.  Put
\[
 y=F(e),\qquad
 D=\overline{\aco(J^*V)}\subset E^*,
\]
where $J:E\to X$ is any contraction from a finite-dimensional Banach space.
If $\psi\in\Ext(B_{X^*})$ and
\[
  a:=\psi(y)>\rho(x_1^*),
\]
then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:localization}
  \dist(J^*\psi,D)
  \le \eps+\frac{1-a}{1-\eps}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since $\Gamma$ is $1$-norming,
$B_{X^*}=\overline{\aco(\Gamma)}^{w^*}$.  Milman's converse to
Krein--Milman yields a net $(\eta_\lambda)\subset\T\Gamma$ converging
weak-star to $\psi$.  Eventually
$\eta_\lambda(y)>\rho(x_1^*)$.  Write
$\eta_\lambda=\theta_\lambda\gamma_\lambda$ with
$\theta_\lambda\in\T$ and $\gamma_\lambda\in\Gamma$.  Condition (iv)$'$
and its invariance under signs imply that $\gamma_\lambda\in V_1$, and hence
\[
 \|\eta_\lambda-F^*\eta_\lambda\|
 \le\frac{1-\|F^*\eta_\lambda\|}{1-\eps}
 \le\frac{1-|\eta_\lambda(y)|}{1-\eps}.
\]
Condition (v)$'$ also remains valid after multiplication by a sign, since
$\aco(\{0\}\cup V)$ is balanced.  Applying the contraction $J^*$ gives
\[
 \dist(J^*\eta_\lambda,D)
 <\eps+\frac{1-|\eta_\lambda(y)|}{1-\eps}.
\]
The space $E^*$ is finite-dimensional, so weak-star convergence after
restriction by $J^*$ is norm convergence.  Passing to the limit proves
\eqref{eq:localization}.
\end{proof}

Geometrically, equation~\eqref{eq:localization} says that any extreme functional that
nearly norms $y$ must restrict close to the absolutely convex hull of the
small local set $V$.

\section{The counterexample}

We now prove Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.  Suppose, toward a contradiction, that the
space $Z$ in \eqref{eq:Z} has quasi-$\ACK$ data
$(\Gamma,\rho,(\Gamma_{e^*})_{e^*})$.

Fix a free ultrafilter $p$ and let $u_0=(1,0)\in H$.  By
Proposition~\ref{prop:extreme},
\[
  \phi_0=Q_p^*u_0\in\Ext(B_{Z^*}).
\]
Put $A=\Gamma_{\phi_0}$ and
\[
  r=\sup_{\gamma\in A}\rho(\gamma)<1.
\]
Since $\phi_0\in\overline{\T A}^{w^*}$ and $\T=\{-1,1\}$, either
$\phi_0\in\overline A^{w^*}$ or
$-\phi_0\in\overline A^{w^*}$.  Choose $\sigma\in\{-1,1\}$ so that, with
\[
  u=\sigma u_0,
  \qquad \phi=Q_p^*u,
\]
we have $\phi\in\overline A^{w^*}$.

Choose an angle $\alpha\in(0,\pi/2)$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:alpha}
  \cos\alpha>r.
\end{equation}
The strict inequality
\[
  \sin\alpha>1-\cos\alpha
\]
holds for every $\alpha\in(0,\pi/2)$.  By continuity, we may choose
$\tau>0$ so small that
\begin{align}
 &0<\tau<\min\{\alpha,1/2\},\qquad
   \alpha+2\tau<\frac\pi2,\qquad
   \cos(\alpha+\tau)>r,                                      \label{eq:tau1}\\
 &\sin(\alpha-\tau)-\tau
   >\tau+\frac{1-\cos(\alpha+\tau)}{1-\tau}.                 \label{eq:tau2}
\end{align}
Choose $N$ so that $h_n<\tau$ for $n\ge N$.  Next choose
$0<\delta<\tau$ with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:delta}
  \arccos(1-\delta)<\tau,
\end{equation}
and finally choose $0<\eps<\tau$.

Let $J:H\to Z$ be the constant-sequence isometry from
Lemma~\ref{lem:fiber-quotient}, and define the tail vector
\[
 w^{(N)}_n=
 \begin{cases}
  0,&n<N,\\
  u,&n\ge N.
 \end{cases}
\]
Because $u=\pm(1,0)$, one has $\|u\|_n=1$ for every $n$, so
$w^{(N)}\in S_Z$ and $\phi(w^{(N)})=1$.

Consider the relatively weak-star open subset of $A$
\begin{equation}\label{eq:U}
 U=\left\{\gamma\in A:
     \|J^*\gamma-u\|_2<\delta,
     \ \gamma(w^{(N)})>1-\delta\right\}.
\end{equation}
It is nonempty because $\phi\in\overline A^{w^*}$, while
$J^*\phi=u$ and $\phi(w^{(N)})=1$.  Apply the local quasi-$\ACK$ condition
to $U$ and $\eps$.  We obtain
\[
 \varnothing\ne V\subset U,
 \quad x_1^*\in V,
 \quad e\in S_Z,
 \quad F\in\Lin(Z).
\]
Put $y=F(e)$.  Then $\|y\|=1$ and $x_1^*(y)=1$.

The set
\[
  \mathcal F_y=\{\psi\in B_{Z^*}:\psi(y)=1\}
\]
is a weak-star compact face of $B_{Z^*}$ containing $x_1^*$.  Since
$x_1^*(w^{(N)})>1-\delta$, Krein--Milman applied to this face gives
$\psi_0\in\Ext(\mathcal F_y)\subset\Ext(B_{Z^*})$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:psi0}
  \psi_0(w^{(N)})>1-\delta.
\end{equation}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:extreme}, write
\[
  \psi_0=Q_q^*v_0,
  \qquad v_0\in\Ext(B_{X_q^*})
\]
for some $q\in\beta\N$.  Inequality \eqref{eq:psi0} forces $q$ to contain
the cofinite tail $\{n\ge N\}$; hence $q$ is free or it is a principal
ultrafilter $n\ge N$.  Moreover,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:v0}
  v_0(u)>1-\delta,
  \qquad t:=Q_qy\in S_{X_q},
  \qquad v_0(t)=1.
\end{equation}
Every extreme functional of every fiber has Euclidean norm one.  Thus
\eqref{eq:delta} and \eqref{eq:v0} imply that the Euclidean angle between
$v_0$ and $u$ is less than $\tau$.

We next find another extreme functional in the same fiber.  If $q$ is free,
then $X_q=H$, and $v_0(t)=1$ forces $t=v_0$.  Rotate $v_0$ through angle
$\alpha$ in the direction away from $u$, obtaining a Euclidean unit
functional $v_1$.  Then
\[
 v_1(t)=\cos\alpha\ge\cos(\alpha+\tau),
 \qquad
 \alpha-\tau\le\angle(v_1,u)\le\alpha+2\tau.
\]

Suppose instead that $q=n\ge N$ is principal.  Rotate coordinates so that
$v_0=(1,0)$.  Since $v_0(t)=1$, write $t=(1,s)$.  The two adjacent extreme
functionals $(\cos h_n,\pm\sin h_n)$ both take value at most one on $t$;
hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:s-bound}
  |s|\le\frac{1-\cos h_n}{\sin h_n}=\tan(h_n/2).
\end{equation}
Let $k=\lceil\alpha/h_n\rceil$ and $\theta=kh_n$.  Then
\[
  \alpha\le\theta<\alpha+h_n<\alpha+\tau.
\]
Choose the extreme functional at angular displacement $\theta$ from $v_0$
in the sign of $s$ (either sign if $s=0$).  Calling it $v_1$, we get
\begin{equation}\label{eq:v1-value}
  v_1(t)=\cos\theta+|s|\sin\theta
  \ge\cos\theta\ge\cos(\alpha+\tau).
\end{equation}
Since $\angle(v_0,u)<\tau$, the triangle inequality for these small angles
gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:v1-angle}
  \alpha-\tau\le\angle(v_1,u)\le\alpha+2\tau<\frac\pi2.
\end{equation}
The same two conclusions, \eqref{eq:v1-value} and
\eqref{eq:v1-angle}, therefore hold in both the free and principal cases.

Set
\[
  \psi_1=Q_q^*v_1.
\]
By Proposition~\ref{prop:extreme}, $\psi_1\in\Ext(B_{Z^*})$, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:a}
  a:=\psi_1(y)=v_1(t)\ge\cos(\alpha+\tau)>r.
\end{equation}
Also $J^*\psi_1=v_1$.

Let
\[
  D=\overline{\aco(J^*V)}\subset H^*\cong\R^2.
\]
Every $v^*\in V$ belongs to $U$, so
$\|J^*v^*-u\|_2<\delta$.  It follows that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:D-tube}
  D\subset[-u,u]+\delta B_2.
\end{equation}
By \eqref{eq:v1-angle}, the Euclidean distance from $v_1$ to the segment
$[-u,u]$ equals $\sin\angle(v_1,u)$ and is at least
$\sin(\alpha-\tau)$.  Hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lower}
  \dist(v_1,D)
  \ge\sin(\alpha-\tau)-\delta
  >\sin(\alpha-\tau)-\tau.
\end{equation}

On the other hand, $\rho(x_1^*)\le r$, so \eqref{eq:a} allows us to apply
Lemma~\ref{lem:localization} to $\psi_1$.  Using $\eps<\tau$ and
$a\ge\cos(\alpha+\tau)$ gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:upper}
 \dist(v_1,D)
 \le\eps+\frac{1-a}{1-\eps}
 <\tau+\frac{1-\cos(\alpha+\tau)}{1-\tau}.
\end{equation}
Inequalities \eqref{eq:lower}, \eqref{eq:upper}, and \eqref{eq:tau2}
contradict one another.  Therefore $Z$ does not have quasi-$\ACK$ structure.
This proves the first assertion of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

Finally, every $X_n$ has property $\beta$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:polygon}, hence also
property quasi-$\beta$.  If $Z$ had property quasi-$\beta$, it would have
quasi-$\ACK$ structure by Choi--Jung's Remark 2.2(b), contrary to what we
have just proved.  Thus property quasi-$\beta$ is not stable under arbitrary
$\ell_\infty$-sums either.  \qed

\section{Interpretation}

The counterexample pinpoints the role of uniformity in
Proposition~\ref{prop:uniform}.  Each polygonal summand has an ordinary
$\ACK_{\rho_n}$ structure, but
\[
  \rho_n=\cos(\pi/m_n)\longrightarrow1.
\]
At a free ultrafilter, the varying polygonal norms converge to the Euclidean
norm.  The local quasi-$\ACK$ set $V$ is forced to cluster near a single
Euclidean functional $u$, so its absolute convex hull restricts close to the
line segment $[-u,u]$.  Yet the norm-one face selected by the local operator
contains an extreme fiber functional $v_0$ near $u$, and round geometry
provides a second extreme functional $v_1$ at a controlled angle that still
nearly norms the same vector.  The localization inequality then says that
$v_1$ must be close to $[-u,u]$, while elementary Euclidean geometry says it
is uniformly far away.

This does not conflict with the positive result that $\ell_\infty(E)$ has
quasi-$\ACK$ structure when one fixed finite-dimensional space $E$ has
quasi-$\ACK$ structure.  Here the summands vary, and their polygonal geometry
converges to a round fiber at infinity.

\section{Literature check}

The source question appears in the 2023 article of Choi and Jung
\cite{ChoiJung}.  Targeted searches through June 23, 2026, using the exact
terms ``quasi-ACK'', ``property quasi-beta'', and ``ell-infinity sum'', found
no published proof or counterexample resolving Remarks 3.4 or 4.8.  A 2025
preprint on range strongly exposing operators has a section using
quasi-$\ACK$ spaces but does not address this sum-stability question
\cite{RSE}.  Thus the counterexample above was developed rather than taken
from a located source.
