\section*{Status}

This is a strengthened partial result for Laustsen and Tradacete~\cite{LT}.  First, it shows that
their printed Question~6.3 is not an independent obstruction: for each target space \(F\), the full
composition-invariance statement of Question~6.3 is equivalent to the equality in Question~4.2 for
\(F\).  Moreover, failure of Question~4.2 is already detected by rank-one positively homogeneous
maps.  Second, it gives a broad positive target class for Question~4.2: every reflexive real Banach
space with the bounded approximation property has, for every \(1\leq p<\infty\),
\[
  \overline{I_{w^*}[E]}=H_{w^*}^p[E].
\]
Consequently Question~6.3 is affirmative for all such targets.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Question~6.3 asks whether every admissible composition operator preserves the closed ideal
\(\overline{I_{w^*}}\).  If Question~4.2 is true for the target \(F\), then this is immediate:
the source paper already proves that such a \(C_\Phi\) sends weak-star continuous \(H^p\)-functions
to weak-star continuous \(H^p\)-functions.  Therefore the image lands in
\(H_{w^*}^p[F]=\overline{I_{w^*}[F]}\).

Conversely, there is no need to search among complicated nonlinear maps.  Given any
\(f\in H_{w^*}^p[F]\), choose a nonzero coordinate \(x_0^*\in E^*\) and set
\(\Phi_f(y^*)=f(y^*)x_0^*\).  Then \(\Phi_f\) satisfies exactly the hypotheses of Question~6.3, and
composition sends a single generator \(\delta_{x_0}\in I_{w^*}[E]\) to \(f\).  Hence Question~6.3
for the fixed target \(F\) forces every \(f\in H_{w^*}^p[F]\) to belong to
\(\overline{I_{w^*}[F]}\), which is precisely Question~4.2 for \(F\).

For reflexive spaces with the bounded approximation property, the representation theorem used in
the source paper can be combined with finite-rank approximation on the support of the representing
measure.  A positive measure \(\mu\) on the weakly compact unit ball defines
\[
  f_\mu^p(x^*)=\left(\int |x^*(x)|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}.
\]
The weakly compact ball is an Eberlein compact, so the Amir--Lindenstrauss separable-support theorem
for Radon measures on Eberlein compacta lets us assume \(\mu\) is carried by a separable closed
subspace \(S\subseteq E\).  The bounded approximation property gives a uniformly bounded finite-rank
sequence \(T_n\) converging strongly to the identity on \(S\).  Replacing \(x\) by \(T_nx\) gives a
function depending only on a finite-dimensional subspace.  By the finite-dimensional equality in the
source paper, this compressed function belongs to \(\FBL^p[E]\), hence to
\(\overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\).  The \(\FBL^p\)-error is controlled by
\[
  \left(\int \|(I-T_n)x\|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p},
\]
which tends to zero by dominated convergence.

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}[Target-space equivalence]
\label{thm:equivalence}
Fix \(1\leq p<\infty\) and an infinite-dimensional Banach space \(F\).  The following are
equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}
\item \(\overline{\Iw[F]}=\Hw[F]\).
\item For every infinite-dimensional Banach space \(E\), and every positively homogeneous map
\(\Phi:F^*\to E^*\) such that \(\norm{\Phi}_p<\infty\) and
\(\Phi|_{B_{F^*}}\) is weak-star to weak-star continuous,
\[
  C_\Phi\bigl(\overline{\Iw[E]}\bigr)\subseteq \overline{\Iw[F]}.
\]
\item For one infinite-dimensional Banach space \(E\), one vector \(x_0\in E\), and one functional
\(x_0^*\in E^*\) with \(x_0^*(x_0)=1\), the following rank-one test holds: for every
\(f\in \Hw[F]\), the map \(\Phi_f:F^*\to E^*\), \(\Phi_f(y^*)=f(y^*)x_0^*\), satisfies
\[
  C_{\Phi_f}(\delta_{x_0})\in \overline{\Iw[F]}.
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\((1)\Rightarrow(2)\).  Let \(g\in\overline{\Iw[E]}\).  By Lemma~2.1(viii) of~\cite{LT},
\(\overline{\Iw[E]}\subseteq H_{w^*}^p[E]\).  Lemma~6.1(ii) of~\cite{LT} says that the hypotheses
on \(\Phi\) imply
\[
  C_\Phi(H_{w^*}^p[E])\subseteq H_{w^*}^p[F].
\]
Using (1), \(H_{w^*}^p[F]=\overline{\Iw[F]}\), so
\[
  C_\Phi g\in \overline{\Iw[F]}.
\]

\((2)\Rightarrow(3)\).  This is immediate, since \(\delta_{x_0}\in\FBL^p[E]\subseteq\Iw[E]\) and
the rank-one maps \(\Phi_f\) are admissible.  For completeness, let us check admissibility.  If
\((y_j^*)_{j=1}^m\subset F^*\) has weak \(p\)-summing norm at most \(1\), then
\[
 \bigl\|(\Phi_f(y_j^*))_{j=1}^m\bigr\|_{p,\mathrm{weak}}
 \leq \norm{x_0^*}\left(\sum_{j=1}^m |f(y_j^*)|^p\right)^{1/p}
 \leq \norm{x_0^*}\norm{f}_{\FBL^p[F]}.
\]
Thus \(\norm{\Phi_f}_p<\infty\).  Positive homogeneity and weak-star continuity on \(B_{F^*}\)
follow from the same properties of \(f\).

\((3)\Rightarrow(1)\).  Take \(f\in H_{w^*}^p[F]\).  With \(\Phi_f(y^*)=f(y^*)x_0^*\), we have
\[
  C_{\Phi_f}(\delta_{x_0})(y^*)
  =\delta_{x_0}(\Phi_f(y^*))
  =f(y^*)x_0^*(x_0)
  =f(y^*)
\]
for every \(y^*\in F^*\).  By (3), \(f\in\overline{\Iw[F]}\).  Since \(f\) was arbitrary and the
reverse inclusion \(\overline{\Iw[F]}\subseteq H_{w^*}^p[F]\) is Lemma~2.1(viii) of~\cite{LT}, we
obtain \(\overline{\Iw[F]}=H_{w^*}^p[F]\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A Positive Reflexive-BAP Case of Question 4.2}

\begin{theorem}[Reflexive BAP targets]
\label{thm:reflexive-bap}
Let \(E\) be a reflexive real Banach space with the bounded approximation property.  Then, for every
\(1\leq p<\infty\),
\[
  \overline{I_{w^*}[E]}=H_{w^*}^p[E],
\]
where the closure is taken in the \(\FBL^p[E]\)-norm.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(1\leq p<\infty\).  By Lemma~2.1(viii) of~\cite{LT}, it is enough to prove
\(H_{w^*}^p[E]\subseteq \overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\).

Let \(f\in H_{w^*}^p[E]\).  Since \(E\) is reflexive, Theorem~5.5 of~\cite{LT} gives
\(H_{w^*}^p[E]=H^p[E]\).  Proposition~7.7 of the source paper cited in~\cite{LT} then gives a
positive Radon probability measure \(\mu\) on the weakly compact unit ball \(B_E\) such that
\[
  |f(x^*)|\leq \|f\|_{\FBL^p[E]} f_\mu^p(x^*)\qquad (x^*\in E^*),
\]
where
\[
  f_\mu^p(x^*)=\left(\int_{B_E}|x^*(x)|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}.
\]
Thus it suffices to prove \(f_\mu^p\in\overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\), because
\(\overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\) is an ideal in \(H_{w^*}^p[E]\).

By the Amir--Lindenstrauss theorem that Eberlein compacta have separable measure supports
\cite{AL}, the Radon measure \(\mu\) is carried by a weakly separable compact subset of \(B_E\).
Mazur's theorem then implies that the closed linear span of this support is a norm-separable
subspace \(S\subseteq E\).  Since \(E\) has the bounded approximation property, we can
choose a sequence \((T_n)\) of finite-rank operators on \(E\) with
\(\sup_n\|T_n\|\leq \lambda<\infty\) and \(T_nx\to x\) in norm for every \(x\in S\): take a
countable dense set in \(S\) and diagonalize the uniformly bounded BAP net on its finite initial
segments.  For each \(n\), set \(F_n=T_n(E)\), and define
\(g_n:F_n^*\to\mathbb R\)
by
\[
  g_n(u^*)=\left(\int_{B_E}|u^*(T_n x)|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}.
\]
Since \(F_n\) is finite-dimensional, the finite-dimensional equality from
Theorem~3.5 of~\cite{LT} gives \(g_n\in H_{w^*}^p[F_n]=\FBL^p[F_n]\).  If
\(J_n:F_n\hookrightarrow E\) denotes inclusion, then
\[
  h_n:=C_{J_n^*}g_n\in \FBL^p[E]\subseteq \overline{I_{w^*}[E]}.
\]
Explicitly,
\[
  h_n(x^*)=\left(\int_{B_E}|x^*(T_nx)|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}.
\]

We now show that \(h_n\to f_\mu^p\) in the \(\FBL^p[E]\)-norm.  Let
\(x_1^*,\ldots,x_m^*\in E^*\) have weak \(p\)-summing norm at most \(1\).  The reverse triangle
inequality in \(L_p(\mu)\) gives
\[
  |f_\mu^p(x_j^*)-h_n(x_j^*)|
  \leq \left(\int_{B_E}|x_j^*((I-T_n)x)|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \sum_{j=1}^m |f_\mu^p(x_j^*)-h_n(x_j^*)|^p
  &\leq \int_{B_E}\sum_{j=1}^m |x_j^*((I-T_n)x)|^p\,d\mu(x)\\
  &\leq \int_{B_E}\|(I-T_n)x\|^p\,d\mu(x).
\end{aligned}
\]
For \(\mu\)-almost every \(x\), we have \(x\in S\), so the integrand tends pointwise to zero and is
bounded by \((1+\lambda)^p\).  Dominated convergence yields
\[
  \|f_\mu^p-h_n\|_{\FBL^p[E]}
  \leq
  \left(\int_{B_E}\|(I-T_n)x\|^p\,d\mu(x)\right)^{1/p}
  \longrightarrow0.
\]
Thus \(f_\mu^p\in\overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\), and the ideal domination above gives
\(f\in\overline{I_{w^*}[E]}\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Universal equivalence]
For a fixed \(1\leq p<\infty\), Question~6.3 of~\cite{LT} has an affirmative answer for all
infinite-dimensional Banach spaces \(E,F\) if and only if Question~4.2 has an affirmative answer for
every infinite-dimensional Banach space \(F\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:equivalence} for each target space \(F\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Known positive target classes]
If \(F\) is an infinite-dimensional Banach space for which
\(\overline{\Iw[F]}=H_{w^*}^p[F]\), then Question~6.3 is affirmative for this target \(F\) and
arbitrary infinite-dimensional domain \(E\).  In particular this holds for \(F=\ell_1(A)\), for any
infinite set \(A\), by the positive result for \(\ell_1(A)\) proved in Section~4 of~\cite{LT}.  It
also holds for every infinite-dimensional reflexive real Banach space with the bounded approximation
property, for every \(1\leq p<\infty\), by Theorem~\ref{thm:reflexive-bap}.  In particular it holds
for Hilbert targets for every \(1\leq p<\infty\).
\end{corollary}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not solve Question~4.2 for all Banach spaces.  Its first contribution is
structural: Question~6.3 is equivalent to Question~4.2 target-by-target, and any future
counterexample to Question~4.2 immediately yields a rank-one counterexample to Question~6.3.  Its
second contribution is the positive reflexive-BAP theorem.  The remaining frontier includes
reflexive spaces without the bounded approximation property and non-reflexive spaces beyond the
\(\ell_1(A)\) class covered by the source paper.  Question~4.1 and the separable-quotient
obstruction around Theorem~4.3 of the source paper are untouched.

\section*{Novelty and Literature Check}

The source paper already observes that rank-one maps built from
\(f\in H_{w^*}^p[F]\setminus\FBL^p[F]\) need not preserve \(\FBL^p\).  The present argument uses the
same rank-one mechanism at the level of the closed ideal \(\overline{I_{w^*}}\), giving an exact
equivalence between the two open questions.  A local index search for arXiv:2404.06497 and the
phrases \(I_{w^*}\), \(H_{w^*}^p\), \(C_\Phi\), and ``positively homogeneous functions'' found no
earlier exact run packet for this paper before this packet was created.  Web searches on
2026-06-23 and again on 2026-06-26 for the title, arXiv id, \(I_{w^*}\), \(H_{w^*}^p\),
\(C_\Phi\), ``positively homogeneous functions'', and related Hilbert/BAP phrases found only the
source arXiv paper and no later resolution or reflexive-BAP treatment.
