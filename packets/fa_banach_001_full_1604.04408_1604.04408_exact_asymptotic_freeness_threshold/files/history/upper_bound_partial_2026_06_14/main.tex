\section*{Source And Target}

The source paper is Jarno Talponen's arXiv:1604.04408, \emph{Unconditional
and bimonotone structures in high density Banach spaces}.  In the notation of
that paper,
\[
  \ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)=\ell_{<\nu^+}^\infty(\mu)
\]
is the space of bounded scalar functions on \(\mu\) whose supports have
cardinality at most \(\nu\).

The final remarks of the source paper ask for the least cardinal
\(\kappa=\kappa(\lambda,\nu,\mu)\) such that
\(\ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)\) is \((\lambda,\kappa)\)-asymptotically free, for
infinite cardinals \(\nu<\mu\) and \(\lambda<2^\nu\).

\section*{Definitions}

We use the source paper's definition.  Let \(X\) be a Banach space, let
\(\lambda<\kappa\), and let \(\kappa\) be an uncountable regular cardinal.
The space \(X\) is \((\lambda,\kappa)\)-asymptotically free if the following
holds.  Whenever \(Z\subset X\) is a closed subspace with \(\dens(Z)\leq
\lambda\), and \((Z_\alpha)_{\alpha<\kappa}\) is a nested family of closed
subspaces such that
\[
  Z^\perp=\bigcup_{\alpha<\kappa} Z_\alpha^\perp,
\]
then, for each \(x\in X\),
\[
  \|x+z\|\geq \|z\|\quad(z\in Z)
\]
implies that there is \(\alpha_0<\kappa\) such that
\[
  \|x+z\|\geq \|z\|\quad(z\in Z_\alpha,\ \alpha_0<\alpha<\kappa).
\]

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:upper-bound}
Let \(\nu<\mu\) be infinite cardinals and let \(\lambda<2^\nu\).  If
\(\kappa\) is an uncountable regular cardinal with \(2^\nu<\kappa\), then
\(\ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)\) is \((\lambda,\kappa)\)-asymptotically free.

In particular, for every regular \(\kappa\) with \(2^\nu<\kappa\leq\mu\), this
gives a positive upper-bound answer in the cardinal range explicitly asked for
in the source paper's final remarks.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea Of The Proof}

Talponen proves that \(\ell_\lambda^\infty(\mu)\) is
\((\lambda,\kappa)\)-asymptotically free when \(2^\lambda<\kappa\leq\mu\).  The
same mechanism gives a uniform bound for \(\ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)\) whenever the
tested subspace has density \(\lambda<2^\nu\).  The point is that a dense set
of size at most \(\lambda\), together with one test vector, uses fewer than
\(2^\nu\) coordinates.  Inside that coordinate band, the density is at most
\(2^\nu\).  Since this is below \(\kappa\), the projected nested family of
subspaces stabilizes there.  The coordinate-band decomposition of the sup norm
then gives the required inequality on a tail of the family.

\section*{Proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:band-density}
Let \(A\) be a set with \(|A|<2^\nu\).  Then
\[
  \dens\bigl(\ell_\nu^\infty(A)\bigr)\leq 2^\nu .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For each \(B\subset A\) with \(|B|\leq\nu\), the rational-valued functions on
\(B\), extended by zero outside \(B\), are dense in the copy of \(\ell^\infty(B)\)
supported on \(B\).  Thus
\[
  \dens\bigl(\ell_\nu^\infty(A)\bigr)
  \leq |[A]^{\leq\nu}|\cdot \aleph_0^\nu
  \leq |A|^\nu\cdot 2^\nu
  \leq (2^\nu)^\nu
  =2^\nu .
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:projection-stabilization}
Let \(\kappa\) be regular, let \(P:X\to I\) be a bounded projection onto a
Banach space \(I\) with \(\dens(I)<\kappa\), and let
\((Z_\alpha)_{\alpha<\kappa}\) be a nested family of closed subspaces of \(X\)
with \(Z^\perp=\bigcup_{\alpha<\kappa}Z_\alpha^\perp\).  Then there is
\(\beta<\kappa\) such that
\[
  PZ_\alpha\subset \overline{PZ}\quad(\beta<\alpha<\kappa).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put \(E_\alpha=\overline{PZ_\alpha}\subset I\) and
\(E=\overline{PZ}\subset I\).  If \(\varphi\in E^\perp\), then
\(\varphi\circ P\in Z^\perp\), so \(\varphi\circ P\in Z_\alpha^\perp\) for
some \(\alpha<\kappa\).  Hence \(\varphi\in E_\alpha^\perp\).  Therefore
\[
  E^\perp=\bigcup_{\alpha<\kappa} E_\alpha^\perp .
\]

If \(E=I\), then there is nothing to prove.  Otherwise, since
\(\dens(I/E)<\kappa\), choose a dense family
\(\{u_i+E:i<\delta\}\) in the unit sphere of \(I/E\), with
\(\delta<\kappa\).  For each \(i<\delta\), Hahn--Banach gives
\(\varphi_i\in E^\perp\), \(\|\varphi_i\|=1\), such that
\(\varphi_i(u_i)>3/4\).  Pick \(\alpha_i<\kappa\) with
\(\varphi_i\in E_{\alpha_i}^\perp\), and let
\(\beta=\sup_{i<\delta}\alpha_i\).  Regularity of \(\kappa\) gives
\(\beta<\kappa\).

If \(y\in E_\beta\setminus E\), put \(r=\dist(y,E)>0\).  Choose \(i<\delta\)
with
\[
  \left\|\,y/r+E-(u_i+E)\,\right\|<1/4 .
\]
Then, for some \(e\in E\), \(\|y/r-u_i-e\|<1/4\).  But
\(\varphi_i(y)=0\) because \(y\in E_\beta\subset E_{\alpha_i}\), and
\(\varphi_i(e)=0\).  Thus \(|\varphi_i(u_i)|<1/4\), contradicting
\(\varphi_i(u_i)>3/4\).  Hence \(E_\beta=E\), and the conclusion follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:upper-bound}]
Let \(X=\ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)\).  Fix \(x\in X\), a closed subspace
\(Z\subset X\) with \(\dens(Z)\leq\lambda\), and a nested family
\((Z_\alpha)_{\alpha<\kappa}\) as in the definition.  Assume that
\[
  \|x+z\|\geq\|z\|\quad(z\in Z).
\]

Choose a dense set \(D\subset Z\) with \(|D|\leq\lambda\), and set
\[
  A=\supp(x)\cup\bigcup_{d\in D}\supp(d).
\]
Each vector in \(X\) has support of cardinality at most \(\nu\), so
\[
  |A|\leq \nu+\lambda\cdot\nu=\max\{\nu,\lambda\}<2^\nu .
\]
Let \(I=\ell_\nu^\infty(A)\), viewed as the coordinate band in \(X\), and let
\(P_A:X\to I\) be the coordinate projection.  The band \(I\) is closed,
contains \(x\), and contains \(D\), hence contains \(Z\).  By
Lemma~\ref{lem:band-density},
\[
  \dens(I)\leq 2^\nu<\kappa .
\]

Applying Lemma~\ref{lem:projection-stabilization} to \(P_A\), there is
\(\beta<\kappa\) such that
\[
  P_AZ_\alpha\subset \overline{P_AZ}=Z
  \quad(\beta<\alpha<\kappa),
\]
where the last equality uses \(Z\subset I\) and the closedness of \(Z\).

Now fix \(\alpha>\beta\) and \(z\in Z_\alpha\).  Since \(P_Az\in Z\), the
assumption on \(Z\) gives
\[
  \|x+P_Az\|\geq\|P_Az\|.
\]
The coordinate band decomposition in the sup norm gives
\[
  \|x+z\|
  =\max\{\|x+P_Az\|,\ \|(\Id-P_A)z\|\}
  \geq \max\{\|P_Az\|,\ \|(\Id-P_A)z\|\}
  =\|z\|.
\]
This is exactly the required tail inequality, so \(X\) is
\((\lambda,\kappa)\)-asymptotically free.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope And Limitations}

This is a partial result for the final-remarks question, not a computation of
the exact least \(\kappa(\lambda,\nu,\mu)\).  It shows that the threshold
\((2^\nu)^+\) is always sufficient, in the regular-cardinal regime of the
definition, for every \(\lambda<2^\nu\).  It gives no lower bound and leaves
open whether \(\ell_\nu^\infty(\mu)\) may be \((\lambda,\kappa)\)-a.f. for
some \(\kappa\leq 2^\nu\).

If \(\mu\leq 2^\nu\), the theorem still applies to regular
\(\kappa>2^\nu\), but it gives no nontrivial instance with
\(\kappa\leq\mu\).  This is another reason it should be read as an upper-bound
packet rather than an exact answer.
