\section{Status}

This packet records a \emph{literature-implied} correction/sharpening, not a
new original lower-bound proof.  The source paper is

\begin{quote}
G. Ivanov, \emph{On Banach Spaces with the Helly Approximation Property},
arXiv:2603.22743.
\end{quote}

The supporting paper is

\begin{quote}
V. Reis and T. Rothvoss, \emph{Approximate Carath\'eodory bounds via
Discrepancy Theory}, arXiv:2207.03614.
\end{quote}

The source paper obtains \(r_k(\ell_1^n)\le C\sqrt{\log n/k}\) and then
conjectures that this bound is optimal.

\section{Claim}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(h_k(\ell_1^n)\) be the optimal radius in the no-dimensional Helly
definition of arXiv:2603.22743.  For \(1\le k\le n\),
\[
  h_k(\ell_1^n)
  \le
  C\sqrt{\frac{\log(2n/k)}{k}}
\]
for a universal constant \(C\).
Consequently, the displayed \(\sqrt{\log n/k}\) bound in the source paper is
not uniformly optimal in \(k\) as written.
\end{theorem}

\section{Supporting Literature}

Reis--Rothvoss define \(\operatorname{ac}_k(P,Q)\) as the worst-case error
when approximating a point of \(\conv(X)\), \(X\subseteq P\), by the uniform
average of \(k\) points of \(X\), measured in the \(Q\)-norm.  Their Corollary
5 states:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/supporting_corollary_crop.png}
\end{center}

Taking \(p=\infty\) and \(m=n\) gives
\[
  \operatorname{ac}_k(B_\infty^n,B_\infty^n)
  \le
  C_0 \sqrt{\frac{\log(2n/k)}{k}}.
\]

\section{Transfer to Helly}

\begin{lemma}
Let \(X\) be finite-dimensional.  If
\[
  \operatorname{ac}_k(B_{X^*},B_{X^*})\le \varepsilon,
\]
then \(3\varepsilon\) is a valid no-dimensional Helly radius for \(X\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\mathcal F\) be a finite family of closed convex subsets of \(X\) such
that every \(k\)-element subfamily intersects \(B_X\).  Put
\[
  f(x)=\max_{K\in\mathcal F}\dist(x,K)
\]
and choose a minimizer \(x_0\).  Since every \(K\in\mathcal F\) intersects
\(B_X\), \(f(0)\le 1\), and hence \(x_0\in 2B_X\).

Let \(r=f(x_0)\).  If \(r=0\) there is nothing to prove.  Otherwise the
standard subdifferential argument used in arXiv:2603.22743 gives active sets
\(K_1,\ldots,K_m\) and functionals \(u_1,\ldots,u_m\in B_{X^*}\) with
\[
  0\in\conv\{u_1,\ldots,u_m\}
\]
and
\[
  \langle u_i,x-x_0\rangle\le -r
  \qquad (x\in K_i).
\]
By the definition of \(\operatorname{ac}_k\), choose indices
\(i_1,\ldots,i_k\) such that
\[
  \left\|\frac1k\sum_{t=1}^k u_{i_t}\right\|\le\varepsilon.
\]
Repeated indices cause no difficulty: the distinct chosen active sets can be
extended to a \(k\)-element subfamily, so the Helly hypothesis supplies
\[
  q\in B_X\cap\bigcap_{t=1}^k K_{i_t}.
\]
Average the displayed inequalities at \(q\):
\[
  r
  \le
  -\left\langle \frac1k\sum_{t=1}^k u_{i_t},q-x_0\right\rangle
  \le
  \varepsilon\|q-x_0\|
  \le
  3\varepsilon.
\]
Thus \(x_0\) lies within \(3\varepsilon\) of every set in \(\mathcal F\).
\end{proof}

Apply the lemma to \(X=\ell_1^n\), so \(X^*=\ell_\infty^n\), and use the
Reis--Rothvoss corollary.  This proves the claimed bound.

\section{Interpretation}

The source paper's conjecture is not wrong if read as a rough statement in
regimes where \(\log(2n/k)\) and \(\log n\) are comparable.  But it cannot be
correct as a uniform-in-\(k\) lower-bound assertion.  For example, when
\(k=n\), the bound above gives
\[
  h_n(\ell_1^n)\le C/\sqrt n,
\]
whereas a matching lower bound of order \(\sqrt{\log n/n}\) would eventually
contradict this by an unbounded factor.

\section{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item This packet does not prove the matching lower bound
  \(\Omega(\sqrt{\log(2n/k)/k})\).
\item It does not settle the exact asymptotic behavior of \(h_k(\ell_1^n)\).
\item It records a separate-literature implication, not a new proof of the
  approximate-Carath\'eodory theorem.
\end{itemize}
