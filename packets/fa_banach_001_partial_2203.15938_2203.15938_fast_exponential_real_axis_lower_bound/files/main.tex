\section*{Status}

This is a partial-result packet.  It proves the lower-bound, or quasimode,
side of the real-axis asymptotic predicted for the fast exponential example in
Arnal--Siegl.  It does not prove the matching upper bound, and therefore does
not close the source open question.

\section*{Source and Open Question}

The source is Antonio Arnal and Petr Siegl, ``Resolvent estimates for
one-dimensional Schrodinger operators with complex potentials'', arXiv:2203.15938,
published in \emph{Journal of Functional Analysis} 284 (2023), Article 109856
\cite{ArnalSiegl2023}.  Section 7.3 considers
\[
    H=-\partial_x^2+i\e^{x^2},\qquad
    \Dom(H)=W^{2,2}(\R)\cap \Dom(\e^{x^2}),
\]
and gives the imaginary-axis asymptotic.  It then states that the real-axis
Theorem 4.2 is not applicable and leaves the critical region next to the real
axis open.

\section*{Transcription of the Relevant Source Point}

The relevant paragraph in Section 7.3 states that for
\(H=-\partial_x^2+i\e^{x^2}\) the real-axis theorem from the paper is not
applicable, so the critical region next to the positive real axis remains open.
This packet addresses one direction of that missing real-axis analysis.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

Near the positive turning frequency \(\xi=\sqrt a\), a wave packet of the form
\[
    u(x)=t_a^{-1/2}\e^{i\sqrt a\,x}v(x/t_a),
    \qquad t_a\e^{t_a^2}=2\sqrt a,
\]
turns the second-order operator \(H-a\) into a first-order transport operator:
\[
    (H-a)u
    =i\e^{t_a^2}t_a^{-1/2}\e^{i\sqrt a\,x}
      \left(-v'+\e^{t_a^2(y^2-1)}v\right)_{y=x/t_a}
      +\hbox{a smaller second-derivative term}.
\]
The normalized first-order model
\[
    B_t=-\partial_y+\e^{t^2(y^2-1)}
\]
converges to Volterra integration on \((-1,1)\), whose norm is \(4/\pi\).
Thus choosing \(v\) from a near-extremizer of \(B_t^{-1}\) produces physical
quasimodes for \(H-a\) with residual at most
\((\pi/4+o(1))\e^{t_a^2}\).  This proves the sharp predicted lower bound for
the resolvent norm.  What remains open is the converse inequality for arbitrary
test functions.

\section*{Definitions}

For \(t>0\), define
\[
    W_t(y):=\e^{t^2(y^2-1)},\qquad
    B_t=-\partial_y+W_t
\]
on \(L^2(\R)\) with maximal domain
\[
    \Dom(B_t)=\{v\in L^2(\R): -v'+W_tv\in L^2(\R)\}.
\]
Let \(T_0\) be the limiting Volterra operator
\[
    (T_0 f)(x)=\int_x^1 f(y)\,\dd y,\qquad -1<x<1,
\]
viewed as an operator on \(L^2(-1,1)\).  Its norm is \(4/\pi\).

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[sharp one-sided real-axis bound]\label{thm:lower}
Let
\[
    H=-\partial_x^2+i\e^{x^2},
    \qquad \Dom(H)=W^{2,2}(\R)\cap\Dom(\e^{x^2}),
\]
and let \(t_a>0\) be determined by
\[
    t_a\e^{t_a^2}=2\sqrt a.
\]
Then
\[
    \liminf_{a\to+\infty} \e^{t_a^2}\|(H-a)^{-1}\|\ge \frac4\pi.
\]
Equivalently, if
\[
    m(a):=\inf_{0\ne u\in\Dom(H)}
      \frac{\|(H-a)u\|}{\|u\|},
\]
then
\[
    \limsup_{a\to+\infty} \e^{-t_a^2}m(a)\le\frac\pi4.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

\begin{lemma}[model inverse convergence]\label{lem:model}
Let \(T_t=B_t^{-1}\).  For every \(f\in C_c^\infty((-1,1))\),
\[
    T_t f \longrightarrow T_0 f
    \quad\hbox{in }L^2(\R),\qquad t\to+\infty,
\]
where \(T_0f\) is extended by zero outside \((-1,1)\) on the right and by its
Volterra value on \((-1,1)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Solving the first-order equation with the \(L^2\) condition at \(+\infty\)
gives
\[
    (T_t f)(x)=\int_x^\infty
       \exp\!\left(-\int_x^y W_t(s)\,\dd s\right) f(y)\,\dd y.
\]
For \(-1<x<1\), \(W_t\to0\) locally uniformly, and for intervals outside
\([-1,1]\), \(W_t\to+\infty\) locally uniformly.  Hence the kernel converges
pointwise to \(\mathbf 1_{\{-1<x<y<1\}}\).  The same tail estimates as in the
standard proof of the fast-model packet give convergence in Hilbert--Schmidt
norm on the support of \(f\), and therefore \(T_tf\to T_0f\) in \(L^2\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[smooth near-extremizers]\label{lem:near-ext}
For every \(\varepsilon>0\), there is \(f\in C_c^\infty((-1,1))\) such that
\[
    \frac{\|f\|_{L^2(-1,1)}}{\|T_0f\|_{L^2(-1,1)}}\le \frac\pi4+\varepsilon.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Volterra operator on an interval of length \(2\) has norm \(4/\pi\).
Choose an \(L^2\) near-extremizer for \(T_0\), then approximate it in \(L^2\)
by a smooth compactly supported function.  Since \(T_0\) is bounded, the ratio
is preserved up to an arbitrarily small error.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[second derivative of the first-order quasimode]\label{lem:vpp}
Fix \(f\in C_c^\infty((-1,1))\), and put \(v_t:=T_tf\).  Then there is a
constant \(C_f\) such that, as \(t\to+\infty\),
\[
    \|v_t''\|_{L^2(\R)}\le C_f t^5.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\supp f\subset[\alpha,\beta]\), with \(-1<\alpha<\beta<1\).  The
identity \(B_tv_t=f\) gives
\[
    v_t'=W_tv_t-f,\qquad
    v_t''=(W_t'+W_t^2)v_t-W_tf-f'.
\]
On \([\alpha,\beta]\), the functions \(W_t\) and \(W_t'\) are uniformly
bounded, and outside \((-\infty,\beta]\) we have \(v_t=0\).  On
\([-1,\alpha]\), \(f=0\), \(0\le W_t\le1\), and \(|W_t'|\le 2t^2W_t\), while
\(\|v_t\|_\infty\le \|f\|_1\).  These pieces contribute \(O_f(t^2)\) to the
\(L^2\)-norm of \(v_t''\).

It remains to consider \(x<-1\).  There \(f=0\), so
\[
    v_t(x)=C_t\exp\!\left(-\int_x^\alpha W_t(s)\,\dd s\right),
    \qquad |C_t|\le \|f\|_1.
\]
Write \(x=-1-r\), \(r>0\).  On \(0<r<1\), set \(z=\e^{2t^2r}\).  Since
\[
    W_t(-1-r)\le z^{3/2},\qquad
    \int_{-1-r}^{-1}W_t(s)\,\dd s
       \ge \frac{z-1}{2t^2},
\]
we get
\[
    \int_{-2}^{-1}|v_t''(x)|^2\,\dd x
       \le C_f\int_1^\infty
          (t^4z^3+z^6)\exp\!\left(-\frac{z-1}{t^2}\right)
          \frac{\dd z}{t^2z}
       \le C_f t^{10}.
\]
For \(x<-2\), the integral \(\int_x^{-1}W_t(s)\,\dd s\) grows faster than any
fixed exponential in \(|x|\), while \(W_t\) and \(W_t'\) grow only like
exponentials in \(x^2\); the same estimate on unit intervals gives a bounded
contribution.  Hence \(\|v_t''\|_2=O_f(t^5)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:lower}]
Fix \(\varepsilon>0\), and choose \(f\in C_c^\infty((-1,1))\) as in
Lemma \ref{lem:near-ext}.  Let \(v_t=T_tf\).  For \(a>0\), put
\[
    t=t_a,\qquad \xi=\sqrt a,\qquad
    u_a(x)=t^{-1/2}\e^{i\xi x}v_t(x/t).
\]
Then \(\|u_a\|_{L^2(\R)}=\|v_t\|_{L^2(\R)}\), and a direct calculation gives,
with \(y=x/t\),
\[
    (H-a)u_a(x)
    =t^{-1/2}\e^{i\xi x}
      \left(-t^{-2}v_t''(y)-2i\xi t^{-1}v_t'(y)
            +i\e^{t^2y^2}v_t(y)\right).
\]
Since \(t\e^{t^2}=2\xi\), this becomes
\[
    (H-a)u_a
    =i\e^{t^2}t^{-1/2}\e^{i\xi x}(B_tv_t)(x/t)
      -t^{-5/2}\e^{i\xi x}v_t''(x/t).
\]
Taking norms and using \(B_tv_t=f\), Lemma \ref{lem:vpp}, and
Lemma \ref{lem:model},
\[
    \e^{-t^2}\frac{\|(H-a)u_a\|}{\|u_a\|}
       \le
       \frac{\|f\|+t^{-2}\e^{-t^2}\|v_t''\|}{\|v_t\|}
       \longrightarrow
       \frac{\|f\|}{\|T_0f\|}
       \le \frac\pi4+\varepsilon.
\]
Therefore
\[
    \limsup_{a\to+\infty}\e^{-t_a^2}m(a)\le \frac\pi4+\varepsilon.
\]
Letting \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) proves the singular-value statement, and
inverting it gives
\[
    \liminf_{a\to+\infty}\e^{t_a^2}\|(H-a)^{-1}\|\ge\frac4\pi.
\]
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This result does not prove the matching upper bound
\[
    \|(H-a)^{-1}\|\le \left(\frac4\pi+o(1)\right)\e^{-t_a^2}.
\]
That upper bound would require a global estimate for arbitrary \(u\), replacing
the Fourier partition and commutator estimates used in Arnal--Siegl Theorem
4.2.  Those estimates rely on regular variation and symbol-class derivative
bounds, while \(V_2(x)=\e^{x^2}\) has \(V_2^{(n)}(x)=P_n(x)\e^{x^2}\) and is
not regularly varying.

The run's cheap indexes were checked on 2026-07-18 for arXiv:2203.15938 and
the fast-exponential real-axis keywords.  They contained the earlier
conditional model packet but no full or partial packet proving this one-sided
source-operator bound.  The companion generalized Airy paper was also checked;
it records applications to the regularly varying real-axis theorem and does
not supply the missing fast-exponential real-axis transfer.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a likely valid partial result.  The most important proof checkpoint
is Lemma \ref{lem:vpp}: it must be enough that \(\|v_t''\|\) grows only
polynomially, because the rescaling multiplies this term by
\(t^{-2}\e^{-t^2}\).  The packet should not be treated as closing the open
critical-region question until the converse, all-functions upper bound is
proved.
