\begin{abstract}
Bible asks in Remark 3.10 of \cite{bible} whether the uncountable Lorentz
preduals \(d_*(w,1,A)\), already shown there to admit \(C^\infty\)-smooth
renormings, admit analytic renormings.  We give a candidate full negative
answer.  For every standard decreasing Lorentz weight \(w\) and every
uncountable set \(A\), the Banach space \(d_*(w,1,A)\) admits no equivalent
analytic norm.  The proof is stronger: no continuous polynomial separates the
origin from the unit sphere.  The finite-\(\ell_q\) cases, where diagonal
high-degree polynomials exist, are handled by normalized flat averages.
\end{abstract}

\section{Definitions}

Let \(w=(w_j)_{j\ge 1}\) be a standard Lorentz weight: \(w_1=1\),
\[
  w_1\ge w_2\ge\cdots>0,\qquad w_j\to0,\qquad
  \sum_{j=1}^\infty w_j=\infty .
\]
Put \(W_m=\sum_{j=1}^m w_j\).  For a set \(A\), \(d_*(w,1,A)\) is the space
of all scalar functions \(x\) on \(A\) such that
\[
  \overline x_m
  =
  \sup\left\{
  \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{m}|x(a_i)|}{W_m}:
  a_1,\ldots,a_m\in A\hbox{ distinct}
  \right\}
\]
defines a null sequence.  The norm is
\[
  \|x\|_*=\sup_m \overline x_m .
\]
Equivalently, if \((x_j^*)\) is the decreasing rearrangement of the nonzero
values of \(|x|\), then
\[
  \|x\|_*=\sup_m \frac{\sum_{j=1}^m x_j^*}{W_m}.
\]

\section{Idea of the proof}

The previous partial packet covered the case \(w\notin\ell_q\) for every
finite \(q\): then every continuous homogeneous polynomial is countably
supported, and a fresh coordinate defeats analytic separation.

The missing finite-\(\ell_q\) case is subtler because diagonal polynomials
such as
\[
  x\mapsto \sum_{a\in A}x(a)^q
\]
may be continuous.  The replacement obstruction is not support, but
flattening.  For a finite set \(F\subset A\) of size \(m\), put
\[
  u_F=\frac{W_m}{m}\sum_{a\in F} e_a .
\]
Then \(\|u_F\|_*=1\).  The main lemma says that, after choosing \(F\) far
enough outside any prescribed countable set, every fixed finite family of
continuous homogeneous polynomials is arbitrarily small on \(u_F\).

For a diagonal \(q\)-polynomial this is the elementary estimate
\[
  \sum_{a\in F} u_F(a)^q
  =
  \frac{W_m^q}{m^{q-1}}\longrightarrow0
  \qquad (w\in\ell_q).
\]
For general polynomials the same mechanism appears after a Ramsey thinning of
the coordinate coefficients by equality pattern.  Any nonzero limiting
coefficient with block sizes \(r_1,\ldots,r_s\) forces
\(w\in\ell_{r_i}\) for every block size \(r_i\); then
\[
  m^s\left(\frac{W_m}{m}\right)^{r_1+\cdots+r_s}
  =
  \prod_{i=1}^s \frac{W_m^{r_i}}{m^{r_i-1}}
  \longrightarrow0 .
\]
Thus no polynomial can separate the unit sphere.  An analytic norm would yield
an analytic separating function; truncating its local Taylor expansion and
applying the flattening lemma gives the contradiction.

\section{The flattening lemma}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:lq-decay}
If \(r\ge1\), \(w\in\ell_r\), and \(r>1\), then
\[
  \frac{W_m^r}{m^{r-1}}\longrightarrow0 .
\]
For \(r=1\) this conclusion is irrelevant here, since Lorentz weights satisfy
\(w\notin\ell_1\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(M<m\).  By Holder's inequality,
\[
  \sum_{j=M+1}^m w_j
  \le
  (m-M)^{1-1/r}
  \left(\sum_{j>M}w_j^r\right)^{1/r}.
\]
After division by \(m^{1-1/r}\), the initial sum
\(\sum_{j\le M}w_j\) disappears as \(m\to\infty\).  Taking the limsup and then
letting \(M\to\infty\) gives \(W_m/m^{1-1/r}\to0\), which is the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[flat averages kill finite polynomial families]\label{lem:flat}
Let \(A\) be uncountable, \(X=d_*(w,1,A)\), \(C\subset A\) countable, and
\(\mathcal F\) a finite family of continuous homogeneous scalar polynomials
on \(X\).  For every \(\eps>0\) there is a finite set
\(F\subset A\setminus C\) such that, with
\[
  u_F=\frac{W_{|F|}}{|F|}\sum_{a\in F}e_a,
\]
one has
\[
  \|u_F\|_*=1
  \quad\hbox{and}\quad
  |P(u_F)|<\eps \qquad(P\in\mathcal F).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First observe that \(\|u_F\|_*=1\) when \(|F|=m\).  Indeed, for
\(k\le m\),
\[
  \frac{k(W_m/m)}{W_k}\le1
\]
because \(W_k/k\ge W_m/m\), while for \(k\ge m\) the ratio is
\(W_m/W_k\le1\).  Equality occurs at \(k=m\).

We prove the polynomial estimate.  Let \(d\) be the largest degree in
\(\mathcal F\), and let \(\check P\) denote the associated symmetric
multilinear form.  A standard Ramsey diagonal thinning gives an infinite
sequence \(B=(b_j)\) in \(A\setminus C\) with the following property: for
every \(P\in\mathcal F\) and every partition \(\pi\) of
\(\{1,\ldots,\deg P\}\), the coefficient
\[
  \check P(e_{b_{i_1}},\ldots,e_{b_{i_{\deg P}}})
\]
has a limit \(L(P,\pi)\) along increasing choices of distinct indices whose
equality pattern is \(\pi\).  This is just the usual finite Ramsey theorem
applied successively to rational approximations of the finitely many bounded
coefficient arrays.

Fix one such pair \((P,\pi)\).  Let the block sizes of \(\pi\) be
\(r_1,\ldots,r_s\), so that \(r_1+\cdots+r_s=\deg P\).  We record the
standard consequence of continuity: if \(L(P,\pi)\ne0\), then
\[
  w\in\ell_{r_i}\qquad(1\le i\le s).
\]
Indeed, if \(w\notin\ell_{r_i}\) for some block size, take \(s\) disjoint
subsequences of \(B\), one for each block of \(\pi\).  On the \(r_i\) argument
positions belonging to the \(i\)-th block, insert vectors
\[
  x_{i,t}=\sum_{j=1}^M \eta_{i,t,j} w_j e_{b^i_j},
  \qquad |\eta_{i,t,j}|=1 .
\]
Each of these vectors has norm at most one.  Averaging the signs separately
inside each block and choosing the last sign in each block to align the
diagonal product kills all mixed terms and leaves the terms with the equality
pattern \(\pi\).  Since the corresponding coefficients are eventually within
\(|L(P,\pi)|/2\) of \(L(P,\pi)\), continuity of \(\check P\) would force
\[
  \prod_{i=1}^s\sum_{j=1}^M w_j^{r_i}
\]
to remain bounded as \(M\to\infty\), impossible if one factor diverges.

Now choose \(m\) large.  In the expansion of \(P(u_F)\), the contribution of
the pattern \(\pi\) is, up to an error made small by taking the block of
indices far enough out in \(B\),
\[
  L(P,\pi)\,(m)_s\left(\frac{W_m}{m}\right)^{\deg P},
\]
where \((m)_s=m(m-1)\cdots(m-s+1)\).  If \(L(P,\pi)=0\), choose the block far
enough out so that the coefficient error is less than
\(\eps\) divided by the finite combinatorial factor.  If \(L(P,\pi)\ne0\),
the preceding paragraph gives \(w\in\ell_{r_i}\) for every \(i\), and Lemma
\ref{lem:lq-decay} gives
\[
  (m)_s\left(\frac{W_m}{m}\right)^{\deg P}
  \le
  \prod_{i=1}^s \frac{W_m^{r_i}}{m^{r_i-1}}
  \longrightarrow0 .
\]
Since there are only finitely many polynomials and equality patterns, a single
large \(m\) and a sufficiently far-out block \(F\subset B\) of size \(m\)
make all contributions smaller than \(\eps\).  This proves the lemma.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:no-separating-polynomial}
The space \(d_*(w,1,A)\), for uncountable \(A\), admits no separating
polynomial.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Let \(Q=\sum_{j=0}^d P_j\) be a continuous polynomial, decomposed into
homogeneous parts, and suppose \(Q(0)=0\).  Apply Lemma \ref{lem:flat} to the
finite family \(\{P_1,\ldots,P_d\}\).  It gives unit vectors \(u_F\) with
\(|Q(u_F)|\) arbitrarily small.  Hence \(Q\) cannot be bounded away from zero
on the unit sphere.
\end{proof}

\section{No analytic norm}

\begin{fact}[analytic separation with local Taylor control]\label{fact:sep}
If a normed space admits an equivalent analytic norm
\(\vertiii{\cdot}\), then there are \(\rho>0\) and an analytic function
\(f\colon X\to\R\), represented by its Taylor series at the origin on a norm
ball containing \(\{x:\vertiii{x}\le\rho\}\), such that
\[
  f(0)=0
  \quad\hbox{and}\quad
  \inf_{\vertiii{x}=\rho} f(x)>1 .
\]
\end{fact}

This is the standard separating-function consequence of an analytic norm used
in \cite{dantas-hajek-russo}.  The usual affine-hyperplane construction of
the coercive analytic separator may be made with the translated hyperplane far
enough from the origin, and then rescaled, so that the separating sphere lies
inside the convergence ball of the Taylor expansion at zero.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let \(A\) be uncountable and let \(w\) be any standard decreasing Lorentz
weight.  Then
\[
  X=d_*(w,1,A)
\]
does not admit an equivalent analytic norm.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume that \(X\) admits an equivalent analytic norm \(\vertiii{\cdot}\).
Choose constants \(0<a\le b<\infty\) such that
\[
  a\|x\|_*\le \vertiii{x}\le b\|x\|_* \qquad(x\in X).
\]
Let \(f\) and \(\rho\) be as in Fact \ref{fact:sep}.  Then \(f(x)>1\) whenever
\(\vertiii{x}=\rho\), and its Taylor series at the origin converges absolutely
and uniformly on a norm ball containing \(\{x:\vertiii{x}\le\rho\}\).
Write
\[
  f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^\infty P_n(x)
\]
on that ball.  Since \(f(0)=0\), \(P_0=0\).  Choose \(N\) so that the tail
\(\sum_{n>N}P_n\) has absolute value \(<1/4\) on
\(\{x:\vertiii{x}\le\rho\}\).

Apply Lemma \ref{lem:flat} to the finite family
\(\{P_1,\ldots,P_N\}\), with \(\eps>0\) so small that
\[
  \sum_{n=1}^N
  \left(\frac{\rho}{a}\right)^n |P_n(u_F)|<\frac14 .
\]
For the resulting vector \(u_F\), put
\[
  x=\frac{\rho}{\vertiii{u_F}}u_F .
\]
Then \(\vertiii{x}=\rho\), while \(\vertiii{u_F}\ge a\) because
\(\|u_F\|_*=1\).  Hence the homogeneous scaling estimate above gives
\[
  \left|\sum_{n=1}^N P_n(x)\right|<\frac14,
\]
and the tail contributes \(<1/4\).  Thus \(|f(x)|<1/2\), contradicting
\(f(x)>1\) on the \(\vertiii{\cdot}\)-sphere of radius \(\rho\).
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and novelty}

The earlier slow-weight packet proved the special case
\(w\notin\ell_q\) for every finite \(q\).  This packet replaces the
countable-support argument by Lemma \ref{lem:flat}, which also covers the
finite-\(\ell_q\) regime where diagonal polynomials are continuous.
