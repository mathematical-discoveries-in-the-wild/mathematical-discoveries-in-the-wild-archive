## Mathematical verification

1. For fixed smooth convex globally Lipschitz \(F\), semigroup differentiation
   gives integrable bounds for the Kim--Milman velocity and its spatial
   derivatives. This justifies the limiting flow and differentiation with
   respect to the small parameter \(\varepsilon\).
2. At first order,
   \(D^2[-\log P_t(e^{-\varepsilon F})]
     =\varepsilon D^2P_tF+O(\varepsilon^2)\).
3. In the second-order Jacobian equation, the direct second variation and the
   displacement-evaluation term are symmetric matrices. The full skew part is
   therefore exactly the time-ordered commutator integral displayed in the
   theorem.
4. For \(F_0=x_1^4+(x_1+x_2)^4\) at \(x_0=(0,1)\), the Mehler formula gives
   \(D^2P_tF_0=12(r_t^2P+r_t(1-r_t)Q)\), \(r_t=e^{-2t}\).
5. The exact integral is \(-1/24\), and
   \([P,Q]=\left(\begin{smallmatrix}0&-1\\1&0\end{smallmatrix}\right)\),
   hence the skew coefficient is
   \(\left(\begin{smallmatrix}0&6\\-6&0\end{smallmatrix}\right)\).
6. Smooth convex Lipschitz truncations of \(s^4\) have second derivatives
   converging pointwise to \(12s^2\) with a common quadratic envelope.
   The OU derivative identity supplies a common \(Ce^{-2t}\) envelope, so the
   commutator integrals converge by dominated convergence. Nonvanishing is
   therefore preserved for a sufficiently late truncation.
7. Nonsymmetry of \(DS_\infty\) rules out a Brenier forward map because the
   inverse of a smooth Brenier diffeomorphism is a convex-gradient map and has
   symmetric derivative.
