\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate full solution, subject to human review, to the
UMD general-local part of Remark 12.10 in Yaroslavtsev's paper
\emph{Local characteristics and tangency of vector-valued martingales}
\cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}.  The source proves the quasi-left-continuous case
for UMD spaces and says that the remaining issue for general local martingales
is a discrete independent-increment comparison.  We prove the finite
independent-increment comparison in every Banach space of finite cotype.  This
is the form needed in the accessible-jump approximation in the source: the
accessible part is first truncated to finitely many predictable jumps and then
one lets the truncation parameter tend to infinity.  Since every UMD space has
finite cotype, this settles the UMD case of the source question.

This does not contradict the previous \(c_0\) counterexample in the run:
\(c_0\) has no finite cotype and is not UMD.

\section*{Main Results}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-cotype discrete aggregate comparison]
\label{thm:discrete}
Let \(X\) be a Banach space of finite cotype, and let \(1\le p<\infty\).
Let \((\xi_i)_{i=1}^n\) and \((\eta_j)_{j=1}^m\) be independent mean-zero
\(X\)-valued random variables with finite \(p\)-moments.  Suppose that
\[
  \sum_i \Pp(\xi_i\in B)
  \le
  \sum_j \Pp(\eta_j\in B)
  \qquad (B\in\mathcal B(X\setminus\{0\})).
\]
Then
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\xi_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  C_{p,X}
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_j\eta_j}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[UMD general local characteristic domination]
\label{thm:umd}
Let \(X\) be a UMD Banach space and \(1\le p<\infty\).  If \(N\) and \(M\)
are \(X\)-valued local martingales and \(N\) is characteristically dominated
by \(M\) in the sense of \cite[Definition 12.9]{Yaroslavtsev2019}, then
\[
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{N_t}^p
  \le
  C_{p,X}
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{M_t}^p.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Relation to the \(c_0\) Counterexample}

There are two closely related questions in Remark 12.10.  The broad discrete
aggregate-domination question is phrased for an arbitrary Banach space \(X\).
That broad question is false: the companion packet
\[
\texttt{1907.11588\_discrete\_aggregate\_domination\_c0\_counterexample}
\]
constructs independent symmetric \(c_0\)-valued families with equal aggregate
jump measures but with unbounded comparison constants.  This is not a
counterexample to the present theorem, because \(c_0\) has no finite cotype and
is not UMD.

The present packet proves the positive UMD/finite-cotype side.  Thus the two
packets should be read as complementary boundary information: arbitrary Banach
spaces are too large, while finite cotype is enough for the discrete comparison
proved here, and UMD spaces fall inside that positive class.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The earlier conditional packet in this run showed that the symmetric
aggregate-domination problem follows from symmetric de-Poissonization: compare
the original independent Bernoulli family to the compound-Poisson sum with the
same aggregate nonzero law.  The new observation is that finite cotype gives
exactly the missing upper de-Poissonization estimate.

The key input is a Maurey--Pisier contraction principle.  If \(X\) has cotype
\(q<\infty\), then iid symmetric scalar coefficients \(\theta_i\) satisfy
\[
 \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\theta_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
 \lesssim_{p,q,X}
 \int_0^\infty \Pp(|\theta_1|>t)^{1/\max(p,q)}\,dt\,
 \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\eps_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
For Bernoulli selectors of density \(a\), the tail integral is
\(a^{1/\max(p,q)}\).  Hence an \(a\)-thinning of a symmetric independent
family costs a summable power of \(a\).  A compound-Poisson sum decomposes into
layers \(\{\operatorname{Pois}(p_i)\ge k\}\), and the \(k\)-th layer is a
\(1/k!\)-thinning of the original family.  Summing over \(k\) proves
de-Poissonization.

\section*{A Finite-Cotype Thinning Lemma}

We use the following standard Maurey--Pisier contraction principle in the
explicit form stated by Ivanisvili--van Handel--Volberg
\cite[Theorem 4.1]{IvanisviliVanHandelVolberg2020}.

\begin{theorem}[Maurey--Pisier contraction]
\label{thm:mp}
Let \(X\) have cotype \(q<\infty\), let
\(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_n\) be iid symmetric scalar random variables, and let
\(\eps_1,\ldots,\eps_n\) be independent Rademacher signs.  Then for every
\(1\le p<\infty\) and every \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\in X\),
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\theta_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  L_{p,q,X}
  \int_0^\infty \Pp(|\theta_1|>t)^{1/\max(p,q)}\,dt\,
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\eps_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{lemma}[Thinning in finite cotype]
\label{lem:thinning}
Let \(X\) have cotype \(q<\infty\), let \(1\le p<\infty\), and set
\[
  \alpha=\frac{1}{\max(p,q)}.
\]
Let \(Z_1,\ldots,Z_n\) be independent symmetric \(X\)-valued random
variables, and let \(R_1,\ldots,R_n\) be independent Bernoulli variables,
independent of the \(Z_i\), with \(\Pp(R_i=1)\le a\le1\).  Then
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i R_iZ_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  K_{p,q,X} a^\alpha
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i Z_i}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First suppose \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\in X\) are deterministic and that
\(R_i\) are iid Bernoulli \(a\).  Apply Theorem \ref{thm:mp} to
\(\theta_i=R_i\eps_i\).  Since
\[
  \Pp(|\theta_1|>t)=
  \begin{cases}
  a, &0\le t<1,\\
  0, &t\ge1,
  \end{cases}
\]
the tail integral equals \(a^\alpha\).  Thus
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i R_i\eps_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  K_{p,q,X}a^\alpha
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i\eps_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]

If the Bernoulli parameters are \(\rho_i\le a\), realize a Bernoulli \(a\)
signed selector as the smaller selector plus an independent centered remainder.
First select \(R_i\) with parameter \(\rho_i\) and sign \(\eps_i\).  If
\(\rho_i<1\), then on the event \(R_i=0\) add an extra selector \(E_i\) with
conditional probability \((a-\rho_i)/(1-\rho_i)\), and give this extra selector
a fresh independent sign \(\delta_i\).  If \(\rho_i=1\), necessarily \(a=1\)
and no extra selector is needed.  The scalar
\[
  \Theta_i=R_i\eps_i+(1-R_i)E_i\delta_i
\]
is symmetric and has the Bernoulli \(a\) signed-selector law.  Moreover,
conditionally on the smaller thinning \((R_i,\eps_i)_i\), the extra sum has
mean zero.  Jensen's inequality for the convex function
\(x\mapsto\norm{x}^p\) gives
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i R_i\eps_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i \Theta_i x_i}^p\right)^{1/p},
\]
so the same bound holds for arbitrary \(\rho_i\le a\).

For random symmetric \(Z_i\), condition on their values \(z_i\) and insert
independent Rademacher signs.  Because \(Z_i\) is symmetric,
\((\eps_i Z_i)_i\) has the same joint law as \((Z_i)_i\), and similarly after
thinning.  Integrating the deterministic estimate over the \(z_i\)'s proves
the lemma.
\end{proof}

\section*{Finite-Cotype De-Poissonization}

For a finite independent symmetric family \(Z_1,\ldots,Z_n\), write
\[
  \mu_Z=\sum_i\mathcal L(Z_i)|_{X\setminus\{0\}}.
\]
Let \(\Pi_{\mu_Z}\) be the compound-Poisson sum with intensity \(\mu_Z\).

\begin{proposition}[Symmetric de-Poissonization in finite cotype]
\label{prop:depoisson}
Let \(X\) have finite cotype and \(1\le p<\infty\).  Then
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\Pi_{\mu_Z}}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  D_{p,X}
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i Z_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
\]
for every finite independent symmetric \(X\)-valued family \(Z_i\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(p_i=\Pp(Z_i\ne0)\).  If \(p_i>0\), write \(U_i\) for the conditional
law of \(Z_i\) given \(Z_i\ne0\); this conditional law remains symmetric.
Let \(N_i\) be independent Poisson variables with means \(p_i\), and let
\((U_{i,k})_{k\ge1}\) be independent copies of \(U_i\).  Then
\[
  \Pi_{\mu_Z}=\sum_i\sum_{k=1}^{N_i}U_{i,k}
  =
  \sum_{k\ge1} L_k,
  \qquad
  L_k=\sum_i \1_{\{N_i\ge k\}}U_{i,k}.
\]
By Minkowski's inequality in \(L^p(\Omega;X)\),
\[
  \norm{\Pi_{\mu_Z}}_{L^p}
  \le
  \sum_{k\ge1}\norm{L_k}_{L^p}.
\]

For \(0\le s\le1\),
\[
  \Pp(\operatorname{Pois}(s)\ge k)
  \le
  \E\binom{\operatorname{Pois}(s)}{k}
  =
  \frac{s^k}{k!}
  \le
  \frac{s}{k!}.
\]
Thus, if \(p_i>0\), the \(i\)-th summand in \(L_k\) is distributed as
\(R_{i,k}Z_i\) with thinning parameter
\[
  \rho_{i,k}=\frac{\Pp(\operatorname{Pois}(p_i)\ge k)}{p_i}
  \le \frac{1}{k!};
\]
the terms with \(p_i=0\) are zero.  Lemma \ref{lem:thinning}
therefore gives
\[
  \norm{L_k}_{L^p}
  \le
  K_{p,X}(1/k!)^\alpha
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_i Z_i}^p\right)^{1/p},
\]
where \(\alpha=1/\max(p,q)>0\) for some finite cotype \(q\) of \(X\).
Since \(\sum_{k\ge1}(1/k!)^\alpha<\infty\), summing over \(k\) proves the
proposition.
\end{proof}

\section*{Aggregate Domination}

\begin{lemma}[Lower Poissonization]
\label{lem:lower}
For every Banach space \(X\), every \(1\le p<\infty\), and every finite
independent symmetric family \(Z_i\),
\[
  \left(\E\norm{\sum_iZ_i}^p\right)^{1/p}
  \le
  2\left(\E\norm{\Pi_{\mu_Z}}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the lower Poissonization lemma from the companion conditional packet,
but we recall the proof.  Write \(Z_i=B_iU_i\), where \(B_i\) is Bernoulli
\(p_i=\Pp(Z_i\ne0)\) and \(U_i\) has the symmetric conditional law of \(Z_i\)
given \(Z_i\ne0\).  Let \(R_i\) be iid Bernoulli variables with
\[
  c=1-e^{-1}>1/2,
\]
independent of everything else, and put \(S=\sum_iB_iU_i\),
\(S_R=\sum_iR_iB_iU_i\).  The complement
\(\sum_i(1-R_i)B_iU_i\) is a \((1-c)\)-thinning of the original family.  Since
\(1-c<c\), it can be obtained as a sub-thinning of a \(c\)-thinning; conditioning
on the smaller thinning and adding the independent symmetric remainder back
gives, by Jensen,
\[
  \left\|\sum_i(1-R_i)B_iU_i\right\|_{L^p}
  \le \norm{S_R}_{L^p}.
\]
The triangle inequality gives \(\norm{S}_{L^p}\le 2\norm{S_R}_{L^p}\).

Now let \(N_i\) be independent Poisson variables with means \(p_i\).  Since
\[
  \Pp(N_i\ge1)=1-e^{-p_i}\ge(1-e^{-1})p_i=cp_i,\qquad 0\le p_i\le1,
\]
the first Poisson layer \(\sum_i\mathbf 1_{\{N_i\ge1\}}U_{i,1}\) contains, in
the same Jensen/sub-thinning sense, a copy of \(S_R\).  Thus
\[
  \norm{S_R}_{L^p}
  \le
  \left\|\sum_i \mathbf 1_{\{N_i\ge1\}}U_{i,1}\right\|_{L^p}.
\]
Finally, the full compound-Poisson sum is this first layer plus further
conditionally mean-zero symmetric layers, so Jensen gives that the first layer
is controlled by \(\Pi_{\mu_Z}\).  Combining the estimates proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Poisson monotonicity]
\label{lem:monotone}
Let \(\mu\le\nu\) be finite symmetric Borel measures on \(X\setminus\{0\}\).
Then
\[
  \norm{\Pi_\mu}_{L^p}\le\norm{\Pi_\nu}_{L^p}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By Poisson superposition, \(\Pi_\nu\) has the same law as
\(\Pi_\mu+\Pi_{\nu-\mu}\), with the two terms independent.  Since
\(\nu-\mu\) is symmetric, \(\Pi_{\nu-\mu}\) is mean zero after truncation.
Conditioning on \(\Pi_\mu\) and applying Jensen's inequality gives the
truncated claim, and then monotone convergence removes the truncation.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Symmetric aggregate comparison in finite cotype]
\label{prop:symmetric}
Theorem \ref{thm:discrete} holds when all \(\xi_i,\eta_j\) are symmetric.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\mu_\xi=\sum_i\mathcal L(\xi_i)|_{X\setminus\{0\}}\) and
\(\mu_\eta=\sum_j\mathcal L(\eta_j)|_{X\setminus\{0\}}\).  By assumption
\(\mu_\xi\le\mu_\eta\).  Lemma \ref{lem:lower}, Lemma \ref{lem:monotone},
and Proposition \ref{prop:depoisson} give
\[
  \norm{\sum_i\xi_i}_{L^p}
  \le
  2\norm{\Pi_{\mu_\xi}}_{L^p}
  \le
  2\norm{\Pi_{\mu_\eta}}_{L^p}
  \le
  2D_{p,X}\norm{\sum_j\eta_j}_{L^p}.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:discrete}]
Let \(\eps_i,\eps'_j\) be independent Rademacher signs, independent of all
variables.  Symmetrizing the laws preserves aggregate domination:
\[
  \sum_i\mathcal L(\eps_i\xi_i)|_{X\setminus\{0\}}
  \le
  \sum_j\mathcal L(\eps'_j\eta_j)|_{X\setminus\{0\}}.
\]
By the standard symmetrization inequalities for independent mean-zero
Banach-valued variables,
\[
  \norm{\sum_i\xi_i}_{L^p}
  \le
  2\norm{\sum_i\eps_i\xi_i}_{L^p},
  \qquad
  \norm{\sum_j\eps'_j\eta_j}_{L^p}
  \le
  2\norm{\sum_j\eta_j}_{L^p}.
\]
Apply Proposition \ref{prop:symmetric} to the symmetric families
\((\eps_i\xi_i)\) and \((\eps'_j\eta_j)\), and combine the three estimates.
\end{proof}

\section*{General Local Martingales}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:umd}]
Yaroslavtsev's quasi-left-continuous characteristic-domination theorem
\cite[Theorem 12.6]{Yaroslavtsev2019} handles the continuous and
quasi-left-continuous parts in UMD spaces.  In Remark 12.10, using the
detangling theorem for martingales with accessible jumps and the accessible
approximation proposition, the source identifies the remaining issue for
general local martingales as the discrete independent-increment
aggregate-domination problem stated there.  The finite form proved above is
the one used at the approximation level: the source's accessible-jump
approximation truncates a purely discontinuous accessible martingale to
finitely many predictable jumps \(M^m\), proves the desired finite discrete
estimate for those jumps, and then lets \(m\to\infty\) in \(L^p\).

Every UMD Banach space has finite cotype.  Therefore Theorem
\ref{thm:discrete} supplies the missing accessible-jump comparison in \(X\).
Substituting this comparison into the source reduction gives the maximal
\(L^p\) estimate for general local martingales.
\end{proof}
