\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate partial result, likely valid, for Remark 12.10 of
Yaroslavtsev's paper \emph{Local characteristics and tangency of vector-valued
martingales} \cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}.  It proves the full
general-local-martingale characteristic-domination conclusion for
\(X=L^q(S)\), \(1<q<\infty\), and \(1<p<\infty\).

It is partial relative to the source's broad UMD-space problem because it only
handles \(L^q\)-spaces, and it does not cover the endpoint \(p=1\).

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let \(1<p,q<\infty\), let \((S,\Sigma,\rho)\) be a measure space, and put
\(X=L^q(S)\).  Let \(N\) and \(M\) be \(X\)-valued local martingales.  Assume
that \(N\) is characteristically dominated by \(M\) in the sense of
\cite[Definition 12.9]{Yaroslavtsev2019}: for every \(x^*\in X^*\),
\[
  |\langle N_0,x^*\rangle|\le |\langle M_0,x^*\rangle|,\qquad
  [\![N^c]\!]_\infty\le [\![M^c]\!]_\infty,
\]
and
\[
  \nu^N(\R_+\times B)\le \nu^M(\R_+\times B)
  \qquad (B\in\cB(X),\;0\notin B)
\]
almost surely.  Then
\[
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{N_t}_{L^q(S)}^p
  \le
  C_{p,q}\,
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{M_t}_{L^q(S)}^p .
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The earlier discrete packet proved the independent-sum bottleneck by using the
sharp \(L^q\)-valued Rosenthal theorem of Dirksen and Yaroslavtsev
\cite{DirksenYaroslavtsev2017}.  The general local case has one extra trap:
characteristic domination compares only the total spatial jump compensators
\(\nu(\R_+\times\cdot)\).  It does not compare the accessible and
quasi-left-continuous jump components separately.

The way around this is to show that, in \(L^q\), the relevant Rosenthal
sum/intersection norm of the identity jump \(x\) depends only on the total
spatial compensator.  Time labels and the accessible/qlc tag can be erased.
For the basic \(S\), \(D_{q,q}\), and \(D_{p,q}\) gauges this is immediate
from the formulas.  For sum spaces, a time-dependent split can be conditionally
averaged over the spatial jump value \(x\); Jensen's inequality decreases all
three gauges.  Thus the jump part has a single monotone norm controlled by
\(\nu(\R_+\times\cdot)\).  The continuous part is already controlled by
Yaroslavtsev's continuous characteristic-domination estimate.

\section*{The Total Jump Rosenthal Norm}

Let \(\bar\nu=\bar\nu_\omega\) be a predictable random measure on
\(X\setminus\{0\}\), where \(X=L^q(S)\).  For a measurable
\(F:\Omega\times X\to X\), define
\[
  \norm{F}_{\mathsf S(\bar\nu)}
  =
  \left(
  \E \left\|
      \left(\int_X |F(\omega,x)|^2\,\bar\nu_\omega(dx)\right)^{1/2}
    \right\|_{L^q(S)}^p
  \right)^{1/p},
\]
\[
  \norm{F}_{\mathsf D_{r}(\bar\nu)}
  =
  \left(
  \E \int_X \norm{F(\omega,x)}_{L^q(S)}^r\,\bar\nu_\omega(dx)
  \right)^{1/r}
  \quad (r=p),
\]
and
\[
  \norm{F}_{\mathsf D_q(\bar\nu)}
  =
  \left(
  \E \left(\int_X \norm{F(\omega,x)}_{L^q(S)}^q\,\bar\nu_\omega(dx)\right)^{p/q}
  \right)^{1/p}.
\]
Let \(\mathsf R_{p,q}(\bar\nu)\) be the norm of the identity map
\(I(\omega,x)=x\) in the same six sum/intersection spaces as in the
Dirksen--Yaroslavtsev theorem:
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
\mathsf S\cap \mathsf D_q\cap \mathsf D_p, & 2\le q\le p<\infty,\\
\mathsf S\cap(\mathsf D_q+\mathsf D_p), & 2\le p\le q<\infty,\\
(\mathsf S\cap \mathsf D_q)+\mathsf D_p, & 1<p<2\le q<\infty,\\
(\mathsf S+\mathsf D_q)\cap\mathsf D_p, & 1<q<2\le p<\infty,\\
\mathsf S+(\mathsf D_q\cap\mathsf D_p), & 1<q\le p\le2,\\
\mathsf S+\mathsf D_q+\mathsf D_p, & 1<p\le q\le2.
\end{array}
\]
Intersections carry the maximum norm, and sums carry the usual infimum norm.

\section*{Collapse and Monotonicity}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:collapse}
Let \(\kappa_\omega(dt,dx)\) be a predictable random measure on
\(\R_+\times X\), and let
\[
  \bar\kappa_\omega(B)=\kappa_\omega(\R_+\times B)
\]
be its total spatial marginal.  The Rosenthal sum/intersection norm of the
identity jump \(x\), computed with respect to \(\kappa\), equals the same norm
computed with respect to \(\bar\kappa\).  In particular it is insensitive to
the time distribution of the compensator.

Moreover, if \(\bar\nu_\omega\le \bar\mu_\omega\) almost surely, then
\[
  \mathsf R_{p,q}(\bar\nu)\le \mathsf R_{p,q}(\bar\mu).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For the basic gauges, the first assertion is just Fubini:
\[
  \int_{\R_+\times X}|x|^2\,\kappa_\omega(dt,dx)
  =
  \int_X |x|^2\,\bar\kappa_\omega(dx),
\]
and similarly for \(\norm{x}_{L^q}^p\) and \(\norm{x}_{L^q}^q\).

The only point is a sum norm, where one may have allowed a decomposition
\[
   x=F_1(\omega,t,x)+\cdots+F_m(\omega,t,x)
\]
depending on time.  Disintegrate \(\kappa_\omega(dt,dx)\) over its
\(x\)-marginal and define
\[
   G_j(\omega,x)=\E_\kappa(F_j(\omega,t,x)\mid \omega,x).
\]
Then \(x=\sum_j G_j(\omega,x)\).  Since \(u\mapsto |u|^2\) in the lattice
order and \(u\mapsto\norm{u}_{L^q}^r\) for \(r=p,q\) are convex, Jensen's
inequality gives
\[
 \int |G_j|^2\,d\bar\kappa\le \int |F_j|^2\,d\kappa,\qquad
 \int \norm{G_j}_{L^q}^r\,d\bar\kappa\le
 \int \norm{F_j}_{L^q}^r\,d\kappa .
\]
After taking the outer \(L^p(\Omega)\) norms, no basic gauge increases.  Thus
time-dependent decompositions give no smaller sum/intersection norm than
spatial decompositions.  The reverse inequality is obtained by viewing any
spatial decomposition as time-independent.

For monotonicity, the three basic gauges are monotone under measure domination.
For sums, argue recursively.  Suppose a sum expression \(E+F\) is under
consideration and choose an almost optimal split \(I=U+V\) for the larger
measure \(\bar\mu\).  Let \(h=d\bar\nu/d\bar\mu\), \(0\le h\le1\).  Restrict
the split measure of \((x,U,V)\) by the weight \(h(\omega,x)\).  This gives a
split of the identity under \(\bar\nu\), and the aggregate measures of the two
pieces are dominated by the corresponding pieces for \(\bar\mu\).  The
recursive hypothesis bounds the \(E\)- and \(F\)-norms of the restricted
pieces by those of \(U\) and \(V\).  Letting the near-optimality error tend to
zero gives the sum step.  Intersections are immediate from the maximum norm.

The argument is first written for simple finite-range decompositions.  The
general case follows by the standard separable-range and monotone/density
approximations used in the Dirksen--Yaroslavtsev spaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:jumpnorm}
Let \(L\) be an \(L^q(S)\)-valued local martingale with \(L_0=0\), and let
\(L^d=L^q+L^a\) be the purely discontinuous part of its canonical
decomposition.  Then, for \(1<p,q<\infty\),
\[
   \left(\E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{L^d_t}_{L^q(S)}^p\right)^{1/p}
   \simeqc
   \mathsf R_{p,q}\bigl(\nu^L(\R_+\times\cdot)\bigr).
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Dirksen and Yaroslavtsev prove sharp estimates for both jump types.  For the
quasi-left-continuous part, their non-atomic random-measure theorem identifies
the maximal \(L^p(\Omega;L^q(S))\) norm of
\(\int x\,d\bar\mu^{L^q}\) with the \(\cI_{p,q}\)-norm computed from the
compensator \(\nu^{L^q}\).  For the accessible part, their accessible-jump
theorem identifies the maximal norm with the accessible \(\cA_{p,q}\)-norm,
whose defining predictable square and \(D\)-gauges are exactly the same
integrals against the accessible compensator \(\nu^{L^a}\).

Their combined general-local estimate, applied with continuous part zero, gives
\[
   \norm{L^d}_{p,*}
   \simeqc
   \norm{x}_{\cI_{p,q}(\nu^{L^q})}
   +
   \norm{L^a}_{\cA_{p,q}},
\]
where \(\norm{L}_{p,*}:=(\E\sup_t\norm{L_t}_{L^q}^p)^{1/p}\).  By Lemma
\ref{lem:collapse}, the right side is equivalent to the single
sum/intersection norm of the identity with respect to the total spatial
measure
\[
  \nu^{L^q}(\R_+\times\cdot)+\nu^{L^a}(\R_+\times\cdot)
  =
  \nu^L(\R_+\times\cdot).
\]
This proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:main}}

It suffices to treat martingales starting at zero; the initial values are
handled at the end.  Since \(L^q(S)\) is UMD for \(1<q<\infty\), Yaroslavtsev's
canonical decomposition theorem \cite[Theorem 3.5]{Yaroslavtsev2019} applies.
Write
\[
   M=M^c+M^d,\qquad N=N^c+N^d,
\]
where \(M^d=M^q+M^a\) and \(N^d=N^q+N^a\).

For the continuous terms, Yaroslavtsev's continuous characteristic-domination
estimate, used in the source proof of the quasi-left-continuous domination
theorem, gives
\[
   \left(\E\sup_t\norm{N^c_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}
   \lesssimc
   \left(\E\sup_t\norm{M^c_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\]

For the discontinuous terms, Corollary \ref{cor:jumpnorm} and the total
compensator domination give
\[
\begin{aligned}
   \left(\E\sup_t\norm{N^d_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}
   &\simeqc
   \mathsf R_{p,q}\bigl(\nu^N(\R_+\times\cdot)\bigr)\\
   &\le
   \mathsf R_{p,q}\bigl(\nu^M(\R_+\times\cdot)\bigr)\\
   &\simeqc
   \left(\E\sup_t\norm{M^d_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Notice that no comparison of accessible parts with accessible parts, or
quasi-left-continuous parts with quasi-left-continuous parts, is used here.
The comparison is made only after the two jump types have been folded into the
total spatial compensator.

Combining the continuous and discontinuous estimates, and using the strong
\(L^p\) equivalence for the canonical decomposition
\cite[Theorem 3.5]{Yaroslavtsev2019}, yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{N_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}
 &\le
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{N^c_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}
 +
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{N^d_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}\\
 &\lesssimc
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{M^c_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}
 +
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{M^d_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}\\
 &\lesssimc
 \left(\E\sup_t\norm{M_t}_{L^q}^p\right)^{1/p}.
\end{aligned}
\]

If the martingales do not start at zero, the hypothesis on initial values
implies \(\norm{N_0}_{L^q}\le\norm{M_0}_{L^q}\).  Apply the zero-start result
to \(N-N_0\) and \(M-M_0\), and use
\[
  \sup_t\norm{N_t}_{L^q}
  \le
  \norm{N_0}_{L^q}+\sup_t\norm{N_t-N_0}_{L^q},
\]
together with the analogous bound for \(M\).  This gives the stated
inequality.

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof was checked against the source passages:
\begin{itemize}
\item Yaroslavtsev's canonical-decomposition theorem: canonical decomposition
and strong maximal \(L^p\) equivalence in UMD spaces.
\item Yaroslavtsev's decoupled-tangent and uniqueness results: independent
increments given local characteristics, used as background for Remark 12.10's
reduction.
\item Yaroslavtsev \cite[Definition 12.9 and Remark 12.10]{Yaroslavtsev2019}:
characteristic domination compares the continuous covariation and the total
spatial jump compensator.
\item Dirksen--Yaroslavtsev's accessible-jump, non-atomic random-measure, and
combined general-local estimates: sharp \(L^q\)-valued estimates for the jump
parts used in Corollary \ref{cor:jumpnorm}.

The main audit point is Lemma \ref{lem:collapse}.  It is the step that prevents
the false componentwise comparison
\(\nu^{N^a}\le\nu^{M^a}\), which characteristic domination does not provide.

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

The result covers arbitrary measure spaces \(S\), all \(1<q<\infty\), and all
\(1<p<\infty\).  It does not cover \(p=1\), because the sharp
Dirksen--Yaroslavtsev \(L^q\) Rosenthal estimates used here are stated for
\(1<p<\infty\).  It does not settle the source question for arbitrary UMD
spaces.

Cheap run-index searches found earlier packets for \(c_0\), Hilbert spaces,
\(\ell_q\) at the natural exponent, and the discrete \(L^q\) aggregate
comparison, but no packet proving the full \(L^q\) general-local conclusion.
The literature checked for this push included arXiv:1907.11588,
arXiv:1707.00109, and nearby BDG/local-characteristics material.  I found the
ingredients above, but not an explicit statement of this total-compensator
\(L^q\) consequence.
