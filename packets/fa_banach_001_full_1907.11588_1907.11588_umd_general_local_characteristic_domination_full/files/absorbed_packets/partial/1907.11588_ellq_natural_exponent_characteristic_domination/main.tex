\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate partial result, likely valid, for Remark 12.10 of
Yaroslavtsev's paper \emph{Local characteristics and tangency of vector-valued
martingales} \cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}.  It proves the general-local-martingale
characteristic-domination estimate for the non-Hilbert UMD spaces
\(\ell_q\), \(1<q<\infty\), at the natural exponent \(p=q\).

The result is partial relative to the source question because it does not cover
all exponents \(p\) in \(\ell_q\), all UMD spaces, or nonatomic \(L^q\)-spaces.
It is nevertheless a genuine non-Hilbert positive case when \(q\ne2\).

\section*{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:ellq-natural}
Let \(1<q<\infty\).  Let \(M\) and \(N\) be \(\ell_q\)-valued local
martingales such that \(N\) is characteristically dominated by \(M\) in the
sense of \cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}.  Then
\[
  \E \sup_{t\geq0}\norm{N_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}
  \leq
  C_q\,\E \sup_{t\geq0}\norm{M_t}_{\ell_q}^{q},
\]
whenever the right hand side is finite.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The Hilbert proof cannot simply be copied into \(\ell_q\), since
\(\norm{x}^2\) is no longer generated by an inner product.  The replacement is
to use the lattice square-function theorem in exactly the exponent where the
\(\ell_q\)-norm is additive across coordinates.

Write \(M^k=\langle M,e_k^*\rangle\) and \(N^k=\langle N,e_k^*\rangle\).
Characteristic domination pushes through the coordinate projection \(e_k^*\),
so each scalar martingale \(N^k\) is characteristically dominated by \(M^k\).
The scalar case is the one-dimensional Hilbert case, already supplied by the
companion Hilbert all-\(p\) packet.  Hence scalar BDG gives
\[
  \E [N^k]_\infty^{q/2}
  \lesssim_q
  \E [M^k]_\infty^{q/2}.
\]
Now apply Yaroslavtsev's UMD Banach-function-space BDG theorem
\cite{Yaroslavtsev2018} to \(\ell_q=L^q(\mathbb N)\):
\[
  \E \sup_t\norm{N_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}
  \simequiv_q
  \E \left\|\bigl([N^k]_\infty^{1/2}\bigr)_{k\ge1}\right\|_{\ell_q}^{q}
  =
  \sum_{k\ge1}\E [N^k]_\infty^{q/2}.
\]
Summing the scalar estimates and applying the same square-function theorem to
\(M\) gives the result.

\section*{Two Inputs}

\begin{lemma}[Scalar characteristic domination]
\label{lem:scalar-input}
Let \(A\) and \(B\) be real-valued local martingales with \(A_0=B_0=0\).  If
\(A\) is characteristically dominated by \(B\), then for every
\(1<q<\infty\),
\[
  \E [A]_\infty^{q/2}
  \leq
  C_q \E [B]_\infty^{q/2}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The companion Hilbert packet \cite{Lane19HilbertPacket} proves the
characteristic-domination estimate for Hilbert-valued martingales for every
finite exponent.  Applied to the one-dimensional Hilbert space \(\R\), it gives
\[
   \E\sup_{t\ge0}|A_t|^q
   \leq
   C_q \E\sup_{t\ge0}|B_t|^q .
\]
The scalar Burkholder-Davis-Gundy inequalities convert both maximal moments to
quadratic-variation moments:
\[
   \E [A]_\infty^{q/2}
   \simequiv_q
   \E\sup_{t\ge0}|A_t|^q,
   \qquad
   \E [B]_\infty^{q/2}
   \simequiv_q
   \E\sup_{t\ge0}|B_t|^q .
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[\(\ell_q\) square function for local martingales]
\label{lem:ellq-bdg}
Let \(1<q<\infty\), and let \(L\) be an \(\ell_q\)-valued local martingale
with \(L_0=0\).  Write \(L^k=\langle L,e_k^*\rangle\).  Then
\[
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{L_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}
  \simequiv_q
  \sum_{k\ge1} \E [L^k]_\infty^{q/2}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The space \(\ell_q\) is the UMD Banach function space
\(L^q(\mathbb N,\#)\).  Yaroslavtsev's Banach-function-space BDG theorem
\cite[Theorem 5.9]{Yaroslavtsev2018} gives, for the coordinate martingale
field \(L^k\),
\[
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{L_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}
  \simequiv_q
  \E \left\|\bigl([L^k]_\infty^{1/2}\bigr)_{k\ge1}\right\|_{\ell_q}^{q}.
\]
The last expression is
\[
  \E \sum_{k\ge1} [L^k]_\infty^{q/2}
  =
  \sum_{k\ge1} \E [L^k]_\infty^{q/2}
\]
by Tonelli's theorem.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of the Main Result}

First assume \(M_0=N_0=0\).  For each coordinate \(k\), let
\(\pi_k:\ell_q\to\R\) be the bounded coordinate projection.  We claim that
\(\pi_kN=N^k\) is characteristically dominated by \(\pi_kM=M^k\).

The continuous part follows directly from the bilinear quadratic-variation
order in the definition of characteristic domination:
\[
   [N^{k,c}]_\infty
   =
   [\langle N^c,e_k^*\rangle]_\infty
   \leq
   [\langle M^c,e_k^*\rangle]_\infty
   =
   [M^{k,c}]_\infty .
\]
For the jump part, if \(A\subset\R\setminus\{0\}\) is Borel, the jump
compensator of the scalar coordinate is the pushforward of the vector jump
compensator:
\[
   \nu^{N^k}(\R_+\times A)
   =
   \nu^N(\R_+\times \pi_k^{-1}(A)).
\]
Since \(\pi_k^{-1}(A)\subset \ell_q\setminus\{0\}\), characteristic domination
of \(N\) by \(M\) gives
\[
   \nu^{N^k}(\R_+\times A)
   \leq
   \nu^M(\R_+\times \pi_k^{-1}(A))
   =
   \nu^{M^k}(\R_+\times A).
\]
The initial condition is zero in the reduced case.  Thus \(N^k\) is
characteristically dominated by \(M^k\).

Lemma \ref{lem:scalar-input} yields
\[
  \E [N^k]_\infty^{q/2}
  \leq
  C_q\,\E [M^k]_\infty^{q/2}
  \qquad(k\ge1).
\]
Summing over \(k\) and applying Lemma \ref{lem:ellq-bdg} to \(N\) and \(M\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{N_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}
  &\lesssim_q
  \sum_{k\ge1}\E [N^k]_\infty^{q/2} \\
  &\leq
  C_q\sum_{k\ge1}\E [M^k]_\infty^{q/2} \\
  &\lesssim_q
  \E\sup_{t\ge0}\norm{M_t}_{\ell_q}^{q}.
\end{aligned}
\]

For general initial values, set \(\widetilde M_t=M_t-M_0\) and
\(\widetilde N_t=N_t-N_0\).  The continuous and jump characteristics are
unchanged, so the zero-initial estimate applies to
\(\widetilde N,\widetilde M\).  The initial part is controlled coordinatewise:
the definition of characteristic domination gives
\(|N_0^k|\leq |M_0^k|\) for every \(k\), hence
\[
   \E\norm{N_0}_{\ell_q}^q
   =
   \sum_k\E |N_0^k|^q
   \leq
   \sum_k\E |M_0^k|^q
   =
   \E\norm{M_0}_{\ell_q}^q
   \leq
   \E\sup_t\norm{M_t}_{\ell_q}^q .
\]
Using \(\sup_t\norm{N_t}\leq \norm{N_0}+\sup_t\norm{\widetilde N_t}\) and the
analogous bound for \(\widetilde M\) proves Theorem
\ref{thm:ellq-natural}.

\section*{Discrete Consequence}

\begin{corollary}
Let \(1<q<\infty\).  Let \((\xi_n)_{n=1}^N\) and
\((\eta_n)_{n=1}^N\) be independent mean-zero \(\ell_q\)-valued random
variables such that
\[
   \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb P(\xi_n\in B)
   \leq
   \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb P(\eta_n\in B)
   \qquad
   (B\subset\ell_q\setminus\{0\}\text{ Borel}).
\]
Then
\[
   \E\max_{1\leq m\leq N}
   \norm{\sum_{n=1}^m\xi_n}_{\ell_q}^q
   \leq
   C_q
   \E\max_{1\leq m\leq N}
   \norm{\sum_{n=1}^m\eta_n}_{\ell_q}^q .
\]
In particular the same estimate holds for the terminal sums.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Put the increments at deterministic times \(1,\ldots,N\).  Independence makes
the predictable jump compensator of the \(\xi\)-sum equal to
\(\sum_n\mathcal L(\xi_n)\), and similarly for the \(\eta\)-sum.  The
aggregate domination hypothesis is exactly characteristic domination of these
discrete-time martingales; the continuous characteristics and initial values
are zero.  Theorem \ref{thm:ellq-natural} gives the maximal estimate, and the
terminal estimate follows by dropping the maximum.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This packet does not settle the source problem for arbitrary \(p\) in
\(\ell_q\).  The proof uses the identity
\[
  \left\|\bigl(a_k^{1/2}\bigr)_k\right\|_{\ell_q}^{q}
  =
  \sum_k a_k^{q/2},
\]
so the exponent \(p=q\) is built into the summation step.  A different tail or
good-lambda argument would be needed to decouple the martingale moment
exponent from the lattice exponent.

The proof also does not directly cover nonatomic \(L^q(S)\).  The coordinate
pushforward argument relies on bounded coordinate functionals and on
\(\ell_q=L^q(\mathbb N)\) being atomic.  Point evaluations are not bounded
linear functionals on a general \(L^q(S)\), so the same proof has no immediate
nonatomic formulation.

Cheap run indexes contained the previous \(c_0\) counterexample and Hilbert
packets, but no non-Hilbert \(\ell_q\) packet for arXiv:1907.11588.  Web
searches for exact phrases around \texttt{characteristically dominated},
\(\ell_q\), \(L^q\), and \texttt{aggregate domination} did not locate an exact
existing result.  The nearby literature check identified the source paper
\cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}, Yaroslavtsev's UMD BDG theorem
\cite{Yaroslavtsev2018}, and the Dirksen--Yaroslavtsev \(L^q\)
Burkholder-Rosenthal paper \cite{DirksenYaroslavtsev2017} as surrounding
context, but not as an already-recorded answer to this exact subcase.
