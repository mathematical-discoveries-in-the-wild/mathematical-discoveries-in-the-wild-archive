\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate partial result, likely valid, for the open
general-local-martingale problem stated in Remark 12.10 of Yaroslavtsev's
paper \emph{Local characteristics and tangency of vector-valued martingales}
\cite{Yaroslavtsev2019}.  The result proves the desired strong estimate for
general Hilbert-valued local martingales at the exponent \(p=2\).  It is not a
solution of the full UMD problem for all exponents.

In the notation of the source, a local martingale \(N\) is characteristically
dominated by \(M\) if the initial weak coordinates are dominated, the
continuous covariation bilinear form of \(N\) is dominated by that of \(M\),
and the compensator \(\nu^N\) of the jump measure is dominated by
\(\nu^M\).  The question is whether the quasi-left-continuous estimate from
Theorem 12.8 remains true for general local martingales.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

At \(p=2\), Hilbert spaces retain exactly the information that characteristic
domination records.  For a Hilbert-valued square-integrable martingale, the
terminal second moment is the initial square norm plus the trace of the
continuous quadratic covariation plus the integral of \(\norm{x}^2\) against
the jump compensator.  Characteristic domination decreases each of these
terms.  Thus the terminal \(L^2\) estimate has constant \(1\).  The strong
maximal estimate follows by Doob's \(L^2\) inequality.

\section*{Hilbert \(L^2\) Subcase of the General Problem}

\begin{lemma}[Hilbert square identity]
Let \(H\) be a separable Hilbert space and let \(M\) be an \(H\)-valued
square-integrable martingale on \([0,T]\).  Let
\(\langle\!\langle M^c\rangle\!\rangle\) denote the predictable covariation
bilinear form of the continuous part, and let \(\nu^M\) be the compensator of
the jump measure.  Then
\[
  \E\norm{M_T}^2
  =
  \E\norm{M_0}^2
  + \E\,\operatorname{tr}\langle\!\langle M^c\rangle\!\rangle_T
  + \E\int_{[0,T]\times H}\norm{x}^2\,d\nu^M(t,x),
\]
with the value \(+\infty\) allowed.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For finite-dimensional \(H\), this is the usual coordinatewise martingale
isometry applied to an orthonormal basis: the continuous part contributes the
trace of its predictable covariation and the purely discontinuous part
contributes the compensator of \(\sum_{s\leq T}\norm{\Delta M_s}^2\).

For separable \(H\), let \(P_m\) be the orthogonal projection onto the span of
the first \(m\) vectors of an orthonormal basis.  Apply the finite-dimensional
identity to \(P_m M\).  Then let \(m\to\infty\) and use monotone convergence for
\(\norm{P_m M_T}^2\), for the finite-dimensional traces, and for
\(\int \norm{P_m x}^2\,d\nu^M(t,x)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(H\) be a separable Hilbert space.  Let \(M\) and \(N\) be \(H\)-valued
local martingales such that \(N\) is characteristically dominated by \(M\) in
the sense of Yaroslavtsev.  Then for every bounded stopping time \(\tau\),
\[
   \E\norm{N_\tau}^2 \leq \E\norm{M_\tau}^2.
\]
Consequently, if \(\E\sup_{t\geq 0}\norm{M_t}^2<\infty\), then
\[
   \E\sup_{t\geq 0}\norm{N_t}^2
   \leq
   4\,\E\sup_{t\geq 0}\norm{M_t}^2.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First assume that \(M^\tau\) and \(N^\tau\) are square integrable.  By the
initial-coordinate domination, outside one null set
\[
   |\ip{N_0}{h}|\leq |\ip{M_0}{h}|
   \qquad\text{for every }h\in H.
\]
Taking \(h=N_0/\norm{N_0}\) when \(N_0\neq0\) gives
\(\norm{N_0}\leq\norm{M_0}\).

Characteristic domination of the continuous characteristics says that the
bilinear form
\[
  \langle\!\langle M^c\rangle\!\rangle_\tau
  -
  \langle\!\langle N^c\rangle\!\rangle_\tau
\]
is nonnegative.  Therefore its trace is nonnegative, so
\[
 \operatorname{tr}\langle\!\langle N^c\rangle\!\rangle_\tau
 \leq
 \operatorname{tr}\langle\!\langle M^c\rangle\!\rangle_\tau .
\]
The jump-compensator domination \(\nu^N\leq \nu^M\) gives
\[
  \int_{[0,\tau]\times H}\norm{x}^2\,d\nu^N(t,x)
  \leq
  \int_{[0,\tau]\times H}\norm{x}^2\,d\nu^M(t,x).
\]
Applying the Hilbert square identity to \(N^\tau\) and \(M^\tau\), and
comparing the three terms, yields
\[
   \E\norm{N_\tau}^2 \leq \E\norm{M_\tau}^2.
\]

For general local martingales, apply this argument to
\(\tau\wedge\rho_k\), where \((\rho_k)\) is a common localizing sequence for
which both stopped martingales are square integrable.  Characteristic
domination is preserved by stopping.  If \(\E\norm{M_\tau}^2=\infty\), there is
nothing to prove.  Otherwise, optional sampling for the submartingale
\(\norm{M_t}^2\) gives
\(\E\norm{M_{\tau\wedge\rho_k}}^2\leq \E\norm{M_\tau}^2\), while Fatou's lemma
gives
\[
   \E\norm{N_\tau}^2
   \leq
   \liminf_k \E\norm{N_{\tau\wedge\rho_k}}^2
   \leq
   \E\norm{M_\tau}^2 .
\]

Finally, for each deterministic \(T\), Doob's \(L^2\) maximal inequality for
the submartingale \(\norm{N_t}\) gives
\[
  \E\sup_{0\leq t\leq T}\norm{N_t}^2
  \leq
  4\,\E\norm{N_T}^2
  \leq
  4\,\E\norm{M_T}^2
  \leq
  4\,\E\sup_{0\leq t\leq T}\norm{M_t}^2.
\]
Letting \(T\to\infty\) proves the stated strong estimate.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Discrete aggregate domination in Hilbert space at \(p=2\)]
Let \((\xi_n)_{n=1}^N\) and \((\eta_n)_{n=1}^N\) be independent mean-zero
\(H\)-valued random variables.  If
\[
  \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb P(\xi_n\in B)
  \leq
  \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb P(\eta_n\in B)
  \qquad
  \text{for every Borel }B\subset H\setminus\{0\},
\]
then
\[
  \E\norm{\sum_{n=1}^N \xi_n}^2
  \leq
  \E\norm{\sum_{n=1}^N \eta_n}^2.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Independence and the mean-zero assumption give orthogonality in \(L^2(H)\):
\[
  \E\norm{\sum_{n=1}^N \xi_n}^2
  =
  \sum_{n=1}^N \E\norm{\xi_n}^2.
\]
The aggregate domination hypothesis, integrated against the nonnegative
function \(x\mapsto\norm{x}^2\), gives
\[
  \sum_{n=1}^N \E\norm{\xi_n}^2
  \leq
  \sum_{n=1}^N \E\norm{\eta_n}^2.
\]
The same orthogonality identity for \((\eta_n)\) completes the proof.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The proof is insensitive to accessible versus quasi-left-continuous jumps:
both jump types enter the Hilbert \(L^2\) identity through the same scalar
quantity \(\int\norm{x}^2\,d\nu\).  Thus the argument addresses precisely the
general-local-martingale issue at \(p=2\), not only the quasi-left-continuous
case already handled in the source paper.

The result is compatible with the previous \(c_0\) counterexample.  That
counterexample exploits the failure of Hilbert square additivity in sup norm.
In Hilbert space, grouping the same aggregate jump mass among independent
variables cannot change the terminal second moment.

\section*{Limitations and Next Routes}

This does not settle the original UMD-space problem for all \(p\), nor even the
Hilbert-space problem for \(p\neq2\).  For \(p>2\), the plausible route is to
combine Hilbert-valued martingale Rosenthal or Bichteler-Jacod inequalities
with domination of the second and \(p\)-th jump characteristic terms.  That
route was not promoted here because this packet keeps only the completely
self-contained \(L^2\) argument.
