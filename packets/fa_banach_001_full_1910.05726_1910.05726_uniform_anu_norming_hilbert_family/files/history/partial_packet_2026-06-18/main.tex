\section*{Source and Target}

The source paper is S. Dantas, M. Jung, and O. Roldan, \emph{Norm attaining operators which satisfy a Bollobas type theorem}, arXiv:1910.05726. After Proposition 2.8, the authors construct compact operators \(T\in \Anu(H)\), where \(H\) is a separable infinite-dimensional real Hilbert space, such that
\[
1=\nu(T)<\|T\|,
\]
and such that the Bollobas modulus in the definition of \(\Anu(H)\) can be chosen independently of the particular operator \(T\). They write:

\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

The phrase ``norming for the whole space'' is informal in the source. We answer the natural numerical-radius interpretation: whether such operators can form a family that norms vectors of \(H\) through their quadratic forms.

\section*{Definitions}

For an operator \(T\in\Lcal(H)\), its numerical radius is
\[
\nu(T)=\sup_{\|x\|=1}|\langle Tx,x\rangle|.
\]
In a real Hilbert space, the states in the definition of \(\Anu(H)\) reduce to pairs \((x,x)\), so \(T\in\Anu(H)\) with \(\nu(T)=1\) means: for every \(\varepsilon>0\) there is \(\eta(\varepsilon,T)>0\) such that whenever \(x\in S_H\) and
\[
|\langle Tx,x\rangle|>1-\eta(\varepsilon,T),
\]
there is \(x_1\in S_H\) with \(\langle Tx_1,x_1\rangle=1\) and \(\|x_1-x\|<\varepsilon\).

Call a family \(\mathscr F\subset\Lcal(H)\) \emph{numerically norming for \(H\)} if
\[
\|x\|^2=\sup_{T\in\mathscr F}|\langle Tx,x\rangle|
\qquad (x\in H).
\]

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Proposition 2.8 uses a finite-dimensional identity block and an infinite compact skew-symmetric tail. The skew-symmetric part does not contribute to \(\langle Tx,x\rangle\) over a real Hilbert space; only the identity block contributes. Thus if the identity block is \(F\), then
\[
\langle Tx,x\rangle=\|P_Fx\|^2.
\]
Near numerical-radius attainment forces \(x\) close to \(F\), and normalizing \(P_Fx\) gives an exact numerical-radius attaining point. Since we are free to choose \(F\), taking \(F=\operatorname{span}\{x\}\) gives a member of the family that norms any prescribed vector \(x\).

\section*{Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(H\) be a separable infinite-dimensional real Hilbert space. There is a family \(\mathscr F\subset\Lcal(H)\) such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item every \(T\in\mathscr F\) is compact;
\item \(1=\nu(T)<\|T\|\) for every \(T\in\mathscr F\);
\item every \(T\in\mathscr F\) belongs to \(\Anu(H)\) with the same admissible modulus \(\eta(\varepsilon)=\varepsilon^2/4\) for \(0<\varepsilon<1\);
\item \(\mathscr F\) is numerically norming for \(H\), that is,
\[
\|x\|^2=\sup_{T\in\mathscr F}|\langle Tx,x\rangle|
\qquad (x\in H).
\]
\end{enumerate}
Indeed, the family can be chosen so that for every nonzero finite-dimensional subspace \(F\subset H\), some \(T_F\in\mathscr F\) satisfies \(T_F|_F=I_F\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix a nonzero finite-dimensional subspace \(F\subset H\). Since \(H\) is separable and infinite-dimensional, choose an orthonormal decomposition
\[
H=F\oplus \bigoplus_{k=1}^{\infty} \operatorname{span}\{u_k,v_k\}.
\]
Choose real scalars \(\beta_1>1\), \(0<\beta_k<1\) for \(k\ge2\), and \(\beta_k\to0\). Define \(T_F\in\Lcal(H)\) by
\[
T_F f=f\quad(f\in F),\qquad
T_Fu_k=-\beta_k v_k,\qquad
T_Fv_k=\beta_k u_k .
\]
The operator is compact: it is the direct sum of the identity on finite-dimensional \(F\) and \(2\times2\) blocks whose norms \(\beta_k\) tend to zero. Also
\[
\|T_F\|=\max\{1,\sup_k\beta_k\}=\beta_1>1.
\]

Let \(x=f+w\), where \(f\in F\) and \(w\in F^\perp\). The block operator on \(F^\perp\) is skew-symmetric, hence
\[
\langle T_Fx,x\rangle=\|f\|^2+\langle T_Fw,w\rangle=\|f\|^2.
\]
Therefore \(\nu(T_F)\le1\), and equality holds on every unit vector of \(F\). Thus \(\nu(T_F)=1\), and \(T_F\) attains its numerical radius.

We next verify the uniform \(\Anu(H)\) estimate. Let \(0<\varepsilon<1\), and suppose \(x\in S_H\) satisfies
\[
|\langle T_Fx,x\rangle|>1-\varepsilon^2/4.
\]
Writing \(x=f+w\) as above, we get
\[
\|f\|^2>1-\varepsilon^2/4,\qquad \|w\|<\varepsilon/2.
\]
Set \(x_1=f/\|f\|\in S_F\). Then \(\langle T_Fx_1,x_1\rangle=1\), and
\[
\|x-x_1\|\le \|w\|+\left|1-\|f\|\right|
<\varepsilon/2+\varepsilon^2/4<\varepsilon .
\]
Thus the same modulus \(\eta(\varepsilon)=\varepsilon^2/4\) works for every \(T_F\) in the family.

Let \(\mathscr F=\{T_F:F\subset H\text{ nonzero finite dimensional}\}\). For any \(T_F\in\mathscr F\) and any \(x\in H\),
\[
|\langle T_Fx,x\rangle|\le \nu(T_F)\|x\|^2=\|x\|^2.
\]
Conversely, if \(x\neq0\), take \(F=\operatorname{span}\{x\}\). Then \(T_Fx=x\), so
\[
|\langle T_Fx,x\rangle|=\|x\|^2.
\]
The equality is trivial for \(x=0\). Hence \(\mathscr F\) is numerically norming for \(H\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and Limitations}

The proof is a direct parameterization of the source construction, with the finite identity block allowed to vary. No computational verification is relevant.

This is recorded as a partial result because the source's phrase ``norming for the whole space'' is not formalized there. The theorem gives an affirmative answer for the standard numerical-vector interpretation:
\[
\|x\|^2=\sup_{T\in\mathscr F}|\langle Tx,x\rangle|.
\]
It does not address a possible interpretation in which the set of operators is meant to be norming for the Banach space \(\Lcal(H)\) in a dual-pairing sense.
