\section{Source question}

Cluckers, Halupczok, Loeser, and Raibaut ask in Example 3.4.6 of
\cite{CHLR}, on PDF page 35, whether wave-front sets of special distributions
are definable; their examples expressly include pushforwards of smooth
distributions under polynomial maps.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The surrounding paper works with uniform families of
\(\cCexp\)-class distributions over non-archimedean local fields.  We use
``definable'' in its generalized Denef--Pas sense, as in \cite{CHLR}.

\section{Construction and statement}

Let \(F\) be a non-archimedean local field of odd residue characteristic,
with valuation \(\operatorname{ord}:F^\times\to\mathbb Z\), valuation ring
\(\cO_F\), and residue-field cardinality \(q_F\).  Haar measure is normalized
by \(\operatorname{vol}(\cO_F)=1\).  Put
\[
  X_F=(\cO_F\setminus\{0\})^2
\]
and, for \(a=(x,y)\in X_F\), define
\[
  h_F(a)=q_F^{\operatorname{ord}x}-\operatorname{ord}y.
\]
The function \(h_F\) is locally constant on \(X_F\).  Uniformly in \(F\), it
is a constructible, hence \(\cCexp\)-class, function: the formalism permits
integer-valued definable functions and generators \(q_F^{\alpha(a)}\).

Let \(u_F\) be the distribution on \(X_F\times\cO_F\) represented by the
smooth density
\[
  h_F(a)\,|da|\,|dt|.
\]
Its B-function is of \(\cCexp\)-class by closure under parameterized
integration, so \((u_F)_F\) is a smooth \(\cCexp\)-class family in the sense
of \cite{CHLR}.  Consider the restriction of the polynomial mapping
\[
\begin{split}
 f_F:X_F\times\cO_F&\longrightarrow X_F\times F,\\
 (a,t)&\longmapsto(a,t^2).
\end{split}
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
For every \(F\) as above, \(f_F\) is proper on \(\operatorname{Supp}(u_F)\),
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wf}
\WF_{F^\times}(f_{F,*}u_F)
=
\left\{
 ((a,0),(0,0,\eta)):
 a\in X_F,\ h_F(a)\ne0,\ \eta\in F^\times
\right\}.
\end{equation}
In particular, this wave-front set is not a definable subset of the cotangent
bundle.  Thus polynomial pushforward of a smooth \(\cCexp\)-class
distribution need not have definable wave-front set.
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof intuition}

The fold \(t\mapsto t^2\) has one critical value, zero.  Its pushforward of
Haar measure is smooth away from zero but has ball masses
\(q_F^{-\lceil r/2\rceil}\) at zero, which is incompatible with any locally
constant density there.  Multiplying the input by a locally constant
coefficient \(h_F(a)\) switches that fold singularity on exactly where
\(h_F(a)\ne0\).  The source paper's pushforward theorem forces every
wave-front covector into the vertical conormal line of the fold, and its
singular-support theorem forces this line to occur wherever the coefficient
is nonzero.  Finally, the switching locus is nondefinable because its
complement encodes the exponential graph \(n=q_F^m\) in the value group,
where only Presburger sets are definable.

\section{Proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:fold}
Let
\[
  \nu_F=(t\mapsto t^2)_*(1_{\cO_F}|dt|)
\]
be the pushforward distribution on \(F\).  Then
\(\Sing(\nu_F)=\{0\}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(r\ge0\), write
\(B_r(0)=\{s\in F:\operatorname{ord}s\ge r\}\).  Directly from the
definition of pushforward,
\[
 \nu_F(B_r(0))
 =\operatorname{vol}\{t\in\cO_F: \operatorname{ord}(t^2)\ge r\}
 =q_F^{-\lceil r/2\rceil}.
\]
If \(\nu_F\) were smooth near zero, it would be represented there by a
locally constant function \(g\).  For some \(R\), the function \(g\) would be
constant, say equal to \(c\), on \(B_R(0)\).  Consequently
\(\nu_F(B_r(0))=c q_F^{-r}\) for every \(r\ge R\), contradicting the displayed
formula.

For \(s_0\ne0\), the square map has no critical preimage: at any preimage
\(t_0\), its derivative is \(2t_0\ne0\).  The non-archimedean inverse function
theorem gives finitely many local inverse branches, and change of variables
represents \(\nu_F\) by a locally constant density near \(s_0\).  If \(s_0\)
has no preimage in \(\cO_F\), the distribution vanishes on a sufficiently
small neighborhood of \(s_0\), since the nonzero squares form a clopen subset
of \(F^\times\).  Hence \(\nu_F\) is smooth away from zero.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:sing}
For \(v_F=f_{F,*}u_F\),
\[
  \Sing(v_F)=\{(a,0):a\in X_F,\ h_F(a)\ne0\}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For a test function \(\varphi\) on \(X_F\times F\), Fubini's theorem gives
\[
 v_F(\varphi)
 =\int_{X_F}h_F(a)\int_{\cO_F}\varphi(a,t^2)\,|dt|\,|da|.
\]
Thus \(v_F=(h_F|da|)\otimes\nu_F\).  Both \(h_F\) and \(\nu_F\) are smooth
away from the claimed set, by local constancy of \(h_F\) and
Lemma~\ref{lem:fold}.

Fix \(a_0\) with \(h_F(a_0)\ne0\).  On a compact open neighborhood \(U\) of
\(a_0\), the function \(h_F\) is a nonzero constant.  If \(v_F\) were smooth
near \((a_0,0)\), test its locally constant representing density against a
Schwartz--Bruhat function in the \(a\)-variable supported in \(U\) and having
nonzero integral.  The resulting distribution in the \(s\)-variable would be
a nonzero scalar multiple of \(\nu_F\) and would be smooth near zero,
contrary to Lemma~\ref{lem:fold}.  Conversely, if \(h_F(a_0)=0\), local
constancy gives a neighborhood \(U\) on which \(h_F=0\), and \(v_F\) vanishes
on \(U\times F\).  This proves the equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
First, the support of \(u_F\) is
\[
  \{a\in X_F:h_F(a)\ne0\}\times\cO_F,
\]
a closed subset of \(X_F\times\cO_F\).  If \(K\subset X_F\times F\) is
compact, then
\(f_F^{-1}(K)\cap\operatorname{Supp}(u_F)\) is a closed subset of the compact
set \(\operatorname{pr}_{X_F}(K)\times\cO_F\).  Hence \(f_F\) is proper on
the support, as required for the pushforward.

The input \(u_F\) is smooth, so its wave-front set is empty.  The pushforward
theorem \cite[Theorem 2.9.6]{CHLR} therefore yields
\[
  \WF_{F^\times}(v_F)\subset N_{f_F}.
\]
The differential of \(f_F\) is
\[
 Df_F(a,t)=\operatorname{diag}(1,1,2t).
\]
It follows directly from the definition of \(N_{f_F}\) that
\[
 N_{f_F}
 =\{((a,0),(0,0,\eta)):a\in X_F,\ \eta\in F^\times\}.
\]
Indeed, a nonzero target covector is killed by the transpose differential
only at \(t=0\), and then only its last component can be nonzero.

The projection of wave-front set to the base equals singular support by
\cite[Proposition 2.8.3]{CHLR}.  Lemma~\ref{lem:sing} therefore implies that,
for each \(a\) with \(h_F(a)\ne0\), at least one point of
\(\WF_{F^\times}(v_F)\) lies over \((a,0)\).  The preceding upper bound makes
that covector \((0,0,\eta_0)\) for some \(\eta_0\ne0\).  Since this is the
\(F^\times\)-wave-front set, conicity under multiplication by \(F^\times\)
then supplies every \((0,0,\eta)\), \(\eta\ne0\).  There is no wave front over
\(h_F(a)=0\), again by Lemma~\ref{lem:sing}.  This proves
\eqref{eq:wf}.

It remains to prove nondefinability.  Let
\[
 Z_F=\{(x,y)\in X_F:h_F(x,y)=0\}.
\]
Its image under the definable valuation map is
\[
 \operatorname{ord}(Z_F)
 =\{(m,n)\in\mathbb N^2:n=q_F^m\}.
\]
If \(Z_F\) were definable, this image would be definable in the induced value
group structure.  By valued-field quantifier elimination in the generalized
Denef--Pas setting, that induced structure is Presburger.  But a
Presburger-definable function on \(\mathbb N\) is eventually affine on each
class modulo some fixed integer (a standard consequence of Presburger cell
decomposition; see \cite{CluckersPresburger}), whereas \(m\mapsto q_F^m\) is
exponential.  Thus \(Z_F\), and hence its complement \(\{h_F\ne0\}\), is not
definable.

Finally, if the wave-front set in \eqref{eq:wf} were definable, then so would
be its definable slice
\[
 \{a\in X_F:((a,0),(0,0,1))\in\WF_{F^\times}(v_F)\}
 =\{a\in X_F:h_F(a)\ne0\},
\]
a contradiction.  The theorem follows.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification notes}

The proof uses no numerical or symbolic computation.  The following checks
are independent and exact.
\begin{itemize}
\item The domain and target are definable open submanifolds, and \(f_F\) is
the restriction of a polynomial map.
\item The density is locally constant and constructible, so the input is both
smooth and of \(\cCexp\)-class.
\item Properness is checked on the actual support, not assumed globally.
\item The singularity of the fold measure is witnessed by its exact ball
masses; no asymptotic estimate is used.
\item The exact wave-front equality combines two source results: the
pushforward upper bound and equality of projected wave front with singular
support.
\item Nondefinability is reduced to the standard one-variable structure of
Presburger-definable functions.
\end{itemize}

\section{Scope and interpretation}

The density \(h_F\) is a legitimate smooth constructible/\(\cCexp\)-class
density, but its zero locus is not definable.  The construction therefore
disproves a blanket claim that polynomial pushforwards of smooth
\(\cCexp\)-class distributions always have definable wave-front sets.  It
does not decide whether a narrower positive theorem holds when the input
density has definable zero and nonzero loci, or when it is a fixed algebraic
measure.  Nor does it characterize all wave-front sets arising from
polynomial pushforwards.

\section{Human-review recommendation}

The packet is recommended for expert review as a likely valid counterexample.
The principal interpretive question is whether the authors intended an
unstated narrower class of smooth densities with definable zero locus.  Under
the broad \(\cCexp\)-class reading provided by the paper's context, every
hypothesis is explicit and the conclusion fails exactly as in
\eqref{eq:wf}.
