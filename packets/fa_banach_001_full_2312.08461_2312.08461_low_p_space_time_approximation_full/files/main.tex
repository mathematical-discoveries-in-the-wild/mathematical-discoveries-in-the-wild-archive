\section*{Status}

This packet upgrades the previous type-$p$ partial note to a candidate full
solution of the low-$p$ future-work line in Abdeljawad--Dittrich
\cite{AbdeljawadDittrich2023}.  The source theorem is stated for
Bochner-Sobolev error exponents $2\leq p_1\leq p_2$, because the proof uses a
type-2 Maurey estimate.  The packet proves that the same $N^{-1/2}$ rate
continues to hold in the entire low range
\[
        1\leq p_1\leq p_2\leq2 .
\]
In particular, the single-exponent case $1\leq p<2$ mentioned in the
introduction is covered, including the endpoint $p=1$.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The source proof already contains more structure than the type-2 explanation
uses.  In the dictionary-bound step, before taking any $L^{p_1,p_2}$ norm, it
proves a uniform pointwise bound for every relevant derivative of every scaled
neuron.  Therefore one does not need Banach-space type to sample the
representing measure in the low-$p$ range.

Sample the explicit signed measure representation of $f$ that the source
constructs.  For each derivative and each point $(x,y)$, the sampling error is
a scalar average of uniformly bounded independent random variables, so its
$p_2$-moment is $O(N^{-p_2/2})$ for $1\leq p_2\leq2$.  Since
$p_1\leq p_2$, Jensen's inequality lets this pointwise estimate pass through
the mixed $L^{p_1}(U;L^{p_2}(V))$ norm.  Summing over finitely many Sobolev
derivatives gives the same $N^{-1/2}$ rate as the high-$p$ theorem, now for
all $1\leq p_1\leq p_2\leq2$.

\section*{A pointwise sampling lemma}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:pointwise-sampling}
Let $U,V$ be bounded measurable sets, let $1\leq p_1\leq p_2\leq2$, and let
$n_1,n_2\in\N$.  Let $\D$ be a family of functions on $U\times V$ such that,
for every $h\in\D$ and every pair of multiindices $|\alpha|\leq n_1$,
$|\beta|\leq n_2$,
\[
        |\partial_x^\alpha\partial_y^\beta h(x,y)|
        \leq H_{\alpha,\beta}
        \qquad ((x,y)\in U\times V).
\]
Suppose
\[
        f=\int h\,d\mu(h)
\]
in distributions, where $\mu$ is a finite signed Borel measure supported on
$\D$.  Put $M=\|\mu\|$.  Then for every $N\geq1$ there is an
$f_N\in\Sigma_{N,M}(\D)$ such that
\[
 \|f-f_N\|_{W_{n_1,p_1}^{n_2,p_2}(U,V)}
 \leq
 C_{\mathbf n,\mathbf p,H}\,
 M\,|U|^{1/p_1}|V|^{1/p_2}N^{-1/2}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $d\mu=s\,d|\mu|$, with $|s|\leq1$, and let $\rho=|\mu|/M$ if
$M>0$; the case $M=0$ is trivial.  Let $h_1,\ldots,h_N$ be independent
$\rho$-distributed samples and set
\[
        f_N=\frac{M}{N}\sum_{j=1}^N s(h_j)h_j .
\]
Then $f_N\in\Sigma_{N,M}(\D)$.  Fix $\alpha,\beta$ and write
\[
        G_{\alpha,\beta}
        =\partial_x^\alpha\partial_y^\beta(f_N-f).
\]
For each $(x,y)$, this is the mean of $N$ centered scalar random variables,
each bounded in modulus by $2M H_{\alpha,\beta}$.  Hence, for
$1\leq p_2\leq2$,
\[
        \mathbb E |G_{\alpha,\beta}(x,y)|^{p_2}
        \leq
        (2M H_{\alpha,\beta})^{p_2}N^{-p_2/2}.
\]
Using Fubini and Jensen's inequality with the concave exponent
$p_1/p_2\leq1$,
\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathbb E\|G_{\alpha,\beta}\|_{L^{p_1}(U;L^{p_2}(V))}^{p_1}
&=
\mathbb E\int_U
   \left(\int_V |G_{\alpha,\beta}(x,y)|^{p_2}\,dy\right)^{p_1/p_2}dx \\
&\leq
\int_U
   \left(\int_V
       \mathbb E |G_{\alpha,\beta}(x,y)|^{p_2}\,dy
   \right)^{p_1/p_2}dx \\
&\leq
(2M H_{\alpha,\beta})^{p_1}
|U|\,|V|^{p_1/p_2}N^{-p_1/2}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Therefore some sample satisfies the corresponding bound for all finitely many
derivatives after summing the expectations over $|\alpha|\leq n_1$ and
$|\beta|\leq n_2$.  This is the asserted Sobolev estimate, with the finite
derivative sum absorbed into $C_{\mathbf n,\mathbf p,H}$.
\end{proof}

\section*{\texorpdfstring{Low-$p$ theorem}{Low-p theorem}}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:low-p-full}
Use the notation and the activation, weight, Fourier-Lebesgue, bounded-domain,
and scaled-dictionary hypotheses from the proof of
\cite[Theorem 3.9]{AbdeljawadDittrich2023}.  Replace the source condition
$2\leq p_1\leq p_2<\infty$ by
\[
        1\leq p_1\leq p_2\leq2 .
\]
Let $1\leq q_i\leq2$ and let $f\in\FL^{q_1,q_2}(\widetilde\omega)$.  For
$M$ sufficiently large there is a constant $C>0$, independent of $N$ and $f$,
such that
\[
 \inf_{f_N\in\SigmaNM(\D_{\widetilde\sigma}^{d_1,d_2})}
        \|f-f_N\|_{W_{n_1,p_1}^{n_2,p_2}(U,V)}
 \leq
 C\,N^{-1/2}|U|^{1/p_1}|V|^{1/p_2}
        \|f\|_{\FL^{q_1,q_2}(\widetilde\omega)}
\]
for every $N\geq1$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The source proof of Theorem 3.9 constructs an explicit signed measure
representation of $f$ by the scaled shallow dictionary
$\D_{\widetilde\sigma}^{d_1,d_2}$.  Its variation estimate gives a measure
$\mu$ with
\[
        \|\mu\|
        \leq
        A\|f\|_{\FL^{q_1,q_2}(\widetilde\omega)}
\]
where $A$ depends only on the structural parameters of the theorem.  The same
argument also proves that
\[
        f\in
        \FL^1\bigl(\omega(\cdot,\cdot)\langle\cdot\rangle
              \langle\cdot\rangle\bigr)
        \subseteq \FL^1(\omega).
\]
By the source's low-degree embedding lemma
\cite[Lemma 3.7]{AbdeljawadDittrich2023}, applied with $t_1=t_2=1$, this
places $f$ in $W_{n_1,p_1}^{n_2,p_2}(U,V)$ for all $1\leq p_i\leq2$.

It remains to approximate the representing measure.  In the dictionary-bound
part of the source proof, the authors show pointwise, before integration, that
for every $|\alpha|\leq n_1$ and $|\beta|\leq n_2$,
\[
 \bigl|
 \partial_x^\alpha\partial_y^\beta
 \widetilde\sigma(\cdot,\cdot;\xi,b)(x,y)
 \bigr|
 \leq H_{\alpha,\beta}
\]
uniformly in $(\xi,b)$ and $(x,y)\in U\times V$; this is the estimate that
then yields their displayed $L^{p_1,p_2}$ dictionary bound
\[
        \|\widetilde\sigma(\cdot,\cdot;\xi,b)\|_
        {W_{n_1,p_1}^{n_2,p_2}(U,V)}
        \leq B|U|^{1/p_1}|V|^{1/p_2}.
\]
Lemma \ref{lem:pointwise-sampling}, applied to this representing measure and
these pointwise derivative bounds, gives an $N$-term network
$f_N\in\Sigma_{N,\|\mu\|}(\D_{\widetilde\sigma}^{d_1,d_2})$ with
\[
\begin{aligned}
 \|f-f_N\|_{W_{n_1,p_1}^{n_2,p_2}(U,V)}
 &\leq
 C_{\mathbf n,\mathbf p,H}\,
 \|\mu\|\,|U|^{1/p_1}|V|^{1/p_2}N^{-1/2} \\
 &\leq
 C\,N^{-1/2}|U|^{1/p_1}|V|^{1/p_2}
        \|f\|_{\FL^{q_1,q_2}(\widetilde\omega)} .
\end{aligned}
\]
Taking $M\geq\|\mu\|$ proves the asserted infimum bound.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and scope}

The proof is symbolic; no numerical verification is needed.  The source crop
was generated by \texttt{code/make\_open\_problem\_crop.py}.  Human review
should check the passage from the source's pointwise derivative estimate to
Lemma \ref{lem:pointwise-sampling}; this is the entire upgrade mechanism.

This packet fully covers the source's stated low-$p$ obstruction for the same
two-block shallow-network theorem, including the single-block case
$p_1=p_2=p<2$ and the endpoint $p=1$.  It does not address separate future
directions in the paper such as Bochner-Besov targets or arbitrary
Bochner-Banach spaces.
