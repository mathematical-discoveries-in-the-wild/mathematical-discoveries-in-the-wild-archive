# Full solution packet: Sobolev spaces have quasi-interior points

## Source

- Paper: Jochen Gluck and Martin R. Weber, *Almost Interior Points in Ordered Banach Spaces and the Long-Term Behaviour of Strongly Positive Operator Semigroups*
- arXiv: `1901.03306`
- Local source PDF: `source_paper.pdf`
- Source location: Example "Sobolev spaces", parsed source lines 823-837.

## Classification

- Status: `full`
- Target question: the source asks whether `W^{k,p}(R^d)`, ordered by the a.e. nonnegative cone, has almost interior or even quasi-interior points.
- Packet claim: yes. For every `k in N_0`, `1 <= p < infinity`, and `d >= 1`, the Gaussian `u(x)=exp(-|x|^2)` is a quasi-interior point of the positive cone of `W^{k,p}(R^d)`.

## Proof Summary

The Gaussian belongs to every finite-order Sobolev space `W^{k,p}(R^d)` and is strictly positive everywhere. Every compactly supported smooth function `g` is dominated by a scalar multiple of the Gaussian, because the Gaussian has a positive minimum on `supp(g)`. Hence `C_c^\infty(R^d)` lies in the principal ideal generated by the Gaussian. Since `C_c^\infty(R^d)` is dense in `W^{k,p}(R^d)` for `1 <= p < infinity`, that principal ideal is dense, so the Gaussian is quasi-interior. Quasi-interior implies almost interior by the source paper's definitions.

## Human Review Notes

The only external theorem used is the standard density of `C_c^\infty(R^d)` in `W^{k,p}(R^d)` for finite `p`. The result does not address the broader open problem asking whether every almost interior point is quasi-interior for arbitrary ordered Banach spaces with generating cone.
