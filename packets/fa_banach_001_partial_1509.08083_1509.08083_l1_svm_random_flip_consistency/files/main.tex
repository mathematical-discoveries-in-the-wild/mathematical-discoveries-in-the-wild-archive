\paragraph{Status.}
This is a candidate partial result, not a full answer to every noise model in
the source question. It proves directional consistency for the original pure
$\ell_1$ constraint under independent symmetric label flips.

\section{Source question}

Kolleck and Vyb\'iral study hinge-risk minimization over an $\ell_1$ ball
\cite{KV}. In Section VI (Discussion), page 15 of the arXiv PDF, they ask for
the behavior of $\ell_1$-SVM when a small probability of label
misclassification is present. They also ask whether their dependence of the
sample count on the accuracy and Gaussian scale is optimal.

\includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

The result below treats the independent random-flip subcase of the first
question. It keeps the source estimator unchanged: there is no added
$\ell_2$ constraint.

\section{Model and theorem}

Fix $d\geq2$, $r,R>0$, and $a\in\R^d$ with
$\|a\|_2=1$ and $\|a\|_1\leq R$. Let $g_i$ be independent standard Gaussian
vectors, set $x_i=rg_i$, and let $\xi_i\in\{-1,1\}$ be independent of the
$g_i$ with
\[
 \Pp(\xi_i=1)=p\in(1/2,1).
\]
The observed labels and empirical risk are
\[
 y_i=\xi_i\,\operatorname{sign}\langle g_i,a\rangle,
 \qquad
 L_m(w)=\frac1m\sum_{i=1}^m[1-y_i\langle x_i,w\rangle]_+.
\]
The ordinary $\ell_1$-SVM minimizes $L_m$ over
$K=\{w:\|w\|_1\leq R\}$.

Put
\[
 c_p=\sqrt{2\log\frac{p}{2p-1}},
 \qquad
 \mu=\frac{1}{r c_p},
\]
and assume the feasibility condition $\mu\|a\|_1\leq R$.
Define
\[
 \phi_p(t)=p[1-t]_+ +(1-p)[1+t]_+.
\]
If $X=|G|$ and $Z$ are independent, with $G,Z\sim N(0,1)$, set
\[
 H(u,s)=\E\,\phi_p\bigl(r(uX+sZ)\bigr),
 \qquad u\in\R,\quad s\geq0.
\]
For $0<\delta<\mu/2$, define the explicit separation modulus
\begin{align*}
 \Delta_\delta=\min\{&H(u,s)-H(\mu,0): |u|\leq R,\ 0\leq s\leq R,\\
                    &(u-\mu)^2+s^2\geq\delta^2\}.
\end{align*}

\begin{theorem}[Random-flip consistency for pure $\ell_1$-SVM]
The population risk $L(w)=\E L_m(w)$ has the unique global minimizer
$w_\star=\mu a$ on all of $\R^d$. In particular, $w_\star$ is the unique
minimizer on $K$, and $\Delta_\delta>0$.

Let
\[
 A_{d,r,R}=8\sqrt{8\pi}+18rR\sqrt{2\log(2d)}.
\]
If
\[
 m\geq \frac{64A_{d,r,R}^2}{\Delta_\delta^2},
\]
then, with probability at least
\[
 1-8\left(
 e^{-m\Delta_\delta^2/2048}
 +e^{-m\Delta_\delta^2/(2048r^2R^2)}
 \right),
\]
every empirical minimizer $\widehat a\in\arg\min_K L_m$ satisfies
\[
 \|\widehat a-\mu a\|_2<\delta,
 \qquad
 \left\|\frac{\widehat a}{\|\widehat a\|_2}-a\right\|_2
 <\frac{2\delta}{\mu}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof intuition}

Write a candidate as $w=ua+v$ with $v\perp a$. Gaussian rotation invariance
reduces the signed margin to $r(u|G|+\|v\|_2Z)$. The noisy hinge profile
$\phi_p$ is convex. Adding the independent centered Gaussian term caused by
$v$ therefore raises population risk, strictly if $v\neq0$. The problem
collapses to a one-dimensional profile $J(u)=H(u,0)$, whose derivative has a
single zero at the displayed value $\mu$. Uniform empirical concentration,
already proved for the source $\ell_1$-SVM, then lifts the unique population
minimizer to a finite-sample directional statement.

\section{Proof}

Decompose $w=ua+v$, where $u=\langle w,a\rangle$ and $v\perp a$, and put
$s=\|v\|_2$. Write a standard Gaussian $g$ as
\[
 g=G_0a+g_\perp.
\]
The random variables $|G_0|$ and
$\operatorname{sign}(G_0)\langle g_\perp,v/s\rangle$ are independent; the
second is standard Gaussian when $s>0$. Consequently,
\[
 \operatorname{sign}\langle g,a\rangle\,\langle rg,w\rangle
 \stackrel{d}{=}r(uX+sZ),
\]
and hence $L(w)=H(u,s)$.

The function $\phi_p$ is convex and is not affine on $\R$: its slopes change
at $-1$ and $1$. Conditional on $X$, Jensen's inequality gives
\[
 H(u,s)\geq H(u,0)=:J(u).
\]
If $s>0$, the conditional Gaussian variable $r(uX+sZ)$ has full support, so
equality in Jensen is impossible. Thus the inequality is strict for $s>0$.

It remains to minimize $J$. For $u<0$, the argument $ruX$ is nonpositive and
all slopes of $\phi_p$ there are negative because $p>1/2$. Hence $J$ is
strictly decreasing on $(-\infty,0]$. For $u>0$, differentiation under the
expectation is legitimate because $X$ has no atoms at the kink, and
\begin{align*}
 J'(u)
 &=r\E X\left((1-p)-p\mathbf1_{\{X<1/(ru)\}}\right)\\
 &=r\sqrt{\frac2\pi}\left(
 1-2p+p\exp\left(-\frac{1}{2r^2u^2}\right)
 \right).
\end{align*}
This expression is strictly increasing in $u$, has a negative limit at
$0+$ and a positive limit at infinity, and vanishes precisely when
\[
 \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2r^2u^2}\right)=\frac{2p-1}{p},
\]
that is, precisely at $u=\mu$. Therefore $J$ has the unique global minimizer
$\mu$. Combining this with the strict Jensen step shows that $H$, and hence
$L$, has the unique global minimizer $(u,s)=(\mu,0)$, corresponding to
$w_\star=\mu a$. The feasibility hypothesis places $w_\star$ in $K$.

Since $\|w\|_2\leq\|w\|_1\leq R$ for $w\in K$, every such $w$ has
$|u|\leq R$ and $0\leq s\leq R$. The parameter set defining
$\Delta_\delta$ is compact, nonempty, and excludes the unique minimizer.
Continuity of $H$ therefore implies $\Delta_\delta>0$.

We now use the source's uniform concentration theorem. Its proof remains valid
under the extra independent flips: in the symmetrized process, every factor
$\varepsilon_i y_i$ is again an independent Rademacher sign conditional on
the data, so the Gaussian and dual-$\ell_1$ estimates are unchanged. Thus for
every $q>0$,
\begin{align*}
 \sup_{w\in K}|L_m(w)-L(w)|
 \leq \frac{A_{d,r,R}}{\sqrt m}+q
\end{align*}
with probability at least
\[
 1-8\left(e^{-mq^2/32}+e^{-mq^2/(32r^2R^2)}\right).
\]
Choose $q=\Delta_\delta/8$. The sample hypothesis makes the right-hand side
of the uniform bound at most $\Delta_\delta/4$. If $\widehat a$ minimizes
$L_m$ on $K$, empirical optimality gives
\[
 L(\widehat a)-L(w_\star)
 \leq 2\sup_{w\in K}|L_m(w)-L(w)|
 \leq\frac{\Delta_\delta}{2}.
\]
By the definition of $\Delta_\delta$, this forces
$\|\widehat a-w_\star\|_2<\delta$. Since $\delta<\mu/2$, the vector
$\widehat a$ is nonzero, and the standard normalization inequality yields
\[
 \left\|\frac{\widehat a}{\|\widehat a\|_2}-a\right\|_2
 \leq\frac{2\|\widehat a-\mu a\|_2}{\mu}
 <\frac{2\delta}{\mu}.
\]
The probability formula follows by substituting
$q=\Delta_\delta/8$ into the preceding concentration estimate.
\qed

\section{Relation to later literature and limitations}

Genzel and Stollenwerk \cite{GS} prove sharper noisy hinge-loss recovery
estimates with the optimal $m^{-1/2}$ oversampling rate when the convex signal
set is contained in the Euclidean unit ball. Their Example 3.4(2) and
Proposition 3.5 cover the same independent random-flip law and compute the
correlation parameter $(2p-1)\sqrt{2/\pi}$. They explicitly identify
Kolleck--Vyb\'iral as a special case and improvement.

The supporting paper also says that extending the noisy argument to arbitrary
convex signal sets is not straightforward. The theorem here covers the pure
$RB_1^d$ constraint even when it is not contained in $B_2^d$, but its rate is
implicit through $\Delta_\delta$. It does not cover adversarial,
feature-dependent, or asymmetric flips, non-Gaussian measurements, or prove
optimal dependence on $p,r$, and the target accuracy.

\section{Novelty check}

The run indexes contained no row for arXiv:1509.08083. Bounded primary-source
searches for combinations of random bit flips, $\ell_1$-SVM, Gaussian hinge
loss, and symmetric label noise found \cite{GS} and the broader
symmetric-label-noise literature, but no exact theorem for a pure $RB_1^d$
constraint with the scale formula above. Novelty is plausible and should be
checked by a human against papers citing \cite{GS}.
