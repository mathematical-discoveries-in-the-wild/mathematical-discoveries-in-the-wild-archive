\section*{Source question}

Michael Megrelishvili's paper \emph{Tame functionals on Banach algebras}
introduces tame functionals on Banach algebras and proves
\(\Tame(\ell^1(G))=\Tame(G)\) for discrete groups.  It then asks whether the
same equality holds for nondiscrete locally compact groups.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\caption{Question 3.6 in arXiv:1710.01044, page 8.}
\end{figure}

The extracted question is:
\[
  \text{Is } \Tame(L_1(G))=\Tame(G) \text{ for every nondiscrete locally compact group } G?
\]

\section*{Supporting literature status}

Matan Komisarchik's paper \emph{The Banach Algebra \(L^1(G)\) and Tame
Functionals} explicitly identifies this as Megrelishvili's question and
answers it affirmatively.  Its introduction states the main result as a
general bornological equivalence and lists the tame equality as an immediate
consequence.

\section*{Idea of the proof identification}

This packet does not claim an original proof.  The identification is direct:
Megrelishvili's Question 3.6 asks exactly for the equality
\(\Tame(L_1(G))=\Tame(G)\) for nondiscrete locally compact groups, and
Komisarchik's Theorem 6.3 proves the stronger statement for every locally
compact group \(G\).

The supporting proof compares two ways for a right uniformly continuous
function \(\varphi\) to be small: smallness of its group orbit
\(\varphi\cdot G\), and smallness of the \(L^1(G)\)-module orbit
\(\varphi\circledast B_{L^1(G)}\).  The central mechanism is the norm-closure
identity
\[
  \overline{\acx}(\varphi\cdot G)
  =
  \overline{\varphi\circledast B_{L^1(G)}}
  \qquad(\varphi\in\RUC(G)),
\]
proved using approximate identities and uniform equicontinuity ideas.  Once
these two closed absolutely convex hulls agree, every norm-saturated convex
bornology gives the same smallness notion on both sides.  Applying this to the
tame bornology yields \(\Tame(L^1(G))=\Tame(G)\).

\begin{theorem}[Literature answer]
For every locally compact group \(G\),
\[
  \Tame(L^1(G))=\Tame(G).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is exactly the tame equality in Komisarchik's Theorem 6.3
\cite[Theorem 6.3]{KomisarchikL1Tame}.  Since Megrelishvili's Question 3.6
asks for the same equality in the nondiscrete locally compact case, the
supporting theorem gives an affirmative answer to the source question.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes and limitations}

The source paper and supporting paper are separate arXiv papers.  The
supporting paper explicitly says in its introduction that it answers
Megrelishvili's question affirmatively, and Theorem 6.3 gives the equality for
all locally compact groups.

the two titles, the author name Komisarchik, and exact variants of
\(\Tame(L^1(G))=\Tame(G)\).  No prior packet or attempt for this literature
