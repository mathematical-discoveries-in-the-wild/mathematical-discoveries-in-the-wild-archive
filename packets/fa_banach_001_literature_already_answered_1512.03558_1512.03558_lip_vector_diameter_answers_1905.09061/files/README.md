# Literature-Already-Answered: Vector-Valued Lipschitz Diameter Questions

- status: `literature_already_answered (scoped)`
- source question paper: Becerra Guerrero--Lopez-Perez--Rueda Zoca,
  arXiv:1512.03558, *Octahedrality in Lipschitz-free Banach spaces*
- supporting answer paper: Langemets--Rueda Zoca, arXiv:1905.09061,
  *Octahedral norms in duals and biduals of Lipschitz-free spaces*

## Source Questions

The source paper asks three questions in Section 3, "Consequences and open
questions."

This packet records the later answers to the following parts.

1. The first question, later cited as Question 3.1, asks whether under the
   hypotheses of the source Theorem 2.4, `Lip(M,X*)` satisfies the strong
   diameter two property, not merely the weak-star strong diameter two
   property.

2. The third question, later cited as Question 3.3, asks for assumptions on
   a metric space `M` and non-zero Banach space `X` ensuring that
   `F(M,X)` has slice-D2P, D2P, or SD2P. This packet records the SD2P answer.

## Answer

arXiv:1905.09061 explicitly cites arXiv:1512.03558 and answers these
questions.

- Theorem 2.4 of arXiv:1905.09061 proves that if the pair `(M,X)` has the
  contraction-extension property and `M` is unbounded or not uniformly
  discrete, then `Lip(M,X)` has the symmetric strong diameter two property
  (SSD2P). The paper states immediately after the theorem that this improves
  arXiv:1512.03558 Theorem 2.4 and gives a positive answer to Question 3.1
  of that paper.
- Theorem 3.1 of arXiv:1905.09061 proves that if `Lip(M)` is octahedral, then
  `Lip(M,X*)` is octahedral for every Banach space `X`.
- Corollary 3.2 of arXiv:1905.09061 proves that, for a metric space `M`, the
  following are equivalent: `F(M,X)` has the strong diameter two property for
  every Banach space `X`; `F(M)` has the strong diameter two property; and
  `F(M)` has no strongly exposed point. The supporting paper says this
  answers Question 3.3 of arXiv:1512.03558.

## Scope

This is not a new proof from the run. It is a literature-status packet for
questions from arXiv:1512.03558 that were explicitly addressed later.

The packet should be read with the following scope limitation: the clean
criterion recorded here is for the SD2P clause of the vector-valued free-space
question. The source question also mentions slice-D2P and D2P; this packet
does not assert a complete classification of those weaker variants beyond what
is implied by the recorded SD2P result.
