\section{Source problem and scope}

Cadilhac, Conde-Alonso, and Parcet prove weak-$L_1$ endpoint estimates for
matrix-valued Calder\'on--Zygmund operators and for Mikhlin multipliers in
group von Neumann algebras~\cite{source}. Their Remark~2.5, on arXiv PDF
page~13, asks for a weak endpoint theory in fully noncommutative geometric
settings and names quantum Euclidean spaces and the hyperfinite
$\mathrm{II}_1$ factor as candidates.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The result below treats a substantial convolution subfamily. It applies to
arbitrary unitary cocycle twists of discrete amenable groups. In particular,
it gives uniform weak endpoints on quantum tori, which are twisted group
von Neumann algebras of $\mathbb Z^d$. It does not construct the requested
Calder\'on--Zygmund decomposition, cover nonconvolution kernels, or settle
the locally compact quantum Euclidean algebra $\mathcal R_\Theta$.

\section{Definitions}

Let $\Gamma$ be a discrete group and let
$\sigma:\Gamma\times\Gamma\to\mathbb T$ be a normalized unitary
$2$-cocycle. Thus
\[
 \sigma(g,h)\sigma(gh,k)=\sigma(h,k)\sigma(g,hk),
 \qquad \sigma(e,g)=\sigma(g,e)=1.
\]
The twisted left regular representation on $\ell_2(\Gamma)$ is
\[
 \lambda_\sigma(g)\delta_h=\sigma(g,h)\delta_{gh}.
\]
Its weak operator closure is the twisted group von Neumann algebra
$L_\sigma(\Gamma)$. It carries the canonical trace
\[
 \tau_\sigma\!\left(\sum_g a_g\lambda_\sigma(g)\right)=a_e.
\]
Given $M:\Gamma\to\C$, define the twisted Fourier multiplier on finite
Fourier sums by
\[
 T_M^\sigma(\lambda_\sigma(g))=M(g)\lambda_\sigma(g).
\]
The corresponding Herz--Schur multiplier on
$\mathcal B(\ell_2\Gamma)$ is
\[
 \mathsf S_M([a_{r,s}])=[M(rs^{-1})a_{r,s}].
\]
For a semifinite tracial von Neumann algebra $(\N,\tau)$, write
\[
 \|x\|_{1,\infty}
 =\sup_{t>0} t\,\tau\big(\one_{(t,\infty)}(|x|)\big).
\]

\section{Idea of the proof}

The ordinary amenable transference argument compresses the left regular
representation to a F\o lner net and places the compressions in a tracial
ultraproduct. A twist changes each nonzero matrix entry only by a scalar of
modulus one. If the $(r,s)$ entry of $\lambda_\sigma(g)$ is nonzero, then
$r=gs$, hence $rs^{-1}=g$. Schur multiplication by $M(rs^{-1})$ therefore
acts on that entry by exactly $M(g)$, independent of the cocycle.

The only remaining issue is the endpoint topology. Finite compressions obey
the assumed Schur weak-$(1,1)$ estimate. Amenability makes them
asymptotically multiplicative in normalized Hilbert--Schmidt norm, producing
a trace-preserving embedding of $L_\sigma(\Gamma)$ into their tracial
ultraproduct. Trace preservation passes spectral distribution functions at
their continuity points, which is enough to recover the weak-$L_1$
quasi-norm. Thus there is no loss in the endpoint constant.

\section{Twisted weak endpoint transference}

\begin{theorem}[Twisted weak-$(1,1)$ transference]\label{thm:transfer}
Let $\Gamma$ be a discrete amenable group, let $\sigma$ be a normalized
unitary $2$-cocycle on $\Gamma$, and let $M:\Gamma\to\C$. Suppose
\[
 \|\mathsf S_M(A)\|_{S_{1,\infty}(\Gamma)}
 \le C\|A\|_{S_1(\Gamma)}
 \qquad(A\in S_1(\ell_2\Gamma)).
\]
Then $T_M^\sigma$ extends uniquely to a bounded map
\[
 T_M^\sigma:L_1(L_\sigma\Gamma)\longrightarrow
 L_{1,\infty}(L_\sigma\Gamma)
\]
with norm at most $C$.

More generally, if the same Schur estimate holds after every matrix
amplification with a common constant $C$, then every matrix amplification of
$T_M^\sigma$ satisfies the corresponding estimate with the same constant.
The bound is independent of $\sigma$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose a left F\o lner net $(F_i)_i$ in $\Gamma$ and let $P_i$ be the
orthogonal projection of $\ell_2(\Gamma)$ onto $\ell_2(F_i)$. Equip
$\mathcal B(\ell_2F_i)$ with normalized trace
$\operatorname{tr}_i=|F_i|^{-1}\Tr$. Put
\[
 j_i(x)=P_i xP_i \qquad (x\in L_\sigma\Gamma).
\]

For $g,h\in\Gamma$,
\begin{align*}
 &j_i(\lambda_\sigma(g))j_i(\lambda_\sigma(h))
   -j_i(\lambda_\sigma(g)\lambda_\sigma(h))\\
 &\hspace{3em}
 =-P_i\lambda_\sigma(g)(\one-P_i)\lambda_\sigma(h)P_i.
\end{align*}
The operator on the right can be nonzero only on basis vectors
$\delta_s$ with $s\in F_i$ and $hs\notin F_i$. Since all cocycle factors
have modulus one,
\[
 \big\|j_i(\lambda_\sigma(g))j_i(\lambda_\sigma(h))
 -j_i(\lambda_\sigma(g)\lambda_\sigma(h))\big\|_{2,\operatorname{tr}_i}^2
 \le \frac{|hF_i\triangle F_i|}{|F_i|}\longrightarrow0.
\]
Also
\[
 \operatorname{tr}_i(j_i(\lambda_\sigma(g)))
 =\begin{cases}1,&g=e,\\0,&g\ne e.\end{cases}
\]
Hence, for a free ultrafilter refining the net, the map
\[
 J:L_\sigma(\Gamma)\longrightarrow
 \prod_{\mathcal U}\big(\mathcal B(\ell_2F_i),\operatorname{tr}_i\big),
 \qquad J(x)=[j_i(x)]_{\mathcal U},
\]
extends from the twisted group algebra to a trace-preserving
$*$-monomorphism. In particular it preserves $L_1$ norms and spectral
distributions.

We next record the exact intertwining. The matrix coefficients of a twisted
group unitary are
\[
 \langle\lambda_\sigma(g)\delta_s,\delta_r\rangle
 =\begin{cases}\sigma(g,s),&r=gs,\\0,&r\ne gs.\end{cases}
\]
Whenever the coefficient is nonzero, $rs^{-1}=g$. Therefore
\[
 \mathsf S_M(j_i(x))=j_i(T_M^\sigma x)                 \tag{4.1}
\]
for every finite twisted Fourier sum $x$ and every $i$.

Regard a finite corner as an operator on $\ell_2\Gamma$ by extending it by
zero. Normalizing both the $L_1$ and weak-$L_1$ quantities by $|F_i|$
does not change the assumed constant. From (4.1), for every $t>0$,
\[
 t\,\operatorname{tr}_i
 \big(\one_{(t,\infty)}(|j_i(T_M^\sigma x)|)\big)
 \le C\operatorname{tr}_i(|j_i(x)|).                 \tag{4.2}
\]

The operators in (4.2) are uniformly bounded in operator norm when $x$ is a
finite Fourier sum. Continuous functional calculus in the tracial
ultraproduct implies
\[
 \lim_{\mathcal U}\operatorname{tr}_i(|j_i(x)|)=\tau_\sigma(|x|)
\]
and, at every continuity point $t$ of the spectral distribution of
$|T_M^\sigma x|$,
\[
 \lim_{\mathcal U}\operatorname{tr}_i
 \big(\one_{(t,\infty)}(|j_i(T_M^\sigma x)|)\big)
 =\tau_\sigma\big(\one_{(t,\infty)}(|T_M^\sigma x|)\big).
\]
Taking the ultralimit in (4.2) gives the desired distribution estimate at
all such $t$. The discontinuity set is countable, and continuity points
from above approximate every $t>0$; consequently their supremum already
equals the weak-$L_1$ quasi-norm. Thus
\[
 \|T_M^\sigma x\|_{1,\infty}\le C\|x\|_1
\]
for all finite twisted Fourier sums $x$. Density and completeness of
$L_{1,\infty}$ give the unique extension.

For matrix amplification, replace $j_i$ by $\id_{M_k}\otimes j_i$ and use
the amplified Schur estimate. Every preceding identity is unchanged.
\end{proof}

\section{Quantum-torus consequence}

Let $\mathcal A_\Theta''$ denote the von Neumann algebra of a $d$-dimensional
quantum torus. It is the twisted group von Neumann algebra
$L_{\sigma_\Theta}(\mathbb Z^d)$ for the standard cocycle associated with
the antisymmetric deformation matrix $\Theta$; see
Gonz\'alez-P\'erez--Junge--Parcet for the quantum Euclidean and torus
framework~\cite{quantum}. Write $U_\Theta^k$ for its Fourier unitaries and
let
\[
 \Delta_\Theta(U_\Theta^k)=|k|^2U_\Theta^k.
\]

\begin{corollary}[Uniform quantum-torus Mikhlin endpoint]
Let $m:\mathbb R_+\to\C$ satisfy
\[
 |m^{(r)}(s)|\le A_rs^{-r}
 \quad(s>0),\qquad
 0\le r\le \left\lfloor\frac d2\right\rfloor+1,
\]
and let $m(0)$ be bounded. Then, for every antisymmetric $\Theta$,
\[
 m(\Delta_\Theta):L_1(\mathcal A_\Theta'')longrightarrow
 L_{1,\infty}(\mathcal A_\Theta'')
\]
is completely bounded. Its completely amplified weak-$(1,1)$ constant
depends only on $d$, $|m(0)|$, and the displayed Mikhlin constants, and is
independent of $\Theta$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
On $\Gamma=\mathbb Z^d$, the conditionally negative length
$\psi(k)=|k|^2$ is represented by the $d$-dimensional cocycle
$\beta(k)=k\in\mathbb R^d$. Theorem~C(i) of
Cadilhac--Conde-Alonso--Parcet gives the completely amplified weak-$(1,1)$
estimate for the Herz--Schur multiplier with symbol $m\circ\psi$ under the
stated Mikhlin condition~\cite{source}. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:transfer} with the arbitrary twist $\sigma_\Theta$.
The value at $0$ contributes only the trace-preserving zero-frequency
projection and is harmless.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
For the standard irrational factor cases, the weak closure of the quantum
torus is a hyperfinite $\mathrm{II}_1$ factor. The corollary therefore also
gives a concrete singular-multiplier family on the second class named in
Remark~2.5. It is not a general weak endpoint theorem for that factor and
does not use its dyadic filtration.
\end{remark}

\section{Verification and novelty status}

The proof was audited through the following independent checks.

\begin{itemize}
\item The cocycle identity is used only to make $\lambda_\sigma$ a
projective representation. The intertwining itself uses solely the support
relation $r=gs$, so no hidden phase cancellation occurs.
\item The F\o lner boundary estimate is normalized and uniform in the two
group elements. It is enough to obtain multiplicativity on finite Fourier
sums and then the trace-preserving ultraproduct embedding.
\item The weak endpoint is passed at continuity points of the spectral
distribution, avoiding an unjustified assertion that spectral projections
commute pointwise with arbitrary ultralimits.
\item The fixed matrix-amplification argument uses exactly the complete
Schur endpoint supplied in Theorem~C(i) of the source.
\end{itemize}

No computational experiment is relevant to the proof. A separate
line-by-line report is included as \texttt{verification.md}.
