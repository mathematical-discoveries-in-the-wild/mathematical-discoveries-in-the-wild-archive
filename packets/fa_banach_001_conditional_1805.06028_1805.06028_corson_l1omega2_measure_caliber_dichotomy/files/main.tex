\section*{Source Question}

On page 11, Problem 4.3 of the source asks:
\begin{quote}
Is the existence of a Corson compact \(K\) such that
\(\ellone(\omegatwo)\embed C(K)\) consistent with \(\ZFC\)?
\end{quote}
The source notes immediately afterward that the answer is negative under CH
and also under \(\mathrm{MA}_{\omega_1}\).

\section*{Conditional Dependencies}

This packet isolates two independent set-theoretic hypotheses.

\begin{assumption}[Measure caliber at \(\omegatwo\)]
\(\omegatwo\) is a caliber of Radon measures: for every compact space \(L\),
every \(\mu\in P(L)\), and every family
\((A_\xi)_{\xi<\omegatwo}\subseteq \Bor(L)\) of \(\mu\)-positive sets, there
is \(x\in L\) such that
\[
|\{\xi<\omegatwo:x\in A_\xi\}|=\omegatwo .
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Point-countable positive family at \(\omegatwo\)]
There are a compact Hausdorff space \(L\), a Radon probability measure
\(\mu\) on \(L\), and closed sets \(C_\xi\subseteq L\), \(\xi<\omegatwo\),
such that \(\mu(C_\xi)>0\) for every \(\xi<\omegatwo\), and
\[
|\{\xi<\omegatwo:x\in C_\xi\}|\le \omega
\quad\text{for every }x\in L.
\]
\end{assumption}

The first hypothesis gives a negative answer.  The second gives a positive
example.  The packet does not prove either hypothesis in \(\ZFC\), nor does it
claim that they exhaust all possible models.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The negative direction uses Talagrand's dual-ball characterization of
large \(\ellone(\kappa)\) subspaces.  If \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\) embeds into
\(C(K)\), then the weak-star compact dual ball of \(C(K)\) maps continuously
onto the cube \([0,1]^{\omegatwo}\).  For a Corson \(K\), the space \(K\) is
also \(\omegatwo\)-Corson.  Under the measure-caliber hypothesis, the later
\(\kappa\)-Corson theory says that \(M_1(K)=B_{C(K)^*}\) is
\(\omegatwo\)-Corson.  Regular \(\kappa\)-Corson compacta are preserved by
continuous images, while \([0,1]^{\omegatwo}\) is not
\(\omegatwo\)-Corson.  This contradiction rules out the desired \(K\).

The positive direction reverses the obstruction.  A point-countable family of
positive-measure closed sets produces an adequate compact
\[
\cA=\{A\subseteq \omegatwo:\bigcap_{\xi\in A} C_\xi\ne\emptyset\}.
\]
Point-countability makes \(\cA\) Corson.  Positive measure supplies a uniform
lower bound on a subfamily of size \(\omegatwo\), and hence the Ptak convex
mean condition for the finite members of \(\cA\).  The source proposition then
embeds \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\) into \(C(\cA)\).

\section*{Conditional Negative Result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:negative}
Assume that \(\omegatwo\) is a caliber of Radon measures.  Then there is no
Corson compact \(K\) such that \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\embed C(K)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(K\) be Corson compact.  Then \(K\) is \(\omegatwo\)-Corson, because
countable supports have cardinal \(<\omegatwo\).

By Theorem 12.4 of Marciszewski--Plebanek--Zakrzewski, for a regular
uncountable cardinal \(\kappa\), the following are equivalent: \(\kappa\) is a
caliber of Radon measures; \(P(L)\) is \(\kappa\)-Corson for every
\(\kappa\)-Corson compact \(L\); and \(M_1(L)\) is \(\kappa\)-Corson for
every \(\kappa\)-Corson compact \(L\).  Applying this with
\(\kappa=\omegatwo\), the weak-star compact unit ball
\[
B_{C(K)^*}=M_1(K)
\]
is \(\omegatwo\)-Corson.

Suppose, toward a contradiction, that \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\embed C(K)\).
Talagrand's theorem, as quoted in the source paper before Theorem 4.2, says
that for cardinals \(\kappa\) with \(\operatorname{cf}(\kappa)\ge\omega_1\),
\(\ellone(\kappa)\embed X\) if and only if there is a continuous surjection
\[
(B_{X^*},\wstar)\longrightarrow [0,1]^\kappa .
\]
Thus there is a continuous surjection
\[
M_1(K)\longrightarrow [0,1]^{\omegatwo}.
\]

Theorem 9.2 of Marciszewski--Plebanek--Zakrzewski states that, for regular
\(\kappa\), continuous images of \(\kappa\)-Corson compacta are
\(\kappa\)-Corson.  Hence \([0,1]^{\omegatwo}\) would be
\(\omegatwo\)-Corson.  This contradicts their Corollary 7.2, which says that
\([0,1]^\kappa\) is not \(\kappa\)-Corson for any \(\kappa\ge\omega\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Conditional Positive Construction}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:positive}
Assume there is a compact Radon probability space \((L,\mu)\) with a
point-countable family of positive-measure closed sets
\((C_\xi)_{\xi<\omegatwo}\).  Then there is a Corson compact space \(K\) such
that \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\embed C(K)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For \(A\subseteq\omegatwo\), write
\[
A\in\cA
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\bigcap_{\xi\in A} C_\xi\ne\emptyset .
\]
Equivalently, by compactness of \(L\), \(A\in\cA\) if and only if every finite
subset of \(A\) has non-empty \(C_\xi\)-intersection.  Therefore \(\cA\) is
an adequate compact family, hence a compact subspace of \(2^{\omegatwo}\).

The family is hereditary: if \(B\subseteq A\in\cA\), then \(B\in\cA\).  It is
also Corson.  Indeed, if \(A\in\cA\), choose \(x\in\bigcap_{\xi\in A}C_\xi\).
Then
\[
A\subseteq \{\xi<\omegatwo:x\in C_\xi\},
\]
and the latter set is countable by the point-countability assumption.  Thus
every point of \(\cA\subseteq 2^{\omegatwo}\) has countable support.

Since \(\omegatwo\) is regular and every \(\mu(C_\xi)\) is positive, there
are \(S\subseteq\omegatwo\) with \(|S|=\omegatwo\) and a number
\(\delta>0\) such that \(\mu(C_\xi)>\delta\) for all \(\xi\in S\).  Let
\[
\cF=\{F\in [S]^{<\omega}:\bigcap_{\xi\in F}C_\xi\ne\emptyset\}.
\]
This is a hereditary family of finite subsets of \(S\), and its closure in
\(2^S\) is the adequate compact
\[
K=\{A\subseteq S:\bigcap_{\xi\in A}C_\xi\ne\emptyset\},
\]
which is a closed subspace of \(\cA\), hence Corson.

We verify the Ptak convex mean lower bound from the source proposition.  Let
\(\lambda\) be any convex mean on \(S\), so \(\lambda\) has finite support and
\(\sum_{\xi\in S}\lambda(\xi)=1\).  Then
\[
\int_L \sum_{\xi\in S}\lambda(\xi)\mathbf{1}_{C_\xi}\,d\mu
=
\sum_{\xi\in S}\lambda(\xi)\mu(C_\xi)
>\delta .
\]
Hence there is \(x\in L\) such that
\[
\sum_{\xi\in S}\lambda(\xi)\mathbf{1}_{C_\xi}(x)>\delta .
\]
Let
\[
F_x=\{\xi\in\supp(\lambda):x\in C_\xi\}.
\]
Then \(F_x\) is finite, belongs to \(\cF\), and
\(\lambda(F_x)>\delta\).  Therefore
\[
\inf_{\lambda}\sup_{F\in\cF}\lambda(F)\ge \delta,
\]
where the infimum ranges over convex means on \(S\).

Proposition 2.1 of Hajek--Russo says that this condition implies
\(\ellone(S)\embed C(\overline{\cF})=C(K)\).  Since \(|S|=\omegatwo\), this is
the desired embedding \(\ellone(\omegatwo)\embed C(K)\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequences}

\begin{corollary}
If the point-countable positive family assumption at \(\omegatwo\) is
consistent, then the source Problem 4.3 has a positive consistency answer.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem \ref{thm:positive} in a model satisfying the assumption.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
In every model in which \(\omegatwo\) is a caliber of Radon measures, the
source Problem 4.3 has a negative answer.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is Theorem \ref{thm:negative}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet does not prove a full positive or negative answer in \(\ZFC\).
The positive theorem depends on the existence of a point-countable family of
positive-measure closed sets of size \(\omegatwo\).  The negative theorem
depends on \(\omegatwo\) being a caliber of Radon measures.  The relationship
between these hypotheses and standard forcing axioms was not settled in this
packet.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

The Banach-space/topological proofs above are short and should be checked
first for correct use of Talagrand's theorem and the \(\kappa\)-Corson
theorems.  The main mathematical next step is set-theoretic: determine whether
the point-countable positive family at \(\omegatwo\) is consistent, or whether
a ZFC argument forces a contradiction without assuming the measure-caliber
hypothesis.
