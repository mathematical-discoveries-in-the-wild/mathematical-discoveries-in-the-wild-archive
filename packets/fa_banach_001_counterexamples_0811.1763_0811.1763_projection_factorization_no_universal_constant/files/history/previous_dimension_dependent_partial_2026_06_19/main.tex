\section*{Status}

This packet records a likely valid strengthened partial result for Problem 3 in
M. I. Ostrovskii, \emph{Compositions of projections in Banach spaces and
relations between approximation properties}, arXiv:0811.1763.  The result
does not solve the full problem, which asks for a constant independent of all
dimensions.  It proves the problem with a constant depending only on
$d=\dim X_1$, namely $C_d=1+2\sqrt d$.  In particular the line case has
$C_1=3$.

\section*{Source Problem}

Ostrovskii asks the following problem on page 7 of the source paper.

\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}

In the paper's notation, $\lambda(Y,X)$ denotes the relative projection
constant of a finite-dimensional subspace $Y$ in a normed space $X$.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

Start with a minimal projection $P:X_3\to X_1$ and a minimal projection
$R:X_3\to X_2$.  The product $(P|_{X_2})R$ usually does not equal $P$.  The
defect is the finite-rank map $P-(P|_{X_2})R$, which vanishes on $X_2$ and has
range in $X_1\subset X_2$.  Adding this defect to $R$ keeps the map fixed on
$X_2$, hence keeps it a projection onto $X_2$, while forcing the composed
projection onto $X_1$ to be the chosen minimal projection $P$.  The price is
controlled by $\lambda(X_1,X_3)$.  The classical Kadets-Snobar theorem says
that every $d$-dimensional subspace of a Banach space is $\sqrt d$-complemented
\cite{KadetsSnobar}.  This gives a constant depending only on
$d=\dim X_1$.

\section*{Theorems}

\begin{theorem}[Correction theorem]
Let $X_1\subset X_2\subset X_3$ be Banach spaces with $X_1$ and $X_2$
finite-dimensional.  Then there exist projections
$P_1:X_2\to X_1$ and $P_2:X_3\to X_2$ such that
\[
  \|P_2\|\leq \bigl(1+2\lambda(X_1,X_3)\bigr)\lambda(X_2,X_3)
  \qquad\hbox{and}\qquad
  \|P_1P_2\|=\lambda(X_1,X_3).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Because $X_1$ and $X_2$ are finite-dimensional, the relative projection
constants are attained.  Let $P:X_3\to X_1$ and $R:X_3\to X_2$ be projections
with
\[
  \|P\|=\lambda(X_1,X_3),\qquad
  \|R\|=\lambda(X_2,X_3).
\]
Define
\[
  P_1=P|_{X_2}:X_2\to X_1
\]
and
\[
  P_2x=Rx+Px-P(Rx),\qquad x\in X_3.
\]
The map $P_1$ is a projection from $X_2$ onto $X_1$, since $P$ is the identity
on $X_1$.  The map $P_2$ has range contained in $X_2$, because both $Rx$ and
$Px-P(Rx)$ lie in $X_2$.  If $x\in X_2$, then $Rx=x$, and hence
$P_2x=x+Px-Px=x$.  Thus $P_2$ is a projection from $X_3$ onto $X_2$.

For every $x\in X_3$,
\[
  P_1P_2x
  =P(Rx)+P(Px)-P(P(Rx))
  =Px.
\]
Thus $P_1P_2$ is the minimal projection $P$ onto $X_1$, and
\[
  \|P_1P_2\|=\lambda(X_1,X_3).
\]

For $\|x\|\leq 1$,
\[
  \|P_2x\|
  \leq \|Rx\|+\|Px\|+\|P(Rx)\|
  \leq \lambda(X_2,X_3)+\lambda(X_1,X_3)
        +\lambda(X_1,X_3)\lambda(X_2,X_3).
\]
Since every projection onto a nonzero subspace has norm at least one,
$\lambda(X_2,X_3)\geq 1$.  Therefore
\[
  \|P_2\|
  \leq \bigl(1+2\lambda(X_1,X_3)\bigr)\lambda(X_2,X_3).
\]
This proves the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fixed-dimensional corollary]
If $\dim X_1=d$, then Ostrovskii's Problem 3 has an affirmative answer for
the triple $X_1\subset X_2\subset X_3$ with
\[
  C_d=1+2\sqrt d.
\]
In particular, if $\dim X_1=1$, the problem has an affirmative answer with
$C_1=3$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the Kadets-Snobar theorem, every $d$-dimensional subspace of a Banach space
is $\sqrt d$-complemented.  Hence $\lambda(X_1,X_3)\leq\sqrt d$, and the
preceding theorem gives
\[
  \|P_2\|\leq (1+2\sqrt d)\lambda(X_2,X_3)
\]
while preserving $\|P_1P_2\|=\lambda(X_1,X_3)$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The correction theorem uses Hahn-Banach-level finite-rank projection algebra
and the standard attainment of relative projection constants for
finite-dimensional ranges.  The latter can be proved by writing projections
onto the finite-dimensional target through a fixed finite basis and using
weak-star compactness of bounded sets in $X_3^*$ to pass to a norm-minimizing
limit.  The fixed-dimensional corollary uses the classical Kadets-Snobar
projection theorem.

No computational verification is needed.  A bounded novelty check searched the
run indexes for arXiv:0811.1763 and projection-factorization keywords, searched
the local parsed corpus for the problem phrases, and searched the web for exact
phrases from Problem 3.  No prior packet or obvious published resolution of
this dimension-dependent correction theorem was found.  The novelty confidence
is modest, because the argument is elementary and the full problem is much
stronger.

\section*{Limitations}

The result is not a full solution of Problem 3.  The constant obtained here
depends on $\sqrt d$, where $d=\dim X_1$ (or more sharply on
$\lambda(X_1,X_3)$).  The original problem asks for a single universal constant
independent of $d$, $X_1$, $X_2$, and $X_3$.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review the correction formula for $P_2$ and the norm estimate.  The most
important point is that the correction $P-(P|_{X_2})R$ vanishes on $X_2$,
which makes $P_2$ a genuine projection onto $X_2$ while forcing $P_1P_2$ to
equal the chosen minimal projection onto $X_1$.
