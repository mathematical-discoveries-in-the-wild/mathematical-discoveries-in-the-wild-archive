\section*{Source and Question}

The source is Gilles Pisier, \emph{Seemingly injective von Neumann
algebras}, arXiv:2010.13743. In Remark 1.7, Pisier asks whether tensor
products preserve seeming injectivity:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

In text, the question is whether the tensor product of two seemingly
injective \vN{} algebras \(N,M\) is seemingly injective, even when
\(N=B(K)\). Pisier points out the obstruction: unlike completely positive
maps, merely unital positive maps do not tensorize in general.

\section*{Definitions Used}

Pisier calls a \vN{} algebra \(M\) \emph{seemingly injective} if
\(\operatorname{Id}_M\) factors through some \(B(H)\),
\[
  M \xrightarrow{u} B(H) \xrightarrow{v} M,
\]
where \(u\) is normal, unital and positive, \(\|v\|_{cb}=1\), and
\(vu=\operatorname{Id}_M\). Since \(v\) is unital and completely contractive,
it is completely positive.

Pisier's Theorem 1.11 gives the equivalent matricial weak* positive
approximation property: \(M\) is seemingly injective if and only if there is
a net of normal finite-rank maps \(T_\alpha=v_\alpha u_\alpha\) converging
pointwise weak* to \(\operatorname{Id}_M\), where
\[
  M \xrightarrow{u_\alpha} M_{n(\alpha)}
  \xrightarrow{v_\alpha} M
\]
has \(u_\alpha,v_\alpha\) unital positive and \(u_\alpha\) normal.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The obstruction in Pisier's remark is real for noncommutative tensor factors:
\(\id_{B(K)}\otimes u\) is positive only when \(u\) is completely positive.
But if the other tensor leg is abelian, positivity can be checked
coordinatewise. More precisely, approximate the abelian algebra by finite
dimensional abelian algebras \(\ell_\infty^r\). After tensoring with the
matricial weak* PAP factorization for \(M\), the intermediate algebra is
\(\ell_\infty^r\otimes M_n\), a finite direct sum of matrix algebras. A block
diagonal inclusion and a positive conditional expectation turn this into a
legitimate matricial factorization. Thus the tensor product has Pisier's
matricial weak* PAP, hence is seemingly injective.

\section*{Main Partial Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(A\) be an abelian \vN{} algebra and let \(M\) be seemingly injective.
Then \(A\otv M\) is seemingly injective.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since \(M\) is seemingly injective, Pisier's Theorem 1.11 provides a net
\[
  M \xrightarrow{u_\alpha} M_{n(\alpha)}
  \xrightarrow{v_\alpha} M
\]
with \(u_\alpha,v_\alpha\) unital positive, \(u_\alpha\) normal, and
\(T_\alpha=v_\alpha u_\alpha\to \operatorname{Id}_M\) pointwise weak*.

Abelian \vN{} algebras are injective, hence semidiscrete. In the commutative
case this gives a net of finite-rank normal unital positive maps
\[
  S_\beta=q_\beta p_\beta:A\to A
\]
converging pointwise weak* to \(\operatorname{Id}_A\), with
\[
  A \xrightarrow{p_\beta} \ell_\infty^{r(\beta)}
  \xrightarrow{q_\beta} A
\]
normal unital positive. Concretely, for \(A=L^\infty(\Omega)\) these are the
usual finite-partition averaging/simple-function maps.

For each pair \((\beta,\alpha)\), define on \(A\otv M\)
\[
  R_{\beta,\alpha}:=S_\beta\otimes T_\alpha .
\]
We claim that \(R_{\beta,\alpha}\) admits a matricial positive factorization.
Indeed, \(p_\beta\otimes u_\alpha\) maps \(A\otv M\) into
\(\ell_\infty^{r(\beta)}\otimes M_{n(\alpha)}\). This tensor map is positive:
if \(p_\beta=(\varphi_1,\ldots,\varphi_r)\) with positive normal coordinate
functionals, then each coordinate is
\[
  (\varphi_i\otimes u_\alpha)
  =u_\alpha\circ(\varphi_i\otimes \id_M),
\]
a composition of positive maps. It is also normal and unital.

Likewise \(q_\beta\otimes v_\alpha\) is positive on
\(\ell_\infty^r\otimes M_n\), because a positive element there is a tuple
\((b_1,\ldots,b_r)\) with \(b_i\geq 0\), and it is sent to
\(\sum_i q_\beta(e_i)\otimes v_\alpha(b_i)\), a sum of positive tensors.

Now identify \(\ell_\infty^r\otimes M_n\) with the block diagonal subalgebra
of \(M_{rn}\). Let \(j:\ell_\infty^r\otimes M_n\to M_{rn}\) be the unital
positive block inclusion and let \(E:M_{rn}\to \ell_\infty^r\otimes M_n\)
be the unital positive block-diagonal conditional expectation. Then
\[
  A\otv M
  \xrightarrow{\,j(p_\beta\otimes u_\alpha)\,} M_{rn}
  \xrightarrow{\,(q_\beta\otimes v_\alpha)E\,} A\otv M
\]
is a matricial factorization through a matrix algebra by unital positive
maps, with normal first leg, and its product is \(S_\beta\otimes T_\alpha\).

The maps \(S_\beta\otimes T_\alpha\) converge pointwise weak* to
\(\operatorname{Id}_{A\otv M}\). This is immediate on algebraic simple
tensors, and then follows for all of \(A\otv M\) by uniform boundedness and
weak* density of the algebraic tensor product. Hence \(A\otv M\) has the
matricial weak* PAP. Pisier's Theorem 1.11 gives that \(A\otv M\) is
seemingly injective.
\end{proof}

\section*{A \(B(K)\)-Tensor Subcase Under Finite Positivity}

The abelian theorem above does not reach Pisier's explicit \(B(K)\) frontier.
There is, however, a clean noncommutative extension once the positive
approximants on \(M\) have arbitrarily high finite positivity.

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:bk-high-positive}
Let \(M\) be a QWEP \vN{} algebra. Suppose that for every integer \(r\geq 1\)
there is a net of normal finite-rank unital \(r\)-positive maps
\[
  T_{\alpha}^{(r)}:M\to M
\]
converging pointwise weak* to \(\operatorname{Id}_M\). Then, for every Hilbert
space \(K\), the \vN{} algebra \(B(K)\otv M\) is seemingly injective.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By semidiscreteness of \(B(K)\), or directly by compressing to finite
dimensional subspaces of \(K\), there is a net of normal finite-rank unital
completely positive maps
\[
  S_\beta:B(K)\to B(K)
\]
converging pointwise weak* to \(\operatorname{Id}_{B(K)}\), where each
\(S_\beta\) factors through a finite dimensional \(C^*\)-algebra
\[
  F_\beta=\bigoplus_{j=1}^{m(\beta)}M_{r_j(\beta)}.
\]
Write \(R_\beta=\max_j r_j(\beta)\).

Choose, for each \(\beta\), an approximating map
\(T_\alpha^{(R_\beta)}\) on \(M\). We claim that
\[
  S_\beta\otimes T_\alpha^{(R_\beta)}:
  B(K)\otv M\to B(K)\otv M
\]
is positive. Factor \(S_\beta=q_\beta p_\beta\), with \(p_\beta\) and
\(q_\beta\) unital completely positive. The map
\[
  p_\beta\otimes \id_M:B(K)\otv M\to F_\beta\otv M
\]
is completely positive, hence positive. On each summand
\(M_{r_j(\beta)}\otv M\), the map
\(\id_{M_{r_j(\beta)}}\otimes T_\alpha^{(R_\beta)}\) is positive because
\(T_\alpha^{(R_\beta)}\) is \(R_\beta\)-positive and \(r_j(\beta)\leq
R_\beta\). Finally \(q_\beta\otimes \id_M\) is completely positive. The
composition is exactly \(S_\beta\otimes T_\alpha^{(R_\beta)}\), so it is a
normal finite-rank unital positive map.

As \((\beta,\alpha)\) varies in the product directed set, these maps converge
pointwise weak* to \(\operatorname{Id}_{B(K)\otv M}\). This is clear on
algebraic simple tensors and then follows by uniform boundedness and weak*
density.

It remains only to invoke Pisier's Theorem 1.11. The algebra \(B(K)\otv M\)
is QWEP: by Kirchberg's metric-surjection characterization of QWEP, tensoring
a u.c.p. metric surjection onto \(M\) with the injective operator space
\(B(K)\) again gives a u.c.p. metric surjection onto the amplification
\(B(K)\otv M\). Thus \(B(K)\otv M\) has the weak* PAP and is QWEP, hence is
seemingly injective.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Let \(G\) be one of the groups for which the Haagerup--de Canniere multiplier
construction cited in Pisier's Remark 1.12 gives, for every fixed
\(r\geq 1\), normal finite-rank unital \(r\)-positive multipliers converging
pointwise weak* to the identity on \(L(G)\). If \(L(G)\) is QWEP, then
\[
  B(K)\otv L(G)
\]
is seemingly injective for every Hilbert space \(K\). In particular this
applies to the free-group factors covered by Pisier's discussion.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:bk-high-positive} to the \(r\)-positive multiplier
approximants.
\end{proof}

\section*{Finite Matrix Companion}

\begin{corollary}
Let \(n\geq 1\). Suppose \(M\) admits a seeming-injectivity factorization
\[
  M \xrightarrow{u} B(H) \xrightarrow{v} M
\]
with \(u\) normal, unital, positive and \(n\)-positive, and with \(v\) unital
completely positive. Then \(M_n\otv M\) is seemingly injective.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The map \(\id_{M_n}\otimes u:M_n\otv M\to M_n\otv B(H)\simeq
B(\mathbb C^n\otimes H)\) is normal, unital and positive exactly because
\(u\) is \(n\)-positive. The map \(\id_{M_n}\otimes v\) is unital completely
positive, and
\[
  (\id_{M_n}\otimes v)(\id_{M_n}\otimes u)
  =\id_{M_n\otv M}.
\]
This is the defining factorization for seeming injectivity.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This does not solve the \(B(K)\) case in Pisier's remark for an arbitrary
seemingly injective \(M\). The proofs isolate the missing point. Either the
finite-dimensional approximants on the other tensor leg must be abelian, or
the approximants on \(M\) must be \(r\)-positive up to the needed matrix size.
For a general seemingly injective algebra, Pisier's theorem gives positive
matricial weak* approximants, but not arbitrarily \(r\)-positive ones.
