# Partial result: abelian tensor factors preserve seeming injectivity

## Status

`partial_result_likely_valid`.

This packet gives a solved positive subcase of the tensor-product question in
Pisier's *Seemingly injective von Neumann algebras* (arXiv:2010.13743).

## Source Question

In Remark 1.7, Pisier writes that it is unclear whether the tensor product of
two seemingly injective von Neumann algebras is seemingly injective, even when
one factor is `B(K)`. The obstruction noted there is that unital positive maps,
unlike completely positive maps, cannot generally be tensorized.

## Result

If `M` is seemingly injective and `A` is abelian von Neumann, then
`A \bar\otimes M` is seemingly injective.

The proof uses Pisier's equivalence between seeming injectivity and matricial
weak* positive approximation property. Abelian von Neumann algebras admit
finite-dimensional normal positive approximations through abelian algebras.
When one tensor leg factors through a finite-dimensional abelian algebra, the
tensor product of positive maps remains positive coordinatewise.

There is also a finite matrix companion: if a factorization of `Id_M` can be
chosen with the embedding leg `n`-positive, then `M_n \bar\otimes M` is
seemingly injective.

The follow-up push adds a noncommutative tensor subcase. If `M` is QWEP and
has weak* positive approximation maps that can be chosen `r`-positive for
every finite `r`, then `B(K) \bar\otimes M` is seemingly injective for every
Hilbert space `K`. This applies to the free-group-factor style approximations
discussed in Pisier's Remark 1.12, where the finite-rank multipliers can be
chosen `k`-positive for any prescribed `k`.

## Scope

This does not solve Pisier's question for `B(K)` or for tensor products of
arbitrary seemingly injective algebras. It isolates exactly the missing point:
one needs either a commutative tensor leg or arbitrarily high finite positivity
of the positive approximants on the noncommutative leg.
