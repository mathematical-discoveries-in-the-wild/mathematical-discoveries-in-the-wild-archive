## Claim

This packet is retained as provenance for the density/basis part of the
argument. The later full packet gives a candidate proof of the pathwise
expansion clause as well.

Remark 2.26 asks two separate Karhunen-Loeve questions for Gaussian random
variables in separable `r`-Banach spaces with separating dual:

1. whether the orthonormal basis in Byczkowski's Hilbert space `E^2(P_X)` can
   be chosen from `E^*`;
2. whether it can be chosen so that the resulting series equals the original
   random variable pathwise.

This packet proves the first point only. If `E^*` separates points of the
separable `r`-Banach space `E`, then the canonical copy of `E^*` is dense in
`E^2(P_X)`. Consequently, `E^2(P_X)` has an orthonormal basis consisting of
continuous linear functionals.

## Proof idea

If an additive Gaussian functional `g` is orthogonal to every continuous
linear functional, then `g(X)` is jointly Gaussian with every finite family
`x^*(X)` and has zero covariance with them. Hence `g(X)` is independent of the
sigma-field generated by the separating dual. But `g` is itself measurable
with respect to that sigma-field, so `g(X)` is independent of itself and must
be constant, hence zero.

## Scope and limitations

This is a superseded partial result. It does not itself prove the stronger
pathwise expansion `sum h_n(X) x_n = X` almost surely; see the full packet for
the candidate upgrade. The packet also does not claim a new Karhunen-Loeve
theorem for spaces without separating dual.
