\begin{abstract}
We answer the countable compact metric \(C(K)\)-Calkin question raised in
Remark 2.23 of Horvath's paper arXiv:1807.10578.  If \(X\) is a complex
Banach space and \(K\) is countable compact metric with
\(\B(X)/\K(X)\cong C(K)\), then \(\B(X)\) has stable rank one.  Hence the
Motakis--Puglisi--Zisimopoulou spaces \(X_K\) cannot provide examples for
which \(\B(X_K)\) is Dedekind-finite but fails stable rank one.
\end{abstract}

\maketitle

\section{Source Question}

Horvath studies the relation between stable rank one and Dedekind-finiteness
for algebras of operators on Banach spaces.  After proving that Tarbard's
space gives a complex example with \(\B(X)\) Dedekind-finite but not
stable-rank-one, the paper considers the Motakis--Puglisi--Zisimopoulou
construction.  For every countable compact metric space \(K\), their
construction gives a Banach space \(X_K\) with
\[
        \B(X_K)/\K(X_K)\cong C(K).
\]
Remark 2.23 observes that \(\B(X_K)\) has left stable rank \(1\) or \(2\), and
then asks whether some such \(K\) may still make \(\B(X_K)\) Dedekind-finite
while not having stable rank one.

\section{Proof Intuition}

The source already supplies one side of the extension: for every Banach space
\(X\), the unitization of \(\K(X)\) has stable rank one.  So if
\(\B(X)/\K(X)\cong C(K)\), the ideal and the quotient both have stable rank
one as soon as \(C(K)\) does.

The only missing point is whether this stable-rank-one information passes
from ideal and quotient to the whole algebra.  It does when quotient
invertibles lift.  For zero-dimensional compact \(K\), \(C(K)\) has
path-connected invertible group: a nonvanishing continuous function has a
continuous logarithm obtained by refining a finite cover to a clopen
partition.  Connected invertibles in the quotient lift by exponentials.  A
standard perturbation argument inside \(1+\K(X)\) then gives density of
invertibles in \(\B(X)\).

\section{Stable-Rank Extension Lemmas}

We use stable rank one in Rieffel's sense: a unital Banach algebra \(A\) has
\(\tsr(A)=1\) if \(\inv(A)\) is norm-dense in \(A\).

\begin{lemma}[Connected quotient invertibles lift]\label{lem:connected-lift}
Let \(A\) be a unital Banach algebra, \(J\triangleleft A\) a closed two-sided
ideal, and \(q:A\to A/J\) the quotient map.  If \(\inv(A/J)\) is connected,
then every invertible element of \(A/J\) has an invertible lift in \(A\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
In a unital Banach algebra, the connected component of the identity in the
invertible group consists of finite products of exponentials.  Since
\(\inv(A/J)\) is connected, each \(b\in\inv(A/J)\) has the form
\[
        b=\exp(b_1)\cdots \exp(b_n)
\]
for some \(b_i\in A/J\).  Choose \(a_i\in A\) with \(q(a_i)=b_i\).  Then
\[
        u=\exp(a_1)\cdots \exp(a_n)
\]
is invertible in \(A\), and \(q(u)=b\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Stable-rank-one extension]\label{lem:extension}
Let \(A\) be a unital Banach algebra and let \(J\triangleleft A\) be a closed
two-sided ideal.  Suppose:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the unitization \(\widetilde J\) has stable rank one;
\item \(A/J\) has stable rank one;
\item every invertible element of \(A/J\) lifts to an invertible element of
      \(A\).
\end{enumerate}
Then \(A\) has stable rank one.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(a\in A\) and let \(\varepsilon>0\).  Since \(A/J\) has stable rank one,
choose \(b\in\inv(A/J)\) with
\[
        \|q(a)-b\|<\delta ,
\]
where \(\delta>0\) will be chosen below.  By the lifting hypothesis, choose
\(u\in\inv(A)\) with \(q(u)=b\).  The quotient norm estimate gives some
\(j\in J\) such that
\[
        \|a-u-j\|<\delta .
\]
Now \(1+u^{-1}j\in \widetilde J\).  Since \(\widetilde J\) has stable rank
one, choose \(v\in\inv(\widetilde J)\) with
\[
        \|v-(1+u^{-1}j)\|<\frac{\varepsilon}{2\|u\|}.
\]
The element \(v\), viewed in \(A\), is invertible in \(A\), hence \(uv\) is
invertible in \(A\).  Moreover
\[
 \|uv-(u+j)\|
 \leq \|u\|\,\|v-(1+u^{-1}j)\|
 < \varepsilon/2.
\]
Choosing \(\delta<\varepsilon/2\) gives
\[
        \|a-uv\|\leq \|a-u-j\|+\|u+j-uv\|<\varepsilon .
\]
Thus invertibles are dense in \(A\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Zero-dimensional \(C(K)\)]\label{lem:zero-dim}
Let \(K\) be a compact Hausdorff zero-dimensional space.  Then \(C(K)\) has
stable rank one and \(\inv(C(K))\) is path-connected.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We use the standard fact that every finite open cover of a compact
zero-dimensional Hausdorff space has a finite pairwise disjoint clopen
refinement.

First let \(f\in C(K)\) and \(\varepsilon>0\).  For each \(z\in f(K)\), choose
a nonzero scalar \(c_z\) and a neighborhood \(U_z\) of \(z\) in \(\C\) such
that \(|w-c_z|<\varepsilon\) for all \(w\in U_z\).  Choose finitely many
\(U_{z_1},\ldots,U_{z_m}\) covering the compact set \(f(K)\), and refine the
open cover \(f^{-1}(U_{z_i})\) to a finite clopen partition
\(E_1,\ldots,E_r\), each \(E_\ell\) contained in some \(f^{-1}(U_{z_i})\).
Define \(g=c_{z_i}\) on \(E_\ell\) whenever \(E_\ell\subseteq f^{-1}(U_{z_i})\).
Then \(g\) is continuous, nonvanishing, and \(\|f-g\|<\varepsilon\).  Hence
\(\inv(C(K))\) is dense.

Now let \(f\in\inv(C(K))\).  Cover the compact set \(f(K)\subseteq\C\setminus
\{0\}\) by finitely many open disks on which a branch of the logarithm is
defined.  After refining the corresponding inverse-image cover by a finite
clopen partition, define a continuous function \(h\in C(K)\) by using the
chosen logarithm branch on each clopen piece.  Then \(\exp(h)=f\), so
\[
        t\mapsto \exp(th)\qquad (0\leq t\leq 1)
\]
is a path in \(\inv(C(K))\) from \(1\) to \(f\).  Thus \(\inv(C(K))\) is
path-connected.
\end{proof}

\section{Main Result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let \(X\) be a complex Banach space and let \(K\) be a countable compact metric
space.  If
\[
        \B(X)/\K(X)\cong C(K)
\]
as unital Banach algebras, then \(\B(X)\) has stable rank one.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A countable compact metric space is zero-dimensional.  Indeed, it is
scattered, hence totally disconnected, and compact metric totally disconnected
spaces are zero-dimensional.  Therefore Lemma~\ref{lem:zero-dim} gives
\[
        \tsr(C(K))=1
        \quad\text{and}\quad
        \inv(C(K)) \text{ is connected}.
\]

Set \(A=\B(X)\) and \(J=\K(X)\).  Horvath's Proposition 2.7 states that if
\(J\) is a closed two-sided ideal of \(\B(X)\) contained in the inessential
operators, then \(\widetilde J\) has stable rank one.  Since compact operators
are inessential, \(\widetilde{\K(X)}\) has stable rank one.

The quotient \(A/J\) is isomorphic to \(C(K)\), so \(A/J\) has stable rank one
and connected invertible group.  By Lemma~\ref{lem:connected-lift}, every
invertible in \(A/J\) lifts to an invertible in \(A\).  Lemma~\ref{lem:extension}
now applies and yields \(\tsr(\B(X))=1\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
For the Motakis--Puglisi--Zisimopoulou spaces \(X_K\) with \(K\) countable
compact metric, \(\B(X_K)\) cannot be Dedekind-finite while failing stable
rank one.  In fact \(\B(X_K)\) has stable rank one.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The MPZ construction gives \(\B(X_K)/\K(X_K)\cong C(K)\), so
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} applies.  Stable rank one implies Dedekind-finiteness,
as recalled in Horvath's Lemma 2.1, so the non-stable-rank-one alternative in
Remark 2.23 is impossible.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof is non-computational.  Its dependencies are:
\begin{itemize}
\item Horvath's Proposition 2.7, giving stable rank one for
      \(\widetilde{\K(X)}\);
\item the standard zero-dimensional topology facts used in
      Lemma~\ref{lem:zero-dim};
\item the elementary Banach-algebra fact that the identity component of the
      invertible group consists of finite products of exponentials.
\end{itemize}

`stable rank', `X\_K', `MPZ', `Calkin', and countable compact \(C(K)\) phrases.
No exact existing packet was found.  Local source checks included Horvath's
paper and Motakis's later arXiv:2110.10868 paper, whose complemented-subspace
section gives related Dedekind-finiteness information but not this stable-rank
extension observation.  A bounded web search for exact phrase combinations
around \(B(X_K)\), stable rank one, \(C(K)\), Calkin algebras, and
arXiv:1807.10578 found no exact prior answer.  Because the argument is short,
the novelty confidence is moderate rather than absolute.
