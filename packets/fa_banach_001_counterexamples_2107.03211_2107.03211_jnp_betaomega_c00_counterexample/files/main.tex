\section{Source problem}

Bargetz, K\k{a}kol, and Sobota ask the following in Problem 4.8 on page 7 of
arXiv:2107.03211v3 \cite{BKS}.

\begin{quote}
Let $X$ be a Tychonoff space and $E$ a locally convex space.  Assume that
$C_p(X,E)$ has the Josefson--Nissenzweig property.  Does it follow that
$C_p(X)$ has the Josefson--Nissenzweig property or $E$ has the
Josefson--Nissenzweig property?
\end{quote}

\section{Definitions}

All spaces may be taken over $\K=\mathbb R$ or $\mathbb C$.  For a locally
convex space $G$, a set $B\subseteq G$ is \emph{barrel-bounded} if every
barrel in $G$ absorbs $B$.  The topology $\beta^*(G',G)$ is uniform
convergence on the barrel-bounded subsets of $G$.  The space $G$ has the JNP
if some sequence in $G'$ is null for $\sigma(G',G)$ but is not null for
$\beta^*(G',G)$ \cite{BG}.

Write $\czerop$ for the Banach space $c_0$ equipped only with its pointwise
topology inherited from $\K^{\N}$.  Let $c_{00}$ denote the finitely supported
scalar sequences, equipped here with the norm inherited from $\ell_1$.
Thus $c_{00}$ is a normed locally convex space, but it is not complete.
The vector $e_n$ is the $n$th standard unit vector, and
$\pi_n(x)=x_n$.

We use the Stone--Cech compactification $\beta\N$ of the discrete space
$\N$.  Restriction identifies $C(\beta\N)$ isometrically with $\ell_\infty$.

\section{Proof intuition}

The earlier finite-slice reduction for this target showed that a
counterexample must escape in both tensor variables: it needs infinitely many
points of $X$ and infinitely many independent coefficient functionals in
$E'$.  The diagonal family
\[
  f\longmapsto \pi_n(f(n))
\]
does exactly this.

The choice $E=c_{00}\subset\ell_1$ makes the coordinate functionals
$\pi_n$ vanish eventually on every fixed vector.  More importantly, they
vanish uniformly on compact subsets of $c_{00}$.  Hence every continuous
$f:\beta\N\to c_{00}$ has
$\pi_n(f(n))\to0$, because its range is compact.  This gives a continuous
diagonal map from $C_p(\beta\N,c_{00})$ into $\czerop$.

In the other direction, a null sequence $a=(a_n)\in c_0$ gives a continuous
map on $\beta\N$ by sending $n$ to $a_ne_n$ and the entire remainder
$\beta\N\setminus\N$ to zero.  These two maps compose to the identity on
$\czerop$.  Thus the vector-valued function space contains a complemented
$\czerop$, although both factors separately fail the JNP.

\section{The counterexample}

\begin{lemma}[uniform coordinate decay on compacta]\label{lem:compact}
If $K\subset c_{00}$ is compact for the inherited $\ell_1$ norm, then
\[
  \sup_{x\in K}|\pi_n(x)|\longrightarrow0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For every fixed $x\in c_{00}$, one has $\pi_n(x)=0$ for all sufficiently
large $n$, and $\|\pi_n\|\leq1$ on $c_{00}\subset\ell_1$.  Given
$\varepsilon>0$, choose a finite $\varepsilon/2$-net
$x^1,\dots,x^m$ for $K$.  For all sufficiently large $n$, every
$\pi_n(x^j)$ is zero.  For such $n$ and any $x\in K$, choose $j$ with
$\|x-x^j\|_1<\varepsilon/2$ to get
$|\pi_n(x)|<\varepsilon/2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[$c_{00}$ is $b$-feral]\label{lem:feral}
Every barrel-bounded subset of $c_{00}$ is contained in a finite-dimensional
subspace.  Consequently, $c_{00}$ with the inherited $\ell_1$ norm does not
have the JNP.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose that a set $B\subset c_{00}$ has infinite-dimensional linear span.
Then the union of the supports of its elements is infinite.  Choose distinct
indices $n_k$ and vectors $x^k\in B$ such that $x^k_{n_k}\neq0$.
Choose positive weights $w_n$ satisfying
\[
  w_{n_k}|x^k_{n_k}|>k
\]
and, for instance, put $w_n=1$ at the remaining indices.  The formula
\[
  q(x)=\sum_n w_n|x_n| \qquad (x\in c_{00})
\]
is finite for every $x$.  Its unit ball
$U=\{x:q(x)\leq1\}$ is absolutely convex and absorbing.  It is also closed
in the inherited $\ell_1$ topology: $q$ is the supremum of its continuous
finite partial sums, hence is lower semicontinuous.  Thus $U$ is a barrel.
But $q(x^k)>k$, so no scalar multiple of $U$ contains $B$.  Therefore $B$
is not barrel-bounded.  This proves the first assertion.

Now let $(\lambda_j)\subset(c_{00})'$ be weak-star null and let $B$ be
barrel-bounded.  The preceding paragraph places $B$ in a finite-dimensional
subspace $F$.  Since the ordinary $\ell_1$ unit ball is a barrel, $B$ is
norm-bounded.  Pointwise convergence of $\lambda_j|_F$ to zero is uniform on
norm-bounded subsets of the finite-dimensional space $F$.  Hence
$\sup_{x\in B}|\lambda_j(x)|\to0$.  Thus every weak-star null sequence is
$\beta^*$-null, and $c_{00}$ has no JNP.
\end{proof}

\Needspace{9\baselineskip}
\begin{proposition}\label{prop:beta}
$C_p(\beta\N)$ does not have the JNP.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This fact is recorded explicitly by Banakh and Gabriyelyan
\cite[Introduction]{BG}, referring to the normalized finitely supported
measure characterization of the JNP for $C_p$-spaces.  Here is a short
independent reduction to classical results.  If $C_p(\beta\N)$ had the JNP,
that characterization would give finitely supported measures
$\mu_j\in C(\beta\N)'$ such that
\[
  \|\mu_j\|=1,\qquad \mu_j(f)\longrightarrow0
  \quad(f\in C(\beta\N)).
\]
The isometry $C(\beta\N)=\ell_\infty$ and Grothendieck's theorem for
$\ell_\infty$ \cite{Grothendieck} imply that $(\mu_j)$ is weakly null in
$C(\beta\N)'$.  The union $S$ of the finite supports of the $\mu_j$ is
countable, and the closed linear span of the point masses $\{\delta_s:s\in
S\}$ is isometric to $\ell_1(S)$.  By Hahn--Banach, weak nullity in the
ambient measure space implies weak nullity in this $\ell_1(S)$.  Schur's
theorem \cite{Schur} then gives $\|\mu_j\|\to0$, a contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[negative answer to Problem 4.8]\label{thm:main}
Let
\[
  X=\beta\N,\qquad E=(c_{00},\|\cdot\|_1),\qquad
  H=C_p(\beta\N,E).
\]
Then neither $C_p(X)$ nor $E$ has the Josefson--Nissenzweig property, while
$H$ has the JNP.  In fact, $H$ contains a complemented subspace linearly
homeomorphic to $\czerop$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The two negative factor assertions are Lemma \ref{lem:feral} and Proposition
\ref{prop:beta}.  It remains to construct the complemented copy and a JNP
witness.

For $a=(a_n)\in c_0$, define $S(a):\beta\N\to c_{00}$ by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:S}
  S(a)(n)=a_ne_n\quad(n\in\N),
  \qquad S(a)(p)=0\quad(p\in\beta\N\setminus\N).
\end{equation}
This map is continuous in the norm topology of $c_{00}$.  Continuity at the
isolated points of $\N$ is immediate.  If $p\in\beta\N\setminus\N$ and
$\varepsilon>0$, the set
$F=\{n:|a_n|\geq\varepsilon\}$ is finite.  The clopen neighborhood
$\beta\N\setminus F$ of $p$ is mapped into the open $\varepsilon$-ball about
zero.  Hence $S(a)\in H$.

The linear map
\[
  S:\czerop\longrightarrow H
\]
is continuous for the pointwise topologies: evaluation at $n\in\N$ is the
coordinate map $a\mapsto a_ne_n$, while evaluation at every remainder point
is zero.

Define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:D}
  D:H\longrightarrow\K^{\N},
  \qquad D(f)=(\pi_n(f(n)))_{n\in\N}.
\end{equation}
For $f\in H$, the range $K=f(\beta\N)$ is a norm-compact subset of $c_{00}$.
Lemma \ref{lem:compact} yields
\[
  |\pi_n(f(n))|\leq\sup_{x\in K}|\pi_n(x)|\longrightarrow0.
\]
Thus $D(f)\in c_0$.  The map $D:H\to\czerop$ is continuous because its
$n$th coordinate is the continuous functional $\pi_n\circ\operatorname{ev}_n$
on $H$.  Equations \eqref{eq:S} and \eqref{eq:D} give
\[
  D\circ S=\operatorname{id}_{\czerop}.
\]
Consequently, $S$ is a linear homeomorphism onto its range and
$P=S\circ D$ is a continuous projection of $H$ onto $S(\czerop)$.

For completeness, the same maps give a direct JNP witness.  Put
\[
  \Phi_n=\pi_n\circ\operatorname{ev}_n\in H'.
\]
For each $f\in H$, the preceding compact-range argument says
$\Phi_n(f)\to0$, so $(\Phi_n)$ is weak-star null.  The set
$A=\{e_n:n\in\N\}$ is barrel-bounded in $\czerop$: every barrel in
$\czerop$ is also a closed, absolutely convex, absorbing subset of the
Banach space $c_0$, hence is a norm neighborhood, and the set $A$ is
norm-bounded.  Continuous linear maps carry barrel-bounded sets to
barrel-bounded sets, so $S(A)$ is barrel-bounded in $H$.  Finally,
\[
  \Phi_n(S(e_n))=1
\]
for every $n$.  Hence $(\Phi_n)$ is not $\beta^*(H',H)$-null, proving that
$H$ has the JNP.
\end{proof}

\section{Scope, novelty, and verification}

The example disproves the converse exactly as stated.  It exploits the
incompleteness of $c_{00}$ in an essential visible way: the completion
$\ell_1$ has the JNP by the classical Josefson--Nissenzweig theorem.  The
argument therefore does not decide whether a positive converse may hold when
$E$ is complete, barrelled, Frechet, or Banach.  Nor does it classify all
mixed JNP witnesses in $L_p(X)\otimes E'$.

The proof is noncomputational.  The main verifier checkpoints are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)},itemsep=0.25em]
  \item $S(a)$ is norm-continuous at every point of the Stone--Cech
        remainder;
  \item compact subsets of $c_{00}\subset\ell_1$ have uniform coordinate
        decay;
  \item $D$ lands in $c_0$ and is continuous for the pointwise topology;
  \item the weighted lower-semicontinuous gauge really gives a barrel proving
        that $c_{00}$ is $b$-feral;
  \item the unit vectors are barrel-bounded in $\czerop$.
\end{enumerate}
Each point is proved explicitly above.
