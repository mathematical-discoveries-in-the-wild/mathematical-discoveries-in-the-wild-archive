\section{Source question}

Gozlan and L\'eonard define
\[
 \omega_2(x)=\operatorname{sgn}(x)\max\{|x|,|x|^2\}
\]
and prove that Poincar\'e inequality for $(\omega_2)_\#\mu$ implies
$\Ttwo$ for $\mu$.  Immediately afterward they ask whether the condition is
necessary \cite[Corollary 9.15 and the following paragraph, PDF p.~54]{GL}.

\section{Definitions and counterexample}

Write
\[
 d\law(t)=\frac12 e^{-|t|}\,dt,\qquad a_n=16^n\quad(n\geq1),
\]
and, for $t\geq0$, define the unit ramp
\[
 r_n(t)=\min\{\max\{t-a_n,0\},1\}.
\]
Set
\[
 b(t)=\sqrt{t+1}-1,
 \qquad F(t)=b(t)+\sum_{n\geq1}r_n(t),
\]
where the sum is locally finite.  Extend $F$ oddly to
\[
 T(t)=\operatorname{sgn}(t)F(|t|),
\]
and let
\[
 \mu=T_\#\law.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Sparse-ramp counterexample]\label{thm:main}
The probability measure $\mu$ is symmetric, absolutely continuous, and has a
positive density.  It satisfies Talagrand's quadratic transport inequality
$\Ttwo$.  Nevertheless $(\omega_2)_\#\mu$ does not satisfy Poincar\'e
inequality.
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof intuition}

The two-sided exponential law has a transport inequality with the mixed cost
$\min\{|s-t|^2,|s-t|\}$.  A map which is both Lipschitz and H\"older of order
$1/2$ turns this mixed cost into a quadratic cost, hence produces a $\Ttwo$
measure.  The square-root background $b(t)$ has exactly these two regularity
properties.

The sparse unit ramps do not destroy either property: exponential spacing
means that an interval of length $d$ meets at most $O(\sqrt d)$ ramps.  But a
ramp far out in the tail makes the reciprocal-density integral in the
weighted Muckenhoupt criterion large.  Its exponential factor cancels the
tail probability, leaving a factor comparable to $a_n$, which diverges.

\section{Regularity of the transport map}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:regularity}
The odd homeomorphism $T$ is $3/2$-Lipschitz and satisfies
\[
 |T(t)-T(s)|\leq 2\sqrt2\,|t-s|^{1/2}
 \qquad(s,t\in\R).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The ramps have disjoint interiors.  Almost everywhere on $[0,\infty)$,
\[
 0<b'(t)\leq\frac12,
 \qquad 0\leq \sum_nr_n'(t)\leq1.
\]
Thus $F$ is $3/2$-Lipschitz.  Since $F(0)=0$, the odd extension has the same
Lipschitz constant, including across zero.

For the half-H\"older bound, take $0\leq s<t$ and put $d=t-s$.  The background
satisfies $b(t)-b(s)\leq\sqrt d$.  If $d<1$, the interval $[s,t]$ meets at
most one ramp interval and the total ramp increment is at most
$d\leq\sqrt d$.  Suppose $d\geq1$ and $N$ ramp intervals meet $[s,t]$.
If $N=1$, their total increment is at most $1\leq\sqrt d$.  If $N\geq2$ and
the first such interval has index $p$, then
\[
 d\geq a_{p+N-1}-(a_p+1)
 \geq16(16^{N-1}-1)-1\geq N^2.
\]
The ramp increment is at most $N\leq\sqrt d$.  Hence
$F(t)-F(s)\leq2\sqrt d$ on either half-line.  For opposite signs,
\[
 |T(t)-T(s)|\leq2(\sqrt{|t|}+\sqrt{|s|})
 \leq2\sqrt2\,|t-s|^{1/2}.
\]
Strict monotonicity, continuity, and divergence follow from $b$, so $T$ is a
homeomorphism.
\end{proof}

\section{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}}

\subsection*{The quadratic transport inequality}

The source recalls that $\law$ satisfies a transport--entropy inequality for
\[
 c_0(s,t)=\min\{|s-t|^2,|s-t|\}
\]
with a finite universal constant \cite[Section 9.3]{GL}.  Put
$\delta=|s-t|$ and $\Delta=|T(s)-T(t)|$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:regularity} gives
\[
 \delta^2\geq\frac49\Delta^2,
 \qquad
 \delta\geq\frac18\Delta^2.
\]
Consequently
\[
 c_0(s,t)\geq\frac18|T(s)-T(t)|^2.
\]
The contraction principle for transport inequalities therefore shows that
$\mu=T_\#\law$ satisfies a quadratic transport--entropy inequality, i.e.
$\Ttwo$ (with an inessential finite change of constant).

Since $T$ is locally absolutely continuous and $T'(t)>0$ almost everywhere,
$\mu$ has a positive density $h$.  On the positive half-line it obeys
\begin{equation}\label{eq:density}
 h(T(t))=\frac{\frac12e^{-t}}{T'(t)}
 \qquad\text{for almost every }t>0.
\end{equation}
Symmetry follows from oddness of $T$.

\subsection*{Failure of Poincar\'e after $\omega_2$}

The median of $\mu$ is zero.  Proposition 9.16 of the source says that
$(\omega_2)_\#\mu$ satisfies Poincar\'e inequality if and only if, in
particular,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:muck}
 D^+_{\omega_2}
 =\sup_{x\geq0}\mu[x,\infty)
   \int_0^x\frac{\omega_2'(u)^2}{h(u)}\,du
 <\infty.
\end{equation}

Let $x_n=T(a_n+1)$ and abbreviate
$\beta_n=b(a_n)=\sqrt{a_n+1}-1$.  Monotonicity gives
\[
 \mu[x_n,\infty)=\frac12e^{-(a_n+1)}.
\]
On the ramp $a_n<t<a_n+1$, one has $T'(t)\geq1$ and
$T(t)\geq\beta_n>1$.  Hence $\omega_2'(T(t))=2T(t)$ there.  Using
\eqref{eq:density} and the change of variables $u=T(t)$,
\begin{align*}
 \int_0^{x_n}\frac{\omega_2'(u)^2}{h(u)}\,du
 &\geq
 \int_{a_n}^{a_n+1}8T(t)^2T'(t)^2e^t\,dt\\
 &\geq8\beta_n^2 e^{a_n}(e-1).
\end{align*}
Multiplying the last two displays yields
\[
 \mu[x_n,\infty)
 \int_0^{x_n}\frac{\omega_2'(u)^2}{h(u)}\,du
 \geq4(1-e^{-1})\beta_n^2\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
Thus $D^+_{\omega_2}=\infty$, so $(\omega_2)_\#\mu$ fails
Poincar\'e inequality.  This proves the theorem. \qed

\section{Verification, limitations, and novelty check}

The accompanying script \texttt{code/verify\_sparse\_ramp.py} samples
100,000 pairs around both signs and the first five ramps.  It checks the
Lipschitz, half-H\"older, and mixed-cost lower bounds and prints the first five
Muckenhoupt lower bounds.  The proof above is symbolic and does not depend on
the computation.

This is a one-dimensional negative answer to the literal necessity question.
No quantitative claim is made about the best $\Ttwo$ constant.  The map is
piecewise smooth rather than globally smooth; the source question only asks
about probability measures, and the resulting density is positive and
absolutely continuous.

The run registry and bounded primary-source searches used arXiv:1003.3852,
the exact source sentence, the authors, and combinations of
``$\omega_2$ image'', ``Talagrand $\Ttwo$'', ``weighted Poincar\'e'',
``necessary'', and ``counterexample''.  They found the source survey and
general weighted-Poincar\'e papers but no explicit answer or this sparse-ramp
construction.  This is not an exhaustive novelty certification.
