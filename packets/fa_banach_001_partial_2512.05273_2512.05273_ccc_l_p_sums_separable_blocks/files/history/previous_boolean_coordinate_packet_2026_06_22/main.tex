\section*{Status}

This is an expanded partial solution packet for the open question in
Salguero-Alarcon, Tradacete, and Trejo-Arroyo, \emph{Free quasi-Banach
lattices}, arXiv:2512.05273.  The claims below give positive
coordinate-decomposition families for their countable-chain-condition
conjecture, subject to human review.

\section*{Source problem}

The source paper fixes \(0<p\leq 1\), defines
\[
  E^\triangle=\mathcal L(E,\Lp),
\]
and gives \(B_{E^\triangle}\) the weak-\(\triangle\) topology
\(\sigma(E^\triangle,E)\), the initial topology for all evaluation maps
\(T\mapsto T(e)\in \Lp\).  The paper proves that
\[
  C_{\ph}^b(B_{\ell_p(\Gamma)^\triangle},\Lp)
\]
has the countable chain condition for every set \(\Gamma\), and then asks
whether the same conclusion holds for every \(p\)-natural quasi-Banach space
\(E\).  The relevant source passage is reproduced in Figure~\ref{fig:source}.

\section*{Transcription of the open question}

Section 7.2 of the source paper states:

\begin{quote}
We conjecture that Theorem 6.10 is true for any \(p\)-natural quasi-Banach
space \(E\); that is,
\[
  C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)
\]
satisfies the countable chain condition regardless of \(E\).
\end{quote}

The paper observes that the separable case follows because \(B_{E^\triangle}\)
is then a subspace of the separable metrizable space
\((B_{\Lp})^{\mathbb N}\), while topological CCC is not hereditary in general.

\section*{Definitions}

A vector lattice \(X\) satisfies the countable chain condition, abbreviated
CCC, if every pairwise disjoint family in \(X_+\) is countable.  A topological
space \(S\) satisfies topological CCC if every pairwise disjoint family of
nonempty open subsets of \(S\) is countable.

For a family \((E_\gamma)_{\gamma\in\Gamma}\) of \(p\)-Banach spaces and
\(p\leq r<\infty\),
\[
  \left(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma} E_\gamma\right)_r
\]
denotes the usual \(\ell_r\)-sum: \(x=(x_\gamma)\) belongs to it when
\(\sum_\gamma \norm{x_\gamma}^r<\infty\), with
\(\norm{x}^r=\sum_\gamma \norm{x_\gamma}^r\).  We also use
\[
  \left(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma} E_\gamma\right)_{c_0}
\]
for the \(c_0\)-sum, with norm
\(\norm{x}=\sup_\gamma\norm{x_\gamma}\) and the usual vanishing-at-infinity
condition.

We shall also use the following abstract form of a coordinate decomposition.
A \(p\)-Banach space \(E\) has a \emph{bounded separable Boolean coordinate
decomposition} over a set \(\Gamma\) if, for every \(A\subseteq\Gamma\), there
is a projection \(P_A:E\to E\) such that:
\[
  P_\emptyset=0,\qquad P_\Gamma=I,\qquad
  P_AP_B=P_{A\cap B},
\]
and \(P_{A\cup B}=P_A+P_B\) whenever \(A\cap B=\emptyset\); for every
\(x\in E\), \(P_Fx\to x\) as \(F\) increases through the finite subsets of
\(\Gamma\); every coordinate range \(P_{\{\gamma\}}E\) is separable; and
\[
  K:=\sup_{A\subseteq\Gamma}\|P_A\|<\infty.
\]

\section*{Proof intuition}

The source proof for \(\ell_p(\Gamma)\) works because
\[
  B_{\ell_p(\Gamma)^\triangle}\cong (B_{\Lp})^\Gamma,
\]
and the right-hand side is a product of separable metric spaces, hence has
topological CCC.  The same product mechanism survives if each scalar
coordinate is replaced by a separable \(p\)-natural block.  Operators from an
\(\ell_p\)-sum into \(L_p\) are exactly uniformly contractive families of
operators on the summands: the \(p\)-triangle inequality in \(L_p\) makes the
coordinatewise series converge with the expected norm bound.

Thus the weak-\(\triangle\) unit ball of the whole sum is not merely embedded
in a product; it is homeomorphic to the product of the block unit balls.  The
block unit balls are separable metrizable, so the product is topologically
CCC.  A general lifting lemma then turns topological CCC of the domain into
lattice CCC for \(L_p\)-valued continuous positive functions.

There is a second, more flexible mechanism for spaces with a bounded
separable Boolean coordinate decomposition.  Their weak-\(\triangle\) unit
balls need not be topologically CCC; \(c_0(\Gamma)\) already has weak-star dual
ball \(B_{\ell_1(\Gamma)}\), which has uncountably many disjoint weak-star
slices.  But those slices use thresholds and are not homogeneous.  Homogeneous
disjoint functions instead give disjoint relatively open cones.  Finite-block
operators are weak-\(\triangle\)-dense; after shrinking them toward zero, a
Delta-system and root-gluing argument shows that uncountably many relatively
open cones cannot be pairwise disjoint.  The proof uses only boundedness of
the Boolean projections and separability of finite coordinate ranges.

\begin{lemma}[Topological CCC lifts to the function lattice]
\label{lem:lift}
Let \(S\) be a topological space satisfying topological CCC.  Then every
sublattice of \(C(S,\Lp)\) consisting of continuous \(\Lp\)-valued functions
satisfies CCC.  In particular,
\[
  C_{\ph}^b(S,\Lp)
\]
satisfies CCC whenever it is defined.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
It suffices to rule out an uncountable family \((f_i)_{i\in I}\) of nonzero
positive pairwise disjoint continuous functions \(S\to\Lp\).  Use the
countable family \(\mathcal B\) of open balls in \(\Lp\) from Lemma 6.9 of the
source paper: it covers \(\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}\), and whenever
\(u,v\in B\in\mathcal B\), one has \(u\wedge v\neq 0\).

For each \(i\), choose \(s_i\in S\) with \(f_i(s_i)\neq0\), and then choose
\(B_i\in\mathcal B\) with \(f_i(s_i)\in B_i\).  Since \(\mathcal B\) is
countable, an uncountable subfamily has a common ball \(B\).  For that
subfamily, the open sets
\[
  U_i=f_i^{-1}(B)
\]
are nonempty.  They are pairwise disjoint: if \(s\in U_i\cap U_j\), then
\(f_i(s),f_j(s)\in B\), hence \(f_i(s)\wedge f_j(s)\neq0\), contradicting
the disjointness \(f_i\wedge f_j=0\).  This gives an uncountable pairwise
disjoint family of nonempty open subsets of \(S\), contradicting topological
CCC.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[A countable cone cover of \(L_p{}_+\)]
\label{lem:cone-cover}
There is a countable family \(\mathcal V\) of open cones in
\(\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}\) such that \(\mathcal V\) covers
\(\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}\), and whenever \(u,v\in V\in\mathcal V\), one has
\(u\wedge v\neq0\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(S_+=\{u\in\Lp{}_+:\|u\|_p=1\}\).  Since \(\Lp\) is separable, so is
\(S_+\); choose a countable \(1/3\)-net \((b_n)\) in \(S_+\).  Put
\[
  V_n=\left\{u\in\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}:
  \left\| \frac{u}{\|u\|_p}-b_n\right\|_p<\frac13\right\}.
\]
These sets are open cones and cover \(\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}\).  If
\(u,v\in V_n\) and \(u\wedge v=0\), then their normalizations are disjoint
positive unit vectors, so
\[
  \left\|\frac{u}{\|u\|_p}-\frac{v}{\|v\|_p}\right\|_p^p=2.
\]
On the other hand the \(p\)-triangle inequality gives this \(p\)-th power as
less than \(2(1/3)^p<2\), a contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The weak-\(\triangle\) ball of a \(p\)-sum]
\label{lem:product}
Let \(0<p\leq1\), and let \(E=(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma}E_\gamma)_p\).
Then the restriction map
\[
  \Phi:B_{E^\triangle}\longrightarrow
  \prod_{\gamma\in\Gamma} B_{E_\gamma^\triangle},\qquad
  \Phi(T)=(T\circ j_\gamma)_{\gamma\in\Gamma},
\]
where \(j_\gamma:E_\gamma\to E\) is the coordinate embedding, is a
homeomorphism when all unit balls are given their weak-\(\triangle\) topologies.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The map is well-defined and injective.  To prove surjectivity, take a family
\((T_\gamma)_\gamma\in\prod_\gamma B_{E_\gamma^\triangle}\).  For
\(x=(x_\gamma)\in E\), the support of \(x\) is countable, and the net of
finite partial sums
\[
  \sum_{\gamma\in F} T_\gamma x_\gamma
\]
is Cauchy in \(\Lp\), since for finite \(F_1\subseteq F_2\),
\[
  \left\|\sum_{\gamma\in F_2\setminus F_1}T_\gamma x_\gamma\right\|_p^p
  \leq \sum_{\gamma\in F_2\setminus F_1}\|T_\gamma x_\gamma\|_p^p
  \leq \sum_{\gamma\in F_2\setminus F_1}\|x_\gamma\|^p.
\]
Define \(Tx=\sum_\gamma T_\gamma x_\gamma\).  The same estimate gives
\[
  \|Tx\|_p^p\leq \sum_\gamma \|x_\gamma\|^p=\|x\|^p,
\]
so \(T\in B_{E^\triangle}\), and \(T\circ j_\gamma=T_\gamma\).

Continuity of \(\Phi\) is immediate, since each coordinate \(T\mapsto T\circ
j_\gamma\) is determined by evaluations at vectors of \(E\).  For continuity
of the inverse, fix \(x=(x_\gamma)\in E\).  We must show that
\[
  (T_\gamma)_\gamma \longmapsto \sum_\gamma T_\gamma x_\gamma
\]
is continuous from the product of weak-\(\triangle\) topologies to the norm
topology of \(\Lp\).  Use the equivalent metric
\(d(u,v)=\|u-v\|_p^p\) on \(\Lp\).  Given \(\eta>0\), choose a finite
\(F\subset\Gamma\) such that
\[
  \sum_{\gamma\notin F}\|x_\gamma\|^p<\eta/3.
\]
The finite-coordinate map
\[
  (T_\gamma)_\gamma\longmapsto \sum_{\gamma\in F}T_\gamma x_\gamma
\]
is continuous.  Thus, near a fixed point \((T_\gamma)_\gamma\), one may force
\[
  \left\|\sum_{\gamma\in F}(S_\gamma-T_\gamma)x_\gamma\right\|_p^p<\eta/3.
\]
For such \((S_\gamma)_\gamma\), the \(p\)-triangle inequality gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \left\|\sum_\gamma(S_\gamma-T_\gamma)x_\gamma\right\|_p^p
  &\leq
  \left\|\sum_{\gamma\in F}(S_\gamma-T_\gamma)x_\gamma\right\|_p^p\\
  &\quad+\left\|\sum_{\gamma\notin F}S_\gamma x_\gamma\right\|_p^p
       +\left\|\sum_{\gamma\notin F}T_\gamma x_\gamma\right\|_p^p\\
  &<\eta .
\end{aligned}
\]
This proves continuity of \(\Phi^{-1}\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Separable blocks have separable weak-\(\triangle\) balls]
\label{lem:separable}
If \(E\) is separable, then \(B_{E^\triangle}\) is separable metrizable in the
weak-\(\triangle\) topology.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(D=\{d_n:n\in\mathbb N\}\) be dense in \(E\).  On the unit ball
\(B_{E^\triangle}\), the topology generated by the countable family of
evaluations \(T\mapsto T(d_n)\) agrees with \(\sigma(E^\triangle,E)\).  Indeed,
if \(d_n\to e\), then uniformly over \(T\in B_{E^\triangle}\),
\[
  \|T(d_n)-T(e)\|_p\leq \|d_n-e\|.
\]
Thus every evaluation at \(e\in E\) is continuous for the countably generated
topology.  Consequently \(B_{E^\triangle}\) embeds topologically into the
countable product \((\Lp)^{\mathbb N}\), a separable metrizable space, and so
\(B_{E^\triangle}\) is itself separable metrizable.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:main}
Let \(0<p\leq1\), and let \((E_\gamma)_{\gamma\in\Gamma}\) be any family of
separable \(p\)-natural quasi-Banach spaces.  Put
\[
  E=\left(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma}E_\gamma\right)_p .
\]
Then \(E\) is \(p\)-natural and
\[
  C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)
\]
satisfies the countable chain condition.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First, \(E\) is \(p\)-natural.  For each \(\gamma\), choose an isometric
embedding of \(E_\gamma\) into a \(p\)-convex \(p\)-Banach lattice \(X_\gamma\)
with \(M^{(p)}(X_\gamma)=1\).  Then
\((\bigoplus_\gamma X_\gamma)_p\) is again a \(p\)-convex \(p\)-Banach lattice
with \(p\)-convexity constant \(1\), and the coordinatewise embedding of \(E\)
is isometric.

By Lemma~\ref{lem:separable}, each \(B_{E_\gamma^\triangle}\) is separable
metrizable.  By the standard product theorem used in the source paper
\cite[2.7.10(d)]{engelking}, arbitrary products of separable metrizable spaces
have topological CCC.  Hence
\[
  \prod_{\gamma\in\Gamma}B_{E_\gamma^\triangle}
\]
has topological CCC.  Lemma~\ref{lem:product} identifies this product with
\(B_{E^\triangle}\).  Therefore \(B_{E^\triangle}\) has topological CCC.
Finally Lemma~\ref{lem:lift} implies that
\(C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)\) has lattice CCC.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
The source conjecture holds for all \(p\)-sums of separable \(p\)-natural
blocks.  This simultaneously recovers the source paper's separable case and
its case \(E=\ell_p(\Gamma)\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If \(\Gamma\) is a singleton, Theorem~\ref{thm:main} is the separable case.
If \(E_\gamma=\mathbb R\) for every \(\gamma\), then
\((\bigoplus_\gamma E_\gamma)_p=\ell_p(\Gamma)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[The coordinate-decomposition case]
\label{thm:coordinate-decomposition}
Let \(0<p\leq1\), and let \(E\) be a \(p\)-natural quasi-Banach space with a
bounded separable Boolean coordinate decomposition
\((P_A)_{A\subseteq\Gamma}\).  Then
\[
  C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)
\]
satisfies the countable chain condition.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(K=\sup_{A\subseteq\Gamma}\|P_A\|\).
Assume, toward a contradiction, that \((f_i)_{i\in I}\) is an uncountable
family of nonzero positive pairwise disjoint elements of
\(C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)\).  By Lemma~\ref{lem:cone-cover}, after
passing to an uncountable subfamily there is a fixed open cone
\(V\subset\Lp{}_+\setminus\{0\}\) such that
\[
  U_i=f_i^{-1}(V)
\]
is nonempty for every \(i\).  The sets \(U_i\) are relatively open cones in
\(B_{E^\triangle}\), and they are pairwise disjoint.

We shall contradict this.  Let \(P_A:E\to E\) denote the coordinate projection
onto a finite set \(A\subset\Gamma\).  For each \(T\in B_{E^\triangle}\),
\(TP_A\to T\) pointwise on \(E\) as \(A\) increases through finite subsets of
\(\Gamma\), by hypothesis.  Since the projections are only assumed bounded,
we apply this to \(T/K\): the operators \(K^{-1}TP_A\) belong to
\(B_{E^\triangle}\) and converge pointwise to \(K^{-1}T\).  Since each \(U_i\)
is a cone, this gives finite-block points of \(U_i\cap B_{E^\triangle}\).
Thus we may choose \(S_i\in U_i\) with finite block support \(A_i\), meaning
\(S_i=S_iP_{A_i}\), and \(\|S_i\|<\eta\), where \(\eta>0\) is fixed so small
that
\((4(K\eta)^p)^{1/p}<1\).

For each \(i\), choose a basic weak-\(\triangle\) neighborhood
\[
  N_i=\{T\in B_{E^\triangle}:\|T x_{i,k}-S_i x_{i,k}\|_p<\varepsilon_i,
        \ 1\leq k\leq m_i\}
\]
with \(N_i\subset U_i\).  Enlarge \(A_i\) to a finite set \(H_i\) so that
\[
  \|P_{\Gamma\setminus H_i}x_{i,k}\|<\varepsilon_i/(8\eta K^2)
  \qquad (1\leq k\leq m_i).
\]
Replacing \(A_i\) by \(H_i\), apply the Delta-system lemma to obtain an
uncountable subfamily, still indexed by \(I\), for which the finite sets
\((A_i)\) have a common root \(R\) and pairwise disjoint tails
\(A_i\setminus R\).

Let \(E_R=P_RE\).  This space is separable because \(R\) is finite and each
\(P_{\{\gamma\}}E\) is separable.
The set
\[
  \mathcal R=\{R_0\in\mathcal L(E_R,\Lp):\|R_0\|<2\eta\},
\]
with the topology of pointwise convergence on \(E_R\), is separable metrizable.
For each \(i\), let \(O_i\subset\mathcal R\) be the set of all root operators
such that, if \(S_i\) is replaced by \(R_0\) on the root coordinates and left
unchanged on \(A_i\setminus R\), then the inequalities defining \(N_i\) hold
with margin \(\varepsilon_i/2\).  Each \(O_i\) is a nonempty relatively open
subset of \(\mathcal R\), and \(S_i|_{E_R}\in O_i\).  Therefore two of them,
say \(O_i\) and \(O_j\), intersect.  Pick \(R_0\in O_i\cap O_j\).

Define \(S\in E^\triangle\) blockwise as follows: on the root \(R\), let
\(S=R_0P_R\); on \(A_i\setminus R\), let \(S=S_iP_{A_i\setminus R}\); on
\(A_j\setminus R\), let \(S=S_jP_{A_j\setminus R}\); and on all remaining
coordinates let \(S=0\).  In other words,
\[
  S=R_0P_R+S_iP_{A_i\setminus R}+S_jP_{A_j\setminus R}.
\]
Since the three displayed pieces are supported on disjoint coordinate sets and
all coordinate projections have norm at most \(K\), for \(\|x\|\leq1\) the
\(p\)-triangle inequality gives
\[
  \|Sx\|_p^p
  \leq \|R_0P_Rx\|_p^p
     + \|S_iP_{A_i\setminus R}x\|_p^p
     + \|S_jP_{A_j\setminus R}x\|_p^p
  \leq (2^p+2)(K\eta)^p
  <4(K\eta)^p<1,
\]
so \(S\in B_{E^\triangle}\).

We claim that \(S\in N_i\cap N_j\).  For \(N_i\), the root part is controlled
by the choice \(R_0\in O_i\), and the \(A_i\setminus R\) part agrees with
\(S_i\).  The only new contribution comes from the tail \(A_j\setminus R\),
which is disjoint from \(A_i\) and lies outside \(H_i=A_i\).  Hence
\(P_{A_j\setminus R}=P_{A_j\setminus R}P_{\Gamma\setminus H_i}\), and for
each \(k\),
\[
  \|S_j P_{A_j\setminus R}x_{i,k}\|_p
  \leq \|S_j\|\,K^2\|P_{\Gamma\setminus H_i}x_{i,k}\|
  <\varepsilon_i/8.
\]
Hence the defining inequalities of \(N_i\) hold.  The argument for \(N_j\) is
identical.  Thus \(S\in U_i\cap U_j\), contradicting pairwise disjointness.
This proves CCC.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Let \(0<p\leq1\), let \(p\leq r<\infty\), and let
\((E_\gamma)_{\gamma\in\Gamma}\) be any family of separable \(p\)-natural
quasi-Banach spaces.  If
\[
  E=\left(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma}E_\gamma\right)_r
  \qquad\text{or}\qquad
  E=\left(\bigoplus_{\gamma\in\Gamma}E_\gamma\right)_{c_0},
\]
then \(C_{\ph}^b(B_{E^\triangle},\Lp)\) satisfies the countable chain
condition.  In particular, for every set \(\Gamma\), the source conjecture
holds for \(E=\ell_r(\Gamma)\) whenever \(p\leq r<\infty\), and for
\(E=c_0(\Gamma)\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The \(p\)-naturality of these sums follows from the coordinatewise embedding
of the blocks into the corresponding sums of their witnessing \(p\)-convex
\(p\)-Banach lattices.  In the finite \(r\) case, the \(p\)-triangle
inequality and \(p\)-convexity follow from Minkowski's inequality in
\(\ell_{r/p}\), since \(r/p\geq1\).  The \(c_0\) case is immediate from
taking suprema coordinatewise.  The usual coordinate projections form a
contractive separable Boolean coordinate decomposition, so the conclusion
follows from Theorem~\ref{thm:coordinate-decomposition}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification notes}

The proof does not settle the full conjecture.  Its structural assumption is
that \(E\) has a bounded separable Boolean coordinate decomposition.  The
source-paper obstruction remains for arbitrary subspaces of products:
topological CCC is not hereditary, so a general \(p\)-natural space embedded
in \(\ell_\infty(J,\Lp)\) need not be covered by this argument.

The bounded literature check for this packet consisted of local index searches
for arXiv id \texttt{2512.05273}, the title, \(p\)-natural, free
quasi-Banach lattice, \(C_{\ph}^b\), and countable chain condition, together
with web searches for the exact title and CCC conjecture phrases.  The search
found the source arXiv page but no later answer to the Section 7.2 conjecture.

\section*{Human-review recommendation}

Review Theorems~\ref{thm:main} and~\ref{thm:coordinate-decomposition} as
likely valid partial positive results.  For Theorem~\ref{thm:main}, the main
technical point is Lemma~\ref{lem:product}, especially the continuity of the
inverse homeomorphism for elements of the \(p\)-sum with infinite countable
support.  For Theorem~\ref{thm:coordinate-decomposition}, the main point is
the finite-block density and root-gluing argument after the operators have
been shrunk inside homogeneous cones.
