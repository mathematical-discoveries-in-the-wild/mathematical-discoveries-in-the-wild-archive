\title[Internal probability lifts]{Kalton--Roberts stability answers an internal probability lifting question}

\begin{abstract}
Duanmu, Schrittesser, and Weiss ask whether internal almost probability
measures are close to internal probability measures, and whether the internal
almost probability appearing in their general Loeb-extension theorem can be
replaced by a genuine internal probability.  The answer is yes, using the
Kalton--Roberts stability theorem for nearly additive set functions.  This
settles these auxiliary lifting questions, though not the separate problem of
proving \(\mathcal H\subset \overline{\mathcal F}\) for arbitrary
Loeb-equivalent internal probability spaces.
\end{abstract}

\section{Source questions}

The source paper is H. Duanmu, D. Schrittesser, and W. Weiss, \emph{Loeb
Extension and Loeb Equivalence II}, arXiv:2112.13955.  After proving an
internal-almost-probability version of the general extension theorem, the
paper asks whether every internal almost probability space is close to an
internal probability space, and then asks whether the almost probability in
Theorem 3.5 can be replaced by an internal probability measure.

The displayed definition of internal almost probability in the source omits
the word ``disjoint'' in its finite-additivity condition.  The literal
all-pairs reading is inconsistent, since \(A=B=X\) would give \(1\approx2\).
Throughout this note we use the intended disjoint version:
\[
  A\cap B=\varnothing
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  P(A\cup B)\approx P(A)+P(B).
\]

\section{Kalton--Roberts input}

\begin{theorem}[Kalton--Roberts]\label{thm:KR}
There is a universal constant \(K\) with the following property.  Let
\(\cA\) be an algebra of subsets of a set \(X\), and let
\(f:\cA\to\mathbb R\) satisfy
\[
  |f(A\cup B)-f(A)-f(B)|\leq \eps
\]
whenever \(A,B\in\cA\) are disjoint.  Then there is a finitely additive signed
measure \(m:\cA\to\mathbb R\) such that
\[
  \sup_{A\in\cA}|f(A)-m(A)|\leq K\eps.
\]
\end{theorem}

This is Theorem 4.1 of Kalton and Roberts \cite{KR}.  The following positive
version is the form needed for Loeb spaces.

\begin{corollary}[Positive normalized form]\label{cor:positive}
There is a universal constant \(C\) such that, whenever \(f:\cA\to[0,1]\)
satisfies \(f(\varnothing)=0\), \(f(X)=1\), and is \(\eps\)-additive on
disjoint pairs, there is a finitely additive probability measure
\(p:\cA\to[0,1]\) such that
\[
  \sup_{A\in\cA}|f(A)-p(A)|\leq C\eps.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem \ref{thm:KR} to obtain a signed charge \(m\) with
\(\|m-f\|_\infty\leq K\eps\).  Then \(m(A)\geq -K\eps\) for every \(A\), and
\(|m(X)-1|\leq K\eps\).  By the Jordan decomposition of a bounded finitely
additive signed measure, \(m=m^+-m^-\), and
\[
  m^-(X)=\sup_{A\in\cA}(-m(A))\leq K\eps.
\]
Hence \(s:=m^+(X)=m(X)+m^-(X)\) satisfies \(s=1+O(\eps)\), and for small enough
\(\eps\) the normalized charge \(p=m^+/s\) is a finitely additive probability.
Moreover
\[
  |p(A)-m(A)|\leq |s-1|+m^-(X)\leq C_1\eps
\]
for a universal \(C_1\), uniformly in \(A\).  Combining this with
\(\|m-f\|_\infty\leq K\eps\) gives the result.  The remaining large-\(\eps\)
case is absorbed by increasing \(C\).
\end{proof}

\section{Nonstandard consequence}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:internal-close}
Let \((X,\cA,P)\) be an internal almost probability space, with approximate
additivity understood on disjoint pairs.  Then there is an internal finitely
additive probability measure \(Q:\cA\to{}^*[0,1]\) such that
\[
  Q(A)\approx P(A)\qquad(A\in\cA).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The assertion that \(P\) is almost additive says that for every standard
\(\eps>0\),
\[
  |P(A\cup B)-P(A)-P(B)|\leq \eps
\]
for all disjoint \(A,B\in\cA\).  By underspill, there is a positive
infinitesimal \(\delta\) such that this inequality holds with \(\delta\) in
place of every standard \(\eps\).  Transfer Corollary \ref{cor:positive} to
the internal algebra \(\cA\) and the internal set function \(P\).  We obtain an
internal finitely additive probability measure \(Q\) on \(\cA\) satisfying
\[
  \sup_{A\in\cA}|Q(A)-P(A)|\leq C\delta.
\]
Since \(C\delta\) is infinitesimal, \(Q(A)\approx P(A)\) for every
\(A\in\cA\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:internalapprox}
Let \((\Omega,\cF,\mu)\) be an internal probability space, let \(\cG\) be an
internal algebra on \(\Omega\), and let \(\cH\) be the internal algebra
generated by \(\cF\cup\cG\).  If
\[
  \cH\subset \Loeb{\cF},
\]
then there is an internal probability measure \(Q\) on \(\cH\) such that
\[
  (\Omega,\cH,Q)
\]
is Loeb equivalent to \((\Omega,\cF,\mu)\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Theorem 3.5 of the source paper constructs an internal almost probability
measure \(P\) on \(\cH\) whose Loeb extension is equivalent to
\((\Omega,\cF,\mu)\).  Apply Theorem \ref{thm:internal-close} to obtain an
internal probability \(Q\) on \(\cH\) with \(Q(H)\approx P(H)\) for all
\(H\in\cH\).  The Loeb constructions from \(P\) and \(Q\) therefore agree on
\(\cH\), and hence have the same completed Loeb measure space.  Thus
\((\Omega,\cH,Q)\) is Loeb equivalent to \((\Omega,\cF,\mu)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Corollary \ref{cor:internalapprox} answers Question \texttt{internalapprox}
from the source paper.  It does not settle the full arbitrary-algebra version
of Keisler--Sun Question 3, because that still requires proving the hypothesis
\(\cH\subset\Loeb{\cF}\) from Loeb equivalence of \((\Omega,\cF,\mu)\) and
\((\Omega,\cG,\nu)\).
\end{remark}
