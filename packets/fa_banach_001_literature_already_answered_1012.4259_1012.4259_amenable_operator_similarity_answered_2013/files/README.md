# Literature status: the amenable operator algebra similarity question

Status: `literature_already_answered`

## Source question

Yemon Choi, *On commutative, operator amenable subalgebras of finite von
Neumann algebras*, arXiv:1012.4259v3 (2011).

The abstract and Introduction on PDF page 1 ask whether every closed amenable
operator algebra in \(B(H)\) is similar to an amenable \(C^*\)-algebra and note
that the question was open even for singly-generated algebras.

## Later answers

The unrestricted question was answered negatively by Yemon Choi, Ilijas Farah,
and Narutaka Ozawa, *A nonseparable amenable operator algebra which is not
isomorphic to a C*-algebra*, arXiv:1309.2145. Their Theorem 1 on PDF page 2
constructs a unital amenable operator algebra that is not isomorphic to any
\(C^*\)-algebra. Since similarity would give such an isomorphism, their example
cannot be similar to a \(C^*\)-algebra.

The source's abelian and singly-generated cases were answered positively by
Laurent W. Marcoux and Alexey I. Popov, *Abelian, amenable operator algebras are
similar to C*-algebras*, arXiv:1311.2982. Theorem 2.10 and Corollary 2.11 on
PDF page 11 show that every closed abelian amenable operator algebra is similar
to a \(C^*\)-algebra and characterize the singly-generated case.

Marcoux and Popov explicitly cite both Choi's source paper and the
Choi--Farah--Ozawa counterexample, stating that the unrestricted question had
been resolved negatively. This is therefore an exact literature answer, not an
agent-inferred reformulation.

## Scope

The counterexample is nonseparable and nonabelian. Both 2013 supporting papers
state that the separable nonabelian version remains open. A bounded arXiv search
through July 2026 found no later resolution, but this packet does not claim an
exhaustive current-status survey of that narrower variant.
