# Strong Exposure Gives Bidual Continuity

Status: `partial_result_likely_valid`

Source paper: S. Daptari, V. Montesinos, and T. S. S. R. K. Rao, "A study of weak$^*$-weak points of continuity in the unit ball of dual spaces", arXiv:2506.08458.

Targeted source signal: Section 5.2 asks for a geometric condition on a point `x in S(X)`, stated in terms of `X` alone, which ensures that the canonical image of `x` is a point of continuity of
`id:(B(X**),w*) -> (B(X**),w)`.

Claim packaged here: if `x` is a strongly exposed point of `B_X`, then `x` is in fact a point of weak-star-to-norm continuity of `B_{X**}`. Therefore it is a point of weak-star-to-weak continuity.

Scope: this supplies one concrete sufficient condition. It does not characterize all such points and does not address the paper's later dense `G_delta` stability questions.

Novelty note: cheap run indexes had no prior packet for arXiv:2506.08458 or this exact route. Local source search found the strong-exposure-to-bidual idea alluded to as a standard fact in an unrelated TeX comment, so the mathematical novelty confidence is low; the value of this packet is as a direct, self-contained identification of a condition for the source question.
