\begin{abstract}
Davidson and Kennedy ask whether, for a compact noncommutative convex set \(K\),
the convex nc functions in \(\mathrm{C}(K)\) always span a dense subset of
\(\mathrm{C}(K)\).  We prove that the answer is yes.  The proof is a
Hahn--Banach consequence of Proposition 7.2.8 in the source paper: equality of
unital completely positive maps on all matrix-valued convex nc functions forces
equality of the maps.
\end{abstract}

\section{Proof intuition}

The source proposition says that convex nc functions separate u.c.p. maps,
provided one tests all matrix-valued convex nc functions.  To turn that into
norm density, take a functional that annihilates the scalar convex functions.
Its Jordan decomposition gives two states.  Since vector compressions of
matrix-valued convex functions are scalar convex functions, these two states
also agree on every matrix-valued convex function.  The source proposition then
forces the states, and hence the original functional, to be equal to zero.
Hahn--Banach finishes the density argument.

\section{A compression lemma}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:compression}
Let \(f\in \Mn(\CK)\) be a self-adjoint convex nc function and let
\(\xi\in \mathbb{C}^n\).  Define
\[
g_\xi(x)=(\xi^*\otimes 1)f(x)(\xi\otimes 1),\qquad x\in K.
\]
Then \(g_\xi\in \CK\) is a scalar-valued self-adjoint convex nc function.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Continuity, grading, direct sums, and unitary equivariance are inherited by
compression.  If \(x=\sum_i \alpha_i^*x_i\alpha_i\) is an nc convex
combination, convexity of \(f\) gives
\[
f(x)\le \sum_i (1_n\otimes\alpha_i^*)f(x_i)(1_n\otimes\alpha_i).
\]
Compressing by \(\xi\otimes 1\) gives
\[
g_\xi(x)\le \sum_i \alpha_i^*g_\xi(x_i)\alpha_i,
\]
which is exactly convexity for the scalar nc function \(g_\xi\).
\end{proof}

\section{Dense span theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:dense}
Let \(K\) be a compact nc convex set.  The real linear span of the
self-adjoint continuous convex nc functions in \(\CK\) is norm dense in
\(\CK_{\sa}\).  Consequently, the complex linear span of the continuous convex
nc functions is norm dense in \(\CK\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(E\) be the real linear span of the scalar-valued self-adjoint continuous
convex nc functions in \(\CK\).  Suppose that \(\overline{E}\ne \CK_{\sa}\).
By the real Hahn--Banach theorem, there is a non-zero bounded real linear
functional \(\phi\) on \(\CK_{\sa}\) such that \(\phi|_E=0\).

Since the constant function \(1\) is affine, hence convex, we have
\(\phi(1)=0\).  Let
\[
\phi=\phi_+-\phi_-
\]
be the Jordan decomposition of the bounded self-adjoint functional \(\phi\) on
the C*-algebra \(\CK\).  The equality \(\phi(1)=0\) gives
\(\phi_+(1)=\phi_-(1)\).  If this common value is zero, then
\(\phi=0\), a contradiction.  Otherwise set
\[
\mu=\phi_+/\phi_+(1),\qquad \nu=\phi_-/\phi_-(1).
\]
These are states on \(\CK\).  Because \(\phi\) vanishes on every scalar convex
nc function, \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) agree on every scalar convex nc function.

We now show that \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) agree on every matrix-valued convex nc
function.  Let \(f\in \Mn(\CK)\) be self-adjoint and convex.  For each
\(\xi\in\mathbb{C}^n\), Lemma~\ref{lem:compression} gives a scalar convex nc
function \(g_\xi\).  Hence
\[
\xi^*(\mu_n(f)-\nu_n(f))\xi
=\mu(g_\xi)-\nu(g_\xi)=0 .
\]
Since \(\mu_n(f)-\nu_n(f)\) is self-adjoint and all of its quadratic forms
vanish, \(\mu_n(f)=\nu_n(f)\).

Thus \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) satisfy the hypothesis of Proposition 7.2.8 of
Davidson--Kennedy: they agree on every \(n<\infty\) and every convex nc
function in \(\Mn(\CK)\).  Therefore \(\mu=\nu\).  It follows that
\(\phi=0\), contradicting the choice of \(\phi\).  Hence
\(\overline{E}=\CK_{\sa}\).

Every element of \(\CK\) is \(a+ib\) with \(a,b\in\CK_{\sa}\), so the complex
linear span of the continuous convex nc functions is dense in \(\CK\).
\end{proof}

\section{Verification and novelty notes}

The argument uses only standard Hahn--Banach separation, the Jordan
decomposition of bounded self-adjoint functionals on a C*-algebra, and
Proposition 7.2.8 of the source paper.  No computational verification is
needed.

Local run indexes had no prior packet for arXiv:1905.08436.  A bounded search
on 2026-07-18 for the exact dense-span formulation found the source paper and
the later survey arXiv:2412.09455, but no explicit statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:dense}.  The result should be understood as a short corollary
of the source paper's own separation proposition.

\section{Limitations}

This proves density of the linear span.  It does not prove the stronger exact
decomposition statement that every element of \(\CK_{\sa}\) is literally the
difference of two continuous convex nc functions.

\section{Human review recommendation}

Review as a likely valid full solution.  The central point to verify is the
compression step: scalar vector compressions of matrix-valued convex nc
functions remain scalar convex nc functions, allowing Proposition 7.2.8 to be
applied to the normalized positive and negative parts of a separating
functional.
