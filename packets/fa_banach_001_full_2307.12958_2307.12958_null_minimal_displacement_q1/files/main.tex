\section*{Source Paper}

This packet concerns the paper
\[
\textit{Retraction methods and fixed point free maps with null minimal
displacements on unit balls}
\]
by Cleon S. Barroso and Valdir Ferreira, arXiv:2307.12958.

The arXiv source paper's introduction recalls a theorem of Lin--Sternfeld:
if \(K\) is a noncompact bounded closed convex subset of a Banach space,
then there is a Lipschitz map \(T:K\to K\) with positive minimal
displacement.  The paper then asks the following two questions.

\section*{Extracted Question}

We address the first displayed question in the crop:

\begin{quote}
\((Q1)\) Is there a Lipschitz map \(T:K\to K\) with
\(\mathrm d(T,K)=0\) and \(\Fix(T)=\varnothing\)?
\end{quote}

Here \(\mathrm d(T,K)=\inf_{x\in K}\|x-Tx\|\).  The paper proves several
important special results and later says that its first main theorem
``particularly'' solves \((Q1)\) in every Banach space.  The theorem below
answers the literal universal formulation of \((Q1)\): every noncompact
bounded closed convex \(K\) already has such a Lipschitz self-map.

The quantifier distinction is the key point.  The source question as written
asks whether every eligible noncompact \(K\) admits such a self-map.  The
source paper's Theorem 4.1 proves instead that, for every Banach space \(X\),
there exists at least one suitable \(K\in\mathcal B(B_X)\) with such maps;
under the additional hypothesis that \(X\) contains a complemented basic
sequence, this \(K\) can be chosen to be \(B_X\).

The journal version shown in the follow-up screenshot phrases \((Q1)\)
slightly differently: for every infinite-dimensional Banach space \(X\) and
every \(\varepsilon>0\), does there exist
\(K\in\mathcal B(B_X)\) and a \((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz mapping
\(T:K\to K\) such that \(\mathrm d(T,K)=0\) and
\(\Fix(T)=\varnothing\)?  The source paper's Theorem 4.1 answers that
existential journal formulation.  The result below supersedes both formulations:
it gives the \((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz conclusion for every preassigned
noncompact bounded closed convex \(K\).

\section*{Candidate Answer}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(X\) be a real Banach space and let \(K\subset X\) be a noncompact
bounded closed convex set.  For every \(\varepsilon>0\), there exists a
\((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz map \(G_\varepsilon:K\to K\) such that
\[
\Fix(G_\varepsilon)=\varnothing
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\mathrm d(G_\varepsilon,K)=0 .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the Lin--Sternfeld theorem cited in the source paper, there is a
Lipschitz map \(F:K\to K\) with positive minimal displacement:
\[
\delta:=\inf_{x\in K}\|F(x)-x\|>0.
\]
In particular \(F\) has no fixed point.  Let \(M\) be a Lipschitz constant
for \(F\), and put \(D=\diam(K)<\infty\).

Since \(K\) is closed in the Banach space \(X\), it is complete.  Since it
is not compact, it is not totally bounded.  Hence there are \(\eta>0\) and
a sequence \((x_n)_{n\ge 1}\subset K\) such that
\[
\|x_n-x_m\|\ge \eta \qquad (n\ne m).
\]
Define \(a(x_n)=1/n\) on the separated set \(A=\{x_n:n\ge 1\}\).  The map
\(a:A\to(0,1]\) is \(1/\eta\)-Lipschitz, because for \(m\ne n\),
\[
|a(x_n)-a(x_m)|\le 1\le \frac{1}{\eta}\|x_n-x_m\|.
\]
Use the McShane extension formula
\[
u(x)=\inf_{n\ge 1}\left(\frac{1}{n}+\frac{1}{\eta}\|x-x_n\|\right),
\qquad x\in K .
\]
Then \(u\) is \(1/\eta\)-Lipschitz and \(u(x_n)=1/n\).
Moreover \(u(x)>0\) for every fixed \(x\in K\): at most one point
\(x_n\) can satisfy \(\|x-x_n\|<\eta/2\), while all remaining terms in the
infimum are at least \(1/2\), and the possible exceptional term is still
strictly positive.

Set
\[
\lambda(x)=\min\{1,u(x)\}.
\]
Then \(\lambda:K\to(0,1]\) is Lipschitz, \(\inf_K\lambda=0\), and
\(\lambda(x_n)=1/n\).

Let \(H=F-I\).  Then \(H\) is \((M+1)\)-Lipschitz and
\(\|H(y)\|\le D\) for every \(y\in K\).  Let \(L_\lambda\) be a Lipschitz
constant for \(\lambda\), and set
\[
C=(M+1)+D L_\lambda .
\]
Choose \(c\in(0,1]\) such that \(cC\le \varepsilon\).  Now define
\[
G_\varepsilon(x)=x+c\lambda(x)(F(x)-x)
     =(1-c\lambda(x))x+c\lambda(x)F(x),\qquad x\in K .
\]
Since \(0<c\lambda(x)\le 1\), the set \(K\) is convex and
\(F(x),x\in K\), so \(G_\varepsilon(x)\in K\).

The map \(G_\varepsilon\) is \((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz.  Indeed,
\[
\begin{aligned}
\|G_\varepsilon(x)-G_\varepsilon(y)\|
&\le \|x-y\|+c\|\lambda(x)H(x)-\lambda(y)H(y)\|\\
&\le \|x-y\|+c\|H(x)-H(y)\|
     +c|\lambda(x)-\lambda(y)|\,\|H(y)\|\\
&\le \bigl(1+c((M+1)+D L_\lambda)\bigr)\|x-y\|\\
&\le (1+\varepsilon)\|x-y\|.
\end{aligned}
\]

If \(G_\varepsilon(x)=x\), then \(c\lambda(x)(F(x)-x)=0\).  Since
\(c>0\) and \(\lambda(x)>0\), this forces \(F(x)=x\), contradicting
\(\delta>0\).  Thus \(G_\varepsilon\) has no fixed point.

Finally,
\[
\|G_\varepsilon(x_n)-x_n\|=c\lambda(x_n)\|F(x_n)-x_n\|
\le \frac{cD}{n}\to 0 .
\]
Therefore \(\mathrm d(G_\varepsilon,K)=0\), while
\(\Fix(G_\varepsilon)=\varnothing\).  This proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification}

No numerical verification is needed.  The proof uses only the cited
Lin--Sternfeld positive-displacement theorem, a separated sequence in a
non-totally-bounded complete metric space, the McShane Lipschitz extension
formula, and convexity of \(K\).

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This answers only \((Q1)\).  It does not answer \((Q2)\), since the
variable damping construction above gives a \((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz
map but does not control the Lipschitz constants of the iterates
\(G_\varepsilon^n\).  The naive estimate still grows like
\((1+\varepsilon)^n\).

The source paper itself constructs null-minimal-displacement maps for suitable
sets and for several important unit-ball situations.  The present argument
targets the literal phrase ``for any such \(K\)'' in the introductory arXiv
formulation of \((Q1)\), and it also strengthens the journal formulation by
allowing \(K\) to be any preassigned noncompact bounded closed convex set.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a likely valid full answer to \((Q1)\).  The most important
checks are:
\begin{itemize}
\item the scalar \(\lambda\) is everywhere positive while having infimum
zero;
\item the scaled variable convex combination
\(G_\varepsilon=(1-c\lambda)I+c\lambda F\) is
\((1+\varepsilon)\)-Lipschitz;
\item the fixed-point-free property is preserved because \(\lambda>0\)
pointwise.
\end{itemize}
