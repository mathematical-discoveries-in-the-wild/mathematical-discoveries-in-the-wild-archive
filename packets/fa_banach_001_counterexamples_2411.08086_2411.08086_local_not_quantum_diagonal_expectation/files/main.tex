\textbf{Claimed status: counterexample, likely valid.}
The inclusion in equation (28) fails for an infinite-dimensional separable
Hilbert space, already for the discrete maximal abelian von Neumann algebra.

\section*{The source question}

For a von Neumann algebra $\D\subseteq\B(H)$, the source writes
$\mathfrak D_{\mathrm{loc},\D}(H)$ for the absolutely dilatable
$\D'$-bimodule maps admitting an abelian ancilla, and
$\mathfrak D_{\mathrm q,\D}(H)$ for those admitting a finite-dimensional
ancilla.  It proves the inclusion in finite dimensions and asks whether
\begin{equation}\label{eq:question}
  \mathfrak D_{\mathrm{loc},\D}(H)
  \subseteq \mathfrak D_{\mathrm q,\D}(H)
\end{equation}
also holds when $H$ is infinite-dimensional.

\section*{Proof intuition}

Use the diagonal conditional expectation on $\B(\ell^2)$.  It is a continuous
random mixture of diagonal unitary conjugations: average conjugation by
$\operatorname{diag}(1,z,z^2,\ldots)$ over the circle.  This gives an abelian
ancilla and hence local factorisability.

The same map has Schur symbol $[\delta_{ij}]$.  A finite-dimensional quantum
ancilla would represent this symbol as
$\delta_{ij}=\tau(v_i^*v_j)$ for unitaries $v_i$ in a single
finite-dimensional tracial algebra.  Thus the $v_i$ would be infinitely many
orthonormal vectors in its finite-dimensional $L^2$-space.  The contradiction
is the finite Gram-rank obstruction in its simplest form.

\section*{Counterexample theorem}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $H=\ell^2(\N)$ and let $\D=\ell^\infty(\N)\subseteq\B(H)$ be the
diagonal masa.  The normal diagonal conditional expectation
\[
  \Phi:\B(H)\longrightarrow\D,
  \qquad
  \Phi([x_{ij}])=\operatorname{diag}(x_{00},x_{11},\ldots),
\]
satisfies
\[
  \Phi\in\mathfrak D_{\mathrm{loc},\D}(H)
  \setminus\mathfrak D_{\mathrm q,\D}(H).
\]
Consequently, the infinite-dimensional inclusion \eqref{eq:question} is
false.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

Let $(e_n)_{n\in\N}$ be the standard orthonormal basis of $H$, let
$p_n=e_ne_n^*$, and let $z:\T\to\mathbb C$ be the coordinate function.
Equip $L^\infty(\T)$ with normalized Haar integration $\tau(f)=\int_\T f$.
The strong diagonal sum
\[
  U=\sum_{n=0}^\infty p_n\otimes z^n
  \in \D\,\overline\otimes\,L^\infty(\T)
\]
is unitary.  Define
\[
  \Psi(x)=(\id\otimes\tau)\bigl(U^*(x\otimes1)U\bigr),
  \qquad x\in\B(H).
\]
For the matrix unit $e_{ij}=e_ie_j^*$,
\[
 U^*(e_{ij}\otimes1)U=e_{ij}\otimes z^{j-i},
 \qquad
 \Psi(e_{ij})=\left(\int_\T z^{j-i}\right)e_{ij}=\delta_{ij}e_{ij}.
\]
Both $\Psi$ and the diagonal expectation $\Phi$ are normal, so equality on
the matrix units gives $\Psi=\Phi$.  The displayed representation is an exact
factorisation through the abelian tracial ancilla $L^\infty(\T)$.  Hence
$\Phi\in\mathfrak D_{\mathrm{loc},\D}(H)$.

Suppose, toward a contradiction, that
$\Phi\in\mathfrak D_{\mathrm q,\D}(H)$.  The discrete masa is the multiplication
algebra of the standard measure space $\N$.  The type-preserving exactness
assertion in the proof of Corollary 3.5 of the source states that, for such a
masa,
\[
 \mathfrak D_{\mathrm t,\D}(H)=
 \mathfrak D^{\mathrm{ex}}_{\mathrm t,\D}(H),
 \qquad \mathrm t\in\{\mathrm{loc},\mathrm q,\mathrm{qa},\mathrm{qc}\}.
\]
Therefore there are a finite-dimensional tracial von Neumann algebra
$(\M,\tau_{\M})$ and a unitary
$V\in\D\,\overline\otimes\,\M$ such that
\[
  \Phi(x)=(\id\otimes\tau_{\M})
  \bigl(V^*(x\otimes1)V\bigr).
\]
Identifying $\D\,\overline\otimes\,\M$ with
$\ell^\infty(\N;\M)$, write
\[
  V=\sum_{n=0}^\infty p_n\otimes v_n,
  \qquad v_n\in\mathcal U(\M).
\]
Evaluation on $e_{ij}$ now gives
\[
  \delta_{ij}e_{ij}=\Phi(e_{ij})
  =\tau_{\M}(v_i^*v_j)e_{ij},
  \qquad i,j\in\N.
\]
Thus $(v_n)_{n\in\N}$ is an infinite orthonormal family in the Hilbert space
$L^2(\M,\tau_{\M})$.  This is impossible because $\M$, and hence its
$L^2$-space, is finite-dimensional.  Therefore
$\Phi\notin\mathfrak D_{\mathrm q,\D}(H)$, completing the proof. \qed

\section*{Verification notes}

The proof has no numerical or computer-assisted component.  Its four checks
are exact:
\begin{itemize}
\item the diagonal field $(z^n)_{n\geq0}$ defines a unitary in the stated
  von Neumann tensor product;
\item Haar orthogonality gives the Schur symbol $\delta_{ij}$;
\item the source's proof of Corollary 3.5 explicitly includes
  $\mathrm t=\mathrm q$ in the equality between factorisable and exactly
  factorisable maps for a standard masa;
\item the unitary coefficients of a finite-dimensional exact ancilla live in
  a finite-dimensional tracial $L^2$-space.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Scope, novelty check, and review recommendation}

The example disproves the universal infinite-dimensional inclusion asked in
equation (28), already for $H=\ell^2$ and an atomic masa.  It does not classify
which other pairs $(H,\D)$ may still satisfy the inclusion.

The run indexes were searched for arXiv:2411.08086, the paper title, the exact
inclusion, local and quantum factorisability, Schur multipliers, and diagonal
conditional expectations.  Bounded web searches on July 21, 2026 used the
exact question and notation, the source title, and diagonal-expectation and
finite-ancilla variants.  The arXiv paper and its 2025 IMRN publication were
found; the published version still states the question as unknown.  No later
answer, correction, or matching counterexample was found.  Novelty confidence
is moderate because the obstruction is elementary and could be known
informally.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Human review recommendation.} Review as a likely valid full
counterexample.  The main point to verify against the source's definitions is
the type-preserving exactness sentence in the proof of Corollary 3.5.  Once
that sentence is accepted in its stated scope, the orthogonality contradiction
is immediate.
