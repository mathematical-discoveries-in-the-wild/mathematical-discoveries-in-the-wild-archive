\section*{Status}

This is a candidate full solution, subject to human review, for the open
converse stated on page 6 of Rueda--Sanchez-Perez
\cite{rueda_sanchez_perez}.  It addresses only that Lipschitz-operator-ideal

\section*{Source Question}

The source discusses the set
\[
  \mathcal O_0(X,\AEs(X))=\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))
\]
and states Proposition 4, quoted from Achour--Rueda--Sanchez-Perez--Yahi
\cite{achour_rueda_sanchez_yahi}: if a pointed metric space \(X\) has the
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz approximation property with respect to
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\), then for every pointed metric space \(Z\)
and every \(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact mapping
\(\phi:Z\to X\), the map \(\delta_X\circ\phi\) can be approximated uniformly
on \(B_Z\) by finite-rank Lipschitz maps in
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(Z,\AEs(X))\).  Immediately before this proposition the
source says that the converse is still open.

\section*{Definitions and Formulation}

Let \(X\) be a pointed metric space with base point \(0\), let \(\AEs(X)\) be
its Arens--Eells, equivalently Lipschitz-free, space, and let
\(\delta_X:X\to \AEs(X)\) be the Dirac map.  A map belongs to
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,E)\) if it is base-point preserving, Lipschitz, and has
finite-dimensional linear span of its range.

For an operator ideal \(\mathcal I\), a subset \(K\subset X\) is relatively
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact when \(\delta_X(K)\) is relatively
\(\mathcal I\)-compact in \(\AEs(X)\).  The
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz approximation property with respect to
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\) means that \(\delta_X\) lies in the topology
of uniform convergence on all relatively \(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact
subsets of \(X\), after closure of \(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\).

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The converse has only one missing ingredient.  To test the
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz approximation property, one fixes a relatively
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact set \(K\subset X\) and must approximate
\(\delta_X\) on \(K\) by a finite-rank Lipschitz map defined on all of \(X\).
The assumed converse condition can be applied to the inclusion of
\(K\cup\{0\}\) into \(X\), after rescaling the metric so that \(K\) is inside
the unit ball.  This gives the right finite-rank approximation on the small
metric space.  Because the range is finite-dimensional, extend the coordinate
functions one at a time by McShane's scalar extension theorem and then
reassemble them.  The extension remains finite rank and agrees with the local
approximant on \(K\).

\begin{lemma}[Finite-rank Lipschitz extension]
\label{lem:finite-rank-extension}
Let \(A\) be a pointed subset of a metric space \(X\), with the inherited
metric and the same base point.  If \(f\in \Lip_{0\mathcal F}(A,E)\), then
there is \(F\in \Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,E)\) such that \(F|_A=f\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(Y=\spanop f(A)\), which is finite-dimensional.  Choose a basis
\(y_1,\ldots,y_m\) of \(Y\) and coordinate functionals
\(\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_m\in Y^*\).  Each scalar function
\(\lambda_j\circ f\) is Lipschitz on \(A\) and vanishes at the base point.
By the McShane extension theorem, in the real case, it extends to a scalar
Lipschitz function \(\widetilde\lambda_j:X\to\mathbb R\) vanishing at the
base point.  In the complex case, apply the same real theorem to real and
imaginary parts.  Then
\[
  F(x)=\sum_{j=1}^m \widetilde\lambda_j(x)y_j
\]
is a base-point preserving Lipschitz map from \(X\) into the finite-dimensional
space \(Y\), and \(F|_A=f\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:converse}
Let \(X\) be a pointed metric space and \(\mathcal I\) an operator ideal.
Assume that for every pointed metric space \(Z\) and every
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact map \(\phi:Z\to X\), the map
\(\delta_X\circ\phi\) can be approximated uniformly on \(B_Z\) by maps in
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(Z,\AEs(X))\).  Then \(X\) has the
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz approximation property with respect to
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(K\subset X\) be relatively \(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact and let
\(\varepsilon>0\).  We must find
\(G\in \Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\) such that
\[
  \sup_{x\in K}\|\delta_X(x)-G(x)\|_{\AEs(X)}<\varepsilon .
\]
The set \(K\) is bounded, since \(\delta_X(K)\) is relatively
\(\mathcal I\)-compact in the Banach space \(\AEs(X)\).  Put
\[
  R=\max\{1,\sup_{x\in K} d_X(x,0)\}
\]
and let \(Z=K\cup\{0\}\), pointed at \(0\), with metric
\[
  d_Z(z,z')=R^{-1}d_X(z,z').
\]
Then \(Z=B_Z\).  Let \(\phi:Z\to X\) be the inclusion.  It is Lipschitz, and
\[
  \delta_X(\phi(B_Z))=\delta_X(K\cup\{0\})
\]
is relatively \(\mathcal I\)-compact in \(\AEs(X)\), so \(\phi\) is
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact.

By the hypothesis, there exists
\(f\in \Lip_{0\mathcal F}(Z,\AEs(X))\) such that
\[
  \sup_{z\in B_Z}\|\delta_X(\phi(z))-f(z)\|_{\AEs(X)}<\varepsilon .
\]
In particular this estimate holds for every \(z\in K\).  Since \(d_Z\) is a
constant multiple of the inherited metric from \(X\), \(f\) is also Lipschitz
on \(Z\) with respect to the inherited metric from \(X\).  Applying
Lemma~\ref{lem:finite-rank-extension} with target \(E=\AEs(X)\), extend \(f\)
to a map \(G\in\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\).  For \(x\in K\), \(G(x)=f(x)\),
and therefore
\[
  \sup_{x\in K}\|\delta_X(x)-G(x)\|_{\AEs(X)}<\varepsilon .
\]
This is exactly the closure condition defining the
\(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz approximation property with respect to
\(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Corollary}

Together with Proposition 4 of Rueda--Sanchez-Perez, equivalently Proposition
4.9 of Achour--Rueda--Sanchez-Perez--Yahi, Theorem~\ref{thm:converse} gives
the equivalence:
\[
\begin{aligned}
X \text{ has the } \mathcal I\text{-Lipschitz approximation property}
&\text{ with respect to } \Lip_{0\mathcal F}(X,\AEs(X))\\
\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\delta_X\circ\phi
&\text{ is uniformly approximable on } B_Z \\
&\text{ by finite-rank Lipschitz maps for every }\\
&\mathcal I\text{-Lipschitz compact }\phi:Z\to X .
\end{aligned}
\]

\section*{Verification Notes}

No computational verification is needed.  The proof uses only:
\begin{itemize}
\item the source definitions of relatively \(\mathcal I\)-Lipschitz compact
sets and the Lipschitz-\(\tau_{\mathcal I}\) closure;
\item the observation that relatively \(\mathcal I\)-compact subsets of a
Banach space are bounded;
\item scalar McShane extension, applied coordinatewise to a finite-dimensional
range.
\end{itemize}

The main reviewer check is whether the source's phrase ``can be approximated
by finite rank operators of \(\Lip_{0\mathcal F}\) uniformly on \(B_Z\)''
uses the standard finite-dimensional-range meaning.  That is the meaning used
in the cited Lipschitz-operator-ideal paper and in the source's preceding
Proposition 3.  Under that reading, the extension lemma is immediate.
