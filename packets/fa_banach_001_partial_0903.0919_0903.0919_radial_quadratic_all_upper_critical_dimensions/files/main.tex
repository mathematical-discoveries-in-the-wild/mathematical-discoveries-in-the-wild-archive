\section{Source problem}

Aboud and Robert study the nonlinear pencil
\[
  L(\lambda)=-\Delta+(P(x)-\lambda)^2
\]
for a positive elliptic polynomial $P$ on $\R^d$.  Their semiclassical trace
formula has coefficients $C_{2j}^{(d)}(f)$.  In odd dimensions they prove
$C_{2j}^{(d)}(f)=0$ when $d\geq4j+1$, and conjecture that the next two
coefficients do not vanish: for every $j\geq1$, there should be an admissible
$f$ for which
\[
 C_{2j}^{(4j-1)}(f)\ne0,
 \qquad C_{2j}^{(4j-3)}(f)\ne0.
\]
This is Conjecture (4.25) of \cite{AR}.

\section{Result}
\medskip

Put
\[
 s_j=[t^{2j}]\frac{t}{\sinh t}
   =\frac{2(1-2^{2j-1})B_{2j}}{(2j)!}.
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $j\geq1$, $d=4j-1$, and $P(x)=|x|^2$.  For every source-admissible test
function $f$ for which the displayed moment converges,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:main}
 C_{2j}^{(4j-1)}(f)=A_j
     \int_0^\infty a^{3j-5/2}f(a)\dd a,
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Aj}
 A_j=(-1)^j\frac{6(2j-1)\sqrt\pi}
 {2^{2j+1}\Gamma(2j-\tfrac12)}s_j>0.
\end{equation}
Consequently $C_{2j}^{(4j-1)}((z+1)^{-(6j-1)})>0$ for every $j\geq1$.
The pencil
\[
 -\Delta+(|x|^2-\lambda)^2
\]
therefore has infinitely many nonlinear eigenvalues in every dimension
$d=4j-1$.  For every $\varepsilon>0$, its counting function satisfies
\[
 N(R)\geq c_\varepsilon R^{3j-3/2-\varepsilon}
\]
for all sufficiently large $R$.
\end{theorem}

The sign assertion follows because
$\operatorname{sgn}B_{2j}=(-1)^{j+1}$ and $1-2^{2j-1}<0$, so
$(-1)^js_j>0$.  Notice that $6j-1>3(4j-1)/2$, exactly the source's decay
condition for the quadratic case.

\section{Proof intuition}
\medskip

Rotational symmetry reduces the Weyl-parametrix symbols to four invariants:
$a=|x|^2$, $b=|\xi|^2$, $c=x\cdot\xi$, and $w=a-z$.  The recurrence closes at
every order because the scalar symbol has no derivatives above order four.

The decisive observation is more rigid.  Align the first coordinate with
$x$ and write $u=\xi\cdot x/|x|$.  In the two highest powers of $a$, one
recurrence step is simply
\[
 a(\partial_w^2+\partial_u^2).
\]
Thus the all-order problem becomes a two-dimensional harmonic-oscillator
resolvent in $(w,u)$; the other $d-1$ components of $\xi$ are passive Gaussian
variables.  Mehler's formula evaluates its generating function.  A change of
variables $y=\tanh t$ turns the relevant coefficient into a residue of
$t/\sinh t$.  The Bernoulli numbers then prove nonvanishing at every order,
not merely at the finitely many orders accessible by brute-force expansion.

\section{Invariant recurrence}
\medskip

Set
\[
 a=|x|^2,\quad b=|\xi|^2,\quad c=x\cdot\xi,\quad w=a-z,
 \quad \ell=b+w^2.
\]
For an invariant $F(a,b,c,w)$, let $D_a=\partial_a+\partial_w$.  Direct
differentiation gives
\begin{align*}
 \Delta_xF&=2dD_aF+4aD_a^2F+4cD_a\partial_cF+b\partial_c^2F,\\
 \Delta_\xi F&=2d\partial_bF+4b\partial_b^2F
                 +4c\partial_b\partial_cF+a\partial_c^2F.
\end{align*}
Define
\begin{align*}
 S_2F={}&\frac14\Delta_xF+\frac w2\Delta_\xi F
     +2aF_b+4c^2F_{bb}+4acF_{bc}+a^2F_{cc},\\
 S_4F={}&\frac1{16}\Delta_\xi^2F.
\end{align*}
The source's Weyl recurrence specializes, for every $n\geq1$, to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:recurrence}
 K_0=\ell^{-1},\qquad
 K_{2n}=-K_0\bigl(S_2K_{2n-2}+S_4K_{2n-4}\bigr),
 \qquad K_{-2}=0.
\end{equation}
Indeed, the fourth $x$ derivative of $\ell$ yields $S_4$, while all higher
derivatives and all mixed $x,\xi$ derivatives vanish.  As a convention audit,
\eqref{eq:recurrence} gives
\[
K_2=\frac{2dw+4a}{\ell^3}
 +\frac{-4wb-8c^2-8aw^2}{\ell^4},
\]
which is the source's displayed $K_2$ formula for $P=|x|^2$.

\section{The top-two residue projection}

We use the source's normalized contour.  Spherical averaging gives
\[
 \operatorname{av}(c^{2k})
  =a^kb^k\frac{(1/2)_k}{(d/2)_k},
\]
and the subsequent radial integral is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mellin}
 \int_{\R^d}\frac{b^r}{(b+w^2)^q}\dd\xi
 =\frac{\pi^{d/2}\Gamma(r+d/2)\Gamma(q-r-d/2)}
 {\Gamma(d/2)\Gamma(q)}(w^2)^{r+d/2-q}.
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[top-two projection]\label{lem:projection}
Let $d=4j-1$.  There is a scalar $H_j$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:twores}
 \int_{\R^d}\oint wK_{2j}(x,\xi,z)f(z)\dd z\dd\xi
 =-H_j\left(a^{j-1}f(a)+\frac{a^j}{3j}f'(a)\right),
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H}
 H_j=\pi^{2j}(-1)^j6\,4^{j-1}j(2j-1)s_j.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First keep only the two highest powers of $a$ in
\eqref{eq:recurrence}.  Put $R=|x|$, align $e_1=x/R$, write
$u=\xi\cdot e_1$, and decompose $\xi=u e_1+v$.  At fixed $z$ and $\xi$,
\begin{align*}
 \Delta_x={}&(\partial_R+2R\partial_w)^2
  +\frac{d-1}{R}(\partial_R+2R\partial_w)\\
 &+\frac1{R^2}\bigl((|\xi|^2-u^2)\partial_u^2-(d-1)u\partial_u\bigr),
\end{align*}
while $(x\cdot\partial_\xi)^2=R^2\partial_u^2$.  Hence, if the top
part of $K_{2n}$ is $R^{2n}g_n(w,u,v)$, then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:g-rec}
 g_0=\ell^{-1},\qquad
 g_n=-\ell^{-1}(\partial_w^2+\partial_u^2)g_{n-1}.
\end{equation}
The $S_4$ term and the spherical part of $\Delta_x$ are two powers of $R$
lower and do not enter this equation.

Keeping one further power gives, for
$K_{2n}=R^{2n}g_n+R^{2n-2}r_n+O(R^{2n-4})$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:r-rec}
\begin{aligned}
 r_n=-\ell^{-1}\{&(\partial_w^2+\partial_u^2)r_{n-1}\\
 &+[\,(2n-2+d/2)\partial_w+(w/2)\Delta_\xi\,]g_{n-1}\}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Equations \eqref{eq:g-rec}--\eqref{eq:r-rec}, followed by the beta shift
\eqref{eq:mellin}, are an induction on $n$.  At $n=j$, terms other than
$a^jw^{-2}$ and $a^{j-1}w^{-1}$ have zero normalized contour integral.
Matching these two terms in \eqref{eq:r-rec}, and integrating the
$\Delta_\xi$ term by parts, gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ratio}
 [a^{j-1}w^{-1}]= -3j[a^jw^{-2}].
\end{equation}
Because
$\oint w^{-1}f(a-w)\dd z=-f(a)$ and
$\oint w^{-2}f(a-w)\dd z=f'(a)$, this is exactly the ratio in
\eqref{eq:twores}.  This coefficient comparison uses only
$\Gamma(x+1)=x\Gamma(x)$; the packet's exact checker performs it in the
unprojected four-invariant recurrence through $j=7$.

It remains to evaluate the $a^jw^{-2}$ coefficient.  Introduce a formal $h$
in \eqref{eq:g-rec}.  The series
$G_h=\sum_{n\geq0}(-1)^nh^{2n}g_n$ solves
\[
 \bigl(-h^2(\partial_w^2+\partial_u^2)+w^2+u^2+|v|^2\bigr)G_h=1.
\]
The elementary two-dimensional Mehler kernel, integrated against the
constant function, yields
\[
 G_h=\int_0^\infty e^{-t|v|^2}\frac1{\cosh(2ht)}
 \exp\left[-\frac{\tanh(2ht)}{2h}(w^2+u^2)\right]\dd t.
\]
After integrating in $(u,v)$ and changing from $t$ to
$s=w^2\tanh(2ht)/(2h)$, the multiplier of the Mellin integral is
\[
 \left(\frac{y}{\operatorname{artanh}y}\right)^{(d-1)/2}
 (1-y^2)^{-1/2},\qquad y=\frac{2hs}{w^2}.
\]
For $d=4j-1$, the coefficient needed at order $h^{2j}$ is therefore
\[
 F_j=[y^{2j}]
 \left(\frac{y}{\operatorname{artanh}y}\right)^{2j-1}
 (1-y^2)^{-1/2}.
\]
This coefficient is explicit.  In its residue formula put $y=\tanh t$:
\begin{align*}
 F_j
 &=\Res_{y=0}y^{-2j-1}
   \left(\frac{y}{\operatorname{artanh}y}\right)^{2j-1}
   (1-y^2)^{-1/2}\dd y\\
 &=\Res_{t=0}t^{-(2j-1)}\frac{\cosh t}{\sinh^2t}\dd t\\
 &=-(2j-1)\Res_{t=0}t^{-2j}\frac1{\sinh t}\dd t
 =-(2j-1)s_j.
\end{align*}
Here the third equality is integration by parts in the formal residue.
Restoring the Gaussian factor
$\pi^{d/2}4^j\Gamma(3/2)$ and the sign between $G_h$ and $g_j$ gives the
$a^jw^{-2}$ coefficient
\[
 -\frac{H_j}{3j}
 =(-1)^{j+1}\pi^{2j}2^{2j-1}(2j-1)s_j.
\]
This is equivalent to \eqref{eq:H}, completing the induction and the proof.
\end{proof}

\section{Completion of the trace computation}

The source's trace prefactor is $-2(2\pi)^{-d}$.  In polar coordinates,
\[
 \int_{\R^d}\Phi(|x|^2)\dd x
 =\frac{\pi^{d/2}}{\Gamma(d/2)}\int_0^\infty
 a^{d/2-1}\Phi(a)\dd a.
\]
Insert Lemma~\ref{lem:projection}.  Integration by parts gives
\begin{align*}
 &\int_0^\infty a^{d/2-1}
 \left(a^{j-1}f(a)+\frac{a^j}{3j}f'(a)\right)\dd a\\
 &\qquad=\left(1-\frac{3j-3/2}{3j}\right)
 \int_0^\infty a^{3j-5/2}f(a)\dd a
 =\frac1{2j}\int_0^\infty a^{3j-5/2}f(a)\dd a.
\end{align*}
Consequently
\[
 C_{2j}^{(4j-1)}(f)
 =\frac{H_j}{j}(2\pi)^{-d}
   \frac{\pi^{d/2}}{\Gamma(d/2)}
   \int_0^\infty a^{3j-5/2}f(a)\dd a.
\]
Substituting $d=4j-1$ and \eqref{eq:H} simplifies the prefactor to
\eqref{eq:Aj}.  This proves the trace formula and its positivity.  The
nonlinear-eigenvalue and counting conclusions are then exactly the source's
trace criterion and scaling corollary, with exponent
\[
 (d-2j)\frac{2+1}{2}=\frac32(2j-1)=3j-\frac32.
\]
This proves Theorem~\ref{thm:main}. \qed

\section{Scope and novelty check}

The theorem settles the full upper-critical family $d=4j-1$ for the radial
quadratic polynomial.  It does not settle the lower family $d=4j-3$ or the
conjecture for arbitrary positive elliptic $P$.  Exact experiments find the
lower-family coefficient positive through $j=6$, but no all-order sign
identity was found, so that pattern is not claimed as a theorem.

A bounded novelty search on 22 July 2026 checked the run's four cheap indexes
and arXiv searches for the source title and authors together with the phrases
``$4j-1$ coefficient'', ``radial quadratic'', ``Bernoulli'', and
``nonlinear eigenvalue pencil''.  It found the source, its authors' later
numerical work, and general nonlinear-eigenvalue reviews, but no analytic
all-order computation of this coefficient family.  Novelty confidence is
moderate, not definitive, because notation-specific searches can miss an
equivalent result.
