\section{Source problem}

E. K. Narayanan, P. K. Sanjay, and K. T. Yasser ask in Remark~4.1 of
\cite{NSY} whether a two-radius theorem holds above their one-radius
injectivity threshold $2m/(m-1)$, including $p=\infty$.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The complete remark appears on physical page 19 of the arXiv PDF.  The part
settled here is:
\begin{quote}
``It is a natural question whether a two radius theorem is true for functions
in $L^p(G)$ for $\frac{2m}{m-1}<p\leq\infty$.''
\end{quote}

\section{Definitions and exceptional sets}

Let $G$ be an $H$-type group whose Lie algebra splits orthogonally as
$\mathfrak v\oplus\mathfrak z$, with $\dim\mathfrak v=2n$ and
$\dim\mathfrak z=m\geq2$.  Write points as
$(z,t)\in\R^{2n}\times\R^m$.  For $u>0$, let $\mu_u$ be normalized
surface measure on $\{(z,0):|z|=u\}$.

For $k\geq1$, write the positive zeros of $L_k^{n-1}$ as
$0<\zeta_{k,1}<\cdots<\zeta_{k,k}$, and set
\[
 \E_n=\left\{\sqrt{\frac{\zeta_{k,i}}{\zeta_{k,j}}}:
 k\geq1,\ 1\leq i,j\leq k\right\}.
\]
Let $0<\beta_1<\beta_2<\cdots$ be the positive zeros of the ordinary
Bessel function $J_{n-1}$, and set
\[
 \B_n=\left\{\frac{\beta_i}{\beta_j}:i,j\geq1\right\}.
\]
Both sets are countable, reciprocal-invariant subsets of $(0,\infty)$.

\section{Proof Intuition}

The bounded spherical spectrum has two branches.  At nonzero central
frequency, the multiplier of $\mu_u$ is a nonvanishing factor times
$L_k^{n-1}(\lambda u^2/2)$; these are the Laguerre branches.  At zero
central frequency, the group becomes its Euclidean horizontal quotient and
the multiplier is the normalized Bessel function $\J_{n-1}(\tau u)$.
Thus two radii have a common spectral zero precisely on $\E_n\cup\B_n$.

For finite $p$, the zero-central-frequency branch cannot support a nonzero
$L^p$ function.  Level-by-level support containment and Abel reconstruction
therefore leave only $\E_n$.  For $p=\infty$, Abel reconstruction is
unavailable and the Bessel branch is real.  The endpoint is instead closed
by a Wiener--Tauberian theorem for the biradial $L^1$ algebra: absence of a
common zero forces the annihilator ideal to be all of $L^1(G)$.

\section{Exact all-p classification}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $G$, $m$, $n$, and $\mu_u$ be as above, and let $r,s>0$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item If $1\leq p\leq2m/(m-1)$, one radius is injective on $L^p(G)$.
\item If $2m/(m-1)<p<\infty$, the pair $(\mu_r,\mu_s)$ is injective on
      $L^p(G)$ if and only if $r/s\notin\E_n$.
\item On $L^\infty(G)$, the pair $(\mu_r,\mu_s)$ is injective if and only
      if $r/s\notin\E_n\cup\B_n$.
\end{enumerate}
Hence the horizontal two-radius problem is completely classified for every
$1\leq p\leq\infty$.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Finite-p classification}

Part (1) is source Theorem~1.1 \cite{NSY}.  We recall the short argument for
the new high finite-$p$ range.  The source spectral projections $\A_k$ are
bounded on $L^p$ for $1<p<\infty$, and for $2\leq p<\infty$ their Abel sums
satisfy
\begin{equation}\label{eq:abel}
 f=\lim_{\rho\uparrow1}\sum_{k=0}^\infty\rho^k\A_kf
 \quad\text{in }L^p(G).
\end{equation}
Before using its low-$p$ Fourier-support theorem, the proof of source
Theorem~1.1 shows that $f*\mu_u=0$ forces
\[
 \operatorname{supp}\mathcal F_t(\A_kf(z,\cdot))
 \subseteq\left\{a:L_k^{n-1}(|a|u^2/2)=0\right\}
\]
for almost every $z$.  All steps through this containment use only the
$L^p$ projection bounds and \eqref{eq:abel}; the upper bound on $p$ enters
only in the following sphere-support theorem.

If both radii annihilate $f$, a common support point at level $k\geq1$
would give, for some $i,j$,
\[
 |a|r^2/2=\zeta_{k,i},\qquad |a|s^2/2=\zeta_{k,j},
\]
and hence $r/s=\sqrt{\zeta_{k,i}/\zeta_{k,j}}$.  For
$r/s\notin\E_n$ every support is empty; also $L_0^{n-1}=1$.  Thus
$\A_kf=0$ for all $k$, and \eqref{eq:abel} gives $f=0$.

Conversely, at a ratio in $\E_n$, choose $k,i,j$ and
$\lambda=2\zeta_{k,i}/r^2=2\zeta_{k,j}/s^2$.  The source constructs
\[
 F_{k,\lambda}(z,t)=
 \frac{J_{m/2-1}(\lambda|t|)}{(\lambda|t|)^{m/2-1}}
 \varphi_k^\lambda(z)
\]
up to normalization, with
$F_{k,\lambda}*\mu_u=c_{k,n}\varphi_k^\lambda(u)F_{k,\lambda}$.
It lies in $L^p$ exactly for $p>2m/(m-1)$, including infinity, and both
eigenvalues vanish.  This proves (2) and one endpoint obstruction.

\subsection{The bounded endpoint}

Let $R=L^1(G)^\natural$ be the commutative Banach algebra of biradial
integrable functions.  In our notation, its Gelfand spectrum
\cite[pp.~5--6]{Sundari} consists of
\[
 \Phi_{\lambda,k}(z,t)=
 c_k e^{-\lambda|z|^2/4}
 L_k^{n-1}(\lambda|z|^2/2)\J_{m/2-1}(\lambda|t|),
 \quad \lambda>0, k\geq0,
\]
and the zero-central-frequency branch
\[
 \Phi_{0,\tau}(z,t)=\J_{n-1}(\tau|z|),\qquad \tau\geq0.
\]
Only their zeros matter; $c_k\neq0$ and $\J_\alpha(0)=1$.

We use the following H-type Wiener--Tauberian result.

\begin{lemma}[Sundari, Proposition 4.3]\label{lem:WT}
If $I$ is a proper closed right ideal in $L^1(G)$, then some character
$\Psi$ of $R$ satisfies $\widehat h(\Psi)=0$ for every $h\in I\cap R$.
\end{lemma}

Sundari also constructs a biradial Poisson kernel $P\in R$ whose Gelfand
transform is nowhere zero \cite[Proposition~4.4]{Sundari}.

Let $f\in L^\infty(G)$ and assume $f*\mu_r=f*\mu_s=0$.  Define
\[
 I_f=\{h\in L^1(G):f*h=0\}.
\]
Young's inequality makes $I_f$ closed, and associativity makes it a right
ideal.  For $u=r,s$, put $h_u=\mu_u*P$.  Convolution by a biradial finite
measure preserves $R$, and
\[
 f*h_u=(f*\mu_u)*P=0,
\]
so $h_u\in I_f\cap R$.  The Gelfand transforms are
\begin{align*}
 \widehat h_u(\lambda,k)
   &=\widehat P(\lambda,k)c_k e^{-\lambda u^2/4}
     L_k^{n-1}(\lambda u^2/2),\\
 \widehat h_u(0,\tau)
   &=\widehat P(0,\tau)\J_{n-1}(\tau u).
\end{align*}

Assume $r/s\notin\E_n\cup\B_n$.  On every Laguerre character, at least
one of $\widehat h_r,\widehat h_s$ is nonzero by the definition of $\E_n$;
the assertion is automatic at $k=0$.  On every Bessel character, at least
one is nonzero by the definition of $\B_n$, including $\tau=0$.  Thus no
character annihilates both members of $I_f\cap R$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:WT}
shows that $I_f$ cannot be proper.  Hence $I_f=L^1(G)$.  Applying an
$L^1$ approximate identity to $f$ yields $f=0$ almost everywhere.

It remains to prove necessity of the Bessel condition.  If
$r/s=\beta_i/\beta_j$, choose
$\tau=\beta_i/r=\beta_j/s$.  The bounded, center-independent function
\[
 H_\tau(z,t)=\J_{n-1}(\tau|z|)
\]
is a Euclidean spherical function on the horizontal quotient and satisfies
$H_\tau*\mu_u=\J_{n-1}(\tau u)H_\tau$.  Both eigenvalues vanish, so the
pair is not injective.  Together with the Laguerre example this completes
the proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

\section{Novelty and human review}

Two bounded primary-source search rounds used the exact source question and
variants involving ``two radius'', ``$H$-type'', $L^\infty$,
Wiener--Tauberian, Laguerre, and Bessel terminology.  They found the source,
its 2024 publication, Sundari's 2008 theorem, and adjacent Heisenberg,
Gelfand-pair, and X-ray results, but no statement of this exact
classification.  Novelty confidence is moderate pending specialist review.

The primary audit point is the standard measure-multiplier step
$\mu_u*P\in R$ and the application of Lemma~\ref{lem:WT}.  Reviewers should
also check the translation of Sundari's spectrum notation and re-audit the
finite-$p$ support containment.  The separate source question about general
$K$-orbits is not addressed.
