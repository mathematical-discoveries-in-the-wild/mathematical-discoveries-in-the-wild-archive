\section{Source question}

Kochanowski, Fawzi and Rouz\'e introduce two-indexed Schatten quasi-norms and
their completely bounded variants \cite{KFR}.  In their Summary and Outlook,
on page 49, they ask whether a completely positive (CP) map satisfies
\[
             \norm{\Phi}_{q\to p}=\norm{\Phi}^{+}_{q\to p}
             \qquad (0<p,q<1),
\]
where the superscript $+$ restricts the input to positive semidefinite
operators.  They note that the equality was known in the norm range
$p,q\geq1$ \cite{Watrous}.

We prove the equality for all positive exponents.  The argument does not use
duality or the triangle inequality, and so crosses the quasi-norm boundary
without modification.

\section{Statement}

For $0<r<\infty$, write
\[
  \norm{X}_r=\bigl(\operatorname{Tr}|X|^r\bigr)^{1/r},
  \qquad
  \norm{X}_\infty=\norm{X}_{\mathrm{op}}.
\]
For a linear map $\Phi:\Md\to\Mm$, set
\begin{align*}
 \norm{\Phi}_{q\to p}
   &=\sup_{X\ne0}\frac{\norm{\Phi(X)}_p}{\norm{X}_q},\\
 \norm{\Phi}^{+}_{q\to p}
   &=\sup_{0\ne A\ge0}\frac{\norm{\Phi(A)}_p}{\norm{A}_q}.
\end{align*}

\begin{theorem}[Positive-input principle]\label{thm:main}
Let $\Phi:\Md\to\Mm$ be completely positive.  For every
$0<p,q\leq\infty$,
\[
                    \boxed{
                    \norm{\Phi}_{q\to p}
                    =\norm{\Phi}^{+}_{q\to p}.}
\]
Both suprema are maxima.  In fact, complete positivity can be weakened to
$2$-positivity.
\end{theorem}

Thus Theorem~\ref{thm:main} includes, and fully answers, the source's open
range $0<p,q<1$.

\section{Proof intuition}

The polar decomposition of an arbitrary input $X$ places it in the
off-diagonal corner of a positive $2\times2$ block matrix whose diagonal
corners are $|X^*|$ and $|X|$.  Two-positivity sends that matrix to another
positive block matrix.  Positivity forces its off-diagonal corner
$\Phi(X)$ to factor through the geometric mean of the two positive diagonal
corners.  Generalized Schatten H\"older then bounds $\norm{\Phi(X)}_p$ by the
geometric mean of the output quasi-norms at the two positive inputs
$|X^*|$ and $|X|$.  Those inputs have exactly the same Schatten $q$-quasi-norm
as $X$, so one of the two positive-input ratios is at least as good as the
ratio at $X$.

The key point is that generalized Schatten H\"older is valid for every
positive exponent, including $p<1$; it is Lemma 2.1 of the source \cite{KFR}.

\section{Two block-matrix lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[Canonical positive block]\label{lem:canonical}
For every $X\in\Md$,
\[
             \begin{pmatrix}|X^*|&X\\X^*&|X|\end{pmatrix}\ge0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $X=U|X|$ be the polar decomposition.  Since
$|X^*|=U|X|U^*$, one has the Gram factorization
\[
 \begin{pmatrix}|X^*|&X\\X^*&|X|\end{pmatrix}
 =\begin{pmatrix}U\\I\end{pmatrix}|X|
  \begin{pmatrix}U^*&I\end{pmatrix},
\]
which is positive semidefinite.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Off-diagonal estimate]\label{lem:block}
If
\[
                  \begin{pmatrix}A&B\\B^*&D\end{pmatrix}\ge0,
                  \qquad A,D\ge0,
\]
then there is a contraction $K$ such that
\[
                         B=A^{1/2}KD^{1/2}.
\]
Consequently, for every $0<p\leq\infty$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:blockholder}
                 \norm{B}_p
                 \leq \norm{A}_p^{1/2}\norm{D}_p^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $\varepsilon>0$, the block matrix remains positive after adding
$\varepsilon I$ to both diagonal corners.  The Schur complement therefore
shows that
\[
 K_\varepsilon=(A+\varepsilon I)^{-1/2}
                 B(D+\varepsilon I)^{-1/2}
\]
is a contraction.  In finite dimensions, a subsequence converges to a
contraction $K$.  Passing to the limit in
\[
 B=(A+\varepsilon I)^{1/2}K_\varepsilon
   (D+\varepsilon I)^{1/2}
\]
gives $B=A^{1/2}KD^{1/2}$, including when $A$ or $D$ is singular.

Generalized Schatten H\"older, first for the product of
$A^{1/2}$ and $KD^{1/2}$ and then with the operator norm of $K$, gives
\begin{align*}
 \norm{B}_p
 &\leq \norm{A^{1/2}}_{2p}
          \norm{KD^{1/2}}_{2p}\\
 &\leq \norm{A^{1/2}}_{2p}\norm{K}_\infty
          \norm{D^{1/2}}_{2p}\\
 &\leq \norm{A}_p^{1/2}\norm{D}_p^{1/2}.
\end{align*}
Here $2p=\infty$ when $p=\infty$.  Generalized H\"older holds for
$0<p<1$ as well as for the norm range, so no convexity is being used.
\end{proof}

\section{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}}

Fix $X\in\Md$.  Applying the $2$-positive map
$\operatorname{id}_{M_2}\otimes\Phi$ to the positive matrix in
Lemma~\ref{lem:canonical} yields
\[
 \begin{pmatrix}
  \Phi(|X^*|)&\Phi(X)\\
  \Phi(X)^*&\Phi(|X|)
 \end{pmatrix}\ge0.
\]
We used here that every positive linear map is adjoint preserving.  By
Lemma~\ref{lem:block},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mainestimate}
 \norm{\Phi(X)}_p
 \leq
 \norm{\Phi(|X^*|)}_p^{1/2}
 \norm{\Phi(|X|)}_p^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

The three matrices $X$, $|X|$, and $|X^*|$ have the same singular values.
Hence
\[
             \norm{X}_q=\norm{|X|}_q=\norm{|X^*|}_q
             \qquad(0<q\leq\infty).
\]
Dividing \eqref{eq:mainestimate} by this common value gives
\begin{align*}
 \frac{\norm{\Phi(X)}_p}{\norm{X}_q}
 &\leq
 \left(
  \frac{\norm{\Phi(|X^*|)}_p}{\norm{|X^*|}_q}
 \right)^{1/2}
 \left(
  \frac{\norm{\Phi(|X|)}_p}{\norm{|X|}_q}
 \right)^{1/2}\\
 &\leq \norm{\Phi}^{+}_{q\to p}.
\end{align*}
Taking the supremum over $X\ne0$ proves
$\norm{\Phi}_{q\to p}\leq\norm{\Phi}^{+}_{q\to p}$.  The reverse
inequality is immediate because positive inputs are a subset of all inputs.

Finally, in finite dimensions the unit Schatten $q$-sphere and its positive
part are compact, while the objective is continuous.  Thus the suprema are
attained.  Only $2$-positivity was used.

\section{Scope and novelty check}

This is a full answer to the ordinary induced Schatten $q\to p$ question in
the Summary and Outlook of \cite{KFR}, and it strengthens the requested range
from $0<p,q<1$ to all $0<p,q\leq\infty$.  It does \emph{not} settle the
separate question about the source's completely bounded mixed two-indexed
quasi-norms.  It also does not address the interpolation conjecture on the
same source page or infinite-dimensional extensions.

The run's cheap indexes were searched by arXiv identifier, exact title, and
the core phrases ``completely positive map'', ``Schatten quasi-norm'', and
``positive input''.  A bounded primary-source search located the source and
the norm-range result \cite{Watrous}, but no paper recording the $p,q<1$
extension or this two-by-two proof.  This is not an exhaustive novelty
certification.
