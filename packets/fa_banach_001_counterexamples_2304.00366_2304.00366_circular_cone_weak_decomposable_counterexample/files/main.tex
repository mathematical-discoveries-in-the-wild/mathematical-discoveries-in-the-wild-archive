\section*{Status}

This packet gives a candidate counterexample to the support-inclusion
formulation of Question 2 in Szusterman,
\emph{Extremizers in Soprunov and Zvavitch's Bezout inequalities for mixed volumes}
\cite{Szusterman2023}. The question asks whether the cone
\[
        C=\conv(e_n,B),\qquad B=\pi_{e_n^\perp}(B_2^n)\subset e_n^\perp,
\]
is weakly indecomposable. Under the displayed Definition 7 condition
\(\supp(S_{K+L})\subset\supp(S_K)\), the answer is negative: \(C\) is weakly
decomposable in every dimension \(n\geq 3\).

\section*{Source question}

The source paper defines weak decomposability as follows: a convex body \(K\)
with non-empty interior is weakly decomposable if there is an \(L\), not
homothetic to \(K\), such that
\[
        \supp(S_{K+L})\subset \supp(S_K).
\]
Immediately after recalling this definition and its consequences, the paper
asks:
\begin{quote}
Is it true that the cone \(C=\conv(e_n,B)\), where
\(B=\pi_{e_n^\perp}(B_2^n)\subset e_n^\perp\) is the unit ball in
\(e_n^\perp\), is weakly indecomposable?
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions and notation}

Write points of \(\R^n\) as \((x,t)\), with
\(x\in H:=e_n^\perp\simeq\R^{n-1}\) and \(t\in\R\). Then
\[
        C=\{(x,t):0\leq t\leq 1,\ \|x\|_2\leq 1-t\}.
\]
Its support function is
\[
        h_C(\xi,s)=\max\{\|\xi\|_2,s\},\qquad (\xi,s)\in H\times\R.
\]
The surface area measure of \(C\) is supported on
\[
        \Omega_C=\{-e_n\}\cup
        \left\{\frac{(\theta,1)}{\sqrt2}:\theta\in \Sph^{n-2}\subset H\right\}.
\]
The point \(-e_n\) is the base normal; the spherical copy of \(\Sph^{n-2}\)
is the set of normals to the lateral conical surface. The apex normal cone
and the normal cones of the base rim carry no \((n-1)\)-dimensional boundary
area.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The circular cone has a large zero-area normal cone at its apex. Sweeping the
whole cone by a small horizontal segment turns the apex into a horizontal
segment, but that top segment still has codimension at least one inside the
boundary and hence contributes no surface area measure. At height \(t\), the
new body has horizontal section equal to a fixed segment plus a Euclidean ball
of radius \(a-t\). Thus all genuine side normals remain exactly the old
lateral cone normals \((\theta,1)/\sqrt2\), and the only facet normal remains
the base normal \(-e_n\). The sweep is therefore a non-homothetic Minkowski
summand that does not enlarge the support of the surface area measure.

\section*{Counterexample}

\begin{theorem}
For every \(n\geq 3\), the cone
\[
        C=\conv(e_n,\pi_{e_n^\perp}(B_2^n))
\]
is weakly decomposable in the sense of Definition 7 of \cite{Szusterman2023}.
Consequently Question 2 of \cite{Szusterman2023} has a negative answer.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H=e_n^\perp\) and choose a nondegenerate compact segment
\[
        P=[-v,v]\subset H,\qquad v\neq 0.
\]
Fix \(\eps>0\), and put
\[
        L=P+\eps C.
\]
Then \(L\) is a full-dimensional convex body because \(\eps C\) is
full-dimensional. Also \(L\) is not homothetic to \(C\): in direction \(e_n\),
the exposed face \(C^{e_n}\) is the single apex \(\{e_n\}\), whereas
\[
        L^{e_n}=P+\eps e_n
\]
is a nondegenerate segment.

It remains to show that \(C+L\) has no surface area measure outside
\(\supp(S_C)\). Since
\[
        C+L=P+(1+\eps)C,
\]
it is enough to analyze bodies of the form \(K_a:=P+aC\), \(a>0\). Their
support functions are
\[
        h_{K_a}(\xi,s)=h_P(\xi)+a\max\{\|\xi\|_2,s\},
        \qquad (\xi,s)\in H\times\R.              \tag{1}
\]
Equivalently,
\[
        K_a=\{(x,t):0\leq t\leq a,\ x\in P+(a-t)B_H\},       \tag{2}
\]
where \(B_H\) is the Euclidean unit ball in \(H\). Formula (2) makes the
boundary geometry explicit.

The base \(t=0\) is the only \((n-1)\)-dimensional facet with horizontal
normal, and its outer normal is \(-e_n\). The top set \(P+ae_n\) has dimension
\(1\leq n-2\), so its normal cone contributes zero to \(S_{K_a}\). Similarly,
the lower rim of the base has dimension at most \(n-2\), so its normal cone
also contributes zero surface area.

On the remaining boundary, \(0<t<a\) and \(x\) lies on the boundary of
\(P+(a-t)B_H\). At every regular such point the horizontal outer normal is
some \(\theta\in\Sph^{n-2}\), and locally the supporting equation is
\[
        \langle x,\theta\rangle+t=h_P(\theta)+a.
\]
Thus the corresponding outer unit normal in \(\R^n\) is
\[
        \frac{(\theta,1)}{\sqrt2},
\]
one of the original lateral normals of \(C\). At nonregular side points,
possibly more than one horizontal normal \(\theta\) is active, but every
supporting normal that occurs is still of the displayed lateral form. Any
positive-area flat side pieces therefore have normals in the same lateral
sphere, and the lower-dimensional intersections between such pieces carry no
\((n-1)\)-dimensional boundary area.

Therefore
\[
        \supp(S_{K_a})\subset
        \{-e_n\}\cup\left\{\frac{(\theta,1)}{\sqrt2}:
        \theta\in\Sph^{n-2}\right\}=\supp(S_C).
\]
Taking \(a=1+\eps\) gives
\[
        \supp(S_{C+L})=\supp(S_{P+(1+\eps)C})\subset\supp(S_C),
\]
with \(L\) non-homothetic to \(C\). This is exactly weak decomposability of
\(C\), so the proposed weak indecomposability fails.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

This packet answers only the support-inclusion version of Question 2 about the
displayed circular cone. The same source discussion also refers to the stronger
condition \(S_{K+L}\ll S_K\) from Saroglou--Soprunov--Zvavitch; the present
segment-sweep construction is not claimed to settle that absolute-continuity
variant. It also does not settle the broader conjecture that \(b_2(K)=1\)
characterizes simplices for all convex bodies in dimensions \(n\geq 4\), and
it does not classify all weakly indecomposable convex bodies.

\section*{Human review recommendation}

Review as a likely-valid counterexample. The main points to check are:
whether the intended target is the displayed support-inclusion definition
rather than the stronger absolute-continuity variant, the claim that
\(L=P+\eps C\) is not homothetic to \(C\), and the assertion that all
positive-area boundary pieces of \(P+aC\) have normals in the old base/lateral
support of \(C\).
