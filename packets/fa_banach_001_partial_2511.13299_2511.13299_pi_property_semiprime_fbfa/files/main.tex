\section*{Open Problem Evidence}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The source question is:
\[
\text{Is }\FBFA{E}\text{ semiprime for every Banach space }E?
\]
The source proves positive answers for finite-dimensional \(E\) and for
\(E=L_1(\mu)\), and proves that semiprimeness of \(\FBFA{E}\) is
equivalent to representability in \(C(B_{E^*})\) \cite[Theorem 5.1 and
Corollaries 5.2, 5.8]{munoz-tradacete}.

\section*{Partial Result}

Say that \(E\) has the metric approximation property, in the net form
used here, if there is a directed net \((R_\lambda)\) of finite-rank
operators \(R_\lambda:E\to E\) such that
\[
\|R_\lambda\|\leq 1
\quad\text{and}\quad
R_\lambda x\longrightarrow x
\quad\text{for every }x\in E.
\]

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The finite-dimensional pieces already have semiprime free Banach
\(f\)-algebras by the source paper.  The approximating maps do not have
to be projections.  A finite-rank contraction \(R_\lambda:E\to E\)
factors as a contraction from \(E\) onto its finite-dimensional range,
followed by the inclusion of that range back into \(E\).  If
\(a\in\FBFA{E}\) satisfies \(a^2=0\), the first factor sends \(a\) to a
nilpotent element in a finite-dimensional free Banach \(f\)-algebra, so
it kills \(a\).  Therefore the induced endomorphism associated to
\(R_\lambda\) kills \(a\).  Since these endomorphisms converge strongly
to the identity on \(\FBFA{E}\), the element \(a\) is a norm limit of
zero elements.

\begin{theorem}
Let \(E\) be a Banach space with the metric approximation property.
Then \(\FBFA{E}\) is semiprime.  Consequently, the canonical
representation of \(\FBFA{E}\) in \(C(B_{E^*})\) is injective.
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof}

We first record the functorial continuity point used in the argument.

\begin{lemma}
Let \(T_\lambda:E\to E\) be contractions such that \(T_\lambda x\to x\)
for every \(x\in E\).  Let
\(\overline{T_\lambda}:\FBFA{E}\to\FBFA{E}\) be the contractive
lattice-algebra homomorphism induced by the universal property.  Then
\[
\overline{T_\lambda}a\longrightarrow a
\quad\text{for every }a\in\FBFA{E}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The normed free algebra \(\FNFA{E}\) is dense in \(\FBFA{E}\).  Every
element \(b\in\FNFA{E}\) is obtained from finitely many generators
\(\eta_E(x_1),\ldots,\eta_E(x_n)\) by finitely many linear, lattice, and
algebra operations.  Since \(T_\lambda x_j\to x_j\) for each \(j\), and
since the Banach lattice and Banach algebra operations are norm
continuous, \(\overline{T_\lambda}b\to b\).

For general \(a\in\FBFA{E}\), choose \(b\in\FNFA{E}\) with
\(\|a-b\|<\varepsilon\).  The maps \(\overline{T_\lambda}\) are
contractions, so
\[
\|\overline{T_\lambda}a-a\|
\leq
\|\overline{T_\lambda}(a-b)\|
+\|\overline{T_\lambda}b-b\|
+\|b-a\|
\leq
2\varepsilon+\|\overline{T_\lambda}b-b\|.
\]
Letting \(\lambda\) tend along the net and then \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\)
proves the claim.
\end{proof}

Now assume \(a\in\FBFA{E}\) satisfies \(a^2=0\).  Let
\((R_\lambda)\) be a net of finite-rank contractions on \(E\) converging
strongly to the identity.  For each \(\lambda\), put
\(E_\lambda=R_\lambda(E)\), equipped with the norm inherited from \(E\),
and view \(R_\lambda\) as a contraction
\[
S_\lambda:E\longrightarrow E_\lambda .
\]
By the universal property, \(S_\lambda\) induces a contractive
lattice-algebra homomorphism
\[
\widetilde{S_\lambda}:\FBFA{E}\longrightarrow \FBFA{E_\lambda}.
\]
Since this is an algebra homomorphism,
\[
(\widetilde{S_\lambda}a)^2
=
\widetilde{S_\lambda}(a^2)
=0.
\]
The source paper proves that \(\FBFA{F}\) is semiprime whenever \(F\) is
finite-dimensional \cite[Corollary 4.23]{munoz-tradacete}.  Hence
\(\widetilde{S_\lambda}a=0\) for every \(\lambda\).

Let \(i_\lambda:E_\lambda\hookrightarrow E\) be the inclusion.  The
composition \(i_\lambda S_\lambda:E\to E\) is exactly \(R_\lambda\), now
regarded as an operator from \(E\) to itself.  By functorial uniqueness,
\[
\overline{R_\lambda}
=
\overline{i_\lambda}\,\widetilde{S_\lambda}
:\FBFA{E}\to\FBFA{E}.
\]
Therefore \(\overline{R_\lambda}a=0\) for every \(\lambda\).  By the
lemma, \(\overline{R_\lambda}a\to a\), and consequently \(a=0\).
Thus \(\FBFA{E}\) is semiprime.

The final statement follows from the source theorem that
\(\FBFA{E}\) is representable in \(C(B_{E^*})\) if and only if it is
semiprime \cite[Theorem 5.1]{munoz-tradacete}.
\qed

\section*{Examples Covered}

The theorem applies to every Banach space with a monotone Schauder
basis, using the canonical initial-segment projections.  It also applies
to spaces with a monotone finite-dimensional decomposition.  More
generally, it applies to every Banach space with MAP, because the proof
uses finite-rank contractions rather than finite-rank projections.  These
give positive territory beyond the source's finite-dimensional case and
the \(L_1(\mu)\) case, although the source question remains open in
general.

\section*{Verification Notes and Limitations}

The proof is formal and non-computational.  The key point to review is
the strong convergence lemma for induced free algebra maps.  It only
uses density of \(\FNFA{E}\), the universal property, and norm continuity
of the lattice and algebra operations.

The hypothesis is genuinely narrower than the bounded approximation
property and the approximation property.  Contractivity is used to invoke
the universal property of \(\FBFA{E}\) and to make the density argument in
the lemma uniform.  A uniformly bounded finite-rank approximation with
norms bounded by a constant greater than \(1\) does not directly give
homomorphisms on the same free Banach \(f\)-algebra, and rescaling the
operators destroys the convergence needed at the end.  The result
therefore does not settle Question 5.10 for general Banach spaces.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review the functorial factorization through \(E_\lambda=R_\lambda(E)\)
and the strong-convergence lemma.  If both are accepted as consequences
of the source universal property and density of \(\FNFA{E}\) in
\(\FBFA{E}\), the MAP subcase appears complete.  The packet should remain
classified as partial unless a separate argument handles Banach spaces
without MAP.
