\paragraph{Status.}
\textbf{full\_solution\_likely\_valid}.  This packet answers the printed
associate-space problem in item (iii), p.~36, of Miquel Saucedo and Sergey
Tikhonov, \emph{The Fourier transform is an extremizer of a class of bounded
operators}, arXiv:2501.17505.  In the Banach range $1\le q<2$, it identifies
the requested associate functional as an explicit block space and gives its
norm as an attained minimum.

\paragraph{Source problem (item (iii), p.~36).}
After finding the optimal Fourier-domain functional, the source says that it
is of interest to find its associate.  It records a known formula for the
simpler of two functionals and states: ``We have not been able to find an
expression for the latter in the literature.''

\section*{The functional from the source}

Fix $1\le q<2$ and set
\[
 \frac1{q^\sharp}=\frac1q-\frac12,
 \qquad
 U(t)=\int_0^t u^*(s)^q\,ds,
\]
\[
 \xi(t)=U(t)+t^{q/2}
 \left(\int_t^\infty u^*(s)^{q^\sharp}\,ds\right)^{q/q^\sharp}.
\]
Write
\[
 a(t)=U(t)^{q^\sharp/2}u^*(t)^q
       \xi(t)^{-q^\sharp/2}t^{-q/2}.                           \tag{1}
\]
The second functional displayed on p.~36 is
\[
 \N_a(F)=
 \left(\int_0^\infty a(t)
 \left[\int_0^t
   \left(\int_0^{1/r}F^*(s)\,ds\right)^2dr
 \right]^{q/2}dt\right)^{1/q}.                                \tag{2}
\]
As in Definition 1.1 of the source, its associate is
\[
 \N_a'(G)=\sup\left\{\int_0^\infty |F(s)G(s)|\,ds:
                         \N_a(F)\le1\right\}.                  \tag{3}
\]

\section*{Result at a glance}

Let
\[
 \Omega=\{(t,r):0<r<t<\infty\}
\]
and, for a measurable kernel $K$ on $\Omega$, define
\[
 \|K\|_{q',a}=
 \left(\int_0^\infty a(t)
       \left[\int_0^t |K(t,r)|^2dr\right]^{q'/2}dt\right)^{1/q'}
                                                                  \tag{4}
\]
when $q>1$.  At $q=1$, (4) means
\[
 \|K\|_{\infty,a}=esssup_{t:a(t)>0}
       \left(\int_0^t|K(t,r)|^2dr\right)^{1/2}.                 \tag{5}
\]
Define the positive adjoint Hardy operator
\[
 (\T_a^*K)(s)=
 \int_0^\infty a(t)
 \int_0^{\min\{t,1/s\}}K(t,r)\,dr\,dt.                         \tag{6}
\]
For nonnegative functions $g,h$ on $(0,\infty)$, write
\[
 g\preccurlyeq_H h
 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
 \int_0^xg(s)\,ds\le\int_0^xh(s)\,ds
 \quad\text{for every }x>0.                                   \tag{7}
\]
Then the requested associate has the exact formula
\[
 \boxed{
 \N_a'(G)=min\left\{\|K\|_{q',a}:
 K\ge0,\quad G^*\preccurlyeq_H\T_a^*K\right\}.}              \tag{8}
\]
Thus the associate is a concrete Hardy-block space.  Expanding the constraint
in (8), admissibility of $K$ means
\[
 \int_0^xG^*(s)\,ds
 \le
 \int_0^\infty a(t)\int_0^t
        K(t,r)\min\{x,1/r\}\,dr\,dt
 \qquad(x>0).                                                   \tag{9}
\]
Formula (8) is valid with value $+\infty$ as well.  Whenever the associate
norm is finite, a minimizing kernel exists.

\section*{Proof intuition}

The nested integral in (2) looks nonlinear only because an $L^2$ norm is
written out.  On decreasing functions it is the norm of the positive linear
map
\[
 (\T f)(t,r)=\mathbf 1_{\{r<t\}}\int_0^{1/r}f(s)\,ds
\]
into the mixed space $L^q(a(t)dt;L^2(0,t))$.  Dualizing that mixed space
introduces the kernel $K$ in (4).  Fubini's theorem gives the explicit
adjoint (6).

The only remaining feature is that an associate of a rearrangement-invariant
functional tests decreasing rearrangements.  A function $h$ dominates $g$
against every nonnegative decreasing test function exactly when
$g\preccurlyeq_Hh$.  Consequently, the adjoint need not equal $G^*$
pointwise; it must dominate it in the cumulative Hardy order.  A separation
argument proves that this necessary condition is also sufficient and that no
norm is lost.

\section*{Formal theorem and proof}

The proof works for a general locally integrable weight $a\ge0$.  We assume
only the natural nondegeneracy condition that (2) is finite on bounded,
compactly supported decreasing functions; this holds in every nontrivial
case in which the source functional defines an associate space.

\begin{lemma}[The Hardy cone]\label{lem:hardycone}
Let $p\in L^1_{\mathrm{loc}}(0,\infty)$.  Then
\[
 \int_0^\infty f(s)p(s)\,ds\ge0
\]
for every nonnegative, nonincreasing, compactly supported step function $f$
if and only if
\[
 \int_0^xp(s)\,ds\ge0\qquad(x>0).                              \tag{10}
\]
Consequently, for nonnegative $g,h$,
\[
 \int_0^\infty f g\le\int_0^\infty f h
 \quad\text{for all nonnegative decreasing }f
\]
if and only if $g\preccurlyeq_Hh$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Necessity follows by taking $f=\mathbf1_{(0,x)}$.  Conversely, every
nonnegative decreasing step function has the form
\[
 f=\sum_{j=1}^m c_j\mathbf1_{(0,x_j)},\qquad c_j\ge0.
\]
Equation (10) then gives $\int fp\ge0$.  General decreasing tests follow by
monotone approximation.  Apply this with $p=h-g$ for the final assertion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Associate of the nested Hardy functional]\label{thm:dual}
Let $1\le q<\infty$ and let $a\ge0$ be locally integrable.  Define
\[
 \N_a(F)=\left(\int_0^\infty a(t)
 \left[\int_0^t\left(\int_0^{1/r}F^*(s)\,ds\right)^2dr
 \right]^{q/2}dt\right)^{1/q}.
\]
If $\N_a$ is finite on compactly supported decreasing step functions, then
its associate is given by (8)--(9), with (5) when $q=1$.  If
$\N_a'(G)<\infty$, the minimum in (8) is attained.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Put
\[
 Z_q=L^q\big((0,\infty),a(t)dt;L^2(0,t)\big).
\]
For $q=1$ its Banach dual is
$Z_\infty=L^\infty(a(t)dt;L^2(0,t))$; for $q>1$ its dual is $Z_{q'}$.
Their duality pairing is
\[
 \langle H,K\rangle
 =\int_0^\infty a(t)\int_0^tH(t,r)K(t,r)\,dr\,dt.              \tag{11}
\]

By the Hardy--Littlewood rearrangement inequality, (3) becomes
\[
 \N_a'(G)=sup\left\{\int_0^\infty f(s)G^*(s)\,ds:
 f\ge0\text{ decreasing},\ \|\T f\|_{Z_q}\le1\right\},      \tag{12}
\]
where
\[
 (\T f)(t,r)=\mathbf1_{\{0<r<t\}}
                 \int_0^{1/r}f(s)\,ds.                         \tag{13}
\]
Indeed, $\|\T f\|_{Z_q}=\N_a(f)$ by inspection.

For $K\in Z_{q'}$, Tonelli's theorem gives
\begin{align*}
 \langle\T f,K\rangle
 &=\int_0^\infty a(t)\int_0^tK(t,r)
       \int_0^{1/r}f(s)\,ds\,dr\,dt\\
 &=\int_0^\infty f(s)(\T_a^*K)(s)\,ds,                        \tag{14}
\end{align*}
first for nonnegative functions and then by decomposition.  This proves the
adjoint formula (6).

Suppose $K\ge0$ and $G^*\preccurlyeq_H\T_a^*K$.  Lemma
\ref{lem:hardycone}, (14), and mixed-norm H\"older give, for every decreasing
$f\ge0$,
\[
 \int fG^*\le\int f\T_a^*K
 =\langle\T f,K\rangle
 \le\|\T f\|_{Z_q}\|K\|_{q',a}.
\]
Taking the supremum in (12) proves
\[
 \N_a'(G)\le
 \inf_{K\ge0,\,G^*\preccurlyeq_H\T_a^*K}\|K\|_{q',a}.        \tag{15}
\]

We prove the reverse inequality by separation.  Let
$C=\N_a'(G)<\infty$, and let $\Pcone$ be the cone of locally integrable $p$
satisfying (10).  In the weak local topology generated by compactly
supported decreasing step functions, consider
\[
 \mathcal B_C=\left\{\T_a^*K-p:\|K\|_{q',a}\le C,\ p\in\Pcone\right\}.
                                                                  \tag{16}
\]
The dual ball is weakly compact when $q>1$ and weak-star compact when $q=1$;
$\Pcone$ is weakly closed by (10).  Since the sum of a compact set and a
closed set is closed, $\mathcal B_C$ is closed.  These statements may
equivalently be verified first on finite intervals and finite step
partitions, then passed to the limit by weak compactness.

If $G^*\notin\mathcal B_C$, Hahn--Banach separation gives a compactly
supported test function $f$ such that
\[
 \int fG^*>
 \sup_{\|K\|_{q',a}\le C,\,p\in\Pcone}
 \left(\int f\T_a^*K-\int fp\right).                           \tag{17}
\]
The supremum on the right can be finite only if $f$ belongs to the polar of
$\Pcone$.  By Lemma~\ref{lem:hardycone}, this means that $f$ is
nonnegative and decreasing.  Equations (11)--(14) then show that the right
side of (17) is
\[
 C\sup_{\|K\|_{q',a}\le1}\langle\T f,K\rangle
 =C\|\T f\|_{Z_q}.
\]
This contradicts (12) and the definition of $C$.  Hence
$G^*=\T_a^*K-p$ for some $\|K\|_{q',a}\le C$ and $p\in\Pcone$, or
equivalently
\[
 G^*\preccurlyeq_H\T_a^*K.
\]
Replacing $K$ by $|K|$ preserves its norm and only increases $\T_a^*K$, so
we may take $K\ge0$.  Together with (15), this proves equality and also
attainment of the minimum.

Finally, integrating (6) over $s\in(0,x)$ and applying Tonelli yields
\[
 \int_0^x(\T_a^*K)(s)\,ds
 =\int_0^\infty a(t)\int_0^tK(t,r)\min\{x,1/r\}\,dr\,dt,
\]
which is exactly (9).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Answer to the problem on p.~36]\label{cor:source}
Let $1\le q<2$, let $u$ be as in Corollary 5.3 of Saucedo--Tikhonov, and
assume $\xi(t)<\infty$ for $t>0$.  Form $a$ from (1).  The associate of the
second functional in item (iii), p.~36, is the Hardy-block functional in
(8), equivalently (4)--(5) subject to the explicit inequalities (9).
Therefore the sharp rearrangement-invariant target norm requested there is
completely determined by an attained convex minimization problem.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Substitution of (1) into Theorem~\ref{thm:dual} gives exactly the second
displayed functional on p.~36.  Definition 1.1 of the source is (3), so the
theorem computes the requested associate.
\end{proof}

\section*{Checks, interpretation, and scope}

\paragraph{Consistency check.}
When $\xi\asymp U$, Corollary 5.3 of the source simplifies (2), up to constants
depending only on $q$, to the first Hardy functional displayed on p.~36.  The
source cites the known associate $\|wG^{**}\|_{q'}$ for that functional.
Since equivalent functionals have equivalent associates, (8) agrees with
that known formula in the simplifying regime.  Formula (8) additionally
covers the genuinely harder case in which $\xi/U$ is not uniformly bounded.

\paragraph{What the formula provides.}
The original supremum in (3) ranges over all input functions.  Formula (8)
replaces it by a convex mixed-norm minimization over positive kernels, with
the elementary linear constraints (9).  On a finite grid it is a
second-order cone program.  More importantly, (8) identifies the functions
in the associate space and their norm without reference to Fourier
transforms or to an unspecified best constant.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The answer is a two-variable block representation, not in general a single
weighted Lorentz or $\Gamma$ norm.  No claim is made that the kernel can be
eliminated for arbitrary irregular $u$.  The theorem covers the Banach
associate range $1\le q<2$, including $q=1$; it does not assert a Banach-dual
formula for $0<q<1$.

\paragraph{Novelty check.}
\begin{sloppypar}
The run indexes were searched for the arXiv id, title, associate norms,
Hardy/Copson duality, and the defining nested integral.  Current arXiv
searches used the exact printed phrase, the authors, the displayed
$U$--$\xi$ structure, K\"othe associates of mixed Hardy/Copson spaces, and
papers citing arXiv:2501.17505.  They found the source paper and general
Ces\`aro/Copson duality literature, but no answer to this exact functional and
no later paper citing the source with such an answer.  Novelty confidence is

\end{sloppypar}

\paragraph{Human-review recommendation.}
Review as a full answer in block-space form.  The main verifier focus should
be the closedness/separation step leading from (12) to (16), especially the
weak-star endpoint $q=1$.  A second useful check is whether a preferred
definition of ``expression'' in this subliterature demands a one-variable
weighted norm; mathematically, (8) computes the associate and its exact best
constant, but it deliberately retains an auxiliary kernel.
