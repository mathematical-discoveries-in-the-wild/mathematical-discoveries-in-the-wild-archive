\title{A Full Affirmative Answer to Kerr--Li Question 9.4\\
\large Simple AF Automorphisms with Arbitrary Prescribed CA Entropy}
\author{}
\date{June 19, 2026}

\begin{abstract}
Kerr and Li asked whether a UHF algebra, or any other simple AF algebra,
can admit a $*$-automorphism with finite nonzero contractive approximation
(CA) entropy.  We give an affirmative answer to the ``any other simple AF
algebra'' part, in a stronger form: every value in $[0,\infty]$ occurs as the
CA entropy of an automorphism of a simple unital separable AF algebra.

Given a minimal homeomorphism $T$ of a Cantor set $X$, consider the
inductive system
\[
 A_n=M_{2^{n-1}}(C(X)),\qquad
 \varphi_n(a)(x)=\operatorname{diag}(a(x),a(Tx)).
\]
The maps $\beta_n(a)=a\circ T$ are compatible with the connecting maps and
induce an automorphism $\beta$ of the limit.  Minimality of $T$ makes the
limit simple, while zero-dimensionality of $X$ makes it AF.  A locality
property of contractive ranks and Kerr--Li's injective-tensor-product
subadditivity show
\[
 \hc(\beta)=\htop(T).
\]
Minimal Cantor systems exist with every prescribed entropy in
$[0,\infty]$.

We also audit the supplied product-type argument.  Its mathematical core is
correct, with two minor corrections: the cited source result is Proposition
2.1 rather than Proposition 3.1, and for $p>2$ the displayed Pauli matrices
in an $M_2$ corner of $M_p$ are norm-one self-adjoint elements, not unitaries
of $M_p$.
\end{abstract}

\section{The question and the answer}

Kerr and Li proved that the bilateral tensor shift on
$M_p^{\otimes \Z}$ has infinite CA entropy and then asked:

\begin{quote}
Is there a $*$-automorphism of $M_p^{\otimes\Z}$ (or any other simple AF
algebra) with finite nonzero CA entropy?
\end{quote}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

The following theorem answers the second alternative affirmatively.

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem]\label{thm:main}
Let $X$ be a Cantor set and let $T\colon X\to X$ be a minimal
homeomorphism.  There are a simple unital separable AF algebra $A$ and an
automorphism $\beta\in\Aut(A)$ such that
\[
   \hc(\beta)=\htop(T).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:all-values}
For every $r\in[0,\infty]$ there are a simple unital separable AF algebra
$A_r$ and an automorphism $\beta_r\in\Aut(A_r)$ satisfying
\[
   \hc(\beta_r)=r.
\]
In particular, simple AF algebras admit automorphisms with finite nonzero CA
entropy.
\end{corollary}

The proof is elementary once three permanence observations are isolated.

\begin{remark}[Scope]\label{rem:scope}
The construction settles the alternative ``or any other simple AF algebra.''
It does not by itself decide whether a prescribed UHF algebra
$M_p^{\otimes\Z}$ admits an automorphism with finite nonzero CA entropy.
\end{remark}

\section{Idea of the proof}

The construction turns topological entropy into CA entropy without using a
fixed tensor-product shift.  Start with a minimal Cantor homeomorphism
\(T\colon X\to X\).  At stage \(n\), use the matrix-valued Cantor algebra
\(M_{2^{n-1}}(C(X))\), and embed it into the next stage by placing the two
orbit samples \(a(x)\) and \(a(Tx)\) on diagonal blocks.  Minimality forces
every nonzero positive element to become full at a later stage, so the limit is
simple; zero-dimensionality of \(X\) makes the limit AF.  The automorphism
induced by composition with \(T\) has the same local entropy at every finite
stage, and locality of contractive rank plus Kerr--Li's tensor-product
subadditivity identify this value with \(\htop(T)\).  Finally, known Cantor
minimal systems realize all prescribed entropy values.

\section{Locality and invariant inductive limits}

We use Kerr--Li's notation $\operatorname{rc}_X(\Omega,\delta)$ for the
contractive rank of a finite set $\Omega$ in a Banach space $X$.

\begin{lemma}[Locality of contractive rank]\label{lem:locality}
Let $Y$ be a closed subspace of a Banach space $X$.  If
$\Omega\subseteq Y$ is finite and $\delta>0$, then
\[
  \operatorname{rc}_X(\Omega,\delta)
  =\operatorname{rc}_Y(\Omega,\delta).
\]
Consequently, if $\alpha$ is an isometric automorphism of $X$, $Y$ is
$\alpha$-invariant, and $\Omega\subseteq Y$, then
\[
  \hc(\alpha,\Omega)=\hc(\alpha|_Y,\Omega).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose a CA embedding $\iota\colon X\to Z$.  Its restriction to $Y$ is a CA
embedding.  Restricting the first map in any contractive approximation for
$X$ gives
$\operatorname{rc}_Y(\Omega,\delta)\leq
 \operatorname{rc}_X(\Omega,\delta)$.

Conversely, suppose
\[
  Y\xrightarrow{\phi}\ell_\infty^d
   \xrightarrow{\psi} Z
\]
is a contractive approximation to $\iota|_Y$ on $\Omega$.  The space
$\ell_\infty^d$ is injective, so $\phi$ extends to a contraction
$\widetilde\phi\colon X\to\ell_\infty^d$.  The pair
$(\widetilde\phi,\psi)$ gives the same approximation on $\Omega$, proving
the reverse inequality.  Applying this equality to orbit sets gives the
entropy assertion.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Increasing invariant unions]\label{prop:union}
Let $X_1\subseteq X_2\subseteq\cdots$ be closed invariant subspaces of a
Banach space $X$ with dense union, and let $\alpha$ be an isometric
automorphism of $X$ preserving every $X_n$.  Then
\[
  \hc(\alpha)=\sup_n \hc(\alpha|_{X_n}).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The inequality ``$\geq$'' is monotonicity.  For the converse, choose an
increasing sequence of finite subsets of $\bigcup_nX_n$ whose union is
dense.  Each such finite set is contained in one $X_n$, and its local
entropy in $X$ equals its local entropy in $X_n$ by
Lemma~\ref{lem:locality}.  Kerr--Li's Kolmogorov--Sinai-type exhaustion
property, Proposition 2.1(ii) of \cite{KerrLi}, now gives the result.
\end{proof}

\section{Finite matrix amplification of a Cantor system}

For a homeomorphism $T\colon X\to X$, write
$\alpha_T(f)=f\circ T$ on $C(X)$ and let
\[
  \alpha_T^{(d)}=\alpha_T\otimes\id_{M_d}
\]
under the identification $C(X,M_d)=C(X)\otimes M_d$.

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:matrix-amplification}
For every compact Hausdorff space $X$, every homeomorphism $T$ of $X$, and
every $d\geq1$,
\[
  \hc\bigl(\alpha_T^{(d)}\bigr)=\htop(T).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The invariant scalar subalgebra $C(X)\otimes 1_d$ gives
\[
  \hc\bigl(\alpha_T^{(d)}\bigr)
  \geq \hc(\alpha_T)=\htop(T),
\]
where the equality is Kerr--Li's Proposition 3.1.

As Banach spaces,
\[
  C(X,M_d)=C(X)\injtensor M_d,
\]
since one tensor factor is commutative (and $M_d$ is finite-dimensional).
Kerr--Li's injective-tensor-product subadditivity, Proposition 7.1, yields
\[
  \hc\bigl(\alpha_T^{(d)}\bigr)
  \leq \hc(\alpha_T)+\hc(\id_{M_d})
  =\htop(T),
\]
because every automorphism of a finite-dimensional Banach space has zero CA
entropy.
\end{proof}

\section{Construction of the simple AF algebra}

Fix a minimal homeomorphism $T\colon X\to X$ of a Cantor set.  For $n\geq1$
put
\[
  A_n=M_{2^{n-1}}(C(X))=C\bigl(X,M_{2^{n-1}}\bigr)
\]
and define a unital injective $*$-homomorphism
\[
  \varphi_n\colon A_n\longrightarrow A_{n+1},\qquad
  \varphi_n(a)(x)=
  \begin{pmatrix}
    a(x)&0\\
    0&a(Tx)
  \end{pmatrix}.
\]
Set
\[
  A=\varinjlim(A_n,\varphi_n).
\]

\subsection{The limit is AF}

Since $X$ is zero-dimensional, locally constant functions are dense in
$C(X)$.  Thus $C(X)$ is AF, each $A_n$ is AF, and an inductive limit of AF
algebras is AF.  The maps are unital and the system is countable, so $A$ is
unital and separable.

\subsection{The limit is simple}

For $m>n$, iteration of the connecting maps gives, up to a permutation of
matrix blocks,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:binomial-blocks}
 \varphi_{n,m}(a)(x)
 \cong
 \bigoplus_{j=0}^{m-n}
 a(T^j x)^{\oplus \binom{m-n}{j}}.
\end{equation}
Indeed, each further application replaces every block $b(x)$ by the two
blocks $b(x)$ and $b(Tx)$, producing Pascal's rule.

Let $0\neq a\in(A_n)_+$ and put
\[
  U=\{x\in X:a(x)\neq0\}.
\]
This is a nonempty open set.  Minimality implies that the forward orbit of
every point meets $U$, and compactness gives an $L\geq0$ such that
\[
  X=\bigcup_{j=0}^{L}T^{-j}(U).
\]
Taking $m\geq n+L$, formula \eqref{eq:binomial-blocks} shows that
$\varphi_{n,m}(a)(x)\neq0$ for every $x\in X$.  A positive element of
$C(X,M_k)$ is full exactly when it is nonzero at every point.  Hence every
nonzero positive element at any stage becomes full at a later stage.  The
standard simplicity criterion for injective inductive limits therefore
shows that $A$ is simple.

\subsection{The compatible automorphism}

Define
\[
  \beta_n(a)(x)=a(Tx),\qquad a\in A_n.
\]
Then
\[
 \varphi_n\circ\beta_n=\beta_{n+1}\circ\varphi_n,
\]
since both sides send $a$ to
$\operatorname{diag}(a(Tx),a(T^2x))$.  The $\beta_n$ therefore induce an
automorphism $\beta\in\Aut(A)$.

By Proposition~\ref{prop:matrix-amplification},
\[
  \hc(\beta_n)=\htop(T)
  \qquad\text{for every }n.
\]
The images of the $A_n$ form an increasing family of closed
$\beta$-invariant subspaces with dense union.  Proposition~\ref{prop:union}
now gives
\[
  \hc(\beta)
  =\sup_n\hc(\beta_n)
  =\htop(T).
\]
This proves Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:all-values}]
Boyle and Handelman constructed minimal homeomorphisms of Cantor sets with
every prescribed topological entropy in $[0,\infty]$; in fact these may all
be chosen strongly orbit equivalent to the dyadic adding machine
\cite{BoyleHandelman}.  Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.
\end{proof}

\section{Audit of the supplied product-type partial result}

The supplied theorem for factor-permuting product-type automorphisms is
correct.  Here are the points that need correction or clarification.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
\item The monotonicity and exhaustion result used in the zero-entropy case is
Kerr--Li Proposition 2.1, not Proposition 3.1.

\item If $p>2$, the two Pauli matrices placed in the upper-left $M_2$ corner
of $M_p$ are not unitaries of $M_p$: they have an additional zero block.
They are, however, norm-one self-adjoint elements, and for
$x=au+bv$ with $a^2+b^2=1$ the spectrum contains $1$.  This is all that the
argument needs.

\item In the infinite-orbit case, the product-state step can be stated
without invoking an infinite product state.  The finitely many distinct
tensor factors generated by the terms form a full matrix algebra.  Take the
tensor product of states attaining value $1$ on the chosen summands, and
extend that state from the finite-dimensional subalgebra to the whole
$C^*$-algebra.
\end{enumerate}

With these edits, the proof gives the claimed dichotomy $0$ or $\infty$ for
that product-type class.  It is fully compatible with the construction
above: our automorphism does not permute a fixed tensor decomposition into
matrix factors; it is assembled from diagonal orbit embeddings of
matrix-valued Cantor function algebras.

\section{Literature status}

Kerr--Li's paper itself gives arbitrary CA entropy values on the simple
Bunce--Deddens algebra, but that algebra is AT rather than AF.  The most
direct later AF result located in the literature search is Kerr's 2007
paper \cite{KerrAF}, which constructs a simple unital AF algebra for which
every non-approximately-inner automorphism has infinite CA entropy.  That
result does not provide a finite positive example.

Exact-phrase searches, citation-chain searches around \cite{KerrLi} and
\cite{KerrAF}, and a check of the recent revisit of noncommutative
approximation entropy in \cite{JacelonNeagu} did not locate a published
resolution of Question 9.4.  Thus no priority claim is made for the argument
above, but it appears to supply the missing affirmative construction.

\section{Human review recommendation}

This packet should be reviewed as a candidate full affirmative answer to the
``any other simple AF algebra'' branch of Kerr--Li Question 9.4.  The main
points to check are the locality lemma for contractive ranks, the entropy
calculation for finite matrix amplifications of \(C(X)\), and the simplicity
criterion for the diagonal Cantor inductive limit.  The packet deliberately
does not claim to settle the fixed-UHF question for \(M_p^{\otimes\Z}\).
