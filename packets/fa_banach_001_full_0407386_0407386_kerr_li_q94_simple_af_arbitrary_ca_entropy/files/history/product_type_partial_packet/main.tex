\section*{Source Question}

The source is David Kerr and Hanfeng Li,
\emph{Dynamical entropy in Banach spaces}, arXiv:math/0407386. Question 9.4
appears after their proof that the tensor product shift on
\(M_p^{\otimes \Z}\) has infinite contractive approximation entropy:

\begin{quote}
Is there a \(*\)-automorphism of \(M_p^{\otimes\Z}\) (or any other simple AF
algebra) with finite nonzero CA entropy?
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions and Source Facts}

We use Kerr--Li's notation \(\hc(\alpha)\) for the contractive approximation
entropy of an isometric automorphism \(\alpha\) of a Banach space.

Two source facts are used.

\begin{itemize}
\item Proposition 3.1: if \(Y\) is an \(\alpha\)-invariant closed subspace,
then \(\hc(\alpha|_Y)\leq \hc(\alpha)\), and \(\hc(\alpha)\) is computed as a
supremum over increasing finite sets whose \(\alpha\)-orbits are total.

\item Lemma 9.1: if a finite set \(\Omega\) of unit vectors satisfies
\(\norm{x+y}<2\) for distinct \(x,y\in\Omega\), and
\[
  \left\|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\alpha^k(x_k)\right\|=n
  \qquad (x_k\in\Omega,\ n\geq 1),
\]
then \(\hc(\alpha,\Omega)\geq \log |\Omega|\).
\end{itemize}

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

For a product-type automorphism, the tensor-factor permutation is the only
source of large orbit geometry. If all permutation orbits are finite, every
finite set of algebra elements lives, up to approximation, inside a
finite-dimensional invariant tensor block, so entropy is zero.

If there is an infinite tensor-factor orbit, a single matrix factor contains a
real two-dimensional Euclidean sphere of self-adjoint unitaries. Iterating
different choices from a finite subset of that sphere places them in distinct
commuting tensor factors. Product states then make the norm of every such
orbit sum exactly additive. Since the finite subset of the Euclidean sphere can
be chosen with arbitrarily large cardinality, Kerr--Li's Lemma 9.1 forces
arbitrarily large entropy lower bounds, hence infinite entropy.

\section*{The Partial Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(J\) be a countably infinite set, let \(p\geq 2\), and let
\[
  A=\bigotimes_{j\in J} M_p
\]
be the spatial infinite tensor product with respect to the units. Suppose
\(\alpha\in\Aut(A)\) is product-type in the following sense: there are a
permutation \(\sigma\) of \(J\) and \(*\)-automorphisms
\(\theta_j:M_p\to M_p\) such that \(\alpha\) sends the \(j\)-th tensor factor
onto the \(\sigma(j)\)-th tensor factor via \(\theta_j\). Then
\[
  \hc(\alpha)=
  \begin{cases}
  0, & \text{if every orbit of }\sigma\text{ is finite},\\
  \infty, & \text{if }\sigma\text{ has an infinite orbit}.
  \end{cases}
\]
In particular, no such product-type automorphism has finite nonzero CA
entropy.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First suppose every orbit of \(\sigma\) is finite. Let \(F\subset J\) be a
finite union of \(\sigma\)-orbits. The finite tensor product
\[
  A_F=\bigotimes_{j\in F} M_p
\]
is finite-dimensional and \(\alpha\)-invariant. The union of these \(A_F\)'s,
over finite unions of orbits, is dense in \(A\). On a finite-dimensional
invariant subspace the orbit of each finite set admits a fixed finite-rank
contractive approximation dimension independent of the orbit length, so the
corresponding CA entropy is zero. Proposition 3.1(ii) from Kerr--Li therefore
gives \(\hc(\alpha)=0\).

Now suppose \(\sigma\) has an infinite orbit. Choose \(j_0\in J\) whose
forward iterates \(\sigma^k(j_0)\), \(k\geq0\), are distinct. Inside the
\(j_0\)-th copy of \(M_p\), identify an \(M_2\) corner and let
\[
  u=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix},
  \qquad
  v=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]
The real span \(E=\mathrm{span}_{\mathbb R}\{u,v\}\) is isometric to
\(\ell_2^2\) on self-adjoint elements: for real \(a,b\),
\(\norm{au+bv}=(a^2+b^2)^{1/2}\). Let \(\Omega\) be any finite subset of the
unit circle of \(E\).

For distinct \(x,y\in\Omega\), the Euclidean identity gives
\(\norm{x+y}<2\). We claim that \(\Omega\) satisfies the additivity hypothesis
of Kerr--Li Lemma 9.1. Given \(x_0,\ldots,x_{n-1}\in\Omega\), the element
\(\alpha^k(x_k)\) lies in the tensor factor indexed by \(\sigma^k(j_0)\), and
these tensor factors are distinct. Each \(\alpha^k(x_k)\) is self-adjoint of
norm one. For each \(k\), choose a state \(\varphi_k\) on that matrix factor
with \(\varphi_k(\alpha^k(x_k))=1\), and take arbitrary states on the remaining
factors. The resulting product state \(\varphi\) satisfies
\[
  \varphi\left(\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\alpha^k(x_k)\right)=n.
\]
The triangle inequality gives the reverse upper bound, hence
\[
  \left\|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\alpha^k(x_k)\right\|=n.
\]
Lemma 9.1 now yields \(\hc(\alpha,\Omega)\geq \log|\Omega|\). Since finite
subsets \(\Omega\) of the unit circle can have arbitrarily large cardinality,
\(\hc(\alpha)=\infty\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations and Novelty Check}

This is not a full answer to Question 9.4. It handles the natural
permutation-product automorphisms of countable UHF tensor products, including
the source paper's tensor shift, but it does not address general AF
automorphisms which mix Bratteli levels or act through more subtle
approximately inner mechanisms.

The run's lightweight indexes were searched for arXiv:0407386, the paper
title, `CA entropy', `simple AF algebra', `finite nonzero CA entropy', and
related UHF/product-type keywords. No prior packet or attempt for this target
was found. Web searches for exact CA-entropy/simple-AF phrases found the source
paper and no later direct resolution during this pass.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

The main point for review is the scope of ``product-type''. The proof is
intended only for automorphisms induced by a tensor-factor permutation and
local matrix-factor automorphisms. Check the finite-orbit zero-entropy
reduction and the product-state norm-additivity argument in the infinite-orbit
case.
