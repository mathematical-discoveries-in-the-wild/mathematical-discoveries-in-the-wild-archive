\section*{Source and Target}

The source is Max Sch\"olpple and Ingo Steinwart,
\emph{Which Spaces can be Embedded in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces?},
arXiv:2312.14711 \cite{SS}. The paper proves that, for Banach function
spaces \(E\hookrightarrow F\), an intermediate reproducing kernel Hilbert
space \(E\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow F\) exists if and only if the
inclusion \(E\hookrightarrow F\) is 2-factorable. It also records the
necessary consequence that such an inclusion must be of cotype \(2\).

For bounded \(X\subset\R^d\) with nonempty interior, the source proves that
no RKHS sandwich
\[
  \operatorname{Hol}^{\alpha}(X)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Hol}^{\beta}(X)
\]
can exist when \(\alpha-\beta<d/2\). In the Besov/Triebel-Lizorkin section,
it proves positive RKHS sandwiches when the smoothness gap is strictly larger
than \(d/2\), and identifies the critical line
\(\alpha-\beta=d/2\) as a border case.

\section*{Critical Border Map}

Write
\[
  \Lam^s([0,1]^d)=B^s_{\infty,\infty}([0,1]^d)
\]
for the Holder-Zygmund space. For noninteger
\(s=n+\theta\), \(n\in\N_0\), \(0<\theta<1\), this is the usual classical
Holder space \(C^{n,\theta}\), up to equivalence of norms. For integer
\(s\), \(\Lam^s\) is the Zygmund endpoint, not the smaller classical space
\(C^s\) and not \(C^{s-1,1}\).

\begin{theorem}[Holder-Zygmund critical line]\label{thm:zygmund-critical}
Let \(d\ge1\), \(\beta>0\), and
\[
  \alpha=\beta+d/2.
\]
Then the inclusion
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
is not cotype \(2\). Consequently, there is no reproducing kernel Hilbert
space \(H\) on \([0,1]^d\) such that
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d).
\]
\end{theorem}

This settles the critical Holder-Zygmund/Besov \(B^s_{\infty,\infty}\) line
negatively, including integer values of \(\alpha\). The integer endpoint is
convention-sensitive, however:

\begin{proposition}[Classical integer domain: positive Zygmund target]\label{prop:classical-positive}
Let \(m\in\N\), \(d<2m\), and put \(\beta=m-d/2>0\). Then the Sobolev
space \(H^m([0,1]^d)\) is a bounded-kernel RKHS and
\[
  C^m([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H^m([0,1]^d)
  \hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d).
\]
The same conclusion holds with the larger classical Lipschitz endpoint
\(C^{m-1,1}([0,1]^d)\) in place of \(C^m([0,1]^d)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Purely classical Holder convention]\label{thm:classical-classification}
Let \(d\ge1\), \(\beta>0\), and \(\alpha=\beta+d/2\). Interpret
\(C^s\) classically: \(C^s=C^{\lfloor s\rfloor,s-\lfloor s\rfloor}\) for
noninteger \(s\), and \(C^s\) is the usual space of \(s\)-times continuously
differentiable functions when \(s\in\N\). Then there exists an RKHS \(H\)
with
\[
  C^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow C^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
if and only if \(\alpha\in\N\) and \(\beta\notin\N\). Equivalently, the
positive classical cases are exactly the odd-dimensional critical endpoints
with integer domain smoothness.

More precisely, if \(\alpha=m\in\N\) and \(\beta=m-d/2\notin\N\), then
\[
  C^{m-1,1}([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H^m([0,1]^d)
  \hookrightarrow C^\beta([0,1]^d).
\]
If \(\alpha=m\in\N\) and \(\beta=\ell\in\N_0\), then no RKHS \(H\) satisfies
\[
  C^m([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow C^\ell([0,1]^d),
\]
and the same negative conclusion holds with \(C^{m-1,1}\) in place of
\(C^m\).
\end{theorem}

Thus Theorem \ref{thm:zygmund-critical} does not contradict the familiar
one-dimensional positive endpoint
\[
  C^1([0,1])\hookrightarrow H^1([0,1])\hookrightarrow C^{1/2}([0,1]).
\]
That positive result uses the smaller classical integer domain; the full
Zygmund domain \(\Lam^1=B^1_{\infty,\infty}\) is too large.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The negative theorem is a multiscale cotype obstruction. At dyadic level
\(j\), place \(2^{jd}\) separated smooth bumps of height \(2^{-j\alpha}\).
In the target \(\Lam^\beta\), each bump has norm comparable from below to
\(2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)}\). Since \(\alpha-\beta=d/2\), one whole dyadic
level contributes a fixed positive amount to the square-sum appearing in
cotype \(2\).

The point that closes the integer endpoint is to estimate the domain norm by
finite differences. If \(r>\alpha\), then the \(r\)-th finite difference of
the level-\(j\) part is bounded by
\[
  C\min\{2^{-j\alpha},\, |h|^r2^{j(r-\alpha)}\}.
\]
Summing this estimate over all levels gives \(O(|h|^\alpha)\), uniformly in
all signs and all finite sets of bumps. Hence the Rademacher norm in
\(\Lam^\alpha\) is uniformly bounded, while the \(\Lam^\beta\) square-sum
over the first \(m\) levels grows like \(\sqrt m\). This violates cotype
\(2\), and the source's 2-factorability theorem excludes every intermediate
RKHS.

The positive classical-integer statement is the standard Sobolev route:
\(C^m\) and \(C^{m-1,1}\) embed into \(H^m=W^{m,2}\), while the critical
Besov embedding sends \(H^m=B^m_{2,2}\) into
\(B^{m-d/2}_{\infty,\infty}=\Lam^{m-d/2}\). If \(m-d/2\) is noninteger,
this target is the classical Holder space \(C^{m-d/2}\).

The remaining classical integer-target obstruction is Fourier-analytic. If
the target is \(C^\ell\), then \(\ell\)-th derivative evaluations are bounded
on \(H\). Applied to characters \(e_n(x)=\exp(2\pi i n\cdot x)\), those
derivatives contribute a factor of order \(|n|^\ell\). The domain
\(C^m\), or even \(C^{m-1,1}\), gives only \(\|e_n\|\lesssim |n|^m\). At the
critical gap \(m-\ell=d/2\), Bessel's inequality would force the divergent
series \(\sum_{n\ne0}|n|^{-d}\) to be bounded.

\section*{Finite Difference Norms}

Let \(r\in\N\). For a function \(f\) on \([0,1]^d\), define
\[
  \Delta_h^r f(x)
  =
  \sum_{\nu=0}^r(-1)^{r-\nu}\binom r\nu f(x+\nu h),
\]
where only admissible \(x,h\) for which all points
\(x,x+h,\ldots,x+rh\) lie in \([0,1]^d\) are used. If \(r>s>0\), the
Holder-Zygmund norm is equivalent to
\[
  \norm{f}_{\Lam^s}
  \simeq
  \norm{f}_{\infty}
  +\sup_{0<\abs h\le1}\abs h^{-s}
    \norm{\Delta_h^r f}_{\infty}.
\]
This is the standard finite-difference characterization of
\(B^s_{\infty,\infty}\) on cubes; see, for example, Triebel's function-space
references \cite{Triebel83}.

\section*{Multiscale Bump Lemmas}

Fix a nonzero function \(\phi\in C_c^\infty((0,1/4)^d)\). For
\(j\ge0\) and \(k=(k_1,\ldots,k_d)\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\), define
\[
  \phi_{j,k}(x)=2^{-j\alpha}\phi(2^j x-k),
  \qquad x\in[0,1]^d.
\]
For each fixed \(j\), the supports of the functions \(\phi_{j,k}\) are
separated, and there are \(2^{jd}\) of them.

\begin{lemma}[Uniform signed domain bound]\label{lem:domain}
For every \(\alpha>0\) there is a constant \(C_\alpha\) such that for every
finite set
\[
  A\subset\{(j,k):j\ge0,\ k\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\}
\]
and every choice of signs \(\eps_{j,k}\in\{-1,1\}\),
\[
  \left\|
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}
  \right\|_{\Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)}
  \le C_\alpha .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For each level \(j\), set
\[
  g_j=\sum_{k:(j,k)\in A}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}.
\]
Because the supports at a fixed level are separated, for every integer
\(r>\alpha\),
\[
  \norm{g_j}_\infty\le C2^{-j\alpha},
  \qquad
  \norm{D^r g_j}_\infty\le C_r2^{j(r-\alpha)}.
\]
Thus for every admissible \(h\),
\[
  \norm{\Delta_h^r g_j}_\infty
  \le
  C_r\min\{2^{-j\alpha},\,\abs h^r2^{j(r-\alpha)}\}.
\]
The first bound is the trivial finite-difference bound by the sup norm, and
the second follows from the integral representation of the \(r\)-th finite
difference in terms of \(D^r g_j\).

Let \(0<\abs h\le1\), and choose \(J\ge0\) with
\[
  2^{-(J+1)}<\abs h\le2^{-J},
\]
with the evident modification for \(\abs h>1/2\). Then
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \sum_{j\ge0}\norm{\Delta_h^r g_j}_\infty
  &\le
  C_r\abs h^r\sum_{j\le J}2^{j(r-\alpha)}
  +C_r\sum_{j>J}2^{-j\alpha}  \\
  &\le C_{\alpha,r}\abs h^\alpha .
\end{aligned}
\]
The sup norm is also uniformly bounded:
\[
  \left\|\sum_j g_j\right\|_\infty
  \le C\sum_{j\ge0}2^{-j\alpha}<\infty .
\]
The finite-difference norm equivalence proves the lemma.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Target lower bound]\label{lem:target}
For every \(\beta>0\) there is \(c_\beta>0\) such that, for all \(j,k\),
\[
  \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)}
  \ge c_\beta\,2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose an integer \(r>\beta\). Since \(\phi\) is nonzero and compactly
supported, its \(r\)-th finite differences are not identically zero. Hence
there exist \(x_0\in(0,1/4)^d\), \(h_0\in\R^d\), and \(\eta>0\) such that
the points \(x_0,x_0+h_0,\ldots,x_0+rh_0\) remain in \((0,1/4)^d\) and
\[
  \abs{\Delta_{h_0}^r\phi(x_0)}\ge\eta .
\]
Put \(x_{j,k}=2^{-j}(k+x_0)\) and \(h_j=2^{-j}h_0\). Scaling gives
\[
  \Delta_{h_j}^r\phi_{j,k}(x_{j,k})
  =
  2^{-j\alpha}\Delta_{h_0}^r\phi(x_0).
\]
Therefore
\[
  \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta}
  \ge
  c\abs{h_j}^{-\beta}
  \abs{\Delta_{h_j}^r\phi_{j,k}(x_{j,k})}
  \ge
  c_\beta2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)} .
\]
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:zygmund-critical}}

Let
\[
  A_m=\{(j,k):0\le j<m,\ k\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\}.
\]
By Lemma \ref{lem:domain}, for independent Rademacher signs
\(\eps_{j,k}\),
\[
  \mathbb{E}
  \left\|
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A_m}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}
  \right\|_{\Lam^\alpha}
  \le C_\alpha .
\]
On the other hand, Lemma \ref{lem:target} and the critical identity
\(\alpha-\beta=d/2\) give
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \left(
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A_m}
    \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta}^2
  \right)^{1/2}
  &\ge
  c_\beta
  \left(
    \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}2^{jd}2^{-2j(\alpha-\beta)}
  \right)^{1/2} \\
  &=c_\beta\sqrt m .
\end{aligned}
\]
Thus the cotype-2 constants of the inclusion
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow\Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
are unbounded. The inclusion is not cotype \(2\).

If an RKHS \(H\) satisfied
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow\Lam^\beta([0,1]^d),
\]
then Sch\"olpple and Steinwart's 2-factorization theorem would make the
inclusion \(\Lam^\alpha\hookrightarrow\Lam^\beta\) 2-factorable
\cite[Theorem 6]{SS}. Their type/cotype obstruction says every
2-factorable inclusion is cotype \(2\) \cite[Lemma 5 and Corollary 8]{SS}.
This contradiction proves the theorem.

\section*{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:classical-positive}}

Assume \(m\in\N\) and \(d<2m\). Then \(m>d/2\), so the Sobolev space
\[
  H^m([0,1]^d)=W^{m,2}([0,1]^d)
\]
embeds continuously into \(C^0([0,1]^d)\). Point evaluations are therefore
bounded, and \(H^m([0,1]^d)\) is a bounded-kernel RKHS on the compact cube.

The inclusion
\[
  C^m([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H^m([0,1]^d)
\]
is immediate because all weak derivatives up to order \(m\) are bounded on
the cube. The same is true for \(C^{m-1,1}\), since its derivatives of order
\(m\) exist weakly and belong to \(L^\infty\).

Finally, the standard critical Besov embedding gives
\[
  H^m([0,1]^d)=B^m_{2,2}([0,1]^d)
  \hookrightarrow
  B^{m-d/2}_{\infty,2}([0,1]^d)
  \hookrightarrow
  B^{m-d/2}_{\infty,\infty}([0,1]^d)
  =
  \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d),
\]
where \(\beta=m-d/2>0\). This proves the asserted RKHS sandwich.
\qed

\section*{Derivative Character Obstruction}

The next lemma handles the classical integer target \(C^\ell\). It is a
derivative version of the finite-character argument used for the
bounded-kernel endpoint.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:derivative-characters}
Let \(m,\ell\in\N_0\), and let \(E\) be a Banach space of functions on
\([0,1]^d\) containing the characters
\[
  e_n(x)=\exp(2\pi i n\cdot x),\qquad n\in\mathbb Z^d.
\]
Assume that for some \(M>0\),
\[
  \norm{e_n}_E\le M(1+\abs n)^m,\qquad n\in\mathbb Z^d.
\]
If the inclusion \(E\hookrightarrow C^\ell([0,1]^d)\) factors through a
Hilbert space, then
\[
  \sum_{n\in\mathbb Z^d\setminus\{0\}}
  \frac{\abs{n}^{2\ell}}{(1+\abs{n})^{2m}}
\]
has uniformly bounded finite partial sums. In particular, if
\(m-\ell=d/2\), no such Hilbert factorization exists.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose there are a Hilbert space \(\mathcal H\) and bounded linear maps
\[
  J:E\to\mathcal H,\qquad R:\mathcal H\to C^\ell([0,1]^d)
\]
such that \(RJf=f\) for all \(f\in E\). Put \(a=\norm J\) and
\(b=\norm R\). Real spaces may be complexified, so it suffices to argue over
complex scalars. We work on the torus through the standard half-open section
\(\sigma:\mathbb T^d\to[0,1]^d\).

For every multi-index \(\gamma\) with \(|\gamma|=\ell\) and every
\(t\in\mathbb T^d\), the functional
\[
  h\longmapsto D^\gamma Rh(\sigma(t))
\]
has norm at most \(b\). Let \(v_t^\gamma\in\mathcal H\) be its Riesz
representative, so that
\[
  D^\gamma Rh(\sigma(t))=\langle h,v_t^\gamma\rangle_{\mathcal H},
  \qquad \norm{v_t^\gamma}\le b .
\]

Fix a finite \(F\subset\mathbb Z^d\setminus\{0\}\), and let
\[
  E_F=\operatorname{span}\{Je_n:n\in F\}\subset\mathcal H
\]
with orthogonal projection \(P_F\). For \(n\in F\), define
\[
  u_n^\gamma=\int_{\mathbb T^d} e_n(t)\,P_Fv_t^\gamma\,dt .
\]
Vector-valued Parseval/Bessel gives, for each fixed \(\gamma\),
\[
  \sum_{n\in F}\norm{u_n^\gamma}_{\mathcal H}^2
  \le b^2 .
\]
Since \(Je_n\in E_F\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \langle Je_n,u_n^\gamma\rangle
  &=
  \int_{\mathbb T^d}
  D^\gamma RJe_n(\sigma(t))\,\overline{e_n(t)}\,dt  \\
  &=
  (2\pi i n)^\gamma .
\end{aligned}
\]
Therefore
\[
  \norm{u_n^\gamma}
  \ge
  \frac{(2\pi)^\ell\abs{n^\gamma}}
       {aM(1+\abs n)^m}.
\]
Consequently, for every \(\gamma\) with \(|\gamma|=\ell\),
\[
  \sum_{n\in F}
  \frac{\abs{n^\gamma}^2}{(1+\abs n)^{2m}}
  \le C_{\gamma}a^2b^2M^2 .
\]
Summing over all \(|\gamma|=\ell\), and using
\[
  \sum_{|\gamma|=\ell}\abs{n^\gamma}^2
  \ge c_{d,\ell}\abs n^{2\ell}
\]
with the evident interpretation when \(\ell=0\), yields the asserted
uniform finite-partial-sum bound.

If \(m-\ell=d/2\), the lower estimate
\[
  \frac{\abs{n}^{2\ell}}{(1+\abs{n})^{2m}}
  \gtrsim \abs{n}^{-d}
\]
on dyadic annuli contradicts the divergence of
\(\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z^d\setminus\{0\}}\abs{n}^{-d}\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:classical-classification}}

If \(\alpha\notin\N\), then \(C^\alpha=\Lam^\alpha\). Since
\(C^\beta\hookrightarrow\Lam^\beta\) for every \(\beta>0\), any RKHS
sandwich
\[
  C^\alpha\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow C^\beta
\]
would also give
\[
  \Lam^\alpha\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow\Lam^\beta,
\]
contradicting Theorem \ref{thm:zygmund-critical}. Hence no positive
classical case exists with noninteger \(\alpha\).

Now let \(\alpha=m\in\N\). If \(\beta=m-d/2\notin\N\), then
\(\Lam^\beta=C^\beta\), and Proposition \ref{prop:classical-positive} gives
the positive sandwich
\[
  C^{m-1,1}\hookrightarrow H^m\hookrightarrow C^\beta .
\]
In particular, the same holds with the smaller domain \(C^m\).

It remains to exclude the case \(\beta=\ell\in\N_0\). Then
\[
  m-\ell=d/2.
\]
For both \(E=C^m([0,1]^d)\) and \(E=C^{m-1,1}([0,1]^d)\), the characters
satisfy
\[
  \norm{e_n}_E\le M(1+\abs n)^m .
\]
Lemma \ref{lem:derivative-characters} therefore rules out every Hilbert
factorization of \(E\hookrightarrow C^\ell([0,1]^d)\), and in particular
rules out every intermediate RKHS. This proves the classification.

\section*{Consequences and Scope}

The critical line is now fully settled for the Holder-Zygmund scale
\(\Lam^s=B^s_{\infty,\infty}\): at
\[
  \alpha-\beta=d/2,\qquad \beta>0,
\]
there is no intermediate RKHS. This includes integer \(\alpha\), closing the
gap left by the earlier noninteger-only multiscale argument.

At integer smoothness, however, ``Holder'' has several common meanings. The
Zygmund endpoint \(B^m_{\infty,\infty}\) is larger than \(C^m\) and
\(C^{m-1,1}\). Theorem \ref{thm:classical-classification} gives the complete
classical convention map: the purely classical critical sandwich is positive
exactly when the domain exponent is an integer but the target exponent is not
an integer. Equivalently, this positive case occurs in odd dimensions with
integer domain smoothness. When both endpoint exponents are integers, the
derivative-character obstruction gives a negative answer.

Together with the companion one-dimensional metric Holder packet, this
explains the apparent endpoint split: the classical
\(C^1([0,1])\to H^1([0,1])\to C^{1/2}([0,1])\) sandwich is real, but it does
not extend to the larger Zygmund domain \(\Lam^1([0,1])\).

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof is noncomputational. The main verification points are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the finite-difference estimate in Lemma \ref{lem:domain}, which is the
      step that handles integer \(\alpha\);
\item the target lower bound in Lemma \ref{lem:target};
\item the critical count \(2^{jd}2^{-2j(\alpha-\beta)}=1\);
\item the source paper's implication from RKHS sandwich to 2-factorability
      and hence cotype \(2\);
\item the standard Sobolev/Besov embedding used in Proposition
      \ref{prop:classical-positive};
\item the derivative-character obstruction in Lemma
      \ref{lem:derivative-characters}, which rules out the classical
      integer-target case.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review as a likely valid full result for the Holder-Zygmund/Besov critical
line and for the purely classical endpoint convention. The key checks are
that the finite-difference proof of Lemma \ref{lem:domain} really gives a
uniform \(\Lam^\alpha\) bound for arbitrary signs and arbitrary finite
collections of dyadic bumps, and that Lemma \ref{lem:derivative-characters}
correctly converts \(C^\ell\)-target derivative evaluations into the critical
lattice-sum contradiction.
