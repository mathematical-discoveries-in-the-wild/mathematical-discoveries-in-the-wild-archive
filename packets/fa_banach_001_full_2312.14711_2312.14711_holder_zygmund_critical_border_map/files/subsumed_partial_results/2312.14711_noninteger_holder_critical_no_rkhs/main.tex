\section*{Source and Target}

The source is Max Sch\"olpple and Ingo Steinwart,
\emph{Which Spaces can be Embedded in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces?},
arXiv:2312.14711 \cite{SS}. The paper proves that the existence of an
intermediate reproducing kernel Hilbert space \(H\) between two Banach spaces
of functions \(E\hookrightarrow F\) is equivalent, in the Banach function
space setting considered there, to the 2-factorability of the inclusion
\(E\hookrightarrow F\). It also records the necessary consequence that such
an inclusion must be of cotype \(2\).

For Holder spaces over bounded subsets of \(\R^d\) with nonempty interior,
the source proves that no RKHS sandwich
\[
  \operatorname{Hol}^{\alpha}(X)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Hol}^{\beta}(X)
\]
can exist when \(\alpha-\beta<d/2\). In the higher smoothness
Holder-Zygmund/Besov scale it proves positive results when
\(\alpha-\beta>d/2\), and identifies the critical line
\(\alpha-\beta=d/2\) as a remaining border case.

\section*{Result}

This packet proves a broad negative result on that critical line. We write
\(\Lam^s([0,1]^d)\) for the Holder-Zygmund space \(B^s_{\infty,\infty}\);
for noninteger \(s=m+\theta\), \(m\in\N_0\), \(0<\theta<1\), this is the
usual classical Holder space \(C^{m,\theta}\), up to equivalence of norms.
One concrete equivalent seminorm is given by finite differences:
if \(r>s\) is an integer and
\[
  \Delta_h^r f(x)=\sum_{\nu=0}^r (-1)^{r-\nu}\binom r\nu f(x+\nu h),
\]
where only admissible \(x,h\) with all points \(x,\ldots,x+rh\) in
\([0,1]^d\) are used, then
\[
  \norm{f}_{\Lam^s}
  \simeq
  \norm{f}_{\infty}
  + \sup_{0<\abs{h}\le 1}\abs{h}^{-s}
    \norm{\Delta_h^r f}_{\infty}.
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:noninteger-critical}
Let \(d\ge1\), \(\beta>0\), and
\[
  \alpha=\beta+d/2.
\]
Assume that \(\alpha\notin \N\). Then the inclusion
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
is not cotype \(2\). Consequently, there is no reproducing kernel Hilbert
space \(H\) on \([0,1]^d\) such that
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d).
\]
\end{theorem}

Since \(\alpha\) is noninteger, the domain in Theorem
\ref{thm:noninteger-critical} is the ordinary classical Holder space
\(C^\alpha([0,1]^d)\). The target exponent \(\beta\) may be integer or
noninteger; the Holder-Zygmund formulation is used so that integer target
endpoints are included in the same statement.

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The source's metric packing obstruction tests one scale at a time. At the
critical line \(\alpha-\beta=d/2\), a single dyadic scale is exactly balanced:
there are \(2^{jd}\) available bumps at scale \(2^{-j}\), and each bump has
target norm of size \(2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)}=2^{-jd/2}\). Thus one level
contributes a fixed amount to the cotype square-sum.

The extra observation is that one may stack all dyadic levels. If
\(\alpha\) is noninteger, signed smooth bumps of height \(2^{-j\alpha}\)
at every level remain uniformly bounded in \(C^\alpha\). The lower levels
are controlled by their Lipschitz constants, and the higher levels by their
small sup norms. But in \(\Lam^\beta\) the square-sum receives a fixed
positive contribution from every level, so the first \(m\) levels force a
\(\sqrt m\) lower bound. This contradicts cotype \(2\), and the source's
2-factorability theorem then excludes an intermediate RKHS.

\section*{Multiscale Bump Lemma}

Fix a nonzero function \(\phi\in C_c^\infty((0,1/4)^d)\). For
\(j\ge0\) and \(k=(k_1,\ldots,k_d)\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\), set
\[
  \phi_{j,k}(x)=2^{-j\alpha}\phi(2^j x-k),
  \qquad x\in[0,1]^d.
\]
The supports of \(\{\phi_{j,k}\}_k\) are separated within each fixed level
\(j\), and there are \(2^{jd}\) of them.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:signed-domain-bound}
Let \(\alpha=n+\theta\) with \(n\in\N_0\) and \(0<\theta<1\). There exists a
constant \(C_\alpha\) such that for every finite set
\[
  A\subset \{(j,k):j\ge0,\ k\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\}
\]
and every choice of signs \(\eps_{j,k}\in\{-1,1\}\),
\[
  \left\|
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}
  \right\|_{C^{n,\theta}([0,1]^d)}
  \le C_\alpha .
\]
Consequently the same signed sums are uniformly bounded in
\(\Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For a fixed level \(j\), define
\[
  g_j=\sum_{k:(j,k)\in A}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}.
\]
Let \(\rho\) be a multi-index with \(\abs{\rho}\le n\). Since supports at
level \(j\) are separated and each scaled bump is supported in a cube of
side length \(2^{-j}/4\),
\[
  \norm{D^\rho g_j}_\infty
  \le C_\rho 2^{-j(\alpha-\abs{\rho})}.
\]
For \(\abs{\rho}=n\), the same scaling and the separation of supports give
the global Lipschitz estimate
\[
  \operatorname{Lip}(D^\rho g_j)
  \le C_\rho 2^{j(1-\theta)}.
\]
Indeed, points in the same support are controlled by differentiating one more
time, points in different supports are separated by a distance comparable to
\(2^{-j}\), and outside the supports the derivative is zero.

The lower-order \(C^n\) part is bounded because
\[
  \sum_{j\ge0}2^{-j(\alpha-\abs{\rho})}<\infty
  \qquad (\abs{\rho}\le n).
\]
It remains to control the \(\theta\)-Holder seminorms of the derivatives
\(D^\rho\) with \(\abs{\rho}=n\). Let \(x,y\in[0,1]^d\), \(x\ne y\), and put
\(h=\abs{x-y}\). Choose \(J\ge0\) so that
\[
  2^{-(J+1)}<h\le 2^{-J},
\]
with the evident modification if \(h>1/2\). For the low levels \(j\le J\),
the Lipschitz estimate gives
\[
  \sum_{j\le J}\abs{D^\rho g_j(x)-D^\rho g_j(y)}
  \le
  C h \sum_{j\le J}2^{j(1-\theta)}
  \le C_\theta h^\theta .
\]
For the high levels \(j>J\), the sup-norm estimate gives
\[
  \sum_{j>J}\abs{D^\rho g_j(x)-D^\rho g_j(y)}
  \le
  C\sum_{j>J}2^{-j\theta}
  \le C_\theta h^\theta .
\]
This proves the uniform \(C^{n,\theta}\) bound. The equivalence
\(C^{n,\theta}=\Lam^\alpha\) for noninteger \(\alpha\) gives the final
assertion.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:target-lower-bound}
For every \(\beta>0\) there exists \(c_\beta>0\) such that, for all \(j,k\),
\[
  \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)}
  \ge c_\beta\,2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose an integer \(r>\beta\). Since \(\phi\) is nonzero and compactly
supported, its \(r\)-th finite differences are not identically zero. Hence
there exist \(x_0\in(0,1/4)^d\), a vector \(h_0\in\R^d\), and a number
\(\eta>0\) such that all points \(x_0,\ldots,x_0+rh_0\) remain in
\((0,1/4)^d\) and
\[
  \abs{\Delta_{h_0}^r\phi(x_0)}\ge \eta .
\]
For each \(j,k\), put \(x_{j,k}=2^{-j}(k+x_0)\) and
\(h_j=2^{-j}h_0\). Then all points used in the finite difference of
\(\phi_{j,k}\) are admissible, and scaling gives
\[
  \Delta_{h_j}^r\phi_{j,k}(x_{j,k})
  =
  2^{-j\alpha}\Delta_{h_0}^r\phi(x_0).
\]
Therefore the finite-difference norm of \(\Lam^\beta\) yields
\[
  \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta}
  \ge
  c \abs{h_j}^{-\beta}
  \abs{\Delta_{h_j}^r\phi_{j,k}(x_{j,k})}
  \ge
  c_\beta 2^{j\beta}2^{-j\alpha}
  =
  c_\beta 2^{-j(\alpha-\beta)}.
\]
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:noninteger-critical}}

Let
\[
  A_m=\{(j,k):0\le j<m,\ k\in\{0,\ldots,2^j-1\}^d\}.
\]
By Lemma \ref{lem:signed-domain-bound}, for independent Rademacher signs
\(\eps_{j,k}\),
\[
  \mathbb{E}
  \left\|
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A_m}\eps_{j,k}\phi_{j,k}
  \right\|_{\Lam^\alpha}
  \le C_\alpha .
\]
On the other hand, Lemma \ref{lem:target-lower-bound} and the critical
identity \(\alpha-\beta=d/2\) give
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \left(
    \sum_{(j,k)\in A_m}
    \norm{\phi_{j,k}}_{\Lam^\beta}^2
  \right)^{1/2}
  &\ge
  c_\beta
  \left(
    \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}2^{jd}2^{-2j(\alpha-\beta)}
  \right)^{1/2}  \\
  &=
  c_\beta
  \left(
    \sum_{j=0}^{m-1}1
  \right)^{1/2}
  =
  c_\beta\sqrt m .
\end{aligned}
\]
Thus the cotype-2 constants of the inclusion
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
are at least \(c_\beta C_\alpha^{-1}\sqrt m\) for every \(m\), and so the
inclusion is not cotype \(2\).

By the source paper's 2-factorization theorem, an RKHS sandwich
\[
  \Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H
  \hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)
\]
would make the inclusion
\(\Lam^\alpha([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow \Lam^\beta([0,1]^d)\) 2-factorable
\cite[Theorem 6]{SS}. By the source paper's type/cotype obstruction, every
2-factorable inclusion is cotype \(2\) \cite[Lemma 5 and Corollary 8]{SS}.
This contradicts the preceding paragraph and proves the theorem.

\section*{Limitations and Remaining Border}

The assumption \(\alpha\notin\N\) is essential for the present argument: it
is exactly what gives the uniform signed multiscale bound in the classical
Holder domain norm. The remaining critical cases are the integer-domain
smoothness endpoints. They are not a formal afterthought. In dimension one,
the endpoint \(\alpha=1\), \(\beta=1/2\) is positive, since
\[
  C^1([0,1])\hookrightarrow H^1([0,1])
  \hookrightarrow C^{1/2}([0,1])
\]
and \(H^1([0,1])\) is a bounded-kernel RKHS. Thus the integer line requires
separate tools and is the next genuine frontier.

\section*{Verification and Novelty Check}

The proof is noncomputational. The verification points are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the signed multiscale bump family is uniformly bounded in
      \(C^\alpha=\Lam^\alpha\) for noninteger \(\alpha\);
\item every bump has the stated \(\Lam^\beta\) lower bound, including integer
      \(\beta\), by finite differences;
\item the critical identity \(\alpha-\beta=d/2\) makes every dyadic level
      contribute a fixed positive amount to the target square-sum;
\item Sch\"olpple and Steinwart's RKHS theorem converts any sandwich into a
      2-factorization, hence into a cotype-2 inclusion.
\end{enumerate}
