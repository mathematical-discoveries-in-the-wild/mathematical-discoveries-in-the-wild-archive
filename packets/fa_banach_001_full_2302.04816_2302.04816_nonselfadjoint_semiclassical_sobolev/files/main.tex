\section*{Status and source question}

This packet gives a likely valid full affirmative answer to the conjecture in
Figure~2 of Lafleche, arXiv:2302.04816v3~\cite{Lafleche}.  In the source,
Theorem~1 proves maximal Sobolev nonregularity for self-adjoint projections.
For non-self-adjoint idempotents it requires either
$p\ge 2d/(d+s)$ or, in the remaining region, a uniform
$\mathcal L^{2+\varepsilon}$ bound.  The caption on page~7 says:
``In this case, Theorem~1 needs more assumptions than a trace condition to be
valid in the top-right zone. We conjecture however that the result holds there
as well.''

Let $\T_z A=W_z A W_z^*$ denote the source's quantum phase-space
translation.  The scaled Schatten norm is
\[
  \|A\|_{\mathcal L^p}=h^{d/p}\|A\|_{\Sp_p}.
\]
For $0<s<1$, the homogeneous quantum Sobolev seminorm is, up to the source's
positive normalization constant,
\[
 \|A\|_{\dot\W^{s,p}}^p
 =\gamma_{s,p}h^d\int_{\mathbb R^{2d}}
   \frac{\|\T_zA-A\|_{\Sp_p}^p}{|z|^{2d+sp}}\,dz.
\]

\section*{Proof Intuition}

An oblique idempotent may have an arbitrarily large norm, so interpolation
through an $\mathcal L^{2+\varepsilon}$ estimate loses the low-$p$ region.
That norm is unnecessary.  Replace the idempotent $P$ by the orthogonal
projection $Q$ onto the same range.  If two ranges move under a unitary, the
distance between their orthogonal projections is no larger, up to the sharp
two-block factor $2^{1/p}$, than the distance between any idempotents having
those ranges.  Thus every translation difference of $Q$ is controlled by the
corresponding translation difference of $P$.  The source's self-adjoint
obstruction applies to $Q$ and transfers back to $P$.

At order one the same mechanism appears infinitesimally.  Relative to
$\Ran P\oplus(\Ran P)^\perp$, the lower-left block of $[A,P]$ is precisely the
lower-left block of $[A,Q]$.  Self-adjointness of the generator supplies the
opposite block of $[A,Q]$, again at a factor $2^{1/p}$.

\section*{The range-projection comparison}

\begin{lemma}[Schatten control of moving ranges]\label{lem:range}
Let $P_0,P_1$ be finite-rank idempotents on a Hilbert space, of the same rank,
and let $Q_i$ be the orthogonal projection onto $E_i=\Ran P_i$.  For
$1\le p<\infty$,
\[
  \|Q_1-Q_0\|_{\Sp_p}
  \le 2^{1/p}\|P_1-P_0\|_{\Sp_p}.
\]
For $p=\infty$, the same statement holds with constant $1$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put $D=P_1-P_0$.  If $x\in E_0$, then
$Dx=P_1x-x$, with $P_1x\in E_1$.  Since $Q_1x$ is the best approximation to
$x$ from $E_1$,
\[
 \|(I-Q_1)Q_0x\|\le \|DQ_0x\|.
\]
Consequently
\[
 Q_0(I-Q_1)Q_0\le Q_0D^*DQ_0
\]
in positive-operator order, and the min--max principle gives
\[
 \|(I-Q_1)Q_0\|_{\Sp_p}\le \|DQ_0\|_{\Sp_p}
 \le \|D\|_{\Sp_p}.
\]
Interchanging the roles of the two ranges (and observing that
$Dx=x-P_0x$ for $x\in E_1$) similarly gives
\[
 \|(I-Q_0)Q_1\|_{\Sp_p}\le \|D\|_{\Sp_p}.
\]

The square $(Q_1-Q_0)^2$ commutes with $Q_0$.  Its restrictions to
$E_0$ and $E_0^\perp$ are respectively
\[
 Q_0(I-Q_1)Q_0,
 \qquad
 (I-Q_0)Q_1(I-Q_0).
\]
It follows that
\[
 \|Q_1-Q_0\|_{\Sp_p}^p
 =\|(I-Q_1)Q_0\|_{\Sp_p}^p
  +\|(I-Q_0)Q_1\|_{\Sp_p}^p
 \le 2\|D\|_{\Sp_p}^p.
\]
For the operator norm, take the maximum of the two diagonal blocks of the
square instead of their trace sum.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Translation and commutator comparisons]\label{cor:compare}
Let $P$ be a finite-rank idempotent and $Q$ the orthogonal projection onto
$\Ran P$.  Then, for every unitary $U$,
\[
 \|UQU^*-Q\|_{\Sp_p}
 \le c_p\|UPU^*-P\|_{\Sp_p},
 \qquad
 c_p=\begin{cases}2^{1/p},&p<\infty,\\1,&p=\infty.\end{cases}
\]
If $A$ is self-adjoint and the commutators belong to $\Sp_p$, then
\[
 \|[A,Q]\|_{\Sp_p}\le c_p\|[A,P]\|_{\Sp_p}.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The first claim is Lemma~\ref{lem:range} applied to $P$ and $UPU^*$, because
the orthogonal projection onto $U\Ran(P)$ is $UQU^*$.

For the second claim, decompose the Hilbert space as
$\Ran P\oplus(\Ran P)^\perp$.  The idempotent and its range projection have
block forms
\[
 P=\begin{pmatrix}I&B\\0&0\end{pmatrix},
 \qquad Q=\begin{pmatrix}I&0\\0&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Writing $A_{21}=(I-Q)AQ$, compression gives
\[
 (I-Q)[A,P]Q=A_{21},
 \qquad
 [A,Q]=\begin{pmatrix}0&-A_{21}^*\\A_{21}&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Hence $\|[A,Q]\|_{\Sp_p}=2^{1/p}\|A_{21}\|_{\Sp_p}$ for finite $p$
(and equality without the factor for $p=\infty$), while
$\|A_{21}\|_{\Sp_p}\le\|[A,P]\|_{\Sp_p}$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Solution of the Sobolev conjecture}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $h\to0$, and let $P_h$ be trace-class idempotents satisfying
\[
 P_h^2=P_h,
 \qquad h^d\Tr(P_h)=1.
\]
Let $1<p\le\infty$ and $0<s\le1$, with $s\ge1/p$.  When $p=\infty$,
assume $s>0$.  Then
\[
 \|P_h\|_{\dot\W^{s,p}}\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
Thus the extra uniform $\mathcal L^{2+\varepsilon}$ hypothesis in the
non-self-adjoint part of Theorem~1 of the source is unnecessary throughout
the Sobolev region conjectured in its Figure~2.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A trace-class idempotent has finite rank, and its trace equals its rank.  Let
$Q_h$ be the orthogonal projection onto $\Ran P_h$.  Then
\[
 Q_h^2=Q_h=Q_h^*,
 \qquad h^d\Tr(Q_h)=h^d\operatorname{rank}(P_h)=1.
\]

First take $0<s<1$.  Apply Corollary~\ref{cor:compare} with the Weyl unitary
implementing $\T_z$.  After multiplying by the semiclassical scaling factor,
\[
 \|\T_zQ_h-Q_h\|_{\mathcal L^p}
 \le c_p\|\T_zP_h-P_h\|_{\mathcal L^p}
 \quad\text{for every }z.
\]
Integrating this pointwise inequality in the definition of the fractional
seminorm yields
\[
 \|Q_h\|_{\dot\W^{s,p}}
 \le c_p\|P_h\|_{\dot\W^{s,p}}.
\]
The self-adjoint part of Theorem~1 in~\cite{Lafleche} says that the left side
tends to infinity whenever $s\ge1/p$ and $p>1$.  The right side must therefore
tend to infinity as well.

It remains to take $s=1$.  The source's Corollary~2.1, applied to $Q_h$, gives
for every $p>1$
\[
 \frac1\hbar\|[x,Q_h]\|_{\mathcal L^p}
 +\|[\nabla,Q_h]\|_{\mathcal L^p}\longrightarrow\infty.
\]
Apply the commutator part of Corollary~\ref{cor:compare} componentwise to the
self-adjoint generators $x_j$ and $-i\partial_j$.  The corresponding sum for
$Q_h$ is at most a dimension-dependent constant times the quantum
$\dot\W^{1,p}$ seminorm of $P_h$.  Hence that seminorm diverges.  If one of
the commutators is not in $\Sp_p$, the conclusion is immediate.
\end{proof}

\section*{A Besov consequence below order one}

The same idea also treats the source's second-difference Besov norm whenever
$0<s<1$.  For completeness, write $\Delta_z=\T_z-I$.  On every Schatten
class, the isometric group identity
\[
 \Delta_z=\tfrac12\Delta_{2z}-\tfrac12\Delta_z^2
\]
iterates to
\[
 \Delta_zA
 =-\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}2^{-k-1}\Delta_{2^kz}^2A.
\]
The remainder vanishes for each fixed Schatten operator.  Minkowski's
inequality and the change of variables $w=2^kz$ show that the first-difference
Besov seminorm is at most
\[
 \frac{1}{2(1-2^{s-1})}
\]
times the source's second-difference seminorm.  Conversely,
$\|\Delta_z^2A\|_{\Sp_p}\le2\|\Delta_zA\|_{\Sp_p}$.  Combining these facts
with Corollary~\ref{cor:compare} gives, for $0<s<1$ and
$1\le p,q\le\infty$,
\[
 \|Q_h\|_{\dot\B^s_{p,q}}
 \le C_{s,p}\|P_h\|_{\dot\B^s_{p,q}}.
\]
The self-adjoint Besov conclusion of Theorem~1 in the source therefore
transfers to arbitrary idempotents for $0<s<1$: divergence holds when
$s>1/p$, or when $s=1/p$ and $q<\infty$.  This includes every critical case
$s=1/p$ with $p>1$.

\section*{Verification and limitations}

The proof is exact and finite-dimensional at its new step.  The script
\path{code/check_projection_inequality.py} is only a regression check: it
tests the range-difference and commutator inequalities on random complex
idempotents, including condition numbers up to $10^4$.  It is not used as
evidence in place of the proof.

On 2026-07-21, the run's lightweight indexes and bounded web searches were
checked using arXiv:2302.04816, the exact title and conjecture sentence, and
combinations of ``non-self-adjoint'', ``idempotent'', ``trace condition'', and
``quantum Sobolev regularity''.  The source itself and adjacent quantum
Sobolev work were found, but no later exact solution.  This does not guarantee
novelty.

The theorem settles the Sobolev conjecture in Figure~2 and the Besov statement
for $0<s<1$.  It does not claim the separate second-difference Besov endpoint
$s=1,p=1$, and it uses rather than reproves the source's self-adjoint
nonregularity theorem.  A human reviewer should focus on
Lemma~\ref{lem:range} and on the compression/domain justification for the
unbounded generators in the $s=1$ argument.
