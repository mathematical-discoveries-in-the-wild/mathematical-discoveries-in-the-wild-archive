\section*{Open Question}

Candido--Cuth--Doucha ask:

\begin{quote}
Is \(\F(\Gamma)\simeq \ellone\) (and \(\Lip_0(\Gamma)\simeq \ellinfty\)) for
every finitely generated hyperbolic group \(\Gamma\)?
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions}

For a pointed metric space \(M\), \(\F(M)\) denotes the Lipschitz-free space
over \(M\), whose dual is canonically \(\Lip_0(M)\), the Banach space of
real-valued Lipschitz functions on \(M\) that vanish at the basepoint, equipped
with the Lipschitz norm. Gartland writes \(\LF(M)\) for the same free space.
The notation \(X\simeq Y\), or \(X\approx Y\), means Banach-space isomorphism.

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The original question asked whether the free space of a finitely generated
hyperbolic group behaves, up to Banach-space isomorphism, like the free space of
a tree. Gartland develops stochastic embeddings into \(\mathbb{R}\)-trees and
shows that such embeddings force the free space to be an \(L_1\)-space; for
infinite uniformly discrete hyperbolic groups, the resulting \(L_1\)-space is
\(\ellone\). Since \(\Lip_0(\Gamma)\) is the dual of \(\F(\Gamma)\), the
corresponding Lipschitz-function space is then \(\ellinfty\).

\section*{Formal Statement}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(\Gamma\) be an infinite finitely generated hyperbolic group, equipped
with a word metric and any basepoint. Then
\[
  \F(\Gamma)\simeq \ellone
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  \Lip_0(\Gamma)\simeq \ellinfty.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cite[Lemma 7.1]{Gartland}, every infinite finitely generated hyperbolic
group stochastically bi-Lipschitzly embeds into a pointed \(\mathbb{R}\)-tree
and has Lipschitz-free space isomorphic to \(\ellone\). Gartland writes this
as \(\LF(\Gamma)\approx\ellone\); this is the same Lipschitz-free space denoted
\(\F(\Gamma)\) in \cite{CCD}. Hence \(\F(\Gamma)\simeq\ellone\).

Taking Banach-space duals of an isomorphism \(\F(\Gamma)\simeq\ellone\) gives
\[
  \Lip_0(\Gamma)
  =
  \F(\Gamma)^*
  \simeq
  \ellone^*
  =
  \ellinfty.
\]
Thus both clauses of Question 1 hold for every infinite finitely generated
hyperbolic group.
\end{proof}

\section*{Scope And Limitations}

The original question is phrased as ``every finitely generated hyperbolic
group.'' Gartland states the result for every infinite finitely generated
hyperbolic group and explicitly writes that it answers \cite[Question 1]{CCD}.
This packet follows that intended scope. If finite groups are included
literally, their free spaces are finite dimensional and therefore are not
isomorphic to the usual infinite-dimensional \(\ellone\).

No new proof is claimed here; the packet records an already-known answer in a
separate later paper.
