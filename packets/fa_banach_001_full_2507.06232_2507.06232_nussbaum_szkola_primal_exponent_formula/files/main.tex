\section{\hspace{0.22em}Source question}

Cheng and Liu prove that the constant-composition random-coding exponent of a
classical--quantum channel $\cN:x\mapsto\rho_x$ with fixed input law $p$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dual}
 E_{\rm P}(R)
 =\sup_{\alpha\in[1/2,1]}
 \frac{1-\alpha}{\alpha}
 \bigl[I_\alpha^{\Aug}(p;\cN)-R\bigr],
\end{equation}
where $I_\alpha^{\Aug}$ is built from the Petz R\'enyi divergence
\cite[Theorem~4.3]{ChengLiu}. In Remark~4.4, PDF page~23, they explain why the
literal quantum analogue of the classical Csisz\'ar--K\"orner formula yields
the wrong, log-Euclidean R\'enyi divergence, and ask whether any primal-domain
expression exists. The complete source context is reproduced below.

\section{\hspace{0.22em}Definitions}

All logarithms and relative entropies below use base two. Let $\mathcal X$ be
finite, let $\cH$ be finite-dimensional, let $p$ be a probability distribution
on $\mathcal X$, and let $\rho_x\in\cS(\cH)$. For $0<\alpha<1$,
\[
 D_\alpha(\rho\Vert\sigma)
 =\frac{1}{\alpha-1}
 \log\Tr(\rho^\alpha\sigma^{1-\alpha}),
 \qquad
 I_\alpha^{\Aug}(p;\cN)
 =\inf_{\sigma\in\cS(\cH)}
 \sum_xp(x)D_\alpha(\rho_x\Vert\sigma).
\]

Fix spectral decompositions
\[
 \rho_x=\sum_i r_{x,i}|u_{x,i}\rangle\langle u_{x,i}|,
 \qquad
 \sigma=\sum_j q_j|v_j\rangle\langle v_j|.
\]
The associated Nussbaum--Szko\l a distributions
\cite{NussbaumSzkola,AndroulakisJohn} on the pairs $(i,j)$ are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:NS}
 P_{x,\sigma}(i,j)=r_{x,i}|\langle u_{x,i},v_j\rangle|^2,
 \qquad
 Q_{x,\sigma}(i,j)=q_j|\langle u_{x,i},v_j\rangle|^2.
\end{equation}

A \emph{primitive primal datum} is
$\omega=(\sigma,(T_x)_{x\in\mathcal X})$, where $\sigma$ is faithful and
each $T_x$ is a probability distribution on the eigen-index pairs. Define
extended nonnegative costs
\begin{equation}\label{eq:AB}
 A(\omega)=\sum_xp(x)D(T_x\Vert P_{x,\sigma}),
 \qquad
 B(\omega)=\sum_xp(x)D(T_x\Vert Q_{x,\sigma}).
\end{equation}
Only data with finite costs are needed. Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K}
 \cK=\cl\conv\bigl\{(A(\omega),B(\omega)):\omega
 \text{ is a finite-cost primitive datum}\bigr\}\subseteq\mathbb R^2.
\end{equation}
Thus $\cK$ permits ordinary finite time-sharing and limiting sequences, but it
does not depend on the R\'enyi order.

\section{\hspace{0.22em}Proof Intuition}

The obstacle identified by the source is noncommutativity: the usual Gibbs
variational argument applied directly to matrices creates the log-Euclidean
R\'enyi divergence. The key idea is to apply Gibbs variation only after the
Nussbaum--Szko\l a spectral lift. That lift preserves the Petz R\'enyi overlap
exactly, while turning it into an ordinary classical overlap.

For $s=(1-\alpha)/\alpha$, the classical tilted distribution converts each
term $sD_\alpha$ into a minimum of $A+sB$. Minimizing over the Augustin state
therefore identifies the dual exponent with
$\sup_{0\le s\le1}\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}[A+s(B-R)]$. Convex time-sharing makes
Sion minimax applicable. After exchanging the two optimizations, maximizing
over $s$ gives precisely the hinge $(B-R)_+$. In the commuting case, $A$ is
conditional relative entropy and $B$ reduces to mutual information, recovering
the classical formula.

\section{\hspace{0.22em}Main result}

\begin{theorem}[Lifted primal formula]\label{thm:main}
For every finite classical--quantum channel $\cN:x\mapsto\rho_x$, input law
$p$, and rate $R\in\mathbb R$, the Petz--Augustin exponent in
\eqref{eq:dual} satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:primal}
 \boxed{
 E_{\rm P}(R)
 =\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}\bigl\{A+(B-R)_+\bigr\}.}
\end{equation}
Every point relevant to this infimum can be approximated by time-sharing among
at most three primitive data. If all $\rho_x$ commute, \eqref{eq:primal}
reduces to the Csisz\'ar--K\"orner formula
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CK}
 \inf_{V:x\mapsto V_x}
 \left\{
 \sum_xp(x)D(V_x\Vert W_x)
 +\bigl(I(p,V)-R\bigr)_+
 \right\},
\end{equation}
where $W_x$ are the common-basis eigenvalue distributions of $\rho_x$.
\end{theorem}

\section{\hspace{0.22em}Proof}

\begin{lemma}[Classical tilt identity]\label{lem:tilt}
For probability distributions $P,Q$ on a finite set and $s\ge0$, put
$\alpha=1/(1+s)$. Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tilt}
 sD_\alpha(P\Vert Q)
 =\inf_T\bigl\{D(T\Vert P)+sD(T\Vert Q)\bigr\}.
\end{equation}
For $s>0$ the minimizer is
\[
 T_s(z)=\frac{P(z)^\alpha Q(z)^{1-\alpha}}
 {\sum_yP(y)^\alpha Q(y)^{1-\alpha}}.
\]
The equality at $s=0$ is understood by continuity and is also immediate.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $Z_s=\sum_zP(z)^{1/(1+s)}Q(z)^{s/(1+s)}$. For any admissible $T$,
\begin{align*}
 D(T\Vert P)+sD(T\Vert Q)
 &=(1+s)D(T\Vert T_s)-(1+s)\log Z_s.
\end{align*}
The first term has minimum zero. The second equals
$sD_{1/(1+s)}(P\Vert Q)$ by definition.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Petz preservation]\label{lem:NS}
The distributions in \eqref{eq:NS} satisfy
\[
 D_\alpha(P_{x,\sigma}\Vert Q_{x,\sigma})
 =D_\alpha(\rho_x\Vert\sigma),\qquad 0<\alpha<1.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Writing $c_{ij}=|\langle u_{x,i},v_j\rangle|^2$, one has
\begin{align*}
 \sum_{i,j}P_{x,\sigma}(i,j)^\alpha
 Q_{x,\sigma}(i,j)^{1-\alpha}
 &=\sum_{i,j}r_{x,i}^\alpha q_j^{1-\alpha}c_{ij}\\
 &=\Tr(\rho_x^\alpha\sigma^{1-\alpha}).
\end{align*}
Taking $(\alpha-1)^{-1}\log$ proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
Write $s=(1-\alpha)/\alpha$, so $\alpha\in[1/2,1]$ corresponds to
$s\in[0,1]$. For $s>0$, Lemmas~\ref{lem:tilt} and \ref{lem:NS}, followed by
separate minimization over each $T_x$, give
\begin{align}\label{eq:support}
 sI_{1/(1+s)}^{\Aug}(p;\cN)
 &=\inf_{\sigma>0}\sum_xp(x)
     sD_{1/(1+s)}(\rho_x\Vert\sigma)\notag\\
 &=\inf_{\omega}\bigl\{A(\omega)+sB(\omega)\bigr\}\notag\\
 &=\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}\{A+sB\}.
\end{align}
Restricting the first infimum to faithful $\sigma$ does not change its value:
replace any $\sigma$ by
$(1-\varepsilon)\sigma+\varepsilon I/\dim\cH$ and let
$\varepsilon\downarrow0$. The required Petz overlaps are continuous for
$\alpha<1$. At $s=0$, both sides of \eqref{eq:support} equal zero.

Substitute \eqref{eq:support} into \eqref{eq:dual}:
\begin{align*}
 E_{\rm P}(R)
 &=\sup_{s\in[0,1]}\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}
   \{A+s(B-R)\}.
\end{align*}
The set $[0,1]$ is compact convex, $\cK$ is convex, and the displayed
function is continuous affine in both variables. Apply Sion's minimax theorem
to its negative (with $s$ as the compact minimization variable) to obtain
\begin{align*}
 E_{\rm P}(R)
 &=\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}\sup_{s\in[0,1]}
   \{A+s(B-R)\}\\
 &=\inf_{(A,B)\in\cK}\{A+(B-R)_+\},
\end{align*}
which is \eqref{eq:primal}. Since $\cK\subseteq\mathbb R^2$, Carath\'eodory's
theorem says that every point of its nonclosed convex hull is a convex
combination of at most three primitive cost pairs; closure points are limits
of such combinations.

It remains to verify the commuting reduction. In a common eigenbasis, each
spectral lift is an ordinary classical refinement of the channel $W_x$. A
primitive $T_x$ can be pushed through the map that forgets the refinement,
giving a channel $V_x$. Data processing yields
\[
 \sum_xp(x)D(V_x\Vert W_x)\le A,
 \qquad
 I(p,V)\le B.
\]
For the unrefined diagonal choice, the sharper chain rule is
\[
 \sum_xp(x)D(V_x\Vert q)
 =I(p,V)+D(pV\Vert q),
\]
so minimizing over $q$ gives $B=I(p,V)$. For a time-sharing mixture, average
the resulting channels $V$; joint convexity of relative entropy and convexity
of $I(p,V)$ show that this averaging does not increase either relevant cost.
Thus no lifted/time-shared datum beats \eqref{eq:CK}, while every candidate in
\eqref{eq:CK} occurs as a single diagonal primitive datum. The two infima are
equal.
\end{proof}

\section{\hspace{0.22em}Verification, scope, and novelty}

The script \texttt{code/check\_variational\_identity.py} performs two checks:
\begin{itemize}
\item 400 seeded random noncommuting matrix/order instances of
Lemma~\ref{lem:tilt} combined with Lemma~\ref{lem:NS};
\item 100 seeded random instances of the classical chain rule used in the
commuting reduction.
\end{itemize}
The command is
\begin{verbatim}
conda run --no-capture-output -n sandbox python \
  code/check_variational_identity.py
\end{verbatim}
The computation is a sanity check, not part of the proof.

The result is exact in the finite setting surrounding source Remark~4.4. It
does not supply an infinite-dimensional extension. More importantly, its
primal variables are classical tilted laws on canonical spectral lifts, with
finite time-sharing. Whether the same exponent admits a comparably simple
formula involving only alternative quantum output states remains open.

The bounded novelty search covered the four run indexes; exact-phrase arXiv
searches for ``primal-domain expression'' with Petz--Augustin information;
Nussbaum--Szko\l a, Csisz\'ar--K\"orner, sphere-packing, and
constant-composition variants; arXiv:2308.02929; and arXiv:2601.06492. No
matching exponent formula or answer to Remark~4.4 was found. Novelty confidence
is therefore provisional rather than definitive.

\textbf{Human-review recommendation.} Review as a scoped candidate full
solution. In particular, verify the faithful-state approximation, the sign in
the Sion step, and whether a spectral-lift formula meets the intended breadth
of the source's phrase ``primal-domain expression.''
