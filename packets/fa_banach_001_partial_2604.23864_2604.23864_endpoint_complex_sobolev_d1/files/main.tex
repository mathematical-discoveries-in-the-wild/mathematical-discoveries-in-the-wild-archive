\section*{Status}

This packet records a candidate partial result for arXiv:2604.23864, Hugues
Moyart, \emph{Bourgain's method for $K$-closedness in the semicommmutative
setting} \cite{Moyart}. The result answers the endpoint complex interpolation
question for the homogeneous Sobolev couple in dimension one. It does not settle
the higher-dimensional problem.

\section*{Source question}

The source paper proves the non-endpoint complex interpolation identity for
noncommutative homogeneous Sobolev spaces on the torus and then states that the
endpoint couple is still undetermined.

\section*{Definitions and notation}

Let $M$ be a semifinite von Neumann algebra and set
\[
  N=L_\infty(\T)\cb M
\]
with its tensor product trace. For an exact interpolation space $E$ between
$L_1$ and $L_\infty$, Moyart defines the homogeneous Sobolev space
$W_{1,E}(N)$ as the space of distributions $u$ on $\T$ with
$\partial u\in E(N)$, equipped with the norm
\[
  \|u\|_{W_{1,E}(N)}=\|\partial u\|_{E(N)}.
\]
As in the source paper, this is understood modulo the zero seminorm, i.e. modulo
constant distributions. We write $W_{1,p}(N)=W_{1,L_p}(N)$.

For $E=L_1,L_\infty,L_p$, let
\[
  E_0(N)=\{f\in E(N): \widehat f(0)=0\}.
\]
Equivalently, $E_0(N)=\ker \mathbb E$ where
\[
  \mathbb E f=\int_\T f(z)\,dz
\]
is the trace-preserving conditional expectation onto the constant copy of $M$.

\section*{Proof intuition}

In one dimension there is no curl condition. The gradient range in Moyart's
Leray-projection formulation is simply the zero-mean subspace of $L_p(N)$.
That subspace is complemented at both endpoints by $Q=I-\mathbb E$. Complex
interpolation is stable under a common bounded projection on the endpoints, so
the endpoint Sobolev question reduces to the standard interpolation identity
$[L_1(N),L_\infty(N)]_\theta=L_p(N)$.

\begin{lemma}[Complemented subcouple interpolation]
Let $(X_0,X_1)$ be a compatible Banach couple and let $Q$ be a compatible
bounded projection on both $X_0$ and $X_1$. Put $A_j=QX_j$. Then for
$0<\theta<1$,
\[
  [A_0,A_1]_\theta = Q [X_0,X_1]_\theta
\]
with equivalent norms. The constants depend only on
$\|Q\|_{X_0\to X_0}$ and $\|Q\|_{X_1\to X_1}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The inclusion $[A_0,A_1]_\theta\subseteq [X_0,X_1]_\theta$ is immediate from
the endpoint inclusions, and every element of the left side lies in the range of
$Q$ because the representing analytic functions have boundary values in the
range of $Q$. Conversely, if $x=F(\theta)$ for an admissible analytic function
$F$ for the couple $(X_0,X_1)$, then $QF$ is admissible for the subcouple
$(A_0,A_1)$ and $(QF)(\theta)=Qx$. Taking infima over $F$ gives the claimed
norm comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[One-dimensional endpoint Sobolev subcase]
Let $M$ be a semifinite von Neumann algebra, let
$N=L_\infty(\T)\cb M$, and let $0<\theta<1$. Define $p$ by
$1/p=1-\theta$. Then
\[
  [W_{1,1}(N),W_{1,\infty}(N)]_\theta = W_{1,p}(N)
\]
with equivalent norms.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The conditional expectation $\mathbb E:L_\infty(N)\to M$ is contractive and
trace preserving, hence it extends contractively to $L_1(N)$. Therefore
$Q=I-\mathbb E$ is a compatible bounded projection on the couple
$(L_1(N),L_\infty(N))$, with range $(L_1^0(N),L_\infty^0(N))$ where
$L_p^0(N)=\{f\in L_p(N):\widehat f(0)=0\}$.

By the complemented subcouple lemma and the standard noncommutative
$L_p$ interpolation identity
\[
  [L_1(N),L_\infty(N)]_\theta=L_p(N),
\]
we obtain
\[
  [L_1^0(N),L_\infty^0(N)]_\theta=L_p^0(N)
\]
with equivalent norms.

It remains to identify this zero-mean couple with the Sobolev couple. In
dimension one, Moyart's description of the range of the derivative says exactly
that $\partial W_{1,E}(N)=E_0(N)$ for $E=L_1,L_\infty,L_p$: the higher-dimensional
curl equations are vacuous and only the condition $\widehat f(0)=0$ remains.
Equivalently, given $f\in E_0(N)$ one defines a distribution $u$ by
\[
  \widehat u(n)=\frac{\widehat f(n)}{in}\quad(n\neq 0),\qquad \widehat u(0)=0,
\]
and then $\partial u=f$. The map $u\mapsto \partial u$ is an isometric
identification of $W_{1,E}(N)$ with $E_0(N)$ by definition of the homogeneous
Sobolev norm.

Transporting the previous interpolation identity through this common
isomorphism of endpoint couples gives
\[
  [W_{1,1}(N),W_{1,\infty}(N)]_\theta=W_{1,p}(N),
\]
as claimed.
\end{proof}

\section*{Limitations}

The argument is specific to $d=1$. For $d\geq 2$, the gradient range is the
subspace of vector fields satisfying the curl-free equations and zero Fourier
mean. The natural projection onto that range is the Leray projection, a matrix
of Riesz-type transforms. The simple complemented-subcouple proof above would
require boundedness of that projection on both $L_1$ and $L_\infty$, which is
precisely the endpoint obstruction. Thus this packet should be read as a
one-dimensional partial result and not as a solution of the full endpoint
problem stated in the source paper.

\section*{Verification and novelty notes}

The proof uses only:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Moyart's derivative-range description for $W_{1,E}$ and $H_E(P^\bot)$;
  \item the standard complex interpolation identity for noncommutative
  $L_p$ spaces;
  \item the elementary projection lemma for complex interpolation.
\end{itemize}
