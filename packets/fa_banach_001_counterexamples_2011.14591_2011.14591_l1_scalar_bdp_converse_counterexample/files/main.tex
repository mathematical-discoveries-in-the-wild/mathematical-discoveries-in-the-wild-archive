\section*{Source Remark}

The source paper is S. Basu and S. Seal,
\emph{Stability results of small diameter properties in Banach spaces},
arXiv:2011.14591.  In the section ``Lebesgue Bochner Function Spaces'', after
Proposition \texttt{bochner bdp}, the authors write:

\begin{quote}
We do not know whether the converse of the Propositions
\texttt{bochner bhp}, \texttt{bochner bdp} are true or not in general.
However, converse of Proposition \texttt{bochner bhp}, fails for \(p=1\).
Indeed, \(\mathbb R\) has \(BHP\) but \(L^1[0,1]\) does not have \(BHP\).
\end{quote}

\section*{Definitions}

A Banach space \(X\) has the Ball Dentable Property (BDP) if, for every
\(\varepsilon>0\), the unit ball \(B_X\) has a slice of diameter less than
\(\varepsilon\).  It has the Ball Huskable Property (BHP) if \(B_X\) has a
nonempty relatively weakly open subset of arbitrarily small diameter.  Every
slice is relatively weakly open, so
\[
  BDP \Longrightarrow BHP.
\]

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
The converse of Proposition \texttt{bochner bdp} is false in general.  More
precisely, for Lebesgue measure on \([0,1]\), the scalar space
\(X=\mathbb R\) has \(BDP\), while \(L^1([0,1],X)=L^1[0,1]\) does not have
\(BDP\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The one-dimensional space \(\mathbb R\) has \(BDP\): its unit ball has slices
of arbitrarily small diameter.

It remains to show that \(L^1[0,1]\) does not have \(BDP\).  Let
\[
  S=\{f\in B_{L^1}:\Phi(f)>\|\Phi\|-\alpha\}
\]
be a nonempty slice of \(B_{L^1[0,1]}\), where \(\Phi\in (L^1)^*=L^\infty\)
and \(\alpha>0\).  Choose \(0<\beta<\alpha\), and write \(\varphi\in
L^\infty[0,1]\) for the function representing \(\Phi\).  The set
\[
  A=\{t\in[0,1]: |\varphi(t)|>\|\varphi\|_\infty-\beta\}
\]
has positive measure.  Since Lebesgue measure is non-atomic, \(A\) contains
two disjoint measurable subsets \(A_1,A_2\) of positive measure.

For \(i=1,2\), define
\[
  f_i(t)=\operatorname{sgn}(\varphi(t))\,\frac{\mathbf 1_{A_i}(t)}{\mu(A_i)}
\]
on \(A_i\), with any measurable choice of sign on the null set where
\(\varphi=0\).  Then \(\|f_i\|_1=1\) and
\[
  \Phi(f_i)=\frac{1}{\mu(A_i)}\int_{A_i}|\varphi(t)|\,d\mu(t)
  > \|\varphi\|_\infty-\beta
  > \|\Phi\|-\alpha.
\]
Thus \(f_1,f_2\in S\).  Their supports are disjoint, hence
\[
  \|f_1-f_2\|_1=\|f_1\|_1+\|f_2\|_1=2.
\]
Every slice of \(B_{L^1[0,1]}\) therefore has diameter \(2\), so
\(L^1[0,1]\) does not have \(BDP\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequence for the Source Remark}

The source already observes that \(X=\mathbb R\), \(p=1\), and Lebesgue
measure on \([0,1]\) disprove the converse of Proposition
\texttt{bochner bhp}.  The theorem above shows that the same scalar example
also disproves the converse of Proposition \texttt{bochner bdp}.  Hence the
two converse questions are both negative in the unrestricted ``in general''
sense.

\section*{Scope and Verification}

The argument is purely analytic and uses only the standard duality
\((L^1)^*=L^\infty\) for finite measure spaces and the non-atomicity of
Lebesgue measure.  It does not address whether any positive converse holds
for \(1<p<\infty\), for purely atomic measures, or under extra hypotheses on
\(X\).

The local run indexes were searched for arXiv:2011.14591, BDP, BHP,
Lebesgue-Bochner, \(L^p(\mu,X)\), and converse.  The local arXiv corpus was
also searched for the same phrases, including the later arXiv:2307.03631
Kothe-Bochner sequel.  Web searches for exact phrases from the source remark
and close variants returned the source paper and the 2023 sequel, but no
separate explicit answer to this BDP-converse consequence.
