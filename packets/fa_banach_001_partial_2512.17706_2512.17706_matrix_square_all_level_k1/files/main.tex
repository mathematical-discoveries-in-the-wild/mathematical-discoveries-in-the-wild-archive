\section*{Source Problem}

The source is Bluhm--Evert--Klep--Magron--Nechita,
\emph{Inclusion constants for free spectrahedra with applications to quantum
incompatibility}, arXiv:2512.17706 \cite{BEKMN}.  The paper proves a level-two
formula for the direct sum of a real line and a real \(k\)-simplex and then
conjectures the same value without a level restriction:

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/conjecture_5_2_crop.png}

\noindent
This packet proves the \(k=1\) case of that all-level conjecture.  Under the
source definitions, \(k=1\) is the matrix square and its dual matrix diamond.

\section*{Result}

Let
\[
  \cC_2(n)=\{(X_1,X_2)\in \M_n(\C)_{\sa}^2:\ -I\le X_i\le I,\ i=1,2\}
\]
be the two-variable matrix cube, and let
\[
  \cD_2(n)=
  \{(Y_1,Y_2)\in \M_n(\C)_{\sa}^2:
       \epsilon_1Y_1+\epsilon_2Y_2\preceq I
       \text{ for every } \epsilon\in\{\pm1\}^2\}
\]
be the matrix diamond.  These are exactly \(\cD_{A(1)}\) and
\(\cD_{A(1)}^\square\) in Conjecture 5.2 of \cite{BEKMN}.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:k-one}
One has
\[
  \sup_{X\in\cC_2,\ Y\in\cD_2}
  \lambda_{\max}\left(X_1\otimes Y_1+X_2\otimes Y_2\right)
  =\sqrt2.
\]
Consequently Conjecture 5.2 of \cite{BEKMN} holds for \(k=1\), since
\[
  \gamma(1)=\frac{2}{\sqrt2}=\sqrt2.
\]
\end{theorem}

\section*{Proof Intuition}

The \(k=1\) dual constraints have a particularly simple form.  The diamond
condition is equivalent to saying that \(A=Y_1+Y_2\) and \(B=Y_1-Y_2\) are
self-adjoint contractions.  On the cube side, the combinations
\[
  P=\frac{X_1+X_2}{2},\qquad Q=\frac{X_1-X_2}{2}
\]
satisfy \(P^2+Q^2\le I\).  Thus the objective is a product of one row
contraction and one column with norm at most \(\sqrt2\).  Equality is the
standard CHSH/Pauli configuration.

\section*{Proof}

\begin{proof}
Let \(X=(X_1,X_2)\in\cC_2(n)\) and \(Y=(Y_1,Y_2)\in\cD_2(m)\).  Put
\[
  A=Y_1+Y_2,\qquad B=Y_1-Y_2.
\]
The four sign inequalities defining \(\cD_2\) are exactly
\[
  -I\le A\le I,\qquad -I\le B\le I,
\]
so \(A\) and \(B\) are self-adjoint contractions.

Next put
\[
  P=\frac{X_1+X_2}{2},\qquad Q=\frac{X_1-X_2}{2}.
\]
Since \(X_1\) and \(X_2\) are self-adjoint contractions,
\[
  P^2+Q^2
  =\frac{X_1^2+X_2^2}{2}
  \preceq I.
\]
Moreover
\[
  X_1\otimes Y_1+X_2\otimes Y_2
  =P\otimes A+Q\otimes B.
\]
Writing this as a row-column product,
\[
  P\otimes A+Q\otimes B
  =
  \begin{bmatrix}P\otimes I_m & Q\otimes I_m\end{bmatrix}
  \begin{bmatrix}I_n\otimes A\\ I_n\otimes B\end{bmatrix},
\]
we obtain
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \|P\otimes A+Q\otimes B\|
  &\le
  \left\|
  \begin{bmatrix}P\otimes I_m & Q\otimes I_m\end{bmatrix}
  \right\|
  \left\|
  \begin{bmatrix}I_n\otimes A\\ I_n\otimes B\end{bmatrix}
  \right\|  \\
  &=\|P^2+Q^2\|^{1/2}\,\|A^2+B^2\|^{1/2}
  \le \sqrt2,
\end{aligned}
\]
because \(A^2\preceq I\) and \(B^2\preceq I\).  Hence the objective value is at
most \(\sqrt2\) at all levels.

For equality, take the Pauli matrices
\[
  Z=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix},
  \qquad
  X=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Set
\[
  X_1=Z,\qquad X_2=X,\qquad
  Y_1=\frac{Z+X}{2},\qquad
  Y_2=\frac{Z-X}{2}.
\]
Then \(X\in\cC_2(2)\).  Also \(Y_1+Y_2=Z\) and \(Y_1-Y_2=X\), so
\(Y\in\cD_2(2)\).  The corresponding objective is
\[
  \frac12\left(Z\otimes Z+Z\otimes X+X\otimes Z-X\otimes X\right),
\]
the CHSH operator divided by \(2\), whose spectrum contains \(\pm\sqrt2\).
Thus the supremum is at least \(\sqrt2\), completing the proof.
\end{proof}

\section*{Consequences and Scope}

By the source's Theorem 6.1 / Theorem 7.2 dictionary between these inclusion
constants and measurement incompatibility, this also recovers the all-dimension
complex value
\[
  s_{\C}(d,2,(2,2))=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\]
for two dichotomic measurements.  The packet does not address \(k\ge2\) in
Conjecture 5.2 / Conjecture 7.3, nor the separate four-dichotomic-qubit
conjecture \(s_{\C}(2,4)=2/\sqrt{13}\).

\section*{Novelty Check}

The search on July 18, 2026 found no exact run duplicate for arXiv:2512.17706.
This is a small subcase and is close to known matrix cube/matrix diamond
dilation facts and the standard two-observable Tsirelson bound.  Web searches
for the source arXiv id, the label \texttt{kSimplexPlusLineOptimum}, matrix
square inclusion constants with \(\sqrt2\), matrix cube/matrix diamond
phrases, and two-dichotomic compatibility-degree phrases found adjacent known
literature but no explicit later resolution of the source conjecture.  The
claim here is therefore scoped only as a modest partial result.
