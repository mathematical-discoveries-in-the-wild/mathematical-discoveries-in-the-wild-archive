# Kadison-Singer equivalents from the 2005 survey

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source/open-problem paper: Peter G. Casazza, Matthew Fickus, Janet C. Tremain,
and Eric Weber, *The Kadison-Singer Problem in Mathematics and Engineering: A
Detailed Account*, arXiv:math/0510024.

Supporting answer paper: Adam W. Marcus, Daniel A. Spielman, and Nikhil
Srivastava, *Interlacing Families II: Mixed Characteristic Polynomials and the
Kadison-Singer Problem*, arXiv:1306.3969.

## Identification

The 2005 source is a detailed survey whose main open target is the
Kadison-Singer problem and its equivalent formulations: Anderson paving,
Weaver `KS_r`/`KS_r'`, the Feichtinger conjecture, the `R_epsilon`
conjecture, and the Bourgain-Tzafriri conjecture. Its many `conjecture`
signals are mostly formulations of this same problem-family.

Marcus--Spielman--Srivastava resolve the family by proving Weaver's `KS_2`
and proving Anderson paving with explicit bounds. Their abstract says that
the two proved theorems are known to imply a positive solution to
Kadison-Singer, and their introduction lists the same equivalent conjectures
from the 2005 survey.

## Scope

This packet records only that the central 2005 Kadison-Singer/Weaver/paving
open-problem family is no longer open after arXiv:1306.3969. It is not an
original proof from this run.

The packet does not attempt to optimize paving constants, classify every
special operator class discussed in the survey, or settle unrelated modern
variants of Feichtinger-type conjectures in Banach-space settings.
