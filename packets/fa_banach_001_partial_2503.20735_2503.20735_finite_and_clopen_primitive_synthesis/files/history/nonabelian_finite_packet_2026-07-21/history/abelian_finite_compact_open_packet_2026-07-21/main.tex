\begin{abstract}
Question 8.12 of Carter's paper asks for geometric conditions on subsets of
the primitive spectrum which imply spectral synthesis for Hermitian weighted
Orlicz algebras on unipotent $p$-adic groups.  We prove a complete criterion
in the abelian locally elliptic case: every finite set and every compact-open
set in the Pontryagin dual is a set of synthesis.  In particular, this applies
to the additive unipotent group $U_2(k)\cong(k,+)$ over every non-archimedean
local field $k$.  The mechanism is an exact compact-open Fourier cutoff which
belongs to Carter's minimal ideal $j(C)$.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Status.}
Candidate partial result, likely valid, subject to human review.  The theorem
fully proves the stated synthesis criteria in the abelian case; it does not
resolve the nonabelian coadjoint-orbit problem.

\section{Definitions and scope}

Let $G$ be an abelian locally elliptic group.  Let $(\Phi,\Psi)$ be a
complementary pair of Young functions with $\Phi\in\Delta_2$, and let $\omega$
be an $L^\Phi$-weight such that $1/\omega\in L^\Psi(G)$.  Put
\[
   \A=L^\Phi(G,\omega),
\]
and assume that $\A$ is Hermitian.  Carter's Theorem 6.2 identifies
$\operatorname{Prim}_*(\A)$ with the unitary dual; because $G$ is abelian,
this is the Pontryagin dual $\wideG$.

For closed $C\subseteq\wideG$, write
\[
 \ker(C)=\{f\in\A:\widehat f(\chi)=0\text{ for all }\chi\in C\}.
\]
Carter defines a closed ideal $j(C)$ using a smooth functional calculus and
proves $j(C)\subseteq\ker(C)$ and
$\operatorname{hull}_*(j(C))=C$ \cite[Theorem 7.1]{Carter}.  The set $C$ is a
\emph{set of synthesis} when $j(C)=\ker(C)$.

Let $\D(G)$ be the span of the indicators of compact-open cosets of $G$.
For the Fourier transform we use
\[
 \widehat f(\chi)=\int_G f(x)\overline{\chi(x)}\,dx.
\]

\section{Proof intuition}

The dual of an abelian locally elliptic group has a compact-open basis.  On
such groups, compactly supported locally constant functions are Fourier
stable.  If $U\subseteq\wideG$ is compact-open and disjoint from $C$, the
inverse Fourier transform $e_U$ of $1_U$ is therefore an honest element of
$\D(G)\subseteq\A$.

The key point is stronger than ordinary Fourier localization: $e_U$ lies in
Carter's specific ideal $j(C)$.  Scale $e_U$ to a self-adjoint $h$ whose
Fourier transform takes only the values $0$ and $a>0$, and apply Carter's
smooth functional calculus with a function that is zero near $0$ and equal to
$1$ at $a$.  The resulting element is exactly $e_U$ and belongs to the
generating set $m(C)$ of $j(C)$.  Density then reduces synthesis to arranging
that approximants have Fourier support away from $C$.  For finite $C$ this is
done by finite interpolation; for compact-open $C$ it is done by the Fourier
projection $1_C$.

\section{Formal proof}

\begin{lemma}[Fourier test functions and continuity]\label{lem:test}
The following hold.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\D(G)$ is dense in $\A$.
\item Fourier transformation maps $\D(G)$ onto $\D(\wideG)$.
\item For every $\chi\in\wideG$, evaluation
$f\mapsto\widehat f(\chi)$ is continuous on $\A$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since $\Phi\in\Delta_2$, compactly supported functions are dense in the
weighted Orlicz space; this is the standard $\Delta_2$ density property
\cite{RaoRen}.  A locally elliptic group has a compact-open basis.  Hence a
continuous function on a compact set is uniformly approximable by locally
constant step functions supported on finitely many compact-open cosets.  On a
fixed compact set $K$, uniform error $\varepsilon$ gives Orlicz error at most
$\varepsilon\|\omega 1_K\|_{L^\Phi}$ (up to the fixed equivalent norm
convention).  This proves (i).

If $K\leq G$ is compact-open and $x\in G$, Haar orthogonality gives
\[
 \widehat{1_{x+K}}(\chi)
   =\mu(K)\overline{\chi(x)}1_{K^\perp}(\chi).
\]
Here $K^\perp$ is compact-open in $\wideG$.  The same formula on $\wideG$,
together with Fourier inversion, proves (ii); see also \cite{Rudin}.

Finally, the Orlicz--H\"older inequality and $1/\omega\in L^\Psi(G)$ give a
constant $H$ such that
\[
 |\widehat f(\chi)|\leq\|f\|_1
 \leq H\|f\|_{L^\Phi(G,\omega)},
\]
which proves (iii).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Exact cutoffs lie in $j(C)$]\label{lem:cutoff}
Let $C\subseteq\wideG$ be closed and let $U\subseteq\wideG$ be compact-open
with $U\cap C=\varnothing$.  If $e_U\in\D(G)$ is defined by
$\widehat{e_U}=1_U$, then $e_U\in j(C)$.  Consequently, every
$g\in\D(G)$ satisfying $\operatorname{supp}\widehat g\subseteq U$ belongs to
$j(C)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The function $e_U$ is self-adjoint because its Fourier transform is real.
Choose $a\in(0,2\pi)$ so small that $a\|e_U\|_1\leq1$, and set
$h=ae_U$.  Then $h=h^*\in C_c(G)$, $\|h\|_1\leq1$, and
\[
 \widehat h=a1_U.
\]
In particular, every representation indexed by $C$ annihilates $h$.

Fix the smooth functional-calculus algebra $A_\gamma$ used in Carter's
definition of $m(C)$, with $\log_2(4/3)<\gamma<1$.  Its compact cutoff
property supplies $\varphi\in A_\gamma$ which is zero on a neighbourhood of
$0$ and satisfies $\varphi(a)=1$ \cite[Proposition 5.5]{Carter}.  Thus
$\varphi\{h\}\in m(C)$.  At every $\chi\in\wideG$, compatibility of the
functional calculus with representations gives
\[
 \widehat{\varphi\{h\}}(\chi)
 =\varphi(\widehat h(\chi))
 =1_U(\chi).
\]
Fourier injectivity on $L^1(G)$ yields $\varphi\{h\}=e_U$.  Therefore
$e_U\in m(C)\subseteq j(C)$.  If
$\operatorname{supp}\widehat g\subseteq U$, then $g=g*e_U$, so the ideal
property gives $g\in j(C)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite and compact-open synthesis]\label{thm:main}
Under the assumptions above:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item every finite subset of $\wideG$ is a set of synthesis for $\A$;
\item every compact-open subset of $\wideG$ is a set of synthesis for $\A$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
First let $F=\{\chi_1,\ldots,\chi_r\}\subseteq\wideG$.  Choose pairwise
disjoint compact-open neighbourhoods $V_i$ of the $\chi_i$, and let
$q_i\in\D(G)$ satisfy $\widehat q_i=1_{V_i}$.  Thus
$\widehat q_i(\chi_j)=\delta_{ij}$.

Take $f\in\ker(F)$ and choose $f_n\in\D(G)$ with $f_n\to f$ in $\A$.  Define
\[
 g_n=f_n-\sum_{i=1}^r\widehat f_n(\chi_i)q_i.
\]
By Lemma \ref{lem:test}(iii), $\widehat f_n(\chi_i)\to\widehat f(\chi_i)=0$,
so $g_n\to f$ in $\A$.  Moreover $\widehat g_n$ vanishes at every point of
$F$.  Since $\widehat g_n$ is locally constant, it vanishes on a neighbourhood
of each such point.  Hence
$U_n=\operatorname{supp}\widehat g_n$ is compact-open and disjoint from $F$.
Lemma \ref{lem:cutoff} gives $g_n\in j(F)$.  Closedness gives
$f\in j(F)$.  Thus $\ker(F)\subseteq j(F)$; the reverse inclusion follows
from the definition of $j(F)$.  This proves (a).

Now let $C\subseteq\wideG$ be compact-open and choose $q_C\in\D(G)$ with
$\widehat q_C=1_C$.  If $f\in\ker(C)$, then $f*q_C=0$ by Fourier injectivity.
For $f_n\in\D(G)$ converging to $f$, put
\[
 g_n=f_n-f_n*q_C.
\]
The Banach algebra inequality gives
\[
 \|g_n-f\|_\A
 \leq \|f_n-f\|_\A+\|(f_n-f)*q_C\|_\A
 \leq (1+\|q_C\|_\A)\|f_n-f\|_\A,
\]
so $g_n\to f$.  Also $\widehat g_n=\widehat f_n(1-1_C)$; hence its compact-open
support is disjoint from $C$.  Lemma \ref{lem:cutoff} gives
$g_n\in j(C)$, and closedness again gives $f\in j(C)$.  Therefore
$\ker(C)=j(C)$, proving (b).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Additive $p$-adic unipotent case]
Let $k$ be a non-archimedean local field and
$N=U_2(k)\cong(k,+)$.  Under Carter's hypotheses
$\Phi\in\Delta_2$, $1/\omega\in L^\Psi(N)$, and Hermitianness of
$L^\Phi(N,\omega)$, every finite subset and every compact-open subset of
\[
 \mathfrak n^*/\operatorname{Ad}^*(N)=\mathfrak n^*
 \cong \operatorname{Prim}_*(L^\Phi(N,\omega))
\]
is a set of synthesis.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The group $U_2(k)$ is the additive group of $k$, hence abelian and locally
elliptic.  Its coadjoint action is trivial, so Theorem \ref{thm:main} applies.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification, limitations, and novelty check}

The proof is structural and uses no numerical computation.  The independent
verification checklist confirms: (1) the $\Delta_2$ hypothesis supplies the
required dense Fourier test algebra; (2) the cutoff element satisfies every
condition in the source definition of $m(C)$; (3) finite interpolation
coefficients tend to zero by the continuous embedding $\A\hookrightarrow
L^1(G)$; and (4) the compact-open projection preserves convergence in the
Banach algebra norm.

The theorem does not address nonabelian $N$, nontrivial coadjoint orbit
spaces, or arbitrary closed subsets of $\wideG$.  A bounded novelty search on
21 July 2026 covered arXiv queries combining ``weighted Orlicz'', ``spectral
synthesis'', ``$p$-adic'', ``finite sets'', and ``compact open sets'', plus
the source's cited discussion.  It found the source paper but no exact prior
statement of this weighted-Orlicz criterion.  This is not an exhaustive
literature claim, so novelty confidence is moderate.
