\section*{Source and question}

The source paper is

\begin{quote}
T. A. Abrahamsen, J. Becerra Guerrero, R. Haller, V. Lima,
and M. Poldvere, \emph{Banach spaces where convex combinations of
relatively weakly open subsets of the unit ball are relatively weakly open},
arXiv:1806.10693.
\end{quote}

In Section 6, the authors ask:
\begin{quote}
If both \(X\) and \(Y\) have property \(\CWO\), does the injective tensor
product \(X\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}Y\) also have property \(\CWO\)?
They also ask the analogous question for \(\CWOS\) and \(\CWOB\).
\end{quote}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{figures/open_problem_crop.png}
\end{center}

This packet records two positive subcases. First, a finite-dimensional space
has \(\CWO\) if and only if it has the source paper's property \(\co\);
therefore \(C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}E\) has \(\CWO\) whenever
\(K\) is scattered locally compact and \(E\) is finite dimensional with
\(\CWO\). Second, scalar scattered \(C_0\)-spaces are stable under finite
injective tensor products.

\section*{Definitions}

A Banach space \(X\) has property \(\CWO\) if every finite convex combination
of relatively weakly open subsets of \(B_X\) is relatively weakly open in
\(B_X\). The source paper also defines the pointwise variants \(\CWOS\) and
\(\CWOB\), and \(\CWO\) implies both.

A topological space is scattered if every nonempty subspace has an isolated
point.

\section*{Idea of the proof}

The source paper proves that, if \(M\) is scattered locally compact
Hausdorff and \(E\) is finite dimensional with property \(\co\), then
\(C_0(M,E)\) has property \(\CWO\) (Theorem 2.5 in the PDF). The new point
recorded here is that, in finite dimensions, \(\CWO\) already forces
\(\co\). Given a convex decomposition \(x=\sum_i\lambda_i x_i\), apply
\(\CWO\) to small relative balls around the \(x_i\)'s. This gives local
surjectivity of the convex-combination map. A standard finite-dimensional
continuous selection theorem for convex fibers then turns local
surjectivity into the continuous local decomposition required by \(\co\).

For scalar scattered \(C_0\)-spaces, the tensor-product question reduces to
topology. The injective tensor product
\(C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps} C_0(L)\) is canonically \(C_0(K\times L)\),
and finite products of scattered spaces are scattered.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(E\) be a finite-dimensional Banach space. Then \(E\) has property
\(\CWO\) if and only if \(E\) has property \(\co\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The implication \(\co\Rightarrow \CWO\) is Proposition 2.2 of the source
paper; the proof is short, so we recall the mechanism. In finite dimension,
weak and norm topologies agree. If \(x=\sum_i\lambda_i x_i\) with
\(x_i\in U_i\), where the \(U_i\)'s are relatively open in \(B_E\), property
\(\co\) gives continuous local decompositions of every \(u\) near \(x\) into
\(u=\sum_i\lambda_i v_i(u)\), with \(v_i(u)\) still inside \(U_i\). Thus
\(\sum_i\lambda_i U_i\) is relatively open in \(B_E\).

Conversely, assume \(E\) has \(\CWO\). Fix
\[
  x=\sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i x_i,\qquad
  x_i\in B_E,\quad \lambda_i>0,\quad \sum_i\lambda_i=1,
\]
and fix \(\varepsilon>0\). Choose \(0<\eta<\varepsilon\), and set
\[
  U_i=\{y\in B_E:\|y-x_i\|<\eta/2\}.
\]
By \(\CWO\), the set \(C=\sum_i\lambda_i U_i\) is relatively open in \(B_E\)
and contains \(x\). Hence there is \(\delta>0\) such that
\[
  D:=B(x,\delta)\cap B_E\subset C.
\]
Now put
\[
  A_i=\{y\in B_E:\|y-x_i\|\leq \eta\},\qquad
  A=\prod_{i=1}^n A_i,
\]
and let \(T:A\to E\) be the affine restriction of the linear map
\[
  T(y_1,\ldots,y_n)=\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i y_i.
\]
For every \(u\in D\), the fiber
\[
  \Phi(u)=\{(y_1,\ldots,y_n)\in A:T(y_1,\ldots,y_n)=u\}
\]
is nonempty, compact, and convex. Nonemptiness follows from \(D\subset C\)
and \(U_i\subset A_i\).

The multifunction \(\Phi:D\to 2^A\) is lower semicontinuous. Indeed, if
\(O\) is relatively open in \(A\), then the elementary open mapping theorem
for linear maps on finite-dimensional convex sets gives that \(T(O)\) is
relatively open in \(T(A)\). Consequently
\[
  \{u\in D:\Phi(u)\cap O\neq\emptyset\}=D\cap T(O)
\]
is relatively open in \(D\). A finite-dimensional form of Michael's
selection theorem for lower semicontinuous convex-valued maps now gives a
continuous selection \(s=(s_1,\ldots,s_n):D\to A\).

For every \(u\in D\),
\[
  u=\sum_i\lambda_i s_i(u),
  \qquad \|s_i(u)-x_i\|\leq\eta<\varepsilon.
\]
This is exactly property \(\co\) at the point \(x\). Since the decomposition
and \(\varepsilon\) were arbitrary, \(E\) has \(\co\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(K\) be a scattered locally compact Hausdorff space, and let \(E\) be a
finite-dimensional Banach space with \(\CWO\). Then
\[
  C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}E\cong C_0(K,E)
\]
has property \(\CWO\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the previous theorem, \(E\) has property \(\co\). The conclusion is
exactly Theorem 2.5 of Abrahamsen--Becerra Guerrero--Haller--Lima--Poldvere,
together with the standard identification
\(C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}E\cong C_0(K,E)\) for finite-dimensional
coefficient spaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}
Let \(K\) and \(L\) be scattered locally compact Hausdorff spaces. Then
\[
  C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps} C_0(L)
\]
has property \(\CWO\). In particular, the tensor-product question from
arXiv:1806.10693 has an affirmative answer for pairs of scalar scattered
\(C_0\)-spaces.

More generally, if \(K_1,\ldots,K_n\) are scattered locally compact Hausdorff
spaces, then
\[
  C_0(K_1)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}\cdots
  \widehat{\otimes}_{\eps} C_0(K_n)
\]
has property \(\CWO\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We first note that a finite product of scattered spaces is scattered. It
suffices to prove the two-factor case. Let \(A\) be a nonempty subset of
\(K\times L\). The projection \(p_K(A)\) is a nonempty subset of \(K\), so
there exist \(x\in p_K(A)\) and an open set \(U\subset K\) such that
\[
  U\cap p_K(A)=\{x\}.
\]
The fiber
\[
  A_x=\{y\in L:(x,y)\in A\}
\]
is a nonempty subset of \(L\), hence has an isolated point \(y\). Choose an
open set \(V\subset L\) with \(V\cap A_x=\{y\}\). Then
\[
  (U\times V)\cap A=\{(x,y)\},
\]
so \(A\) has an isolated point. Therefore \(K\times L\) is scattered. Since
finite products of locally compact Hausdorff spaces are locally compact
Hausdorff, \(K\times L\) is scattered locally compact Hausdorff.

The canonical bilinear map
\[
  (f,g)\mapsto [(s,t)\mapsto f(s)g(t)]
\]
extends to the standard isometric identification
\[
  C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}C_0(L)\cong C_0(K\times L).
\]
By Theorem 2.5 of Abrahamsen--Becerra Guerrero--Haller--Lima--Poldvere,
applied to the scalar field as the finite-dimensional coefficient space,
\(C_0(M)\) has property \(\CWO\) whenever \(M\) is scattered locally compact
Hausdorff. Applying this with \(M=K\times L\) proves the two-factor case.
The finite-factor statement follows by induction, using the same argument
and associativity of the injective tensor product for these canonical
\(C_0\)-identifications.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification and novelty notes}

The scalar \(C_0\)-tensor proof uses three ingredients:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Theorem 2.5 of the source paper, specialized to scalar \(C_0(M)\).
\item The standard locally compact tensor identity
  \(C_0(K)\widehat{\otimes}_{\eps}C_0(L)\cong C_0(K\times L)\).
\item The elementary projection-fiber proof that finite products of scattered
  spaces are scattered.
\end{enumerate}

The finite-dimensional coefficient upgrade additionally uses a standard
finite-dimensional form of Michael's continuous selection theorem for
lower-semicontinuous convex-valued maps.

Cheap run indexes were searched for arXiv:1806.10693, ``property CWO'',
``property (co)'', ``finite dimensional'', ``injective tensor product'', and
related phrases. No prior packet for this finite-dimensional equivalence or
the tensor upgrade was found. External exact-phrase searches for the CWO
tensor question found the source paper but no later explicit full resolution.

The novelty confidence is low for the scalar \(C_0\)-tensor corollary and
moderate for the finite-dimensional \(\CWO\Rightarrow\co\) selection
argument. The packet is not a full solution to Question 6(c).

\section*{Limitations}

This does not address arbitrary pairs of \(\CWO\) spaces, nor the question
for spaces with only \(\CWOS\) or only \(\CWOB\). It also does not produce a
counterexample or obstruction for the general injective tensor product.

\section*{Human-review recommendation}

Check the finite-dimensional selection step proving \(\CWO\Rightarrow\co\).
The key point is the lower semicontinuity of the convex fiber map. Then check
the locally compact \(C_0\)-tensor identifications used in the corollaries.
