\section*{Source and Target}

The source is Max Sch\"olpple and Ingo Steinwart,
\emph{Which Spaces can be Embedded in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces?},
arXiv:2312.14711 \cite{SS}. In Section 4.2, Theorem 12 proves that for bounded
subsets of \(\R^d\) with non-empty interior there is no bounded-kernel RKHS
\[
  \Hol^\alpha(X)\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow \linf(X)
\]
when \(\alpha<d/2\). The paper then says that this is tight up to the border
case \(\alpha=d/2\).

This packet settles the bounded-kernel critical endpoint \(\alpha=d/2\) on
cubes in every dimension, under the standard Holder endpoint conventions
explained below.

\section*{Endpoint Convention}

For non-integer \(s>0\), \(\Hol^s([0,1]^d)\) denotes the usual bounded
\(C^{\lfloor s\rfloor,s-\lfloor s\rfloor}\) Holder class. For \(s=1\), it
denotes the bounded Lipschitz class. At higher integer \(s\), the source
paper's generalized Holder notation is convention-dependent; the proof below
applies to the classical \(C^s\) endpoint, to \(C^{s-1,1}\), and to the
Holder-Zygmund/Besov endpoint \(B^s_{\infty,\infty}\). The common property
used by the proof is the elementary character estimate
\[
  \norm{e_m}_{\Hol^s([0,1]^d)}
  \le C_{d,s}(1+\abs m)^s,\qquad
  e_m(x)=\exp(2\pi i\,m\cdot x),\quad m\in\Z^d .
\]
For \(C^{k,\theta}\) this follows by differentiating \(e_m\) up to order
\(k=\lfloor s\rfloor\) and taking the \(\theta\)-Holder seminorm of those
derivatives; for Lipschitz and \(C^k\) it is immediate.

\section*{Result}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:all-d}
Let \(d\ge1\) and put \(s=d/2\). There is no bounded-kernel reproducing kernel
Hilbert space \(H\) on \([0,1]^d\) such that
\[
  \Hol^s([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow \linf([0,1]^d)
\]
continuously.
\end{theorem}

In particular, the bounded-kernel Holder endpoint left open by the source
paper has a negative answer for every dimension \(d\).

\section*{Idea of the Proof}

The proof uses only Fourier characters. If such an RKHS sandwich existed, then
each periodic character \(e_m(x)=\exp(2\pi i\,m\cdot x)\) would have a Hilbert
feature vector \(J e_m\). Bounded point evaluations give uniformly bounded
Riesz representatives \(v_t\), indexed by \(t\in\T^d\).

For a finite frequency set \(F\subset\Z^d\setminus\{0\}\), project the
representatives \(v_t\) onto the finite-dimensional space spanned by
\(\{J e_m:m\in F\}\). The Fourier coefficients \(u_m\) of this projected
feature map satisfy a Bessel bound
\[
  \sum_{m\in F}\norm{u_m}^2\le C .
\]
On the other hand, reproducing the character \(e_m\), together with
\(\norm{e_m}_{\Hol^{d/2}}\lesssim |m|^{d/2}\), forces
\[
  \norm{u_m}\gtrsim |m|^{-d/2}.
\]
Thus the Bessel bound would imply
\[
  \sum_{m\in F}\frac{1}{(1+|m|)^d}\le C .
\]
Letting \(F\) exhaust \(\Z^d\setminus\{0\}\) contradicts the critical
divergence of \(\sum_{m\ne0}|m|^{-d}\).

\section*{Finite Character Lemma}

Let \(\T^d=\R^d/\Z^d\), with normalized Haar measure. We write the proof over
complex scalars. A real factorization may be complexified in the usual way, so
proving the complex statement rules out both real and complex RKHS sandwiches.
Throughout this section Hilbert inner products are linear in the first
variable.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:characters}
Let \(E\) be a Banach space of functions on \([0,1]^d\). Assume that for some
\(s>0\) and \(M>0\), every character
\[
  e_m(x)=\exp(2\pi i\,m\cdot x),\qquad m\in\Z^d,
\]
belongs to \(E\) and satisfies
\[
  \norm{e_m}_E\le M(1+\abs m)^s .
\]
Suppose there are a Hilbert space \(\mathcal H\) and bounded linear maps
\[
  J:E\to\mathcal H,\qquad R:\mathcal H\to\linf([0,1]^d)
\]
such that \(RJf=f\) for every \(f\in E\). Put
\[
  a=\norm J,\qquad b=\norm R .
\]
Then for every finite \(F\subset\Z^d\setminus\{0\}\),
\[
  \sum_{m\in F}\frac{1}{(1+\abs m)^{2s}}\le a^2b^2M^2 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose the standard half-open fundamental-domain section
\(\sigma:\T^d\to[0,1]^d\), and define
\[
  (Bh)(t)=Rh(\sigma(t)),\qquad t\in\T^d .
\]
Then \(\norm B\le b\), and
\[
  BJe_m(t)=e_m(\sigma(t))=e_m(t).
\]

For each \(t\in\T^d\), the functional \(h\mapsto (Bh)(t)\) on \(\mathcal H\)
has norm at most \(b\). Let \(v_t\in\mathcal H\) be its Riesz representative,
so that
\[
  (Bh)(t)=\langle h,v_t\rangle_{\mathcal H},
  \qquad \norm{v_t}_{\mathcal H}\le b .
\]

Fix a finite set \(F\subset\Z^d\setminus\{0\}\), and let
\[
  E_F=\operatorname{span}\{J e_m:m\in F\}\subset\mathcal H .
\]
Let \(P_F\) be the orthogonal projection onto \(E_F\). The map
\[
  t\longmapsto P_F v_t
\]
is a continuous \(E_F\)-valued function, because \(E_F\) is finite-dimensional
and, for every \(h\in E_F\), the scalar function
\(\langle h,P_Fv_t\rangle=\langle h,v_t\rangle=(Bh)(t)\) is a finite linear
combination of continuous characters.

For \(m\in F\), define the vector-valued Fourier coefficient at index \(-m\)
\[
  u_m=\int_{\T^d}e_m(t)\,P_Fv_t\,dt\in E_F .
\]
Parseval's inequality for the continuous \(E_F\)-valued function
\(t\mapsto P_Fv_t\) gives
\[
  \sum_{m\in F}\norm{u_m}_{\mathcal H}^2
  \le \int_{\T^d}\norm{P_Fv_t}_{\mathcal H}^2\,dt
  \le b^2 .
\]
For each \(m\in F\), since \(Je_m\in E_F\),
\[
  1
  =
  \int_{\T^d}e_m(t)\overline{e_m(t)}\,dt
  =
  \int_{\T^d}\langle Je_m,P_Fv_t\rangle\overline{e_m(t)}\,dt
  =
  \langle Je_m,u_m\rangle .
\]
Consequently,
\[
  1
  \le \norm{Je_m}_{\mathcal H}\norm{u_m}_{\mathcal H}
  \le aM(1+\abs m)^s\norm{u_m}_{\mathcal H}.
\]
Thus
\[
  \norm{u_m}_{\mathcal H}^2
  \ge \frac{1}{a^2M^2(1+\abs m)^{2s}}.
\]
Combining this lower bound with the Bessel upper bound proves the lemma.
\end{proof}

\section*{Proof of Theorem \ref{thm:all-d}}

Assume, toward a contradiction, that such an RKHS \(H\) exists, and let
\[
  J:\Hol^s([0,1]^d)\to H,\qquad
  R:H\to\linf([0,1]^d)
\]
be the two continuous inclusions, where \(s=d/2\). After complexifying
\(H,J,R\) if necessary, use the same notation for the corresponding maps on
complex-valued functions.

The endpoint Holder norm satisfies the character estimate
\[
  \norm{e_m}_{\Hol^s([0,1]^d)}\le M(1+\abs m)^s
\]
for a constant \(M\) depending only on \(d\) and the chosen equivalent endpoint
norm. Therefore Lemma \ref{lem:characters} gives
\[
  \sum_{m\in F}\frac{1}{(1+\abs m)^d}\le \norm J^2\norm R^2M^2
\]
for every finite \(F\subset\Z^d\setminus\{0\}\).

Taking \(F_N=\{m\in\Z^d\setminus\{0\}:\abs m\le N\}\) gives a uniform bound on
the partial sums of
\[
  \sum_{m\in\Z^d\setminus\{0\}}\frac{1}{(1+\abs m)^d}.
\]
But this series diverges. Indeed, the annulus
\(\{m\in\Z^d:2^j\le \abs m<2^{j+1}\}\) contains at least \(c_d2^{jd}\)
lattice points for all large \(j\), and each term on that annulus is at least
\(C_d2^{-jd}\). Hence each dyadic annulus contributes a positive amount
bounded below independently of \(j\). This contradiction proves the theorem.
\qed

\section*{Verification and Novelty Notes}

The proof is deterministic and uses no numerical computation. The only
analytic inputs are the Riesz representation theorem in Hilbert space,
finite-dimensional Hilbert projections, vector-valued Parseval/Bessel
inequality on the torus \cite{Rudin}, the elementary Holder norm estimate for
Fourier characters, and the critical lattice-sum divergence
\(\sum_{m\in\Z^d\setminus\{0\}}|m|^{-d}=\infty\).

The local run indexes were searched for arXiv id 2312.14711 and for the
endpoint keywords Hol to smoothness \(d/2\), \(\alpha=d/2\), bounded kernel,
Holder-Zygmund, Besov critical endpoint, and Hilbert factorization. The exact
hits were the previous two-dimensional packet, its bundled one-dimensional
companion packet, and the superseded attempt note.

Bounded web searches for the source title plus \texttt{border}, for

\texttt{Besov critical RKHS bounded kernel} found the source
arXiv record and no exact later answer to this endpoint.

\section*{Scope and Remaining Border Cases}

This packet settles the bounded-kernel endpoint
\[
  \Hol^{d/2}([0,1]^d)\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow \ell_\infty([0,1]^d)
\]
for every \(d\ge1\), in the standard endpoint conventions above. It does not
settle every Holder-to-Holder border case
\[
  \Hol^\alpha\hookrightarrow H\hookrightarrow \Hol^\beta,
  \qquad \alpha-\beta=d/2.
\]
Those are harder classification questions and may have mixed positive and
negative outcomes.

\section*{Human Review Recommendation}

Review should focus on Lemma \ref{lem:characters}. In particular, check the
finite-dimensional projection \(P_Fv_t\), the Fourier coefficient convention,
and the reduction from a cube RKHS sandwich to bounded feature vectors for
characters on the torus. Once these are accepted, the all-dimensional
contradiction is exactly the critical divergence of
\(\sum_{m\ne0}|m|^{-d}\).
