\section{The source problem}

Chung and Shapiro consider one-dimensional local Hamiltonians \(H\) for
which \(\Ker H\) is finite dimensional and \(\operatorname{sgn}(H)\) is
local.  They call this their tentative mobility-gap space
\(\mathcal I_{\mathrm{mg,v2}}\).  Their sign-initial topology has too many
components, so they propose either changing the topology---for example to
the operator-norm subspace topology---or strengthening the space, and
postpone both investigations \cite[Definition 7.5 and Example 7.6]{CS1}.
The exact source passage is reproduced below.

The norm-subspace proposal has the opposite problem from the sign-initial
topology: it has too \emph{few} components.  In class AIII the entire
tentative space is path connected.  The proof also identifies the precise
boundary.  If an essential gap is imposed, the chiral block is Fredholm and
integer indices survive.  Without it, compact singular values suppress
arbitrarily large changes in the polar part.

\section{Proof intuition}

An AIII Hamiltonian is determined by one off-diagonal operator \(A\).  The
tentative mobility-gap assumptions say exactly that \(A\) and \(A^*\) have
finite-dimensional kernels and that the polar partial isometry of \(A\) is
local.  First deform \(A\) to that polar part.  Pair as many kernel and
cokernel directions as possible by a finite-rank path.  What remains is
either an isometry or a coisometry.

Every isometry is a strong-operator limit of unitaries which differ from the
identity by finite rank.  Such a strong path need not be norm continuous,
and its half-space index may jump.  Now multiply on the right by a fixed
positive, injective compact operator \(D\).  Strong continuity becomes norm
continuity because multiplication by a compact operator upgrades strong
convergence on bounded sets to norm convergence.  Since \(D\) has dense
range, the polar part remains the chosen isometry or unitary.  Thus the path
stays inside the tentative space while erasing all index information.

\section{The off-diagonal model}

Let \(\HH\) be a separable infinite-dimensional complex Hilbert space and
let \(\Lambda\) be an orthogonal projection with both
\(\Lambda\HH\) and \(\Lambda^\perp\HH\) infinite dimensional.  Put
\[
 \LL_\Lambda
 :=\{A\in\BB(\HH):[\Lambda,A]\in\KK(\HH)\}.
\]
For an operator \(A\), write \(A=U|A|\) for its polar decomposition, with
\(U=0\) on \(\Ker A\).  Define
\[
 \MM_\Lambda
 :=\left\{A\in\LL_\Lambda:
 \dim\Ker A<\infty,\ \dim\Ker A^*<\infty,\
 U\in\LL_\Lambda\right\},
\]
with the operator-norm subspace topology.

On \(\widehat\HH=\HH\oplus\HH\), fix the chiral grading
\(\Gamma=\operatorname{diag}(I,-I)\) and spatial projection
\(\widehat\Lambda=\Lambda\oplus\Lambda\).  The map
\[
 A\longmapsto H_A:=
 \begin{pmatrix}0&A^*\\A&0\end{pmatrix}
\]
is an isometry onto the self-adjoint operators anticommuting with \(\Gamma\).
Moreover,
\[
 \Ker H_A=\Ker A\oplus\Ker A^*,\qquad
 \operatorname{sgn}(H_A)=
 \begin{pmatrix}0&U^*\\U&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Consequently \(A\in\MM_\Lambda\) is exactly the off-diagonal-block form of
the source's tentative AIII v2 conditions.  The chiral bulk
non-triviality condition is unaffected: all polar parts below are local
Fredholm partial isometries, and finite-rank modifications preserve the
essential non-triviality of the two spatial halves.

\section{Two smoothing lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[compact smoothing of strong paths]\label{lem:smoothing}
Let \(T:[0,1]\to\BB(\HH)\) be strongly continuous and uniformly bounded.
If \(D\) is compact, then \(t\mapsto T(t)D\) is norm continuous.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix \(t_0\) and approximate \(D\) in norm by a finite-rank operator \(F\).
Strong continuity is uniform in operator norm on the finite-dimensional
space \(\Ran F\), so
\(\|(T(t)-T(t_0))F\|\to0\).  If
\(\sup_t\|T(t)\|\leq M\), then
\[
 \|(T(t)-T(t_0))(D-F)\|\leq2M\|D-F\|.
\]
First choose \(F\) and then let \(t\to t_0\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[a local strong path to an isometry]\label{lem:strong}
Let \(V\in\LL_\Lambda\) be an isometry with finite-dimensional cokernel.
There is a strongly continuous path \(W:[0,1]\to\BB(\HH)\) such that
\[
 W(0)=I,\qquad W(1)=V,
\]
and, for every \(t<1\), \(W(t)\) is a unitary satisfying
\(W(t)-I\in\KK(\HH)\).  In particular every \(W(t)\) is
\(\Lambda\)-local.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose finite-dimensional subspaces
\[
 \{0\}=E_0\subset E_1\subset E_2\subset\cdots
\]
with dense union.  For every \(n\geq1\), extend the finite-dimensional
isometry \(V|_{E_n}:E_n\to VE_n\) to a unitary \(W_n\) on \(\HH\) which is
the identity on the orthogonal complement of \(E_n+VE_n\).  Thus
\[
 W_n|_{E_n}=V|_{E_n},\qquad W_n-I\text{ has finite rank}.
\]
Set \(W_0=I\).  The unitaries \(W_n\) and \(W_{n+1}\) agree with \(V\) on
\(E_n\).  Therefore \(W_{n+1}W_n^*\) fixes \(VE_n\), differs from \(I\)
by finite rank, and has a finite-rank self-adjoint logarithm \(X_n\) which
vanishes on \(VE_n\):
\[
 W_{n+1}W_n^*=e^{iX_n}.
\]
Join \(W_n\) to \(W_{n+1}\) by
\(s\mapsto e^{isX_n}W_n\), and place these paths on consecutive intervals
whose right endpoints tend to \(1\).  Every intermediate unitary differs
from \(I\) by finite rank and agrees with \(V\) on \(E_n\).

For \(x\in\HH\), approximation by \(E_n\) gives, uniformly on the
\(n\)-th tail interval,
\[
 \|(W(t)-V)x\|\leq2\,\operatorname{dist}(x,E_n)\longrightarrow0.
\]
Defining \(W(1)=V\) therefore gives the required strongly continuous path.
\end{proof}

\section{Complete collapse in the norm-subspace topology}

\begin{theorem}[path connectedness]\label{thm:main}
The space \(\MM_\Lambda\) is path connected.  Equivalently, the tentative
AIII v2 mobility-gap Hamiltonian space, equipped with the operator-norm
subspace topology, satisfies
\[
 \pi_0(\mathcal I_{\mathrm{mg,v2,AIII}})=\{*\}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose once and for all a positive, injective compact operator
\(D\in\LL_\Lambda\).  For example, choose orthonormal bases separately in
\(\Lambda\HH\) and \(\Lambda^\perp\HH\), let \(D\) be diagonal with
strictly positive entries tending to zero, and note that \(D\Lambda=\Lambda
D\).  We show that every \(A\in\MM_\Lambda\) can be joined to \(D\).

\emph{Step 1: polar deformation.}
Write \(A=U|A|\) and put \(P=U^*U=I-P_{\Ker A}\).  The path
\[
 A_s=U\big((1-s)|A|+sP\big),\qquad 0\leq s\leq1,
\]
joins \(A\) to \(U\).  On \(P\HH\), the positive factor is injective with
dense range; for \(s>0\) it is bounded below.  Thus every \(A_s\) has polar
part \(U\), kernel \(\Ker U\), and cokernel \(\Ker U^*\).  It belongs to
\(\MM_\Lambda\) because \(\LL_\Lambda\) is a C\(^*\)-algebra and
\(|A|,U,P\in\LL_\Lambda\).

\emph{Step 2: pair finite defects.}
Let
\[
 k=\dim\Ker U,\qquad c=\dim\Ker U^*,\qquad m=\min(k,c).
\]
Choose \(m\)-dimensional subspaces \(K_0\subseteq\Ker U\) and
\(C_0\subseteq\Ker U^*\), and a partial isometry \(J:K_0\to C_0\).
The path
\[
 U_s=U+sJ,\qquad 0\leq s\leq1,
\]
lies in \(\MM_\Lambda\).  For \(s>0\), its polar part is \(U+J\);
all changes are finite rank.  At the endpoint \(V=U+J\), either
\[
 V^*V=I\quad(k\leq c)
 \qquad\text{or}\qquad
 VV^*=I\quad(c\leq k).
\]
Thus \(V\) is an isometry or a coisometry (and is unitary if \(k=c\)).

\emph{Step 3: erase an isometry by compact attenuation.}
Suppose first that \(V\) is an isometry.  The path
\[
 V\big((1-s)I+sD\big),\qquad 0\leq s\leq1,
\]
joins \(V\) to \(VD\) inside \(\MM_\Lambda\), with constant polar part
\(V\).  Let \(W\) be the strong path from Lemma~\ref{lem:strong}.
Lemma~\ref{lem:smoothing} says that
\[
 t\longmapsto W(1-t)D
\]
is a norm-continuous path from \(VD\) to \(D\).  Its polar part is
\(W(1-t)\): at interior parameters this is a local unitary, and at the
initial endpoint it is \(V\).  The kernels and cokernels are always finite.

If \(V\) is a coisometry, apply the preceding construction to the isometry
\(V^*\) and take adjoints.  This again joins \(V\) to the self-adjoint
operator \(D\).  Concatenating the three steps joins every \(A\) to the same
base point \(D\), proving path connectedness.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:index}
The map
\[
 A\longmapsto
 \ind\!\left(\Lambda\,\operatorname{polar}(A)\,\Lambda+\Lambda^\perp\right)
\]
is not continuous, and is not constant on norm paths in
\(\MM_\Lambda\).
\end{corollary}

\section{An explicit dynamically localized index jump}

The preceding theorem classifies the tentative deterministic space.  The
failure of the raw norm topology persists even if one keeps the source's
stronger dynamical localization and uniformly finite spectral-degeneracy
conditions.

Let \(\HH=\ell^2(\Z)\), with basis \((\delta_n)_{n\in\Z}\), and let
\(\Lambda\) project onto the span of \(\{\delta_n:n\geq1\}\).  Let
\[
 R\delta_n=\delta_{n+1},\qquad
 D\delta_n=2^{-|n|}\delta_n.
\]
For \(N\geq1\), define a finite cyclic shift
\[
 C_N\delta_n=
 \begin{cases}
 \delta_{n+1},&-N\leq n<N,\\
 \delta_{-N},&n=N,\\
 \delta_n,&|n|>N.
 \end{cases}
\]
Then \(C_N-I\) has finite coordinate support and \(C_N\to R\) strongly.

\begin{proposition}[explicit index-changing mobility-gap path]\label{prop:explicit}
There is a strongly continuous path of local unitaries
\(U:[0,1]\to\BB(\HH)\) such that
\[
 U(0)=I,\qquad U(1)=R,
\]
and \(U(t)-I\) has finite coordinate support for every \(t<1\).
Consequently
\[
 A_t:=U(t)D,\qquad
 H_t:=\begin{pmatrix}0&A_t^*\\A_t&0\end{pmatrix}
\]
is an operator-norm-continuous path in the dynamically localized AIII
mobility-gap space, while
\[
 \ind_\Lambda(\operatorname{polar}A_t)=
 \begin{cases}
 0,&t<1,\\
 -1,&t=1.
 \end{cases}
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Put \(t_N=1-2^{-N}\).  Connect \(I\) to \(C_1\) on
\([0,t_1]\).  On \([t_N,t_{N+1}]\), connect \(C_N\) to \(C_{N+1}\)
inside the finite-dimensional unitary group supported on
\(\operatorname{span}\{\delta_n:|n|\leq N+1\}\), choosing the path to agree
with \(R\) on
\[
 E_N:=\operatorname{span}\{\delta_n:-N\leq n<N\}.
\]
This is possible because \(C_N\) and \(C_{N+1}\) already agree there.
Define \(U(1)=R\).  Agreement on \(E_N\) proves strong continuity at \(1\).

More is true after attenuation.  For \(t\in[t_N,t_{N+1}]\),
\[
 (U(t)-R)DP_{E_N}=0,\qquad
 \|DP_{E_N^\perp}\|=2^{-N}.
\]
Hence
\[
 \|(U(t)-R)D\|\leq2^{1-N}\longrightarrow0.
\]
Thus \(t\mapsto A_t\), and therefore \(t\mapsto H_t\), is norm continuous.
Since \(D\) is positive, injective, and has dense range,
\(\operatorname{polar}(A_t)=U(t)\).  For \(t<1\), \(U(t)\) is a
finite-rank perturbation of \(I\), so its half-space index is zero.  At the
endpoint, the compression of \(R\) is the unilateral shift and has index
\(-1\).

It remains to check the stronger mobility-gap conditions.  Let
\[
 H_D=\begin{pmatrix}0&D\\D&0\end{pmatrix},
\qquad Q_t=\operatorname{diag}(I,U(t)).
\]
Then \(H_t=Q_tH_DQ_t^*\), so the spectrum is independent of \(t\):
it consists of the eigenvalues \(\pm2^{-|n|}\), with uniformly bounded
multiplicity, accumulating only at zero, and zero is not an eigenvalue.
For \(t<1\), \(Q_t-I\) has finite coordinate support.  Therefore every
bounded Borel function \(f(H_t)=Q_tf(H_D)Q_t^*\) is position diagonal
outside a finite coordinate set and satisfies the source's dynamical decay
estimate (with constants depending on \(t\)).  At \(t=1\), \(R\) has
propagation one, so the same conclusion holds.  Taking, for example,
\(\Delta=(-2,2)\) verifies the mobility-gap conditions along the path.
\end{proof}

The parenthetical qualification is essential: the raw norm-subspace
topology does not require localization constants to be uniform along a
path.  A stronger topology controlling those constants or controlling the
Fermi projection may still preserve the index; this is precisely the type
of topology studied in \cite{Sha}.

\section{Why the essential-gap result does not collapse}

For comparison, let
\[
 \FF_\Lambda:=\{A\in\LL_\Lambda:A\text{ is Fredholm}\}.
\]
Relative to
\(\HH=\Lambda^\perp\HH\oplus\Lambda\HH\), locality gives
\[
 A=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix},
\qquad b,c\in\KK.
\]
Thus \(A\) is Fredholm exactly when \(a\) and \(d\) are Fredholm.  Scaling
the compact off-diagonal blocks to zero and using fixed-index connectivity
of Fredholm operators gives
\[
 \pi_0(\FF_\Lambda)\cong\Z^2,\qquad
 A\longmapsto(\ind A,\ind d).
\]
On the ordinary-index-zero sector, the second coordinate alone yields
\(\Z\), the expected AIII entry.  This is the earlier substantial partial
result, now seen as the sharp boundary of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}: the
compact attenuator \(D\) is not Fredholm, so the collapsing path necessarily
leaves the essential-gap space.

\section{Verification, novelty, and scope}

Every construction above has an explicit membership check:
\begin{enumerate}
\item polar deformation keeps the polar part and finite defect spaces fixed;
\item defect pairing changes the polar part only by finite rank;
\item strong unitary paths consist of finite-rank perturbations of the
identity before their endpoint;
\item compact attenuation converts those strong paths into norm paths;
\item the explicit path is unitarily equivalent to a fixed diagonal compact
Hamiltonian, so spectral multiplicities and localization are transparent.
\end{enumerate}

The companion script checks finite cyclic shifts, the exact agreement
subspaces, polar identities on finite windows, and the decay of
\(\|(C_N-R)D\|\).  It is a diagnostic, not part of the proof.

A bounded search of the run indexes and primary arXiv sources found no prior
statement of this path-connectedness theorem or of the explicit
index-changing path.  Shapiro's earlier topology deliberately controls
mobility-gap data so that invariants remain continuous \cite{Sha}.  The
authors' 2026 Part~III paper proves the spectral-gap classification and
still postpones the mobility-gap Hamiltonian/projection connection
\cite[\S2]{CS3}.  Novelty confidence is \emph{moderate}: the compact
smoothing lemma is classical, but its use here gives a direct and apparently
new resolution of the source's proposed norm topology.

The result is full for the tentative v2 AIII space with the raw norm-subspace
topology.  It does not classify all ten symmetry classes, nor a topology
which imposes uniform localization constants or continuity of the Fermi
projection.
