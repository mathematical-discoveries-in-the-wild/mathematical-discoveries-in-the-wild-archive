\section*{Source and Target}

The source paper is Braga--Lancien--Petitjean--Prochazka's
arXiv:1909.12132 paper \cite{BLPP}.  The paper asks whether \(c_0\)
coarsely Lipschitz embeds into a separable dual space.  Its Theorem 6.2
proves a quantitative obstruction: if \(c_0\) coarse-Lipschitz embeds into
\(X^*\) with distortion strictly smaller than \(3/2\), then \(X\) contains
\(\ell_1\).  Immediately afterward, the paper obtains a distortion \(<2\)
obstruction for an auxiliary graph \(M\), while noting that it is not clear
whether the graph \(M\) is isometric to a subset of \(c_0\).

\section*{Result}

The \(3/2\) threshold in the source's grid argument can be replaced by \(2\).
No auxiliary graph is needed.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:grid}
Let \(X\) be a Banach space and let
\[
  f:\Grid(c_0)\cap 2B_{c_0}\longrightarrow X^*
\]
be a Lipschitz embedding with distortion strictly smaller than \(2\).  Then
\(X\) contains an isomorphic copy of \(\ell_1\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
After multiplying \(f\) by a scalar, choose \(D<2\) such that
\[
  \|u-v\|_\infty \le \|f(u)-f(v)\|\le D\|u-v\|_\infty
\]
for all \(u,v\in \Grid(c_0)\cap 2B_{c_0}\).  Pick \(\varepsilon>0\) with
\[
  4-\varepsilon-2D>0.
\]
Let \((e_k)\) be the canonical basis of \(c_0\).  For each \(k\in\N\), choose
\(x_k\in S_X\) such that
\[
  \langle x_k, f(2e_k)-f(-2e_k)\rangle \ge 4-\varepsilon.
\]
We prove that \((x_k)\) has no weakly Cauchy subsequence.

Let \(M=\{m_1<m_2<\cdots\}\) be an infinite subset of \(\N\).  Put
\[
  A=\{m_{2j+1}:j\in\N\}.
\]
For \(m\in\N\), define the signed truncation
\[
  s_A^m=\sum_{i\in A\cap[1,m]}e_i-\sum_{i\in [1,m]\setminus A}e_i
\]
and similarly \(s_{\N\setminus A}^m\).  These vectors belong to
\(\Grid(c_0)\cap B_{c_0}\).

If \(k=m_{2j+1}\in A\) and \(m\ge k\), then
\[
  \|s_A^m-2e_k\|_\infty=1
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \|s_{\N\setminus A}^m+2e_k\|_\infty=1.
\]
Therefore
\[
\begin{aligned}
  &\langle x_k, f(s_A^m)-f(s_{\N\setminus A}^m)\rangle \\
  &\quad\ge
  \langle x_k, f(2e_k)-f(-2e_k)\rangle
  -\|f(s_A^m)-f(2e_k)\|
  -\|f(s_{\N\setminus A}^m)-f(-2e_k)\|  \\
  &\quad\ge 4-\varepsilon-2D.
\end{aligned}
\]
If \(k=m_{2j}\notin A\) and \(m\ge k\), the same calculation with signs
reversed gives
\[
  \langle x_k, f(s_A^m)-f(s_{\N\setminus A}^m)\rangle
  \le -4+\varepsilon+2D.
\]

Let \(\mathcal U\) be a nonprincipal ultrafilter on \(\N\), and set
\[
  x^*=w^*\!-\!\lim_{m,\mathcal U}
  \bigl(f(s_A^m)-f(s_{\N\setminus A}^m)\bigr).
\]
The vectors inside the limit are norm bounded, so this weak-star ultralimit
exists in \(X^*\).  The preceding inequalities imply that, for every \(j\),
\[
  x^*(x_{m_{2j+1}})\ge 4-\varepsilon-2D
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  x^*(x_{m_{2j}})\le -4+\varepsilon+2D.
\]
Thus \((x_{m_j})\) is not weakly Cauchy.  Since \(M\) was arbitrary,
\((x_k)\) has no weakly Cauchy subsequence.  By Rosenthal's \(\ell_1\)
theorem, \(X\) contains an isomorphic copy of \(\ell_1\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:coarse}
If \(c_0\) coarse-Lipschitz embeds into a dual space \(X^*\) with
coarse-Lipschitz distortion strictly smaller than \(2\), then \(X\) contains
an isomorphic copy of \(\ell_1\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let \(F:c_0\to X^*\) be such an embedding.  There are constants
\(A,B,C,E>0\), with \(AC<2\), such that
\[
  \frac1A\|x-y\|_\infty-B
  \le \|F(x)-F(y)\|
  \le C\|x-y\|_\infty+E
\]
for all \(x,y\in c_0\).

For \(n\in\N\), define \(F_n(x)=F(nx)/n\) on
\(\Grid(c_0)\cap 2B_{c_0}\).  If \(u\ne v\) in this grid, then
\(\|u-v\|_\infty\ge 1\), and hence
\[
  \left(\frac1A-\frac{B}{n}\right)\|u-v\|_\infty
  \le \|F_n(u)-F_n(v)\|
  \le \left(C+\frac{E}{n}\right)\|u-v\|_\infty .
\]
For \(n\) large enough, the lower coefficient is positive and
\[
  \frac{C+E/n}{1/A-B/n}<2.
\]
Thus \(F_n\) is a Lipschitz embedding of the grid with distortion strictly
smaller than \(2\).  Theorem \ref{thm:grid} gives \(\ell_1\subset X\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
The space \(c_0\) does not coarse-Lipschitz embed with distortion strictly
smaller than \(2\) into any separable dual Banach space.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If \(X^*\) is separable and \(X\) contained an isomorphic copy of \(\ell_1\),
then the adjoint of the embedding \(\ell_1\hookrightarrow X\) would give a
bounded quotient map from \(X^*\) onto \(\ell_\infty\), impossible because a
quotient of a separable Banach space is separable.  Apply Theorem
\ref{thm:coarse}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Verification Notes}

The only new ingredient relative to Proposition 6.1 of the source paper is
the signed truncation \(s_A^m\).  For \(k\in A\), it is at distance \(1\) from
\(2e_k\), while \(s_{\N\setminus A}^m\) is at distance \(1\) from \(-2e_k\).
This keeps the two Lipschitz-error terms equal to \(D\), but the main
separation is \(\|2e_k-(-2e_k)\|_\infty=4\) rather than \(3\).  The
Rosenthal theorem step and the coarse scaling step are otherwise exactly the
same as in the source.

The result is partial.  It does not answer whether \(c_0\) coarse-Lipschitz
embeds into a separable dual with distortion at least \(2\), nor whether
\(c_0\) coarsely embeds into a separable dual without a distortion bound.
