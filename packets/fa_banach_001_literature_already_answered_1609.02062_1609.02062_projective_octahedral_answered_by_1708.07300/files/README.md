# Projective Octahedrality Question Answered By arXiv:1708.07300

Status: `literature_already_answered`.

Source/open-problem paper: Juhan Langemets, Vegard Lima, and Abraham Rueda
Zoca, *Octahedral norms in tensor products of Banach spaces*,
arXiv:1609.02062.

Source question: near the end of arXiv:1609.02062, after a partial positive
result for non-reflexive L-embedded spaces, the authors write that for general
Banach spaces \(X\) and \(Y\) the question whether \(X \widehat\otimes_\pi Y\)
has an octahedral norm whenever \(X\) and/or \(Y\) do remains open. This is the
same projective tensor product one-factor question cited there as
Question 4.4 of Langemets--Lima--Rueda Zoca, *Almost square and octahedral
norms in tensor products of Banach spaces*.

Supporting answer paper: Abraham Rueda Zoca, *Stability results of
octahedrality in tensor product spaces*, arXiv:1708.07300.

Answer: no. The supporting paper proves that there is a two-dimensional
complex Banach space \(Y\) such that
\[
  L_\infty^\mathbb C([0,1]) \widehat\otimes_\pi Y
\]
does not have an octahedral norm, while \(L_\infty^\mathbb C([0,1])\) has the
Daugavet property and hence has an octahedral norm. The abstract and the
remark after Theorem 2.2 explicitly state that this gives a negative answer to
Question 4.4.

This records an already-known later answer, not new run mathematics. It clears
the general projective tensor product octahedrality question left open at the
end of arXiv:1609.02062. The source paper's injective tensor product questions
are not the point of this packet; those are already handled within the source
paper itself.
