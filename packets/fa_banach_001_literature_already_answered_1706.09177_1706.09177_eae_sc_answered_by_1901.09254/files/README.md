# Literature Status: EAE/MC => SC Already Answered

Status: `literature_already_answered`

Source/open-problem paper: arXiv:1706.09177, S. ter Horst, M. Messerschmidt,
A. C. M. Ran, M. Roelands, and M. Wortel, "Equivalence after extension and
Schur coupling coincide for inessential operators."

Supporting answer paper: arXiv:1901.09254, S. ter Horst, M. Messerschmidt,
A. C. M. Ran, and M. Roelands, "Equivalence after extension and Schur
coupling do not coincide, on essentially incomparable Banach spaces."

## Identification

The source paper records in its Introduction, page 2, that Bart and
Tsekanovskii asked whether the three Banach-space operator relations EAE, MC,
and SC coincide, equivalently whether EAE/MC implies SC. The source paper says
this matter had not been settled and then proves the affirmative special case
for inessential operators as Theorem 1.1.

The later paper arXiv:1901.09254 explicitly takes up the same question. Its
abstract on page 1 says the general answer is negative and gives forward shift
operators on `ell^p` and `ell^q`, `p != q`, as EAE but not SC. The formal
statement is Theorem 2.1 on page 3: for essentially incomparable Banach spaces,
SC and EAE have different Fredholm-index requirements, and in particular SC
and EAE do not coincide.

## Scope

This packet does not claim new mathematical progress. It clears the broad
EAE/MC => SC question mentioned in arXiv:1706.09177 as already answered in the
later literature. It does not change the source paper's positive inessential
operator theorem, and it does not address later more refined questions about
Fredholm or relatively regular variants.
